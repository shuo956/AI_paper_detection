\section{Introduction}
Online academic submission platforms rely heavily on automated systems to filter out spam and ensure the integrity of submitted content. One common method employed by these systems is the use of reCAPTCHA, a challenge-response test designed to distinguish humans from bots. However, the integration of reCAPTCHA forms has been criticized for negatively impacting user experience and accessibility. This paper aims to explore the trade-offs between spam prevention and user satisfaction, with a particular focus on accessibility for individuals with disabilities.

\section{Related Work}
Previous studies have highlighted the importance of accessibility in web forms, emphasizing the need for inclusive design (Smith et al., 2020). Additionally, research on reCAPTCHA has primarily focused on its effectiveness in spam mitigation rather than its impact on user experience and accessibility (Doe et al., 2019). There is a gap in the literature regarding the specific challenges faced by users with disabilities when interacting with reCAPTCHA forms and potential solutions to improve their user experience.

\section{Methods/Experiments}
To evaluate the impact of reCAPTCHA forms, we conducted a user study involving participants with varying levels of disability. The study was divided into two phases:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Phase 1:} Participants were asked to submit a research paper using a platform with a traditional reCAPTCHA form.
    \item \textbf{Phase 2:} Participants were then asked to submit the same paper using a modified reCAPTCHA form designed to enhance accessibility.
\end{itemize}

Participants were surveyed before and after each phase to assess their satisfaction and ease of use. Data was collected through both quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews.

\subsection{Accessibility Issues}
Qualitative feedback from participants indicated several accessibility issues with traditional reCAPTCHA forms, including difficulties with visual elements and audio challenges. In contrast, the enhanced reCAPTCHA form received more positive feedback, with fewer reported issues.

\section{Discussion}
The results suggest that while reCAPTCHA forms are effective in reducing spam, they can significantly impact user satisfaction and accessibility. The enhanced reCAPTCHA form showed improved performance in terms of user satisfaction and ease of use. These findings highlight the need for further research and development to create reCAPTCHA forms that balance spam prevention with user experience and accessibility.

\section{Conclusion}
This study provides valuable insights into the challenges of integrating reCAPTCHA forms into online academic submission platforms. While reCAPTCHA is an essential tool for preventing spam, its implementation should be carefully considered to minimize negative impacts on user experience and accessibility. Future work should focus on developing more accessible and user-friendly reCAPTCHA alternatives.
