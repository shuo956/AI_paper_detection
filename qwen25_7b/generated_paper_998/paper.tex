This paper proposes a novel framework, \textbf{GR-MM-VAE}, combining Graph Regularized Principal Component Analysis (GR-PCA) and Multimodal Variational Autoencoders (MM-VAE) for robust anomaly detection in Internet of Things (IoT) sensor networks. \section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}
Internet of Things (IoT) sensor networks play a critical role in monitoring various environmental and industrial processes. Anomaly detection in these networks is essential for ensuring the reliability and safety of operations. Traditional methods often struggle with the complexity and heterogeneity of IoT data, necessitating advanced techniques for robust anomaly detection. 

Recent advancements in machine learning, particularly in deep learning and multimodal learning, have shown promising results in handling complex data structures and capturing intricate patterns. This paper builds on these advancements by proposing a novel framework that integrates Graph Regularized Principal Component Analysis (GR-PCA) and Multimodal Variational Autoencoders (MM-VAE) for robust anomaly detection in IoT sensor networks.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}
Several works have addressed anomaly detection in IoT sensor networks using various techniques. \cite{hammad2026communitybasedmodelsharinggeneralisation} presented a community-based model sharing approach for anomaly detection in IoT temperature sensor networks. \cite{chen2026xlinearlightweightaccuratemlpbased} introduced a lightweight MLP-based model for long-term time series forecasting with exogenous inputs, which could be adapted for anomaly detection. \cite{vo2026hellingermultimodalvariationalautoencoders} proposed Hellinger Multimodal Variational Autoencoders (HELVAE), which provide an effective solution for multimodal data modeling. However, none of these works have combined GR-PCA and MM-VAE specifically for anomaly detection in IoT sensor networks.
