\section{Introduction}
Image captioning is a critical task in computer vision that involves generating natural language descriptions for images. Despite advancements in deep learning techniques, current models often struggle with generating contextually rich and semantically meaningful captions. This paper proposes a new approach that leverages contextual understanding and semantic embedding to enhance the performance of image captioning systems.

\section{Related Work}
Recent advances in deep learning have led to the development of various architectures for image captioning, including Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), and Transformer models. While these approaches have shown promising results, they often rely solely on visual features extracted from images, which can limit their ability to capture complex contextual relationships. Our work builds upon these existing methods and introduces novel techniques to improve contextual understanding and semantic embedding.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
The proposed model consists of two main components: a Contextual Encoder and a Semantic Decoder. The Contextual Encoder captures the context within an image by analyzing local and global features, while the Semantic Decoder generates captions by embedding the contextual information into a semantic space.

\subsection{Contextual Encoder}
The Contextual Encoder is designed to extract both local and global features from the input image. It employs a multi-scale convolutional network to capture detailed local structures and a max-pooling layer to summarize global information. The combined features are then fed into a recurrent neural network to capture the temporal dynamics and contextual dependencies.

\subsection{Semantic Decoder}
The Semantic Decoder takes the contextual features generated by the encoder and maps them into a semantic space. This is achieved through a series of attention mechanisms that dynamically weigh the importance of different contextual elements. The decoder then generates the caption word-by-word, ensuring that each word is grounded in the semantic context of the image.
