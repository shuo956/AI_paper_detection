\section{Literature Review}
ReCAPTCHA has evolved significantly over the years to combat various forms of automated spam and abuse. Early versions relied on simple puzzles and tests that were easy for humans but difficult for bots (von Ahn \& Blum, 2003). However, modern CAPTCHAs have become increasingly sophisticated, incorporating visual and audio elements designed to challenge bots while remaining accessible to humans (Kohli et al., 2021).

Machine learning approaches have been increasingly applied to reCAPTCHA mechanisms. For example, Kohli et al. (2021) utilized CNNs to analyze image-based challenges, achieving high accuracy in distinguishing between human users and bots. Similarly, Wang and Zhang (2022) employed RNNs to analyze temporal patterns in user behavior, further reducing false positives. These studies highlight the potential of ML in improving reCAPTCHA systems but also point to ongoing challenges in terms of scalability and real-time performance.
