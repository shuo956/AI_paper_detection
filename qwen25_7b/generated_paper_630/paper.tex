\section{Introduction}
Scientific communication relies heavily on concise and informative abstracts to convey the essence of research findings. Manual generation of abstracts is time-consuming and prone to errors. Recent advancements in machine learning have made it possible to automate this process, leading to more efficient and consistent summaries. This study aims to assess the effectiveness of different machine learning models in generating high-quality abstracts for scientific papers.

\section{Related Work}
Previous studies have explored various approaches to automated abstract generation, including rule-based systems, statistical models, and neural networks. Rule-based systems rely on predefined templates and heuristics, which can be inflexible and limited in scope. Statistical models, such as sequence-to-sequence (seq2seq) models, have shown promise but often struggle with capturing complex sentence structures. Neural network-based models, especially those based on transformers, have recently demonstrated superior performance by leveraging self-attention mechanisms to capture long-range dependencies in text.

\section{Methods/Experiment}

Each model is fine-tuned using the collected dataset to generate abstracts.
