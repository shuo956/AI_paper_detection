\section{Introduction}
The rapid growth of digital communication has led to an explosion in textual data, making text classification a critical task in various applications such as sentiment analysis, spam detection, and document categorization. Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), particularly Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, have shown remarkable success in handling sequential data. However, the performance of these models can be significantly influenced by the preprocessing techniques applied to the input text. This paper investigates the impact of various preprocessing methods on the efficiency and accuracy of RNN-based text classification models.

\section{Related Work}
Previous studies have explored the effectiveness of different preprocessing techniques in text classification tasks. For instance, \cite{Li2019} evaluated the impact of stemming and lemmatization on the performance of LSTM models and found that while stemming improved speed, lemmatization enhanced accuracy. Similarly, \cite{Chen2020} compared tokenization methods and concluded that byte pair encoding (BPE) outperformed other methods. However, there is limited research on the combined effect of multiple preprocessing techniques on RNN models.

\begin{enumerate}

    \item Stemming and Lemmatization: These techniques will be compared to evaluate their impact on model performance.
    \item Feature Extraction: Word embeddings (e.g., GloVe, Word2Vec) will be used to convert raw text into numerical vectors.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Model Architecture}
The RNN model will consist of LSTM layers with different configurations. Hyperparameters such as learning rate, batch size, and number of hidden units will be optimized using grid search.
