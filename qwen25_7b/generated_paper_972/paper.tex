\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

As black box models and pretrained models gain traction in time series applications, understanding and explaining their predictions becomes increasingly vital, especially in high-stakes domains where interpretability and trust are essential. However, most of the existing methods involve only in-distribution explanation, and do not generalize outside the training support, which requires the learning capability of generalization. In this work, we aim to provide a framework to explain black-box models for time series data through the dual lenses of sparse autoencoders (SAEs) and causality. We show that many current explanation methods are sensitive to distributional shifts, limiting their effectiveness in real-world scenarios. Building on the concept of SAEs, we introduce CausalSparseAE, a framework for black-box model explanation.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{CausalSparseAE Framework}
CausalSparseAE consists of two main components: a sparse autoencoder (SAE) and a causal model. The SAE is trained to capture the underlying structure of the time series data, while the causal model is used to reason about the causal relationships between variables. The framework operates as follows:

1. \textbf{Data Preprocessing:} Raw time series data is preprocessed to remove noise and normalize the data.
2. \textbf{Sparse Autoencoder Training:} An SAE is trained to encode the input time series into a lower-dimensional representation.
3. \textbf{Causal Model Training:} A causal model is trained to infer the causal relationships between variables in the SAE representation.
4. \textbf{Explanation Generation:} The SAE and causal model are used to generate explanations for the model's predictions.

The synthetic dataset includes time series with known causal relationships, while the real-world dataset comes from financial markets.
