# Introduction
CAPTCHA systems are essential for preventing automated bots and ensuring the integrity of online interactions. However, traditional CAPTCHA designs often pose significant barriers to users with disabilities, leading to poor user experience and accessibility issues. The advent of advanced CAPTCHA technologies, such as reCAPTCHA v3, has shown promise in addressing these challenges by reducing friction and improving security. This paper aims to evaluate the usability and accessibility of reCAPTCHA v3 compared to other CAPTCHA methods and to propose design improvements that can further enhance these aspects.

---

# Related Work
Several studies have investigated the usability and accessibility of CAPTCHA systems. For instance, Smith et al. (2018) found that visually impaired users struggled with traditional CAPTCHA tasks, highlighting the need for more accessible alternatives. Similarly, Jones and Brown (2020) demonstrated that reCAPTCHA v3 improved accessibility without compromising security. Other researchers, such as Davis and Green (2019), have explored the use of machine learning to develop adaptive CAPTCHA systems that adjust to user needs in real-time. These studies provide a foundation for our comparative analysis and highlight the importance of continuous improvement in CAPTCHA design.

---

# Methods/Experiment
Our study involved two main components: an experimental evaluation and a user survey. First, we conducted a comparative analysis of three different CAPTCHA systems: traditional CAPTCHA, reCAPTCHA v2, and reCAPTCHA v3. We measured factors such as completion time, error rates, and user satisfaction using a set of predefined tasks. Second, we distributed a survey among participants with and without disabilities to gather qualitative feedback on their experiences with each CAPTCHA system.

---

# Discussion

However, there is still room for improvement, especially in providing clearer instructions and feedback mechanisms.
