### Proposed Research Idea

**Title:** **Cross-Dataset Knowledge Transfer in Multi-Modality Learning Using Meta-Learning**

**Motivation:** The scarcity and heterogeneity of multimodal datasets pose significant challenges in developing robust and generalizable multi-modality learning models. Existing methods often struggle to effectively transfer knowledge across different datasets, particularly when the datasets have varying structures and distributions. This study aims to address these challenges by leveraging meta-learning techniques to facilitate cross-dataset knowledge transfer in multi-modality learning.

**Objective:** To develop a meta-learning framework that can effectively transfer knowledge across multiple multimodal datasets, thereby improving the generalization and robustness of multi-modality learning models.

**Main Contribution:** The proposed framework will include two key components:
1. **Meta-Learning Algorithms:** Two gradient-based meta-learning algorithms (MAML and Reptile) will be adapted and extended to handle multimodal data.
2. **Cross-Dataset Knowledge Transfer Mechanism:** A mechanism to enable seamless knowledge transfer across datasets with different structures and distributions.

**Methods/Experiment:**
- **Dataset Compilation:** Compile a diverse set of multimodal datasets covering various domains such as medical imaging, natural language processing, and computer vision.
- **Framework Development:** Develop and implement the meta-learning framework, incorporating the selected algorithms and the cross-dataset knowledge transfer mechanism.
- **Evaluation:** Evaluate the proposed framework across multiple multimodal datasets, comparing its performance to baseline models and existing meta-learning approaches.

**Expected Results:**
- Improved generalization and robustness of multi-modality learning models.
- Enhanced capability to handle diverse and heterogeneous multimodal datasets.
- Demonstration of the effectiveness of the proposed framework through empirical results and case studies.
