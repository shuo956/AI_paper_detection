### Abstract

This paper introduces Unified Free-Thinking Decoding (UFTD), a novel approach that combines the expressive power of natural language generation with the structured output guarantees of constrained decoding. By allowing LLMs to reason freely until specific trigger tokens are generated, and then switching to structured generation, UFTD preserves the richness of natural language reasoning while ensuring reliable structured outputs. We evaluate UFTD on various datasets, demonstrating a substantial gain in accuracy compared to natural generation, while requiring only a small overhead of 10-20 extra tokens.

### Introduction

Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in natural language generation, enabling the creation of free-form, contextually rich responses. However, these models often produce outputs that lack structured consistency, making them challenging to parse or verify. On the other hand, constrained decoding techniques ensure that generated outputs conform to standardized formats, ensuring consistency and reliability. Unfortunately, constrained decoding can inadvertently limit the model's reasoning capabilities, leading to suboptimal performance on complex tasks.

In this paper, we propose Unified Free-Thinking Decoding (UFTD), a hybrid approach that integrates the strengths of both natural and structured generation. UFTD allows LLMs to reason freely until specific trigger tokens are generated, signaling the switch to structured generation. This approach preserves the expressive power of natural language reasoning while ensuring the reliability of structured outputs. We evaluate UFTD on several datasets, covering both classification and reasoning tasks, and demonstrate its effectiveness in achieving substantial gains in accuracy.

### Related Work

#### Natural Language Generation

Natural language generation (NLG) techniques enable LLMs to produce free-form, contextually rich responses. These approaches leverage the model’s latent semantic space to generate fluent and coherent text. However, NLG often produces outputs that lack structured consistency, making them difficult to parse or verify (Nguyen et al., 2026).

#### Constrained Decoding

Constrained decoding techniques ensure that generated outputs conform to predefined templates or structures, providing consistency and reliability. These methods often involve manually crafting templates or using templates derived from existing data. While constrained decoding guarantees structured outputs, it can limit the model's reasoning capabilities, leading to suboptimal performance on complex tasks (Nguyen et al., 2026).

#### Hybrid Approaches

Hybrid approaches that combine NLG and constrained decoding have been explored to balance expressiveness and structure. For example, conditional generation methods condition the model’s output on specific contexts or templates, ensuring structured outputs while preserving some degree of flexibility (Cui et al., 2026). However, these approaches often require significant manual effort in template design and may still suffer from limited reasoning capabilities.

### Methods/Experiment

#### Unified Free-Thinking Decoding (UFTD)

UFTD operates in two phases: reasoning and structured generation. During the reasoning phase, the model generates free-form text until a specific trigger token is encountered. Once the trigger token is generated, the model switches to structured generation, producing outputs in a predefined format. The key components of UFTD are:

1. **Trigger Tokens**: Specific tokens that signal the transition from reasoning to structured generation.
2. **Structured Templates**: Predefined templates or formats that guide the structured generation phase.
3. **Switch Mechanism**: A mechanism that detects the generation of trigger tokens and initiates the switch to structured generation.

#### Implementation Details

- **Reasoning Phase**: The model generates text until a trigger token is encountered.
- **Structured Generation Phase**: Once the trigger token is generated, the model switches to structured generation using predefined templates.

#### Datasets

We evaluate UFTD on several datasets, including:

1. **Classification Tasks**: Datasets containing labeled examples for classification, such as sentiment analysis and topic classification.
2. **Reasoning Tasks**: Datasets involving complex reasoning, such as logical inference and commonsense reasoning.

#### Evaluation Metrics

We use standard evaluation metrics for NLG and structured generation, including accuracy, F1-score, and BLEU score. Additionally, we measure the overhead in terms of extra tokens required for the trigger tokens and structured templates.
