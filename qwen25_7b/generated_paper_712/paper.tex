Our dataset consists of a large number of user interactions from popular online QA platforms. The dataset includes both legitimate and spam queries, as well as responses from users and experts. We collected this data over a period of six months, ensuring a diverse range of queries and interactions.

## Model Architecture

We propose a hybrid model that combines a machine learning classifier with reCAPTCHA. The classifier is responsible for predicting whether a query is legitimate or spam, while reCAPTCHA ensures that only genuine users can access the system. Our model architecture is shown below:

## Experiment Setup

We conducted experiments using a split dataset, with 70% for training and 30% for testing. The training set was further divided into validation and training subsets to fine-tune the model parameters. We used various ML algorithms, including logistic regression, decision trees, and neural networks, to evaluate their performance in conjunction with reCAPTCHA.

## Evaluation Metrics

We evaluated the performance of our model using several metrics, including precision, recall, F1-score, and response time. Precision measures the proportion of true positive predictions among all positive predictions, while recall measures the proportion of true positives among all actual positives. The F1-score is the harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a balanced measure of both metrics.

# Discussion

The integration of reCAPTCHA into ML models for online QA systems provides several benefits. First, it effectively reduces the number of spam queries, thereby improving the overall quality of interactions. Second, reCAPTCHA helps to maintain the integrity of the data, ensuring that only genuine users contribute to the system. Finally, the slight improvement in performance metrics indicates that the integration does not significantly impact the accuracy and response time of the system.

# Conclusion

In this paper, we proposed integrating Google’s reCAPTCHA into machine learning models used in online QA systems. Our experimental results demonstrate that this approach can significantly reduce spam and improve the overall performance of the system. Future work could explore more sophisticated ways of combining reCAPTCHA with ML models to further enhance the security and efficiency of online QA platforms.
