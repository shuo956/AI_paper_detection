\section{Introduction}
Image classification is a fundamental task in computer vision and has numerous applications in fields such as healthcare, autonomous driving, and security. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have been the state-of-the-art approach for this task. However, the performance of CNNs can vary greatly depending on the quality and characteristics of the input images. Pre-processing techniques can play a crucial role in enhancing the performance of these models. This paper aims to evaluate the effectiveness of different pre-processing techniques on the accuracy of CNNs in image classification tasks.
