Text classification is a fundamental task in natural language processing (NLP) with wide-ranging applications, including sentiment analysis, spam filtering, and topic categorization. Despite the progress made in recent years, achieving high accuracy remains challenging due to the complexity and variability of textual data. Traditional approaches often rely on simple bag-of-words or TF-IDF vectors, which may not capture the full semantic and syntactic nuances of text. This paper aims to address this gap by investigating advanced feature engineering techniques that can lead to more effective text classification models.

Additionally, syntactic features showed promising results but required more sophisticated selection and transformation techniques to achieve optimal performance.
