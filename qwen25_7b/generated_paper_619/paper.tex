\section{Introduction}
The digital transformation of academic research has led to the proliferation of online platforms such as arXiv, which provide access to scholarly articles and preprints. However, the accessibility and usability of these platforms remain critical issues, especially for users with disabilities and those using assistive technologies. This paper aims to address these gaps by exploring the potential of adaptive user interfaces (AUIs) to improve the user experience on academic research platforms.

\section{Related Work}
Previous research has highlighted the importance of accessibility in digital environments. Studies such as \cite{smith2020accessibility} have shown that poorly designed websites can create barriers for users with disabilities, leading to frustration and decreased usage. Similarly, \cite{johnson2019user} have explored the role of user interfaces in enhancing usability, emphasizing the need for dynamic and adaptable designs. However, there is limited research specifically focusing on the implementation and evaluation of AUIs in academic research platforms.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
Our study involves the development and testing of an adaptive user interface for arXiv. The AUI is designed to dynamically adjust based on user preferences and device capabilities, ensuring optimal viewing conditions and ease of navigation. Participants were recruited from various backgrounds, including researchers with and without disabilities, and were asked to complete a series of tasks using both the traditional and AUI versions of arXiv. The study utilized a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative measures (such as task completion time and user satisfaction) with qualitative feedback through interviews.

The findings suggest that the AUI developed for arXiv significantly improves both task completion time and user satisfaction. These results underscore the potential of AUIs to enhance the accessibility and usability of academic research platforms, making them more inclusive and user-friendly.
