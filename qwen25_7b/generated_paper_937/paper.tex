\begin{abstract}
This paper explores the integration of machine learning (ML) techniques into web accessibility tools, focusing on reCAPTCHA as a case study. Despite significant advancements in ML, web accessibility remains a critical but often overlooked aspect of digital platforms. This study aims to enhance the accessibility features of reCAPTCHA by incorporating advanced ML models, thereby improving user experience for individuals with disabilities. Through a comparative analysis of traditional and ML-enhanced reCAPTCHA versions, we evaluate the effectiveness of these enhancements in terms of accuracy, usability, and accessibility. The results indicate that ML-based solutions offer substantial improvements in accessibility while maintaining security standards.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The rapid advancement of digital technologies has led to a proliferation of online platforms and services. However, these advancements often fail to cater adequately to users with disabilities, leading to a digital divide. Web accessibility tools, such as reCAPTCHA, play a crucial role in ensuring that digital environments are inclusive and usable by everyone. Traditional reCAPTCHA systems rely on simple challenges to distinguish between human users and bots, but they lack the sophistication needed to address the diverse needs of individuals with disabilities. This paper investigates how machine learning can be leveraged to enhance the accessibility features of reCAPTCHA, making it more inclusive and user-friendly.

\section{Related Work}
Previous studies have explored various aspects of web accessibility and reCAPTCHA. For instance, Smith et al. (2020) discussed the limitations of traditional reCAPTCHA in accommodating users with disabilities and proposed integrating audio cues to improve accessibility. Similarly, Lee and Kim (2021) examined the impact of visual impairments on reCAPTCHA and suggested the use of alternative text descriptions. However, these approaches primarily focused on augmenting existing features rather than fundamentally altering the system's architecture. Our work builds upon these studies by employing advanced ML techniques to create a more accessible reCAPTCHA system.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
To evaluate the effectiveness of ML-enhanced reCAPTCHA, we conducted a series of experiments. First, we developed a dataset consisting of images and text inputs from users with various disabilities. We then trained several ML models, including Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Support Vector Machines (SVMs), to classify these inputs accurately. The trained models were integrated into a modified version of reCAPTCHA, which was tested against a baseline version. User feedback and performance metrics were collected to assess the usability and accessibility of the ML-enhanced system.

\section{Discussion}
The results of our experiments demonstrate that ML-enhanced reCAPTCHA significantly improves the accuracy of human detection and overall usability. The enhanced version also shows better accessibility scores, indicating that it is more inclusive for users with disabilities. However, there are still areas for improvement, particularly in terms of reducing false positives and ensuring real-time processing.

\section{Conclusion}
In conclusion, this study highlights the potential of machine learning in enhancing web accessibility tools like reCAPTCHA. By integrating advanced ML models, we can create more inclusive and user-friendly systems. Future work should focus on refining the ML models and expanding the dataset to include a wider range of disabilities.

\section{Contributions}
This paper makes the following contributions:
1. Enhanced Accessibility: ML techniques have been successfully integrated into reCAPTCHA to improve its accessibility.
2. Improved Usability: The ML-enhanced version demonstrates higher usability scores compared to the traditional version.
3. Scalability: The approach can be applied to other web accessibility tools to enhance their inclusivity.
