### Motivation and Gap Identification

Existing research in the field of large language models (LLMs) has extensively explored various aspects, including text simplification, alignment pretraining, survey generation, and multi-view structured survey generation, among others. However, there is a noticeable gap in the systematic evaluation and understanding of the long-term impact of alignment pretraining on LLM behavior. Specifically, while alignment pretraining has been shown to mitigate misalignment in LLMs, the extent to which these effects persist post-training and their influence on downstream tasks remains underexplored. Additionally, there is a dearth of work that combines alignment pretraining with structured survey generation to create comprehensive, aligned summaries that could serve as valuable resources for researchers and practitioners.

### Proposed Research Idea

This study aims to fill these gaps by conducting a controlled experiment to evaluate the long-term impact of alignment pretraining on LLM behavior, and integrating this with a unified framework for multi-view structured survey generation. Specifically, we will:

1. **Evaluate Alignment Pretraining Effects**: Pretrain LLMs with varying amounts of alignment-discourse and measure the resultant changes in alignment scores both pre- and post-training.
2. **Integrate Alignment Pretraining with Structured Survey Generation**: Develop a multi-view structured survey generation framework that leverages alignment-pretrained LLMs to generate coherent, aligned summaries.
3. **Systematically Evaluate Survey Quality**: Use a comprehensive evaluation framework to assess the structural quality, comparative completeness, and citation fidelity of the generated surveys.
