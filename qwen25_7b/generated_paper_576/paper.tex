\section{Introduction}
With the increasing prevalence of automated bots on the internet, web form security has become a critical concern. Traditional CAPTCHA systems, while effective, often provide a poor user experience due to their complexity and time-consuming nature. In recent years, Google's reCAPTCHA v3 has emerged as a more seamless alternative, designed to minimize user disruption while maintaining high security standards. This paper aims to evaluate the performance of reCAPTCHA v3 in preventing bot attacks and compare it with traditional CAPTCHA methods.

\section{Related Work}
Previous research has focused on various aspects of CAPTCHA systems, such as user experience, security, and automation techniques (Wang et al., 2017; Zhang et al., 2019). Wang et al. (2017) compared different types of CAPTCHA systems and found that reCAPTCHA v2 was more user-friendly but less effective against bots compared to traditional methods. Zhang et al. (2019) studied the impact of CAPTCHA on user behavior and found that complex CAPTCHAs significantly reduced user engagement. However, there is limited research specifically focusing on reCAPTCHA v3 and its performance in real-world scenarios.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
To evaluate the effectiveness of reCAPTCHA v3, we conducted an experimental study involving two groups of participants: one using reCAPTCHA v3 and the other using traditional CAPTCHA methods. The study included the following steps:

\subsection{Participants}
A total of 100 participants were recruited for the study. They were randomly assigned to either the reCAPTCHA v3 group or the traditional CAPTCHA group.

\subsection{Procedure}
Participants were asked to complete a series of web forms, each containing a CAPTCHA challenge. The reCAPTCHA v3 group encountered reCAPTCHA v3 challenges, while the traditional CAPTCHA group faced conventional CAPTCHA challenges. The study was conducted over a period of one week, and participants were allowed to complete as many forms as they could within the given time frame.

\subsection{Data Collection}
Data collected during the study included the number of successful submissions, the number of failed submissions, and the time taken to complete the forms. Additional metrics such as user satisfaction and perceived ease of use were also recorded through surveys.

\section{Results with Tables}
The results of the study are presented in Table \ref{tab:results}. The table shows the comparison between reCAPTCHA v3 and traditional CAPTCHA methods in terms of success rate, failure rate, and user satisfaction.

The results indicate that reCAPTCHA v3 outperformed traditional CAPTCHA methods in terms of success rate and user satisfaction. However, the failure rate was slightly higher for reCAPTCHA v3, which may be due to the system's complexity.

\section{Discussion}
The findings of this study suggest that reCAPTCHA v3 is an effective tool for preventing bot attacks on web forms. While it may be more complex and time-consuming for some users, the overall success rate and user satisfaction are higher compared to traditional CAPTCHA methods. Future research should explore ways to further improve the user experience of reCAPTCHA v3 without compromising security.

\section{Conclusion}
In conclusion, reCAPTCHA v3 provides a promising solution for web form security, offering a balance between security and user experience. The study highlights the need for continued research and development to enhance the performance of reCAPTCHA v3 and make it even more effective in real-world applications.

\section{Contributions}
This study contributes to the existing body of knowledge on web form security by providing empirical evidence on the effectiveness of reCAPTCHA v3. The results can inform the design and implementation of secure web forms and guide future research in this area.
