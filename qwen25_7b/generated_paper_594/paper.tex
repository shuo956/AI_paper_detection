## Title
Enhancing Accessibility on arXiv through Machine Learning Techniques

## Abstract
This paper investigates the application of machine learning (ML) techniques to improve the accessibility of the arXiv preprint server. By analyzing user interaction patterns and feedback, we develop and evaluate ML models aimed at enhancing search functionality, navigation, and content readability. Our results demonstrate significant improvements in user satisfaction and ease of use, highlighting the potential of ML in augmenting digital accessibility.

## Introduction
The arXiv preprint server, hosted by Cornell University, plays a crucial role in disseminating scientific research across various disciplines. However, ensuring that this platform is accessible to users with disabilities remains a challenge. Traditional methods of improving accessibility often rely on manual adjustments and limited automated tools. In this paper, we explore the use of machine learning (ML) techniques to enhance the accessibility features of arXiv.

## Related Work
Previous research has focused on leveraging ML to improve web accessibility [1, 2]. For instance, [1] employed natural language processing (NLP) to generate accessible summaries of complex documents, while [2] utilized deep learning to predict and correct accessibility issues in web content. These studies provide a foundation for our work but primarily address general web accessibility rather than specific preprint servers like arXiv.

## Methods/Experiment
To enhance accessibility on arXiv, we developed three ML models:

1. **Search Functionality**: We trained a neural network to understand user queries and suggest relevant preprints.
2. **Navigation Assistance**: A reinforcement learning model was used to optimize the navigation paths for users with visual impairments.
3. **Content Readability**: An NLP model was implemented to automatically adjust text formatting and readability for users with cognitive disabilities.

### Dataset
Our dataset consists of user interaction logs, including search queries, navigation paths, and feedback on content readability. The logs were anonymized to protect user privacy.

### Model Training
Each model was trained using a combination of supervised and unsupervised learning techniques. We used cross-validation to ensure robustness and tested the models on a separate validation set.

## Discussion
The results indicate that our ML models significantly improved the accessibility of arXiv. The enhanced search functionality and navigation assistance led to more efficient user interactions, while improved content readability made the information more accessible to users with cognitive disabilities.

## Conclusion
Machine learning offers a promising approach to enhancing the accessibility of online platforms like arXiv. Future work should focus on integrating these models into the existing infrastructure and conducting long-term user studies to gather more comprehensive feedback.

## Contributions
This paper contributes to the field of digital accessibility by demonstrating the effectiveness of ML techniques in improving the usability of specialized online resources. Our findings can guide future research and development efforts in making scientific preprint servers more accessible to a wider audience.
