\section{Introduction}
Sentiment analysis is a critical task in natural language processing (NLP) with applications in various fields such as marketing, customer service, and social media monitoring. Despite advancements in machine learning techniques, several challenges remain, including limited evaluation metrics, data imbalance, lack of cross-domain generalization, and inadequate handling of rare sentiments. This paper proposes a hybrid model combining deep learning with traditional NLP methods to address these issues and improve overall performance.

\section{Related Work}
\subsection{Sentiment Analysis Techniques}
Existing approaches to sentiment analysis primarily rely on supervised learning methods using deep neural networks like LSTM and BERT. These models have shown high accuracy but often suffer from data imbalance and limited generalization across different domains.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
Most studies focus on accuracy as the primary metric, which can be misleading when dealing with imbalanced datasets. Precision, recall, F1-score, and AUC-ROC provide a more comprehensive view of model performance.

\subsection{Handling Data Imbalance}
Techniques such as oversampling, undersampling, and SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) have been proposed to address data imbalance. However, these methods may introduce noise or overfitting.

\subsection{Cross-Domain Generalization}
Transfer learning and domain adaptation techniques have been explored to improve cross-domain generalization, but they often require large labeled datasets for fine-tuning.

\subsection{Rare Sentiment Detection}
Handling rare sentiments remains challenging due to their low frequency in training data. Advanced techniques like ensemble learning and anomaly detection can be employed to improve detection rates.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
\subsection{Proposed Model}
Our proposed model, called HybridSent, combines a pre-trained BERT model with a traditional bag-of-words (BoW) approach. The BoW component captures semantic information, while the BERT component handles contextual nuances. We use a hybrid loss function that incorporates accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score.

\subsection{Data Preprocessing}
We use a publicly available dataset for sentiment analysis, which includes both common and rare sentiments. To handle data imbalance, we apply SMOTE to balance the classes. We also perform text preprocessing steps such as tokenization, stop-word removal, and lemmatization.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
We evaluate our model on two datasets: a generic dataset for common sentiments and a specialized dataset for rare sentiments. We compare our model against state-of-the-art baselines using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score, and AUC-ROC.

\section{Discussion}
The results show that our proposed HybridSent model outperforms the baselines on both common and rare sentiments datasets. The hybrid approach effectively leverages the strengths of deep learning and traditional NLP methods. The advanced evaluation metrics provide a more robust assessment of model performance.

\section{Conclusion}
This paper presents a novel hybrid model for sentiment analysis that addresses key challenges such as data imbalance, cross-domain generalization, and rare sentiment detection. The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. Future work could explore further improvements in model architecture and additional evaluation metrics.
