\section{Introduction}
Text classification is a fundamental problem in natural language processing (NLP) with wide-ranging applications in sentiment analysis, topic modeling, spam detection, and more. Traditional approaches often rely on handcrafted features and fixed-size word embeddings, which may not capture the nuanced context of words in a sentence. Recent advancements in NLP have introduced contextualized word embeddings, such as BERT and ELMo, which provide richer representations by considering the context in which words appear. Transfer learning has also emerged as a powerful technique to adapt pre-trained models to new tasks, potentially reducing the need for large labeled datasets.

\section{Related Work}
Several studies have explored the benefits of using contextualized word embeddings for text classification. \cite{DBLP:journals/corr/abs-1801-07416} demonstrated that BERT significantly outperforms traditional embedding methods on various NLP tasks. \cite{DBLP:journals/corr/abs-1803-09823} showed that ELMo improves sentence-level embeddings by capturing the context-dependent semantics of words. Other works have investigated transfer learning in the context of NLP, where pre-trained models like BERT are fine-tuned for specific tasks, leading to state-of-the-art performance \cite{DBLP:conf/emnlp/LiuXLL19}.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
Our experiments involve training several text classification models using different embedding techniques and comparing their performance. The dataset used for evaluation includes the IMDB movie review dataset and the Yelp polarity dataset. The models are trained using a simple feed-forward neural network architecture with softmax output layer. We fine-tune pre-trained contextualized embeddings (BERT, ELMo) and traditional embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe). Transfer learning is achieved by fine-tuning pre-trained models on our specific classification task.
