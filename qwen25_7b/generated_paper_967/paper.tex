### Title
Embedding-Based Retrieval for Educational Content Search: Balancing Efficiency, Accuracy, and User Engagement

### Abstract
The rapid expansion of online educational platforms necessitates efficient and accurate retrieval systems to match learners with appropriate courses and resources. This paper proposes a novel embedding-based retrieval (EBR) system for educational content search, specifically designed for a multi-sided marketplace such as Coursera. Our system aims to balance retrieval efficiency, accuracy, and user engagement by integrating advanced techniques such as dynamic inventory management, multi-stage user engagement models, and robust evaluation frameworks. We validate our approach through extensive experiments, demonstrating significant improvements in key metrics such as query processing speed, relevance, and user satisfaction.

### Introduction
The proliferation of online educational platforms has transformed the way people acquire knowledge. However, finding relevant and high-quality educational content remains a challenging task due to the vast amount of available resources and diverse user preferences. Traditional retrieval systems often struggle to provide timely and accurate results, leading to poor user experiences. This paper introduces a novel embedding-based retrieval (EBR) system designed to address these challenges. Our system leverages advanced embedding techniques and incorporates unique features such as dynamic inventory management, multi-stage user engagement models, and robust evaluation frameworks to enhance retrieval efficiency, accuracy, and user engagement.

### Related Work
Previous research has explored various aspects of educational content retrieval, including keyword-based search [1], collaborative filtering [2], and deep learning-based approaches [3]. While these methods have shown promising results, they often fall short in terms of scalability and real-time performance. More recently, embedding-based retrieval systems have gained popularity due to their ability to handle large-scale datasets and provide fast query processing [4]. However, most existing systems are tailored for single-sided marketplaces, such as e-commerce platforms, and may not fully address the unique challenges of multi-sided marketplaces like Coursera.

### Methods/Experiment
Our EBR system consists of several key components: (1) Dynamic Inventory Management, (2) Multi-Stage User Engagement Models, and (3) Robust Evaluation Frameworks. 

1. **Dynamic Inventory Management**: To handle the dynamic nature of the inventory, our system uses a combination of historical data and real-time user behavior to update the embedding vectors of courses and resources. This ensures that the retrieval system remains up-to-date and relevant.

2. **Multi-Stage User Engagement Models**: We propose a multi-stage user engagement model that captures different stages of the user journey, from initial interest to final enrollment. This model helps in tailoring the search results to the user's current stage and preferences.

3. **Robust Evaluation Frameworks**: To evaluate the performance of our system, we employ both offline and online evaluation methods. Offline evaluations include precision-recall curves, mean reciprocal rank (MRR), and normalized discounted cumulative gain (NDCG). Online evaluations involve A/B testing to measure the impact on user engagement and satisfaction.
