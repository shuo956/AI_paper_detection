Previous studies have highlighted the limitations of traditional CAPTCHA systems in terms of usability and accessibility \cite{capthausability}. Additionally, \cite{motorcaptha} explored the challenges faced by users with motor impairments when interacting with CAPTCHA systems. Despite these efforts, there remains a need for a comprehensive evaluation of CAPTCHA systems across different disability categories.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
Our study involved a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative and qualitative assessments. Participants were recruited from a pool of individuals with varying levels of visual, auditory, and motor impairments. The experiment consisted of two phases: 
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Usability Testing}: Participants were asked to complete different types of CAPTCHA systems, including visual, audio, and alternative text-based CAPTCHAs. Their responses were recorded and analyzed using statistical methods.
    \item \textbf{Interviews}: Post-experiment interviews were conducted to gather qualitative feedback on the challenges faced during the CAPTCHA tasks.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Discussion}
The results of our study suggest that alternative text-based CAPTCHAs offer a promising solution for enhancing both usability and accessibility. These CAPTCHAs provide a textual description of the image or task, which can be easily read and understood by users with visual impairments. Moreover, they do not rely on auditory cues, making them suitable for users with hearing impairments as well. However, further research is needed to explore the scalability and security implications of this approach.

\section{Conclusion}
In conclusion, our study highlights the importance of designing CAPTCHA systems that are accessible to users with disabilities. Alternative text-based CAPTCHAs show great potential in achieving this goal. Future work should focus on developing more robust and secure alternative text-based CAPTCHA systems that can be implemented across various web platforms.
