\section{Abstract}
This paper introduces a unified framework for real-time, trustworthy, and physiologically plausible explainable AI in healthcare applications. The framework integrates concept drift detection, diagnosis, and remediation with physiologically plausible counterfactual explanations to ensure robust and interpretable AI models. We leverage an ensemble of detection methods, Shapley Additive Explanations (SHAP) for diagnosis, and a cost-aware retraining strategy for remediation. Our framework is validated through extensive experiments on healthcare datasets, demonstrating superior performance in both concept drift detection and counterfactual explanation generation.

\section{Background}
\subsection{Concept Drift in Healthcare}
Concept drift refers to changes in the underlying data distribution that can lead to model degradation. In healthcare, these changes can be due to shifts in patient demographics, medical treatments, or new diagnostic technologies. Detecting and addressing concept drift is crucial for maintaining the accuracy and reliability of predictive models.

\subsection{Explainable AI in Healthcare}
Explainable AI (XAI) is essential for building trust in AI models used in healthcare. XAI techniques provide insights into how models make predictions, helping clinicians understand and verify the results. Physiologically plausible counterfactual explanations are particularly valuable in healthcare, as they provide actionable insights that can inform clinical decision-making.
