Despite these advancements, there remains room for improvement in terms of optimization efficiency and solution quality.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
Our proposed method integrates adaptive learning rate scheduling into the mutation and crossover operations of an EA. The adaptive learning rate is adjusted based on the fitness landscape, allowing the algorithm to adaptively explore different regions of the search space. We evaluate our method on several benchmark datasets, including classification and regression tasks.

\subsection{Algorithm Overview}
The proposed algorithm, referred to as ALEA (Adaptive Learning Rate Evolutionary Algorithm), operates as follows:

1. Initialize the population with random solutions.
2. Evaluate the fitness of each individual in the population.
3. Apply adaptive learning rate scheduling to the mutation and crossover operations.
4. Select the best individuals for the next generation based on their fitness.
5. Repeat steps 2-4 until a stopping criterion is met.

\subsection{Adaptive Learning Rate Scheduling}
The adaptive learning rate is calculated based on the fitness difference between consecutive generations. If the fitness improvement is small, the learning rate is reduced to allow finer exploration. Conversely, if significant improvement is observed, the learning rate is increased to facilitate faster convergence.
