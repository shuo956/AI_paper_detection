\section{Introduction}
Emotion recognition and sentiment analysis are crucial tasks in natural language processing, with applications ranging from mental health to customer service. Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) have shown promise in handling multimodal inputs, but there are several gaps in existing research. This paper aims to address these gaps by proposing a unified framework that enhances the understanding and generation of multilingual and multimodal emotional expressions, while ensuring the contextual safety of LLMs in multi-turn dialogues. 

\section{Related Work}
\subsection{Multimodal Emotion Recognition and Sentiment Analysis}
Qiao et al. \cite{qiao2026unifiedframeworkemotionrecognition} introduced EGMF, a unified framework combining expert-guided multimodal fusion with large language models. Their approach uses three specialized expert networks for local, semantic, and global context emotions, which are adaptively integrated through hierarchical dynamic gating. This framework demonstrates consistent improvements over state-of-the-art methods on bilingual benchmarks (MELD, CHERMA, MOSEI, SIMS-V2).

\subsection{Contextual Safety in Multimodal Large Language Models}
Liu et al. \cite{liu2026mtmcsbenchevaluatingcontextualsafety} introduced the Multi-Turn Multimodal Contextual Safety Benchmark (MTMCS-Bench), which evaluates contextual safety in MLLMs under two complementary settings, escalation-based risk and context-switch risk. This benchmark offers a more realistic evaluation of contextual safety in multi-turn dialogues.

\subsection{Alignment Pretraining}
Tice et al. \cite{tice2026alignmentpretrainingaidiscourse} conducted a controlled study of alignment pretraining, showing that discussion of AI misalignment leads to increased misaligned behavior. This highlights the importance of carefully designing pretraining data to align model behavior with desired outcomes.

\subsection{Multilingual and Multimodal Idiomatic Expression Understanding}
Torunoğlu-Selamet et al. \cite{torunoğluselamet2026parallelcrosslingualbenchmarkmultimodal} presented XMPIE, a parallel multilingual and multimodal dataset of potentially idiomatic expressions. This dataset allows comparative analyses of idiomatic patterns across different languages and modalities, supporting the development of models that can understand and generate idiomatic expressions in multiple languages.

\subsection{Truthfulness and Hallucination in Large Language Models}
Luo et al. \cite{luo2026pathwaystruthfulnessintrinsicencoding} demonstrated that truthfulness cues arise from two distinct information pathways: a Question-Anchored pathway and an Answer-Anchored pathway. This work provides new insights into how LLMs internally encode truthfulness and offers directions for more reliable and self-aware generative systems.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
Our proposed framework, named MEFUS (Multilingual and Multimodal Emotional Fusion for Understanding and Safety), integrates expert-guided multimodal fusion with large language models. The key components include:
\begin{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Pseudo Token Injection}: Enhanced multimodal representations are integrated with LLMs via pseudo token injection and prompt-based conditioning.
    \item \textbf{LORA Fine-Tuning}: Efficient fine-tuning of LLMs is achieved using Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA).
\end{itemize}
