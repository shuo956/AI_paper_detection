\section{Introduction}
The proliferation of online scientific publishing platforms has made it easier for researchers to share their work with a global audience. However, these platforms also face increasing challenges in ensuring the security and accessibility of their services. One common approach to address security concerns is the use of reCAPTCHA, a tool designed to distinguish human users from bots. Despite its widespread adoption, there is limited research on how reCAPTCHA implementations impact user experience and web accessibility in scientific publishing contexts.

This paper evaluates the impact of reCAPTCHA on user experience and web accessibility in scientific publishing platforms. We specifically focus on the Cornell University arXiv system, which uses reCAPTCHA as part of its login and registration processes. Our objectives include understanding the effectiveness of reCAPTCHA in preventing automated attacks, assessing the usability of the reCAPTCHA interface, and determining its compliance with web accessibility standards.

\section{Related Work}
Previous studies have explored various aspects of reCAPTCHA in different contexts. For instance, \cite{reCAPTCHA_effectiveness} demonstrated that reCAPTCHA can effectively reduce automated spam and abuse on websites. However, these studies often do not consider the broader implications on user experience and web accessibility.

In the context of scientific publishing, several papers have discussed the importance of web accessibility (e.g., \cite{web_accessibility}). Web accessibility ensures that digital content can be accessed and used by people with disabilities, which is crucial for inclusivity in scientific research. The implementation of reCAPTCHA in scientific publishing platforms could potentially hinder accessibility if not designed properly.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
To evaluate the impact of reCAPTCHA on user experience and web accessibility, we conducted a mixed-methods study involving both quantitative and qualitative assessments. 

\subsection{Quantitative Assessment}
We collected data on the time taken to complete the reCAPTCHA challenge and the number of failed attempts before successful submission. We also measured the success rates of users who encountered the reCAPTCHA during registration and login processes.

\subsection{Qualitative Assessment}
We conducted user interviews and usability testing sessions to gather feedback on the reCAPTCHA interface and its impact on user experience. Participants were asked about their perceptions of the reCAPTCHA process, any difficulties they faced, and suggestions for improvement.

\subsection{Accessibility Compliance}
We evaluated the reCAPTCHA implementation against established web accessibility guidelines such as WCAG 2.1. This involved checking for the presence of alternative text for images, keyboard navigability, and screen reader compatibility.

\section{Results}
Our results indicate that the current reCAPTCHA implementation in the arXiv platform significantly reduces automated attacks but introduces some usability issues. Users reported longer wait times and occasional difficulties in completing the reCAPTCHA challenge. However, the success rate was high, suggesting that the reCAPTCHA is effective in preventing abuse.

From the qualitative assessments, participants generally found the reCAPTCHA process cumbersome and time-consuming. Some users expressed concerns about potential accessibility barriers, particularly for users with visual impairments. Our accessibility compliance analysis revealed that the reCAPTCHA implementation did not fully meet all WCAG 2.1 criteria, especially regarding keyboard navigation and screen reader support.

\section{Discussion}
The findings suggest that while reCAPTCHA effectively mitigates automated threats, its implementation needs to be optimized to enhance user experience and ensure accessibility. The extended wait times and occasional difficulties faced by users highlight the need for a more streamlined reCAPTCHA process. Additionally, addressing the accessibility issues identified in our study would help make the platform more inclusive.

\section{Conclusion}
This study provides insights into the impact of reCAPTCHA on user experience and web accessibility in scientific publishing platforms. While reCAPTCHA is a valuable tool for enhancing security, its implementation should be carefully considered to balance security needs with user convenience and accessibility requirements.

\section{Contributions}
This research contributes to the body of knowledge on web accessibility and security in scientific publishing. It highlights the importance of designing reCAPTCHA solutions that are not only effective in preventing abuse but also user-friendly and accessible to all users, including those with disabilities.
