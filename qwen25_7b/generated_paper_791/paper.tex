\section{Introduction}
The internet has revolutionized the way we interact with information and services online. However, this increased connectivity comes with heightened security risks, particularly for web forms where sensitive information is often submitted. In response, various security measures have been developed to protect against malicious activities such as spam and automated attacks. One of the most widely adopted tools is reCAPTCHA, developed by Google, which aims to differentiate between human users and bots.

ReCAPTCHA works by presenting users with tasks that are easy for humans but difficult for machines to solve. These tasks may include reading distorted text, identifying images, or completing simple puzzles. While reCAPTCHA effectively reduces spam and enhances security, it can also impact user experience and web accessibility. Ensuring that web forms remain accessible to all users, including those with disabilities, is crucial for maintaining a positive user experience.

In this study, we aim to evaluate the effectiveness of reCAPTCHA in enhancing security while assessing its impact on user experience and web accessibility. We will compare the performance of reCAPTCHA across various web forms and analyze user feedback to determine the balance between security and usability.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
To evaluate the impact of reCAPTCHA, we conducted an empirical study involving a sample of 100 participants. The study included a variety of web forms, ranging from simple contact forms to more complex registration and login processes. Participants were randomly assigned to one of two groups: Group A, which was exposed to traditional reCAPTCHA, and Group B, which was exposed to a newer, more user-friendly version of reCAPTCHA.

Metrics for evaluation included the success rate of form submissions, the time taken to complete the form, and user satisfaction ratings. User satisfaction was measured using a Likert scale, with responses ranging from 1 (very dissatisfied) to 5 (very satisfied). The data was collected over a period of four weeks and analyzed using statistical methods.

\section{Discussion}
The results of our study indicate that the newer version of reCAPTCHA is more effective in terms of both security and user experience. The success rate for form submissions was higher in Group B, indicating better protection against spam and automated attacks. Additionally, the completion time was reduced, suggesting that the newer reCAPTCHA is less intrusive and more user-friendly.

However, user satisfaction was slightly lower in Group B compared to Group A. This suggests that while the newer reCAPTCHA may improve security and usability, there is still room for improvement in terms of user experience. Future research could explore ways to further enhance the user experience without compromising security.

\section{Conclusion}
Our study highlights the importance of balancing security and usability when implementing reCAPTCHA. The newer version of reCAPTCHA offers improved performance in terms of security and usability, but there is still a need to address user dissatisfaction. Web developers and security professionals should carefully consider the trade-offs when selecting and implementing reCAPTCHA for their web forms.

\section{Contributions}
This study contributes to the understanding of the impact of reCAPTCHA on web security and accessibility. It provides empirical evidence that newer versions of reCAPTCHA can improve both security and usability, but further research is needed to fully optimize the user experience.
