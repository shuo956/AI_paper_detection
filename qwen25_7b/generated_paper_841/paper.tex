### Title
An Empirical Study on the Impact of Recaptcha on Web Accessibility and User Experience

### Abstract
This paper presents an empirical investigation into the impact of reCAPTCHA on web accessibility and user experience. Through a series of controlled experiments and surveys, we analyze how different versions of reCAPTCHA affect user engagement and satisfaction. Our results highlight areas where current implementations can be improved to better serve both security and usability goals.

### Introduction
With the increasing reliance on online services, ensuring web accessibility has become a critical concern. One common challenge faced by websites is distinguishing between human users and automated bots. Google’s reCAPTCHA is widely used for this purpose. However, its impact on user experience and web accessibility remains underexplored. This study aims to fill this gap by evaluating the effects of reCAPTCHA on various aspects of web usage.

### Related Work
Several studies have examined the usability and accessibility of reCAPTCHA. For instance, [Smith et al., 2018] found that reCAPTCHA v2 had a significant negative impact on user satisfaction. Similarly, [Johnson & Lee, 2019] reported higher error rates for visually impaired users. Despite these findings, there is a lack of comprehensive empirical evidence on how different versions of reCAPTCHA affect overall user experience.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Participants
The study involved 300 participants recruited from Amazon Mechanical Turk. Participants were stratified based on age (18-65), visual acuity (normal, mild, moderate, severe), and technological proficiency (basic, intermediate, advanced).

#### Design
A within-subjects design was employed, where each participant interacted with three versions of reCAPTCHA (v2, v3, and no reCAPTCHA) across three different websites (e-commerce, social media, news). The order of reCAPTCHA versions was counterbalanced to control for order effects.

#### Procedure
Participants were asked to complete a series of tasks on each website, including registration, commenting, and purchasing. Their performance was measured using metrics such as time taken, errors made, and satisfaction ratings. A post-test survey collected qualitative feedback on their experiences.

### Discussion
The results indicate that reCAPTCHA v3 performs better than v2 in terms of both time taken and error rate. However, user satisfaction is highest when no reCAPTCHA is used. These findings suggest that while reCAPTCHA aims to enhance security, it may come at the cost of user experience and accessibility.

### Conclusion
Our study provides empirical evidence that reCAPTCHA can negatively impact user experience and web accessibility. Future research should focus on developing more user-friendly and accessible alternatives that balance security needs with user satisfaction.
