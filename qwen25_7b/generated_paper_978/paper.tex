The primary objective of this research is to develop and evaluate the Residual Power Flow (RPF) formulation as a foundational approach for power flow analysis. Specifically, we aim to:

3. **Integrate RPF in a Predict-then-Optimize Framework**: Combine the RPF with a Predict-then-Optimize (PO) approach to leverage the strengths of both speed and flexibility.
4. **Evaluate the RPF on Real-World Systems**: Test the RPF formulation on real-world power systems to assess its accuracy and flexibility.

### Methodology

#### Formulation of the Residual Power Flow Problem

The Residual Power Flow (RPF) formulation is based on the principle of residual functions, which quantify the deviation from a desired operating condition. Given a set of nodal voltages \( \mathbf{V} \) and line flows \( \mathbf{S} \), the RPF formulation aims to minimize the residual functions \( \mathbf{r}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) \):

\[ \mathbf{r}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) = \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) - \mathbf{P} \]

where \( \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) \) represents the actual power flow equations based on Kirchhoff's laws, and \( \mathbf{P} \) is the desired power flow vector.

To ensure AC feasibility, an additional slack variable \( \mathbf{\lambda} \) is introduced:

\[ \mathbf{\lambda} = \mathbf{V} - \mathbf{V}_{ref} \]

where \( \mathbf{V}_{ref} \) is the reference voltage vector.

#### Learning the RPF with Neural Solvers

We will train neural networks to approximate the RPF problem. The neural network architecture will consist of multiple layers designed to capture the complex relationships between nodal voltages, line flows, and the residual functions. The training process will involve minimizing a loss function that measures the discrepancy between the predicted residual functions and the actual residual functions.

#### Integration in a Predict-then-Optimize Framework

The Predict-then-Optimize (PO) approach involves two main stages: prediction and optimization. During the prediction stage, the neural network approximates the RPF problem to obtain a preliminary solution. In the optimization stage, this solution is refined using traditional optimization techniques to satisfy the operational constraints.

To validate the effectiveness of the RPF formulation, we will conduct extensive simulations on real-world power systems, including the IEEE 9-bus system and other representative systems. The performance of the RPF formulation will be compared against traditional power flow methods to assess its accuracy and flexibility.

This limitation is particularly problematic in the context of power flow analysis, where a wide variety of operational conditions and constraints must be considered. To address this gap, we propose the Residual Power Flow (RPF) formulation, which leverages the principles of residual functions and Kirchhoff's laws to provide a foundational and flexible approach to power flow analysis.

\section{Methods/Experiment}

### Formulation of the Residual Power Flow Problem

The Residual Power Flow (RPF) formulation is based on the principle of residual functions, which quantify the deviation from a desired operating condition. Given a set of nodal voltages \( \mathbf{V} \) and line flows \( \mathbf{S} \), the RPF formulation aims to minimize the residual functions \( \mathbf{r}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) \):

\[ \mathbf{r}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) = \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) - \mathbf{P} \]

where \( \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{V}, \mathbf{S}) \) represents the actual power flow equations based on Kirchhoff's laws, and \( \mathbf{P} \) is the desired power flow vector.

To ensure AC feasibility, an additional slack variable \( \mathbf{\lambda} \) is introduced:

\[ \mathbf{\lambda} = \mathbf{V} - \mathbf{V}_{ref} \]

where \( \mathbf{V}_{ref} \) is the reference voltage vector.

### Learning the RPF with Neural Solvers

We will train neural networks to approximate the RPF problem. The neural network architecture will consist of multiple layers designed to capture the complex relationships between nodal voltages, line flows, and the residual functions. The training process will involve minimizing a loss function that measures the discrepancy between the predicted residual functions and the actual residual functions.

### Integration in a Predict-then-Optimize Framework

The Predict-then-Optimize (PO) approach involves two main stages: prediction and optimization. During the prediction stage, the neural network approximates the RPF problem to obtain a preliminary solution. In the optimization stage, this solution is refined using traditional optimization techniques to satisfy the operational constraints.

### Evaluation on Real-World Systems

To validate the effectiveness of the RPF formulation, we will conduct extensive simulations on real-world power systems, including the IEEE 9-bus system and other representative systems. The performance of the RPF formulation will be compared against traditional power flow methods to assess its accuracy and flexibility.
