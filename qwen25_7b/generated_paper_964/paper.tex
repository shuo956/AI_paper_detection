### Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) have shown remarkable progress in various natural language processing tasks, yet their potential in multimodal recommendation systems remains underexplored. This paper proposes a hybrid retrieval-augmented multimodal recommendation framework, PixRec++, that integrates both textual and visual information to enhance recommendation accuracy. By leveraging a vision-language model backbone and a dual-tower structure, our approach aligns multimodal feature projections to better capture item-item and user-item interactions. Experimental results on the Amazon Reviews dataset, augmented with product images, demonstrate significant improvements in top-rank and top-10 rank accuracy over text-only recommenders. Our work underscores the importance of integrating visual information in recommendation systems and opens new avenues for future research.
