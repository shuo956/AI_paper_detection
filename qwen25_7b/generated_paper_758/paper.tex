\section{Introduction}
Captcha systems play a crucial role in ensuring the integrity of online platforms by preventing automated bots from performing malicious activities such as account takeover, spamming, and scraping. In the context of academic research platforms, these systems are essential to maintain the quality and reliability of scholarly communication. Despite their importance, there has been limited research on the impact of captcha systems on user experience and accessibility. This study aims to address this gap by evaluating different captcha designs and their effects on usability and accessibility.

\section{Related Work}
Previous studies have focused on the security benefits of captcha systems (Smith et al., 2021) and their effectiveness in blocking automated bots (Jones & Brown, 2020). However, the impact of captcha systems on user experience and accessibility has received less attention. For instance, Smith et al. (2021) found that traditional captcha designs can significantly reduce user satisfaction, while Jones & Brown (2020) reported that captcha systems often pose challenges for users with visual impairments.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
This study employed a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews. A total of 200 participants were recruited from an academic institution, with 100 participants using traditional captcha systems and 100 using alternative designs. The survey assessed user satisfaction, perceived ease of use, and time taken to complete the captcha task. Qualitative interviews were conducted with 20 participants to gather detailed feedback on their experiences.

\section{Discussion}
The results indicate that traditional captcha systems can negatively impact user experience and accessibility. Alternative designs, such as audio captcha and image-based challenges, offer better usability and accessibility for users with disabilities. These findings suggest that there is a need for captcha systems that are not only secure but also user-friendly and accessible.

\section{Conclusion}
This study highlights the importance of considering user experience and accessibility when designing captcha systems for academic research platforms. Future research should focus on developing more inclusive captcha designs that strike a balance between security and user convenience.
