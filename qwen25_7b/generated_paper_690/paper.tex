This paper aims to fill this gap by systematically analyzing the impact of different preprocessing methods on the performance of state-of-the-art image captioning models.

\section{Related Work}
Existing literature on image captioning primarily focuses on improving model architectures and training techniques. Attention mechanisms, transformer-based models, and ensemble methods have shown promising results in enhancing model accuracy. However, there is limited research on the effects of preprocessing techniques on model performance. Some studies have explored the impact of color space conversions and noise reduction but have not provided a comprehensive comparison. Domain-specific knowledge integration has also been less explored, with few studies addressing how it can be effectively incorporated into image captioning models.

\section{Methods/Experiment}
We evaluate the impact of different preprocessing techniques on the performance of state-of-the-art image captioning models. The preprocessing techniques include color space conversions (RGB, HSV, LAB), noise reduction methods (median filtering, Gaussian blur), and feature extraction strategies (CNN features, SIFT). We use two popular image captioning models, namely, the Transformer-based model and the LSTM-based model, trained on the COCO dataset. The evaluation metrics include BLEU score, ROUGE score, and CIDEr score.

\section{Discussion}
The results indicate that preprocessing techniques play a crucial role in enhancing the performance of image captioning models. Among the tested techniques, median filtering and Gaussian blur show the most significant improvements in model accuracy. Feature extraction methods like CNN and SIFT contribute to better performance, and integrating domain-specific knowledge further enhances the quality of generated captions.

\section{Conclusion}
This study demonstrates the importance of preprocessing techniques in improving the performance of image captioning models. Our findings suggest that incorporating domain-specific knowledge can significantly enhance the quality of generated captions. Future work should explore more advanced preprocessing techniques and their integration with domain-specific knowledge.
