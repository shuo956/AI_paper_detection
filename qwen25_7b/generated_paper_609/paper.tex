### Introduction

Machine learning has become an integral part of modern technology, with applications spanning across various domains such as healthcare, finance, and autonomous systems. The success of machine learning models largely depends on the optimization algorithms used during the training process. One crucial aspect of these algorithms is the choice of hyperparameters, particularly the learning rate, which controls the step size at each iteration. A well-tuned learning rate can lead to faster convergence and better performance, while an ill-tuned one can result in suboptimal solutions or even divergence.

In recent years, various adaptive learning rate methods have been proposed to address the challenges associated with manual tuning of the learning rate. These methods dynamically adjust the learning rate based on the historical gradients, providing a more adaptive and efficient optimization process. Examples include AdaGrad, which accumulates past squared gradients to reduce the learning rate over time, and RMSProp, which normalizes the gradient using a moving average of squared gradients. More recently, Adam (Adaptive Moment Estimation) has gained popularity due to its combination of first-order and second-order moment estimates, leading to improved performance in a wide range of tasks.

Despite the success of these methods, there remains a need for further advancements in terms of computational efficiency and generalizability. This paper aims to contribute to the existing body of knowledge by proposing a novel adaptive learning rate technique that leverages momentum and second-order gradient information. We evaluate our approach on a diverse set of datasets and neural network architectures, demonstrating its effectiveness in enhancing the training process.

### Related Work

Several studies have explored the use of adaptive learning rate methods in machine learning. For instance, Huang et al. [1] compared the performance of AdaGrad, RMSProp, and Adam on deep neural networks and found that Adam generally outperforms the other two methods in terms of convergence speed and final accuracy. Similarly, Reddi et al. [2] introduced the AMSGrad variant of Adam, which addresses the potential vanishing bias problem and improves performance on certain types of optimization problems. Another notable contribution is the work by Li et al. [3], who proposed the Lookahead optimizer, which uses a lookahead mechanism to guide the search direction, resulting in faster convergence and better generalization.

While these methods have shown promising results, they often require careful hyperparameter tuning and may not perform optimally in all scenarios. For example, AdaGrad tends to have a very small learning rate as training progresses, which can slow down the optimization process. RMSProp, on the other hand, can suffer from unstable behavior when dealing with sparse gradients. Adam, although effective, may not always converge to the optimal solution, especially in non-convex optimization landscapes.

To address these limitations, we propose a novel adaptive learning rate technique that combines the strengths of different methods while mitigating their weaknesses. Our approach incorporates momentum to accelerate convergence and uses second-order gradient information to adaptively adjust the learning rate. This hybrid approach aims to strike a balance between computational efficiency and generalizability, making it suitable for a wide range of machine learning tasks.

&u_t = \beta_2 u_{t-1} + (1 - \beta_2) g_t^2 \\
&\hat{v}_t = v_t / (1 - \beta_1^t) \\
