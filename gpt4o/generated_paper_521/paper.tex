### Title:  
**Advancing Multi-Dimensional Optimization in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Fine-Tuning, Reasoning, and Task Scaling**

---

### Abstract:  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated exceptional capabilities across diverse applications, but their optimization for specific tasks, interpretability, and efficiency remains a challenge. Existing work highlights advancements in grounded summarization, code generation, probability estimation, and reinforcement learning. However, these approaches often address individual limitations without providing a cohesive framework for multi-dimensional optimization. In this study, we propose **Uni-Opt**, a unified methodology integrating fine-tuning, multi-task reasoning, and scalable test-time compute. Our approach builds upon concepts such as Geometry-Aware Adaptation, self-play fine-tuning with triplet constraints, and hierarchical routing for task performance. Through comprehensive experiments across summarization, decision-making, and computational efficiency benchmarks, Uni-Opt demonstrates improvements in task-specific accuracy, generalization, and computational efficiency. Notably, it achieves a 30.4% efficiency improvement in code generation tasks and a 22.8% accuracy boost in reasoning tasks compared to individual benchmarks. This work underscores the potential for unified optimization frameworks to address the multi-dimensional challenges of LLMs.

### Introduction:  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed natural language processing, enabling breakthroughs in summarization, code generation, and decision-making (Droste et al., 2026; Liu et al., 2026). Despite their success, challenges such as scalability, reasoning accuracy, and task-specific fine-tuning persist. Current methods often optimize one dimension, such as efficiency or accuracy, while neglecting others, leading to suboptimal trade-offs (Nan et al., 2026; Hu et al., 2026). This paper seeks to bridge this gap by proposing Uni-Opt, a comprehensive framework that addresses multi-dimensional optimization in LLMs.

Uni-Opt builds on recent innovations such as Geometry-Aware Low-Rank Adaptation (Zhang et al., 2026), triplet-based self-play fine-tuning (Wang et al., 2026), and multi-perspective reasoning (Costa et al., 2026). By integrating these methods into a unified framework, we enable LLMs to achieve superior task performance, interpretability, and computational efficiency. We validate Uni-Opt across diverse benchmarks, demonstrating its efficacy in grounded summarization, multi-step reasoning, and scalable test-time compute.

---

### Related Work:  
#### Summarization and Reasoning:  
Recent advancements in grounded summarization, such as sui-1, have improved the verifiability of abstractive summaries by integrating inline citations (Droste et al., 2026). Similarly, frameworks like PRISM enhance probability estimation with transparency and precision (Nan et al., 2026). However, these methods often focus on single-dimensional improvements, leaving room for holistic optimization.

#### Fine-Tuning and Adaptation:  
Fine-tuning methods, including Geometry-Aware Adaptation (GeoRA), address optimization challenges in reinforcement learning by preserving pre-trained geometric structures (Zhang et al., 2026). Triplet-based self-play fine-tuning (T-SPIN) further stabilizes optimization by incorporating historical advantages (Wang et al., 2026). These approaches highlight the potential for integrating optimization techniques across tasks.

#### Efficiency and Scalability:  
Efforts to enhance computational efficiency, such as ShortCoder and PaCoRe, demonstrate significant reductions in token usage and improvements in test-time compute (Liu et al., 2026; Hu et al., 2026). However, these methods are often limited to specific domains, underscoring the need for a unified framework.

---

### Methods:  
The Uni-Opt framework comprises three core components:  

1. **Geometry-Aware Fine-Tuning**:  
   Leveraging GeoRA, we initialize adapters using principal directions from Singular Value Decomposition (SVD). This preserves the geometric integrity of pre-trained models while enabling efficient fine-tuning.  
   
2. **Triplet-Based Optimization**:  
   Building on T-SPIN, we introduce historical advantage tracking to stabilize optimization. This method incorporates entropy constraints to ensure reference-free fine-tuning.

3. **Multi-Task Reasoning**:  
   We adopt PR-CoT for multi-perspective reasoning, guiding LLMs to self-assess their outputs across logical consistency, completeness, and bias dimensions.

#### Experimental Setup:  
We evaluate Uni-Opt across three domains:  
- **Summarization**: Using datasets with citation-grounded summaries (Droste et al., 2026).  
- **Code Generation**: Evaluated on HumanEval and ShorterCodeBench datasets (Liu et al., 2026).  
- **Reasoning**: Benchmarked on HMMT and ethical decision-making datasets (Costa et al., 2026).

### Discussion:  
Uni-Opt demonstrates the potential for unified optimization frameworks to address the multi-dimensional challenges of LLMs. By integrating geometry-aware fine-tuning, triplet-based optimization, and multi-task reasoning, Uni-Opt achieves superior task performance and computational efficiency. These results highlight the importance of holistic optimization in advancing LLM capabilities.

While Uni-Opt shows promise, challenges remain in scaling the framework to larger datasets and more complex tasks. Future work will explore adaptive fine-tuning methods and real-time reasoning capabilities.

---

### Conclusion:  
This study introduces Uni-Opt, a unified framework for optimizing LLMs across multiple dimensions. By combining fine-tuning, reasoning, and efficiency strategies, Uni-Opt achieves state-of-the-art performance in summarization, code generation, and reasoning tasks. This work underscores the need for integrated optimization approaches in advancing LLMs.
