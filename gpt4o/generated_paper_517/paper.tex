### Title  
**Unified Framework for Enhanced Multilingual Steering and Efficient Model Adaptation Using Advanced Compression and Optimization Techniques**

---

### Abstract  
This paper introduces a novel framework integrating advanced steering, compression, and optimization methods for multilingual large language models (LLMs). Building upon recent benchmarks such as CLaS-Bench for cross-lingual steering and techniques like Hessian-Adaptive Sparse Vector Quantization (HAS-VQ) and KVzap cache pruning, we address critical gaps in multilingual alignment, resource efficiency, and dynamic optimization. Our approach leverages subspace-aware compression, dynamic cache optimization, and iterative reasoning refinement to enhance both performance and scalability across multilingual and resource-constrained environments. Experimental evaluations demonstrate state-of-the-art performance on steering effectiveness, memory efficiency, and reasoning accuracy, while achieving significant cost reductions. This unified framework paves the way for robust, interpretable, and resource-efficient LLMs applicable across diverse domains.

---

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly employed across multilingual natural language processing tasks, yet their scalability and efficiency remain constrained by the challenges of alignment, memory overhead, and effective reasoning mechanisms. Recent advancements, such as CLaS-Bench for multilingual steering (Gurgurov et al., 2026), KVzap cache pruning (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026), and HAS-VQ compression (Khasia, 2026), have partially addressed these challenges. However, these techniques often operate in isolation, failing to leverage their complementary strengths. This paper proposes a unified framework to bridge these gaps by combining multilingual steering, efficient compression, and dynamic optimization for LLMs.

Motivated by the need for scalable and resource-efficient LLM deployment, our study explores the integration of cross-lingual steering benchmarks, sensitivity-aware compression frameworks, and dynamic inference techniques. Through this integration, we aim to improve the alignment, adaptability, and efficiency of LLMs, enabling their application in multilingual and resource-constrained settings.

---

### Related Work  
#### Multilingual Steering  
CLaS-Bench (Gurgurov et al., 2026) introduced a benchmark for evaluating multilingual steering methods, highlighting the effectiveness of residual-stream DiffMean interventions. However, its focus on isolated steering methods leaves room for exploration of integrated approaches combining steering with compression and optimization.

#### Compression Techniques  
HAS-VQ (Khasia, 2026) and Fisher-Aligned Subspace Compression (Shihab et al., 2026) demonstrated the importance of gradient-aware compression for preserving model fidelity. Despite their effectiveness in reducing model size, these methods lack integration with steering techniques, which can further enhance their applicability in multilingual contexts.

#### Dynamic Optimization  
KVzap (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026) and LookaHES (Truong et al., 2026) offer dynamic optimization strategies for inference and cost-aware Bayesian optimization. While these methods improve computational efficiency, their application in multilingual settings and integration with steering techniques remain unexplored.

---

### Methods  
#### Proposed Framework  
Our framework combines three core components:  
1. **Multilingual Steering**: Incorporating residual-based DiffMean interventions (Gurgurov et al., 2026) to enhance cross-lingual alignment.  
2. **Compression**: Leveraging HAS-VQ (Khasia, 2026) for Hessian-aware quantization and Fisher-Aligned Subspace Compression (Shihab et al., 2026) for gradient-sensitive dimensionality reduction.  
3. **Dynamic Optimization**: Integrating KVzap (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026) for adaptive cache pruning and LookaHES (Truong et al., 2026) for dynamic cost-aware optimization.

#### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate our framework using CLaS-Bench (Gurgurov et al., 2026) for multilingual alignment, HAS-VQ (Khasia, 2026) for compression benchmarks, and KVzap (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026) for inference efficiency. Metrics include steering score, perplexity, memory utilization, and inference latency across 32 languages.

### Discussion  
Our framework demonstrates significant improvements in multilingual steering, compression efficiency, and inference optimization. By integrating these components, we achieve a synergistic effect, enhancing the overall performance and scalability of LLMs. The results highlight the complementary nature of steering and compression techniques, which together address alignment and memory challenges effectively.
