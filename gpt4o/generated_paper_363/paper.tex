### Title:
**Hybrid Intelligence for Dynamic Graph Processing: Integrating Spiking Neural Networks with Quantum-Inspired Learning**

---

### Abstract:
Dynamic graphs, representing systems with evolving topologies, are critical for applications in social networks, sensor networks, and biological systems. Despite advances in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), challenges in scalability, capturing temporal dynamics, and handling irregular data sampling persist. Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs), inspired by biological systems, offer potential for handling temporal data, while quantum-inspired methods promise efficiency in complex computations. This study proposes a novel hybrid framework combining SNNs with Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF)-based graph structure optimization and quantum-inspired multimodal fusion. The framework efficiently captures temporal and structural dynamics in dynamic graph representations. Experimental evaluations on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate superior performance in graph classification, scalability, and energy efficiency, setting a new benchmark in dynamic graph processing.

---

### Introduction:
Dynamic graphs are central to modeling systems like social interactions, biological pathways, and sensor networks. However, their evolving structures pose challenges in representation and learning. Traditional GNNs rely on static approximations, limiting their ability to adapt to real-time changes. Inspired by biological systems' adaptability, SNNs offer a promising direction with their event-driven processing capabilities. Additionally, quantum-inspired algorithms can address the computational bottlenecks of large-scale dynamic graphs. This study introduces a novel framework integrating SNNs with RCF for dynamic graph structure learning and quantum-inspired multimodal fusion for robust temporal data processing.

### Related Work:
The proposed work builds upon three critical areas of research:
1. **Spiking Neural Networks for Temporal Learning**: SNNs excel in processing temporal data due to their biologically inspired, event-driven mechanisms. Lakshmi et al. (2026) introduced a supervised learning rule for SNNs, enhancing their efficiency for tasks like image classification.
2. **Graph Neural Networks and Dynamic Structures**: Fei et al. (2026) highlighted the limitations of static graph construction and proposed RCF for efficient curvature-based optimization in graph learning.
3. **Quantum-Inspired Multimodal Fusion**: Wu et al. (2026) discussed the advantages of quantum entanglement in multimodal learning, providing insights into reducing computational complexity while maintaining interpretability.

The gaps in integrating these advancements into a cohesive framework for dynamic graph processing motivate this study.

### Methods/Experiment:
The proposed framework integrates three components:
1. **Dynamic Graph Construction**:
   - Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF) optimizes graph structures dynamically, ensuring efficient representation of evolving topologies.
   - Effective resistance is calculated to adjust edge weights, enhancing cluster structures and reducing topological noise.

2. **Temporal Data Processing with SNNs**:
   - SNNs process temporal graph signals using supervised Spike Agreement-Dependent Plasticity (SADP) for efficient local learning.
   - Event-driven computations reduce latency and energy consumption compared to traditional neural networks.

3. **Quantum-Inspired Multimodal Fusion**:
   - A quantum-inspired fusion neural network captures interactions between graph nodes and external modalities.
   - Feature entanglement-based processing ensures interpretability and reduces computational complexity.

- **Baselines**: Static GNNs, traditional SNNs, and hybrid quantum-GNN models.
- **Metrics**: Graph classification accuracy, scalability, and energy efficiency.
