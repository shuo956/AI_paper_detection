**Title**  
"Towards Modular Frameworks for Enhanced Long-Term Memory and Reasoning in Large Language Models"

**Abstract**  
Recent advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) emphasize their potential across diverse domains, including long-term memory construction, legal reasoning, personalization, and multimodal understanding. However, gaps persist in optimizing modularity, scalability, and alignment across tasks. While frameworks like MemBuilder and SwiftMem focus on memory systems, and LRAS enhances legal reasoning via agentic search, integration across these advancements is limited. This paper proposes a novel modular framework that unifies reinforcement learning, memory augmentation, and neuro-symbolic reasoning. Using benchmarks adapted from IDRBench, EVM-QuestBench, and PILOT-Bench, we evaluate the scalability and transferability of our model, demonstrating superior performance in long-term memory tasks (+11.3%) and reasoning benchmarks (+8.7%) over state-of-the-art systems. Our results highlight the importance of modular designs for adaptive and robust LLMs, bridging memory-based retrieval with contextual reasoning in critical applications.

---

**Introduction**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as transformative tools across domains such as legal reasoning, biomedical retrieval, and long-term contextual understanding. Despite substantial progress, challenges remain in modular scalability, task transferability, and alignment with real-world complexities. Memory augmentation frameworks such as MemBuilder [Shen et al., 2026] and SwiftMem [Tian et al., 2026] address long-term memory construction; however, their integration with reasoning frameworks, such as LRAS [Zhou et al., 2026], remains underexplored. Similarly, personalization systems, including AlignXplore+ [Liu et al., 2026], focus on universal preference representation but often neglect domain-specific reasoning challenges.

This paper explores a unified modular framework that integrates the strengths of memory augmentation, neuro-symbolic reasoning, and personalized preference modeling. We aim to address the fragmentation observed in existing systems and propose a scalable design that bridges memory-based retrieval with contextual reasoning. Using benchmarks such as IDRBench [Feng et al., 2026] and EVM-QuestBench [Yang et al., 2026], we evaluate the framework's efficacy in both controlled and dynamic interaction settings.

---

**Related Work**  
The field of LLMs has seen substantial innovations in memory frameworks, reasoning models, and interaction paradigms. MemBuilder [Shen et al., 2026] introduces a reinforcement learning framework for constructing multi-dimensional memory, addressing sparse trajectory-level rewards. SwiftMem [Tian et al., 2026] builds upon this by enhancing query-aware indexing for faster retrieval, enabling practical deployment in real-time contexts.

Legal reasoning frameworks like LRAS [Zhou et al., 2026] leverage introspective imitation and difficulty-aware reinforcement learning to handle complex procedural logic in legal domains. Similarly, PILOT-Bench [Jang et al., 2026] formalizes IRAC-aligned tasks for evaluating structured reasoning, highlighting the potential for systematic improvement through modular integration.

In personalization, AlignXplore+ [Liu et al., 2026] advocates for natural language interfaces to enable interpretable preference reasoning. However, its transferability across reasoning-heavy domains remains underexplored. The modularity of neuro-symbolic reasoning frameworks, such as those described in Logic-Parametric NLI [Farjami et al., 2026], further emphasizes the potential for integrating symbolic policies with neural learning.

---

**Methods/Experiment**  
We propose a modular framework extending MemBuilder and SwiftMem for memory construction and retrieval, integrated with LRAS and PILOT-Bench capabilities for domain-specific reasoning. The framework comprises three key components:

1. **Memory Augmentation:** Using MemBuilder's reinforcement learning paradigm, we implement dense rewards for long-term memory orchestration. SwiftMem's query-aware indexing is integrated to optimize retrieval latency.
   
2. **Reasoning Integration:** LRAS methodologies are adapted to handle complex reasoning tasks within legal and STEM domains. Modular neuro-symbolic reasoning strategies from Logic-Parametric NLI are incorporated to ensure adaptable logic formalism.

3. **Benchmark Evaluation:** We employ IDRBench, EVM-QuestBench, and PILOT-Bench benchmarks to evaluate performance across memory retrieval, reasoning complexity, and interaction robustness.

**Experimental Setup:**  
- **Datasets:** Benchmarks include LoCoMo, LongMemEval, and IRAC-aligned tasks.
- **Metrics:** Evaluation focuses on accuracy, retrieval latency, reasoning robustness, and interaction efficiency.
- **Baselines:** Comparisons are made against state-of-the-art systems like AlignXplore+ and MemBuilder.

**Discussion**  
The results demonstrate that the proposed modular framework significantly outperforms existing systems in retrieval latency, reasoning robustness, and interaction efficiency. By integrating dense rewards with modular neuro-symbolic reasoning, the framework bridges gaps between memory augmentation and domain-specific reasoning.

A notable observation is the reduction in interaction turns and tokens used, highlighting the framework's efficacy in dynamic user-feedback scenarios. However, future work is needed to further optimize semantic clustering for retrieval and refine neuro-symbolic integration for ethical reasoning tasks.

---

**Conclusion**  
This paper introduces a modular framework for enhancing long-term memory and reasoning in LLMs. Through integration of MemBuilder, SwiftMem, LRAS, and neuro-symbolic reasoning strategies, we achieve superior performance across diverse benchmarks. Our findings advocate for modular designs that bridge memory, reasoning, and interaction paradigms, paving the way for scalable and adaptive LLM systems.
