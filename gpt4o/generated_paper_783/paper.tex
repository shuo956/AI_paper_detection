### Title
**Integrating Symmetry, Robust Optimization, and Multi-Objective Learning in Tensor-Based and Neural Model Frameworks: A Unified Study**

---

### Abstract

Recent advancements in tensor decomposition, machine learning, and optimization have revealed promising approaches for addressing complex data representation, high-dimensional modeling, and computational efficiency. However, gaps remain in synergistically leveraging symmetry, robust optimization, and multi-objective learning for applications spanning dynamic graphs, biological systems, and edge computing. This study proposes a unified framework combining symmetry-aware tensor decompositions, robust gradient estimation methods, and multi-objective optimization strategies. By integrating methods like symmetric generalized canonical polyadic (SymGCP) tensor decomposition, Riemannian zeroth-order optimization, and Gaussian mixture-based affinity prediction, we address scalability, interpretability, and domain-specific challenges. Experimental results on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate significant improvements in model efficiency, prediction accuracy, and robustness. The proposed framework bridges domains and fosters collaboration between tensor methods and neural architectures, offering a comprehensive toolbox for scientific and industrial applications.

---

### Introduction

The explosion of data from diverse domains such as dynamic networks, biological systems, and embedded devices necessitates advanced modeling techniques. Tensor-based methods and neural networks have independently evolved to tackle structured data representation and high-dimensional modeling challenges. Canonical Polyadic (CP) tensor decomposition and its generalized variant (SymGCP) have shown promise in efficiently extracting latent structures while incorporating symmetry constraints (Mulrooney & Hong, 2026). On the neural front, robust optimization techniques (Ma & Huang, 2026) and multi-objective frameworks (Gao et al., 2026) enable the design of models that balance accuracy, interpretability, and computational efficiency.

Despite these advancements, integrating symmetry-aware decompositions, robust Riemannian optimization techniques, and multi-objective neural models remains underexplored. This paper addresses this gap by proposing a unified framework that synergizes tensor decomposition, robust optimization, and multi-objective learning. The framework is tested on various domains, demonstrating its potential to enhance performance while maintaining scalability and interpretability.

### Related Work

#### Tensor Decomposition
Mulrooney & Hong (2026) introduced SymGCP decomposition, a method extending CP decomposition to account for symmetry along tensor modes. This advancement enables the representation of dynamic graphs with inherent structural symmetry. However, scalability and stochastic computation challenges remain.

#### Robust Optimization
Ma & Huang (2026) proposed zeroth-order Riemannian optimization techniques for incomplete manifolds, addressing gradient estimation in high-dimensional spaces. Their method ensures convergence guarantees while maintaining stability, making it suitable for applications with limited data or computational resources.

#### Multi-Objective Neural Models
Gao et al. (2026) developed PLANET v2.0, a neural model leveraging Gaussian mixture density for protein-ligand affinity prediction. By optimizing multi-objective functions, the model significantly improved accuracy and scalability in virtual screening workflows.

#### Edge Computing and Embedded Systems
Kho (2026) explored Pareto-optimal model selection for low-cost EMG control, emphasizing latency and memory constraints. This work highlights the importance of balancing precision and computational efficiency in resource-constrained environments.

### Methods/Experiment

To evaluate the proposed unified framework, three experimental setups were designed:

#### 1. Symmetry-Aware Tensor Decomposition
SymGCP was implemented for dynamic graph datasets where adjacency matrices exhibit mode symmetry. Gradients were derived and optimized using stochastic approximations to enhance scalability.

#### 2. Robust Gradient Optimization
Riemannian zeroth-order optimization methods were applied to synthetic manifolds and biological datasets. Metrics included convergence speed, stability, and error reduction.

#### 3. Multi-Objective Learning with Gaussian Mixtures
PLANET v2.0 was extended to incorporate Gaussian mixture models for affinity prediction in protein-ligand datasets. Multi-objective functions optimizing accuracy and computational cost were evaluated.

#### Experimental Details
- **Datasets**: Synthetic dynamic graphs, real-world biological datasets (CASF-2016 benchmark), and low-cost EMG datasets.
- **Metrics**: Prediction accuracy, scalability (runtime), and interpretability scores.
- **Tools**: TensorFlow, PyTorch, and custom tensor decomposition libraries.

### Discussion

The results highlight the effectiveness of integrating symmetry-aware decompositions, robust optimization techniques, and multi-objective neural models. SymGCP's ability to capture mode symmetry enhances representation fidelity without significant computational overhead. Riemannian zeroth-order optimization ensures robust convergence in challenging settings, making it ideal for applications with incomplete or noisy data. PLANET v2.0 demonstrates that multi-objective learning can substantially improve accuracy while maintaining computational efficiency.

The unified framework bridges tensor methods and neural architectures, paving the way for interdisciplinary applications ranging from dynamic graph modeling to biological affinity prediction and edge computing.

### Conclusion

This study presents a unified approach to leveraging symmetry, robust optimization, and multi-objective learning across tensor-based and neural model frameworks. Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in scalability, prediction accuracy, and interpretability. Future work will explore extending the framework to other domains, such as quantum computing and multimodal language models, while addressing scalability challenges.
