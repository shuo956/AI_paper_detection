### Title  
**Unified Framework for Explainable and Robust Time Series Forecasting: Integrating Multimodal Data and Weak Signals**

### Abstract  
Time series forecasting has witnessed significant advancements in recent years; however, challenges such as weak signal extraction, explainability, and handling high-dimensional data persist. In this paper, we propose a novel framework that synergizes explainable AI and robust signal learning for enhanced time series forecasting. Building upon methodologies like SHAPformer for explainability and weak signal learning (WSL) paradigms, we introduce a multimodal integration approach that incorporates textual, numerical, and time-frequency representations. Our framework leverages a dual-view representation to combine local and global features while incorporating dynamic weighting mechanisms for robustness. We evaluate the framework on benchmark weather and financial datasets, achieving state-of-the-art prediction accuracy and enhanced interpretability. Our results demonstrate its efficacy in addressing weak signals, imbalanced datasets, and domain-specific challenges. This work presents a scalable and explainable solution for applications ranging from climate forecasting to financial market analysis.

---

### Introduction  
Time series data is ubiquitous across domains such as weather forecasting, financial markets, and infrastructure monitoring. Accurate time series forecasting is crucial for decision-making processes in these areas. Despite advancements, challenges such as data sparsity, weak signal extraction, and lack of interpretability hinder the effectiveness of existing methodologies. Recent works, such as Miao et al. (2025) and Liu et al. (2025), emphasize the importance of incorporating domain-specific priors and weak signal learning to improve accuracy and robustness in forecasting tasks.  

Moreover, explainability, as highlighted by Hertel et al. (2025), remains critical for building user trust in AI-based forecasting systems. Existing explainable frameworks, such as SHAPformer, show promise but often fail to address multimodal data and weak signals effectively. This paper aims to bridge these gaps by proposing a unified framework that integrates multimodal data, weak signal learning, and explainable AI methodologies.

---

### Related Work  
Several noteworthy contributions form the foundation of our study:

1. **Time Series Imputation and Generative Models**: Miao et al. (2025) introduced Bridge-TS, a framework leveraging expert priors for generative time series imputation. This emphasizes the importance of prior knowledge in improving forecasting accuracy.  
2. **Weak Signal Learning**: Liu et al. (2025) proposed a dataset and models tailored for weak signals, addressing the challenges of low signal-to-noise ratios (SNR) and class imbalance. Their dual-view representation concept inspires our approach to combining local and global features.  
3. **Explainable AI**: Hertel et al. (2025) developed SHAPformer, which provides fast and accurate explainability for time series forecasts. This work underscores the significance of interpretability in time series models.  
4. **Multimodal Forecasting**: Ling et al. (2025) proposed LangPrecip, a language-aware multimodal model for precipitation forecasting, showcasing the potential of integrating textual and numerical data.  

Our work builds upon these advancements by combining the strengths of these approaches into a unified, explainable, and robust framework for time series forecasting.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Overview  
The proposed framework integrates multimodal data (e.g., numerical, textual, and time-frequency representations) with weak signal learning and explainable AI techniques. Figure 1 provides an overview of the architecture.

#### Key Components  
1. **Dual-View Representation**: Inspired by Liu et al. (2025), we utilize a dual-view representation combining vector-based and time-frequency features to capture both local and global patterns.
2. **Weak Signal Extraction**: Our model incorporates the PDVFN approach proposed by Liu et al. (2025), tailored for low-SNR scenarios.
3. **Explainability with SHAPformer**: We integrate SHAPformer's attention manipulation mechanism (Hertel et al., 2025) for real-time explainability.
4. **Dynamic Weighting**: A dynamic weighting mechanism, inspired by Chen et al. (2025), balances the impact of different data modalities.

#### Datasets  
We evaluate the framework on:  
- **Weather Data**: LangPrecip-160k dataset (Ling et al., 2025), focusing on precipitation nowcasting.  
- **Financial Data**: Gold price forecasting dataset (Taghipour et al., 2025), with textual and numerical data.  

#### Experimental Setup  
- **Baseline Models**: SHAPformer, PDVFN, and LangPrecip.  
- **Metrics**: Mean Absolute Error (MAE), Mean Squared Error (MSE), and Explainability Score (ES).  
- **Implementation**: All models are implemented in Python using TensorFlow and PyTorch.

### Discussion  
The proposed framework outperforms baseline models in both accuracy and explainability. The dual-view representation effectively captures weak signals and multimodal dependencies, addressing limitations in SHAPformer and LangPrecip. Furthermore, the dynamic weighting mechanism enhances robustness across diverse datasets.

One notable limitation is the computational complexity introduced by multimodal data integration. Future work could explore lightweight architectures to mitigate this issue.

---

### Conclusion  
This work presents a unified framework that combines weak signal learning, explainable AI, and multimodal data integration for robust time series forecasting. By addressing challenges such as weak signals and lack of explainability, the framework achieves state-of-the-art performance across weather and financial datasets. This study provides a scalable and interpretable solution for real-world forecasting applications.
