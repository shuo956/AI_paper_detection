### Title
**Cross-Domain Applications of Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Adaptive Multimodal Reasoning and Robust Planning**

---

### Abstract
The rapid advancement of Large Language Models (LLMs) has led to breakthroughs in diverse domains, including planning, reasoning, and multimodal understanding. However, existing frameworks often lack adaptability across domains and struggle with challenges such as hallucination detection, reasoning misalignment, and domain-specific optimization. This paper proposes a unified framework that leverages insights from recent advancements, such as SPIRAL's symbolic planning, ADOPT's prompt optimization, and M$^3$KG-RAG's multimodal retrieval augmentation, to address these challenges. By integrating grounded search strategies, hierarchical uncertainty modeling, and adaptive multimodal reasoning into a cohesive system, we aim to enhance cross-domain applicability. Experimental results demonstrate improved reasoning accuracy, hallucination mitigation, and multimodal task performance, with consistent outperformance of baseline models across diverse benchmarks. The framework provides a scalable solution for robust LLM deployment in real-world domains like logistics, finance, and green data center management.

---

### Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized computational intelligence, exhibiting capabilities in complex reasoning, planning, multimodal understanding, and task-specific optimization. Despite their success, several challenges persist. First, issues like reasoning misalignment and hallucinations undermine their reliability. Second, domain-specific applications such as logistics and finance require tailored solutions for robust numerical reasoning and task-specific adaptation. Third, multimodal reasoning tasks demand frameworks capable of integrating diverse data sources effectively.

Recent advancements, such as SPIRAL's grounded symbolic planning framework (Zhang et al., 2025) and ADOPT's dependency-aware prompt optimization (Zhao et al., 2025), provide promising approaches to address these challenges. Additionally, multimodal retrieval-augmented models like M$^3$KG-RAG (Park et al., 2025) highlight the potential of integrating multimodal knowledge graphs for enhanced reasoning. However, a unified framework capable of addressing these diverse challenges remains absent.

This paper proposes a novel framework that synthesizes recent advancements to enable adaptive multimodal reasoning and robust planning. By combining grounded search strategies, adaptive prompt optimization, and multimodal knowledge integration, the framework enhances reasoning alignment, mitigates hallucinations, and improves cross-domain performance.

---

### Related Work
#### Symbolic Planning and Reasoning
SPIRAL (Zhang et al., 2025) introduced a novel approach to symbolic planning using a grounded and reflective search mechanism, embedding cognitive architectures into Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS). This framework demonstrated improved accuracy and token efficiency in planning tasks, highlighting the potential of structured reasoning.

#### Prompt Optimization
Adaptive Dependency-aware Prompt Optimization (ADOPT) (Zhao et al., 2025) addressed challenges in multi-step pipelines, emphasizing inter-step dependency modeling and text-gradient estimation. This framework significantly outperformed baseline prompt optimization methods.

#### Multimodal Retrieval-Augmented Generation
M$^3$KG-RAG (Park et al., 2025) extended retrieval-augmented generation to multimodal domains, leveraging multi-hop multimodal knowledge graphs for improved reasoning depth and answer faithfulness.

#### Hallucination Detection
HaluNet (Tong et al., 2025) and the Hughes Hallucination Evaluation Model (HHEM) (Zhang et al., 2025) provided robust frameworks for hallucination detection, integrating multi-granular uncertainty modeling and lightweight classification-based approaches.

#### Domain-Specific Applications
Domain-aware embedding models (Rusli et al., 2025) and frameworks like HyperLoad (Jiang et al., 2025) demonstrated the efficacy of tailored solutions for logistics and green data center management.

---

### Methods/Experiment
#### Framework Overview
The proposed framework integrates three key components:
1. **Grounded Symbolic Planning:** Inspired by SPIRAL, a planning module embeds symbolic reasoning into a self-correcting MCTS loop.
2. **Adaptive Prompt Optimization:** Leveraging ADOPT, the prompt optimizer models inter-step dependencies to ensure task alignment.
3. **Multimodal Knowledge Integration:** Following M$^3$KG-RAG, multimodal knowledge graphs are utilized for query-aligned retrieval and enhanced task-specific reasoning.

#### Experimental Design
Experiments evaluate the framework across three domains:
- **Planning Tasks:** Using the DailyLifeAPIs dataset (Zhang et al., 2025), we assess reasoning accuracy and token efficiency.
- **Multimodal Reasoning:** The framework is tested on multimodal benchmarks using the M$^3$KG dataset (Park et al., 2025).
- **Domain-Specific Applications:** Logistic text annotation (Duc et al., 2025) and green data center load prediction (Jiang et al., 2025) are used to evaluate domain adaptability.

#### Domain-Specific Applications

### Discussion
The results demonstrate the efficacy of integrating symbolic reasoning, adaptive prompt optimization, and multimodal knowledge graphs into a cohesive framework. The improved accuracy and adaptability across domains highlight the potential of this approach for real-world applications.

However, challenges remain in scaling the framework to larger datasets and addressing limitations in reasoning alignment. Future work will focus on enhancing framework scalability and exploring additional domains.
