### Title  
**Enhancing Contextual Understanding and Robustness in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Cyber Threat Detection and Personalization**

---

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated exceptional capabilities across diverse domains, yet challenges persist in accurately detecting cyber threats and achieving efficient personalization under constrained conditions. This study proposes a unified framework integrating methodologies from recent advancements in threat detection using generative AI and personalization optimization, with a focus on improving contextual understanding and robustness. By leveraging techniques such as RoBERTa-based contextual encoding, lightweight adaptation frameworks like PRISP, and positional decay reweighting from PDR, the framework aims to address limitations in existing approaches. Experimental results show improved performance in detecting cyber threats and adapting models for personalized contexts, with enhancements in accuracy, contextual relevance, and computational efficiency. This work contributes to bridging the gap between detection and personalization by introducing novel mechanisms for simultaneous optimization.

---

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as transformative tools in natural language processing (NLP), enabling advancements in tasks ranging from sentiment analysis to cyber threat detection. However, significant challenges remain in two key areas: cyber threat detection, where contextual precision is critical, and personalization, which often occurs in data-constrained environments. 

The growing sophistication of cyber-attacks necessitates models capable of nuanced contextual interpretation, as demonstrated by Keerthi Kumar et al. (2026). Meanwhile, personalization frameworks like PRISP (Park et al., 2026) highlight the importance of adapting LLMs efficiently under privacy constraints. Despite their individual successes, existing methodologies often operate independently, leaving a gap in unified systems that can simultaneously optimize for both detection and contextual adaptation. This paper proposes a unified framework that integrates advancements in contextual encoding, lightweight adaptation, and positional decay techniques to address these challenges.

---

### Related Work  
#### Cyber Threat Detection  
Keerthi Kumar et al. (2026) introduced a RoBERTa-based framework for detecting cyber threats, surpassing traditional BERT models in accuracy and recall. The methodology employed Byte Pair Encoding (BPE) and Fully Harmonic Encryption (FHE) to enhance vocabulary representation and secure data preprocessing. Despite its success, the model's scalability to diverse contexts remains limited.

#### Personalization in LLMs  
PRISP (Park et al., 2026) offered a lightweight personalization framework using Text-to-LoRA hypernetworks, demonstrating efficacy in few-shot settings. By optimizing LoRA parameters, PRISP achieved robust adaptation under constrained computational resources. However, its application in real-time, context-sensitive scenarios remains underexplored.

#### Positional Decay Reweighting  
Positional Decay Reweighting (PDR) (Liu et al., 2026) introduced a novel approach to enhance pre-training data detection, emphasizing the importance of early token positions. This technique offers potential synergies when combined with contextual encoding methods, as it prioritizes salient data features.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Design  
The proposed framework integrates three core components:  
1. **Contextual Encoding for Threat Detection**: Extending the RoBERTa model, the framework incorporates Byte Pair Encoding with enriched contextual understanding mechanisms to improve threat detection.  
2. **Lightweight Personalization**: Using PRISP's Text-to-LoRA hypernetwork, the model adapts to user-specific contexts by optimizing LoRA parameters based on limited data.  
3. **Positional Decay Optimization**: PDR techniques are applied to reweight token scores, amplifying contextually relevant data and reducing noise.

#### Data Collection  
Two datasets were utilized for evaluation:  
- **Cyber Threat Dataset (CTD)**: Consisting of Packet Capture (PCAP) files annotated for cyber-attack types.  
- **Personalization Dataset (PD)**: Few-shot user data extracted from diverse domains, optimized for lightweight adaptation experiments.

#### Experimental Setup  
The experiments were conducted in Python using TensorFlow and PyTorch frameworks. Metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and computational efficiency were used for evaluation.

### Discussion  
The results demonstrate the efficacy of integrating contextual encoding, lightweight adaptation, and positional decay techniques. The unified framework achieved superior performance in both threat detection and personalization tasks, highlighting its versatility. Notably, the improvement in computational efficiency and privacy risk reduction positions the framework as a practical solution for real-world applications.

While the proposed model demonstrated robustness across diverse datasets, further exploration into domain-specific challenges, such as multilingual contexts and adversarial scenarios, is necessary. Additionally, real-time testing in production environments would provide insights into scalability and user experience.

### Conclusion  
This study presents a unified framework that leverages advancements in contextual encoding, lightweight personalization, and positional decay techniques to enhance the robustness and efficiency of Large Language Models. Experimental results indicate significant improvements in cyber threat detection accuracy and personalized adaptation metrics. The framework bridges existing gaps by providing a scalable and efficient solution for simultaneous optimization.
