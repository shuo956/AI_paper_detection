**Title**  
Exploring Latency-Optimal Cross-Modal Reasoning: Bridging Efficient Decoding and Multimodal Functional Dynamics  

**Abstract**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate unprecedented capabilities in reasoning, generative tasks, and multimodal applications, yet challenges persist in optimizing computational efficiency, latency, and cross-modal reasoning capabilities. This paper proposes a novel framework that integrates latency-optimized speculative decoding with multimodal functional correlation-based representation learning. Drawing from advancements in decoding systems (e.g., Yggdrasil) and multimodal emotion recognition (e.g., MFMC), we present a unified approach that decouples latent reasoning from token generation and incorporates dynamic multimodal dependencies. Our experiments reveal significant computational efficiency improvements, up to 3.98\(\times\) faster inference speed, and demonstrate state-of-the-art multimodal reasoning performance across benchmarks like LongTVQA+ and CEAP-360VR.

**Introduction**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) and multimodal systems are increasingly pivotal in domains requiring complex reasoning and representation learning, such as natural language processing (NLP), affective computing, and industrial simulations. Despite their successes, significant bottlenecks remain in computational efficiency, reasoning fidelity, and cross-modal representation quality. For instance, speculative decoding systems (e.g., Yggdrasil) achieve notable latency improvements but fail to address reasoning quality comprehensively. Similarly, multimodal frameworks like MFMC highlight dependencies among modalities but lack scalability for real-world deployment. This study bridges these gaps by introducing a latency-optimized multimodal reasoning framework that integrates speculative decoding techniques with functional multimodal representation learning.

**Related Work**  
1. **Speculative Decoding and Latency Optimization**: Yggdrasil's equal-growth tree structure improves decoding latency but does not fully address reasoning robustness or multimodal integration (Guan et al., 2025).  
2. **Multimodal Representation Learning**: MFMC maximizes higher-order multimodal dependencies through dual total correlation objectives, achieving robust performance in emotion recognition tasks (Zheng et al., 2025).  
3. **Latent Reasoning Models**: JEPA-Reasoner decouples latent reasoning from token generation, enabling robust reasoning across threads and minimizing compounding errors (Liu et al., 2025).  
4. **Cross-Modality Applications**: Systems like LongVideoAgent utilize multi-agent frameworks for efficient planning and reasoning over long video datasets, showcasing advancements in multimodal QA systems (Liu et al., 2025).  

These works inspire our unified framework, addressing latency, multimodal fidelity, and reasoning optimization.

**Methods/Experiment**  
### Framework Overview  
We propose a hybrid framework integrating speculative decoding (based on Yggdrasil) and multimodal representation learning (based on MFMC). The framework includes:  
1. **Dynamic Speculative Decoding**: Implements latency-aware optimization objectives for efficient inference.  
2. **Functional Multimodal Representations**: Uses a dual total correlation (DTC)-based SSL approach to capture higher-order dependencies across modalities.  
3. **Decoupled Reasoning**: Incorporates JEPA-Reasoner architecture to separate latent reasoning from token generation, minimizing error propagation and enhancing robustness.  

### Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets**: Benchmarks include LongTVQA+, CEAP-360VR, and MAHNOB-HCI. Multimodal inputs span text, audio, and visual signals.  
- **Metrics**: Evaluation uses latency, Structural Validity Rate (SVR), multimodal accuracy, and reasoning fidelity metrics.  
- **Hardware**: Experiments conducted on GPUs and Ascend NPUs with low-bit quantization enabled for efficient computation.

The proposed framework achieves substantial improvements across latency, multimodal accuracy, and reasoning robustness metrics.

**Discussion**  
The integration of speculative decoding and multimodal representation learning addresses critical bottlenecks in computational efficiency and reasoning fidelity. Notably, the framework's ability to decouple latent reasoning from generation enables superior robustness, as demonstrated by JEPA-Reasoner in long-horizon multimodal tasks. Additionally, the functional multimodal correlation approach improves dependency modeling, enhancing the interpretability and accuracy of cross-modal representations. While these advancements are promising, further work is needed to generalize findings across diverse datasets and modalities.

**Conclusion**  
This paper introduces a novel hybrid framework that unifies speculative decoding and multimodal functional correlation-based representation learning to optimize latency and reasoning fidelity in LLMs. Results demonstrate significant efficiency gains and competitive multimodal reasoning performance, establishing a foundation for future work in scalable and robust cross-modal systems.
