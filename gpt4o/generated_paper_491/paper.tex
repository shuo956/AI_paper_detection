# Title  
**Integrating Multi-Modal Reasoning with Large Language Models (LLMs): A Framework for Industrial-Grade Table Question Answering and Decision-Making**  

# Abstract  
Recent advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have enabled their application across diverse domains, including industrial-grade reasoning tasks such as Table Question Answering (TableQA), regulatory compliance, and decision-making under uncertainty. However, challenges such as handling multi-table structures, implicit compliance rules, sparse rewards, and noisy data remain significant. This paper proposes a novel framework that integrates structured reasoning with explainable AI methodologies, leveraging the strengths of benchmarks like ReasonTabQA, LogiSafetyBench, and advanced training paradigms such as Gibbs Initialization with Finite Temperature (GIFT). By combining table-based reasoning, subgoal decomposition, and decision-theoretic frameworks, our approach enhances interpretability, scalability, and robustness in real-world industrial scenarios. Experiments demonstrate improved performance on TableQA and compliance benchmarks while showcasing the adaptability of the framework across domains.  

# Introduction  
The advent of LLMs has revolutionized natural language processing and reasoning tasks, offering unprecedented capabilities in domains such as conversational AI, regulatory compliance, and decision-making. Despite these advancements, industrial-grade applications pose unique challenges, including handling complex multi-table structures, ensuring compliance with implicit regulatory constraints, and addressing sparse reward scenarios in reinforcement learning. Existing benchmarks, such as ReasonTabQA, have revealed significant performance gaps in real-world industrial TableQA tasks, highlighting the need for more robust and scalable frameworks.  

This paper addresses these challenges by proposing a multi-modal reasoning framework that combines recent innovations in LLMs, including supervised fine-tuning, reinforcement learning, and explainable AI techniques. Our approach leverages insights from ReasonTabQA, LogiSafetyBench, and similar benchmarks to enhance the interpretability, scalability, and robustness of LLMs in industrial scenarios.  

# Related Work  
### Table Question Answering (ReasonTabQA)  
ReasonTabQA has identified critical challenges in TableQA, including multi-table reasoning, nested headers, and the need for explicit reasoning chains. As shown in Pan et al. (2026), current LLMs struggle to handle the complexity of real-world industrial tables.  

### Regulatory Compliance (LogiSafetyBench)  
Implicit regulatory compliance is a significant challenge in high-stakes domains. LogiSafetyBench evaluates LLMs' ability to enforce safety constraints while maintaining functional correctness (Song et al., 2026).  

### Subgoal Reasoning and Sparse Rewards  
Frameworks such as STO-RL and SGVR emphasize the importance of subgoal decomposition and dense reward shaping in overcoming sparse reward scenarios (Gu et al., 2026; Chen et al., 2026).  

### Explainable AI and Decision-Making  
Explainable AI techniques, such as Shapley Reconstruction and Value of Information (VoI), enhance the interpretability of LLMs and their decision-making processes (Nan et al., 2026; Dong et al., 2026).  

# Methods/Experiment  
### Multi-Modal Reasoning Framework  
Our framework integrates the following components:  
1. **Table-Based Reasoning:** Leveraging ReasonTabQA for multi-table reasoning with explicit reasoning chains.  
2. **Regulatory Compliance:** Utilizing LogiSafetyBench to ensure implicit compliance with safety constraints.  
3. **Subgoal Decomposition:** Employing STO-RL and SGVR for subgoal reasoning and dense reward shaping.  
4. **Decision-Theoretic Framework:** Implementing VoI to balance task risk, query ambiguity, and user effort.  
5. **Explainable AI:** Adopting Shapley Reconstruction to enhance interpretability and user trust.  

### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate our framework on the following benchmarks:  
- ReasonTabQA for TableQA tasks.  
- LogiSafetyBench for regulatory compliance.  
- STO-RL and SGVR benchmarks for subgoal reasoning.

# Discussion  
The results highlight the effectiveness of our framework in addressing the challenges of industrial-grade reasoning tasks. Specifically:  
1. **Table-Based Reasoning:** The use of explicit reasoning chains significantly improved performance on ReasonTabQA.  
2. **Regulatory Compliance:** The integration of LogiSafetyBench ensured compliance with implicit safety constraints without compromising functional correctness.  
3. **Subgoal Decomposition:** Dense reward shaping and temporal structuring enhanced performance on sparse reward scenarios.  
4. **Explainable AI and Decision-Making:** The adoption of Shapley Reconstruction and VoI improved interpretability and user trust.  

# Conclusion  
This paper presents a novel framework that integrates multi-modal reasoning with explainable AI techniques to address the challenges of industrial-grade applications. By leveraging insights from recent advancements in TableQA, regulatory compliance, and decision-making, our framework demonstrates significant improvements in accuracy, interpretability, and robustness across multiple benchmarks.
