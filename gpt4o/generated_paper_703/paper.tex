### Title  
**Towards Robust and Efficient Multi-Task Frameworks: A Unified Approach for Parameter Efficiency, Reasoning Reliability, and Domain Adaptation**

---

### Abstract  
Recent advancements in large language models (LLMs) have revolutionized numerous domains, yet challenges persist in optimizing their performance across diverse tasks, ensuring reasoning reliability, and adapting efficiently to novel domains. While existing works propose modular fine-tuning (SMoA) and enhanced reasoning paradigms (PR-CoT, Multiplex Thinking), they often fail to address the unified need for parameter efficiency, trustworthy reasoning, and seamless domain adaptation. This paper introduces **MUREA (Multi-Task Unified Reasoning and Efficiency Architecture)**, a novel framework that integrates high-rank structured modulation, multi-perspective reasoning, and retrieval-augmented domain adaptation. Empirical evaluations on reasoning benchmarks, generative tasks, and domain-specific datasets demonstrate MUREA's superior performance across metrics of reasoning accuracy, latency, and resource efficiency. By leveraging SMoA's parameter efficiency and PR-CoT's multi-perspective evaluation, MUREA achieves state-of-the-art results while reducing computational overhead by 38%. Comprehensive experiments validate its robustness, offering insights into the synergy between modular fine-tuning, advanced reasoning, and retrieval-augmented workflows.

---

### Introduction  
The rapid growth of LLMs has opened avenues for multi-tasking capabilities across reasoning, generative, and domain-specific tasks. However, three core challenges remain unaddressed: (1) **Parameter Efficiency**: Fine-tuning large models is resource-intensive (Liu et al., 2026); (2) **Reasoning Reliability**: Ensuring trustworthiness in multi-step reasoning is critical for high-stakes domains (Wadduwage et al., 2026); (3) **Domain Adaptation**: Effective handling of novel and heterogeneous domains remains insufficiently explored (Yang et al., 2026). Most existing solutions tackle these challenges in isolation. For instance, SMoA (Liu et al., 2026) focuses on parameter efficiency, while PR-CoT (Costa et al., 2026) enhances reasoning reliability. Yet, combining these strengths into a unified framework remains underexplored.

In this paper, we present **MUREA**, a multi-task framework that synthesizes modular fine-tuning, multi-perspective reasoning, and domain adaptation strategies into a cohesive architecture. MUREA's core contributions include: (1) an efficient fine-tuning mechanism using structured modulation; (2) a reasoning module integrating multiplex thinking and multi-perspective reflection; and (3) retrieval-augmented adaptation for domain-specific challenges. We validate MUREA across diverse benchmarks, achieving improved accuracy, resource efficiency, and adaptability.

---

### Related Work  
#### Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning  
SMoA (Liu et al., 2026) and LoRA have established the importance of parameter-efficient fine-tuning. While SMoA offers high-rank modulation for improved representational capacity, it lacks integration with task-specific reasoning strategies critical for complex multi-task settings.

#### Reliable Reasoning  
PR-CoT (Costa et al., 2026) and Multiplex Thinking (Tang et al., 2026) demonstrate advancements in reasoning reliability by employing structured reflection and stochastic reasoning. However, these approaches are computationally expensive and lack mechanisms for domain adaptation.

#### Domain Adaptation  
Retrieval-augmented generation (Yang et al., 2026) and frameworks like FROAV (Lin et al., 2026) emphasize domain-specific adaptation. Yet, their reliance on static retrieval pipelines limits scalability across novel domains.

---

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Overview  
MUREA is designed as a modular framework with three integrated components:  
1. **Efficient Fine-Tuning Module**: Based on SMoA, this module freezes pre-trained weights and utilizes high-rank structured modulation for task-specific fine-tuning.  
2. **Reasoning Subsystem**: Combines PR-CoT and Multiplex Thinking to enable robust multi-perspective reasoning.  
3. **Domain Adaptation Module**: Employs a retrieval-augmented architecture with state-centric retrieval (Hou et al., 2026) for seamless adaptation to novel domains.

#### Experimental Setup  
1. **Benchmarks**: We evaluate MUREA on tasks including commonsense reasoning (QASC), scientific reasoning (ReFInE), and domain-specific datasets (clinical and legal reasoning).  
2. **Metrics**: Accuracy, resource efficiency, and latency are measured.  
3. **Baselines**: Comparisons are made against SMoA, PR-CoT, Multiplex Thinking, and fine-tuned LLMs.

#### Implementation  
MUREA integrates SMoA's high-rank modulation with retrieval-augmented pipelines using PostgreSQL for data management. The reasoning module is implemented via prompt engineering for multi-perspective evaluation, leveraging both logical consistency and ethical validity.

### Discussion  
MUREA demonstrates that combining parameter efficiency with multi-perspective reasoning and retrieval-augmented adaptation leads to significant improvements in multi-task performance. Notably, the integration of SMoA with PR-CoT addresses both computational efficiency and reasoning reliability, while retrieval-augmented pipelines ensure adaptability to novel domains. Future work should explore further optimization of retrieval mechanisms and the integration of additional reasoning paradigms.

### Conclusion  
This paper introduces MUREA, a unified framework addressing parameter efficiency, reasoning reliability, and domain adaptation. Experimental results highlight its state-of-the-art performance across benchmarks, demonstrating the value of integrating modular fine-tuning with advanced reasoning and retrieval-augmented workflows. MUREA sets a foundation for future advancements in multi-task frameworks, offering both scalability and robustness.
