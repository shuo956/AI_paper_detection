# Title  
**Integrating Shape-Awareness and Adaptive Frameworks for Multi-Objective Time Series Modeling**

---

# Abstract  
Time series modeling has witnessed transformative advancements through foundation models like UniShape and adaptive architectures such as MQ-GNN. However, a critical gap remains in developing a unified framework that incorporates shape-awareness, domain heterogeneity, and task adaptability across multi-objective settings. Addressing this gap, we propose **Shape-Adaptive Multi-Objective Modeling Network (SAMON)**, a hybrid framework that leverages shape-aware representation, adaptive multi-queue pipelines, and task-specific optimization techniques. By integrating shapelet-based classification with efficient graph-based processing, SAMON achieves high performance across diverse time series benchmarks, including classification, regression, and anomaly detection. Empirical results demonstrate that SAMON outperforms state-of-the-art models in accuracy, scalability, and computational efficiency. We further validate the proposed framework's robustness through ablation studies and case-specific evaluations, showcasing significant advancements in interpretability and domain adaptability for multi-objective time series modeling.

---

# Introduction  
The proliferation of time series data across domains such as healthcare, finance, and climate science demands robust and adaptive modeling frameworks. Traditional methods, including shapelet-based classification models like UniShape [Liu et al., 2026], focus primarily on classification challenges, often neglecting multi-objective complexities such as regression or anomaly detection. Meanwhile, scalable architectures like MQ-GNN [Ullah et al., 2026] emphasize computational efficiency but lack interpretability critical for domain-specific applications.

To bridge these gaps, we propose SAMON, a novel framework designed to integrate shape-aware temporal representations with adaptive multi-objective pipelines. SAMON introduces a unified architecture that combines shapelet extraction, multi-queue training, and task-specific optimization strategies. Our contributions include:

1. **Shape-Aware Temporal Representation:** Building on UniShape's multiscale discriminative subsequences, SAMON enhances interpretability through adaptive shapelet selection.
2. **Multi-Queue Adaptive Training:** Leveraging MQ-GNN’s pipelined architecture, SAMON optimizes resource utilization for scalability across large datasets.
3. **Multi-Objective Refinement Layer:** Incorporates domain-specific loss functions for classification, regression, and anomaly detection tasks.

Through extensive experimentation, we demonstrate SAMON's superiority in accuracy, scalability, and domain adaptability.

# Related Work  
The foundation of SAMON is informed by several key advancements:

1. **Shapelet-Based Models:** UniShape [Liu et al., 2026] introduced a shape-aware adapter for time series classification. While effective for capturing class-discriminative features, its application is limited to classification tasks, leaving regression and anomaly detection unexplored.

2. **Scalable Architectures:** MQ-GNN [Ullah et al., 2026] enables efficient Graph Neural Network training through multi-queue pipelining. Despite its efficiency, it is not inherently designed for shape-aware time series analysis or multi-objective tasks.

3. **Multi-Objective Optimization:** Platforms like DataScribe [Singh et al., 2026] and CalPro [Shihab et al., 2026] focus on optimizing multi-objective workflows. However, they lack integration with foundational modeling techniques, such as shapelet-based representations.

4. **Task-Specific Enhancements:** Other relevant works, such as BEAT-Net [Ma et al., 2026], integrate domain-specific priors for improved interpretability. SAMON builds on these ideas to unify shape-awareness with adaptive, scalable architectures.

# Methods  
SAMON is structured into three core modules:

### 1. **Shapelet Extraction and Representation**  
Inspired by UniShape, SAMON employs a shape-aware adapter to extract multiscale discriminative subsequences. These shapelets are aggregated into class and task tokens, which serve as inputs for subsequent processing.

### 2. **Multi-Queue Adaptive Training**  
The MQ-GNN pipelined architecture is adapted for time series data by introducing asynchronous gradient sharing and global neighbor sampling. This ensures efficient utilization of computational resources, particularly for large, multi-objective datasets.

### 3. **Task-Specific Refinement Layer**  
A multi-objective refinement layer is added to optimize domain-specific loss functions. For classification, cross-entropy loss is used; for regression, mean squared error (MSE) is employed; and for anomaly detection, a novel uncertainty-aware loss function is introduced.

| Multi-Queue Training      | Optimizes resource utilization                | Asynchronous gradient sharing         |
| Task Refinement Layer     | Tailors loss for specific objectives          | Supports classification, regression   |
