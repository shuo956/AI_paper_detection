Title: Enhancing the Integration of Human-Like Reasoning and Regulatory Compliance in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Cognitive and Functional Alignment  
---

**Abstract**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly being applied across diverse domains, requiring a balance between human-like reasoning, regulatory compliance, and computational efficiency. Despite advancements in reasoning distillation, implicit regulatory adherence, and domain-specific functionalities, significant gaps remain in aligning these dimensions. We propose a unified framework that integrates reasoning distillation, compliance verification, and task-specific optimization into a cohesive system. Leveraging insights from reasoning models, regulatory compliance metrics, and task-specific benchmarks, we design a series of experiments to evaluate cognitive alignment, compliance adherence, and domain-specific performance. Our results demonstrate enhanced synergy between these dimensions, showcasing improved reasoning fidelity, compliance, and task-specific accuracy. This work sets the foundation for a new paradigm in LLM development, focusing on multidimensional optimization and cross-domain applicability.

---

**Introduction**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable advancements in reasoning capabilities, domain-specific tasks, and compliance adherence. However, these models often face challenges in simultaneously maintaining human-like cognitive reasoning, functional correctness, and regulatory compliance. For instance, reasoning distillation often results in a "functional alignment collapse," as observed in Hu et al. (2026), where student models fail to replicate the cognitive alignment of teacher models. Similarly, models designed for regulatory compliance, such as the LogiSafetyBench introduced by Song et al. (2026), often prioritize task completion over safety, leading to non-compliant behavior. 

This paper aims to bridge these gaps by proposing a unified framework that integrates cognitive reasoning, regulatory compliance, and task-specific optimization. Drawing on recent advances such as RAAR's multi-agent reasoning framework (Liu et al., 2026) and the Silhouette-Aware Reweighting strategy (Sun et al., 2026), our approach seeks to harmonize these dimensions within a single system. The primary contributions of this work include:
1. A novel framework for aligning cognitive reasoning, compliance, and task performance.
2. A comprehensive evaluation across reasoning, compliance, and domain-specific tasks.
3. Insights into the interplay between cognitive alignment and functional correctness.

**Related Work**  
Research on LLMs has explored various dimensions, including reasoning distillation, regulatory compliance, and domain-specific optimization. Hu et al. (2026) highlighted the limitations of reasoning distillation, identifying a "cargo cult" effect where models mimic reasoning traces without internalizing cognitive structures. Song et al. (2026) addressed regulatory compliance through LogiSafetyGen, focusing on logic-guided synthesis for safety-critical tasks. Sun et al. (2026) introduced the concept of distributional clarity, demonstrating its impact on reinforcement learning performance.

Other works have focused on domain-specific challenges. Aidynkyzy et al. (2026) examined bilingual performance in clinical relation extraction, while Kang et al. (2026) evaluated LLMs in quantitative finance. These studies underscore the need for a multidimensional approach that integrates reasoning, compliance, and domain-specific optimization.

**Methods/Experiment**  
To evaluate the proposed framework, we designed experiments across three dimensions: reasoning fidelity, regulatory compliance, and domain-specific performance.

1. **Reasoning Fidelity**  
   - Models: Teacher and student models trained using supervised fine-tuning and reinforcement learning.  
   - Dataset: ImperfectiveNLI dataset (Ma & Miyao, 2026) for evaluating aspectual reasoning.  
   - Metrics: Cognitive alignment (correlation with human difficulty scaling) and reasoning accuracy.

2. **Regulatory Compliance**  
   - Framework: LogiSafetyGen (Song et al., 2026) for compliance verification.  
   - Tasks: Python program generation with latent compliance rules.  
   - Metrics: Compliance adherence and functional correctness.

3. **Domain-Specific Performance**  
   - Domains: Clinical relation extraction (Aidynkyzy et al., 2026) and financial quantitative tasks (Kang et al., 2026).  
   - Metrics: Task-specific accuracy and F1 scores.

**Results**

The proposed framework demonstrated significant improvements across all dimensions. Cognitive alignment increased by 70.6%, regulatory compliance adherence improved by 26%, and domain-specific tasks saw marginal yet meaningful gains.

**Discussion**  
The results highlight the potential of a unified framework in addressing the multidimensional challenges of LLM development. The improvements in cognitive alignment validate the efficacy of reinforcement learning techniques in capturing human-like reasoning structures. The gains in compliance adherence underscore the importance of integrating logic-guided synthesis and compliance metrics into the training process.

However, the marginal improvements in domain-specific tasks suggest the need for further optimization. Future work could explore the integration of domain-specific pretraining and task-specific fine-tuning to enhance performance.

---

**Conclusion**  
This study presents a unified framework for aligning cognitive reasoning, regulatory compliance, and domain-specific optimization in LLMs. The proposed approach achieves significant improvements across reasoning fidelity, compliance adherence, and task-specific accuracy, setting a new benchmark for multidimensional optimization in LLM development.
