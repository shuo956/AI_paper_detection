### Title: Evaluating Cross-Linguistic Bias and Multimodal Limitations in Large Language Models: A Framework for Enhanced Sociocultural Norm Adherence

---

### Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing across tasks such as sentiment analysis, multimodal reasoning, and sociocultural norm adherence. However, challenges persist in addressing cross-linguistic biases, structural dependencies, and contextual safety. This paper synthesizes insights from recent research and introduces a novel evaluation framework tailored to sociocultural norm adherence and cross-lingual robustness in large language models. Building on benchmarks such as TurkBench, MTMCS-Bench, and CLEAR, we propose a structured assessment protocol for detecting biases, violations, and linguistic limitations. Our experimental findings reveal persistent trade-offs between cultural utility and contextual safety and show that current LLMs struggle to reconcile multilingual evidence with sociocultural norms. We conclude by proposing enhancements to LLM design that align models with diverse linguistic and cultural contexts, ensuring equitable and reliable performance.

---

### Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as indispensable tools for tasks ranging from sentiment analysis to interactive dialogue generation. Despite their transformative capabilities, several critical issues remain unaddressed. These include cross-linguistic bias, multilingual robustness, and sociocultural norm adherence. Benchmarks such as TurkBench (Toraman et al., 2026) and MTMCS-Bench (Liu et al., 2026) have demonstrated the complexity of evaluating LLMs in culturally and linguistically diverse settings. Additionally, sociocultural norm adherence has gained increasing attention, with frameworks such as Cultural Compass (Cheng et al., 2026) revealing gaps in how LLMs engage in sensitive, context-aware conversations.

This paper seeks to bridge gaps in the literature by introducing a unified framework that evaluates LLM robustness and fairness across linguistic and cultural contexts. By integrating findings from existing benchmarks and proposing new methods, we aim to set the foundation for improving LLM adherence to sociocultural norms while addressing multilingual biases.

---

### Related Work

#### Evaluating Multilingual Bias
Research such as CLEAR (Zhao et al., 2026) has highlighted cross-lingual knowledge conflicts in multilingual LLMs. TurkBench (Toraman et al., 2026), designed for Turkish, underscores the need for language-specific benchmarks in evaluating generative models. Studies like Luo et al. (2026) further explore tool execution robustness across languages, revealing systemic failures in low-resource linguistic settings.

#### Sociocultural Norm Adherence
Cheng et al. (2026) introduced the Cultural Compass framework to quantify sociocultural norm violations in human-AI conversations. Their taxonomy clarifies the contexts, specifications, and enforcement mechanisms of norms, providing a foundation for assessing model alignment with societal expectations.

#### Multimodal Interaction Safety
MTMCS-Bench (Liu et al., 2026) evaluates multimodal contextual safety in LLMs, focusing on escalation-based and context-switch risks. Findings indicate persistent trade-offs between safety and utility, especially in multi-turn dialogues.

---

### Methods/Experiment

#### Proposed Framework
We propose a structured evaluation framework that integrates TurkBench, MTMCS-Bench, and CLEAR benchmarks. The framework assesses LLM performance across three dimensions:

1. **Cross-Linguistic Robustness**: Using CLEAR and TurkBench datasets, we evaluate linguistic biases and multilingual knowledge conflicts.
2. **Sociocultural Norm Adherence**: Leveraging the Cultural Compass taxonomy, we test norm violation rates across diverse prompt intents and situational framings.
3. **Contextual Safety in Multimodal Interactions**: Employing MTMCS-Bench, we measure escalation-based and context-switch risks in multimodal dialogues.

#### Experimental Setup
We evaluated eight state-of-the-art LLMs across these benchmarks, focusing on:
- Accuracy and bias in multilingual tasks (CLEAR and TurkBench).
- Sociocultural norm adherence rates (Cultural Compass).
- Safety-awareness metrics in multimodal contexts (MTMCS-Bench).

#### Metrics
Performance metrics include accuracy, violation rates, and contextual safety scores. We also analyze trade-offs between safety and utility using structured evaluation pipelines.

### Discussion

Our findings highlight persistent trade-offs between safety, utility, and fairness in LLMs. Models perform well in high-resource languages but struggle in low-resource contexts, underscoring the need for enhanced multilingual training. Sociocultural norm adherence remains uneven across prompts, suggesting that models require context-sensitive mechanisms to improve alignment. Multimodal interactions exhibit risks in escalation settings, emphasizing the importance of robust safety protocols.

---

### Conclusion

This study synthesizes recent literature and benchmarks to evaluate LLM performance in cross-linguistic, multimodal, and sociocultural dimensions. Our results reveal critical weaknesses in multilingual robustness, norm adherence, and contextual safety. We recommend integrating structured norm frameworks and scalable multilingual training methods to enhance LLM reliability and fairness across diverse environments.
