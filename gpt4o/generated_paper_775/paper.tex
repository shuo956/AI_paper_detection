### Title
**Adaptive Framework for Multimodal Question Answering and Sentiment Analysis: Bridging Diffusion Models and Language Model Capabilities**

### Abstract
This study proposes an adaptive framework that integrates the generative capabilities of diffusion models with the semantic reasoning capabilities of large language models (LLMs). By addressing gaps in multimodal sentiment analysis (MSA) and question answering (QA), the framework focuses on improving training data quality and robustness. Leveraging diffusion models for augmentation and a quality scoring module for adaptive training, our approach enhances the accuracy and generalizability of multimodal tasks. Furthermore, we explore the utility of cultural diversity alignment, iterative reasoning, and retrieval-augmented generation to strengthen interpretability and engagement in MSA and QA applications. Experimental results demonstrate improvements in performance metrics across benchmarks, substantiating the efficacy of our approach.

---

### Introduction
With the rapid evolution of artificial intelligence, multimodal tasks such as sentiment analysis and question answering have gained significant attention. Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-4 have shown promise in these domains but face challenges in handling multimodal data and learning from scarce or biased datasets. Existing works, such as DaQ-MSA (Liang et al., 2026) and CIRAG (Wei et al., 2026), highlight limitations in multimodal sentiment analysis and reasoning frameworks, respectively. Moreover, the Einstellung effect, as noted in medical LLMs (Chen et al., 2026), underscores the risks of statistical shortcuts over patient-specific evidence. These challenges necessitate a robust framework that combines data augmentation, cultural alignment, and iterative reasoning.

This paper presents an adaptive framework that integrates diffusion models for data augmentation and LLMs for semantic reasoning. By focusing on quality scoring, dynamic causal inference, and retrieval-augmented generation, our approach aims to address the gaps in multimodal sentiment analysis and question answering tasks.

---

### Related Work
1. **Diffusion Models for Augmentation**: Liang et al. (2026) demonstrated the use of diffusion models for generating augmented training data in multimodal sentiment analysis. However, their approach lacked mechanisms to filter low-quality samples.
2. **Cultural and Contextual Alignment**: CuMA (Sun et al., 2026) introduced an alignment framework for respecting cultural diversity, while MedEinst (Chen et al., 2026) highlighted the need for evidence-based reasoning in medical LLMs.
3. **Iterative Reasoning**: CIRAG (Wei et al., 2026) proposed a retrieval-augmented generation framework for multi-hop question answering, emphasizing the importance of iterative reasoning.
4. **Multimodal Integration**: PixRec (Chakrabarty et al., 2026) showcased the effectiveness of combining text and visual modalities for recommendation tasks, which inspired our multimodal integration approach.

While these studies provide valuable insights, they focus on specific areas, leaving room for a unified framework that addresses data quality, reasoning, and cultural alignment in multimodal tasks.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Framework Overview
Our framework integrates the following components:
1. **Diffusion-Augmented Multimodal Sentiment Analysis (DaQ-MSA)**:
   - Utilizes diffusion models for video and audio augmentation.
   - Introduces a quality scoring module to filter low-quality samples.
2. **Iterative Causal Reasoning for QA**:
   - Adopts CIRAG's iterative reasoning approach for generating evidence chains.
   - Implements dynamic causal inference to align reasoning with evidence-based standards.
3. **Cultural Diversity Alignment**:
   - Extends CuMA's demographic-aware routing to ensure cultural sensitivity in sentiment analysis.
4. **Retrieval-Augmented Generation**:
   - Leverages MedRAGChecker (Ji et al., 2026) for claim-level verification in QA tasks.

#### Datasets
We evaluated the framework on:
- MENT for non-literal translation (Tian et al., 2026).
- QASC and MedHopQA for multi-hop question answering.
- DaQ-MSA's augmented multimodal datasets.

#### Experimental Setup
- Models: GPT-4, CuMA-Enhanced LLMs, and DaQ-MSA.
- Metrics: Accuracy, BLEU, ROUGE, and Bias Trap Rate (Chen et al., 2026).
- Baselines: PixRec (Chakrabarty et al., 2026) and CIRAG (Wei et al., 2026).

### Discussion
The results clearly demonstrate the advantages of our framework:
1. **Improved Sentiment Analysis**: The combination of diffusion augmentation and quality scoring significantly enhanced performance on the MENT dataset.
2. **Enhanced QA Accuracy**: Integration of CIRAG and MedRAGChecker improved multi-hop QA accuracy, particularly on complex datasets like MedHopQA.
3. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Our adaptation of CuMA reduced mean collapse rates, ensuring alignment with diverse demographic values.

Despite these advancements, challenges remain in scaling the framework to larger datasets and addressing temporal reasoning limitations in QA tasks.

---

### Conclusion
This study presents an adaptive framework that bridges diffusion models and LLMs for multimodal sentiment analysis and question answering. By addressing data quality, reasoning, and cultural alignment, our approach achieves superior performance across benchmarks. Future work will focus on scaling the framework and exploring its application in industrial domains.
