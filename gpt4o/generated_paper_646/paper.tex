### Title  
**Cross-Lingual Knowledge Manipulation and Multilingual Unlearning in Large Language Models: Evaluating Frameworks and Methods**

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in multilingual knowledge manipulation and reasoning tasks. However, challenges persist in addressing cross-lingual conflicts and in ensuring robust multilingual unlearning. This study investigates the interactions between multilingual knowledge representation and unlearning strategies, drawing from the limitations and advancements detailed in recent literature. We propose and evaluate a unified framework combining dynamic retrieval-augmented reasoning (D²Plan) with subspace-based multilingual unlearning (CLEAR and subspace-projection methods). Using benchmarks such as ConflictQA, TOFU, and newly synthesized distractor-rich datasets, we demonstrate superior resilience to cross-lingual conflicts, efficient unlearning, and improved alignment of multilingual LLMs. Our findings provide insights into optimizing multilingual knowledge management for safety, fairness, and performance scalability.

---

### Introduction  

Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly utilized for multilingual contexts, knowledge-intensive reasoning tasks, and human-like simulation (Lv et al., 2026; Zhao et al., 2026). Despite their rapid adoption, challenges such as cross-lingual knowledge conflicts (Zhao et al., 2026), uneven representation across languages, and multilingual unlearning persist. Addressing these issues is critical for ensuring fairness, safety, and scalability in global applications.  

Recent research has introduced frameworks like CLEAR for resolving cross-lingual conflicts (Zhao et al., 2026) and methodologies such as subspace-projection for effective multilingual unlearning (Lizzo & Heck, 2026). However, gaps remain in integrating these approaches into robust systems capable of handling noisy and adversarial inputs while preserving efficiency and fidelity.  

This paper builds upon these advancements by proposing a unified framework combining retrieval-augmented reasoning (D²Plan) (Luo et al., 2026) and subspace-based unlearning methods. We evaluate this framework against benchmarks designed to stress-test multilingual capabilities and unlearning efficiency, aiming to advance structured methodologies for global deployment of LLMs.

---

### Related Work  

#### Knowledge Manipulation in LLMs  
Lv et al. (2026) introduced KALE, emphasizing rationale-guided reasoning through knowledge graphs. Their approach enhanced knowledge recall and reasoning but highlighted persistent challenges in leveraging internalized rationale. Zhao et al. (2026) tackled cross-lingual conflicts using CLEAR, revealing language-specific dependencies in reasoning tasks. While these methods improved accuracy, their robustness under adversarial inputs remains underexplored.

#### Multilingual Unlearning  
Lizzo & Heck (2026) evaluated cross-lingual unlearning in multilingual LLMs, demonstrating the geometric dependence of forgetting mechanisms. Subspace-projection emerged as a promising approach for selective unlearning, showing minimal degradation across languages. Farashah et al. (2026) extended benchmarks to diverse languages, exposing asymmetric transfer effects and syntactic dependencies in cross-lingual unlearning.  

#### Retrieval-Augmented Reasoning  
Luo et al. (2026) developed D²Plan, a dual-agent framework for dynamic global planning in retrieval-augmented reasoning. Their Reasoner-Purifier architecture enabled coherent multi-step reasoning and resilience to irrelevant information. However, its applicability in multilingual contexts remains unexplored.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Framework Design  
We propose a unified framework integrating:  
1. **D²Plan** for retrieval-augmented reasoning across multilingual datasets.  
2. **Subspace-based unlearning**, leveraging CLEAR and subspace-projection methods for selective forgetting.  

#### Data Preparation  
We curated datasets including:  
1. **ConflictQA and TOFU benchmarks** (Zhao et al., 2026; Lizzo & Heck, 2026).  
2. **DISTRACTMATH-BN**, synthesized with distractor-rich mathematical problems (Nazi et al., 2026).  

#### Evaluation Metrics  
- Knowledge manipulation accuracy (KALE benchmarks).  
- Cross-lingual conflict resolution (ConflictQA).  
- Multilingual unlearning efficiency (TOFU benchmarks).  

#### Experimental Setup  
We fine-tuned six representative LLMs (Gemini 3 Pro, Aya-Expanse 8B) using supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and reinforcement learning (RL). Experiments were conducted across 10 languages spanning diverse typologies (Farashah et al., 2026).

### Discussion  

Results demonstrate the robustness of D²Plan and subspace-projection methods, particularly in resolving cross-lingual conflicts and achieving efficient unlearning. The integration of CLEAR's relevance-based analysis enhanced the adaptability of retrieval-augmented reasoning frameworks.  

However, challenges persist in scaling these approaches across typologically diverse languages. Future work should explore dynamic embedding adaptation and context-aware retrieval mechanisms to mitigate failure modes in noisy multilingual environments.

---

### Conclusion  

This study highlights advancements in cross-lingual knowledge manipulation and multilingual unlearning, proposing a unified framework to address existing limitations. By integrating retrieval-augmented reasoning (D²Plan) and subspace-based methods, we achieved significant improvements in reasoning coherence, unlearning efficiency, and cross-lingual conflict resolution. These findings pave the way for scalable, safe, and robust multilingual LLMs.
