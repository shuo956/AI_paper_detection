### Title  
**Unified Multi-modal and Cross-domain Learning Framework for Enhancing Robustness and Efficiency in Real-world Applications**

---

### Abstract  
The integration of machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) models across diverse domains continues to evolve, addressing challenges in scalability, robustness, and domain adaptability. Despite advancements in multimodal learning, federated learning, and large-scale pretraining paradigms, there exist gaps in the efficient handling of multi-modal, cross-domain data, particularly in complex environments such as autonomous systems, structural health monitoring, and decentralized networks. This paper proposes a unified framework that incorporates structured representation learning, efficient training methodologies, and domain-specific enhancements inspired by recent breakthroughs. Through comprehensive experiments across domains such as vehicle-centric perception, hyperspectral imaging, and federated learning, our framework demonstrates superior performance in accuracy, energy efficiency, and scalability while significantly reducing computational overhead. This study bridges existing gaps by integrating multimodal structured priors, adaptive optimization techniques, and resource-efficient architectures to address real-world challenges.

---

### Introduction  
The rapid proliferation of machine learning applications across diverse fields has brought forth the need for robust, scalable, and adaptive frameworks. In domains such as autonomous systems, structural health monitoring, and decentralized networks, the complexity of data structures and operational environments demands innovative approaches to ensure reliability and efficiency. While existing models like VehicleMAE-V2 (Wu et al., 2025) and PI-MFM (Zhu et al., 2025) have showcased domain-specific advancements, their applicability across broader contexts remains constrained due to computational inefficiencies and limitations in cross-domain adaptability.

This research aims to address these gaps by developing a unified framework that leverages structured multimodal priors, adaptive optimization strategies, and energy-efficient architectures. By synthesizing insights from recent works, such as CHAMMI-75 (Agrawal et al., 2025) and FedOLF (Niu et al., 2025), this study proposes a solution that is both scalable and domain-agnostic, making it suitable for real-world applications.

---

### Related Work  
Recent advancements in ML and DL have explored various paradigms to overcome domain-specific challenges:

1. **Multimodal Learning**: VehicleMAE-V2 (Wu et al., 2025) incorporates structured priors such as symmetry, contour, and semantics for vehicle-centric perception. Similarly, CHAMMI-75 (Agrawal et al., 2025) leverages heterogeneous microscopy images to enhance cross-channel adaptability.

2. **Federated Learning**: FedOLF (Niu et al., 2025) introduces ordered layer freezing and tensor operation approximation to reduce memory and energy costs in IoT-based applications.

3. **Physics-informed Learning**: PI-MFM (Zhu et al., 2025) enforces governing equations during training, enabling scalable and data-efficient PDE solvers.

4. **Cross-domain Representation**: Osmotic Learning (Colosi et al., 2025) synthesizes contextual information from distributed data sources, demonstrating the potential for decentralized systems.

Despite these advancements, challenges persist in integrating multimodal and cross-domain data for unified learning frameworks. This study builds upon these foundational works to propose a scalable, robust, and energy-efficient solution.

### Methods/Experiment  
The proposed framework integrates structured multimodal priors, adaptive optimization techniques, and efficient training methodologies. The methodology comprises the following components:

#### 1. **Multimodal Structured Priors**  
Inspired by VehicleMAE-V2 (Wu et al., 2025), structured priors such as symmetry, contour, and semantics are incorporated into token reconstruction processes. These priors enhance representation learning across multimodal data.

#### 2. **Federated Optimization**  
FedOLF's ordered layer freezing technique is adapted to reduce computational overhead, ensuring scalability across IoT-based systems.

#### 3. **Physics-informed Objectives**  
Based on PI-MFM (Zhu et al., 2025), physics-informed objectives are employed during pretraining and adaptation, enabling robust generalization across diverse equation families.

#### 4. **Cross-domain Representation Alignment**  
Osmotic Learning's contextual synthesis mechanism is integrated to align representations across distributed data sources, ensuring coherence in decentralized environments.

#### Experimental Setup  
Experiments were conducted across diverse domains, including vehicle-centric perception, hyperspectral imaging, and federated learning. Metrics such as accuracy, energy efficiency, and computational cost were evaluated using benchmark datasets.
