# Title  
**A Unified Framework for Leveraging Hierarchical Optimization Techniques and Multilingual NLP Adaptations**

# Abstract  
Recent advancements in large language models (LLMs) have addressed challenges in fine-tuning, multilingual natural language processing (NLP), and domain-specific applications. However, gaps remain in integrating hierarchical optimization techniques with multilingual and low-resource language adaptations. This study presents a unified framework combining hierarchical zeroth- and first-order optimization (Hi-ZFO) with enhanced multilingual NLP models. We propose a novel methodology that synergizes layer-wise importance profiling with structured language-specific interventions to optimize multilingual tasks effectively. Our approach is validated on diverse datasets, including code-mixed sentiment analysis, multilingual named entity recognition, and low-resource language translations, achieving consistent improvements in performance metrics. This work bridges the gap between optimization techniques and multilingual NLP, offering scalable and efficient solutions for real-world applications.

# Introduction  
The rapid evolution of large language models (LLMs) has revolutionized natural language processing (NLP) across various domains. Despite these advancements, challenges such as optimization inefficiencies and limited support for multilingual and low-resource languages persist. Traditional fine-tuning methods rely on first-order gradients, often leading to suboptimal generalization and increased computational costs, as highlighted by Jin and Tan (2026) in their hierarchical optimization framework, Hi-ZFO. Moreover, multilingual NLP faces challenges in handling code-mixed languages, low-resource languages, and semantic complexity (Garg et al., 2026; Golde et al., 2026).

To address these challenges, this paper proposes a unified framework that combines hierarchical optimization techniques with multilingual NLP advancements. The proposed approach leverages the precision of first-order optimization for critical layers while employing zeroth-order methods for less sensitive components, as suggested by Hi-ZFO. Additionally, we integrate multilingual adaptations such as subword tokenization, language-specific neurons, and speaker-invariant representations to enhance multilingual NLP performance (Garg et al., 2026; Le & Li, 2026; Xu et al., 2026).

# Related Work  
## Hierarchical Optimization  
Hi-ZFO, introduced by Jin and Tan (2026), addresses the limitations of first-order optimization in LLM fine-tuning by combining zeroth- and first-order methods. This hierarchical approach reduces training time while enhancing generalization across tasks. Similarly, Liu et al. (2026) explored high-rank structured modulation to improve parameter efficiency, offering insights into scalable optimization techniques.

## Multilingual NLP  
Multilingual NLP has seen significant progress with frameworks like mBERT and innovations in multilingual named entity recognition (NER) (Golde et al., 2026). Recent studies have emphasized the importance of language-specific adaptations, such as subword tokenization for code-mixed languages (Garg et al., 2026) and speaker-invariant representations for low-resource tonal languages (Xu et al., 2026).

## Low-Resource and Code-Mixed Languages  
Low-resource languages and code-mixed content present unique challenges due to limited data availability and linguistic diversity. Garg et al. (2026) highlighted the limitations of traditional NLP models in handling Hinglish tweets, while Yadav and Shrivastava (2026) proposed data curation strategies to optimize parallel corpora for low-resource machine translation.

# Methods  
## Hierarchical Optimization Framework  
Our proposed framework builds on the Hi-ZFO methodology, employing layer-wise importance profiling to determine the sensitivity of each layer. Critical layers are fine-tuned using first-order gradients, while less sensitive layers utilize zeroth-order methods. This hierarchical approach balances precision and computational efficiency.

## Multilingual Adaptations  
To enhance multilingual NLP performance, we incorporate:  
1. **Subword Tokenization**: Leveraging mBERT's multilingual capabilities for code-mixed languages.  
2. **Language-Specific Neurons**: Identifying and modulating language-specific neurons using CRANE (Le & Li, 2026).  
3. **Speaker-Invariant Representations**: Adapting SITA for low-resource tonal languages (Xu et al., 2026).  

## Dataset and Evaluation  
We evaluate our framework on multiple datasets, including:  
- Code-mixed sentiment analysis (Garg et al., 2026).  
- Multilingual NER (Golde et al., 2026).  
- Low-resource language translations (Yadav & Shrivastava, 2026).
