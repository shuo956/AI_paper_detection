### Title
**A Unified Framework for Multi-Domain Learning and Optimization Using Constraint-Aware and Physics-Informed Methods**

---

### Abstract
Recent advances in machine learning, optimization, and computational frameworks have addressed key challenges in high-dimensional modeling, domain generalization, and resource-efficient learning across diverse scientific domains. However, gaps persist in integrating physics-informed constraints and interpretable learning mechanisms to enhance generalization, robustness, and computational efficiency. In this study, we propose a unified framework combining constrained density estimation, domain generalization, and physics-informed modeling for addressing challenges in high-dimensional data, such as spatio-temporal dynamics, signal disentanglement, and optimization under physical constraints. Our proposed architecture synergistically integrates methods like optimal transport, recurrent neural networks, and decomposition-aware autoencoders. Experimental validation on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates improved accuracy, computational efficiency, and robustness compared to state-of-the-art baselines. These results highlight the potential of interdisciplinary techniques in advancing scientific and engineering applications.

### Introduction
Machine learning (ML) and optimization frameworks are increasingly employed in solving high-dimensional and multi-domain problems, from wireless communication to biomedical signal processing and computational physics. However, traditional approaches often fail to integrate domain-specific constraints, leading to inefficiencies in generalization, robustness, and interpretability. Recent advancements, such as constrained density estimation via optimal transport [1], domain generalization for recurrent neural networks [2], and decomposition-aware representations [4], highlight the potential of integrating physical constraints and optimization principles into ML workflows.

This paper aims to unify these disparate advancements into a cohesive framework. We propose a hybrid approach that leverages constrained optimization, domain-informed neural architectures, and physics-aware modeling to address challenges in multi-domain scenarios. By applying this framework to tasks such as signal disentanglement, spatio-temporal dynamics, and high-dimensional optimization, we demonstrate its potential to bridge gaps in generalization and efficiency.

### Related Work
#### Constrained Optimization
Constrained density estimation via optimal transport [1] and Wasserstein-Fisher-Rao metrics [7] have demonstrated success in modeling unbalanced dynamics with physical constraints. These methods provide robust frameworks for density estimation under expectation constraints.

#### Domain Generalization and Representation Learning
Generalization in recurrent neural networks (RNNs) under domain shifts has been explored through Koopman operator theory [2], while variational decomposition autoencoding (VDA) [4] has improved disentanglement in highly dynamic signals. These techniques are foundational in understanding temporal and spectral patterns.

#### Physics-Informed Methods
Physics-informed digital twins [5] and tree search algorithms [21] have been applied to parameter estimation and high-dimensional optimization, leveraging domain-specific physical laws. Such methods demonstrate the importance of integrating physics into ML workflows.

#### Computational Efficiency
Efforts to reduce computational overhead include one-shot federated learning [8] and crossbar-based deep learning accelerators [16]. These studies emphasize the necessity of efficient architectures for large-scale applications.

### Methods/Experiment
We propose a unified framework combining three key components:
- **Constraint-Aware Density Estimation**: Based on [1,7], we utilize Wasserstein distance to model constrained density functions, ensuring robustness under expectation constraints.
- **Physics-Informed Neural Networks**: Inspired by [5,21], we incorporate physics-informed loss functions to guide training and improve interpretability.
- **Decomposition-Aware Representations**: Building on [4], we employ variational decomposition autoencoders to disentangle temporal and spectral components in high-dimensional datasets.

#### Experimental Setup
Simulations were conducted across three domains:
1. **Spatio-Temporal Data**: A synthetic dataset modeled using constrained density estimation.
2. **Biomedical Signals**: Real-world datasets for speech classification and autism spectrum disorder detection [4,19].
3. **High-Dimensional Optimization**: Benchmark functions and constrained design problems from [21].
