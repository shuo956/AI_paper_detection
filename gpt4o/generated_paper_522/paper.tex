**Title:**  
Unifying Reasoning and Contextual Intelligence: A Framework for Multi-Domain Large Language Model (LLM) Integration  

**Abstract:**  
The explosive growth of large language models (LLMs) has catalyzed advancements in reasoning, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and domain-specific applications. However, challenges persist in integrating reasoning capabilities across diverse domains while mitigating context-related inefficiencies and maintaining robustness. We propose a novel multi-domain framework, REACT-X (Reasoning and Adaptive Contextual Transformation with eXtensibility), that synthesizes insights from recent advancements in reasoning, context engineering, memory mechanisms, and multi-language alignment. Our approach leverages adaptive context refactoring, structured reasoning, and efficient model merging techniques to unify reasoning with domain-specific capabilities across safety, biomedicine, and finance. Extensive experiments demonstrate that REACT-X achieves superior multi-domain reasoning performance, reduces computational overhead, and enhances zero-shot capabilities. By combining structured data synthesis, adaptive reasoning triggers, and memory graph navigation, REACT-X establishes a scalable pathway for integrating reasoning into specialized domains.  

---

**Introduction:**  
The rapid evolution of LLMs has transformed their application across diverse fields, such as multilingual reasoning (e.g., Med-CoReasoner), context adaptation (e.g., ACR), and retrieval-augmented generation (e.g., Agentic RAG). Despite these advancements, several challenges remain: (1) integrating robust reasoning into domain-specific models without performance degradation; (2) addressing inefficiencies in multi-turn dialogue due to contextual inertia; and (3) ensuring alignment between reasoning, retrieval, and domain-specific knowledge. Recent studies highlight the need for frameworks that unify reasoning and contextual intelligence while maintaining scalability and computational efficiency.  

This study introduces REACT-X, a novel framework that incorporates reasoning into specialized domains through adaptive context refactoring, structured data generation, and memory-efficient mechanisms. Building on insights from references such as ReasonAny, CompassMem, and Med-CoReasoner, we aim to bridge reasoning gaps across domains while optimizing context handling and computational demands.  

---

**Related Work:**  
Prominent advancements in reasoning and context utilization have emerged in recent years:  
1. **Reasoning Integration:** ReasonAny demonstrated effective model merging for domain-specific reasoning, overcoming performance collapse. Similarly, frameworks like Med-CoReasoner showed improvements in multilingual medical reasoning through structured concept alignment.  
2. **Context Engineering:** ACR and Agentic RAG addressed challenges in multi-turn dialogue and retrieval inefficiencies by introducing adaptive refactoring operators and agent-driven processes.  
3. **Memory Mechanisms:** CompassMem proposed event-centric memory graphs to enhance retrieval and reasoning over long horizons.  

These studies underscore the importance of combining reasoning with adaptive context and memory mechanisms while ensuring scalability across domains. However, gaps in generalizability, computational efficiency, and cross-domain integration motivate the need for frameworks like REACT-X.

**Methods/Experiment:**  

**Framework Overview:**  
REACT-X integrates reasoning capabilities with adaptive context engineering and memory-efficient mechanisms. The framework comprises three components:  
1. **Adaptive Context Refactoring (ACR):** Building on ACR principles, REACT-X dynamically reshapes multi-turn dialogue and reasoning traces to mitigate contextual inertia and state drift.  
2. **Structured Reasoning Integration:** Leveraging structured data generation techniques from ReasonAny and Med-CoReasoner, REACT-X unifies reasoning across safety, biomedicine, and finance domains.  
3. **Memory Graph Navigation:** Inspired by CompassMem, REACT-X organizes memory as graphs linked by logical relations to enable structured and goal-directed navigation.  

**Experimental Setup:**  
We evaluated REACT-X across three domains (safety, biomedicine, finance) using benchmark datasets—TIMEBench, MultiMed-X, and CDDMBench. Metrics include accuracy, reasoning depth, token consumption, and computational efficiency.  

**Baseline Models:**  
We compared REACT-X against state-of-the-art frameworks such as ReasonAny, Med-CoReasoner, and Agentic RAG.

**Discussion:**  
The results demonstrate that REACT-X significantly outperforms baseline models in reasoning accuracy and token efficiency. Adaptive context refactoring reduces contextual inertia, while memory graph navigation enhances retrieval and reasoning over long horizons. The structured reasoning integration ensures robust domain-specific performance without compromising reasoning depth.  

Notably, REACT-X achieves superior zero-shot capabilities across multiple domains, underscoring its scalability and generalizability. The framework's ability to unify reasoning and contextual intelligence positions it as a promising solution for domain-specific LLM applications.  

---

**Conclusion:**  
REACT-X bridges the gap between reasoning and domain-specific models by integrating adaptive context refactoring, structured reasoning, and memory graph navigation. By addressing challenges in contextual inertia, reasoning integration, and computational efficiency, REACT-X establishes a scalable pathway for multi-domain LLM applications. Future work will explore extensions to additional domains and further optimizations in memory mechanisms.
