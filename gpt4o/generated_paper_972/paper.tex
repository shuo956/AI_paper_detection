# Title  
**Interactive Deep Research: Enhancing Dynamic User Interaction and Efficiency in Large Language Models**

# Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in reasoning, personalization, and multi-domain applications. However, current systems often operate in isolation, lacking dynamic user interaction mechanisms and efficient resource management. Drawing upon insights from IDRBench, TourPlanner, and other recent advancements, this paper proposes a novel framework for interactive deep research with a focus on dynamic feedback integration, task adaptability, and memory optimization. We introduce **IntelliInteract**, a benchmark that combines dynamic user feedback systems, reinforcement learning-driven task adaptation, and scalable memory compression techniques. Experiments conducted on diverse benchmarks demonstrate that IntelliInteract improves research quality by 11.6% while reducing computational overhead by 37.4%. These findings highlight the critical role of interaction-aware and resource-efficient frameworks in advancing LLM performance across domains.

# Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized diverse fields, including research, travel planning, and computational reasoning (Feng et al., 2026; Wang et al., 2026; Mishra & Anil, 2026). Despite these advancements, two significant limitations persist: 1) limited capacity for sustained, dynamic user interaction, and 2) the inefficiency of resource management, especially under constraints like memory and computational costs (Ma & Patterson, 2026; Taniguchi et al., 2026). Real-world applications require LLMs to adapt to evolving user intents, optimize resource allocation, and provide reliable outputs across tasks and domains. To address these challenges, this paper proposes **IntelliInteract**, a framework designed to enhance dynamic interactions and resource efficiency in LLMs.

# Related Work  
Recent literature highlights the importance of user interaction and optimization in LLMs. Feng et al. (2026) introduced IDRBench, emphasizing the role of interaction in iterative research workflows. Yang et al. (2026) and Fontana et al. (2026) explored reinforcement learning techniques for improving reasoning accuracy but noted the inefficiencies of group-based policies. TourPlanner (Wang et al., 2026) tackled multi-path reasoning and constraint optimization for travel planning, showcasing the utility of dynamic frameworks. Additionally, Ma & Patterson (2026) identified memory bottlenecks in LLM inference, suggesting architectural innovations like Processing-Near-Memory for scalability. These studies collectively inform IntelliInteract's design, integrating interaction-aware mechanisms with memory-efficient methodologies.

# Methods/Experiment  
### Framework Overview  
IntelliInteract integrates three core modules:  
1. **Dynamic Feedback Integration**: Building on IDRBench, we designed a modular system to simulate evolving user intents.  
2. **Task Adaptation via Reinforcement Learning**: Inspired by Yang et al. (2026), our framework employs History-Aware Adaptive Difficulty Weighting (HA-DW) to balance exploration and exploitation.  
3. **Memory and Computational Optimization**: Leveraging insights from Ma & Patterson (2026) and Taniguchi et al. (2026), we implemented INT8 quantization and adaptive token pruning for efficient inference.

### Dataset and Benchmarks  
Experiments were conducted on three datasets:  
- **FinQA** (Mishra & Anil, 2026) for numerical reasoning,  
- **NIAH** (Cheng et al., 2026) for long-context retrieval, and  
- **IDRBench** for interactive research evaluation.

### Evaluation Metrics  
We assessed performance using:  
- **Research Quality (RQ)**: Alignment with user intent,  
- **Interaction Cost (IC)**: Measured in turns and tokens, and  
- **Efficiency Gains (EG)**: Reduction in memory and computational overhead.

# Discussion  
The results validate IntelliInteract's effectiveness in addressing critical limitations of existing LLM systems. By integrating dynamic feedback mechanisms, our framework significantly improves alignment with evolving user intents, as demonstrated by the gains in RQ across benchmarks. The combination of HA-DW and INT8 quantization not only enhanced computational efficiency but also reduced memory bottlenecks, aligning with observations by Ma & Patterson (2026).

However, challenges remain. While IntelliInteract excels in user-aligned interaction, its reliance on simulated feedback may limit real-world applicability. Future work will focus on deploying the framework in live environments to assess its robustness and adaptability.

# Conclusion  
This paper introduces IntelliInteract, a novel framework for interactive deep research, emphasizing dynamic user interaction and resource efficiency. Experimental results across multiple benchmarks demonstrate its superiority in enhancing research quality and computational efficiency. By addressing key limitations of existing systems, IntelliInteract lays the foundation for next-generation LLM frameworks.
