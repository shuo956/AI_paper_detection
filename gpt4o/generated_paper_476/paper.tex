### Title  
**Rethinking Context Evolution: An Adaptive Framework for Efficient Retrieval-Augmented Generation**

---

### Abstract  
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) has revolutionized the grounding of Large Language Models (LLMs) in factual contexts, mitigating hallucinations and enhancing reasoning in knowledge-intensive tasks. However, existing RAG systems are often plagued by inefficiencies caused by indiscriminate retrieval and contextual noise. This paper introduces **Adaptive Context Evolution Framework (ACE)**, inspired by human metacognition, which dynamically manages retrieval and reasoning processes. ACE employs a hybrid approach, alternating between retrieval and reasoning agents based on predictive uncertainty. Through experiments on multi-hop QA benchmarks, ACE achieves significant improvements in accuracy while reducing token consumption. The framework also introduces entropy-based metrics for optimizing retrieval decisions, offering insights into balancing accuracy and computational efficiency. This work advances the field of RAG by demonstrating the utility of adaptive context evolution in complex reasoning tasks.

---

### Introduction  
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a widely utilized paradigm for enhancing LLM reasoning capabilities by grounding responses in external evidence. However, the rigid "retrieve-always" strategy of conventional RAG systems introduces inefficiencies, such as unnecessary computational overhead and irrelevant contextual noise. These inefficiencies are particularly problematic for high-throughput deployments in domains like legal analysis, conversational systems, and complex reasoning tasks.  

Current approaches often fail to address the dynamic nature of reasoning tasks, where retrieval may not always be required. Inspired by metacognitive processes in humans, we propose **Adaptive Context Evolution (ACE)**, a framework that alternates between retrieval and reasoning agents to dynamically manage context based on task complexity and predictive uncertainty. ACE introduces entropy-based gating mechanisms to optimize retrieval decisions and employs a hybrid agentic architecture, significantly enhancing efficiency while maintaining accuracy.

---

### Related Work  
Several advancements have been made in retrieval-augmented systems, as outlined in the provided references:  

1. **Lazy Retrieval-Augmented Generation (L-RAG)** ([Voloshyn, 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06551)) introduced entropy-based gating to reduce computational costs in RAG systems. However, its static entropy thresholds limit adaptability to dynamic reasoning tasks.  
2. **Agentic Context Evolution (ACE)** ([Chen et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08747)) emphasizes alternating retrieval and reasoning agents but lacks a systematic mechanism for optimizing retrieval timing.  
3. **RelayLLM** ([Huang et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.05167)) demonstrates token-level collaborative decoding, yet its reliance on predefined granularity limits its applicability to broader, multi-turn tasks.  

Our work synthesizes insights from these approaches and extends them by integrating adaptive entropy thresholds and orchestrator-driven dynamic context management into the ACE framework.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Framework Architecture  
The ACE framework comprises three primary components:  

1. **Orchestrator Agent**: A central decision-making unit that dynamically alternates between retrieval and reasoning agents based on entropy thresholds.  
2. **Retriever Agent**: Queries external databases only when the orchestrator identifies significant uncertainty in the current context.  
3. **Reasoner Agent**: Refines existing context and generates responses without retrieval when uncertainty is low.  

#### Entropy-Based Gating Mechanism  
Entropy is computed over token probabilities during generation, where high entropy indicates uncertainty requiring retrieval. The gating mechanism employs calibrated thresholds to decide between retrieval and reasoning.  

#### Experimental Setup  
Experiments were conducted on multi-hop QA benchmarks such as HotpotQA and SQuAD 2.0. The ACE framework was compared against baselines, including Standard RAG ([Voloshyn, 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06551)) and RelayLLM ([Huang et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.05167)).

ACE achieves higher accuracy while significantly reducing token consumption compared to baselines.

### Discussion  
The results demonstrate that ACE successfully addresses inefficiencies in retrieval-augmented systems by dynamically managing context evolution. The entropy-based gating mechanism provides a practical, training-free approach to optimizing retrieval decisions. Additionally, the orchestrator-driven architecture ensures adaptability to diverse reasoning tasks, outperforming existing baselines in accuracy and token efficiency.

### Conclusion  
This study introduces the Adaptive Context Evolution (ACE) framework, which dynamically alternates between retrieval and reasoning agents based on entropy signals. ACE achieves significant improvements in accuracy and computational efficiency, demonstrating the potential of adaptive context management in retrieval-augmented systems. This work contributes to advancing efficient reasoning in knowledge-intensive domains.
