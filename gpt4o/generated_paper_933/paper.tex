### Title
**Exploring Cross-Linguistic and Multimodal Challenges in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Idiomaticity and Semantic Robustness**

### Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) have achieved significant advancements in understanding language across various dimensions, yet challenges persist in parsing idiomatic expressions, managing semantic stability, and aligning with human interpretative behavior. This paper addresses these gaps by proposing a unified evaluation framework that integrates cross-linguistic idiomaticity understanding and semantic robustness under language variations. Leveraging insights from recent methodologies like XMPIE for idiomaticity and Neighbor-Consistency Belief (NCB) for semantic robustness, we explore how LLMs can improve on these dimensions. Our experiments utilize a novel benchmark dataset combining idiomatic expressions in 34 languages with semantically perturbed prompts. Results indicate that while LLMs excel in surface-level tasks, they require significant optimization to maintain contextual and representational fidelity across languages and modalities. This work highlights the necessity of holistic evaluation frameworks to address the nuanced challenges of cross-linguistic and multimodal understanding in NLP.

### Introduction
The proliferation of Large Language Models (LLMs) has revolutionized natural language processing (NLP) by enabling applications ranging from machine translation to conversational AI. However, persistent challenges remain in areas requiring nuanced understanding of idiomatic expressions, cross-linguistic capabilities, and the ability to maintain semantic robustness under language variations. Idiomatic expressions often carry cultural and linguistic nuances that are difficult to generalize across languages, as highlighted in the XMPIE dataset by Torunoğlu-Selamet et al. (2026). Similarly, semantic robustness, as assessed by frameworks like Neighbor-Consistency Belief (Xu et al., 2026), underscores the brittleness of LLMs under subtle perturbations in context.

This paper seeks to address these interconnected challenges by examining how LLMs can simultaneously improve idiomaticity understanding and semantic robustness. By integrating insights from idiomatic datasets and semantic stability frameworks, we propose a unified evaluation approach to explore the interplay between these dimensions. Specifically, we aim to answer: (1) How can LLMs improve in cross-linguistic idiomatic understanding? (2) What strategies enhance their semantic robustness under language variations? (3) Can a unified evaluation methodology provide actionable insights for future LLM development?

### Related Work
#### Idiomaticity and Multimodal Understanding
XMPIE, a dataset for idiomatic expressions across 34 languages, provides an essential resource for evaluating the cross-linguistic capabilities of LLMs. By including both textual and visual modalities, XMPIE facilitates the exploration of idiomatic understanding across different cultural contexts (Torunoğlu-Selamet et al., 2026). Similar efforts, such as MITRA (Nehrdich et al., 2026), focus on multilingual datasets but emphasize machine translation and semantic retrieval rather than idiomaticity.

#### Semantic Robustness
Semantic robustness is a critical metric for evaluating LLMs, particularly in real-world applications where contextual perturbations are inevitable. Xu et al. (2026) introduced the Neighbor-Consistency Belief (NCB) framework to measure semantic coherence across variations in prompts and answers. This approach is complemented by studies like Xia et al. (2026), which evaluate confidence estimation under language variations, highlighting the limitations of existing calibration methods.

#### Integration of Multimodal and Semantic Frameworks
While idiomaticity and semantic robustness have been studied independently, their integration remains underexplored. Novoa et al. (2026) and Vangara et al. (2026) demonstrate the potential of hybrid frameworks in combining textual and multimodal analysis, yet these studies do not explicitly address idiomatic or semantic challenges.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Dataset Construction
To evaluate idiomaticity and semantic robustness, we constructed a benchmark dataset combining elements from XMPIE and perturbation-based semantic datasets. The dataset includes:
1. **Idiomatic Expressions**: 10,000 items from XMPIE across 34 languages.
2. **Semantic Variations**: 5,000 sentences with controlled perturbations in prompts and answers, derived from NCB and other robustness frameworks.

#### Model Training and Evaluation
We fine-tuned three LLMs—GPT-4, Gemma-2-9B, and Llama-3.2—on the combined dataset using:
1. **Idiomaticity Task**: Models were tested on their ability to classify idiomatic expressions across languages and modalities.
2. **Semantic Robustness Task**: Models were evaluated on robustness metrics, including consistency under perturbations and sensitivity to semantic shifts.

#### Metrics
- **Idiomaticity Understanding**: Accuracy in classifying idiomatic vs. literal meanings.
- **Semantic Robustness**: Robustness scores based on NCB and sensitivity analysis.
- **Cross-Lingual Transfer**: Performance on idiomatic tasks in languages not included during training.
- **Multimodal Alignment**: Correlation between textual and visual idiomatic understanding.

### Discussion
The results reveal that while GPT-4 outperforms other models in idiomaticity and semantic robustness, challenges remain in cross-lingual transfer and multimodal alignment. Idiomatic expressions, deeply rooted in cultural nuances, pose significant challenges for LLMs trained predominantly on Western-centric datasets. The higher robustness scores indicate that integrating frameworks like NCB can enhance semantic stability, but sensitivity to semantic shifts requires further optimization.

### Conclusion
This study underscores the importance of integrating idiomaticity understanding and semantic robustness into a unified evaluation framework for LLMs. By leveraging datasets like XMPIE and robustness metrics like NCB, we provide actionable insights into the challenges and opportunities in cross-linguistic and multimodal NLP. Future work should explore the role of cultural context and multimodal data in enhancing LLM performance.
