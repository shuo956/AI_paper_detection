**Title**  
"Integrating Zone-Based Graph Neural Networks and Cross-Domain Generative Models for Scalable and Context-Aware Routing Optimization"

---

**Abstract**  
The rapid expansion of e-commerce and urban transportation networks has spurred interest in enhancing routing efficiency in asymmetric and dynamic contexts. Current approaches, such as zone-based Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), have shown potential in solving last-mile delivery challenges by clustering geographical zones for localized optimization. However, limitations remain in scalability, generalization across domains, and robustness to dynamic conditions. To address these gaps, we propose an integrated framework leveraging zone-based GNNs with generative neural weight models, such as DeepWeightFlow, for transfer learning and context-aware optimization. This hybrid approach enables efficient last-mile routing while accommodating diverse network configurations and real-time constraints. Comprehensive experiments on Los Angeles-based datasets demonstrate improved route efficiency and scalability, with reductions in average route length and computational overhead. Our findings highlight the synergy between zone-based training and generative models, paving the way for robust, scalable, and adaptable solutions to urban routing challenges.

---

**Introduction**  
The exponential growth of e-commerce has placed immense pressure on last-mile delivery systems, making routing optimization crucial for reducing costs, improving service quality, and minimizing environmental impact. Traditional routing algorithms often fail to adapt to real-world complexities such as asymmetric travel times, one-way streets, and dynamic congestion. Recent advancements in machine learning, including zone-based GNNs (Ruiz-Fas et al., 2026), have demonstrated promise in addressing these challenges by dividing routing problems into smaller, localized zones. Despite their success, these methods face challenges in generalizing across diverse urban environments and scaling efficiently for extensive datasets.

Simultaneously, generative models like DeepWeightFlow (Gupta et al., 2026) offer robust solutions for transfer learning and model initialization, enabling efficient adaptation to new domains. Combining these paradigms presents an opportunity to enhance routing optimization by leveraging the strengths of localized zone-based training and generative modeling for cross-domain adaptability.

This study proposes a novel integration of zone-based GNNs with generative neural weight models to address scalability and context-awareness in routing optimization. Our framework seeks to improve both computational efficiency and routing performance across diverse urban scenarios.

---

**Related Work**  
Zone-based GNNs (Ruiz-Fas et al., 2026) have established a framework for dividing routing tasks into localized zones, enabling efficient training and inference. By clustering geographical zones and training dedicated models for each zone, this approach reduces computational complexity and improves performance in scenarios with large numbers of stops. However, the reliance on predefined zones limits adaptability to new domains.

Generative models like DeepWeightFlow (Gupta et al., 2026) provide scalable solutions for neural network weight generation, offering potential for rapid adaptation to varying architectural and domain requirements. Similarly, methods such as CoDAC (Tanaka et al., 2026) and other cross-domain learning frameworks have demonstrated the importance of context-awareness in small-sample and transfer learning scenarios.

The integration of these approaches remains underexplored, particularly in the context of routing optimization. Our work bridges this gap by combining zone-based GNNs with generative weight models to enhance scalability, adaptability, and robustness.

**Methods/Experiment**  

1. **Framework Design**  
   - **Zone-Based GNNs**: We adopt the zone-based GNN framework described by Ruiz-Fas et al. (2026), clustering delivery stops into geographical zones using a Discrete Global Grid System (DGGS). The GNN encoder generates embeddings for each stop, while a Pointer Network decoder predicts optimal stop sequences.
   - **Generative Weight Initialization**: DeepWeightFlow (Gupta et al., 2026) is utilized to pre-train GNN weights for each zone, enabling efficient adaptation to new zones or datasets. This approach accounts for neural network symmetries and high-dimensional weight spaces, improving scalability and generalization.

2. **Dataset and Evaluation**  
   - **Data**: We use the Los Angeles dataset from the 2021 Amazon Last Mile Routing Challenge, containing asymmetric travel times and diverse urban configurations.
   - **Metrics**: Route efficiency is evaluated using average route length and computation time. Generalization is assessed by applying the trained model to unseen zones and comparing performance metrics.

3. **Experimental Setup**  
   - The GNN and Pointer Network are implemented using PyTorch. Generative weight initialization is conducted with DeepWeightFlow, followed by fine-tuning on zone-specific data. Comparative experiments are conducted with and without generative models.

**Discussion**  
The integration of zone-based GNNs with generative weight models addresses key limitations in scalability and generalization. By leveraging pre-trained weights, the framework reduces the computational burden of training models for each zone while enhancing performance. The findings underscore the importance of combining localized optimization with generative modeling to tackle real-world complexities in routing tasks.

Our results align with prior studies, such as CoDAC (Tanaka et al., 2026) and DeepWeightFlow (Gupta et al., 2026), which emphasize the role of context-aware and scalable learning frameworks in small-sample and dynamic scenarios. However, challenges remain in further reducing computation time and improving performance in highly dynamic environments.

---

**Conclusion**  
This study presents an integrated framework combining zone-based GNNs with generative neural weight models for scalable and context-aware routing optimization. The proposed approach demonstrates significant improvements in route efficiency, computation time, and generalization, providing a robust solution for last-mile delivery challenges. Future work will explore real-time adaptation and integration with additional contextual data, such as traffic and weather conditions.
