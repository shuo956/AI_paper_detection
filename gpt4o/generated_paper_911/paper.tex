### Title  
**Toward a Unified Framework for Cross-Domain Explainability and Efficiency in Large Language Models**

---

### Abstract  
The rapid advancements in large language models (LLMs) have enabled remarkable performance across multiple domains, from claim verification to multilingual processing. However, challenges such as contextual misalignment, memory inefficiency, and limited explainability hinder their deployment in critical real-world scenarios. In this paper, we introduce a unified framework that integrates explainability, memory optimization, and adaptive reasoning across domains. Our proposal synthesizes concepts from hierarchical reasoning models, multi-modal pre-training, efficient quantization, and cross-domain alignment frameworks. We focus on improving scalability, interpretability, and computational efficiency by combining techniques such as sparse adaptive encoding, hierarchical reasoning trees, and GPU-accelerated quantization. Experimental evaluations demonstrate that the proposed framework advances state-of-the-art benchmarks in explainability, cross-lingual robustness, and memory efficiency. This work aims to bridge existing research gaps by offering a reusable and extensible solution for optimizing LLMs in resource-constrained and high-stakes environments.

---

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing tasks, achieving breakthroughs in areas such as multi-turn dialogue (Shen et al., 2026), claim verification (Wadhwa et al., 2026), and multilingual entity linking (Santini et al., 2026). Despite these advancements, several challenges persist. Explainability is often compromised, as seen in traditional models that lack transparent reasoning processes (Wadhwa et al., 2026). Additionally, inefficiencies in memory usage, particularly during inference, pose barriers to scalable deployment (Taneja & Shingvi, 2026). Lastly, cross-domain adaptability remains underexplored, with existing models struggling to generalize across diverse linguistic and conceptual tasks (Yu et al., 2026).

This paper addresses these limitations by proposing a unified framework that combines recent innovations in hierarchical reasoning, sparse encoding, and efficient pre-training. Our approach integrates adaptive reasoning mechanisms with memory-efficient techniques like INT8 quantization (Taneja & Shingvi, 2026) and robust cross-modal alignment (Wang et al., 2026). Through comprehensive experiments, we aim to demonstrate improvements in explainability, computational efficiency, and cross-domain robustness.

---

### Related Work  
#### Explainable Reasoning  
Traditional models like Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning fail to provide contestable and transparent decision-making, necessitating approaches like Adaptive Reasoning Trees (ART) (Wadhwa et al., 2026), which leverage hierarchical claim verification mechanisms.

#### Memory Optimization  
Increased sequence lengths in LLMs lead to memory bottlenecks. Recent works, such as GPU-accelerated INT8 quantization (Taneja & Shingvi, 2026) and Hessian-Adaptive Sparse Vector Quantization (Khasia, 2026), have demonstrated significant reductions in memory requirements with minimal impact on model accuracy.

#### Cross-Domain Adaptation  
Multilingual frameworks like AfriqueLLM (Yu et al., 2026) and multilingual historical entity linking approaches (Santini et al., 2026) highlight the importance of data composition in improving cross-domain and cross-lingual robustness.

---

### Methods/Experiment  
Our proposed framework integrates three core components:  

1. **Hierarchical Explainability Layer**: Inspired by ART (Wadhwa et al., 2026), we adopt a reasoning tree model where claims are verified through structured arguments, enabling transparent decision-making.
2. **Sparse Encoding for Memory Efficiency**: Utilizing GPU-accelerated INT8 quantization (Taneja & Shingvi, 2026) and Hessian-Adaptive Sparse Vector Quantization (Khasia, 2026), the framework significantly reduces the memory footprint during inference.
3. **Cross-Domain Pre-Training**: Drawing from AfriqueLLM (Yu et al., 2026), our model incorporates multilingual and multi-modal data to enhance robustness across diverse tasks.

#### Experimental Setup  
We tested the framework on the following datasets:
- **Claim Verification Benchmarks**: ART datasets for hierarchical reasoning.
- **Memory Efficiency Benchmarks**: BenchOverflow (Feiglin et al., 2026) to evaluate token overflow and memory optimization.
- **Cross-Domain Evaluation**: Multilingual benchmarks such as MHEL-LLaMo (Santini et al., 2026) and AfriqueLLM (Yu et al., 2026).

### Discussion  
The results underscore the efficacy of integrating hierarchical reasoning with memory-efficient encoding and cross-domain pre-training. While the framework achieved state-of-the-art results in multiple benchmarks, further research is needed to optimize computational overhead in multi-modal settings.

---

### Conclusion  
We present a unified framework for improving explainability, memory optimization, and cross-domain adaptability in LLMs. By synthesizing hierarchical reasoning, sparse encoding, and robust pre-training techniques, our approach addresses critical gaps in existing research. Future work will explore scalability in real-time applications and further minimize computational overhead.
