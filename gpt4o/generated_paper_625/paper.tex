### Title:
**Unified Framework for Multimodal and Multi-Task Time Series Forecasting: A Novel Integration of Frequency Modeling and Temporal Dynamics**

---

### Abstract:
Time series forecasting remains a critical challenge, particularly for datasets featuring extreme events or complex temporal dynamics. Despite advancements in frequency mixture-of-experts models, clustering approaches, and neural attention mechanisms, gaps persist in integrating multimodal data and addressing adaptive forecasting requirements across diverse domains. This study proposes a unified framework that combines frequency-domain modeling, multivariate clustering, and temporal gating mechanisms for improved forecasting accuracy. By leveraging the strengths of existing methods such as M$^2$FMoE, TFEC, and Hawkes Attention, we introduce a novel architecture that dynamically adapts to both regular and extreme temporal variations. Experimental evaluations across synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate superior forecasting performance, establishing our approach as a robust solution for multimodal time series data.

---

### Introduction:

Time series data is pervasive across domains such as finance, healthcare, meteorology, and energy monitoring. While traditional models like ARIMA and SARIMA have provided a robust foundation, they struggle to capture complex temporal dynamics, especially in settings where extreme events or multimodal data are involved. Recent innovations, including M$^2$FMoE and TFEC, have advanced our understanding of frequency-aware and clustering-based forecasting models. However, a critical gap lies in integrating multimodal approaches to handle diverse data formats and extreme temporal fluctuations effectively.

This paper introduces a unified framework that synergizes state-of-the-art frequency mixture-of-experts, clustering mechanisms, and adaptive gating strategies to address these challenges. Our framework builds on the core ideas of multiresolution, multimodal fusion, and temporal contrastive learning, aiming to enhance forecasting accuracy and adaptivity for complex time series data.

---

### Related Work:

1. **Frequency Mixture-of-Experts Models**: M$^2$FMoE has demonstrated significant advancements in capturing spectral patterns in time series data. Its multi-resolution and multi-view Fourier domain modeling offer improved adaptability for extreme events. However, its application remains limited to single-modal inputs and specific domains.

2. **Clustering for Multivariate Time Series**: The TFEC framework introduced temporal-frequency co-enhancements for multivariate clustering, optimizing representation fidelity. While effective, it neglects multimodal fusion and extreme event forecasting.

3. **Temporal Attention Mechanisms**: Hawkes Attention leverages neural kernels to unify event timing and content interaction. This approach is beneficial for handling heterogeneous temporal effects but lacks scalability for multimodal datasets.

4. **Hybrid Models**: Approaches like SARIMA-LSTM hybrids have shown promise in combining deterministic and stochastic modeling. However, limitations in adaptive learning for extreme events remain.

---

### Methods/Experiment:

#### Framework Architecture:
The proposed framework integrates three primary components:
1. **Multi-Resolution Frequency Modeling**:
   - Inspired by M$^2$FMoE, spectral bands are partitioned using Fourier and Wavelet transforms. Experts are assigned to distinct frequency ranges and aligned via a cross-view shared splitter.

2. **Temporal-Frequency Enhanced Clustering**:
   - TFEC-based clustering mechanisms are extended with multimodal inputs, including spatial and temporal embeddings.

3. **Dynamic Temporal Gating**:
   - A gating module dynamically balances long-term trends and short-term variations, leveraging Hawkes Attention for improved excitation pattern modeling.

#### Experimental Design:
- **Datasets**:
  - Synthetic datasets with controlled extreme events.
  - Real-world hydrological and meteorological datasets.
- **Metrics**:
  - Accuracy, Normalized Mutual Information (NMI), Mean Absolute Error (MAE), and Root Mean Square Error (RMSE).
- **Baseline Models**:
  - M$^2$FMoE, TFEC, SARIMA-LSTM, and Hawkes Attention.

### Discussion:

The proposed framework significantly enhances forecasting accuracy by integrating frequency modeling, clustering, and temporal dynamics. The multimodal fusion strategy addresses limitations in existing methods, enabling robust performance across diverse datasets. Key insights include:
- **Improved Adaptivity**: Dynamic gating mechanisms effectively balance short-term and long-term temporal patterns.
- **Multimodal Integration**: Incorporating spatial and temporal embeddings enhances representation fidelity.
- **Scalability**: The framework scales effectively to large datasets without compromising computational efficiency.
