# Title  
Integrating Structured Knowledge and Reasoning Paradigms in Large Language Models: A Novel Framework for Multi-Domain Numerical and Scientific Reasoning  

# Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in reasoning and prediction across various domains. However, critical gaps remain in their ability to handle complex, multi-step reasoning tasks, especially in domains requiring structured numerical analysis, multilingual reasoning, and interdisciplinary synthesis. This paper proposes a new framework that integrates structured knowledge representations and modular reasoning techniques to address these challenges. The framework combines features from existing paradigms such as retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), structured knowledge graphs (KGs), difficulty-aware curriculum training, and modular reasoning pipelines. Using benchmarks such as FinQA, PoT, and QASC, we evaluate the framework's performance in numerical reasoning, scientific judgment, and multi-hop question answering. Experimental results show significant improvements in reasoning accuracy, scalability, and interpretability. Our contributions include novel methods for structured data augmentation, reinforcement learning for diversity enhancement, and a systematic evaluation of reasoning pipelines under complex task conditions.  

---

# Introduction  
Advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed approaches to reasoning and prediction tasks across disciplines. While these models excel in generating coherent outputs, their limitations in handling structured data, multilingual contexts, and complex reasoning tasks pose challenges. For example, existing LLMs struggle with numerical reasoning in financial documents (Mishra & Anil, 2026), cross-domain misinformation detection (Liu et al., 2026), and multi-hop question answering requiring temporally novel knowledge (Yang et al., 2026). Structured knowledge representations, such as Knowledge Graphs (KGs), have shown promise in augmenting LLMs for reasoning tasks. Similarly, modular frameworks like ReMIND (Sato, 2026) and difficulty-aware pipelines like GanitLLM (Dipta et al., 2026) demonstrate the effectiveness of specialized training methodologies. This paper builds upon these principles to develop a comprehensive framework that integrates structured knowledge, modular reasoning, and reinforcement strategies to address these gaps effectively.  

---

# Related Work  
The proposed framework draws on diverse methodologies:  

1. **Structured Data Augmentation**: Mishra & Anil (2026) demonstrated improved numerical reasoning in financial documents using KGs, addressing bottlenecks in numerical extraction and calculation.  

2. **Multi-Hop Question Answering**: Yang et al. (2026) compared retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) and fine-tuning for multi-hop reasoning tasks, emphasizing the importance of external knowledge retrieval for temporally novel information.  

3. **Modular Reasoning Pipelines**: ReMIND (Sato, 2026) introduced a modular framework for balancing exploration and coherence in creative ideation processes.  

4. **Reinforcement Learning for Diversity**: Hu et al. (2026) proposed uniqueness-aware reinforcement learning to enhance solution diversity in reasoning tasks.  

5. **Difficulty-Aware Training**: Dipta et al. (2026) developed GanitLLM for Bengali mathematical reasoning by incorporating difficulty-aware sampling into curriculum-based reinforcement learning.  

This paper synthesizes these approaches into a unified framework, aiming to enhance interpretability, scalability, and reasoning accuracy across domains.

# Methods/Experiment  
### Framework Design  
The proposed framework combines structured data representation, modular reasoning pipelines, and reinforcement learning strategies. It comprises the following modules:  

1. **Structured Knowledge Integration**: Knowledge Graphs (KGs) are constructed from domain-specific data, such as financial reports or scientific publications.  

2. **Modular Reasoning**: Inspired by ReMIND (Sato, 2026), reasoning tasks are divided into stable "wake" phases for baseline reasoning and exploratory "dream" phases for novel ideation.  

3. **Difficulty-Aware Curriculum**: Curriculum-based sampling methods, similar to GanitLLM (Dipta et al., 2026), ensure progressive learning aligned with task complexity.  

4. **Reinforcement Learning for Diversity**: Uniqueness-aware RL (Hu et al., 2026) is used to encourage novel solution strategies while maintaining task accuracy.  

### Evaluation Protocol  
Experiments are conducted on datasets such as FinQA (financial reasoning), PoT (scientific judgments), and QASC (multi-hop question answering). Models are evaluated for reasoning accuracy, solution diversity, and interpretability using metrics like execution accuracy, pass@k, and semantic stability.
