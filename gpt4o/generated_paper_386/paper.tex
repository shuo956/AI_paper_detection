### Title
**Exploring the Integration of Structure-Aware Evolution in Forecasting: A Multi-Domain Approach Using Prophet and EvolSQL**

### Abstract
Forecasting methodologies are critical for decision-making across domains such as finance, retail, and scientific research. However, challenges in reproducibility, structural diversity, and interpretability persist. This study proposes an innovative integration of structure-aware evolution and reproducible frameworks by combining the Prophet forecasting model and EvolSQL data synthesis framework. Prophet ensures reproducibility and transparency, while EvolSQL addresses the structural diversity of input datasets. A hybrid methodology was used to evaluate forecasting performance on financial and retail datasets, leveraging synthetic, structurally diverse SQL queries. Results demonstrate significant improvements in forecast accuracy, structural interpretability, and cross-domain adaptability. Our findings underscore the importance of integrating structured data evolution with reproducible forecasting frameworks for robust, versatile analytics.

---

### Introduction
Forecasting is a cornerstone of decision-making in various domains, such as finance, retail, and scientific research. Accurate forecasts enable organizations to allocate resources effectively, mitigate risks, and capitalize on emerging opportunities. Despite advancements in machine learning and statistical models, challenges such as reproducibility, interpretability, and structural data diversity remain persistent.

Prophet, an open-source forecasting framework by Meta, has emerged as a reproducibility-enabling solution, balancing simplicity and performance (Shapiro & Panvelwala, 2026). However, existing forecasting models, including Prophet, often rely on structured datasets with limited diversity, reducing their efficacy in complex, multi-domain settings. On the other hand, EvolSQL, a structure-aware data synthesis framework, introduces advanced SQL query evolution to enhance structural diversity in datasets (Pan et al., 2026). This study explores the integration of Prophet and EvolSQL to address these challenges.

We hypothesize that combining Prophet’s reproducibility capabilities with EvolSQL’s structural enhancements will improve forecasting accuracy, interpretability, and adaptability across domains. This paper presents a hybrid approach to forecasting, evaluated through multi-domain datasets, and highlights implications for reproducible research and methodological rigor.

---

### Related Work
1. **Reproducible Forecasting Frameworks**: Prophet has been proven effective in providing transparent, reproducible workflows for forecasting tasks (Shapiro & Panvelwala, 2026). Its additive modeling structure and integration capabilities make it particularly suitable for Python-based research workflows.

2. **Structural Data Synthesis**: EvolSQL addresses the scarcity of diverse and complex datasets by evolving seed SQL queries into more sophisticated forms (Pan et al., 2026). This enhances schema coverage and query complexity, which are crucial for improving model generalization.

3. **Hybrid Approaches**: Combining structured data synthesis with machine learning models has gained traction in tasks like misinformation detection (Liu et al., 2026) and numerical reasoning (Mishra & Anil, 2026). However, its application in forecasting remains underexplored.

This study builds on these advancements by integrating Prophet and EvolSQL to evaluate their combined impact on forecasting performance.

### Methods/Experiment

#### Dataset
Two datasets were utilized:
- **Financial Dataset**: Daily stock prices and trading volumes from publicly available sources.
- **Retail Dataset**: Weekly sales data across multiple product categories.

#### Experimental Setup
1. **Data Synthesis with EvolSQL**:
   - EvolSQL generated synthetic, structurally diverse SQL queries for both datasets. Query evolution was guided by schema coverage and complexity metrics, ensuring a variety of relational, aggregation, and nesting structures.

2. **Forecasting with Prophet**:
   - Prophet was trained on both original and synthesized datasets. Hyperparameters were kept consistent to ensure reproducibility.

3. **Evaluation Metrics**:
   - Forecast accuracy (Mean Absolute Percentage Error - MAPE).
   - Structural diversity (measured by SQL Abstract Syntax Tree complexity).
   - Model interpretability (qualitative assessment of Prophet’s decomposition plots).

### Discussion
The combination of Prophet and EvolSQL yields significant improvements in forecasting accuracy and structural diversity. Synthesized datasets introduced by EvolSQL enhanced the generalizability of Prophet, particularly in multi-domain scenarios. Improvements in SQL query complexity highlight the effectiveness of structure-aware evolution in enriching input datasets.

One limitation is the computational overhead introduced by EvolSQL, which requires careful optimization for scalability. Additionally, while Prophet provides interpretable results, its additive model structure may not fully capture nonlinear relationships in highly complex datasets.

Future work could explore integrating other reproducible frameworks, such as RAAR for misinformation detection (Liu et al., 2026), to further enhance forecasting performance.

---

### Conclusion
This study demonstrates that integrating EvolSQL with Prophet significantly enhances forecasting accuracy, structural diversity, and interpretability. The hybrid approach addresses key challenges in reproducibility and cross-domain adaptability, paving the way for robust, versatile forecasting methodologies.
