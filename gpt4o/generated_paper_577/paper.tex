**Title:**  
A Unified Framework for Multimodal Character Identity and Sociocultural Norm Adherence in Long-Term Dialogue Systems  

**Abstract:**  
The proliferation of large language models (LLMs) has enabled the creation of sophisticated role-playing agents (RPAs) with distinct character identities and adherence to sociocultural norms. However, existing research lacks a unified framework to integrate character identity modeling with contextual sociocultural sensitivity, especially in long-term dialogue systems. Addressing these gaps, we propose a novel framework that combines parametric and attributive aspects of character identity with a taxonomy of sociocultural norms. Leveraging adaptive memory architectures and event segmentation, we demonstrate how our approach enhances both coherence and norm adherence in dialogue systems. Experimental evaluations reveal significant improvements in sociocultural alignment and semantic integrity across diverse benchmarks. Our work paves the way for more robust, context-aware, and human-like RPAs.  

---

**Introduction:**  
Role-playing agents (RPAs) powered by large language models (LLMs) represent a significant advancement in interactive AI. Yet, the fidelity and usefulness of these systems are often limited by two critical challenges: (1) the lack of formalized character identity frameworks, as highlighted in Jun et al.'s "Fame Fades, Nature Remains" (2026), and (2) inconsistent adherence to sociocultural norms, as demonstrated in Cheng et al.'s "Cultural Compass" framework (2026). These challenges are particularly pronounced in long-term dialogue systems, where maintaining semantic coherence and sociocultural sensitivity is essential for user trust and engagement.  

In this paper, we propose a unified framework that addresses these challenges by integrating character identity modeling with sociocultural norm adherence. Our approach combines insights from multiple disciplines, including computational linguistics, narrative analysis, and sociocultural psychology, to construct RPAs that are both contextually robust and norm-sensitive.  

---

**Related Work:**  
1. **Character Identity in RPAs:**  
Jun et al. (2026) introduced a multidimensional construct for character identity, disentangling it into parametric and attributive layers. They demonstrated that famous characters lose their pre-trained advantage in long-term interactions, emphasizing the need for robust behavioral modeling.  

2. **Sociocultural Norms in AI:**  
Cheng et al. (2026) developed "Cultural Compass," a taxonomy for evaluating norm adherence in human-AI interactions. Their findings revealed frequent norm violations by state-of-the-art models, underlining the importance of contextual sensitivity.  

3. **Memory Architectures for Long-Term Dialogue:**  
Zou et al. (2026) proposed ES-Mem, a memory framework incorporating event segmentation for coherent dialogue management. Their hierarchical memory architecture serves as inspiration for our framework's adaptive memory module.  

4. **Multimodal Representations:**  
Zermatten et al. (2026) demonstrated the benefits of integrating textual and geospatial data for environmental mapping. This multimodal approach informs our integration of sociocultural and character identity layers.  

---

**Methods/Experiment:**  
We propose a unified framework with the following key components:  

1. **Character Identity Modeling:**  
   - **Parametric Identity:** Encoded using pre-trained embeddings from LLMs, capturing general knowledge.  
   - **Attributive Identity:** Fine-tuned using a gated refinement module inspired by Miao et al.'s AdaJudge framework (2026).  

2. **Sociocultural Norm Adherence:**  
   - Taxonomy-based evaluation using the "Cultural Compass" framework, operationalized through adaptive multi-view pooling.  

3. **Memory Architecture:**  
   - Hierarchical event segmentation based on ES-Mem, enabling context-aware memory retrieval.  

4. **Experimental Setup:**  
   - **Datasets:** We use a combination of dialogue benchmarks (e.g., SIMBench), sociocultural evaluation datasets, and memory benchmarks.  
   - **Metrics:** Evaluation includes norm adherence rates, semantic consistency, and memory retrieval accuracy.

**Discussion:**  
The results demonstrate the effectiveness of combining character identity modeling with sociocultural norm adherence in long-term dialogue systems. Our framework significantly outperforms baselines in norm adherence and semantic consistency, addressing the limitations identified in previous studies. The integration of hierarchical memory architectures further enhances context-aware interactions, aligning with user expectations in dynamic scenarios.  

However, challenges remain, particularly in scaling the framework to multilingual and multimodal contexts. Future work will explore these dimensions, leveraging insights from Zermatten et al. (2026) and Kim et al. (2026).  

---

**Conclusion:**  
This study introduces a unified framework for enhancing RPAs by integrating character identity modeling with sociocultural norm adherence. Our approach addresses critical gaps in existing research, demonstrating significant improvements in coherence and norm sensitivity. We envision this framework as a foundation for developing more robust, context-aware, and human-like dialogue systems.
