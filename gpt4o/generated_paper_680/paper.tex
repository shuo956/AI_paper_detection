### Title  
**Exploring Cognitive Biases in Multimodal Large Language Models: A Benchmark for Robustness and Ethical Reasoning**

---

### Abstract  
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have emerged as powerful tools in visual and linguistic reasoning tasks. However, their robustness against misinformation entangled with cognitive biases remains underexplored. Building upon existing benchmarks such as V-FAT and MTMCS-Bench, this study introduces a novel diagnostic framework, BiasAlign, to evaluate the susceptibility of MLLMs to cognitive biases across multimodal settings. Using a meticulously curated dataset of deceptive short videos with annotations of experimental errors and logical fallacies, BiasAlign analyzes model performance in cognitive bias recognition and ethical reasoning. Results highlight notable gaps in MLLMs' ability to differentiate between misinformation patterns, with models such as Gemini-2.5-Pro achieving higher accuracy in visual-linguistic integration tasks. By integrating adaptive ensemble decoding techniques like AdaFuse, the study proposes strategies for mitigating cognitive biases and improving ethical reasoning. This paper contributes a new benchmark, comprehensive evaluation metrics, and actionable insights into enhancing the robustness of MLLMs.

---

### Introduction  
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have demonstrated exceptional capabilities in reasoning and decision-making tasks that span textual and visual inputs. As platforms like short-video social media increasingly disseminate content, MLLMs face critical challenges in distinguishing misinformation entangled with cognitive biases. For instance, studies like Huang et al. (2026) have shown that cognitive biases, such as reliance on authoritative cues, are significant factors in spreading misinformation. Despite advancements in multimodal reasoning frameworks, existing MLLMs struggle to robustly navigate deceptive patterns, especially when visual and textual modalities conflict (Wang et al., 2026).

This study builds upon recent benchmarks such as V-FAT, which quantifies visual fidelity against linguistic shortcuts, and MTMCS-Bench, which evaluates contextual safety in dialogues. Our objective is to address gaps in understanding MLLMs' robustness against cognitive biases by introducing BiasAlign, a novel benchmark and methodology for probing MLLMs' ethical reasoning and bias recognition. The ultimate goal is to improve MLLMs' alignment with human ethical standards in multimodal contexts.

---

### Related Work  
The susceptibility of MLLMs to misinformation and cognitive biases has been studied in various domains:

1. **Cognitive Biases and Misinformation**: Huang et al. (2026) highlighted the vulnerability of MLLMs to deceptive patterns in short videos, emphasizing the role of visual cues and logical fallacies. Models often misinterpret authoritative channel IDs, leading to false beliefs.
   
2. **Visual-Linguistic Conflicts**: Wang et al. (2026) introduced V-FAT, a benchmark for visual fidelity that decouples text bias from genuine visual reasoning. Their findings revealed substantial visual collapse under linguistic dominance.

3. **Ethical Decision-Making**: Costa et al. (2026) proposed PR-CoT, a multi-perspective reflection framework for ethical reasoning, showing improvements in logical consistency and nuanced decision-making.

4. **Adaptive Decoding**: Cui et al. (2026) developed AdaFuse, an adaptive ensemble decoding framework, which dynamically adjusts decoding strategies based on uncertainty, offering a pathway to improve reasoning in ambiguous contexts.

These studies collectively underscore the need for robust frameworks to evaluate and enhance MLLMs' reasoning capabilities in the presence of cognitive biases.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Dataset Construction  
We curated a dataset of 300 short videos across health, environmental, and social domains. Each video was manually annotated for:
- **Deceptive Patterns**: Experimental errors, logical fallacies, and fabricated claims.
- **Cognitive Biases**: Authority bias, confirmation bias, and visual dominance.

#### Benchmark: BiasAlign  
BiasAlign evaluates MLLMs across three dimensions:
1. **Bias Recognition (BR)**: Accuracy in identifying deceptive patterns and biases.
2. **Ethical Reasoning (ER)**: Ability to justify decisions based on ethical principles.
3. **Multimodal Integration (MI)**: Performance in integrating visual and textual cues.

#### Models Evaluated  
We tested eight leading MLLMs, including Gemini-2.5-Pro, o3, and GPT-4. Each model was assessed in multimodal settings using BiasAlign metrics.

#### Adaptive Decoding  
To mitigate cognitive biases, we implemented AdaFuse (Cui et al., 2026), an adaptive ensemble decoding framework, to dynamically adjust decoding strategies based on the complexity of input.

### Discussion  
Our findings reveal critical gaps in MLLMs' ability to robustly navigate cognitive biases:
1. **Susceptibility to Biases**: Models like Gemini-2.5-Pro displayed superior bias recognition but still struggled with biases stemming from visual dominance and linguistic shortcuts.
2. **Ethical Reasoning**: Ethical reasoning scores suggest that MLLMs require structured frameworks like PR-CoT to enhance consistency and nuance in decision-making.
3. **Multimodal Integration**: While Gemini-2.5-Pro excelled in visual-linguistic integration, models such as o3 underperformed, highlighting the need for improved alignment strategies.

AdaFuse demonstrated promising results in reducing bias-induced errors, showcasing the potential of adaptive decoding frameworks in enhancing model robustness.

---

### Conclusion  
BiasAlign provides a systematic framework for evaluating MLLMs' susceptibility to cognitive biases, offering actionable insights into improving ethical reasoning and multimodal integration. Our results underscore the importance of adaptive decoding techniques like AdaFuse in mitigating biases and enhancing alignment with human ethical standards.
