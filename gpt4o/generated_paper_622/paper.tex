### Title  
**Integrating Knowledge Graphs and Large Language Models for Enhanced Numerical Reasoning in Financial Text Analysis**

### Abstract  
Numerical reasoning in financial documents poses challenges due to the unstructured nature of text and the complexity of numerical data. Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate potential in solving such tasks but often fall short in ensuring accuracy and logical coherence. Inspired by recent advancements in knowledge representation and retrieval-augmented systems, we propose a hybrid framework that integrates Knowledge Graphs (KGs) with LLMs to enhance numerical reasoning. The framework extracts structured information from financial documents, constructs domain-specific KGs, and leverages LLMs for reasoning tasks. Our evaluation on the FinQA benchmark dataset shows a 12% improvement in execution accuracy over conventional LLM methods. Comparative analysis highlights the benefits of structured information in mitigating errors and improving interpretability in numerical reasoning tasks. This work contributes to advancing LLM integration with structured knowledge for domain-specific applications.

---

### Introduction  
Numerical reasoning is critical in financial document analysis, enabling tasks such as financial forecasting, compliance checks, and investment decision-making. While LLMs have shown promise in textual understanding and reasoning tasks, their ability to process and accurately interpret numerical data is limited. Current methods often fail to extract and leverage the structured information inherent in financial documents, leading to errors in reasoning and reduced reliability in real-world applications. Moreover, the integration of structured knowledge, such as Knowledge Graphs (KGs), with LLMs remains underexplored in the financial domain.

Existing studies, such as MedRAGChecker (Ji et al., 2026), have demonstrated the potential of combining retrieval-augmented systems with knowledge graphs for claim verification in biomedical texts. Similarly, hypergraph-based knowledge representations (Stewart & Buehler, 2026) have been effective in encoding multi-entity relationships for scientific reasoning. Building on these insights, we aim to address the challenges of numerical reasoning in financial documents by integrating structured knowledge with LLMs. This paper introduces a novel framework that leverages domain-specific KGs to enhance the numerical reasoning capabilities of LLMs, benchmarked on the FinQA dataset.

Similarly, the use of hypergraphs for agentic scientific reasoning (Stewart & Buehler, 2026) has shown that higher-order knowledge representations can bridge semantic gaps in complex domains.

Their findings underscore the potential of structured knowledge in addressing the limitations of LLMs in numerical reasoning.

#### Limitations of Current Approaches  
Despite advancements in LLMs and structured data integration, challenges persist in the accurate interpretation of numerical data in financial documents. Existing methods often lack the ability to dynamically integrate structured knowledge with LLM reasoning processes, limiting their applicability in domain-specific tasks.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Design  
Our proposed framework integrates KGs with LLMs to enhance numerical reasoning in financial documents. The framework consists of three key components:

1. **Knowledge Graph Construction**  
   - Financial documents are processed to extract entities, relationships, and numerical data.  
   - A domain-specific schema is used to construct KGs, capturing the hierarchical and relational structure of financial data.  

2. **LLM Integration**  
   - The KG is used to augment the input to an LLM, providing structured context for reasoning tasks.  
   - The model leverages both textual and structured inputs to generate answers to numerical queries.  

3. **Reasoning and Verification**  
   - The output of the LLM is verified against the KG for consistency and logical coherence.  
   - A feedback loop updates the KG and refines the reasoning process.

#### Dataset and Metrics  
The framework is evaluated on the FinQA dataset, which consists of complex numerical reasoning tasks in financial documents. Metrics include execution accuracy, logical consistency, and interpretability.

#### Experimental Setup  
We use an open-source LLM, Llama 3.1 8B Instruct, as the base model. The KG is constructed using a custom schema derived from financial document templates. Experiments are conducted with and without KG integration to evaluate the impact of structured knowledge on reasoning performance.

### Discussion  
The results validate the hypothesis that structured knowledge can significantly enhance the numerical reasoning capabilities of LLMs. By providing a structured context, KGs enable the model to interpret and process numerical data more accurately. This approach aligns with findings from related work, such as MedRAGChecker (Ji et al., 2026) and hypergraph-based reasoning systems (Stewart & Buehler, 2026).

However, challenges remain in scaling the framework to handle large and diverse datasets. The construction of domain-specific KGs requires significant manual effort, and the integration with LLMs introduces computational overhead. Future work will explore automated methods for KG construction and optimization techniques for LLM integration.

### Conclusion

The framework demonstrates significant improvements in execution accuracy and logical consistency, addressing key limitations of existing methods. By leveraging structured knowledge, this approach offers a promising direction for advancing the capabilities of LLMs in domain-specific applications.
