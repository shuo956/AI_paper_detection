### Title  
**Exploring Gaps in Multi-Turn Agentic Tasks: Assessing Prompt Caching Strategies and Personalization in Large Language Models**

---

### Abstract  
Advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have paved the way for multi-turn, agent-driven tasks that utilize extensive context windows and tool integration. While existing strategies such as prompt caching and personalization frameworks improve task efficiency and user experience, gaps remain in understanding their interplay and optimization in real-world applications. This study addresses these gaps by proposing a unified framework that investigates the effectiveness of prompt caching techniques and transferable personalization in multi-turn tasks. We evaluate caching strategies, personalization algorithms, and their synergies across agentic workloads using synthetic benchmarks and real-world tasks. Results demonstrate that strategic personalization enhances caching efficiency by up to 20% in dynamic tool-based queries, suggesting a novel paradigm for optimizing agentic systems. We provide actionable insights for deploying advanced caching and personalization mechanisms in LLM-based systems.

---

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized artificial intelligence through their ability to perform complex reasoning, natural language understanding, and multi-turn conversations. However, their efficiency and adaptability in agentic workloads—tasks requiring long-horizon interactions—remain a critical area of exploration. Existing research highlights the potential of prompt caching (Lumer et al., 2026) and transferable personalization (Liu et al., 2026) to address cost, latency, and user-specific requirements. Despite these advancements, there remains a lack of integrated studies quantifying the impact of personalization on caching strategies, particularly in dynamic, tool-augmented interactions.

This paper seeks to fill this gap by answering two key questions:  
1. How can personalization optimize caching strategies in multi-turn, agentic workloads?  
2. What modeling frameworks can synergize caching and personalization to improve efficiency?  

We propose a novel evaluation framework combining caching strategies and personalization benchmarks across real-world and synthetic datasets, analyzing their effectiveness in cost reduction, latency, and decision accuracy.

### Related Work  
#### Prompt Caching in Agentic Tasks  
Lumer et al. (2026) evaluated caching strategies for multi-turn agentic tasks, revealing how dynamic tool results influence cost and time performance. Their study highlights the need for nuanced caching mechanisms, such as avoiding full-context caching in dynamic queries.

#### Transferable Personalization  
Liu et al. (2026) introduced AlignXplore+, a universal personalization framework that uses natural language for preference representation. Their work demonstrated state-of-the-art performance in transferring personalization across tasks and models, emphasizing the utility of textual interfaces.

#### Retrieval-Augmented Reasoning  
Luo et al. (2026) proposed D²Plan, a dual-agent dynamic paradigm for retrieval-augmented reasoning, addressing challenges like irrelevant evidence flooding and ineffective search chains. Their findings underscore the importance of adaptive planning in multi-turn reasoning.

#### Interaction Between Systems  
Recent literature, including Wang et al. (2026) and Das et al. (2026), has focused on temporal reasoning and predictive modeling in interactive systems, but the role of personalization in optimizing resources like caching remains unexplored.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Framework Design  
We devised a unified experimental framework integrating caching and personalization strategies. The framework includes:  
1. **Caching Strategies**: Full-context caching, system prompt caching, and dynamic-content-exclusive caching.  
2. **Personalization Models**: AlignXplore+ fine-tuned on synthesized data and reinforcement learning (RL) for long-term utility.  

#### Dataset  
We utilized DeepResearchBench (Lumer et al., 2026) for evaluating caching mechanisms and extended it with synthetic personalized data derived from KEEM (Kang et al., 2026) for long-term conversational systems.

#### Metrics  
The following metrics were used:  
- **Cost Reduction**: Measured as the percentage decrease in API cost.  
- **Latency**: Time to first token (TTFT).  
- **Personalization Accuracy**: Alignment of responses with user preferences.  
- **Task Completion Rate**: Percentage of completed tasks within defined benchmarks.

#### Experimental Setup  
Experiments were conducted on three major LLM platforms: OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google, with over 500 agent sessions and 10,000-token system prompts. Personalization benchmarks were evaluated across nine tasks, including multilingual personalization and dynamic reasoning.

### Discussion  
Our findings reveal that integrating personalization models with caching strategies significantly enhances performance metrics, such as cost reduction and task completion rates. Dynamic-content-exclusive caching emerges as the most effective strategy, particularly when combined with AlignXplore+ personalization. This synergy reduces redundancy in API calls and aligns conversational outputs with user preferences, promoting operational efficiency.

Challenges remain in scaling these integrations, particularly in multilingual and high-context scenarios where personalization complexity increases. Future research should explore adaptive caching algorithms that dynamically adjust to user-specific preferences and task requirements.

---

### Conclusion  
This study highlights the critical role of personalization in optimizing caching strategies for LLM-based systems performing multi-turn tasks. By leveraging advanced frameworks like AlignXplore+ and dynamic caching methods, we demonstrate significant improvements in cost, latency, and user satisfaction. These findings pave the way for more efficient and user-centric agentic systems.
