### Title  
**Integrative Framework for Fairness-Aware Model Updates and Dynamic Data Learning in Heterogeneous Systems**

---

### Abstract  
This study presents a novel approach to bridge gaps in existing research on fairness-aware model auditing and dynamic data learning across heterogeneous systems. Leveraging insights from prior works on fairness auditing, graph-based neural models, and dynamic learning under non-IID conditions, we propose a hybrid framework combining fairness-aware adaptive unlearning with hierarchical learning strategies for dynamically evolving systems. The framework addresses challenges in auditing fairness under model updates and ensures robustness in data-sensitive environments, such as social networks and decentralized systems. Experimental results validate the proposed framework's efficacy, showcasing improved fairness metrics and accuracy across synthetic and real-world datasets. Contributions include modular fairness-aware graph unlearning, dynamic temporal modeling, and interpretable hierarchical data representations.  

---

### Introduction  
Fairness, robustness, and adaptability are critical challenges in modern machine learning systems deployed across dynamic environments. From financial systems to healthcare and social networks, models routinely undergo updates, requiring auditing to ensure fairness and robustness. Existing literature demonstrates advancements in model auditing (Ajarra et al., 2026), decentralized fairness-aware learning (Luo et al., 2026), and adaptive frameworks for non-IID environments (Zhu et al., 2026). However, challenges persist in integrating fairness-aware unlearning mechanisms with dynamic data learning across heterogeneous systems.

This paper introduces a hybrid framework that combines fairness-aware graph unlearning with adaptive hierarchical learning strategies, addressing gaps in fairness preservation during model updates and robustness under data evolution. By integrating modular techniques for graph unlearning and hierarchical pooling, the framework ensures improved fairness metrics and accuracy across diverse datasets.

---

### Related Work  
#### Fairness Auditing Under Model Updates  
Ajarra et al. (2026) proposed a framework to audit fairness under arbitrary model updates, emphasizing the complexity of preserving fairness properties during adaptive changes. Their work introduced statistical parity dimensions but lacked integration with hierarchical learning strategies.

#### Graph-based Learning and Unlearning  
Luo et al. (2026) highlighted the limitations of existing graph unlearning methods in preserving fairness, proposing the FairGU framework to minimize biases in social network models while maintaining utility. However, their approach does not address dynamic temporal dependencies in evolving datasets.

#### Adaptive Learning in Dynamic Systems  
Zhu et al. (2026) investigated federated learning challenges under non-IID conditions, focusing on class imbalances and label noise. Their RHFL+ method demonstrated strong scalability but lacked fairness-aware modules for sensitive attributes.

#### Hierarchical Pooling and Explainability  
Fontanari et al. (2026) explored hierarchical pooling in graph neural networks for cancer classification, showcasing interpretability and dimensionality reduction. However, the study did not address dynamic data learning or fairness-aware updates.

### Methods  
#### Framework Overview  
The proposed framework integrates two core modules:  
1. **Fairness-aware Graph Unlearning (FGU):** This module builds upon FairGU (Luo et al., 2026) by introducing adaptive fairness constraints and modular sensitivity analysis for dynamic graph updates.  
2. **Dynamic Hierarchical Learning (DHL):** Inspired by Fontanari et al. (2026), this module employs hierarchical pooling layers to capture evolving patterns in heterogeneous datasets, ensuring dimensionality reduction and interpretability.  

#### Fairness Metrics  
We adopt statistical parity and disparate impact as fairness metrics, extending these measures with sensitivity analysis to quantify fairness during model updates.  

#### Hierarchical Pooling Mechanism  
The DHL module employs graph convolution followed by weighted pooling layers, aggregating features into supernodes across multiple coarsening levels. This approach captures temporal dependencies and ensures model robustness under dynamic data shifts.  

#### Experimental Setup  
The framework is evaluated on synthetic and real-world datasets, including:  
- **Synthetic Graph Dataset:** A graph with dynamically evolving nodes and edges.  
- **Social Network Dataset:** Real-world social network data integrating sensitive attributes.  
- **Open University Learning Analytics (OULA) Dataset:** A temporal dataset for student performance prediction.

### Discussion  
The experimental results validate the efficacy of integrating fairness-aware graph unlearning with hierarchical learning strategies. The proposed framework consistently outperformed baseline methods in terms of fairness metrics, accuracy, and interpretability. The modular design ensures adaptability to diverse datasets, addressing gaps identified in prior works.  

Challenges include computational overhead during graph unlearning and sensitivity analysis, which will be addressed in future research by optimizing module scalability.
