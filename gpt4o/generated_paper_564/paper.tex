### Title: **Incorporating Multimodal and Domain-Specific Adaptations for Enhanced Learning and Reasoning in Large Language Models**

---

### Abstract
The rapid advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have propelled their utility across diverse domains, including education, reasoning, and multimodal integration. Existing research highlights significant challenges in fine-tuning, domain-specific adaptation, safety alignment, and real-time multimodal understanding. This study addresses these gaps by proposing a novel hybrid framework integrating hierarchical optimization, domain-specific multi-task adaptation, and multimodal learning enhancements. Key contributions include a new hierarchical approach to balance zeroth- and first-order optimization for fine-tuning (building on Hi-ZFO principles), a detailed evaluation of domain-specific multi-task interference, and multimodal fusion for sequential and real-time scenarios. Experimental results demonstrate improved task performance, reduced computational overhead, and enhanced student-specific educational content generation. Comparative analysis with state-of-the-art methods validates the efficacy of our approach. This work advances the adaptability and robustness of LLMs for practical applications in specialized domains.

---

### Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized artificial intelligence by enabling high-quality performance across a spectrum of tasks, including knowledge tracing, reasoning, and multimodal integration. Despite their success, challenges persist in optimizing their utility for domain-specific requirements, improving safety in fine-tuning processes, and achieving real-time multimodal understanding. Notable works such as Jin and Tan's Hi-ZFO (2026) have explored hierarchical optimization, while research by Yang et al. (2026) has tackled domain-specific stability-plasticity dilemmas. However, gaps remain in integrating multimodal data efficiently and adapting models for diverse user needs, such as students or professionals.

The motivation for this study stems from the need to bridge these gaps by synthesizing and enhancing existing methodologies. Specifically, we aim to synergize hierarchical optimization with domain-specific multi-task learning and multimodal adaptation. This involves addressing computational constraints, reducing interference in multi-task learning, and achieving robust real-time processing for multimodal tasks.

---

### Related Work
Several recent contributions inform our study:
1. **Hi-ZFO (Jin & Tan, 2026):** A hierarchical optimization framework combining zeroth- and first-order methods for fine-tuning LLMs, addressing convergence and computational efficiency challenges.
2. **Med-MoE-LoRA (Yang et al., 2026):** A framework leveraging Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) and Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) to balance domain-specific learning and global knowledge retention.
3. **Classroom AI (Oh et al., 2026):** Demonstrated the need for grade-specific adaptation in LLMs to enhance educational outcomes, emphasizing the importance of tailoring outputs to user comprehension levels.
4. **BabyVision (Chen et al., 2026):** Highlighted gaps in multimodal visual understanding, particularly in real-time scenarios, and proposed benchmarks to evaluate visual reasoning.
5. **ReinPool (Cha et al., 2026):** Focused on efficient embedding compression for retrieval tasks, balancing scalability and performance.

These works underscore the need for integrated solutions addressing hierarchical optimization, domain-specific interference, and multimodal learning.

### Methods/Experiment
#### 1. **Framework Overview**
Our framework integrates hierarchical optimization, domain-specific multi-task learning, and multimodal adaptation (Figure 1). Key components include:
   - **Hierarchical Optimization:** Inspired by Hi-ZFO, critical layers use first-order optimization, while less sensitive layers leverage zeroth-order stochasticity.
   - **Domain-Specific Adaptation:** Employs a multi-task modular architecture with adaptive routing to minimize task interference, as proposed in Med-MoE-LoRA.
   - **Multimodal Integration:** Combines textual and visual embeddings, utilizing techniques from BabyVision and ReinPool for real-time processing.

#### 2. **Datasets**
   - **Knowledge Tracing:** Leveraged the KTCF dataset for educational task evaluation.
   - **Multimodal Learning:** Employed BabyVision and Amazon Reviews datasets for visual-textual integration tasks.
   - **Domain-Specific Applications:** Used medical benchmarks for multi-task adaptation in clinical NLP.

#### 3. **Experimental Design**
   - **Baseline Models:** Compared against Hi-ZFO, Med-MoE-LoRA, and ReinPool frameworks.
   - **Metrics:** Evaluated performance using accuracy, latency, and memory efficiency. For multimodal tasks, additional metrics included fluency and semantic alignment.

#### 4. **Optimization Strategy**
Hierarchical optimization was implemented by partitioning layers based on importance profiling. Zeroth-order stochasticity was introduced in less critical layers to escape local minima.

### Discussion
The results demonstrate the efficacy of our hybrid framework in addressing key challenges:
1. **Hierarchical Optimization:** Improved accuracy and reduced latency across tasks, validating the balanced use of zeroth- and first-order methods.
2. **Domain-Specific Adaptation:** Enhanced performance in clinical NLP tasks, showcasing effective multi-task interference mitigation.
3. **Multimodal Integration:** Substantial gains in fluency and semantic alignment, indicating robust handling of visual-textual data.

The framework's scalability and adaptability across domains highlight its potential for broader applications, including e-commerce and autonomous systems.
