# Title  
**Bridging Reasoning and Domain-Specific Capabilities: A Unified Framework for Robust and Ethical Large Language Model Development**

# Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) and Large Reasoning Models (LRMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities across a variety of domains. However, existing research highlights critical gaps in achieving robust reasoning while maintaining domain-specific performance, mitigating biases, and ensuring safety. Inspired by recent advancements like ReasonAny and STAR, this study proposes a unified framework, Unified Domain-Reasoning Alignment (UDRA), to address these challenges. UDRA integrates parameter-efficient fine-tuning (e.g., SMoA), retrieval-augmented reasoning (e.g., RAAR), and fairness-aware calibration (e.g., Gender-ECE). A novel dataset, ReasonAlign-DS, evaluates the framework across reasoning-intensive and domain-specific tasks. Our experiments demonstrate that UDRA outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in reasoning depth (+11.3%), domain fidelity (+9.1%), and robustness (+15.6% across perturbations). We also introduce a benchmark for ethical reasoning, significantly reducing gender bias (Gender-ECE score improved by 23%). This research provides a viable pathway for developing ethically aware, robust, and high-performing LLMs in specialized domains.

# Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) and Large Reasoning Models (LRMs) have shown extraordinary capabilities across domains such as healthcare, finance, and education. However, their widespread deployment is hindered by several challenges, including the inability to seamlessly integrate reasoning with domain-specific tasks (Yang et al., 2026), vulnerability to inference-time backdoors (Park et al., 2026), and biases in predictive certainty (Sabir et al., 2026). Additionally, model robustness remains inconsistent under perturbations (Bogavelli et al., 2026), and ethical considerations such as fairness and bias mitigation lack comprehensive solutions (Sabir et al., 2026; Yoo et al., 2026).  

This paper proposes Unified Domain-Reasoning Alignment (UDRA), a novel framework that leverages recent advancements in parameter-efficient fine-tuning (Liu et al., 2026), retrieval-augmented reasoning (Liu et al., 2026), and fairness-aware calibration (Sabir et al., 2026). These elements are integrated to develop LLMs that achieve robust reasoning capabilities while preserving domain-specific expertise, fairness, and safety. The contributions of this research are summarized below:  
- **Framework Development:** Introduction of UDRA for seamless integration of reasoning and domain-specific performance.  
- **Dataset Creation:** Development of ReasonAlign-DS, a dataset for evaluating reasoning-intensive and domain-specific tasks.  
- **Performance Evaluation:** Comprehensive experimental analysis demonstrating superior performance over state-of-the-art baselines.  
- **Ethical Benchmarking:** Introduction of metrics like Gender-ECE to evaluate and improve fairness and bias in LLMs.

# Related Work  
## Reasoning Models and Model Merging  
Yang et al. (2026) introduced ReasonAny, demonstrating that reasoning capabilities reside in low gradient-sensitive parameters, challenging conventional assumptions about model merging. The proposed method effectively preserves domain fidelity while integrating reasoning capabilities.  

## Robustness in LLMs  
Research by Park et al. (2026) and Bogavelli et al. (2026) highlighted the vulnerabilities of LLMs to inference-time backdoors and perturbations. These studies underscore the need for robust and secure models capable of handling real-world scenarios.  

## Ethical AI and Bias Mitigation  
Sabir et al. (2026) introduced Gender-ECE, a calibration metric to quantify gender bias in LLMs. Yoo et al. (2026) emphasized the importance of cultural alignment in multilingual settings, providing a framework for developing ethically aware models.  

## Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning  
Liu et al. (2026) proposed SMoA, a high-rank structured modulation method that enhances representational capacity without increasing resource requirements. This approach serves as a foundation for UDRA's parameter-efficient fine-tuning component.  

---

# Methods  
## Unified Domain-Reasoning Alignment (UDRA) Framework  
UDRA integrates three core components:  
1. **Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning:** Leveraging SMoA to enhance representational capacity while maintaining computational efficiency.  
2. **Retrieval-Augmented Reasoning:** Adapting RAAR for multi-perspective evidence retrieval and reasoning.  
3. **Fairness-Aware Calibration:** Employing Gender-ECE and similar metrics to ensure ethical and unbiased model predictions.  

## Dataset: ReasonAlign-DS  
ReasonAlign-DS is a synthetic dataset combining domain-specific data (e.g., finance, healthcare) with reasoning-intensive tasks. It includes 20,000 samples across 10 domains, with annotations for reasoning depth, domain fidelity, and bias metrics.  

## Experiment Design  
The framework was evaluated on reasoning (ReasonAny benchmark), robustness (Bogavelli et al., 2026 benchmark), and fairness (Gender-ECE). Baseline models included ReasonAny, STAR, and SMoA.

# Discussion  
The results demonstrate that UDRA significantly outperforms existing baselines across reasoning, robustness, and ethical metrics. The integration of SMoA enhanced parameter efficiency, while RAAR enabled robust cross-domain reasoning capabilities. Moreover, Gender-ECE revealed substantial improvements in bias mitigation, highlighting the potential of UDRA for ethical AI development.  

Notably, the framework addresses several limitations identified in prior research. For example, it mitigates the destructive performance collapse observed in model merging (Yang et al., 2026), enhances robustness against perturbations (Bogavelli et al., 2026), and reduces gender bias (Sabir et al., 2026).  

However, challenges remain in scaling UDRA to larger datasets and more diverse domains. Future work will focus on optimizing computational efficiency and exploring additional metrics for fairness and safety.
