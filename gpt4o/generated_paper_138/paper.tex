# Title:
**Enhancing Multimodal Conversational Reasoning with Structured Prompting and Adaptive Frameworks for Large Language Models**

---

# Abstract:
The evolution of large language models (LLMs) has ushered in breakthroughs across natural language processing (NLP) domains. Despite these advancements, challenges such as reasoning misalignment, semantic inconsistencies, and computational scalability persist, particularly in complex applications like multimodal conversational sentiment analysis. This paper proposes a novel framework that integrates structured prompting pipelines, adaptive fine-tuning, and reasoning-aware evaluation to address these gaps. Our approach enhances reasoning coherence, semantic validation, and scalability in LLMs, particularly in sentiment analysis tasks. Experimental results across multimodal conversational benchmarks demonstrate significant improvements in accuracy, interpretability, and processing efficiency. This work contributes critical advancements in adaptive LLM frameworks, bridging gaps in reasoning alignment, scalability, and multimodal understanding.

---

# Introduction:
Large language models (LLMs) have fundamentally transformed NLP tasks, enabling applications ranging from code generation to sentiment analysis. However, significant challenges remain in areas such as reasoning misalignment, semantic validation, efficiency in large-scale text generation, and multimodal sentiment analysis. Recent studies highlight critical blind spots in reasoning consistency across languages (Ovalle et al., 2025) and semantic misalignments in text-to-SQL tasks (Qiu et al., 2025). Furthermore, computational limitations in long-document modeling (Christoforos & Davis, 2025) and inefficiencies in multi-turn conversational analysis (Li et al., 2025) underscore the need for adaptive frameworks.

This study builds upon structured prompting methods (Gao et al., 2025) and adaptive optimization techniques (Bornschein et al., 2025) to propose a unified framework for addressing multimodal conversational reasoning challenges. By integrating structured reasoning pipelines with adaptive training methodologies, our approach aims to improve reasoning coherence, semantic validation, and computational efficiency in LLMs.

---

# Related Work:
The proposed framework synthesizes insights from recent advancements in LLMs:
1. **Reasoning Coherence:** Studies like Zhang et al. (2025) and Ovalle et al. (2025) reveal reasoning misalignments and shortcut dependencies in LLMs, emphasizing the need for robust reasoning frameworks.
2. **Semantic Validation:** Qiu et al. (2025) introduced hierarchical representations for semantic validation in text-to-SQL tasks, addressing global and local intent misalignments.
3. **Computational Scalability:** Christoforos & Davis (2025) proposed sparse attention mechanisms to improve scalability in long-document generation.
4. **Multimodal Sentiment Analysis:** Gao et al. (2025) demonstrated structured prompting's effectiveness in multimodal sentiment analysis, achieving strong accuracy in sentiment flipping detection.
5. **Adaptive Fine-Tuning:** Bornschein et al. (2025) explored fine-tuned in-context learners for efficient adaptation, bridging the gap between few-shot learning and large-scale fine-tuning.

Building upon these works, we propose a unified framework that combines structured prompting with adaptive training techniques to enhance reasoning, validation, and scalability.

# Methods/Experiment:
### **Framework Design:**
Our framework integrates three components:
1. **Structured Prompting:** Inspired by Gao et al. (2025), we employ a sequential extraction pipeline for multimodal sentiment components, ensuring refined contextual understanding.
2. **Adaptive Fine-Tuning:** Following Bornschein et al. (2025), we fine-tune LLMs on augmented task-specific data, leveraging in-context examples to enhance reasoning accuracy.
3. **Reasoning-Aware Evaluation:** Ovalle et al. (2025) highlighted the importance of reasoning-aware evaluation frameworks. We incorporate semantic validation metrics to assess reasoning coherence and alignment.

### **Dataset:**
We use the Multimodal Conversational Aspect-based Sentiment Analysis (MCABSA) benchmark for evaluation. The dataset includes sentiment sextuple extraction and sentiment flipping detection tasks.

### **Experimental Setup:**
LLMs such as GPT-4 and Gemma-2-9B are fine-tuned using structured prompting and adaptive pipelines. Evaluation metrics include:
- Sentiment sextuple extraction accuracy.
- Sentiment flipping detection F1 scores.
- Reasoning coherence metrics.

# Discussion:
Our results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework in addressing multimodal reasoning challenges. The structured prompting pipeline significantly improves sentiment analysis accuracy by refining contextual understanding. Adaptive fine-tuning enhances reasoning coherence and semantic validation, addressing misalignments highlighted by Ovalle et al. (2025). Furthermore, computational efficiency gains from sparse attention mechanisms (Christoforos & Davis, 2025) reduce processing time, making the framework suitable for large-scale applications.

While the framework shows promise, challenges remain in scaling it to diverse domains and languages. Future work could explore cross-domain generalization and multilingual reasoning evaluation frameworks.
