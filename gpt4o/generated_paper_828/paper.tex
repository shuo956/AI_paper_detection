# Title  
**Dynamic Frameworks for Enhanced Temporal and Logical Reasoning in Large Language Models**

# Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in various domains, from symbolic reasoning to temporal navigation. However, challenges remain in areas such as reasoning alignment across languages, temporal reasoning, and unsupervised post-training optimization. This paper introduces a novel framework that integrates insights from post-training optimization techniques, temporal manifold modeling, and reasoning-specific supervision to enhance LLMs' capabilities in temporal and logical reasoning tasks. By leveraging methods such as Gibbs Initialization with Finite Temperature (GIFT), trajectory-aware intrinsic reasoning supervision (iReasoner), and temporally grounded frameworks like the Time Travel Engine (TTE), we propose a unified model that bridges gaps in reasoning coherence, robustness, and generalization. Experimental results show significant improvements in multilingual reasoning consistency, temporal pattern recognition, and logical reasoning accuracy compared to baseline models. Our framework also demonstrates scalability and adaptability across diverse benchmarks, setting a foundation for future advancements in LLM reasoning capabilities.

# Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing, excelling in tasks such as translation, summarization, and reasoning. Despite these advancements, limitations persist in their ability to navigate complex reasoning scenarios, particularly in multilingual, temporal, and logical domains. Current challenges include:  
1. Optimization mismatches in post-training paradigms, leading to suboptimal reasoning pathways.  
2. Lack of robust temporal modeling to encode and traverse chronological patterns effectively.  
3. Limited reasoning alignment for low-resource languages and underexplored domains.  

This paper addresses these gaps by synthesizing insights from cutting-edge research. We propose a unified framework that integrates post-training optimization (e.g., GIFT), temporal reasoning (e.g., TTE), and reasoning supervision methods (e.g., iReasoner) to enhance LLM capabilities. Our contributions include:  
1. A novel approach to unify temporal and logical reasoning in LLMs.  
2. Enhanced methods for reasoning coherence and robustness across tasks.  
3. Benchmarking results showcasing improvements in multilingual and temporal reasoning tasks.

# Related Work  
Recent research has explored diverse methodologies to address LLM reasoning limitations. Zhao et al. (2026) introduced GIFT, a post-training paradigm that mitigates optimization mismatches and enhances Reinforcement Learning (RL) initialization. An et al. (2026) demonstrated the utility of TTE, a framework that models temporal patterns within LLMs' latent space, enabling fluid navigation of chronological information. Similarly, Sunil et al. (2026) proposed iReasoner, which employs intrinsic reasoning supervision to improve intermediate reasoning steps in multimodal models.  

Other notable contributions include Kim et al.'s (2026) work on reasoning alignment in low-resource languages and Ghimire et al.'s (2026) PRISM framework for post-training without human supervision. These studies highlight the pressing need for unified frameworks that address reasoning challenges across temporal, multilingual, and logical dimensions.

# Methods/Experiment  

### Proposed Framework  
Our framework integrates three core components:  
1. **Post-Training Optimization with GIFT**: Reformulates supervised fine-tuning (SFT) as a finite-temperature Gibbs initialization process, ensuring consistent optimization throughout the training pipeline.  
2. **Temporal Reasoning with TTE**: Introduces a shared chronological manifold to encode and traverse temporal patterns seamlessly.  
3. **Reasoning Supervision with iReasoner**: Enhances intermediate reasoning steps by rewarding coherent chain-of-thought pathways.  

### Experimental Setup  
We evaluated our framework on three benchmarks:  
1. **Multilingual Reasoning**: Assessing logical reasoning accuracy in English, Korean, and Chinese datasets.  
2. **Temporal Navigation**: Evaluating the ability to identify and traverse diachronic patterns in historical text corpora.  
3. **Logical Reasoning**: Testing symbolic translation and logical inference tasks.  

### Baseline Models  
The following models were used for comparison:  
- Standard supervised fine-tuning (SFT).  
- Reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR).  
- Temporal reasoning models based on TTE.  
- Logical reasoning models using symbolic translation.

# Discussion  
The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of our unified framework in addressing reasoning challenges across multilingual, temporal, and logical domains. Key observations include:  
1. **Improved Reasoning Coherence**: GIFT's finite-temperature initialization mitigates optimization mismatches, leading to more consistent reasoning pathways.  
2. **Enhanced Temporal Modeling**: TTE's chronological manifold enables accurate temporal navigation, highlighting its potential for historical and predictive tasks.  
3. **Robust Multilingual Performance**: iReasoner's trajectory-aware supervision aligns reasoning pathways across languages, improving performance in low-resource settings.  

While our framework shows significant advancements, future work should explore its scalability to larger datasets and more complex reasoning tasks.
