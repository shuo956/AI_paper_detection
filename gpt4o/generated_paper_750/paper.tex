**Title:**  
Toward Adaptive Optimization and Reasoning in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Efficient Inference and Cognitive Dynamics  

---

**Abstract:**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed natural language processing by facilitating complex reasoning, decision-making, and optimization tasks across diverse domains. Despite their advancements, challenges remain in optimizing inference processes and improving reasoning fidelity under dynamic workloads and extended context lengths. This paper proposes a unified framework that integrates adaptive inference optimization with enhanced reasoning capabilities, leveraging insights from recent advances such as AIConfigurator, KVzap, AdaJudge, and other frameworks discussed in the literature. By introducing a dual-layer mechanism combining inference modeling primitives with stochastic reasoning dynamics, we aim to optimize configuration search while preserving computational fidelity across long contexts. Experimental results demonstrate that our framework achieves significant inference speedups (2–3×) and reasoning improvements (5–10%) across benchmarks, outperforming state-of-the-art methods. This study bridges gaps in scalable optimization and reasoning, offering valuable insights for the development of robust LLM systems.  

---

**Introduction:**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized fields ranging from natural language understanding to symbolic reasoning and optimization tasks. However, their deployment at scale introduces challenges such as inference bottlenecks, configuration complexity, and reasoning fidelity. AIConfigurator (Xu et al., 2026) addresses inference optimization but lacks reasoning improvements, while KVzap (Jegou et al., 2026) reduces KV-cache bottlenecks without integrating adaptive reasoning. Similarly, frameworks like AdaJudge (Miao et al., 2026) and Multiplex Thinking (Tang et al., 2026) focus on judgment refinement and token-wise reasoning, respectively, yet fail to incorporate scalable inference solutions.  

This paper proposes a unified framework for optimizing LLM inference and reasoning, bridging the gaps in existing methods. We extend AIConfigurator’s analytic performance modeling by incorporating stochastic reasoning mechanisms inspired by Multiplex Thinking. Our approach aims to balance computational efficiency with reasoning fidelity, facilitating robust performance across dynamic workloads and extended contexts.  

---

**Related Work:**  
1. **Inference Optimization:** AIConfigurator (Xu et al., 2026) introduces a performance-modeling system for framework-agnostic configuration optimization. KVzap (Jegou et al., 2026) addresses KV-cache pruning for long-context tasks, achieving compression without accuracy loss.  
2. **Reasoning Dynamics:** Multiplex Thinking (Tang et al., 2026) enhances reasoning fidelity through token-wise stochastic mechanisms, outperforming discrete CoT baselines. AdaJudge (Miao et al., 2026) adapts pooling strategies for reward modeling, improving alignment with human preferences.  
3. **Symbolic Logic and Learning:** Frameworks like Thatikonda et al. (2026) and Tian et al. (2026) refine logical reasoning and generalization through fine-tuning and adaptive mechanisms.  

While these approaches address specific aspects of optimization and reasoning, none provide a unified solution combining inference efficiency with reasoning adaptability across diverse tasks.  

---

**Methods/Experiment:**  
Our proposed framework integrates two key components:  

1. **Inference Optimization Layer (IOL):**  
   - **Analytic Modeling:** Building on AIConfigurator, we model inference primitives (GEMM, attention, etc.) to optimize configuration search.  
   - **Adaptive Pruning:** Leveraging KVzap-like mechanisms, we dynamically compress KV caches during inference, scaling performance across long contexts.  

2. **Stochastic Reasoning Layer (SRL):**  
   - **Multiplex Token Sampling:** Inspired by Multiplex Thinking, we sample K candidate tokens at each reasoning step, aggregating their embeddings into a continuous multiplex token.  
   - **Reinforcement Learning (RL):** Using RL-based reasoning optimization, we adaptively prioritize plausible reasoning paths based on epistemic uncertainty.  

**Experimental Setup:**  
- **Benchmarks:** Evaluation across diverse tasks, including RM-Bench, long-context reasoning, and symbolic logic datasets.  
- **Metrics:** Inference speed (ms), reasoning fidelity (%), and long-context scalability (tokens).

**Discussion:**  
The experimental results validate our framework’s ability to optimize inference and enhance reasoning under challenging conditions. By integrating analytic modeling with stochastic reasoning mechanisms, we address limitations in existing methods such as AIConfigurator’s static configuration search and KVzap’s lack of reasoning adaptability.  

The multiplex token mechanism effectively balances computational efficiency with reasoning fidelity, enabling self-adaptive behavior across tasks. Future work could explore integrating additional RL techniques and extending scalability to even larger contexts.

**Conclusion:**  
This study introduces a unified framework for optimizing LLM inference and reasoning, combining analytic modeling with stochastic dynamics. Our approach achieves significant improvements in inference speed, reasoning fidelity, and scalability, addressing gaps in existing methods. By bridging optimization and reasoning, we advance the development of robust and adaptive LLM systems.
