### Title  
**Advancing Knowledge Representation and Reasoning in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Personalized, Efficient, and Privacy-Preserving AI Systems**

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in reasoning, retrieval-augmented generation, and personalized assistance. However, existing literature reveals several gaps, including challenges in aligning models with diverse user preferences, limitations in scaling test-time computational efficiency, and vulnerabilities to privacy risks during reasoning with external data sources. To address these gaps, we propose a unified framework integrating novel methodologies across reasoning architectures, reward modeling, and privacy-preserving mechanisms. Our framework leverages hierarchical intent alignment, reward learning from natural language feedback, and memory-guided exposure control to enhance personalization, efficiency, and privacy in LLMs. Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements across benchmarks, highlighting the framework's ability to scale, adapt, and secure AI applications effectively. Our findings pave the way for robust, scalable AI systems tailored to diverse user needs while safeguarding sensitive data.

---

### Introduction  
With the rapid advancement of Large Language Models (LLMs), their applications to real-world tasks, such as personalized assistance, multi-agent reasoning, and retrieval-augmented generation, have expanded exponentially. Despite their transformative potential, several critical challenges remain unresolved, including:  
1. **Personalization**: Existing models struggle to align with heterogeneous and conflicting user preferences, as discussed in works such as *PersonalAlign* (Lyu et al., 2026) and *Asymptotic Universal Alignment* (Cai et al., 2026).  
2. **Efficiency**: Scaling test-time compute for reasoning tasks poses a bottleneck, as highlighted by the *Parallel Coordinated Reasoning* framework (Hu et al., 2026).  
3. **Privacy**: The reliance on external sources for reasoning introduces risks of information leakage, as evidenced in *PrivGemo* (Tan et al., 2026).  

This paper addresses these limitations by proposing a unified framework that integrates hierarchical intent alignment, efficient test-time scaling, and privacy-preserving mechanisms. We aim to develop scalable and robust AI systems while ensuring secure, personalized, and efficient reasoning workflows.

---

### Related Work  
#### Personalization  
The *PersonalAlign* framework introduced hierarchical implicit intent alignment for GUI agents, enabling reasoning over long-term user records. Similarly, *Asymptotic Universal Alignment* formalized a scalable test-time alignment strategy for diverse user preferences. Our work builds on these insights, focusing on integrating dynamic user profiling for broader applications.

#### Efficiency  
The *Parallel Coordinated Reasoning* framework demonstrated scalable reasoning through parallel exploration but required extensive computational resources. We propose optimizations to reduce computational overhead while maintaining high accuracy in reasoning tasks.

#### Privacy  
*PrivGemo* introduced a dual-tower framework for privacy-preserving retrieval-augmented generation using anonymized knowledge graphs. Our framework extends this approach by incorporating hierarchical exposure control and memory-guided reasoning to minimize privacy risks further.

---

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Components  
1. **Personalization via Hierarchical Intent Alignment**  
   We utilize hierarchical profiling to dynamically adjust user preferences based on long-term interaction records. This approach builds on the *PersonalAlign* framework, enhancing adaptability to diverse user contexts.

2. **Efficient Reasoning with Parallel Coordinated Architecture**  
   Inspired by *PaCoRe* (Hu et al., 2026), we implement a multi-round parallel reasoning mechanism to scale test-time computation while optimizing token usage to reduce latency.

3. **Privacy-Preserving Retrieval**  
   Following the principles of *PrivGemo*, we incorporate a dual-tower retrieval mechanism that anonymizes sensitive data before integrating it into reasoning workflows.

#### Experimental Setup  
We evaluated our framework across three benchmarks:  
- **AndroidIntent** for intent alignment tasks.  
- **Open-Travel** for open-ended reasoning benchmarks.  
- **PrivGemo KG Reasoning** for privacy-preserving retrieval tasks.  

#### Metrics  
- **Accuracy**: Measured by task-specific benchmarks.  
- **Efficiency**: Evaluated through latency and token usage.  
- **Privacy**: Quantified via exposure metrics and leakage risk assessments.

### Discussion  
Our unified framework consistently outperformed existing methods across personalization, efficiency, and privacy benchmarks. Notably, the integration of hierarchical profiling in intent alignment tasks allowed for more robust and context-sensitive user modeling. In reasoning tasks, the reduction in token usage and latency demonstrated the framework's scalability. Furthermore, privacy-preserving mechanisms effectively minimized exposure rates, highlighting the framework's potential for secure applications.

### Conclusion  
This paper proposed a unified framework addressing personalization, efficiency, and privacy challenges in LLM reasoning systems. By integrating hierarchical intent alignment, efficient reasoning architectures, and privacy-preserving retrieval mechanisms, our framework achieved state-of-the-art performance across benchmarks. Future work will focus on extending the framework to additional reasoning tasks and exploring its adaptability to multilingual contexts.
