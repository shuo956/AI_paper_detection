### Title  
**Integrating Compositional Neural Operators and Multi-Objective Optimization for Advanced Analysis of Parametric PDEs**

---

### Abstract  
Partial differential equations (PDEs) underpin numerous scientific and engineering problems, yet their computational solutions remain challenging, especially under multi-objective settings involving diverse parameters and constraints. While existing approaches, such as Compositional Neural Operators (CompNO) and advanced optimization platforms like DataScribe, have demonstrated significant performance in parametric PDEs and multi-objective trade-offs, they remain disjointed in application. This paper proposes a novel integration of CompNO and multi-objective optimization frameworks using DataScribe's closed-loop propose-measure-learn paradigm. By combining CompNO's compositional neural operator framework with DataScribe's Bayesian optimization capabilities, we aim to enhance the exploration of parametric PDE solutions while addressing sustainability and performance objectives. Experimental validation on Burgers' equation, convection-diffusion systems, and multi-objective benchmarks demonstrates substantial improvements in prediction accuracy, optimization efficiency, and computational scalability. This study bridges the gap between PDE modeling and multi-objective optimization, offering a robust pathway for scalable and interpretable scientific modeling.

---

### Introduction  
Partial differential equations (PDEs) are fundamental to modeling dynamic systems in physics, engineering, and other scientific domains. However, solving PDEs computationally is resource-intensive, particularly when simulations span diverse parameter spaces or require optimization across multiple objectives. Recent advancements such as Compositional Neural Operators (CompNO) have introduced modular frameworks for parametric PDE analysis, enabling efficient surrogate modeling through specialized differential operator blocks. Simultaneously, platforms like DataScribe have revolutionized multi-objective optimization workflows by embedding AI-native capabilities into scientific infrastructures.

Despite these advancements, a critical gap persists in aligning the strengths of PDE-specific neural operators with robust optimization frameworks. Multi-objective scenarios, common in engineering applications, demand solutions that consider trade-offs among performance, sustainability, and computational cost. This study seeks to integrate CompNO's compositional framework with DataScribe's optimization capabilities to address this gap, enabling scalable and interpretable analysis of parametric PDEs under multi-objective constraints.

---

### Related Work  
#### Compositional Neural Operators (CompNO)  
The CompNO framework, introduced by Hmida et al. (2026), leverages parametric Fourier neural operators and compositionality to model fundamental differential operators such as convection and diffusion. Its ability to enforce boundary conditions with high precision and generalize across varied physical systems has established its utility in PDE modeling.

#### DataScribe Optimization Framework  
DataScribe, proposed by Singh et al. (2026), integrates Bayesian optimization and FAIR-compliant metadata capture to enable closed-loop propose-measure-learn workflows. Its application in materials design has demonstrated robust multi-objective optimization capabilities, making it suitable for complex scientific problems with diverse constraints.

#### Multi-Objective Modeling and Sparse Data  
Comlek et al. (2026) highlighted the challenges of sparse high-fidelity data in multi-objective engineering applications and proposed Multi-Task Gaussian Processes (MTGP) as a solution. Integrating such frameworks with neural operator-based models could further enhance predictive accuracy and optimization efficiency.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Integration Framework  
The integration framework leverages CompNO to model parametric PDEs and DataScribe for multi-objective optimization. The workflow involves:  
1. **Foundation Block Construction:** PDEs are decomposed into modular components (convection, diffusion, etc.), modeled using Fourier neural operators.  
2. **Optimization Pipeline:** DataScribe's Bayesian optimization engine is employed to iteratively refine parameters across multiple objectives, including computational cost and prediction accuracy.  
3. **Boundary Condition Enforcement:** Dirichlet constraints are enforced using specialized neural operators.  
4. **Validation:** The integrated framework is validated on benchmarks including Burgers' equation and convection-diffusion systems.

#### Experimental Setup  
The experiments include:  
- **Benchmark Tests:** Linear and nonlinear PDEs from PDEBench.  
- **Multi-Objective Optimization Scenarios:** Trade-offs among prediction accuracy, computational efficiency, and sustainability metrics.  
- **Comparison Baselines:** PFNO, PDEFormer, and MTGP frameworks.

### Discussion  
The integration of CompNO with DataScribe demonstrates marked improvements in accuracy and efficiency. The compositional neural operator framework effectively models complex PDEs while DataScribe's Bayesian optimization enables adaptive tuning across multi-objective trade spaces. Notably, the framework maintains exact boundary conditions, critical for physical interpretability, and offers robust generalization across varied parameter regimes. However, computational overhead associated with iterative optimization remains a limitation, necessitating further exploration into scalable parallelization strategies.

---

### Conclusion  
This study presents an integrated approach combining Compositional Neural Operators and multi-objective optimization frameworks for advanced parametric PDE analysis. By bridging modular PDE modeling with adaptive optimization, the proposed framework achieves superior accuracy, efficiency, and scalability. Future work will extend this integration to dynamic systems and real-time optimization scenarios.
