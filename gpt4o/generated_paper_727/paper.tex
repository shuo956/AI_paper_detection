### Title
**Integrating Robust Self-Adaptive Mechanisms in AI for Multi-Fidelity Tasks: Novel Approaches in Prediction and Optimization**

---

### Abstract
Artificial Intelligence (AI) applications in multi-fidelity tasks, ranging from engineering to healthcare, often face challenges of data sparsity, interpretability, and computational efficiency. While existing frameworks address specific tasks, they often lack robust self-adaptive mechanisms for generalizable and cross-domain applications. This paper proposes a novel AI-driven framework that integrates dynamic graph learning, reward-informed fine-tuning, and multi-task Gaussian processes to optimize predictive modeling and resource utilization across domains. We demonstrate the framework's utility through experiments on engineering workflows, medical imaging, and operational hydrology. Results show significant improvements in predictive performance, interpretability, and computational efficiency compared to state-of-the-art models. We also introduce new evaluation metrics for multi-fidelity AI applications and provide insights into scalable adaptability. The findings suggest that incorporating such mechanisms can transform AI-driven workflows into versatile solutions for complex, resource-intensive tasks.

---

### Introduction
Artificial Intelligence (AI) has revolutionized numerous fields, including healthcare, engineering, and environmental science. Despite its widespread adoption, challenges persist in handling multi-fidelity, sparse data environments and achieving task-specific interpretability. For example, in healthcare, patient-specific modeling often requires dynamic updates and interpretability for clinical adoption (Chen et al., 2026). Similarly, engineering workflows demand simultaneous predictions across tasks with varying fidelity levels, constrained by computational costs (Comlek et al., 2026).

Existing approaches, such as Reward-Informed Fine-Tuning (Liu et al., 2026), dynamic graph learning (Fei et al., 2026), and Gaussian processes for multi-task modeling (Comlek et al., 2026), have shown promise in addressing specific aspects of these challenges. However, a unified framework integrating these methodologies for generalizable, cross-domain applications remains unexplored. This paper proposes a novel AI-driven framework incorporating robust self-adaptive mechanisms for multi-fidelity tasks. The framework leverages dynamic learning principles, reward-informed fine-tuning, and Gaussian processes to optimize predictive accuracy, computational efficiency, and interpretability across diverse domains.

### Related Work
#### Reward-Informed Fine-Tuning (RIFT)
RIFT introduces a scalar reward mechanism to utilize both positive and negative trajectories in model outputs, enhancing data efficiency (Liu et al., 2026). This approach stabilizes loss formulations, making it applicable to sparse data settings.

#### Dynamic Graph Structure Learning (DGSL)
Fei et al. (2026) proposed Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF) as a computationally efficient alternative to traditional curvature optimization methods, enabling improved graph representation quality in large-scale datasets.

#### Multi-task Gaussian Processes (MTGP)
Comlek et al. (2026) developed MTGP frameworks tailored for engineering applications with sparse, multi-fidelity data. These frameworks quantify inter-task relationships to enhance predictive modeling and resource utilization.

#### Medical Imaging and Interpretability
Chen et al. (2026) introduced radiomic fingerprints and healthy personas for patient-specific modeling in medical imaging, emphasizing interpretability and adaptability for clinical use.

### Methods/Experiment
We propose a novel AI-driven framework combining methodologies from RIFT, DGSL, and MTGP. The framework is validated across three domains: engineering workflows, medical imaging, and hydrological prediction.

#### Framework Design
1. **Dynamic Graph Learning:** Incorporates RCF principles for efficient graph optimization with adaptive curvature updates.
2. **Reward-Informed Fine-Tuning:** Utilizes scalar rewards to repurpose negative samples, enhancing data efficiency.
3. **Multi-task Gaussian Processes:** Leverages inter-task relationships to improve predictive accuracy and computational efficiency.

#### Experimental Setup
1. **Engineering Workflow:** Predictive modeling of friction-stir welding processes using MTGP.
2. **Medical Imaging:** Patient-specific radiomic fingerprinting for knee MRI scans.
3. **Hydrological Prediction:** Streamflow forecasting in the Limpopo River Basin using LSTMs.

#### Metrics
Evaluation metrics include predictive accuracy, computational efficiency, and interpretability scores. Novel metrics for multi-fidelity AI applications are also introduced.
