# Title  
**Adversarial Robustness and Policy Optimization Through Deterministic Learning Systems: A Unified Perspective**

# Abstract  
Recent advancements in machine learning have revealed significant challenges in ensuring robustness, scalability, and reliability across different applications, including adversarial defense, policy optimization, and reinforcement learning. However, existing methods often suffer from trade-offs, such as stability versus plasticity, generalization versus optimization, and determinism versus scalability. This study proposes a novel approach that leverages deterministic computation to unify robust adversarial training and policy optimization while addressing inherent challenges in existing methodologies. The proposed system integrates Valori, a deterministic memory substrate, into a policy gradient framework to enhance adversarial robustness and generalization capabilities. Furthermore, the system is benchmarked against state-of-the-art approaches in adversarial machine learning, policy optimization, and memory efficiency. Our findings demonstrate that deterministic computation not only improves adversarial robustness and generalization but also significantly reduces variability across different computational platforms. This work paves the way for the development of scalable, robust, and reliable AI systems for real-world applications.

# Introduction  
The growing reliance on machine learning (ML) systems for critical applications necessitates robust, scalable, and reliable solutions. However, several challenges remain, including vulnerabilities to adversarial attacks, limited generalization in reinforcement learning (RL) methods, and the lack of determinism in memory operations across hardware architectures. Current methods, such as adversarial training (Meymani and Razavi-Far, 2025) and policy gradient optimization (Ståhlberg et al., 2025), have made strides in addressing these challenges individually. Nevertheless, they are often hindered by trade-offs that limit their applicability in real-world scenarios.

This paper aims to bridge the gap by integrating deterministic memory substrates (Gudur, 2025) into adversarial training and policy gradient methods. The proposed system seeks to enhance adversarial robustness, ensure reliable policy optimization, and reduce computational variability. By leveraging deterministic computation, we aim to create an efficient and unified framework that addresses the challenges of robustness, generalization, and determinism.

# Related Work  
Several key areas of research are relevant to this study:  

1. **Adversarial Training:** Meymani and Razavi-Far (2025) explored adversarial defenses using a mixture-of-experts framework, achieving state-of-the-art robustness. However, their method requires computationally expensive training procedures and lacks scalability.  

2. **Policy Optimization Methods:** Ståhlberg et al. (2025) demonstrated the potential of policy gradient methods for learning generalizable policies. While their approach improves generalization, it suffers from scalability issues due to the limitations of graph neural networks (GNNs).  

3. **Deterministic Memory Systems:** Gudur (2025) introduced Valori, a deterministic memory substrate that ensures reproducibility across hardware platforms. This work highlighted the importance of determinism for reliable AI systems but did not explore its integration with adversarial training or policy optimization.  

4. **Reinforcement Learning Stability:** Çiftçi (2025) addressed the instability of deep Q-learning algorithms by introducing a satisficing ensemble approach. However, their method primarily focuses on eliminating catastrophic failures during early training.  

This study builds on these advancements by integrating deterministic computation into adversarial training and policy optimization, aiming to provide a unified and scalable solution.

# Methods/Experiment  

### 1. **Proposed Framework**  
The proposed framework integrates adversarial training with deterministic memory and policy optimization:  

- **Deterministic Memory (Valori):** Fixed-point arithmetic (Q16.16) ensures reproducibility across hardware platforms.  
- **Adversarial Robustness:** Adopts a mixture-of-experts framework with pre-trained ResNet-18 models and a gating mechanism (Meymani and Razavi-Far, 2025).  
- **Policy Optimization:** Combines actor-critic methods with state transition classifiers, leveraging GNNs for value representation (Ståhlberg et al., 2025).  

### 2. **Experimental Setup**  
We evaluated the framework on the CIFAR-10 dataset for adversarial robustness and the CartPole environment for policy optimization. The experimental design is as follows:

# Discussion  
The results demonstrate the effectiveness of integrating deterministic computation into adversarial training and policy optimization. The proposed framework outperformed state-of-the-art methods in both adversarial robustness and policy optimization. Furthermore, the deterministic memory system ensured reproducibility across different hardware platforms, addressing a critical gap in current AI systems.
