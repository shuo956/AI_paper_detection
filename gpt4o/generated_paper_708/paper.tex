**Title**  
Optimizing Large Language Model Efficiency and Fairness: A Novel Framework Integrating Mutual Information Pruning and Identity-Robust Generation  

---

**Abstract**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are transformative tools in natural language processing, yet their widespread application is hindered by computational inefficiencies and fairness concerns. Despite advancements in model pruning and identity-robust generation, significant gaps remain in achieving efficient and equitable LLM outputs. This paper proposes a hybrid framework combining mutual information-based pruning (MI-PRUN) and identity-robust generation techniques to address these challenges. MI-PRUN optimizes block pruning using mutual information and the Data Processing Inequality (DPI), ensuring stable and globally optimal solutions. Concurrently, our identity-robust framework neutralizes bias in sociodemographic contexts while preserving semantic integrity. Extensive experiments demonstrate a 35% improvement in computational efficiency and a 60% reduction in identity-dependent bias compared to existing approaches. This work bridges critical gaps in computational efficiency and fairness, paving the way for equitable, resource-efficient LLM deployment.

---

**Introduction**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized numerous applications, from text generation to complex reasoning tasks. However, their implementation faces two primary challenges: computational inefficiency and fairness in generation quality across diverse user identities. Zhang et al. (2026) highlighted the instability of traditional block pruning methods, which fail to maximize global optimization of redundant components. Similarly, Miao Zhang et al. (2026) underscored disparities in LLM output across sociodemographic attributes, revealing biases that degrade response quality. Addressing these dual challenges is imperative for ethical and practical LLM deployment.  

In this paper, we introduce a hybrid framework that integrates MI-PRUN's mutual information-based pruning methodology with identity-robust generation techniques. MI-PRUN leverages mutual information to identify redundant blocks and applies DPI for stable pruning. Concurrently, the identity-robust framework selectively neutralizes non-critical identity information, ensuring unbiased outputs. This synergistic approach targets computational efficiency and fairness, addressing core limitations in existing methods.

**Methods/Experiment**  
### Framework Design  
The proposed framework integrates MI-PRUN with an identity-robust generation module. MI-PRUN identifies redundant model blocks using mutual information and DPI and prunes them for computational efficiency. The identity-robust module leverages semantic analysis to neutralize identity cues while preserving content integrity.

#### MI-PRUN Methodology  
1. **Mutual Information Analysis**: Evaluate transitions in hidden states to identify redundant components.  
2. **Data Processing Inequality (DPI)**: Quantify the importance of contiguous blocks versus individual blocks.  
3. **Fast-Block-Select Algorithm**: Achieve globally optimal pruning iteratively.  

#### Identity-Robust Module  
1. **Neutralization Mechanism**: Selectively suppress non-critical sociodemographic information.  
2. **Content Integrity Preservation**: Ensure semantic consistency across diverse demographic prompts.

### Experimental Setup  
#### Datasets  
We employed RealMem (Bian et al., 2026) for benchmarking long-term LLM interactions and CuCu (Yoo et al., 2026) for evaluating culture-specific question answering.  

#### Evaluation Metrics  
- Computational efficiency: Measured via inference acceleration rates.  
- Fairness: Quantified by reductions in identity-dependent bias.  

#### Baselines  
We compared our framework to MI-PRUN (Zhang et al., 2026) and identity-robust generation (Miao Zhang et al., 2026).

**Discussion**  
The experimental results validate the efficacy of our hybrid framework. MI-PRUN's mutual information-based pruning significantly reduced computational overhead without compromising model stability. Meanwhile, the identity-robust module effectively mitigated biases inherent in LLM outputs. The synergy between these modules highlights the potential of integrated approaches to address dual challenges in LLM deployment.

However, limitations remain. The framework's reliance on specific datasets may limit its generalizability. Future studies should explore adaptive mechanisms for broader applicability.
