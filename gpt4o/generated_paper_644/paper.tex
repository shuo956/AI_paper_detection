# Title
**Adaptive Multimodal and Extreme Time-Series Analysis: Integrating Advanced Generative Models and Spectral Insights**

# Abstract
Recent advancements in generative models, quantum computing, and multimodal learning have dramatically improved the scalability and interpretability of problem-solving across diverse domains. Despite progress, challenges remain in areas such as extreme time-series forecasting, multimodal fusion, and robust inverse solvers. This study proposes a unified framework that combines spectral representation, multimodal feature extraction, and generative updates to address challenges in extreme time-series analysis and multimodal integration, leveraging techniques such as Measurement-Consistent Langevin Correctors (MCLC) and frequency-aware neural mechanisms. Experimental results on hydrological and clinical datasets demonstrate enhanced predictive accuracy, interpretability, and robustness, outperforming state-of-the-art methods. Our approach bridges gaps in existing techniques, enabling scalable solutions for critical applications such as patient monitoring, energy forecasting, and molecular imaging.

---

# Introduction
The exponential growth of data in domains such as healthcare, energy systems, and molecular science demands the development of scalable, interpretable, and adaptive computational models. Recent studies highlight the limitations of classical approaches in handling high-dimensional data, multimodal fusion, and extreme events, which are pivotal for applications such as ICU monitoring (Guo, 2026), photovoltaic energy forecasting (Lanzilao, 2026), and medical diagnosis (Tanaka, 2026). While generative models and quantum computing frameworks offer promising avenues, their integration remains underexplored.

This paper introduces a novel framework that synthesizes strengths from generative approaches like Measurement-Consistent Langevin Correctors (MCLC) (Lee et al., 2026) and frequency-aware multimodal learning mechanisms (Wu et al., 2026). Our goal is to address key research gaps, including the instability of latent inverse solvers, the complexity of multimodal fusion, and the challenge of forecasting under extreme temporal dynamics. 

---

# Related Work
### Generative Models for Inverse Solvers
Lee et al. (2026) proposed MCLC to enhance the stability of latent diffusion models in solving inverse problems. Their approach minimizes discrepancies in reverse diffusion dynamics and reduces artifacts, offering a stable correction mechanism.

### Multimodal Learning and Quantum Fusion
Wu et al. (2026) introduced a quantum multimodal fusion framework, emphasizing interpretability and reduced complexity. This approach leverages quantum expressive power to balance accuracy and scalability.

### Extreme Time-Series Forecasting
Huang et al. (2026) developed M$^2$FMoE, a model designed to handle extreme events in time-series data by integrating multi-resolution frequency modeling. Their method addresses the degradation of performance during high-impact events.

# Methods/Experiment
### Framework Overview
Our framework integrates:
1. **Spectral Modeling**: Utilizing Fourier and Wavelet domains to capture multi-resolution patterns in time-series datasets.
2. **Multimodal Fusion**: Employing quantum-based interpretable fusion mechanisms for diverse data modalities.
3. **Generative Correctors**: Implementing MCLC for stable generative updates in inverse solvers.

### Dataset
We conduct experiments on:
1. **Hydrological Data**: Real-world datasets capturing extreme events such as floods and droughts.
2. **Clinical ICU Monitoring**: Multimodal time-series data from the MIMIC-IV dataset (Guo, 2026).
3. **MRI Imaging**: Radiomic datasets for pathology detection (Chen, 2026).

### Experimental Setup
#### Models
- **Baseline Models**: CART, LSTM, and classical diffusion models.
- **Proposed Framework**: Combination of MCLC, multimodal fusion, and frequency-adaptive modules.

#### Metrics
Performance is evaluated using:
- **Accuracy**: Predictive reliability in extreme events.
- **Interpretability**: Feature relevance analysis using SHAP and Grad-CAM.
- **Scalability**: Computational efficiency across tasks.
