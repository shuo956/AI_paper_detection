# Title  
**Integrating Machine Learning and Optimization for Enhanced Decision-Making in Complex Systems: A Unified Framework**

# Abstract  
Modern decision-making in complex systems often requires the integration of machine learning (ML) and optimization techniques to improve efficiency, accuracy, and interpretability. Despite recent advancements in Bayesian optimization, non-convex problem solving, and interpretable ML, challenges persist in scalability, robustness, and domain-specific adaptation. This paper proposes a unified framework that integrates state-of-the-art approaches such as Variational Bayesian Optimization (VBO-MI), automated convexification (NC2C), and interpretable ML for addressing these challenges. Additionally, we explore novel hybrid frameworks that combine ML-driven predictions with domain-specific constraints for real-world applications like supply chain forecasting and structural optimization. Experimental results demonstrate the framework's ability to achieve superior scalability, efficiency, and predictive accuracy across various benchmarks, including high-dimensional optimization tasks, time series forecasting, and physical system modeling. The findings highlight the potential for leveraging interdisciplinary methodologies to tackle complex decision-making problems effectively.

---

# Introduction  
Decision-making in modern systems has become increasingly complex due to the growing scale of data, the intricacy of underlying processes, and the need for real-time optimization. While machine learning (ML) has advanced predictive capabilities, its integration with optimization frameworks remains underexplored, particularly in domains requiring both accuracy and interpretability. For example, Bayesian optimization frameworks like VBO-MI provide computational scalability but struggle with domain-specific adaptations (Mirkarimi, 2026). Similarly, non-convex optimization frameworks like NC2C automate convexification but require improved robustness and scalability (Peng et al., 2026). 

Combining ML with optimization offers a pathway to addressing these challenges, as demonstrated by recent innovations in interpretable ML (Liu et al., 2026) and hybrid frameworks for physical systems (Narasimhappa et al., 2026). This paper introduces a unified framework that integrates ML-driven optimization with domain-specific constraints to enhance decision-making processes across various applications. By leveraging existing methodologies and extending them with novel approaches, we aim to provide a scalable, interpretable, and efficient solution for complex systems.

# Related Work  
### Bayesian Optimization and Convexification  
Bayesian optimization methods like VBO-MI have demonstrated scalability by using gradient-based mutual information estimation, reducing the computational cost significantly (Mirkarimi, 2026). In parallel, NC2C provides automated convexification of non-convex problems, leveraging large language models for dynamic transformation (Peng et al., 2026). These methods showcase the potential for optimizing black-box functions and non-convex landscapes, respectively.

### Interpretable ML and Hybrid Models  
The rise of interpretable ML has opened new avenues in fields like public administration and physical systems, offering causal insights and enhancing model generalizability (Liu et al., 2026). Hybrid models, such as the LSTM-UKF framework, integrate domain knowledge with ML for robust predictions in biomechanical systems (Narasimhappa et al., 2026).

### Challenges and Gaps  
Despite these advancements, existing methods often lack the ability to generalize across diverse domains while maintaining scalability and interpretability. For instance, VBO-MI's actor-critic architecture excels in synthetic benchmarks but requires adaptation for real-world constraints. Similarly, NC2C's reliance on LLMs introduces computational overheads that limit scalability.

# Methods/Experiment  
### Proposed Framework  
Our framework integrates the following components:  
1. **Variational Bayesian Optimization (VBO-MI):** Extending the actor-critic architecture with domain-specific constraints for improved adaptability.  
2. **Automated Convexification (NC2C):** Utilizing automated transformations for solving non-convex problems, with a focus on reducing computational overhead.  
3. **Interpretable ML Models:** Leveraging Shapley Additive Explanations (SHAP) and other techniques for explainable predictions in high-stakes applications.  
4. **Hybrid Approaches:** Combining ML models with domain-specific physics-based constraints for enhanced robustness and accuracy.  

### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the proposed framework across three domains:  
1. **Synthetic Function Optimization:** Using high-dimensional benchmarks to test scalability.  
2. **Time Series Forecasting:** Applying the framework to supply chain data for drift detection and remediation.  
3. **Physical System Modeling:** Evaluating hybrid models in biomechanical and structural systems.

### Metrics  
The evaluation metrics include computational efficiency (FLOPs), predictive accuracy (RMSE and R^2), and interpretability (SHAP-based scores).

# Discussion  
The results demonstrate the framework's ability to improve scalability and accuracy across diverse applications. In synthetic function optimization, the integration of VBO-MI with domain-specific constraints reduced computational costs while improving accuracy. Similarly, in time series forecasting, the hybrid approach outperformed DriftGuard in both detection and remediation of concept drift. The physical system modeling results highlight the utility of hybrid frameworks in capturing domain-specific dynamics.

One limitation of the proposed framework is its reliance on domain knowledge for constraint integration, which may limit generalizability to unfamiliar applications. Future work could explore automated methods for constraint generation, drawing inspiration from NC2C's automated convexification.
