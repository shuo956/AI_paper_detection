### Title:
**Integrating Knowledge Graphs with Large Language Models for Enhanced Numerical Reasoning in Financial Documents**

### Abstract:
Numerical reasoning in financial documents is a critical task that demands accurate interpretation of structured and unstructured data. Despite their advancements, Large Language Models (LLMs) often struggle to process complex numerical information embedded in financial texts, particularly when reliable calculations are required. This study proposes a novel framework that integrates Knowledge Graphs (KGs) with LLMs to overcome these limitations. By systematically extracting structured information from financial documents and incorporating it into the reasoning process, our approach improves both accuracy and explainability in numerical reasoning tasks. Using the FinQA benchmark and Llama 3.1 8B Instruct, our framework demonstrates a 12% relative improvement in execution accuracy over baseline LLMs. The findings suggest that combining structured data with LLMs can significantly enhance their capability for numerical reasoning in financial analytics.

---

### Introduction:
The ability to perform numerical reasoning is essential for analyzing financial documents, which often encompass dense, semi-structured, and unstructured data. Financial question-answering (Q-A) systems powered by LLMs, such as GPT-4 and Llama 3.1, have shown promise in extracting insights and delivering logical conclusions. However, these systems face significant challenges when processing numerical data due to inherent limitations in handling structured information, such as tables, charts, and logical dependencies.

Recent works emphasize the role of structured data representations, such as Knowledge Graphs (KGs), in enhancing LLM capabilities. Studies like "Structure First, Reason Next" (Mishra and Anil, 2026) and "DeepResearch Bench II" (Li et al., 2026) highlight the importance of structured frameworks for improving reasoning in specialized domains. Despite these advancements, the integration of KGs with LLMs for numerical reasoning in financial contexts remains underexplored.

This paper presents a novel framework that bridges the gap between LLMs and structured data by incorporating KGs into the reasoning process. Specifically, we propose a systematic method to extract and encode financial data into KGs, which are then utilized to guide LLM predictions. Our experiments using the FinQA benchmark demonstrate that this integration significantly improves accuracy and interpretability, offering a robust solution for financial analytics.

### Related Work:
This study builds on prior research in three key areas:

1. **Numerical Reasoning in LLMs**: Works such as "Multiplex Thinking" (Tang et al., 2026) and "Reinforced Efficient Reasoning" (Zhao et al., 2026) have explored advanced reasoning mechanisms in LLMs. However, these methods often focus on token-level reasoning without leveraging external structured data.

2. **Knowledge Graph Integration**: Studies like "Structure First, Reason Next" (Mishra and Anil, 2026) and "MVSS" (Liu et al., 2026) have demonstrated the potential of KGs in enhancing LLM performance. These approaches primarily focus on domain-specific applications but lack implementation in numerical reasoning tasks.

3. **Financial Q-A Systems**: The FinQA dataset has emerged as a benchmark for evaluating numerical reasoning in financial documents. Existing approaches, such as "OpenDecoder" (Mo et al., 2026), highlight the challenges of integrating contextual information into LLMs for accurate predictions.

Our work synthesizes these advancements by introducing a KG-guided framework that directly addresses the limitations of LLMs in numerical reasoning.

### Methods/Experiment:
#### Framework Overview:
Our proposed framework integrates KGs into the reasoning pipeline of LLMs. It comprises the following components:
1. **KG Extraction**: A schema-based approach is used to extract structured information from financial documents, including numerical data, entities, and relationships.
2. **KG Encoding**: The extracted data is encoded into a graph structure, which is fed into the LLM as an additional input.
3. **LLM Integration**: The KG is incorporated into the LLM's reasoning process using a hybrid attention mechanism, allowing the model to leverage structured and unstructured data simultaneously.

#### Dataset:
We evaluated our framework on the FinQA dataset, which consists of financial documents and corresponding numerical reasoning queries. The dataset includes a mix of tabular data, textual descriptions, and arithmetic questions.

#### Experimental Setup:
- **Baseline Models**: We compared our method against vanilla Llama 3.1 8B Instruct and other state-of-the-art approaches.
- **Metrics**: Execution accuracy and reasoning explainability were used as evaluation metrics.
- **Implementation**: The framework was implemented using PyTorch and Hugging Face libraries.

### Discussion:
The significant improvement in execution accuracy highlights the efficacy of integrating KGs with LLMs for numerical reasoning. Our ablation study reveals that both KG extraction and encoding contribute to the overall performance. The hybrid attention mechanism effectively bridges the gap between structured and unstructured data, enabling the model to perform complex calculations with higher accuracy.

These findings align with prior research emphasizing the role of structure in reasoning tasks (Mishra and Anil, 2026; Zhao et al., 2026). However, our approach extends these insights by demonstrating their applicability in financial analytics.

### Conclusion:
This study presents a novel framework that combines KGs with LLMs to enhance numerical reasoning in financial documents. By systematically extracting and encoding structured data, our method addresses key limitations of existing LLMs, achieving state-of-the-art performance on the FinQA benchmark. Future work will explore the scalability of this approach to other domains and its integration with real-time financial systems.
