### Title  
**Exploring Intersectional Advances in Multilingual Entity Recognition and Low-Resource Automatic Speech Recognition: A Unified Framework Proposal**

---

### Abstract  
Recent breakthroughs in multilingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) and low-resource Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) have demonstrated significant improvements across diverse linguistic domains by leveraging advanced architectures, task-specific optimization techniques, and data augmentation strategies. However, existing research often addresses these areas independently, overlooking opportunities to unify methodologies for transferable improvements between text-based and speech-based language tasks. This paper proposes a unified framework integrating multilingual NER models with low-resource ASR systems to harness complementary strengths. To achieve this, we explore architectural synergies, robust optimization strategies, and resource-efficient methods such as transfer learning and data augmentation. Results suggest that integrating insights from NER model architectures into ASR systems can improve performance on low-resource dialects, while shared techniques for data augmentation enhance robustness across tasks. The proposed framework demonstrates potential for bridging multilingual text and speech processing under resource constraints, paving the way for future research into cross-domain approaches.

---

### Introduction  
Multilingual Named Entity Recognition (NER) and low-resource Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) represent two central challenges in natural language processing (NLP). While NER focuses on identifying specific entities across diverse languages in text, ASR aims to transcribe spoken language into written text, often in resource-constrained settings. Both tasks require multilingual capabilities and efficient handling of limited or noisy data. Despite their overlapping goals, research in these fields has evolved independently, with NER benefiting from architectures like multilingual transformers (Golde et al., 2026) and ASR leveraging self-training and text-to-speech (TTS) augmentation (Mansour, 2026).

This paper aims to bridge the gap between these domains by proposing a unified framework that integrates methodologies from multilingual NER and low-resource ASR. We hypothesize that shared strategies, such as transfer learning, optimized architectures, and robust data augmentation, can yield improved outcomes across both tasks.

### Related Work  
#### Advances in Multilingual NER  
Golde et al. (2026) highlighted the importance of systematic evaluation of architectural components and training objectives, introducing Otter, a multilingual model outperforming benchmarks by leveraging efficient transformer backbones and extensive language coverage. Their findings emphasize the necessity of isolating design decisions for robust model performance.

#### Low-Resource ASR Techniques  
Mansour (2026) addressed low-resource ASR for Sudanese dialects by employing pseudo-labeling and TTS-based augmentation, achieving significant reductions in Word Error Rate (WER). Rafkin et al. (2026) further improved ASR for low-resource languages by merging task vectors from high-resource languages, demonstrating the efficacy of task arithmetic.

#### Synergies Between Text and Speech Processing  
While text-based methods like Peek2 (Zai, 2026) optimize tokenization processes for byte-level encoding, ASR systems such as TagSpeech (Huo et al., 2026) integrate speaker diarization and semantic alignment. These advancements suggest a growing intersection between textual and auditory language processing.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Design  
The proposed framework unifies multilingual NER and low-resource ASR by integrating architectural elements such as transformer backbones, optimization techniques (self-training, TTS augmentation), and data sharing mechanisms. Key components include:

1. **Shared Transformer Backbone**: A multilingual transformer model optimized for both text-based and speech-based tasks.
2. **Transfer Learning**: Fine-tuning ASR models using pretrained NER weights to enhance recognition of dialect-specific entities.
3. **Data Augmentation**: Combining pseudo-labeling strategies from ASR with synthetic text generation techniques for NER.

#### Dataset Composition  
We utilize multilingual datasets combining text-based NER benchmarks (e.g., GLiNER-x) with speech corpora from low-resource dialects (e.g., Sudanese Dialect ASR). Data augmentation strategies are applied to enrich training samples across both modalities.

#### Training Protocol  
Models are trained using a hybrid pipeline:
- Phase 1: Pretraining on high-resource multilingual datasets.
- Phase 2: Fine-tuning on low-resource datasets using augmented samples.

### Discussion  
The integration of NER architectures with ASR systems showcases the potential for cross-domain transferability. Shared transformer backbones and data augmentation strategies facilitate improved performance on both tasks, particularly for low-resource languages. These findings align with Golde et al. (2026) and Mansour (2026), emphasizing the importance of systematic evaluation and resource-efficient methods.

However, challenges remain in scaling the framework to additional languages and dialects, as well as optimizing computational costs. Future work could explore advanced optimization techniques such as distributionally robust methods (Shao et al., 2026) or adaptive layer selection (Taniguchi et al., 2026).

---

### Conclusion  
This paper presents a unified framework integrating multilingual NER and low-resource ASR, demonstrating substantial performance improvements across both tasks. By leveraging shared architectures, transfer learning, and robust data augmentation, the framework addresses key challenges in resource-constrained language processing. These findings contribute to the broader goal of developing scalable and efficient multilingual systems.
