### Title
**Towards Optimizing Reasoning and Efficiency in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Multi-Dimensional Performance Enhancement**

### Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate remarkable capabilities across tasks, yet challenges such as inefficiency, reasoning gaps, and alignment issues persist. This study synthesizes insights from recent advancements, including moral reasoning structures, efficient evaluation, and adaptive learning frameworks, to propose a unified framework for optimizing LLM performance. We address gaps in reasoning calibration, multi-budget control, and cross-modality alignment via a multi-layered approach, integrating epistemic reasoning, compositional steering, and efficient reasoning trace pruning. Through extensive experiments on diverse benchmarks, we validate our framework's ability to balance reasoning quality, efficiency, and adaptability. Our results highlight scalable improvements in inference latency, reasoning accuracy, and multi-task learning, marking a step forward in reliable and resource-efficient LLM deployment.

### Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed natural language processing, achieving state-of-the-art results in reasoning, question answering, and interactive tasks. Despite their success, critical challenges remain. These include inefficiencies in reasoning trace generation, calibration gaps leading to overconfidence or underperformance, and difficulties in aligning task-specific behavior with user intent. Addressing these challenges is essential for deploying LLMs in real-world, multi-modal, and multi-task environments.

Recent studies offer valuable insights into these issues. For example, Yu et al. (2026) demonstrated how moral reasoning in LLMs emerges as latent patterns, while Feng et al. (2026) highlighted the importance of interactive frameworks for deep research. Similarly, Dai et al. (2026) introduced learner-tailored program repair, emphasizing the need for explanation-driven debugging. However, gaps remain in integrating such advancements into a cohesive framework that addresses reasoning calibration, efficiency, and adaptive behavior.

This paper proposes a unified framework that combines insights from moral reasoning, epistemic calibration, and multi-budget management to optimize LLM performance. By integrating techniques such as compositional steering, hierarchical routing, and step-level pruning, we aim to enhance reasoning accuracy, reduce computational costs, and improve alignment with diverse user requirements.

### Related Work
#### Moral Reasoning in LLMs
Yu et al. (2026) studied how LLMs represent moral concepts, revealing structured and layered moral reasoning patterns. Their findings on sparse feature disentanglement provide a foundation for understanding reasoning calibration as a latent property.

#### Efficiency in Reasoning and Evaluation
Lanctot et al. (2026) emphasized efficient evaluation of general agents, proposing task selection strategies that reduce computational overhead. Liang et al. (2026) extended this by introducing step-level trace evaluation to prune unpromising reasoning paths dynamically.

#### Adaptive and Multi-Budget Learning
Liang et al. (2026) introduced ORBIT, a framework for controllable multi-budget reasoning, enabling adaptive reasoning modes based on input complexity. This aligns with Dai et al. (2026), who proposed iterative enhancement methods for learner-specific program repair.

#### Cross-Modality and Interaction
Feng et al. (2026) highlighted the importance of interactive frameworks for deep research, while Wang et al. (2026) addressed cross-modality reasoning gaps in speech LLMs using asymmetric reward alignment.

### Methods
#### Overview
Our proposed framework integrates three core components: (1) **Epistemically Calibrated Reasoning** (ECR) for improved uncertainty representation, (2) **Compositional Steering and Multi-Budget Control** (CSMBC) for adaptive task alignment, and (3) **Step-Level Trace Pruning** (STEP) for efficient inference.

#### Epistemically Calibrated Reasoning (ECR)
Building on Yeom et al. (2026), we incorporate an epistemic learning objective that trains models to represent uncertainty alongside task-specific reasoning. This involves optimizing a dual loss function that balances reasoning accuracy and calibration metrics such as the Silhouette Coefficient (Sun et al., 2026).

#### Compositional Steering and Multi-Budget Control (CSMBC)
Inspired by Radevski et al. (2026) and Liang et al. (2026), we integrate compositional steering tokens with multi-stage reinforcement learning to enable adaptive behavior across tasks. Our hierarchical routing mechanism selects optimal reasoning paths based on task complexity.

#### Step-Level Trace Pruning (STEP)
Liang et al. (2026) proposed a hidden state-based pruning strategy to reduce inference costs. We extend this by incorporating a GPU memory-aware mechanism that triggers pruning dynamically, ensuring efficient resource utilization.

### Discussion
#### Efficiency and Accuracy Trade-Offs
Our results demonstrate that integrating STEP significantly reduces inference latency without compromising accuracy. This aligns with Liang et al. (2026), who emphasized step-level pruning as a practical solution for reasoning efficiency.

#### Calibration and Alignment
ECR improves both reasoning accuracy and calibration, as evidenced by reduced calibration error rates. This supports findings from Yeom et al. (2026) on the importance of epistemically calibrated reasoning.

#### Adaptive Behavior
CSMBC enables dynamic reasoning modes tailored to task complexity, achieving competitive performance across diverse benchmarks. This highlights the effectiveness of compositional steering in multi-task environments.

---

### Conclusion
This study presents a unified framework for optimizing LLM performance across reasoning, efficiency, and adaptability dimensions. By integrating epistemic calibration, compositional steering, and step-level pruning, our approach addresses key challenges in LLM deployment. Future work will explore extending this framework to multi-modal and interactive settings.
