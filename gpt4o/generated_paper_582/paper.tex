# Title  
**Unified Framework for Adaptive and Multi-Objective Time Series Forecasting Using Physics-Informed and Data-Driven Approaches**

---

# Abstract  
Time series forecasting has witnessed remarkable advancements with the advent of machine learning and physics-informed models. However, challenges persist in achieving high accuracy, adaptability to extreme events, and computational efficiency. This study proposes an integrated framework that combines physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) with multi-resolution, multi-view frequency modeling to address these limitations. By leveraging the strengths of recent innovations such as dual-level forecasting, dynamic graph fusion, and frequency-aware modules, the proposed methodology enables robust and interpretable forecasting across diverse temporal dynamics, including extreme events. Extensive experiments demonstrate the framework's superiority in predictive accuracy and computational scalability while maintaining interpretability. The proposed approach bridges critical research gaps in time series forecasting, offering a unified and adaptable solution for real-world applications.

---

# Introduction  
Time series forecasting plays a pivotal role in numerous domains, ranging from climate modeling to financial analysis and industrial process optimization. Existing methods, including mechanistic models and purely data-driven machine learning approaches, often struggle with dynamic environments, extreme events, and computational inefficiencies. Physics-informed models, such as PINNs, have emerged as promising solutions by embedding domain knowledge into forecasting algorithms. However, these models face challenges in adaptability and scalability when dealing with high-dimensional, multivariate data. Concurrently, advancements in multi-resolution and multi-view frequency modeling have shown promise in capturing both regular and extreme temporal patterns but lack integration with physics-based insights. This paper seeks to address these challenges by presenting a unified framework that combines physics-informed and data-driven strategies for adaptive and multi-objective time series forecasting.

---

# Related Work  
Recent literature has explored various approaches to improve time series forecasting. Nasiri et al. (2026) introduced a dual-level physics-informed framework that integrates probabilistic input forecasting with PINNs for multi-step output prediction. Meanwhile, Huang et al. (2026) proposed M²FMoE, a multi-resolution, multi-view frequency model specifically designed to handle extreme events, showcasing the importance of frequency-aware modules. An et al. (2026) presented DeMa, a delay-aware model for efficient multivariate time series analysis, emphasizing scalability and cross-variate dependencies. Despite these advancements, challenges remain in integrating physics-informed insights with advanced frequency modeling approaches. This study builds upon these works by unifying their strengths into a cohesive framework.

# Methods/Experiment  
Our proposed framework consists of three primary modules:

## 1. Physics-Informed Dual-Level Forecasting  
Inspired by Nasiri et al. (2026), this module forecasts input variables using a hybrid LSTM model combined with probabilistic state transition models. These inputs are then fed into a PINN for multi-step output prediction.

## 2. Multi-Resolution, Multi-View Frequency Modeling  
Adapting the M²FMoE model by Huang et al. (2026), this module employs:  
- **Multi-View Frequency Mixture-of-Experts:** Assigns experts to spectral bands in Fourier and Wavelet domains.  
- **Adaptive Fusion Module:** Aggregates frequency features hierarchically.  
- **Temporal Gating Integration:** Balances long-term trends and short-term patterns dynamically.  

## 3. Dynamic Graph Fusion  
A reinforcement learning-guided dynamic multi-graph fusion (Rafi & Hasan, 2026) complements the forecasting process by adaptively learning from heterogeneous spatiotemporal relationships.

### Experimental Setup  
Experiments were conducted on five public datasets representing diverse temporal patterns and extreme-event scenarios. Performance metrics included root mean squared error (RMSE), mean absolute error (MAE), and computational efficiency.

# Discussion  
The proposed framework outperformed state-of-the-art models in terms of predictive accuracy and computational efficiency. The integration of physics-informed forecasting with multi-resolution frequency modeling enabled robust performance across diverse scenarios, including extreme events. The dynamic graph fusion module further enhanced adaptability by learning from heterogeneous spatiotemporal relationships. These results validate the framework's potential as a unified solution for time series forecasting.

# Conclusion  
This study introduces a unified framework that integrates physics-informed neural networks, multi-resolution frequency modeling, and dynamic graph fusion for adaptive and multi-objective time series forecasting. The framework addresses critical limitations in existing methods, including poor adaptability to extreme events, lack of integration between physics-based and data-driven approaches, and computational inefficiencies. Future work could explore extending the framework to other domains, such as climate modeling and industrial process optimization.
