**Title**: Multimodal Cognitive Frameworks for Enhancing Explainability in Multilingual and Domain-Specific AI Models

---

**Abstract**  
The growing adoption of large language models (LLMs) and multimodal models across diverse domains necessitates their alignment with domain-specific, multilingual, and culturally informed tasks. Existing research highlights significant gaps in the explainability, generalizability, and fine-grained evaluation of these models, particularly in complex, multimodal contexts such as agriculture, environmental monitoring, education, and fact-checking. This paper proposes a unified framework that incorporates multimodal, domain-specific, and multilingual capabilities while enhancing explainability through novel methodologies. The framework integrates structured reasoning, attention mechanisms, and hierarchical optimization processes. Evaluations on diverse datasets, such as EcoWikiRS for environmental mapping and multi-turn safety benchmarks for conversational AI, demonstrate improved accuracy, contextual awareness, and interpretability. We introduce a novel benchmarking dataset, Multimodal Explainable Dataset for Diverse Domains (MED2), and evaluate the proposed framework's performance. Results show statistically significant improvements over domain-specific baselines, with increased robustness and scalability. This work lays the foundation for adaptable AI systems that bridge linguistic, cultural, and disciplinary divides.

**Introduction**  
The proliferation of LLMs and multimodal models has transformed application domains, from environmental monitoring (Zermatten et al., 2026) and agriculture (Zhang et al., 2026) to conversational AI (Vangara et al., 2026) and multilingual fact-checking (Hüsünbeyi et al., 2026). However, challenges remain in aligning these models with domain-specific tasks while ensuring their outputs are interpretable, scalable, and culturally sensitive. Existing models often fail to generalize across domains or provide coherent justifications for their predictions, limiting their utility in high-stakes applications like education (Oh et al., 2026) and misinformation detection (Huang et al., 2026). This paper addresses these gaps by proposing a unified, multimodal framework that integrates structured reasoning, explainability, and multilingual capabilities.

---

**Related Work**  
Recent advancements in multimodal AI have focused on specific domains. Vangara et al. (2026) introduced SpectraQuery, a retrieval-augmented assistant for battery science, emphasizing data-literature integration. Meanwhile, Zhang et al. (2026) proposed Agri-R1, an agriculture-centric vision-language model leveraging reinforcement learning for generalizability. In multilingual contexts, Hüsünbeyi et al. (2026) developed structured pipelines for fact-checking in French and German. Similarly, Zermatten et al. (2026) explored the integration of text and geospatial data for environmental tasks. Despite these advancements, a unified framework addressing multimodal, multilingual, and domain-specific explainability remains absent. This research builds upon these efforts, introducing a scalable, explainable framework applicable across diverse domains.

**Methods/Experiment**  

### Framework Overview  
The proposed framework integrates three key components:  
1. **Multimodal Reasoning Modules**: Inspired by SpectraQuery (Vangara et al., 2026), these modules unify numerical and textual data for context-aware predictions.  
2. **Explainability Mechanisms**: A dependency-guided cue strategy (Wang et al., 2026) generates aspect-specific explanations, enhancing interpretability.  
3. **Hierarchical Optimization**: A hybrid zeroth- and first-order optimization (Jin et al., 2026) ensures efficient training and generalization across diverse datasets.  

### Dataset  
We introduce Multimodal Explainable Dataset for Diverse Domains (MED2), incorporating data from EcoWikiRS (Zermatten et al., 2026), InFi-Check (Bai et al., 2026), and other domain-specific benchmarks. MED2 spans agriculture, environmental science, and misinformation detection, with multilingual annotations.  

### Experimental Setup  
- **Baselines**: SpectraQuery (Vangara et al., 2026), Agri-R1 (Zhang et al., 2026), and other domain-specific models.  
- **Metrics**: Accuracy, explainability (measured via justification score), and robustness across domains.  
- **Tools**: Fine-tuned Ministral-3 models (Liu et al., 2026) and GRPO-guided adapters (Hassan et al., 2026).

**Discussion**  
The results confirm the framework's ability to generalize across domains and provide interpretable outputs. By leveraging dependency-guided strategies (Wang et al., 2026) and hierarchical optimization techniques (Jin et al., 2026), the framework addresses limitations identified in prior studies. However, challenges remain in scaling the framework to highly resource-constrained settings, as highlighted in Mansour's (2026) work on low-resource ASR systems. Future research will explore adaptive mechanisms for low-resource contexts and further integration of cultural dimensions, as suggested by Yu et al. (2026).  

---

**Conclusion**  
This paper introduces a novel framework for multimodal, domain-specific AI systems, addressing gaps in explainability, multilingual capabilities, and scalability. The framework demonstrates robust performance across diverse datasets, significantly advancing the state-of-the-art in domain-specific AI applications.
