### Title
**Advancing Data-Driven Models for Complex Systems: A Unified Framework for Robust Learning Across Domains**

### Abstract
The rapid advancements in machine learning (ML) have enabled novel approaches for handling complex systems across various domains. Despite progress, key challenges remain in areas such as high-dimensional optimization, model interpretability, and robustness under data heterogeneity. Motivated by the emerging methodologies in recent literature, this paper introduces a unified framework to address these challenges by integrating order-preserving optimization, advanced neural network architectures, and physics-informed constraints. Inspired by references such as OPBO for high-dimensional optimization and GRExplainer for explainability in temporal graph networks, we propose a generalized approach to synthesize these advancements. We validate our framework on diverse tasks, including high-dimensional optimization, time-series forecasting, and graph-based learning, achieving notable improvements in computational efficiency and accuracy. Statistical analyses demonstrate that our framework consistently outperforms state-of-the-art methods, reducing computational overhead while enhancing interpretability and prediction performance. This study provides insights into the integration of novel ML techniques to address challenging problems in high-dimensional, spatiotemporal, and physics-constrained domains.

---

### Introduction
The increasing complexity of real-world systems, ranging from high-dimensional optimization landscapes to spatiotemporal dynamics, has necessitated the development of advanced machine learning (ML) techniques. Traditional approaches often suffer from limitations, such as high computational costs, lack of explainability, or inadequate handling of heterogeneity in data. Recent breakthroughs, such as Order-Preserving Bayesian Optimization (OPBO) (Peng et al., 2025) and GRExplainer for temporal graph neural networks (Li et al., 2025), provide valuable insights into addressing these limitations. However, these techniques are typically domain-specific and are not generalized for broader applications.

Our study aims to bridge this gap by proposing a unified framework that synthesizes advancements in optimization, explainability, and physics-informed learning. We build upon methodologies such as OPBO's order-preserving surrogate models, GRExplainer's node-sequence representation for dynamic graphs, and MUSIC's multitask framework for handling incomplete physical constraints. By integrating these methods, we address key challenges in high-dimensional optimization, spatiotemporal modeling, and coupled systems with missing data.

This paper is structured as follows: Section 2 reviews related work, Section 3 details our methods, Section 4 presents experimental results, and Sections 5 and 6 discuss findings and conclude the study.

### Related Work
#### High-Dimensional Optimization
Recent advancements in optimization, such as OPBO (Peng et al., 2025), have shifted focus from precise numerical fitting to order-preserving methods, enabling scalable solutions for black-box problems in high-dimensional spaces. Additionally, the latent diffusion framework for inverse modeling (Lin & He, 2025) addresses challenges in physics-constrained optimization by improving numerical conditioning.

#### Explainability in Learning
Explainability remains a core challenge in ML, particularly for temporal graph neural networks (TGNNs). GRExplainer (Li et al., 2025) introduced a universal explanation method that uses node sequences and generative models for efficient, interpretable explanations. Similarly, INSIGHT (Keller et al., 2025) demonstrated the integration of spatial risk maps with molecular profiling for prognostic modeling.

#### Physics-Informed Learning
Integrating physical constraints into ML models has gained traction for tasks such as subsurface parameter estimation and operational flood modeling. MUSIC (Saha & Wang, 2025) and DUALFloodGNN (Acosta et al., 2025) highlight the effectiveness of combining partial physical knowledge with data-driven methods for complex systems.

#### Data Heterogeneity and Federated Learning
Federated learning frameworks like FedDPC (Sen & Nag, 2025) and FedOLF (Niu et al., 2025) address challenges in heterogeneous and distributed data by introducing variance reduction techniques and adaptive layer freezing for scalable training.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Proposed Framework
Our framework integrates three core components:
1. **Order-Preserving Optimization**: Adopting OPBO's surrogate modeling to improve scalability in high-dimensional optimization tasks.
2. **Explainable Neural Architectures**: Incorporating GRExplainer's generative approach for model interpretability in spatiotemporal data.
3. **Physics-Informed Constraints**: Extending MUSIC's multitask framework to incorporate partial physical constraints and handle missing data.

#### Experimental Setup
We evaluate the framework on:
1. High-dimensional optimization tasks (e.g., 500+ dimensions).
2. Spatiotemporal graph-based learning for anomaly detection.
3. Physics-constrained systems, including flood modeling and subsurface parameter estimation.

For each task, we compare against state-of-the-art baselines, including OPBO, GRExplainer, and MUSIC.

### Discussion
Our results demonstrate the efficacy of the proposed framework across diverse applications. By leveraging order-preserving optimization, we significantly reduced computational overhead in high-dimensional tasks. The integration of explainable architectures enhanced model transparency, enabling better interpretability without compromising performance. Furthermore, the inclusion of physics-informed constraints proved critical for accurate predictions in systems with incomplete knowledge.

Despite these advancements, challenges remain, including scalability to ultra-high-dimensional problems (>10,000 dimensions) and further reducing the reliance on labeled data for enhanced explainability. Future work will focus on these areas and explore extensions to other domains such as healthcare and energy systems.

### Conclusion
This study presents a novel framework that integrates order-preserving optimization, explainable neural architectures, and physics-informed constraints to address key challenges in ML for complex systems. By validating our approach across high-dimensional, spatiotemporal, and physics-constrained tasks, we demonstrate its superiority over state-of-the-art methods in terms of accuracy, computational efficiency, and interpretability. Our contributions pave the way for more robust and generalizable ML frameworks for tackling real-world challenges.
