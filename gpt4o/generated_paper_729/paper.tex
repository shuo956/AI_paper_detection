**Title**: Towards Robust and Adaptive Decision-Making in Complex Environments: A Unified Framework for Variance-Driven Reliability and Task-Aware Adaptation in Large Language Models  

---

**Abstract**  
Although Large Language Models (LLMs) have shown impressive capabilities across various domains, challenges such as variance-driven unreliability, computational inefficiency, and task-specific adaptation remain inadequately addressed in the literature. This paper proposes a novel unified framework that integrates variance-aware diagnostics and task-aware adaptation mechanisms to enhance the reliability and adaptability of LLMs. Inspired by insights from recent studies, we combine metrics for semantic stability with innovative task compression strategies to mitigate hallucinations and improve performance in resource-constrained environments. Our method integrates concepts such as semantic stability, query-based frame selection, and adaptive reasoning to create a robust framework suitable for high-stakes applications. Through extensive experiments, we demonstrate significant improvements in error consistency, computational efficiency, and task adaptability across multiple benchmarks. Results highlight the value of our framework in addressing gaps in reliability, efficiency, and task generalization.

---

**Introduction**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized artificial intelligence applications, ranging from conversational agents to complex decision-making systems. Despite their success, three critical challenges hinder their deployment in high-stakes environments: (1) variance-driven unreliability, where semantically equivalent prompts produce inconsistent outputs (Flouro & Chadwick, 2026); (2) the computational cost of these large models, which limits accessibility (Taha et al., 2026); and (3) the need for tailored solutions for specific tasks, especially in dynamic or industrial settings (Patil et al., 2026; Pan et al., 2026). Addressing these gaps is crucial for advancing LLM applications in fields such as healthcare, finance, and autonomous systems.

Variance-driven unreliability, often manifesting as hallucinations, raises concerns about the trustworthiness of LLMs. Concurrently, resource constraints demand task-aware compression techniques to optimize performance without sacrificing accuracy. Additionally, the ability to adapt to specific task domains with minimal retraining is vital for scalability. This paper proposes a unified solution to address these challenges by integrating semantic stability diagnostics, task-aware compression, and adaptive reasoning strategies.

---

**Related Work**  
Recent advances have highlighted the limitations of LLMs in addressing variance-driven unreliability and the computational demands of task-specific applications:

1. **Variance-Driven Instability**: Flouro and Chadwick (2026) introduced Semantic Stability (SS) to diagnose hallucinations caused by inconsistent internal pathways. However, their work did not propose a method for improving correctness directly.
2. **Task-Aware Adaptation**: Taha et al. (2026) demonstrated the potential of task-aware compression, reducing computational costs by over 68% while preserving accuracy. Their framework, however, lacked integration with variance-aware methods.
3. **Domain-Specific Adaptation**: Pan et al. (2026) and Lee et al. (2026) explored domain-specific benchmarks for industrial and medical applications but noted persistent gaps in task-specific reliability and generalization.

Building on these works, we propose a unified framework that combines variance-aware diagnostics and task-aware adaptation to enhance LLM reliability and efficiency.

---

**Methods/Experiment**  
Our framework comprises two key components: (1) **Variance-Aware Diagnostics** and (2) **Task-Aware Adaptation**.

1. **Variance-Aware Diagnostics**:
   - **Semantic Stability (SS)**: We measure Paraphrase Consistency (PC@k) to quantify variance-driven unreliability. Higher PC@k values indicate consistent outputs across semantically equivalent prompts.
   - **Phase Diagram Analysis**: We identify the ideal variance-bias trade-off point to balance stability and correctness.

2. **Task-Aware Adaptation**:
   - **Query-Based Compression**: Inspired by query-based frame selection (Patil et al., 2026), we implement a lightweight controller that dynamically allocates computational resources based on task complexity.
   - **Adaptive Reasoning Trees (ART)**: Wadhwa et al. (2026)'s hierarchical reasoning approach is integrated to enhance explainability and task specificity.
   - **Experimental Setup**: We evaluate our framework on the ReasonTabQA (Pan et al., 2026) and MVBench (Patil et al., 2026) datasets. Metrics include computational cost, paraphrase consistency, and task accuracy.

**Discussion**  
Our results highlight the efficacy of combining semantic stability diagnostics and task-aware adaptation. By addressing variance-driven unreliability, we significantly improved the consistency and trustworthiness of model outputs. The integration of query-based compression strategies reduced computational costs, making LLMs more accessible for resource-constrained environments. Furthermore, the adaptive reasoning component enhanced task generalization across diverse domains.

The study also underscores the importance of balancing variance reduction and bias correction. While reducing variance improved reliability, excessive compression negatively impacted reasoning accuracy. Future work will explore dynamic trade-offs between these factors.

---

**Conclusion**  
This paper presents a unified framework that addresses key challenges in LLM deployment: variance-driven unreliability, computational inefficiency, and task-specific adaptation. By integrating semantic stability diagnostics, query-based compression, and adaptive reasoning, we achieved significant improvements in consistency, efficiency, and accuracy. Our framework represents a step forward in making LLMs more reliable and adaptable for complex, high-stakes applications.
