**Title: Advancing Low-Resource Algorithm Adaptation and Trustworthy Machine Learning through Unified Optimization Frameworks**

**Abstract**  
The rapid advancements in machine learning (ML) and large language models (LLMs) have introduced novel methodologies for optimization, reasoning, and trustworthy applications. However, challenges persist in areas such as low-resource fine-tuning, multi-objective optimization, and clinical trustworthiness. Drawing upon recent literature, this paper proposes a unified framework that integrates low-resource adaptation, multi-reward reinforcement learning, and hybrid explainability to address these challenges. We introduce a novel optimization paradigm inspired by the Covariance-Driven Regression Trees (CovRT) and Group reward-Decoupled Policy Optimization (GDPO), which enhances stability and robustness. Additionally, to address trust gaps in sensitive domains, such as maternal healthcare, we incorporate a hybrid explainable AI (XAI) approach combining fuzzy logic and SHAP explanations. The framework is validated across synthetic benchmarks and real-world datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in accuracy, resilience, and interpretability. This work contributes to advancing parameter-efficient learning, optimizing multi-reward systems, and enabling trustworthy deployment in resource-constrained environments.

---

**1. Introduction**  
Machine learning (ML) and large language models (LLMs) are transforming various domains, including natural language processing, healthcare, and optimization. Despite these advancements, critical gaps remain in the areas of low-resource learning, multi-objective optimization, and trustworthiness. For instance, low-resource environments often face catastrophic forgetting during adaptation (Nur’aini et al., 2026), while multi-reward reinforcement learning suffers from reward collapsing (Liu et al., 2026). Additionally, trust in ML systems, particularly in sensitive fields like healthcare, remains low due to opaque decision-making processes (Yesmin et al., 2026). This paper aims to address these challenges by proposing a unified optimization framework combining methods from covariance-driven regression, multi-reward optimization, and hybrid explainable AI.  

We draw inspiration from CovRT (Zhang & Ma, 2026), which mitigates overfitting in decision trees, and GDPO (Liu et al., 2026), which decouples multi-reward signals to improve training stability. Furthermore, to address the trust gap in healthcare, we build upon the hybrid XAI framework proposed by Yesmin et al. (2026), integrating fuzzy logic with SHAP analysis. The proposed framework is validated across multiple domains, including mathematical reasoning, synthetic benchmarks, and maternal healthcare datasets.

**2. Related Work**  
Several methodologies have been proposed to address the challenges of low-resource learning, multi-reward optimization, and trustworthiness:  

- **Low-Resource Adaptation:** Circuit-Targeted Supervised Fine-Tuning (CT-SFT) (Nur’aini et al., 2026) introduced a mechanism to adapt LLMs to low-resource languages by targeting sparse, task-relevant attention heads. The approach significantly reduces catastrophic forgetting while improving cross-lingual accuracy.  
- **Multi-Reward Optimization:** GDPO (Liu et al., 2026) resolves reward signal collapses in reinforcement learning by decoupling the normalization of distinct rewards. This method enhances training stability and convergence.  
- **Trustworthiness in ML:** Yesmin et al. (2026) proposed a hybrid XAI framework for maternal health risk assessment, combining interpretable fuzzy rules with post-hoc SHAP explanations to improve clinical trust.  

These methods highlight the need for innovative approaches that optimize performance while ensuring interpretability and trustworthiness.

**3. Methods**  

We propose a unified framework that integrates low-resource adaptation, multi-reward optimization, and hybrid explainability. The methodology includes:  

- **Optimization Kernel:** Inspired by CovRT, our framework employs a covariance-driven splitting criterion to enhance robustness in decision trees.  
- **Multi-Reward Decoupling:** GDPO principles are incorporated to handle multi-reward settings, ensuring stable optimization across diverse objectives.  
- **Hybrid XAI:** The framework integrates fuzzy logic with SHAP explanations to provide interpretable and trustworthy predictions.  

**Experimental Setup:**  
Three datasets were used:  
1. Synthetic benchmarks for mathematical reasoning.
2. Multi-objective optimization tasks for reinforcement learning.  
3. Maternal health risk assessment in resource-constrained environments.
