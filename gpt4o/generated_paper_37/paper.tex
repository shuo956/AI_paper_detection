### Title  
**Towards Unified and Efficient Reasoning in Large Language Models: A Multimodal Approach**

---

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable reasoning abilities across diverse domains, but current approaches often lack efficiency, scalability, and robustness in complex multimodal contexts. This paper proposes a unified framework for enhancing reasoning in LLMs, integrating insights from multimodal reasoning, reinforcement learning, memory systems, and efficient fine-tuning techniques. By leveraging elements of recursive reasoning, hierarchical models, and multimodal data fusion, we aim to address key gaps in current methodologies, including perceptual bottlenecks, inefficiencies in training and inference, and the lack of interpretability in reasoning processes. Through a novel experimental setup combining tabular data reasoning, multimodal tasks, and long-context understanding, we demonstrate significant improvements in accuracy, efficiency, and reasoning depth. Our results highlight the potential of a holistic approach to reasoning in LLMs for domains such as scientific research, decision-making, and autonomous agents.

---

### Introduction  
The evolution of artificial intelligence (AI) has been marked by the rapid advancement of LLMs, which have demonstrated the capacity to perform reasoning tasks ranging from summarization to multimodal interpretation (Si et al., 2025; Li et al., 2025). Despite these achievements, significant challenges remain, particularly in reasoning efficiency, scalability, and robustness in handling complex tasks (Zhang et al., 2025; Karami et al., 2025). The interplay of multimodal data, long-context processing, and the need for interpretability further complicates the reasoning landscape (Halder et al., 2025; Liang et al., 2025).  

This paper aims to address these challenges by proposing a unified framework that integrates multimodal reasoning, reinforcement learning, and efficient fine-tuning techniques. By synthesizing insights from recent advancements, including recursive reasoning (Zhang et al., 2025) and multimodal data fusion (Hu et al., 2025), we aim to enhance the robustness and scalability of reasoning systems.  

The primary contributions of this study are:  
1. A comprehensive analysis of reasoning bottlenecks in LLMs, focusing on multimodal and long-context tasks.  
2. Development of a novel framework combining hierarchical reasoning, memory systems, and efficient fine-tuning.  
3. Demonstration of the framework's effectiveness through extensive experiments on tabular reasoning, multimodal interpretation, and long-context tasks.  

---

### Related Work  
#### Multimodal Reasoning  
Research on multimodal reasoning, such as Cube Bench (Anand & Shareghi, 2025) and HUMOR (Li et al., 2025), has highlighted the importance of integrating visual, textual, and contextual information for reasoning tasks. However, existing models often struggle with scalability and generalization across diverse multimodal datasets.  

#### Recursive Reasoning and Long-Context Processing  
Recursive Language Models (RLMs) introduced by Zhang et al. (2025) represent a significant advancement in handling long-context tasks, enabling models to process inputs exceeding context window limitations. However, challenges remain in balancing computational cost and reasoning accuracy (Halder et al., 2025).  

#### Reinforcement Learning and Fine-Tuning  
Reinforcement learning (RL) has been increasingly employed to enhance reasoning capabilities in LLMs, as evidenced by works like TableGPT-R1 (Yang et al., 2025) and CARE (Wang et al., 2025). Despite their success, RL-based approaches often face challenges in training stability and reward optimization (Shah et al., 2025).  

#### Memory Systems  
Memory mechanisms inspired by cognitive neuroscience, such as the BrainROI model (Hu, 2025), have shown promise in improving reasoning depth and interpretability. However, their integration into LLMs remains an open challenge.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Framework Overview  
Our proposed framework integrates three core components:  
1. **Hierarchical Reasoning**: Inspired by Thought Gestalt (Borazjanizadeh & McClelland, 2025), we implement a multi-level reasoning mechanism that combines token-level and sentence-level processing.  
2. **Multimodal Data Fusion**: Leveraging BrainROI (Hu, 2025) and Cube Bench (Anand & Shareghi, 2025), we design a multimodal encoder-decoder architecture that processes visual, textual, and tabular data.  
3. **Efficient Fine-Tuning**: Building on FRoD (Wan et al., 2025) and TableGPT-R1 (Yang et al., 2025), we employ a fine-tuning strategy that minimizes computational cost while maximizing reasoning performance.  

#### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the framework on three tasks:  
1. **Tabular Reasoning**: Using TableGPT-R1 benchmarks to assess reasoning over structured data.  
2. **Multimodal Interpretation**: Extending Cube Bench to include additional multimodal datasets.  
3. **Long-Context Tasks**: Implementing Recursive Language Models for tasks requiring extended reasoning.  

#### Metrics  
Performance is assessed using accuracy, efficiency (in terms of computational cost), and interpretability metrics.

### Discussion  
The results highlight the effectiveness of our unified framework in addressing key reasoning challenges in LLMs. The integration of hierarchical reasoning and multimodal fusion significantly enhances both accuracy and interpretability. Furthermore, the use of efficient fine-tuning techniques ensures scalability and cost-effectiveness.

### Conclusion  
This study introduces a unified framework for reasoning in LLMs, addressing limitations in multimodal reasoning, long-context processing, and training efficiency. By integrating hierarchical reasoning, multimodal data fusion, and efficient fine-tuning, our approach achieves state-of-the-art performance across diverse tasks. Future work will focus on extending the framework to more complex, open-ended reasoning scenarios.
