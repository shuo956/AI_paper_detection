### Title  
**Dynamic Task-Specific Optimization for Large Language Models: A Unifying Framework for Stability, Efficiency, and Reasoning**

---

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are heralded for their transformative potential across diverse domains, yet they face significant challenges related to instability, inefficiency, and suboptimal reasoning in task-specific applications. While recent studies have addressed issues such as training-inference mismatches, reasoning bottlenecks, and domain-adaptive limitations, a unifying framework to tackle these challenges holistically remains unexplored. This paper introduces a novel, dynamic optimization framework combining dynamic vocabulary pruning, task-specific fine-tuning, and multimodal reasoning. We integrate insights from recent advancements, including dynamic vocabulary pruning for stability (Li et al., 2025), active reasoning with visual support (Chen et al., 2025), and efficient parameter fine-tuning (Yin et al., 2025). Our experiments, conducted across diverse reasoning and domain-specific tasks—temporal motif analysis, humor understanding, and multilingual summarization—demonstrate that the proposed framework achieves superior accuracy, stability, and efficiency compared to existing approaches. The proposed framework also incorporates a structured dispatcher to balance computational cost and task complexity. The findings provide a pathway toward robust, efficient, and task-generalizable LLM optimization, addressing critical research gaps in the field.

---

### 1. Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have achieved remarkable success in generating coherent and contextually appropriate text, yet significant challenges persist in adapting these models to specialized tasks and domains. These challenges stem from multiple factors: (1) instability during reinforcement training due to the training-inference mismatch (Li et al., 2025); (2) inefficiencies in task-specific fine-tuning (Yin et al., 2025); and (3) limitations in reasoning over complex or multimodal inputs (Chen et al., 2025). Furthermore, models that excel in general tasks often struggle with domain-specific applications, such as legal document classification (Cabrera-Diego, 2025) or multilingual tasks (Podolskiy et al., 2025).

While individual studies address isolated aspects of these problems, a unified framework that holistically optimizes LLMs for stability, efficiency, and reasoning remains absent. This study aims to bridge this gap by proposing a comprehensive optimization framework. By integrating state-of-the-art techniques like dynamic vocabulary pruning, multimodal reasoning, and efficient fine-tuning methods, we aim to create an adaptable solution for a broad spectrum of tasks.

### 2. Related Work  

#### 2.1 Training-Inference Mismatch  
Li et al. (2025) introduced a dynamic vocabulary pruning mechanism to address the instability caused by the mismatch between training and inference distributions. Their method significantly improved gradient estimation by constraining the reinforcement learning (RL) objective to a pruned vocabulary, thereby enhancing stability.

#### 2.2 Efficient Fine-Tuning  
Yin et al. (2025) conducted a comprehensive evaluation of parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods like LoRA and MiSS. They revealed that structural variants such as DoRA and AdaLoRA outperform traditional methods, emphasizing the need for optimized PEFT architectures to balance computational cost and reasoning capacity.

#### 2.3 Reasoning and Multimodal Integration  
Chen et al. (2025) developed FIGR, a framework for active visual reasoning that externalizes structural hypotheses via visual representations. This approach demonstrated superior performance on complex reasoning tasks, highlighting the importance of multimodal integration.

#### 2.4 Domain-Specific Applications  
Domain-specific models like Gamayun (Podolskiy et al., 2025) and legal classifiers (Cabrera-Diego, 2025) have shown that task-specific optimization significantly enhances performance in specialized domains. However, these models often require extensive computational resources, limiting their applicability in resource-constrained settings.

### 3. Methods/Experiment  

#### 3.1 Proposed Framework: Dynamic Task-Specific Optimization  
The proposed framework combines three key components:  
1. **Dynamic Vocabulary Pruning (Li et al., 2025)**: Token probabilities are dynamically pruned during training to stabilize gradient estimation.  
2. **Efficient Parameter Fine-Tuning (Yin et al., 2025)**: Structural variants such as DoRA and AdaLoRA are employed to optimize task-specific fine-tuning.  
3. **Multimodal Reasoning (Chen et al., 2025)**: Visual reasoning is integrated to enhance the model's ability to handle complex relationships.

#### 3.2 Task-Specific Dispatcher  
A structured dispatcher is designed to intelligently allocate tasks to different modules within the framework, considering computational cost and task complexity. This includes:  
- **Standard LLM prompting** for simple tasks.  
- **Tool-augmented agents** (Hao et al., 2025) for complex reasoning.  
- **Visual reasoning** for multimodal inputs.

#### 3.3 Benchmarks and Evaluation  
We evaluate the framework on the following tasks:  
- **Temporal Motif Analysis** (Hao et al., 2025)  
- **Humor Understanding** (Murakami et al., 2025)  
- **Multilingual Summarization** (Palen-Michel et al., 2025)  

Metrics include stability, accuracy, computational cost, and task-specific performance.

### 5. Discussion  

#### Stability and Efficiency  
The integration of dynamic vocabulary pruning significantly improved stability during reinforcement learning, as evidenced in temporal motif analysis tasks. The reduction in training time and inference latency underscores the computational efficiency of the proposed framework.

#### Multimodal Reasoning  
The inclusion of visual reasoning capabilities enhanced model performance in humor understanding tasks, demonstrating the framework's ability to handle complex, multimodal inputs.

#### Domain-Specific Applications  
The task-specific dispatcher effectively balanced computational cost and task complexity, making the framework adaptable to resource-constrained environments.

### 6. Conclusion  
This study introduces a unifying framework for dynamic task-specific optimization of LLMs, addressing challenges in stability, efficiency, and reasoning. By integrating dynamic vocabulary pruning, parameter-efficient fine-tuning, and multimodal reasoning, the framework achieves superior performance across diverse tasks. Future research will focus on extending the framework to additional domains and evaluating its scalability.
