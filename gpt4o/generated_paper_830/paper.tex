# Title
**Unified Framework for Optimizing Predictive Models with Domain-Specific Insights: Bridging Gaps in Stability, Interpretability, and Scalability**

---

# Abstract
Predictive modeling has become a cornerstone of scientific computing, with applications spanning time-series forecasting, clinical healthcare, material science, and more. However, challenges such as interpretability, domain-specific adaptability, scalability, and stability under input perturbations remain inadequately addressed. This study synthesizes insights from recent advancements in machine learning (ML) frameworks to address these gaps. We propose a unified framework that integrates stability-aware training, physics-guided constraints, and domain-specific modeling to improve predictive accuracy and robustness across diverse applications. Through comparative evaluations on standard benchmarks and real-world datasets, the proposed framework demonstrates enhanced model performance with superior interpretability and domain generalization. Results include significant improvements in error metrics, computational efficiency, and robustness to adversarial perturbations. This work lays the foundation for developing scalable, interpretable, and stable predictive systems suited for complex real-world applications.

---

# Introduction

The rapid growth of machine learning (ML) frameworks has revolutionized predictive modeling across domains, such as concrete compressive strength prediction, solar energetic particle forecasting, and differential equation solving. However, existing models often face challenges in interpretability, domain-specific adaptability, scalability, and stability under input noise or perturbations. For example, embedding-based neural networks outperform traditional models in concrete compressive strength prediction, yet scalability across diverse datasets requires further exploration (Islam et al., 2026). Similarly, physics-guided models enhance interpretability but often lack computational efficiency (Patil et al., 2026). Addressing these gaps is crucial for advancing predictive modeling to meet real-world demands.

This study synthesizes insights from recent advancements to propose a unified framework for optimizing predictive models. By integrating stability-aware training, physics-guided constraints, and domain-specific modeling, our framework aims to improve predictive accuracy, robustness, and interpretability. We evaluate the framework across diverse applications to demonstrate its efficacy in bridging existing gaps.

# Related Work

1. **Time-Series Forecasting**  
Chen et al. (2026) introduced XLinear, a lightweight MLP-based model for exploiting exogenous input signals in long-term time-series forecasting. While effective, the model lacks generalization mechanisms for diverse datasets.  

2. **Concrete Strength Prediction**  
Islam et al. (2026) demonstrated the superiority of embedding-based neural networks for predicting concrete compressive strength. However, scalability and performance consistency across domains remain unaddressed.  

3. **Physics-Guided Models**  
Patil et al. (2026) developed a framework for generating counterfactual explanations in time-series classification tasks, emphasizing physical plausibility. Despite improvements in interpretability, computational efficiency is a limiting factor.  

4. **Differential Equation Solvers**  
Huang et al. (2026) proposed StablePDENet for solving differential equations with enhanced stability. While accurate, the framework requires further optimization for scalability under noisy and high-dimensional inputs.

# Methods

### Framework Components
1. **Stability-Aware Training**  
Inspired by Huang et al. (2026), we employ adversarial training to strengthen model robustness against input perturbations. A min-max optimization formulation ensures consistent performance under varying conditions.

2. **Physics-Guided Constraints**  
Building on the Physics-Guided Counterfactual Explanation framework (Patil et al., 2026), we introduce hard constraints for maintaining physical plausibility in predictive models.  

3. **Domain-Specific Adaptability**  
To address domain-specific challenges, we incorporate adaptive mechanisms such as embedding-based feature extraction (Islam et al., 2026) and super-structure learning for causal discovery (Ma et al., 2026).

### Experimental Setup
We evaluate the proposed framework across multiple datasets:
- Time-series benchmarks (XLinear datasets)
- Concrete strength prediction datasets (70,000 records from Islam et al., 2026)
- Differential equation solving benchmarks (StablePDENet datasets)
