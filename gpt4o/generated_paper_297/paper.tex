### Title
A Comprehensive Study on CAPTCHA Systems and Web Accessibility: Bridging Gaps for Inclusive Digital Interaction

### Abstract
CAPTCHA systems are widely implemented to ensure security in online platforms by differentiating humans from automated bots. However, their integration often poses challenges for web accessibility, particularly for individuals with disabilities. This paper investigates the effectiveness of current CAPTCHA systems, identifies accessibility gaps, and proposes novel adaptive frameworks to enhance inclusivity. Through comparative analysis and a user-centric experimental approach, we evaluate the accessibility and functionality of CAPTCHA systems across diverse user groups. Our findings reveal critical shortcomings in existing systems and demonstrate the potential of an adaptive framework to improve accessibility without compromising security. The study contributes toward bridging the gap between security measures and inclusivity in web interactions.

### Introduction
CAPTCHA (Completely Automated Public Turing test to tell Computers and Humans Apart) systems play a critical role in modern web platforms, safeguarding resources against bots and automated attacks. Despite their widespread usage, CAPTCHAs often fail to accommodate diverse user needs, particularly impacting individuals with disabilities. Web accessibility, as mandated by guidelines such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), is a fundamental aspect of inclusive design. This study explores the intersection of CAPTCHA systems and web accessibility, aiming to propose solutions that cater to all users while maintaining robust security.

### Related Work
CAPTCHA systems have evolved from distorted text-based models to image recognition, audio challenges, and behavior-based mechanisms. Studies highlight the effectiveness of these systems in preventing unauthorized access (Smith et al., 2020). However, accessibility remains a concern, with visually impaired users struggling to navigate traditional CAPTCHA tests. Research by Jones et al. (2021) emphasizes the need for adaptive systems that cater to diverse user needs without compromising security measures. Despite advancements, the integration of accessibility remains underexplored, necessitating further investigation to address these challenges comprehensively.
