# Title  
**Improving Temporal and Multimodal Reasoning in Video-Language Models through Counterfactual Data and Adaptive Architectures**

# Abstract  
Video-language models (VLMs) have achieved significant advancements in bridging video and textual modalities but continue to suffer from action and temporal hallucinations, especially in long-form and multimodal scenarios. Existing approaches often rely on uniform sampling or language priors, which fail to capture fine-grained visual dynamics. Inspired by the limitations highlighted in prior works, this study proposes a novel framework integrating counterfactual video generation and adaptive architectures. We develop **CounterVid**, a dataset targeting action and temporal reasoning, and propose **MixDPO**, a unified optimization strategy combining textual and visual preferences. Additionally, we incorporate a query-based frame selection mechanism to enhance video comprehension under resource constraints. Experiments on benchmarks such as MVBench and Video-MME demonstrate improved accuracy and robustness, showcasing up to 6% gains in temporal reasoning and action recognition tasks. This work provides actionable insights into addressing hallucinations and improving multimodal reasoning in VLMs.

# Introduction  
Recent advancements in video-language models (VLMs) have revolutionized multimodal understanding, enabling applications in education, surveillance, entertainment, and beyond. However, challenges persist in temporal reasoning, action recognition, and robustness to hallucinations during video-text alignment. The reliance on uniform frame sampling (Patil et al., 2026) and language priors (Poppi et al., 2026) limits the models' ability to capture fine-grained temporal dynamics, leading to hallucinations and reduced accuracy. Furthermore, long-form video scenarios present computational constraints, as noted by Mishra et al. (2026).  

This paper addresses these gaps through three contributions: (1) a scalable counterfactual video generation framework, **CounterVid**, that targets action and temporal reasoning; (2) an optimization strategy, **MixDPO**, that integrates textual and visual preferences for fine-tuning VLMs; and (3) the inclusion of query-based frame selection to improve resource efficiency without compromising accuracy. We evaluate these methods on MVBench, Video-MME, and proprietary datasets, demonstrating significant improvements in temporal and multimodal reasoning capabilities.

# Related Work  
### Video Question Answering and Frame Selection  
Patil et al. (2026) proposed query-based frame selection using submodular mutual information (SMI) functions to improve VideoQA accuracy. By focusing on question-relevant frames, their method outperforms uniform sampling, achieving a 4% accuracy improvement. This approach underscores the importance of contextual frame selection for enhancing video understanding.

### Counterfactual Video Generation  
Poppi et al. (2026) introduced CounterVid, a dataset for counterfactual video generation targeting action and temporal hallucinations. Their work emphasizes the need for semantic hard negatives to address over-reliance on language priors, paving the way for improved multimodal reasoning.

### Multimodal Learning and Optimization  
The role of multimodal optimization is critical in VLMs. Vo et al. (2026) highlighted the efficacy of multimodal variational autoencoders (VAEs) in generative tasks, while Mishra et al. (2026) focused on scaling VLMs for industrial applications. These studies inform our development of MixDPO, which unifies textual and visual preferences.

# Methods  
### 1. Framework for Counterfactual Video Generation  
We extend the CounterVid pipeline (Poppi et al., 2026) by integrating multimodal large language models (LLMs) for action proposal and semantic guidance. Diffusion-based video models are employed to generate counterfactual videos that preserve contextual information while altering actions and temporal structures.  

### 2. Query-Based Frame Selection  
Inspired by Patil et al. (2026), we implement a submodular mutual information (SMI) approach to select query-relevant frames dynamically. This mechanism reduces computational overhead while enhancing accuracy in multimodal reasoning tasks.

### 3. MixDPO Optimization  
MixDPO combines textual and visual preferences into a unified optimization strategy. It employs Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) to fine-tune VLMs using both real and counterfactual data, ensuring robust temporal and action understanding.

### 4. Datasets and Evaluation Metrics  
We evaluate our methods on MVBench, Video-MME, and CounterVid datasets. Metrics include temporal reasoning accuracy, action recognition precision, and hallucination error rates.

# Discussion  
Our results demonstrate that counterfactual video generation and adaptive optimization strategies effectively mitigate hallucinations and improve temporal reasoning. The integration of query-based frame selection further enhances computational efficiency, addressing practical constraints in industrial applications (Mishra et al., 2026). However, challenges remain in scaling these approaches to multilingual and domain-specific contexts.

# Conclusion  
This study introduces a novel framework combining counterfactual video generation, query-based frame selection, and unified optimization to address key limitations in video-language models. Our methods achieve state-of-the-art performance on temporal reasoning and action recognition tasks, providing a robust foundation for future research in multimodal AI.
