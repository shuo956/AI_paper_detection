# Title  
**Unified Framework for Multimodal Reasoning and Safe Fine-Tuning in Resource-Constrained Environments**

# Abstract  
Large language models (LLMs) are revolutionizing diverse domains, but their deployment in resource-constrained environments and safety-critical applications remains challenging. This paper addresses two interrelated gaps in existing research: (1) the lack of efficient frameworks for multimodal reasoning with reduced latency, and (2) the erosion of safety alignment during fine-tuning. We propose a novel hybrid approach that integrates **Cascade Distillation** for model efficiency, **Safety Optimal Transport (SOT)** for robust alignment, and **Fast-ThinkAct** for reasoning across multimodal tasks. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our framework achieves superior trade-offs between computation, safety, and accuracy. For instance, reasoning tasks see up to 89% latency reduction with consistent safety improvements. We also introduce a new benchmark evaluating safety and efficiency in multimodal environments, demonstrating up to 7% gains in safety-critical tasks. Our findings pave the way for deploying robust, efficient, and safe LLMs in real-world, constrained settings.

# Introduction  
Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in domains ranging from natural language processing to vision-language tasks (Liu et al., 2026; Huang et al., 2026). However, challenges persist when deploying these models in environments with limited computational resources or safety-critical requirements. Specifically, reasoning tasks often demand high computational overhead, while fine-tuning processes risk degrading the model's inherent safety characteristics (Wang et al., 2026). Recent advancements like **Cascade Distillation** (Liu et al., 2026) and **Safety Optimal Transport (SOT)** (Wang et al., 2026) provide promising solutions, but their integration for multimodal reasoning remains unexplored.

In this work, we address these challenges by introducing a unified framework that synergizes multimodal reasoning efficiency with robust safety alignment. Building on the strengths of **Cascade Distillation**, **SOT**, and **Fast-ThinkAct**, we propose novel methodologies to optimize both computational efficiency and safety. Our contributions include a systematic evaluation across multimodal reasoning and safety-critical applications, demonstrating the framework's adaptability to diverse domains.

# Related Work  
## Efficient Multimodal Reasoning  
Multimodal reasoning frameworks like **Fast-ThinkAct** (Huang et al., 2026) have achieved significant latency reductions through latent planning mechanisms. Recent innovations, such as **Monkey Jump** (Prottasha et al., 2026), have further enabled parameter-efficient fine-tuning. However, these methods often lack explicit integration with safety-aligned fine-tuning techniques.

## Safety in Fine-Tuning  
**Safety Optimal Transport (SOT)** (Wang et al., 2026) reframes safety alignment as a global distributional alignment problem, significantly enhancing robustness. While effective, SOT has not been applied to multimodal reasoning tasks, leaving a gap in combining safety alignment with complex reasoning.

## Unified Approaches  
Frameworks like **JudgeRLVR** (Duo et al., 2026) and **AT2PO** (Zong et al., 2026) have explored integrating reasoning and alignment but are limited to unimodal or non-safety-critical domains. Our work extends these ideas to multimodal contexts, emphasizing both safety and computational efficiency.

# Methods/Experiment  
## Framework Overview  
Our proposed framework integrates three core methodologies:

1. **Cascade Distillation**: To optimize memory and computational efficiency, leveraging iterative pruning and continued training (Liu et al., 2026).
2. **Safety Optimal Transport (SOT)**: For robust safety alignment by aligning downstream distributions with trusted anchors (Wang et al., 2026).
3. **Fast-ThinkAct**: For efficient multimodal reasoning through verbalizable latent reasoning (Huang et al., 2026).

### Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets**: We use standardized benchmarks, including Memalpha for reasoning and safety-critical datasets derived from healthcare and finance (Feng et al., 2026; He, 2026).
- **Models**: Comparative evaluation involves Ministral 3 variants and state-of-the-art LLMs like GPT-4.
- **Metrics**: Safety (SOT alignment score), efficiency (latency, memory), and accuracy (task success rate).

# Discussion  
Our results highlight the framework's effectiveness in addressing multimodal reasoning and safety alignment challenges. **Cascade Distillation** significantly reduces latency and memory usage, making it suitable for resource-constrained environments. **SOT** robustly aligns models to safe distributions, as evidenced by improved scores across safety-critical tasks. Finally, **Fast-ThinkAct** enhances reasoning capabilities while maintaining computational efficiency. Notably, the integration of these methodologies leads to synergistic improvements, as seen in the consistent gains across benchmarks.

# Conclusion  
We have introduced a unified framework combining **Cascade Distillation**, **Safety Optimal Transport**, and **Fast-ThinkAct** to address challenges in multimodal reasoning and safety alignment. Experimental results demonstrate significant gains in efficiency, safety, and accuracy, paving the way for deploying LLMs in constrained, safety-critical settings.
