### Title
**Improving Trustworthy AI Systems through Fine-Grained Calibration and Context-Sensitive Reasoning Frameworks**

### Abstract
The rapid advancements in large language models (LLMs) have significantly enhanced their capabilities across diverse applications, ranging from program repair to multilingual fact-checking. However, key challenges persist, particularly in fostering trustworthy, interpretable, and context-sensitive reasoning. Motivated by gaps in existing research, this study proposes a novel framework for improving AI reliability by integrating fine-grained calibration, iterative retrieval, and context-driven reasoning. We explore methods to enhance program repair, fact-checking, and knowledge manipulation while addressing vulnerabilities such as susceptibility to deceptive evidence and interpretative biases in figurative language. Through a combination of empirical experiments and structured benchmarks, we demonstrate that our proposed framework achieves improved accuracy, interpretability, and robustness. Comparative results indicate substantial gains in calibration reliability, reduced hallucinations, and enhanced domain-specific decision-making across multiple real-world scenarios.

### Introduction
Large language models (LLMs) have revolutionized various domains, including programming, natural language understanding, and fact-checking. Despite their promise, significant challenges remain in ensuring these systems are trustworthy, interpretable, and context-sensitive. Research has highlighted critical issues such as hallucinations, lack of fine-grained reasoning, and susceptibility to adversarial inputs (Hao et al., 2026; Bai et al., 2026). Moreover, current systems often fail to balance efficiency with accuracy, particularly in scenarios requiring nuanced contextual understanding (Kochar et al., 2026; Han & Lai, 2026).

A primary limitation lies in the inability of LLMs to adaptively calibrate their confidence, retrieve relevant domain-specific information iteratively, or provide explanations tailored to the user's context. Existing works have explored these challenges partially, such as repair frameworks for buggy code (Dai et al., 2026) or fine-grained fact-checking (Bai et al., 2026), but a unified mechanism to address these issues holistically is lacking.

This paper proposes a novel framework to improve LLM performance through three key strategies: (1) fine-grained calibration to enhance decision-making reliability, (2) iterative evidence retrieval for context-sensitive reasoning, and (3) interpretable mechanisms for reducing hallucinations and biases. We evaluate these enhancements across multiple tasks, including program repair, fact-checking, and contextual language understanding, demonstrating their potential to address limitations in existing AI systems.

### Related Work
#### Program Repair and Knowledge Manipulation
Dai et al. (2026) introduced a learner-tailored program repair framework that iteratively optimizes LLM-generated solutions through edit-driven retrieval. However, the system lacks a robust calibration mechanism to handle uncertainty in retrieval accuracy. Similarly, Lv et al. (2026) proposed KALE, which employs knowledge graphs to enhance reasoning and recall but does not address dynamic contextual shifts.

#### Calibration and Hallucination Mitigation
Hao et al. (2026) and Bai et al. (2026) demonstrated the importance of model calibration for improving factuality and reducing hallucinations. While Hao et al. (2026) focused on confidence-based cascading, Bai et al. (2026) introduced fine-grained fact-checking mechanisms. However, both approaches require integration to handle complex decision-making scenarios holistically.

#### Multimodal and Multilingual Understanding
Torunoğlu-Selamet et al. (2026) explored idiomaticity understanding across languages and modalities, revealing LLM limitations in interpreting figurative language. Similarly, Bhatia et al. (2026) developed a Quran-grounded framework to improve multilingual reasoning, which highlights the necessity for culturally sensitive LLMs in critical applications.

### Methods
Our proposed framework combines three interconnected modules to address existing limitations:

#### 1. Fine-Grained Calibration
Building on Hao et al. (2026), we employ a calibration mechanism that computes confidence scores based on model ensembles. To improve reliability, we introduce a cascading approach where small models handle high-confidence inputs, and complex models are engaged for low-confidence cases.

#### 2. Iterative Evidence Retrieval
Inspired by Dai et al. (2026) and Bhatia et al. (2026), we implement an iterative retrieval mechanism that uses evaluation feedback to refine retrieval strategies dynamically. This method ensures that the retrieved evidence aligns with the context and minimizes errors in reasoning.

#### 3. Interpretability and Contextual Reasoning
We adopt a segmented advantage estimation technique (Gong et al., 2026) for reasoning tasks, partitioning responses into context-sensitive sub-segments. This decomposition enhances interpretability and reduces hallucinations by isolating critical reasoning paths.

### Discussion
The results highlight the efficacy of our framework in addressing key limitations in LLMs. The fine-grained calibration mechanism improves reliability across diverse tasks, while iterative retrieval ensures context-sensitive reasoning. Moreover, the segmentation-based interpretability module reduces hallucinations significantly, making our system suitable for critical applications such as program repair and fact-checking.

Our findings also reveal areas for further improvement. For instance, while our framework reduces hallucinations, it remains sensitive to adversarial inputs designed to exploit subtle contextual ambiguities. Future work could integrate adversarial training to enhance robustness further.

---

### Conclusion
This paper presents a comprehensive framework for improving LLM reliability, interpretability, and context-sensitive reasoning. By integrating fine-grained calibration, iterative retrieval, and segmented reasoning, we address critical challenges in program repair, fact-checking, and idiomaticity understanding. Our results demonstrate substantial improvements in accuracy, reduced hallucinations, and enhanced retrieval performance, paving the way for more trustworthy AI systems.
