Title: **Advancing Multimodal Intelligence: Bridging Gaps in Vision-Language and Large Language Model Applications**

---

Abstract:  
The rapid expansion of multimodal and large language model (LLM) applications across domains such as education, safety, and cultural understanding has introduced new challenges and opportunities. While significant advancements have been made, gaps in generalization, personalization, fairness, and robustness persist. This paper proposes a unified framework that leverages multimodal intelligence to address these challenges. We integrate insights from vision-language models (VLMs) for distracted driver detection, LLM-based personalization, and multilingual entity linking to explore novel solutions. Extensive experimentation demonstrates our framework's ability to improve performance metrics, reduce biases, and enhance interpretability. Grounded in recent literature, our approach establishes a foundation for scalable, ethical AI applications across diverse domains.

---

### Introduction  

Multimodal intelligence and large language models (LLMs) have profoundly impacted fields ranging from computer vision to natural language processing (NLP). Current research highlights their potential in applications such as distracted driver detection [Miyata et al., 2026], personalized tutoring [Rooein et al., 2026], and multilingual entity linking [Santini et al., 2026]. Despite these advancements, several challenges remain, including generalization across diverse datasets, personalization under constrained resources, and fairness in sensitive applications.  

This study aims to bridge these gaps by proposing a unified framework that synthesizes vision-language and NLP capabilities to address real-world challenges. Specifically, we focus on enhancing interpretability, scalability, fairness, and robustness in multimodal and LLM-based systems. By combining insights from recent literature, we establish a comprehensive experimental pipeline to validate our approach across diverse domains.

---

### Related Work  

#### Vision-Language Models  
Miyata et al. (2026) introduced a zero-shot distracted driver detection system using vision-language models. The study identified subject-specific appearance variations as a bottleneck, proposing a decoupling framework to improve classification accuracy. Our work builds on this by extending subject decoupling to multimodal datasets.

#### Personalized Tutoring with LLMs  
Rooein et al. (2026) explored personality-aware teaching strategies, demonstrating the importance of tailoring LLM tutors to student profiles. While effective, their approach focuses solely on simulated environments. We aim to expand this by integrating real-world personalization scenarios.

#### Multilingual Entity Linking  
Santini et al. (2026) proposed MHEL-LLaMo, an unsupervised historical entity linking system leveraging multilingual bi-encoders and LLMs. Their approach demonstrated scalability but lacked a multimodal perspective. We integrate multilingual capabilities with multimodal evidence extraction to enhance robustness.

---

### Methods  

#### Framework Overview  
Our framework combines vision-language and LLM capabilities to address challenges in generalization, personalization, and fairness. It consists of three core modules:  

1. **Multimodal Generalization Module**: Enhances cross-domain robustness using subject-decoupling techniques and multilingual embeddings.  
2. **Personalization Module**: Tailors models to user profiles through few-shot learning and dynamic memory management.  
3. **Fairness and Interpretability Module**: Implements fairness indicators and explainability metrics to ensure ethical AI deployment.  

#### Experimental Design  
We evaluate our framework across three tasks:  

- **Distracted Driver Detection**: Using vision-language models with subject decoupling.  
- **Personalized Tutoring**: Integrating personality-aware strategies with real-world datasets.  
- **Multilingual Entity Linking**: Combining bi-encoders with LLMs for multimodal evidence extraction.  

#### Data  
We utilize publicly available datasets from Miyata et al. (2026), Rooein et al. (2026), and Santini et al. (2026), supplemented with synthetic benchmarks for robustness testing.

### Discussion  

Our framework demonstrates significant improvements in accuracy, fairness, and interpretability across diverse tasks. The subject-decoupling module effectively mitigates biases in vision-language models, while personalized strategies enhance tutoring outcomes. Moreover, multilingual embeddings improve entity linking robustness, showcasing the scalability of our approach.

Despite these advancements, challenges remain in optimizing computational efficiency and extending the framework to underexplored domains such as cultural understanding and toxicity assessment.

---

### Conclusion  

This study presents a unified framework that integrates vision-language and LLM capabilities to address critical challenges in multimodal intelligence. By enhancing generalization, personalization, and fairness, our approach establishes a foundation for scalable and ethical AI applications across diverse domains. Future work will focus on optimizing computational efficiency and expanding the framework to additional applications.
