### Title  
**Synthetic Data Loops and Bias Evolution in Large Language Models: A Framework for Performative Feedback Analysis and Mitigation**

---

### Abstract  
The increasing reliance on synthetic data for training Large Language Models (LLMs) introduces a self-consuming performative loop, where models are iteratively refined based on their own outputs, leading to potential biases and performance degradation. This study introduces the Self-Consuming Performative Loop (SCPL) framework, which explores bias dynamics under controlled iterative training via synthetic data. By analyzing feedback-driven evolution of preference bias and disparate bias across multiple real-world tasks, SCPL reveals critical insights into the interplay between retraining settings and bias amplification. To mitigate these biases, we propose a reward-based rejection sampling strategy that reduces preference bias without compromising performance. Our findings contribute to the development of more trustworthy and bias-aware synthetic data training pipelines for LLMs.

---

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized applications spanning natural language processing, multimodal reasoning, and domain-specific tasks. However, their reliance on synthetic data for iterative refinement introduces risks of bias proliferation and performance degradation, forming what we term a **Self-Consuming Performative Loop (SCPL)**. In this loop, models trained on their own outputs may amplify biases, underserve specific user groups, and degrade overall fairness. The dynamic nature of user feedback in real-world systems further complicates this issue, necessitating robust methods to analyze and mitigate bias evolution.

Existing studies highlight biases in LLMs through gender patterns in persuasive language (Pauli et al., 2026) and disparities in calibrated confidence scores (Sabir et al., 2026). Additionally, works like Liu et al. (2026) and Wang et al. (2026) propose frameworks for cross-domain misinformation detection and continual learning, respectively, emphasizing the need for robust strategies to prevent bias amplification during iterative training. Our work builds on these insights by systematically studying the SCPL framework across two training paradigms: retraining and incremental fine-tuning. We propose a reward-based rejection sampling strategy to address synthetic data-induced bias, contributing to the development of equitable and efficient LLMs.

### Related Work  
Several research efforts underline the challenges posed by bias in LLMs:  

1. **Bias in synthetic data**: Studies such as Pauli et al. (2026) and Sabir et al. (2026) reveal gender and calibration biases in persuasive text generation and predictive certainty, respectively.  
2. **Iterative training and feedback loops**: Sato (2026) and Wang et al. (2026) explore feedback mechanisms in modular LLMs and continual learning, emphasizing the importance of balancing task performance and fairness.  
3. **Mitigation strategies**: Liu et al. (2026) and Zhang et al. (2026) propose retrieval-augmented reasoning and mutual information-based pruning to reduce biases during training.  

Our study complements these works by focusing explicitly on the SCPL framework, analyzing bias evolution, and proposing a novel reward-based sampling strategy to mitigate bias in iterative training settings.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Framework Design  
We investigate the SCPL framework under controlled performative feedback settings. Two training paradigms are explored:  

1. **Retraining**: Models are iteratively trained on synthetic data generated by prior iterations.  
2. **Incremental Fine-Tuning**: Models are fine-tuned incrementally on synthetic data without complete retraining.  

#### Bias Metrics  
To quantify bias evolution, we measure:  
- **Preference Bias**: Imbalance in model predictions favoring specific demographic groups.  
- **Disparate Bias**: Differences in prediction accuracy across demographic groups.  

#### Mitigation Strategy  
We propose a **reward-based rejection sampling** strategy. Synthetic data samples are assigned reward scores based on fairness criteria and task relevance. Samples with low scores are rejected, ensuring that training data is balanced while maintaining task performance.

#### Experimental Setup  
Experiments were conducted on three real-world tasks:  
1. **Gendered Pronoun Resolution** (Sabir et al., 2026).  
2. **Cross-Domain Misinformation Detection** (Liu et al., 2026).  
3. **Environmental Variable Mapping** (Zermatten et al., 2026).  

Bias evolution was analyzed across multiple iterations, comparing retraining and fine-tuning settings.

### Discussion  
Our results demonstrate that bias evolves significantly during iterative training, particularly under the retraining paradigm. Preference bias and disparate bias increase across iterations, correlating with performance degradation. Incremental fine-tuning exhibits milder bias evolution but still suffers from fairness issues.

The reward-based rejection sampling strategy effectively reduces bias metrics while mitigating performance loss. By prioritizing fairness and task relevance in synthetic data selection, this approach ensures equitable model improvement over iterations.

---

### Conclusion  
The SCPL framework provides a robust mechanism for analyzing bias evolution in synthetic data-driven training loops. Our reward-based rejection sampling strategy addresses the critical challenge of bias amplification, contributing to the development of more trustworthy and equitable LLMs.
