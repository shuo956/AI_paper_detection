### Title
**Enhancing Multimodal Large Language Model Understanding: A Novel Framework for Cross-Domain Generalization**

---

### Abstract
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) face challenges in achieving efficient cross-domain understanding due to redundant attention mechanisms and fragmented data insights. The study builds upon foundational work in multimodal fusion, hierarchical attention optimization, and domain-specific fine-tuning techniques. We propose a novel framework integrating hierarchical redundancy pruning with adaptive cross-modal alignment. Our method reduces processing latency while improving semantic fidelity across diverse applications, including sentiment analysis, video understanding, and multilingual retrieval. Experimental evaluations demonstrate significant gains in model efficiency and accuracy, outperforming state-of-the-art approaches. The framework generalizes effectively across linguistic, visual, and task-specific modalities, paving the way for robust, scalable MLLM solutions.

---

### Introduction
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have emerged as cutting-edge tools for tasks requiring the integration of text, image, and audio modalities. Despite their advancements, challenges persist in achieving efficient cross-domain generalization, semantic fidelity, and computational scalability. Prior research (e.g., Qiao et al., 2026; Zhou et al., 2026) highlights the importance of expert-guided multimodal fusion and hierarchical attention mechanisms but lacks comprehensive strategies for addressing redundancy and aligning domain-specific nuances. This study aims to bridge these gaps by introducing a framework for optimizing cross-domain generalization in MLLMs, leveraging adaptive attention pruning and multimodal alignment techniques inspired by recent advancements in large-scale pretraining and cross-modal integration.

---

### Related Work
Research into multimodal frameworks has seen significant advancements. Qiao et al. (2026) introduced a unified approach for emotion recognition, integrating hierarchical dynamic gating with large language models (LLMs). Zhou et al. (2026) tackled the challenge of LLM-generated text detection, emphasizing statistical inference and robustness. Černý et al. (2026) proposed visualizing lexical surprisal for enhancing text readability, while Le et al. (2026) explored neuron-level interventions for multilingual LLMs. These works provide the foundation for multimodal optimization but do not address redundancy in attention mechanisms or cross-domain scalability.

Li et al. (2026) developed MMViR for long-range video understanding, emphasizing multi-granularity representations. Phuong et al. (2026) and Luo et al. (2026) investigated domain-specific fine-tuning and structural analysis, respectively, while Liang et al. (2026) focused on constituency parsing for historical languages. These studies highlight the need for efficient cross-domain generalization, which we aim to address through our proposed framework.

### Methods/Experiment

#### Framework Overview
The proposed framework incorporates:
1. **Hierarchical Redundancy Pruning**: Efficiently identifies and eliminates redundant attention heads, inspired by the BOS sink phenomenon (Sok et al., 2026).
2. **Adaptive Cross-Modal Alignment**: Aligns multimodal representations through embedding-space optimization, leveraging techniques from SafeRedir (Liu et al., 2026).

#### Dataset
We utilized datasets across diverse domains:
- **Emotion Recognition**: MELD and MOSEI (Qiao et al., 2026).
- **Video Understanding**: MMViR structured datasets (Li et al., 2026).
- **Multilingual Retrieval**: Qwen3-VL benchmarks (Li et al., 2026).

#### Experimental Design
Our experiment involved:
1. **Baseline Models**: Comparison with MMViR and Qwen3-VL.
2. **Evaluation Metrics**:
   - Accuracy
   - Latency
   - Semantic Fidelity
