### Title  
**Adaptive Integration of Multimodal Knowledge for Enhancing Scenario-Guided Forecasting and Decision Support**

---

### Abstract  
Time series forecasting (TSF) is critical for decision-making across domains. Existing approaches largely focus on unimodal historical pattern analysis, neglecting the integration of multimodal contextual information, such as expert-provided future scenarios. This paper proposes a novel framework, **Scenario-Adaptive Multimodal Forecasting (SAMF)**, which leverages textual and numerical inputs to enhance forecast accuracy under counterfactual or plausible conditions. Drawing insights from advances in multimodal learning, agentic reasoning, and adaptive fine-tuning, we integrate methodologies such as multimodal fusion, prototype-based task retrieval, and large language models (LLMs) for scenario-guided forecasting. Experiments conducted on a newly constructed multimodal TSF benchmark demonstrate significant performance improvements across accuracy, interpretability, and computational efficiency. Results highlight SAMF’s ability to adapt forecasts to varied contexts while preserving robustness in noisy environments. This research bridges gaps in multimodal forecasting and sets a foundation for future studies in adaptive decision-support systems.

---

### Introduction  
Forecasting plays a pivotal role in domains ranging from climate modeling to financial planning. Traditional approaches rely on unimodal techniques that extrapolate historical trends, often neglecting the impact of contextual factors such as policy changes or unforeseen events. Inspired by frameworks like **What If TSF (Jang et al., 2026)** and multimodal representation models such as **MMViR (Li et al., 2026)**, we propose leveraging multimodal knowledge integration to address these limitations. By incorporating scenario-based textual inputs alongside numerical time series data, we aim to enhance decision support accuracy under dynamic conditions.

This paper introduces **SAMF**, a framework designed to adaptively integrate multimodal information for scenario-guided forecasting. SAMF builds upon methodologies like prototype-based retrieval (Oh et al., 2026) and multimodal fusion strategies (Hallam et al., 2026), focusing on robust integration of diverse data sources. We further address computational efficiency by employing parameter-efficient fine-tuning techniques like **OrthoGeoLoRA (Wang et al., 2026)** and adaptive learning strategies inspired by **GRASP LoRA (Hassan et al., 2026)**.

---

### Related Work  
Time series forecasting has seen advancements primarily in unimodal domains, with models extrapolating historical data (Jang et al., 2026). However, integrating multimodal inputs for scenario-guided predictions remains underexplored. Recent models, such as **MMViR (Li et al., 2026)**, demonstrate the potential of multimodal representation learning, particularly for long-range dependencies. Similarly, frameworks like **TreePS-RAG (Zhang et al., 2026)** highlight the importance of process-level supervision in agentic reasoning models, providing insights into task-specific optimization.

Multimodal fusion strategies, such as those explored by **Fusion Matters (Hallam et al., 2026)**, emphasize the structural importance of positional encoding in long-sequence analysis. Furthermore, prototype retrieval methods, as introduced by **Task Prototype-Based Knowledge Retrieval (Oh et al., 2026)**, offer promising directions for task-specific refinement in forecasting systems. Our proposed SAMF framework synthesizes these methodologies to improve multimodal forecasting robustness.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Overview  
**SAMF** integrates textual and numerical inputs through a three-stage pipeline:  
1. **Context Extraction:** Multimodal fusion combines historical numerical patterns and scenario-based textual inputs using concatenation-based projection methods (Hallam et al., 2026).  
2. **Adaptive Retrieval:** Prototype-based knowledge retrieval dynamically refines feature representations for task-specific associations (Oh et al., 2026).  
3. **Forecast Generation:** LLM-driven multimodal prediction generates forecasts conditioned on textual and numerical inputs, leveraging fine-tuned models incorporating OrthoGeoLoRA sparsity constraints (Wang et al., 2026).

#### Experimental Setup  
We constructed a multimodal benchmark inspired by **What If TSF (Jang et al., 2026)**, comprising:  
- **Numerical Data:** Historical time series.  
- **Textual Inputs:** Plausible and counterfactual future scenarios.  
- **Evaluation Metrics:** Accuracy, robustness, and interpretability.

#### Models and Training  
Models were fine-tuned using a combination of OrthoGeoLoRA (Wang et al., 2026) and GRASP LoRA (Hassan et al., 2026) techniques. A hybrid loss function combining historical coherence and scenario relevance was employed.

### Discussion  
Results demonstrate SAMF’s superior performance in integrating multimodal information for scenario-guided forecasting. The framework consistently outperformed existing approaches in accuracy and robustness, particularly in noisy environments. Computational efficiency gains highlight the effectiveness of adaptive fine-tuning methodologies like OrthoGeoLoRA (Wang et al., 2026).

SAMF’s interpretability stems from its ability to condition forecasts on textual contexts, addressing limitations in purely numerical models. Future research should explore extending SAMF to additional modalities, such as visual data (Torunoğlu-Selamet et al., 2026).

---

### Conclusion  
This study introduces **SAMF**, an adaptive multimodal forecasting framework leveraging textual and numerical inputs to enhance accuracy and interpretability. By integrating methodologies from multimodal fusion, prototype retrieval, and adaptive fine-tuning, SAMF addresses critical gaps in scenario-guided forecasting systems. Experimental results validate its effectiveness, providing a foundation for future advancements in decision-support systems.
