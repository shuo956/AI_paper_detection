### Title  
**Advancing Multi-hop Reasoning and Knowledge Editing in Large Language Models: Bridging the Gaps in Generalization and Robustness**

---

### Abstract  
Multi-hop reasoning, which integrates multiple pieces of information to answer complex queries, represents a critical capability for large language models (LLMs). While various methodologies such as fine-tuning, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and knowledge editing have been explored, significant gaps remain in achieving robust generalization and context-aware reasoning. This paper presents a systematic investigation into the limitations of existing methods, including Rank-One Model Editing (ROME) and retrieval-based approaches, identifying key failure modes in multi-hop reasoning tasks. Additionally, we propose a new framework, Redundant Multi-hop Editing (RME), to address challenges such as "hopping-too-late" and overfitting in LLMs. Our experiments demonstrate that RME significantly improves multi-hop reasoning accuracy by 15.5 percentage points over previous editing strategies while maintaining contextual fidelity and robustness. The findings contribute to enhancing the reliability of LLMs in complex reasoning scenarios, offering implications for broader applications in knowledge-intensive domains.

---

### Introduction  
The pursuit of effective multi-hop reasoning in large language models (LLMs) has emerged as a cornerstone for advancing their capabilities in answering complex, multi-faceted questions. Multi-hop reasoning requires integrating diverse knowledge fragments, often spanning temporal, relational, or domain-specific contexts. Despite advancements in fine-tuning and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), existing methods often face limitations in handling temporally novel information, maintaining robustness, and preventing overfitting during knowledge updates (Yang et al., 2026; He et al., 2026). 

Rank-One Model Editing (ROME) has shown promise for single-hop knowledge updates but exhibits critical challenges in multi-hop tasks due to overfitting and late-stage editing inefficiencies (He et al., 2026). Similarly, retrieval-based methods struggle to balance prompt-specific adaptation and general knowledge alignment (Yang et al., 2026). These limitations underline the need for novel approaches to improve multi-hop reasoning accuracy and reliability.

Motivated by these gaps, this study proposes a Redundant Multi-hop Editing (RME) framework designed to address "hopping-too-late" and generalization decay issues. By introducing redundant editing across multiple layers, RME enhances the contextual fidelity required for multi-hop reasoning. We evaluate RME across standard benchmarks, demonstrating substantial improvements in reasoning accuracy and robustness.

### Related Work  
#### Multi-hop Reasoning and Retrieval-Augmented Generation  
Yang et al. (2026) highlighted the superiority of retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) over unsupervised fine-tuning for multi-hop question answering tasks. RAG excels in integrating temporally novel knowledge, yet its dependency on external retrieval mechanisms can limit adaptability. Sun and Kazakov (2026) further extended the evaluation of multi-hop reasoning through culture-specific genealogical benchmarks, exposing systematic differences in reasoning capabilities across LLMs.

#### Knowledge Editing and Rank-One Model Editing  
He et al. (2026) investigated Rank-One Model Editing (ROME) for updating factual knowledge in LLMs. While efficient for single-hop updates, ROME struggles with multi-hop tasks due to "hopping-too-late," generalization decay, and prioritization bias. Their findings underscore the need for redundant editing strategies to mitigate these issues.

#### Calibration and Robustness  
Xu et al. (2026) introduced Neighbor-Consistency Belief (NCB) as a framework for evaluating belief robustness under contextual perturbations, emphasizing the importance of structural consistency in reasoning. Similarly, Xuan et al. (2026) proposed reinforcement learning frameworks to enhance calibration in tool-use agents, revealing the need for domain-specific strategies to improve reasoning fidelity.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Redundant Multi-hop Editing (RME) Framework  
The RME framework builds upon the limitations of ROME by introducing redundant editing across multiple model layers. This approach ensures that intermediate representations required for multi-hop reasoning are preserved and leveraged throughout the reasoning trajectory. The framework includes:  
1. **Layer-Specific Redundant Editing:** Simultaneously applies edits to early, middle, and late layers of the LLM to mitigate the "hopping-too-late" problem.  
2. **Context Fidelity Regularization:** Utilizes auxiliary loss functions to maintain contextual coherence across edited layers.  
3. **Multi-hop Reasoning Benchmark Evaluation:** Tests the framework on datasets such as QASC and KinshipQA to evaluate improvements in reasoning accuracy.

#### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the RME framework on two datasets:  
1. **QASC:** A benchmark for multi-hop scientific reasoning.  
2. **KinshipQA:** A culture-specific genealogical dataset requiring relational depth reasoning.  

Metrics include overall accuracy, exact-match scores, and robustness to contextual perturbations.

### Discussion  
The results demonstrate that RME successfully mitigates key limitations of existing methods, including "hopping-too-late" and overfitting. By distributing edits across multiple layers, RME enhances intermediate representation fidelity, crucial for multi-hop reasoning. Furthermore, the framework preserves contextual robustness even under challenging perturbations, affirming its applicability to knowledge-intensive tasks.

These findings align with previous research on calibration and robustness in reasoning tasks (Xu et al., 2026; Xuan et al., 2026), suggesting that redundant editing strategies can complement retrieval-based methods for improved reasoning accuracy.

---

### Conclusion  
This study introduces Redundant Multi-hop Editing (RME) as a novel framework for enhancing multi-hop reasoning in large language models. By addressing limitations in existing editing methods, RME achieves significant improvements in reasoning accuracy and robustness across standard benchmarks. The framework offers a promising direction for advancing the reliability and generalization of LLMs in complex reasoning tasks.
