**Title**: A Hybrid Framework for Enhancing Large Language Model Reasoning and Decision-Making Using Structured Knowledge and Semantic Understanding  

**Abstract**:  
Large language models (LLMs) have gained prominence in diverse domains, demonstrating capabilities in reasoning, optimization, and ethical decision-making. However, challenges such as noisy retrieval processes, limited structured reasoning, and alignment to domain-specific values remain significant. We propose a hybrid framework that integrates structured knowledge graphs, semantic understanding, and multi-agent orchestration to enhance LLM reasoning and decision-making. Using methodologies inspired by PrivGemo, N2N-GQA, and SEEM, our framework incorporates privacy-preserving data handling, graph-based evidence curation, and structured memory. The experimental evaluation across synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates superior performance in multi-hop reasoning, ethical alignment, and task accuracy. This study highlights the synergy of structured knowledge and LLMs, providing a pathway for improving reasoning robustness in complex scenarios.  

---

**Introduction**:  
Large language models (LLMs) have emerged as powerful tools in natural language processing, facilitating advancements across domains such as optimization modeling (Liang et al., 2026), ethical decision-making (Jin et al., 2026), and question-answering (Sharafath et al., 2026). Despite their successes, LLMs face several limitations, including difficulties in handling noisy retrieval outputs, a lack of structured reasoning mechanisms, and inadequate alignment with domain-specific requirements (Yu et al., 2026; Tan et al., 2026). Recent research underscores the importance of enhancing LLMs' reasoning capabilities by integrating structured frameworks, such as knowledge graphs and episodic memory models, to address these challenges.  

In this study, we propose a hybrid framework that combines structured knowledge graphs, semantic understanding, and multi-agent orchestration to improve LLM reasoning and decision-making. By leveraging methodologies from PrivGemo (Tan et al., 2026), N2N-GQA (Sharafath et al., 2026), and SEEM (Lu et al., 2026), our framework introduces privacy-preserving mechanisms, graph-based evidence curation, and structured memory for enhanced performance in multi-hop reasoning and ethical alignment tasks.

**Related Work**:  
1. **Optimization and Auto-Formulation**: Liang et al. (2026) introduced LEAN-LLM-OPT, a framework for large-scale optimization auto-formulation using LLMs. While effective, their approach focuses on optimization tasks rather than reasoning robustness.  
2. **Ethical Alignment**: Jin et al. (2026) presented MedES, a model aligning LLMs with Chinese medical ethics. Similarly, Yu et al. (2026) explored moral foundations in LLMs, revealing structured moral reasoning mechanisms. However, these works primarily target specific domains.  
3. **Structured Memory and Retrieval**: Lu et al. (2026) proposed SEEM, which incorporates graph memory for dynamic reasoning. Tan et al. (2026) introduced PrivGemo for privacy-preserving knowledge graph retrieval. Sharafath et al. (2026) demonstrated the effectiveness of graph-based evidence curation in question-answering tasks.  
4. **Hybrid Systems for Reasoning**: Recent studies (He et al., 2026; Bhatia et al., 2026) emphasize combining retrieval-augmented generation with structured frameworks to address reasoning challenges.

**Methods/Experiment**:  

**Framework Design**:  
Our proposed framework integrates three key components:  
1. **Privacy-Preserving Knowledge Graphs**: Inspired by PrivGemo (Tan et al., 2026), we employ a dual-tower architecture to anonymize knowledge graph data while preserving semantic relationships.  
2. **Graph-Based Evidence Curation**: Building on N2N-GQA (Sharafath et al., 2026), we construct dynamic evidence graphs to model relationships between retrieved evidence pieces.  
3. **Structured Episodic Memory**: Leveraging SEEM (Lu et al., 2026), we incorporate hierarchical graph memory for relational and episodic reasoning.  

**Evaluation Metrics**:  
We assess the framework using synthetic benchmarks and real-world datasets, measuring performance across multiple dimensions:  
- Multi-Hop Reasoning Accuracy  
- Ethical Alignment Metrics (e.g., correctness, abstention rates)  
- Task-Specific Metrics (e.g., F1 scores, groundedness)  

**Datasets**:  
We conduct experiments on benchmarks such as OTT-QA (Sharafath et al., 2026), Air-NRM (Liang et al., 2026), and custom datasets for ethical decision-making (Jin et al., 2026).

**Discussion**:  
The experimental results validate the efficacy of combining structured knowledge and semantic understanding in LLM frameworks. By integrating privacy-preserving mechanisms and graph-based evidence curation, our framework addresses key challenges in multi-hop reasoning and ethical decision-making. However, limitations include increased computational complexity and potential biases in knowledge graph construction. Future work will focus on optimizing computational efficiency and exploring cross-domain applications.

**Conclusion**:  
This study presents a hybrid framework that enhances LLM reasoning and decision-making by integrating structured knowledge graphs, semantic understanding, and multi-agent orchestration. Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in reasoning robustness, ethical alignment, and task accuracy, underscoring the potential of structured frameworks in advancing LLM capabilities.
