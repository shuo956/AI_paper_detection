### Title  
**Hybrid Multi-Hop Reasoning with Graph-Enhanced Retrieval-Augmented Frameworks for Domain-Specific Applications**

---

### Abstract  
Advancements in Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) frameworks have significantly enhanced the performance of large language models (LLMs) in multi-hop reasoning and domain-specific tasks. However, challenges such as noisy retrieval, lack of structured evidence organization, and inefficient resource utilization persist. We propose a novel hybrid framework, Graph-Enhanced Retrieval-Augmented Reasoning (GE-RAR), which integrates graph-based evidence curation with reinforcement learning techniques to optimize reasoning pathways and retrieval mechanisms. Using benchmarks from agricultural diagnostics, clinical relation extraction, and multi-hop table-text question answering, we demonstrate that GE-RAR achieves superior accuracy and computational efficiency. Our results reveal that graph-based evidence organization improves interpretability and robustness in multi-hop reasoning tasks, while reinforcement learning-based module selection ensures resource-efficient performance. This study bridges the gap between structured information representation and scalable, domain-specific reasoning, providing a foundation for future advancements in RAG-based systems.

---

### Introduction  
The growing adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) across various domains, from agriculture to healthcare, underscores their promise in tackling complex reasoning tasks. However, several challenges remain unaddressed, such as handling noisy retrieval outputs, ensuring interpretability in reasoning chains, and maintaining computational efficiency. Multi-hop reasoning, which requires synthesizing information from diverse sources, is particularly susceptible to errors arising from these challenges.  

Recent studies, such as N2N-GQA (Sharafath et al., 2026) and CIRAG (Wei et al., 2026), have highlighted the potential of graph-based evidence organization and adaptive reasoning modules for improving multi-hop reasoning. Similarly, methodologies like Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) and reinforcement learning (Zhang et al., 2026) have demonstrated their efficacy in optimizing reasoning frameworks. However, the integration of these approaches into a cohesive, scalable framework remains underexplored.  

In this work, we propose GE-RAR, a hybrid framework that combines graph-enhanced evidence curation with reinforcement learning-based reasoning for domain-specific applications. Our contributions are threefold:  
1. Introducing a graph-based evidence organization mechanism for structuring retrieval results.  
2. Employing reinforcement learning to dynamically optimize reasoning pathways.  
3. Demonstrating the efficacy of GE-RAR across diverse domain-specific benchmarks, including agricultural diagnostics, clinical text analysis, and table-text question answering.

### Related Work  
N2N-GQA demonstrated the importance of organizing evidence into structured graphs, yielding significant improvements in multi-hop reasoning tasks. Similarly, CIRAG introduced multi-granular evidence integration for robust reasoning, while Agri-R1 applied reinforcement learning to domain-specific reasoning tasks, achieving notable accuracy gains in agricultural diagnostics. In the realm of clinical NLP, Aidynkyzy et al. (2026) emphasized the importance of high-quality retrieval and reasoning for bilingual relation extraction tasks. Despite these advances, challenges such as retrieval noise and reasoning inefficiencies persist. Our work aims to address these gaps by integrating graph-based evidence structuring with reinforcement learning for dynamic reasoning optimization.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### 1. Framework Architecture  
The GE-RAR framework consists of two primary components:  
1. **Graph-Based Evidence Organization**: Retrieval outputs are processed and organized into dynamic evidence graphs. Each node represents a retrieved document or table, and edges denote semantic relationships between evidence pieces. This graph-based structure facilitates the identification of critical reasoning pathways.  
2. **Reinforcement Learning for Module Selection**: Using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO), the framework dynamically selects reasoning modules based on task requirements, balancing computational efficiency with reasoning accuracy.  

#### 2. Datasets  
We evaluated GE-RAR on three benchmarks:  
- **Agricultural Diagnostics (Agri-R1)**: Focused on disease recognition and agricultural knowledge QA.  
- **Clinical Relation Extraction**: A bilingual dataset for English and Turkish relation extraction tasks.  
- **OTT-QA**: A multi-hop table-text question answering dataset.  

#### 3. Evaluation Metrics  
We used domain-specific metrics such as EM (Exact Match) for question answering tasks and micro-F1 score for relation extraction. Computational efficiency was measured using Time to First Token (TTFT) and overall resource utilization.  

#### 4. Baselines  
We compared GE-RAR against state-of-the-art RAG frameworks, including CIRAG, N2N-GQA, and conventional RAG pipelines.

### Discussion  
The results demonstrate that GE-RAR outperforms existing RAG frameworks across multiple benchmarks, achieving substantial accuracy gains. The graph-based evidence organization proved particularly effective for multi-hop reasoning tasks, as it enabled the identification of critical reasoning pathways while mitigating retrieval noise. Furthermore, the use of reinforcement learning for module selection significantly improved computational efficiency, making the framework suitable for domain-specific applications with limited resources.  

These findings highlight the potential of integrating graph-based evidence structuring and reinforcement learning in RAG frameworks. However, challenges such as scalability to larger datasets and generalization across diverse domains remain. Future work will explore the integration of more advanced graph construction techniques and the application of GE-RAR to additional domains.  

---

### Conclusion  
This study introduces GE-RAR, a novel hybrid framework for multi-hop reasoning in domain-specific applications. By combining graph-based evidence organization with reinforcement learning, GE-RAR addresses key challenges in retrieval noise, reasoning efficiency, and interpretability. Our empirical results underscore its efficacy in improving accuracy and computational efficiency across diverse benchmarks. GE-RAR represents a significant step toward the development of scalable and robust RAG frameworks.
