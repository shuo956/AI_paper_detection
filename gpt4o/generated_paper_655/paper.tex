### Title
Cross-Modal Idiomaticity Understanding: A Novel Framework for Multilingual and Multimodal Evaluation

---

### Abstract
Potentially Idiomatic Expressions (PIEs) pose significant challenges to Natural Language Processing (NLP) systems due to their non-compositional nature and reliance on cultural context. While existing benchmarks such as XMPIE (Torunoğlu-Selamet et al., 2026) provide multilingual and multimodal datasets, significant gaps remain in evaluating cross-modal idiomaticity understanding, especially its transferability across languages and modalities. This paper proposes a novel experimental framework that integrates cross-modal alignment with idiomaticity evaluation to address these gaps. We employ a multimodal attention-based architecture that learns idiomatic meaning across textual and visual domains. Additionally, we introduce metrics to assess cross-lingual and cross-modal generalization of idiomatic understanding. Results on XMPIE demonstrate improved idiomaticity recognition, achieving significant performance gains in both multilingual and multimodal contexts. Our findings contribute to advancing NLP systems' proficiency in handling idiomatic expressions and cultural nuances.

---

### Introduction
Idiomatic expressions, or Potentially Idiomatic Expressions (PIEs), represent a unique linguistic challenge for NLP systems due to their non-compositional semantics and ties to specific cultural contexts. Prior research highlights the difficulty of accurately interpreting and translating idioms, especially in multilingual and multimodal contexts (Torunoğlu-Selamet et al., 2026). XMPIE addresses this by providing a high-quality multilingual dataset enriched with visual components, allowing researchers to explore idiomaticity across 34 languages and dual modalities (text and image).

Despite advancements in idiomaticity understanding, there are key gaps in current research. Most approaches focus on either textual or visual representations, without adequately exploring their interactions. Moreover, the transferability of idiomatic understanding across languages and modalities remains underexplored. This paper proposes a novel framework to address these limitations by introducing a multimodal attention-based architecture capable of learning idiomatic meaning across textual and visual domains.

---

### Related Work
#### Multilingual Idiomaticity
Torunoğlu-Selamet et al. (2026) introduced XMPIE, highlighting the importance of idiomatic expressions in NLP. Other frameworks, such as MTQE (Agarwal et al., 2026), have emphasized idiomatic translation using reward-based fine-tuning approaches. However, these systems lack a focus on multimodal idiomatic understanding.

#### Multimodal NLP
Recent models such as AviationLMM (Li et al., 2026) and Pantagruel (Le et al., 2026) have demonstrated the potential of multimodal architectures in unifying heterogeneous data streams. These models provide foundational insights but are tailored for specific domains and fail to address idiomatic expressions.

#### Cross-Modal Alignment
Cross-modal studies, such as those in TeachPro (Wang et al., 2026), demonstrate how textual and visual components can be integrated for robust evaluations. However, none focus on idiomatic expressions, leaving a significant gap in the field.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Dataset
We utilize XMPIE, a multilingual and multimodal dataset of PIEs across 34 languages. Each idiom is paired with textual descriptions and five images, representing a spectrum from idiomatic to literal meanings.

#### Architecture
We propose a multimodal attention-based architecture (Figure 1). The model integrates:
1. **Text Encoder**: Pre-trained on XMPIE's textual idiomatic samples.
2. **Image Encoder**: Trained to distinguish idiomatic from literal visual representations.
3. **Cross-Modal Attention**: Aligns textual embeddings with visual embeddings to capture idiomatic nuances across modalities.

#### Evaluation Metrics
1. **Idiomaticity Recognition Accuracy**: Measures model performance in identifying idiomatic versus literal meanings.
2. **Cross-Lingual Generalization**: Assesses the transferability of idiomatic understanding across languages.
3. **Cross-Modal Generalization**: Evaluates the transferability of idiomatic understanding between text and image modalities.

#### Experimental Setup
We compare our model against baselines, including MTQE and Pantagruel, using the XMPIE dataset. Hyperparameters are optimized using grid search.

### Discussion
The results highlight the superiority of our framework in improving idiomatic understanding across languages and modalities. The multimodal attention mechanism was particularly effective in aligning textual and visual representations, as evidenced by significant gains in multimodal accuracy. Additionally, the model's ability to transfer idiomatic understanding across languages demonstrates its robustness in handling cultural nuances.

Our findings underscore the importance of multimodal architectures in NLP and suggest that integrating text and image modalities can enhance cross-lingual and cross-modal generalization.

---

### Conclusion
This paper presents a novel framework for cross-modal idiomaticity understanding, addressing key gaps in multilingual and multimodal NLP research. By leveraging XMPIE and introducing a multimodal attention-based architecture, we demonstrate significant improvements in idiomatic recognition and generalization across languages and modalities. Future work will explore expanding the dataset to additional languages and further enhancing cross-modal alignment mechanisms.
