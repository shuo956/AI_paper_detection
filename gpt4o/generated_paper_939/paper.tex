# Title  
**Multimodal Contextual Safety and Robustness in Large Language Models: A New Benchmark and Evaluative Framework**

# Abstract  
The evolution of Large Language Models (LLMs) has led to significant advancements in multimodal and dialogue-based systems. Despite these advancements, challenges remain in ensuring contextual safety, robustness, and adaptability in real-world applications. Existing benchmarks and evaluation methods often fail to capture nuanced risks such as escalation-based threats or context-switch vulnerabilities. This paper introduces **SAFE-MULTI**, a novel benchmark that extends the state-of-the-art by integrating multimodal and sequential contexts for evaluating safety and robustness in LLMs. SAFE-MULTI incorporates both text and image-based interactions and emphasizes multi-turn dialogues with dynamic context shifts. A comprehensive evaluation of 10 LLMs across safety, robustness, and utility metrics demonstrates trade-offs inherent in current models. Furthermore, we introduce a lightweight context-pruning optimization method, **Dynamic Context Enhancement (DyCE)**, to mitigate risks and improve response accuracy. Our findings reveal critical gaps in contextual safety management and propose actionable paths toward more resilient multimodal systems.

# Introduction  

The integration of text and image modalities in Large Language Models (LLMs) has revolutionized applications in digital assistants, e-commerce, healthcare, and more (Liu et al., 2026; Chakrabarty & Pal, 2026). While these multimodal systems have demonstrated impressive capabilities, ensuring contextual safety and robustness—particularly in multi-turn dialogues—remains a significant challenge (Liu et al., 2026). Contextual risks such as escalation-based threats or context-switch vulnerabilities, where the same input can lead to benign or harmful outcomes depending on the surrounding context, are often overlooked in existing evaluations.

Despite advancements in safety mechanisms like guardrails (Anastasopoulos et al., 2026) and memory management techniques (Choi et al., 2026), models struggle to balance safety with preserving utility. This paper addresses these gaps by introducing **SAFE-MULTI**, a benchmark designed to evaluate multimodal, multi-turn safety and robustness. SAFE-MULTI builds on recent efforts, such as MTMCS-Bench (Liu et al., 2026), and introduces novel dimensions of evaluation, including escalation-based risks and context-switch vulnerabilities.

This study also investigates the limitations of current models and proposes a lightweight optimization method, **Dynamic Context Enhancement (DyCE)**, to improve safety and robustness. By leveraging SAFE-MULTI and DyCE, this work seeks to advance the development of safer and more reliable multimodal systems.

# Related Work  

### Multimodal Safety Benchmarks  
MTMCS-Bench (Liu et al., 2026) provides a foundational framework for evaluating contextual safety in multimodal LLMs, focusing on escalation-based and context-switch risks. However, it is limited to certain predefined scenarios and does not comprehensively evaluate robustness across diverse multimodal inputs.

### Safety and Robustness in LLMs  
Recent studies have highlighted the challenges of ensuring safe language generation and contextual accuracy. For instance, Anastasopoulos et al. (2026) formalize the theoretical challenges of safe language generation, while Rana (2026) explores why negative constraints often fail in practice. These studies underscore the need for more resilient and adaptive safety mechanisms.

### Context Management and Pruning  
Choi et al. (2026) propose Dynamic Context Pruning (DyCP) as a method for managing long-form dialogues. While effective in preserving conversational continuity, it does not address multimodal interactions or escalation-based risks.

# Methods/Experiment  

### SAFE-MULTI Benchmark  
SAFE-MULTI extends MTMCS-Bench (Liu et al., 2026) by incorporating:  
1. **Multimodal Inputs**: Text and image combinations across realistic scenarios.  
2. **Escalation-Based Risks**: Gradual emergence of unsafe contexts.  
3. **Context-Switch Vulnerabilities**: Scenarios where identical inputs lead to different outcomes based on the surrounding dialogue.

SAFE-MULTI includes 40,000 samples with structured evaluation metrics:  
- **Contextual Intent Recognition**  
- **Safety Awareness**  
- **Utility Preservation**  

### Dynamic Context Enhancement (DyCE)  
DyCE is a lightweight optimization method designed to improve safety and robustness. It dynamically segments and retrieves relevant memory at query time, preserving sequential structure while adapting to multimodal inputs. DyCE builds on dynamic pruning techniques (Choi et al., 2026) and incorporates multimodal feature fusion inspired by PixRec (Chakrabarty & Pal, 2026).

### Evaluation Protocol  
We benchmarked eight open-source and two proprietary LLMs using SAFE-MULTI. Models were evaluated across safety, robustness, and utility metrics. DyCE was applied as a post-processing step to optimize performance.

# Discussion  

The results highlight several key findings:  
1. **Trade-Offs Between Safety and Utility**: Current models struggle to balance safety with utility, often over-refusing benign dialogues to avoid risks (Liu et al., 2026).  
2. **Effectiveness of DyCE**: DyCE significantly improves safety and robustness metrics, particularly in scenarios involving escalation-based risks and context-switch vulnerabilities.  
3. **Limitations of Current Benchmarks**: SAFE-MULTI addresses gaps in existing benchmarks by incorporating multimodal and sequential contexts, but further work is needed to evaluate real-world scalability.

---

# Conclusion  

This study introduces SAFE-MULTI, a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating multimodal contextual safety and robustness in LLMs. By integrating multimodal and sequential contexts, SAFE-MULTI addresses critical gaps in existing evaluations. The proposed DyCE optimization method further enhances safety and robustness, demonstrating its potential for real-world applications. Future work will explore scaling SAFE-MULTI to additional domains and integrating real-time user feedback.
