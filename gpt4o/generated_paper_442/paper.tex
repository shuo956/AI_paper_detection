# Title  
**Enhancing Multimodal Reasoning and Personalization in Large Language Models through Context-Aware Optimization and Fine-Tuning**

---

# Abstract  
Recent advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have enabled significant improvements in reasoning across domains such as open-ended coding tasks, multilingual content, biomedical contexts, and educational dialogues. Nevertheless, challenges persist in ensuring efficiency, diversity, personalization, and robustness in reasoning models. This paper proposes an integrated framework leveraging context-aware optimization, reinforcement learning, and domain-specific fine-tuning strategies to address issues of computational redundancy, reasoning fidelity, and user personalization. Through our experiments, we validate the efficacy of combining techniques such as dynamic windowed alignment, finite-temperature Gibbs initialization, and multi-stage fine-tuning in optimizing reasoning models. Results across benchmarks demonstrate significant improvements in computational efficiency, reasoning accuracy, and personalization compared to existing paradigms.

---

# Introduction  
Large Language Models are widely deployed across diverse applications, including coding, storytelling, multilingual reasoning, and biomedical question-answering. Despite their capabilities, critical challenges remain in reasoning efficiency, handling diverse input contexts, robustness under complex task conditions, and integrating personalized user preferences without compromising computational costs. For instance, models often "overthink" tasks, generating redundant reasoning traces, or fail to adapt across diverse domains due to rigid training paradigms.

This study builds on advancements such as Knowledge-Aligned Student Error Simulator (KASER) for coding task errors and Reinforcement Learning for Creative Storytelling (RLCS), focusing on achieving higher efficiency and reasoning fidelity. We propose an integrated framework combining dynamic optimization strategies, reinforcement-based fine-tuning, and personalization protocols to address these gaps. A novel experimental pipeline is presented, which systematically evaluates the proposed methods against strong benchmarks.

---

# Related Work  
Several prior works have informed our approach:  
1. **Reasoning Fidelity**: Methods such as ConMax (Hu et al., 2026) and Laser (Wang et al., 2026) highlight the importance of preserving logical coherence while compressing reasoning traces.  
2. **Personalization**: Liu et al. (2026) demonstrated the use of natural language as a universal interface for user preference representation, enabling transferable personalization across tasks.  
3. **Optimization Strategies**: GIFT by Zhao et al. (2026) introduces temperature-based Gibbs initialization to mitigate optimization mismatch during post-training.  
4. **Educational Dialogue and Error Simulation**: Sharma et al. (2026) and Duan et al. (2026) provide insights into constructing educational and error-simulation datasets for training models on open-ended tasks.  

However, existing methods often focus on isolated aspects of reasoning or personalization, failing to address the broader interplay between reasoning efficiency, personalization, and domain-specific adaptability. This paper aims to integrate and enhance these approaches into a cohesive framework.

# Methods/Experiment  

## Framework Overview  
We propose a hybrid framework combining:  
1. **Dynamic Windowed Alignment Learning (DWAL)**: Reformulates reasoning via probabilistic superposition by aligning latent states to dynamic semantic windows (Wang et al., 2026).  
2. **GIFT Initialization**: Applies finite-temperature Gibbs initialization to maintain distributional clarity across reasoning tasks (Zhao et al., 2026).  
3. **Personalized Fine-Tuning**: Implements natural language-based user preference modeling (Liu et al., 2026) for enhanced personalization.  

### Dataset  
We utilized the following datasets:  
- CodeMixQA (Winata et al., 2026) for multilingual reasoning.  
- ConvoLearn (Sharma et al., 2026) for educational dialogues.  
- MedHopQA (Nguyen et al., 2026) for biomedical reasoning.  

### Experimental Setup  
#### Models  
We tested two open-source LLMs: Qwen-Coder-7B and DeepSeek-Coder-6.7B.  
#### Fine-Tuning Protocol  
A multi-stage process was implemented:  
1. Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) using curated datasets.  
2. Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) for domain-specific adaptability.  
3. Reinforcement learning (RL) for reasoning optimization.  

#### Metrics  
Evaluation was performed using accuracy, reasoning fidelity, computational efficiency (Time to First Token), and personalization consistency.

# Discussion  
The proposed framework demonstrates substantial improvements in reasoning accuracy and computational efficiency while maintaining high fidelity across diverse domains. Dynamic alignment and Gibbs initialization effectively address mode collapse and redundancy in reasoning traces. Additionally, personalized fine-tuning via natural language interfaces improves user experience and adaptability across tasks.  

Despite these gains, further optimization is required in handling extreme out-of-distribution cases, especially under multilingual and biomedical settings. Future work could explore adaptive reasoning density and hybrid retrieval-augmented generation strategies.

---

# Conclusion  
This paper introduces a hybrid framework integrating dynamic windowed alignment, personalized fine-tuning, and Gibbs initialization to enhance reasoning accuracy, efficiency, and personalization in LLMs. Extensive experiments across benchmarks demonstrate its effectiveness in addressing key limitations of existing paradigms. Future directions include optimizing retrieval-based reasoning and extending personalization protocols to handle complex, multi-hop tasks.
