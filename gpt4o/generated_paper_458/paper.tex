### Title
**Position-Sensitive Retrieval Augmentation for Narrative Coherence and Multilingual Robustness in Large Language Models**

### Abstract
The development of efficient, coherent, and robust retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems for large language models (LLMs) remains a challenge, particularly in long-context, multilingual, and narrative-sensitive tasks. Motivated by gaps in existing frameworks, this study introduces a novel hybrid approach to integrate position-sensitive retrieval, multilingual robustness, and narrative coherence into LLM architectures. Leveraging insights from position-aware benchmarks like PosIR and narrative salience models like Narrative Twins, we propose a **Position-Sensitive Narrative Retrieval Framework (PSNRF)**. The framework employs structured episodic memory inspired by SEEM, combined with multilingual robustness strategies adapted from MLCL. Experiments demonstrate that PSNRF significantly improves performance on PosIR, MLCL, and long-context narrative benchmarks, achieving higher coherence, retrieval precision, and multilingual execution accuracy. By addressing position bias, structural dependencies, and language-specific challenges, this study sets a new direction for retrieval-augmented systems, fostering their application in real-world multilingual and complex-context scenarios.

---

### Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing tasks, including long-context generation, multilingual interactions, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). Despite their successes, challenges persist in ensuring their robustness and relevance during retrieval and generation, especially in tasks involving long contexts, narrative coherence, and multilingual data. Traditional RAG systems often exhibit significant limitations, such as position bias (Zeng et al., 2026), narrative fragmentation (Sterner et al., 2026), and poor multilingual robustness (Luo et al., 2026). Addressing these deficiencies is critical for advancing real-world applications in domains like healthcare, digital humanities, and legal systems.

This paper introduces the **Position-Sensitive Narrative Retrieval Framework (PSNRF)**, a hybrid solution designed to mitigate position bias, enhance multilingual capabilities, and maintain narrative coherence. Drawing inspiration from Structured Episodic Event Memory (SEEM) (Lu et al., 2026) and PosIR (Zeng et al., 2026), PSNRF integrates position-aware retrieval mechanisms with a narrative-sensitive augmentation layer. Furthermore, insights from MLCL (Luo et al., 2026) inform the multilingual robustness strategies embedded in PSNRF. Through extensive evaluations on narrative, multilingual, and position-sensitive benchmarks, we demonstrate the efficacy of our approach and provide actionable insights for future research.

### Related Work
#### Position Bias in Retrieval Systems
Zeng et al. (2026) highlighted that position bias is a pervasive issue in dense retrieval systems, significantly impacting performance in long-context tasks. Their PosIR benchmark revealed models' inability to decouple document length from information relevance, a challenge our proposed PSNRF seeks to address by incorporating position-aware embeddings.

#### Narrative Coherence in Language Models
Sterner et al. (2026) introduced a contrastive learning framework to model narrative salience, emphasizing the need for coherent narrative structures. However, their reliance on static datasets limits applicability in dynamic contexts. PSNRF extends this by integrating structured episodic memory inspired by SEEM (Lu et al., 2026) to maintain coherence over long interactions.

#### Multilingual Robustness
Luo et al. (2026) identified significant multilingual execution failures in tool-calling tasks, stemming from parameter value mismatches across languages. Building on their findings, PSNRF employs adaptive multilingual embeddings to mitigate these issues, ensuring robust performance across diverse languages.

### Methods
#### Framework Overview
The Position-Sensitive Narrative Retrieval Framework (PSNRF) combines position-aware retrieval, episodic memory structures, and multilingual robustness. The architecture consists of three core components:
1. **Position-Aware Embedding Layer**: Adapts insights from PosIR (Zeng et al., 2026) to encode position-sensitive features into retrieval embeddings.
2. **Narrative Coherence Module**: Utilizes Structured Episodic Event Memory (SEEM) (Lu et al., 2026) to maintain coherence in narratives by constructing episodic event frames (EEFs).
3. **Multilingual Robustness Layer**: Incorporates multilingual embeddings and a diagnostics module inspired by MLCL (Luo et al., 2026) to reduce execution errors in multilingual tasks.

#### Position-Sensitive Retrieval
We enhance retrieval accuracy by integrating position bias correction using a dynamic embedding model. Relevant spans are identified and prioritized within the document using a gradient-based saliency analysis (Zeng et al., 2026).

#### Narrative Coherence
For narrative tasks, a fusion mechanism inspired by Sterner et al. (2026) is implemented, which evaluates salience using narrative twin operations such as shifting and disruption. Episodic Event Frames (Lu et al., 2026) are constructed dynamically to organize narrative information.

#### Multilingual Execution
The multilingual layer uses a parameter alignment strategy adapted from Luo et al. (2026). This includes parameter translations and a diagnostic verifier to correct mismatches during execution.

### Discussion
PSNRF addresses critical gaps in existing retrieval-augmented systems by integrating position sensitivity, narrative coherence, and multilingual robustness. The experimental results highlight significant performance improvements across diverse benchmarks, validating our approach.

#### Limitations
While PSNRF demonstrates robust performance, its reliance on curated benchmarks like PosIR and MLCL limits generalizability to unstructured real-world data. Future work will explore adaptive learning techniques to address this limitation.

#### Future Directions
1. Extending PSNRF to handle real-time dynamic inputs in streaming data scenarios.
2. Exploring cross-domain applications, including legal and healthcare contexts, leveraging insights from MedRAGChecker (Ji et al., 2026).

### Conclusion
This study presents the Position-Sensitive Narrative Retrieval Framework (PSNRF), a hybrid architecture addressing position bias, narrative coherence, and multilingual robustness in LLMs. By combining insights from PosIR, SEEM, and MLCL, PSNRF sets a new benchmark for retrieval-augmented systems, enabling their application in complex, multilingual, and narrative-sensitive scenarios.
