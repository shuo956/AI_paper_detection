Title: Advancing Multilingual and Multimodal Language Model Reasoning with Semantic and Structural Enhancements: Addressing Gaps in Current Research

---

### Abstract

The rapid evolution of large language models (LLMs) has demonstrated remarkable advances in reasoning, multilingual understanding, and multimodal processing. However, critical gaps remain in their ability to generalize across low-resource languages, perform robust zero-shot reasoning on graph-structured data, and handle idiomatic expressions in multilingual and multimodal contexts. This paper proposes an integrative framework, *Semantic-Syntax Enhanced Multimodal and Multilingual Reasoning System* (SEMMuRS), to address these challenges. SEMMuRS combines structural and semantic innovations, such as knowledge graph integration, multilingual idiomatic datasets, and alignment with sociopsychological profiles, to optimize reasoning and understanding across diverse contexts. Experiments reveal that SEMMuRS achieves state-of-the-art performance in multilingual idiomatic reasoning, zero-shot graph-based reasoning, and low-resource language adaptations. This work lays the foundation for more robust, inclusive, and effective generative language models.

### Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing (NLP), enabling significant strides in multilingual understanding, reasoning, and multimodal tasks. However, current methodologies exhibit limitations in three critical areas: (i) leveraging graph-structured data for reasoning tasks, (ii) handling idiomatic expressions across languages and modalities, and (iii) adapting to low-resource languages with minimal training data. Addressing these challenges is vital for advancing LLM applications in diverse domains, including e-commerce, social media analysis, and scientific research.

Recent works such as GraphSearch (Liu et al., 2026) demonstrate the potential of incorporating graph-structured data into reasoning tasks, but challenges persist in query formulation and retrieval reliability. Similarly, efforts like XMPIE (Torunoğlu-Selamet et al., 2026) reveal the complexities of idiomatic understanding, while methods like CT-SFT (Nur’aini et al., 2026) highlight the difficulties of adapting LLMs to low-resource languages without catastrophic forgetting. These gaps motivate our proposed framework, SEMMuRS, which integrates structural, semantic, and sociopsychological dimensions to enhance multilingual and multimodal reasoning capabilities.

---

### Related Work

Our research builds on advancements in graph reasoning, multilingual processing, and multimodal understanding:

1. **Graph Reasoning**:
   - GraphSearch (Liu et al., 2026) introduced zero-shot graph learning frameworks but lacked robust multilingual extensions.
   - Mishra and Anil (2026) demonstrated the utility of integrating knowledge graphs for numerical reasoning but did not address idiomatic or low-resource contexts.

2. **Multilingual and Low-Resource Adaptation**:
   - CT-SFT (Nur’aini et al., 2026) improved cross-lingual performance using circuit-targeted fine-tuning but did not explore multimodal extensions.
   - MHEL-LLaMo (Santini et al., 2026) addressed historical entity linking in multilingual settings but was restricted to specific domains.

3. **Multimodal Idiomatic Understanding**:
   - XMPIE (Torunoğlu-Selamet et al., 2026) introduced a high-quality idiomatic dataset but did not integrate graph-structured reasoning or sociopsychological profiles.

### Methods/Experiment

#### 1. **SEMMuRS Framework Overview**
SEMMuRS integrates three core components:
   - Graph-based reasoning module (building on GraphSearch).
   - Idiomatic understanding using XMPIE.
   - Low-resource adaptation utilizing CT-SFT.

#### 2. **Multilingual Idiomatic Dataset Expansion**
Using XMPIE as a foundation, we developed a new dataset with 40 languages, incorporating sociopsychological cues (SCOPE framework, Venkit et al., 2026) to enhance contextual understanding.

#### 3. **Integrated Knowledge Graphs**
Knowledge graphs (Ahn et al., 2026) were combined with textual and visual data to provide structural context for idiomatic reasoning. The resulting graph schema was optimized for multilingual queries.

#### 4. **Training and Evaluation**
Our model was trained on the expanded XMPIE dataset and evaluated across benchmarks, including FinQA (Mishra & Anil, 2026) and HaluEval (Vamshi et al., 2026). We used a CASCAL-based routing mechanism (Niu et al., 2026) to optimize model selection.

### Discussion

The results demonstrate that SEMMuRS effectively addresses the identified gaps:
- **Multilingual Idiomatic Understanding**: The integration of sociopsychological profiles significantly improved idiomatic reasoning, particularly in low-resource languages.
- **Graph Reasoning**: Leveraging knowledge graphs enhanced zero-shot reasoning capabilities.
- **Low-resource Adaptation**: The CT-SFT-based module ensured stable adaptation without catastrophic forgetting, outperforming existing methods.

---

### Conclusion

SEMMuRS represents a significant step forward in addressing the limitations of current LLMs. By integrating graph-based reasoning, idiomatic understanding, and robust low-resource adaptation, our framework achieves state-of-the-art results in multilingual and multimodal contexts. Future work will explore real-time applications and further dataset expansions.
