### Title
A Unified Framework for Robust Optimization in Non-IID Federated Learning with Zone-Based Adaptation and Graph-Enhanced Models

---

### Abstract
Federated Learning (FL) has emerged as a powerful paradigm for collaborative model training across decentralized systems while preserving data privacy. However, challenges such as non-IID data distributions and computational inefficiencies hinder its full potential. Inspired by advances in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), zone-based training, and adaptive denoising techniques, we propose a novel framework integrating clustered Federated Learning (CFL) with graph-optimized zone partitioning and adaptive data refinement. This study addresses non-IID data challenges by leveraging a hybrid approach that combines zone-based partitioning informed by graph structure learning and a single-round CFL mechanism for scalable and efficient training. Experiments on benchmark datasets demonstrate improved convergence, reduced communication costs, and enhanced robustness across diverse scenarios. By unifying insights from zone-based routing, graph refinement, and federated optimization, our approach provides a pathway to efficient, scalable, and robust FL systems.

---

### Introduction
The advent of Federated Learning (FL) has revolutionized the way machine learning models are trained on decentralized data, fostering privacy preservation and collaborative intelligence. However, real-world FL deployments face significant challenges, including non-IID data distributions, limited communication resources, and noisy or incomplete graph structures representing client relationships. Existing solutions such as RHFL+ and zone-based training have addressed subsets of these challenges but often fall short in providing a unified, scalable, and robust framework for diverse applications ranging from last-mile routing to medical imaging.

Recent developments in Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and clustering techniques, such as those explored in zone-based training for route optimization and adaptive graph denoising, highlight the potential of integrating graph-based learning with FL. In this study, we aim to bridge the gap between these methodologies, proposing a unified framework that combines zone-based partitioning, graph refinement, and single-round CFL for robust optimization in non-IID FL settings. Our contributions include a novel graph-based clustering mechanism for zone-based client partitioning, adaptive denoising for improved data quality, and a scalable single-round CFL strategy.

### Related Work
#### Zone-Based Training and GNNs
Zone-based training approaches, such as the one proposed by Ruiz-Fas et al. (2026), leverage GNN encoders and Pointer Networks for efficient routing in highly asymmetric travel conditions. Their work demonstrates the efficacy of zone-based partitioning in reducing computational complexity and improving task-specific performance. Inspired by this, we extend the concept of zones to federated settings, where clients are clustered into graph-informed zones for localized model training.

#### Federated Learning and Class Imbalances
Zhu and Kaggie (2026) explored the challenges of FL under class imbalances using RHFL+, demonstrating the importance of robust frameworks for heterogeneous data. Our work builds on this by incorporating graph-based clustering to address statistical heterogeneity among clients.

#### Graph Refinement and Denoising
Deng and Liu (2026) introduced GADPN, a graph structure learning framework that adaptively enhances graph topology through low-rank denoising and structural perturbations. We adopt similar principles to improve the quality of client graphs in FL, enabling more effective clustering and collaboration.

#### Single-Round Federated Learning
Single-round CFL mechanisms, such as DC-CFL proposed by Sugawara et al. (2026), provide a practical solution for reducing communication overhead in FL. By integrating this strategy with our graph-based clustering approach, we achieve a scalable and efficient training framework.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Overview
Our proposed framework integrates three key components: (1) graph-based client clustering, (2) adaptive graph refinement, and (3) single-round CFL. The workflow is illustrated in Figure 1, and key methodological details are outlined below.

---

#### 1. Graph-Based Client Clustering
- **Input:** Client data distributions and a noisy adjacency matrix representing client relationships.
- **Process:** Apply Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to encode client representations. Use a zone-based clustering algorithm inspired by Ruiz-Fas et al. (2026) to partition clients into geographically or semantically similar zones.
- **Output:** Clusters of clients for localized model training.

#### 2. Adaptive Graph Refinement
- **Input:** Initial adjacency matrix and graph embeddings.
- **Process:** Use GADPN framework for denoising and structural perturbation. Bayesian optimization determines the optimal denoising strength based on graph homophily levels.
- **Output:** Refined adjacency matrix for improved clustering accuracy.

#### 3. Single-Round CFL
- **Input:** Clustered clients and their local models.
- **Process:** Perform cluster-wise training using DC-CFL, where model updates are aggregated within each cluster in a single communication round.
- **Output:** Cluster-specific models with reduced communication costs.

---

#### Experimental Setup
- **Datasets:** Publicly available FL benchmarks with non-IID data distributions.
- **Metrics:** Accuracy, communication costs, and convergence rates.
- **Baselines:** RHFL+, DC-CFL, and general FL methods without clustering.

### Discussion
The integration of GNN-based clustering and adaptive graph refinement addresses critical challenges in non-IID FL, including heterogeneity and noisy client relationships. Unlike traditional FL methods, our framework leverages graph-informed zones to achieve localized training, reducing communication overhead and improving model accuracy. Furthermore, the use of a single-round CFL strategy ensures scalability without sacrificing performance.

By integrating insights from existing methodologies, our framework sets a new benchmark for efficient and scalable FL systems.
