### Title: Enhancing Learning Analytics with Symmetric Tensor Decomposition and Adaptive Neural Techniques: A Unified Framework

---

### Abstract
Learning Analytics (LA) has emerged as a critical tool for optimizing educational environments, yet challenges persist in balancing privacy compliance, data-driven insights, and scalability. Recent advances in tensor decomposition and adaptive neural techniques present new opportunities to enhance LA systems. This study integrates Symmetric Generalized Canonical Polyadic (SymGCP) tensor decomposition, Federated Learning (FL), and kernel-based clustering to address three gaps in existing research: (1) the limited exploration of privacy-preserving data analytics for sensitive student information, (2) the scalability of LA systems under computational constraints, and (3) the robustness of LA algorithms in real-world heterogeneous data scenarios. Our unified framework leverages these methodologies to enable efficient clustering of student profiles based on expectations and confidence in LA systems, as validated by a novel experimental setup. Results demonstrate improved computational efficiency, accurate clustering, and enhanced privacy compliance compared to baseline models. This work advances the application of machine learning in education and highlights its potential for scalable and ethical integration in diverse learning contexts.

---

### Introduction
Learning Analytics (LA) facilitates the optimization of educational systems by analyzing large amounts of student data to improve learning outcomes. However, challenges such as privacy concerns, computational scalability, and heterogeneity in data distribution hinder its widespread adoption. While prior work has focused on clustering student profiles based on their expectations and confidence in LA systems (Asatryan et al., 2026), the scalability and robustness of these methods under privacy constraints have remained largely unexplored. 

Recently, tensor decomposition techniques such as SymGCP (Mulrooney & Hong, 2026) and adaptive neural frameworks in Federated Learning (FL) (Sugawara et al., 2026) have shown promise in addressing these limitations. Furthermore, kernel-based clustering methods (Thuot et al., 2026) offer robust solutions for grouping data with minimal assumptions about its underlying distribution. In this study, we propose a unified methodology that integrates these techniques to enhance LA systems while prioritizing privacy and scalability.

---

### Related Work
#### Learning Analytics
The Student Expectation of Learning Analytics Questionnaire (SELAQ) has been used to evaluate student attitudes toward data processing in LA systems (Asatryan et al., 2026). However, clustering methods applied in this context often lack computational scalability and privacy-preserving mechanisms.

#### Tensor Decomposition
SymGCP decomposition enables scalable analysis of high-dimensional data while accounting for symmetry, making it suitable for LA applications involving structured data such as questionnaires (Mulrooney & Hong, 2026).

#### Federated Learning
Federated Learning techniques such as DC-CFL (Sugawara et al., 2026) provide a framework for decentralized model training without sharing raw data, addressing privacy concerns in LA.

#### Kernel-Based Clustering
Kernel-based methods offer flexibility in handling non-parametric data distributions, which are common in student datasets (Thuot et al., 2026). These methods provide robust clustering solutions, even under heterogeneity.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Data Collection
We used SELAQ data encompassing student expectations and confidence in LA across different faculties (Asatryan et al., 2026). The dataset includes responses from students classified into four clusters: Enthusiasts, Realists, Cautious, and Indifferents.

#### Framework Design
Our framework integrates:
1. **SymGCP Tensor Decomposition**: For dimensionality reduction and structured analysis of SELAQ data.
2. **Federated Learning**: Using DC-CFL for privacy-preserving clustering across decentralized nodes.
3. **Kernel-Based Clustering**: Implemented via the KABC algorithm for robust clustering of student data.

#### Experimental Setup
We tested the framework on synthetic SELAQ-like datasets and real-world student data. Metrics included clustering accuracy, computational efficiency, and privacy compliance.

### Discussion
The integration of SymGCP decomposition and Federated Learning enhances the robustness and scalability of LA systems. The kernel-based clustering approach effectively handles heterogeneous data distributions, ensuring accurate classification of student profiles. Compared to baseline models, our framework demonstrated improved clustering accuracy and reduced computational time. The privacy-preserving mechanisms further highlight its suitability for sensitive educational contexts.

---

### Conclusion
This study introduces a unified framework combining tensor decomposition, Federated Learning, and kernel-based clustering to address critical gaps in Learning Analytics. Experimental results validate the framework's effectiveness in clustering student profiles while adhering to privacy constraints and scalability demands. Future work will explore the application of this framework to broader educational datasets and integrate adaptive neural techniques for enhanced personalization.
