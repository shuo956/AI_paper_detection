### Title
**Integrating Explainable AI in Material Science and Engineering: A Unified Framework for Predictive Modeling and Decision Support**

### Abstract
Advances in Artificial Intelligence (AI) have revolutionized predictive modeling across various domains, including material science, construction, and healthcare. Despite the success of existing models in addressing specific challenges such as phonon thermodynamics, concrete strength, and anomaly detection, significant gaps remain in achieving robust explainability and generalization under domain shifts or noisy inputs. This study proposes a unified framework integrating explainable AI methodologies with physics-informed models and data-driven algorithms to address these challenges. We design hybrid models combining Neural Additive Models (NAMs), Gaussian Process Regression, and domain-adversarial training to enhance interpretability, generalization, and uncertainty quantification across diverse applications. By synthesizing insights from recent literature, we showcase the framework's potential in improving predictions in phonon fine-tuning, concrete quality classification, and anomaly detection under structured temporal dynamics. Empirical evaluations demonstrate superior accuracy, computational efficiency, and model interpretability compared to state-of-the-art baselines. This work establishes a foundation for applying explainable AI in critical scientific and industrial domains, bridging theoretical understanding and practical deployment.

### Introduction
Artificial Intelligence (AI) has emerged as a transformative force in addressing complex scientific and industrial challenges. From phonon fine-tuning in material science to predictive modeling in construction and healthcare, recent studies have demonstrated the potential of AI to significantly improve computational efficiency, predictive accuracy, and decision support systems. However, critical gaps persist in terms of model interpretability, robustness under noisy conditions, and generalization across diverse domains.

The importance of explainable AI in bridging these gaps cannot be overstated. For instance, EviNAM integrates intelligibility with uncertainty estimation, offering insights into feature contributions while preserving predictive accuracy. Similarly, frameworks such as DP-FedSOFIM and DT-ICU highlight the necessity of domain-specific adaptations for improving robustness and ensuring fairness. Despite these advancements, there remains a need for a unified framework that integrates explainable AI methodologies with physics-informed and data-driven approaches to address challenges across domains.

This study aims to address these gaps by proposing a novel framework that synthesizes insights from existing literature into a cohesive approach for predictive modeling and decision support. We focus on applications in material science, construction, and anomaly detection, leveraging methodologies such as Neural Additive Models (NAMs), Gaussian Process Regression, and domain-adversarial training to enhance interpretability, generalization, and robustness.

### Related Work
Recent literature provides a plethora of methodologies addressing specific challenges across domains. Koker et al. (2026) introduced Phonon Fine-Tuning (PFT) to improve phonon thermodynamics predictions by supervising second-order force constants. Islam et al. (2026) compared traditional machine learning models with neural networks for concrete compressive strength prediction, emphasizing the superiority of embedding-based models in industry-scale datasets. Schleibaum et al. (2026) proposed EviNAM, combining intelligibility and uncertainty estimation for regression tasks.

Physics-informed methodologies such as KAPI-ELM (Dwivedi et al., 2026) and Physics-Informed Gaussian Process Regression (Bai et al., 2026) have shown promise in solving differential equations and eigenvalue problems, respectively. Additionally, frameworks like DT-ICU (Guo, 2026) and DP-FedSOFIM (Nair et al., 2026) have advanced domain-specific adaptations in healthcare and federated learning.

Despite these advancements, existing models often lack cohesive integration of explainability, domain adaptation, and uncertainty quantification. This study aims to bridge these gaps by synthesizing insights into a unified framework.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Framework Design
The proposed framework integrates three key components:
1. **Explainable AI Models**: Neural Additive Models (NAMs) are employed to enhance interpretability by providing explicit feature contributions. Gaussian Process Regression is integrated to quantify uncertainty and address physics-informed challenges.
2. **Domain-Adversarial Training**: Adaptation techniques are used to improve model robustness under domain shifts, leveraging insights from DT-ICU and DP-FedSOFIM.
3. **Hybrid Predictive Modeling**: Multi-task architectures are designed to simultaneously address structured temporal dynamics and static prediction tasks.

#### Evaluation Metrics
The framework is evaluated using a combination of accuracy, computational efficiency, and interpretability metrics. Real-world datasets from material science, construction, and healthcare are utilized, alongside synthetic datasets to assess generalization under domain shifts.

#### Experimental Setup
Experiments are conducted on three representative tasks:
1. Phonon thermodynamics prediction using PFT data.
2. Concrete compressive strength classification using embedding-based neural networks.
3. Anomaly detection in structured temporal datasets using reconstruction-based methods.
