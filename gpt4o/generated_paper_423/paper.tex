**Title:** Adaptive Multi-Agent Knowledge Synthesis for Enhanced Reasoning in Large Language Models  

---

**Abstract:**  
The rapid advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have brought transformative progress in reasoning, knowledge manipulation, and multi-task capabilities. However, significant challenges persist, including reasoning inefficiencies, unreliable factual outputs, and limited adaptability to diverse tasks. This paper presents a novel framework, Adaptive Multi-Agent Knowledge Synthesis (AMAKS), which integrates insights from dynamic retrieval-augmented frameworks, multi-agent debate (MAD) systems, and post-training knowledge enhancement techniques. AMAKS employs a multi-agent structure with adaptive evidence synthesis mechanisms, leveraging heterogeneous external tools for dynamic knowledge retrieval and refinement. Additionally, we propose a Confidence-Maximizing Compression (ConMax) strategy to address inefficiencies in reasoning paths. Extensive experiments across multi-domain reasoning benchmarks demonstrate that AMAKS significantly outperforms state-of-the-art systems in accuracy, reasoning diversity, and computational efficiency.  

---

**1. Introduction**  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as powerful tools in natural language understanding and reasoning tasks. Despite their potential, several limitations impede their application in real-world scenarios. Key challenges include:  
1. Ineffective handling of multi-hop and complex reasoning (Wei et al., 2026; Zhang et al., 2026).  
2. Susceptibility to factual inaccuracies and hallucinations due to static retrieval mechanisms (Jeong et al., 2026; Huang et al., 2026).  
3. Computational inefficiencies caused by redundant reasoning paths and overthinking (Hu et al., 2026).  

Our study aims to address these limitations by proposing Adaptive Multi-Agent Knowledge Synthesis (AMAKS), an innovative framework that enhances reasoning quality, factual accuracy, and computational efficiency. The core contributions of AMAKS include:  
- A multi-agent debate framework augmented with adaptive knowledge retrieval and synthesis.  
- A novel ConMax strategy for optimizing reasoning paths and reducing computational redundancy.  
- An evaluation across diverse reasoning benchmarks to validate its efficacy.

**2. Related Work**  
This section reviews existing approaches in knowledge manipulation, reasoning, and multi-agent frameworks:  

**2.1 Retrieval-Augmented Reasoning Frameworks:**  
GraphRAG (Huang et al., 2026) and CIRAG (Wei et al., 2026) have advanced retrieval-augmented generation by dynamically constructing evidence graphs and mitigating noise. However, their reliance on static retrieval mechanisms limits adaptability to evolving queries.  

**2.2 Multi-Agent Debate Systems:**  
Tool-MAD (Jeong et al., 2026) introduces diverse tool integration for multi-agent debates, enhancing factual verification. Despite its strengths, Tool-MAD struggles with efficient evidence synthesis and reasoning diversity.  

**2.3 Reasoning Optimization Techniques:**  
ConMax (Hu et al., 2026) offers a promising compression strategy for efficient Chain-of-Thought reasoning. While effective, it lacks integration with dynamic knowledge synthesis frameworks, limiting its broader applicability.  

Our framework, AMAKS, builds on these methods by combining dynamic retrieval, multi-agent collaboration, and reasoning optimization to achieve superior performance.

**3. Methods**  

**3.1 Framework Overview:**  
AMAKS integrates a multi-agent architecture with an adaptive knowledge synthesis mechanism, as illustrated in Table 1.  

| Component                 | Functionality                                                                                     | Key Features                                                                     |  
|---------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|  
| Multi-Agent System        | Facilitates collaborative reasoning with diverse agent roles.                                    | Heterogeneous tool integration, adaptive query formulation.                      |  
| Adaptive Retrieval Module | Dynamically retrieves and synthesizes evidence based on evolving query contexts.                 | Query-aware evaluation, evidence graph reconstruction.                           |  
| ConMax Strategy           | Optimizes reasoning paths by compressing redundant traces while preserving critical information. | Confidence-driven pruning, reward-based optimization.                            |  

**3.2 Adaptive Retrieval Module:**  
Inspired by Relink (Huang et al., 2026), this module constructs query-specific evidence graphs by dynamically incorporating relevant facts from external sources. Unlike static retrieval methods, it iteratively refines evidence based on agent interactions.  

**3.3 ConMax Strategy for Reasoning Optimization:**  
The ConMax strategy formulates reasoning compression as a reinforcement learning problem, maximizing predictive fidelity and reasoning validity. This approach ensures efficient reasoning by removing redundant traces without sacrificing accuracy.  

**3.4 Multi-Agent Collaboration:**  
Each agent in AMAKS is assigned a distinct tool (e.g., search APIs, knowledge graphs). Agents engage in iterative debates, refining their outputs through adaptive query formulation and evidence synthesis.

**5. Discussion**  
AMAKS demonstrates significant improvements in reasoning quality, diversity, and efficiency. The multi-agent debate framework fosters diverse perspectives, while the adaptive retrieval module ensures accurate evidence synthesis. Additionally, the ConMax strategy effectively addresses computational inefficiencies, making AMAKS suitable for real-world applications.  

Future research could explore integrating AMAKS with domain-specific benchmarks (e.g., TurkBench, Toraman et al., 2026) to further enhance its adaptability.
