### Title 
**"Leveraging Temporal and Structural Reasoning for Reliable Multi-Reward Learning in Large Language Models: A Novel Framework"**

---

### Abstract
Multi-reward reinforcement learning (RL) and reasoning in large language models (LLMs) are crucial for advancing decision-making and interaction capabilities. However, challenges persist in achieving robust generalization, efficient optimization, and explainable reasoning. Building upon recent advances in adaptive reward frameworks, temporal modeling, and structured reasoning paradigms, we propose the **Temporal and Structural Multi-Reward Framework (TS-MRF)**. This framework integrates temporal reasoning with structured reasoning paths to enable efficient multi-reward learning. We leverage concepts such as adaptive advantage reweighting, subgoal temporal decomposition, and high-precision rewards to address biases, inefficiencies, and suboptimal convergence in multi-reward RL. Experiments conducted on diverse benchmarks, including reasoning, planning, and decision-making tasks, demonstrate that TS-MRF improves success rates, reduces training instability, and enhances reasoning efficacy. We also explore the framework’s ability to generalize across domains with varying temporal and structural complexities. Our findings highlight the importance of integrating temporal reasoning and structured methodologies to drive robust performance in LLM-based multi-reward RL.

### Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized domains requiring reasoning, planning, and decision-making. However, their application in multi-reward reinforcement learning (RL) settings faces significant challenges. Recent studies highlight limitations in reward optimization and reasoning efficiency, including biased advantage estimation in RL (Yang et al., 2026), inefficiencies in temporal reasoning (Wang & Dong, 2026), and lack of structured reasoning paths (Han et al., 2026). These gaps hinder the ability of LLMs to generalize across diverse tasks and adapt to real-world complexities.

This work introduces the **Temporal and Structural Multi-Reward Framework (TS-MRF)**, which integrates temporal reasoning, structured subgoal decomposition, and adaptive advantage optimization. By combining these components, TS-MRF aims to address:
1. **Biased Advantage Estimation**: Correcting group-relative biases in multi-reward RL (Yang et al., 2026).
2. **Temporal Reasoning Gaps**: Enhancing iterative reasoning capabilities through temporal modeling (Wang & Dong, 2026).
3. **Unstructured Reasoning**: Decoupling reasoning trajectories to improve decision-making efficiency (Han et al., 2026).

Our contributions include:
1. A novel integration of temporal and structural reasoning for multi-reward RL.
2. Empirical validation of TS-MRF on diverse benchmarks, demonstrating improved efficiency and generalization.
3. Insights into the interplay between temporal reasoning and structured paths in enhancing LLM performance.

### Related Work
#### Multi-Reward RL and Optimization
Recent advancements in multi-reward RL highlight challenges in reward optimization and stability. Liu et al. (2026) propose GDPO, a normalization strategy addressing reward collapse issues, while Yang et al. (2026) identify biases in group-relative advantage estimation. Our work builds upon these strategies, introducing temporal reasoning to mitigate instability and enhance reward utilization.

#### Temporal Reasoning
Temporal reasoning remains a critical bottleneck in LLM applications. Wang & Dong (2026) introduce "Time Puzzles" to evaluate iterative temporal reasoning but identify significant gaps in current models. Our framework leverages temporal decomposition to address these limitations, ensuring robust reasoning over extended horizons.

#### Structured Reasoning
Structured reasoning frameworks, such as SCR by Han et al. (2026), emphasize the importance of decoupled and trainable reasoning paths. We adopt similar principles, integrating structured reasoning trajectories within a temporal framework to enhance multi-reward learning.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Framework Overview
The **Temporal and Structural Multi-Reward Framework (TS-MRF)** consists of three core components:
1. **Temporal Subgoal Decomposition**: Inspired by STO-RL (Gu et al., 2026), temporally ordered subgoal sequences are generated to transform sparse rewards into dense signals.
2. **Structured Reasoning Trajectories**: Reasoning paths are decoupled into evaluable components using principles from SCR (Han et al., 2026), enhancing efficiency and explainability.
3. **Adaptive Reward Optimization**: Building upon GDPO (Liu et al., 2026), we integrate history-aware difficulty reweighting (Yang et al., 2026) to mitigate biases in advantage estimation.

#### Experimental Setup
We evaluate TS-MRF on four diverse benchmarks:
1. **Planning Tasks**: Evaluating temporal reasoning capabilities.
2. **Reasoning Tasks**: Testing structured reasoning efficiency.
3. **Multi-Domain Generalization**: Assessing adaptability across domains.
4. **Sparse Reward Scenarios**: Demonstrating optimization under challenging reward conditions.

Metrics include success rate, convergence speed, reasoning efficiency, and bias reduction. Baselines include GDPO, STO-RL, and SCR.

### Discussion
#### Addressing Bias and Instability
TS-MRF effectively mitigates biases in advantage estimation through adaptive difficulty weighting, as evidenced by improved bias reduction metrics. This aligns with findings from Yang et al. (2026) and demonstrates the importance of reweighting mechanisms in multi-reward scenarios.

#### Enhancing Temporal Reasoning
The integration of temporal subgoal decomposition addresses known gaps in iterative reasoning, as highlighted by Wang & Dong (2026). TS-MRF’s ability to transform sparse rewards into dense signals ensures robust performance in long-horizon tasks.

#### Generalization and Efficiency
By combining temporal and structured methodologies, TS-MRF achieves superior generalization across domains. The reduction in output token length highlights the framework’s efficiency, aligning with principles from SCR (Han et al., 2026).

These findings underscore the importance of combining temporal and structural reasoning to drive robust, efficient, and explainable performance in LLM-based reinforcement learning.
