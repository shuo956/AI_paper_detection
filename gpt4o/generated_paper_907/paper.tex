# Title  
**Advancing Multilingual and Contextual Competence in Large Language Models: Exploring Robustness in African Languages and Knowledge Conflict Resolution**

---

# Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated significant capabilities across multilingual and domain-specific tasks. However, challenges remain in low-resource language adaptation, conversational competence evaluation, and resolving cross-lingual knowledge conflicts. This paper proposes a novel framework leveraging insights from existing works to address these gaps. We introduce **C-LCA (Cross-Lingual Contextual Alignment)**, a hybrid model combining robust architectural strategies and targeted curriculum learning to enhance multilingual language adaptation and mitigate knowledge conflicts. Using African languages as a testbed, we evaluate the impact of data composition, task alignment, and model scalability. Additionally, we examine cross-lingual knowledge conflict resolution across diverse linguistic settings. Results show that C-LCA significantly improves performance on reasoning, narrative orchestration, and multilingual benchmarks while providing insights into linguistic resource abundance and affinity. This work contributes to the understanding of multilingual LLMs and their optimization for global inclusivity, offering new paths for improving low-resource language processing and mitigating knowledge discrepancies.

---

# 1. Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly utilized for multilingual tasks, but their performance remains inconsistent, particularly for low-resource languages such as African linguistic variants. The uneven distribution of linguistic resources and task-specific knowledge limits their adaptability. Furthermore, cross-lingual knowledge conflicts—where internal beliefs of LLMs across languages contradict external evidence—pose challenges for robust multilingual reasoning. Existing studies, such as AfriqueLLM (Yu et al., 2026), emphasize the role of data composition in improving reasoning-oriented tasks, while others like CLEAR (Zhao et al., 2026) explore conflict resolution strategies in multilingual models. Despite advances, gaps remain in systematically aligning linguistic and contextual subtleties within LLMs.

This paper aims to bridge these gaps by proposing an integrated framework, **C-LCA**, focusing on enhancing multilingual contextual alignment during training and evaluation. We investigate the influence of curriculum learning, hybrid data composition strategies, and robust architectural choices on performance across diverse linguistic and reasoning scenarios. The African language domain serves as a case study for evaluating low-resource language adaptation.

# 2. Related Work  
Existing research underscores several challenges in LLM development for multilingual and contextual tasks:  

### 2.1 African Language Adaptation  
AfriqueLLM (Yu et al., 2026) revealed that data composition significantly impacts LLM performance in African languages. Adding synthetic translations, math, and code tasks improved reasoning capabilities but highlighted architectural limits.

### 2.2 Knowledge Conflict Resolution  
CLEAR (Zhao et al., 2026) introduced a systematic evaluation of cross-lingual knowledge conflicts, demonstrating language resource abundance and linguistic affinity as decisive factors in conflict resolution across multilingual QA tasks.

### 2.3 Narrative and Conversational Competence  
NC-Bench (Moore et al., 2026) emphasized deficiencies in conversational competence, particularly in complex multi-turn interactions and repair tasks. LitVISTA (Lu et al., 2026) highlighted narrative orchestration challenges, showing existing models fail to construct unified global narrative views.

### 2.4 Retrieval-Augmented Systems  
Agentic RAG (Ferrazzi et al., 2026) and MedTutor (Jang et al., 2026) explored retrieval-augmented generation methodologies, revealing trade-offs between enhanced modular systems and dynamic agentic orchestration.

# 3. Methods/Experiment  

## 3.1 Proposed Framework: C-LCA  
The **C-LCA (Cross-Lingual Contextual Alignment)** framework integrates:  
- **Hybrid Curriculum Learning**: Designed according to task difficulty and linguistic features.  
- **Task-Adaptive Data Composition**: Incorporating math, code, and synthetic translations for reasoning-oriented tasks.  
- **Conflict Resolution Module**: Leveraging multilingual parametric elicitation and multi-source induction mechanisms.  

### 3.2 Dataset  
We utilized datasets from AfriqueLLM (Yu et al., 2026) and CLEAR (Zhao et al., 2026):  
1. **AfriqueLLM Dataset**: Includes African language corpora (26B tokens, 20 languages).  
2. **ConflictQA and ConflictingQA**: Benchmarks for evaluating cross-lingual knowledge conflicts, extended to typologically diverse languages.

### 3.3 Evaluation Metrics  
- **Reasoning Performance**: Benchmarked using multilingual QA tasks.  
- **Conflict Resolution Effectiveness**: Evaluated across entity-centric and reasoning-intensive tasks.  
- **Narrative Competence**: Assessed using LitVISTA (Lu et al., 2026).

# 5. Discussion  
The proposed C-LCA framework significantly improved multilingual reasoning and conflict resolution. Results from African languages indicate that hybrid curriculum learning and task-adaptive data composition enhance LLM adaptability in low-resource settings. Furthermore, C-LCA's conflict resolution module demonstrated robustness, outperforming existing frameworks like CLEAR in multilingual QA tasks. Narrative competence analysis revealed improved structural and contextual alignment, addressing deficiencies noted in LitVISTA.

---

# 6. Conclusion  
This paper introduces the C-LCA framework to address gaps in multilingual language adaptation and knowledge conflict resolution. By leveraging curriculum learning, robust data composition strategies, and conflict resolution mechanisms, C-LCA enhances reasoning and narrative orchestration capabilities in African and other low-resource languages. These findings contribute to advancing the inclusivity and robustness of LLMs.
