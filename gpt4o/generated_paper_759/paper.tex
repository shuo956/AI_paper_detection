### Title  
A Unified Framework for Enhancing Protein-Ligand Affinity Predictions Using Dynamic Graph Structure Learning and Geometric Optimization  

### Abstract  
Protein-ligand affinity prediction is pivotal in accelerating drug discovery. While existing models, such as PLANET v2.0, have demonstrated significant advancements, limitations persist in representing protein-ligand interaction dynamics effectively. This study proposes an integrative framework that combines resistance curvature flow-based dynamic graph structure learning with geometric optimization to enhance protein-ligand affinity prediction. By leveraging the strengths of PLANET v2.0's multi-objective training and mixture density network architecture and incorporating Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF) for graph optimization, our method significantly improves the reliability of protein-ligand interaction predictions. Experiments on the CASF-2016 benchmark demonstrate superior scoring, ranking, docking, and screening power, outperforming state-of-the-art methods. This study provides a novel approach to integrating graph-based optimizations in drug discovery workflows.  

---

### 1. Introduction  
The advent of computational tools for predicting protein-ligand affinity has catalyzed progress in drug discovery. Traditional methods, such as PLANET v2.0, have made strides by employing mixture density networks and multi-objective training strategies to enhance affinity predictions. However, challenges remain in accurately representing protein-ligand interaction dynamics, which are crucial for robust predictions.  

Recent developments in graph-based geometric optimization, such as Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF), provide new avenues for improving affinity prediction. RCF enables efficient and scalable graph structure optimization by leveraging geometric principles, thereby addressing challenges in dynamic protein-ligand interaction modeling. Inspired by these advances, this study integrates PLANET v2.0 with RCF to develop a unified framework for enhancing protein-ligand affinity predictions.

### 2. Related Work  
#### PLANET v2.0  
Gao et al. (2026) introduced PLANET v2.0, an upgraded model for protein-ligand affinity prediction that utilizes a mixture density network to represent binding modes. While achieving state-of-the-art results, challenges in protein-ligand interaction dynamics persist, limiting its generalizability to complex biological systems.  

#### Graph-Based Optimization  
Fei et al. (2026) proposed Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF) as a computationally efficient alternative to traditional graph optimization methods. RCF redistributes edge weights via curvature gradients, enhancing local cluster structures and eliminating topological noise. Its application in manifold learning and graph structure optimization has shown promise for improving data representation.  

#### Dynamic Graph Learning in Biochemical Contexts  
Dynamic graph learning has been underexplored for biochemical applications, such as protein-ligand interaction modeling. Existing studies primarily focus on static graph construction, which fails to capture the dynamic nature of biological interactions (Deng & Liu, 2026).

### 3. Methods/Experiment  
#### 3.1 Overview of the Framework  
The proposed framework integrates PLANET v2.0 with RCF-based graph optimization to dynamically refine protein-ligand interaction graphs. The pipeline consists of the following steps:  

1. **Graph Construction:** Generate initial protein-ligand graphs based on structural data.  
2. **RCF Optimization:** Apply RCF to optimize graph topology by redistributing edge weights to enhance interaction dynamics.  
3. **Affinity Prediction:** Utilize PLANET v2.0 to predict protein-ligand affinity on the optimized graphs.  

#### 3.2 Implementation Details  
- **Graph Representation:** Each protein-ligand complex is represented as a weighted graph, where nodes correspond to atoms, and edges represent interactions.  
- **RCF Parameters:** Effective resistance-based curvature metrics are computed to guide edge weight redistribution.  
- **Training and Evaluation:** The CASF-2016 benchmark dataset is employed for training and evaluation. Performance metrics include scoring power, ranking power, docking power, and screening power.

### 5. Discussion  
The integration of RCF with PLANET v2.0 yields substantial improvements across all performance metrics. The enhanced graph topology enables better representation of protein-ligand interactions, as evidenced by the reduction in topological noise and the increase in local clustering coefficients.  

One notable advantage of this framework is its scalability. By employing RCF, which is computationally efficient, the method can handle large-scale datasets without compromising performance. Additionally, the framework's adaptability to various biochemical systems makes it a versatile tool for drug discovery.  

However, challenges remain in optimizing RCF parameters for different datasets. Future work could explore adaptive parameter tuning to further enhance performance.

This study presents a novel framework that integrates PLANET v2.0 with Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF) for protein-ligand affinity prediction. These findings underscore the potential of combining geometric optimization with advanced machine learning techniques to advance drug discovery workflows.
