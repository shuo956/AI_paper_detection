### Title  
**Unified Framework for Robust, Efficient, and Generalized Reasoning in Large Language Models**

### Abstract  
The rapid evolution of large language models (LLMs) has led to remarkable advancements in reasoning, decision-making, and task-specific applications across diverse domains. However, significant challenges persist regarding robustness, efficiency, and generalization. This study proposes a unified framework that synthesizes and builds upon state-of-the-art methodologies, including role-conditioned neuron transplantation, self-reflective decision-making, test-time optimization, and structured reasoning. Drawing from recent advances such as ARM, Speech-Hands, STEP, and ConMax, the proposed framework aims to integrate dynamic reasoning strategies, efficient pruning mechanisms, and context-aware calibration methods. We evaluate the framework across multiple reasoning benchmarks, demonstrating superior performance in cross-domain generalization, computational efficiency, and robustness against adversarial perturbations. Our findings provide a pathway for scalable, reliable, and interpretable LLM-based systems.

---

### Introduction  
Large language models (LLMs) have emerged as transformative technologies, demonstrating exceptional capabilities in reasoning, decision-making, and specialized tasks such as legal reasoning, speech recognition, and time-series forecasting. However, these capabilities often come at the cost of computational inefficiency, brittleness under adversarial conditions, and limited generalization across domains. Recent work, such as ARM for model merging (Feng et al., 2026), Speech-Hands for self-reflective decision-making (Wan et al., 2026), and STEP for test-time trace pruning (Liang et al., 2026), highlights the potential of modular and adaptive frameworks to address these challenges.

Despite these advances, existing solutions are often domain-specific and fail to provide a generalized framework for robust, efficient, and scalable reasoning. This study aims to bridge this gap by synthesizing state-of-the-art methodologies into a unified framework. By integrating dynamic reasoning strategies, efficient pruning mechanisms, and context-aware calibration methods, we aim to provide a scalable and interpretable solution that adapts seamlessly across diverse tasks and domains.

---

### Related Work  
1. **Role-Conditioned Neuron Transplantation**: ARM (Feng et al., 2026) introduced a training-free method for merging specialized LLMs into a generalist model using role-conditioned neuron transplantation. ARM demonstrated improved cross-domain generalization and efficiency.  
2. **Self-Reflective Decision-Making**: Speech-Hands (Wan et al., 2026) proposed a novel self-reflection mechanism for speech recognition and audio reasoning, enabling models to determine when external consultation is necessary.  
3. **Test-Time Optimization**: STEP (Liang et al., 2026) employed a dynamic pruning framework to improve inference latency and reasoning accuracy by evaluating hidden states during reasoning.  
4. **Efficient Reasoning Pathways**: ConMax (Hu et al., 2026) focused on compressing reasoning traces while preserving logical coherence, reducing computational costs without sacrificing accuracy.  
5. **Structured Reasoning**: SCR (Han et al., 2026) emphasized the importance of structured reasoning trajectories to improve reasoning efficiency and reduce redundancy.  

These approaches collectively underscore the need for a unified solution that integrates efficiency, robustness, and generalization.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Overview  
The proposed framework integrates the following components:  
1. **Role-Conditioned Reasoning**: Extending ARM’s neuron transplantation to dynamically adapt reasoning pathways based on task roles.  
2. **Self-Reflective Calibration**: Incorporating Speech-Hands' self-reflective decision-making to balance internal and external information sources.  
3. **Step-Level Optimization**: Employing STEP's dynamic pruning to eliminate unpromising reasoning traces during inference.  
4. **Compression and Calibration**: Using ConMax-inspired logic compression and context-aware calibration for efficient and accurate reasoning.

#### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the framework across three domains:  
1. **Legal Reasoning**: Using the LRAS dataset (Zhou et al., 2026).  
2. **Temporal Reasoning**: Using Time Puzzles (Wang et al., 2026).  
3. **General Reasoning**: Using multi-benchmark tasks from ARM and SCR.

### Discussion  
The experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed framework in addressing key challenges in LLM reasoning:  
1. **Robustness**: Integrating self-reflective calibration significantly improved robustness against adversarial perturbations.  
2. **Efficiency**: Step-level optimization reduced computational overhead without compromising accuracy.  
3. **Generalization**: Role-conditioned reasoning enabled superior cross-domain performance compared to standalone methods.  

While the framework shows promise, limitations include computational complexity during setup and potential trade-offs in fine-grained domain-specific tasks.

---

### Conclusion  
This study presents a unified framework for robust, efficient, and generalized reasoning in LLMs. By synthesizing state-of-the-art methodologies, the framework achieves superior performance across diverse benchmarks. Future work will focus on further optimizing computational efficiency and exploring applications in high-stakes domains such as healthcare and autonomous systems.
