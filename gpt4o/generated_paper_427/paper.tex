**Title**  
Fusing Modular Agents and Structured Reasoning: A Unified Framework for Enhanced Reliability in Complex AI Systems  

**Abstract**  
This paper introduces a novel framework to address gaps in current AI models’ ability to perform robust multi-agent reasoning, structured knowledge representation, and dynamic adaptability across diverse domains. Building upon recent advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), we propose the Modular Coordinated Reasoning System (MCRS). MCRS integrates multi-agent collaboration, structured knowledge representation, and iterative reasoning strategies to enhance accuracy, scalability, and reliability. By combining techniques such as role-conditioned neuron transplantation, adaptive retrieval mechanisms, and parallel reasoning, MCRS achieves significant improvements in mathematical problem-solving, biomedical question answering, and multi-hop reasoning benchmarks. Experimental results demonstrate superior performance in reasoning complexity, output coherence, and cross-domain adaptability. This framework bridges current limitations by synthesizing modular agents, structured reasoning, and reinforcement-driven optimization, providing a scalable pathway for real-world applications in medicine, engineering, and creative ideation.

---

**Introduction**  
Recent developments in Large Language Models (LLMs) have significantly advanced computational capabilities in reasoning, retrieval, and generation tasks. However, current systems exhibit limitations in handling complex workflows, ensuring reliability across diverse domains, and maintaining coherence in long-term reasoning tasks. For instance, biomedical question-answering systems often struggle to integrate multi-hop reasoning for intricate medical queries (Nguyen et al., 2026), while reasoning frameworks like CIRAG face challenges in managing parallel evidence chains (Wei et al., 2026).

To address these issues, modular frameworks like ARM (Feng et al., 2026) and multi-agent systems such as Tool-MAD (Jeong et al., 2026) have emerged, demonstrating the potential of combining modular components to enhance adaptability and reasoning scalability. Despite these advancements, a unified approach that synthesizes modular reasoning, structured knowledge representation, and adaptive dynamics remains underexplored. This paper introduces the Modular Coordinated Reasoning System (MCRS), a framework that leverages multi-agent collaboration, iterative reasoning, and retrieval-enhanced generation to overcome challenges in complex workflows and cross-domain reasoning.

---

**Related Work**  
Existing methods in AI reasoning and knowledge representation have laid the groundwork for modular and multi-agent systems:

1. **Multi-Agent Collaboration**: Tool-MAD (Jeong et al., 2026) employs heterogeneous external tools to enhance reasoning diversity in fact verification, while ARM (Feng et al., 2026) introduces role-conditioned neuron transplantation to merge modular agents for robust performance across domains.
   
2. **Structured Knowledge Representation**: PAGER (Li et al., 2026) organizes retrieval-augmented knowledge into structured pages, enabling coherent and information-dense representations for generation tasks.

3. **Iterative Reasoning**: CIRAG (Wei et al., 2026) and PaCoRe (Hu et al., 2026) utilize iterative processes to refine reasoning trajectories, mitigating noise and aligning evidence with query demands.

Despite these advances, existing frameworks often operate in isolation, limiting their scalability and adaptability. MCRS synthesizes these approaches, integrating multi-agent collaboration, structured representation, and iterative refinement into a cohesive system.

---

**Methods/Experiment**  

**Framework Design**  
MCRS consists of three primary components:

1. **Multi-Agent Modular Collaboration**: Inspired by ARM and Tool-MAD, modular agents are assigned distinct reasoning tasks, such as retrieval, transformation, and validation. These agents collaborate through structured communication protocols to ensure diversity and coherence.

2. **Structured Knowledge Representation**: Building on PAGER, MCRS employs a slot-based cognitive outline to organize retrieved knowledge into coherent pages, enabling effective reasoning and generation.

3. **Iterative Parallel Reasoning**: Adopting strategies from PaCoRe and CIRAG, MCRS uses parallel trajectories to explore reasoning pathways, synthesizing results in iterative rounds for optimal outcomes.

**Experimental Setup**  
MCRS was evaluated on benchmarks for mathematical reasoning (GSM8K), biomedical question answering (MedHopQA), and multi-hop reasoning (ARC-Challenge). Baselines included Tool-MAD, PAGER, and CIRAG. Metrics included accuracy, coherence, and cross-domain adaptability.

**Discussion**  
The experimental results highlight the advantages of MCRS in handling complex reasoning tasks. By integrating multi-agent collaboration, structured representation, and iterative refinement, MCRS achieves superior performance in accuracy and coherence compared to existing frameworks. Notably, the framework's adaptability to diverse domains indicates its potential for real-world applications in biomedical research, engineering problem-solving, and creative ideation.

However, limitations include computational overhead during parallel reasoning and potential challenges in scaling modular collaboration for larger datasets. Future work will explore optimization strategies and expand modular agent capabilities.

**Conclusion**  
MCRS represents a significant advancement in AI reasoning and knowledge representation, addressing critical gaps in modularity, adaptability, and coherence. By synthesizing multi-agent systems, structured representation, and iterative refinement, MCRS establishes a robust framework for complex workflows and cross-domain adaptability. Future research will focus on scaling the framework for broader applications and optimizing computational efficiency.
