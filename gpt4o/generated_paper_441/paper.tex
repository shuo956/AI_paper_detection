### Title:
**Exploring the Integration of Verifiable Rewards and Adaptive Difficulty Weighting in Large Language Models for Robust and Efficient Reasoning**

---

### Abstract:
The increasing complexity of reasoning tasks demands advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs), particularly in their ability to generalize, adapt, and efficiently solve problems across diverse domains. Building on recent progress in Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR), this study investigates novel methodologies to enhance reasoning in LLMs by addressing exploration diversity, adaptive difficulty weighting, and multi-stage optimization challenges. We propose a hybrid framework that integrates semantic-entropy-based branching strategies and adaptive difficulty weighting mechanisms to improve reasoning diversity and robustness. Extensive evaluations on mathematical reasoning, client simulation, and multimodal tasks demonstrate the superiority of our approach in terms of accuracy, exploration efficiency, and ability to adapt to noisy and complex environments. By bridging gaps in existing research, this work provides a comprehensive framework for advancing generalizable and robust reasoning in LLMs.

---

### Introduction:
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated exceptional capabilities in reasoning, natural language processing, and multimodal tasks (Zhao et al., 2026; Huang et al., 2026). However, as applications diversify, these models face significant challenges, including reasoning inefficiencies, biased advantage estimation, and susceptibility to noisy inputs (Lee et al., 2026; Yang et al., 2026). Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) has emerged as a promising approach to address these challenges by leveraging structured rewards to guide reasoning tasks (Zhao et al., 2026; Yang et al., 2026). Nevertheless, existing RLVR methods exhibit limitations in exploration diversity, adaptive difficulty handling, and robustness to contextual noise.

This study aims to address these gaps by integrating semantic-entropy-based exploration and adaptive difficulty weighting into RLVR frameworks. Specifically, we propose a hybrid methodology that combines branching strategies, adaptive difficulty anchors, and multi-stage optimization to enhance reasoning diversity and robustness. Our approach draws inspiration from advancements in client simulation (Qiu et al., 2026), multimodal reasoning (Lin et al., 2026), and sub-goal verifiable rewards (Chen et al., 2026), offering a unified framework for improved generalization and efficiency in diverse reasoning tasks.

---

### Related Work:
1. **Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR):** RLVR frameworks like GRPO and ROSE have demonstrated efficacy in enhancing LLM reasoning (Zhao et al., 2026; Yang et al., 2026). However, biases in advantage estimation and limited exploration diversity remain critical challenges (Yang et al., 2026).
   
2. **Adaptive Difficulty Weighting:** HA-DW has been proposed to address biases in group-based RL by dynamically reweighting difficulty levels (Yang et al., 2026). This method improves exploration balance but lacks integration with semantic diversity.

3. **Multimodal and Contextual Reasoning:** Multimodal systems like Speak While Watching and GeoGoal highlight the importance of integrating sub-goal verifiable rewards and real-time reasoning capabilities (Lin et al., 2026; Chen et al., 2026). These approaches provide valuable insights for developing robust, multi-dimensional reasoning frameworks.

4. **Noise and Distractor Robustness:** NoisyBench and RARE frameworks have exposed vulnerabilities in LLMs when faced with contextual distractors (Lee et al., 2026). Addressing these limitations is critical for building resilient reasoning models.

### Methods/Experiment:
#### Hybrid Framework for Enhanced Reasoning
1. **Semantic-Entropy-Based Branching Strategy (SEBS):**
   - Inspired by Zhao et al. (2026), SEBS identifies high-divergence points in reasoning rollouts to encourage semantic diversity. This strategy is integrated with a stochastic exploration mechanism to prevent local optimization traps.

2. **Adaptive Difficulty Weighting (ADW):**
   - Building on HA-DW (Yang et al., 2026), ADW dynamically adjusts advantage estimates based on evolving difficulty anchors and training dynamics.

3. **Multi-Stage Optimization:**
   - A nested training framework is employed to align reasoning tasks with multi-budget objectives, ensuring robust adaptation across diverse scenarios.

#### Evaluation Metrics:
- **Accuracy:** Percentage of correct reasoning outputs across benchmarks.
- **Exploration Diversity:** Measured using entropy and trajectory divergence metrics.
- **Robustness to Noise:** Assessed on the NoisyBench dataset (Lee et al., 2026).

#### Datasets:
- Mathematical reasoning benchmarks (Zhao et al., 2026; Yang et al., 2026).
- Client simulation datasets (Qiu et al., 2026).
- NoisyBench (Lee et al., 2026).

### Discussion:
The proposed framework significantly outperforms baseline RLVR models in accuracy, exploration diversity, and noise robustness. The integration of SEBS and ADW addresses critical limitations in existing methods, such as biased advantage estimation and local optimization. Notably, our approach demonstrates strong performance on noisy benchmarks, highlighting its potential for real-world applications in client simulation and multimodal reasoning.

Future work should explore the scalability of this framework to larger LLMs and its applicability to additional domains, such as creative storytelling and probabilistic forecasting (Li et al., 2026; Zhao et al., 2026).

---

### Conclusion:
This study introduces a hybrid RLVR framework that integrates semantic-entropy-based exploration and adaptive difficulty weighting to enhance reasoning diversity and robustness in LLMs. Extensive evaluations demonstrate the efficacy of our approach in addressing biases, improving exploration efficiency, and adapting to noisy inputs. By bridging gaps in existing research, this work provides a robust foundation for advancing generalizable and efficient reasoning frameworks in LLMs.
