# Title

Enhancing Large Language Model (LLM) Efficiency through Adaptive Context Compression and Collaborative Reasoning

# Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities in reasoning and retrieval but are often hindered by computational inefficiencies, particularly in scenarios requiring long-context comprehension. This paper proposes a novel framework integrating Adaptive Recursive Context Compression (ArcAligner) and Collaborative Decoding (RelayLLM) to address these challenges. ArcAligner leverages dynamic gating mechanisms to optimize understanding of compressed contexts, while RelayLLM introduces token-level collaborative reasoning between small and large language models. Extensive experiments across diverse benchmarks demonstrate significant improvements in reasoning accuracy, computational efficiency, and retrieval robustness. By combining adaptive context compression and token-level collaboration, our approach achieves a cost reduction of over 98% while maintaining high accuracy. This study establishes a foundation for designing efficient, scalable, and context-aware LLMs for knowledge-intensive tasks.

# Introduction

Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing tasks, ranging from question answering to recommendation systems. However, their computational demands often limit their scalability, particularly in scenarios involving long-context comprehension or collaborative reasoning. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) serves as a promising solution for improving performance in knowledge-intensive tasks by integrating retrieval mechanisms. Yet, feeding lengthy documents into LLM prompts results in inefficiencies, raising the need for context compression and adaptive reasoning frameworks.

This paper builds upon recent advancements in adaptive context compression (Li et al., 2026) and collaborative reasoning frameworks (Huang et al., 2026) to enhance LLM efficiency. We propose a combined approach using ArcAligner and RelayLLM, aiming to optimize context utilization and refine token-level collaborative reasoning. Our contributions include a detailed exploration of adaptive context compression mechanisms, token-level dynamic reasoning, and empirical validation across diverse benchmarks.

# Related Work

Several studies have explored the optimization of LLMs in knowledge-intensive tasks. ArcAligner (Li et al., 2026) introduced adaptive recursive gating mechanisms to improve the utilization of compressed context representations. RelayLLM (Huang et al., 2026) demonstrated the efficacy of token-level collaborative reasoning, significantly reducing computational costs. Additionally, studies on Retrieval-Augmented Generation (Bhatia et al., 2026; Ferguson et al., 2026) have highlighted the importance of efficient context retrieval and reasoning. Despite these advancements, gaps remain in integrating compression and collaboration to address computational inefficiencies comprehensively.

# Methods/Experiment

## Framework Design

### Adaptive Recursive Context Compression (ArcAligner)
ArcAligner employs gating systems within LLM layers to dynamically allocate processing power based on context complexity. Its recursive mechanism enables efficient handling of compressed contexts, particularly in multi-hop reasoning tasks.

### Token-Level Collaborative Decoding (RelayLLM)
RelayLLM facilitates collaboration between Small Language Models (SLMs) and LLMs at the token level. A two-stage training process—warm-up and Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)—teaches SLMs to invoke LLMs selectively for critical tokens.

### Combined Workflow
The ArcAligner and RelayLLM frameworks are integrated into a modular architecture, enabling seamless interaction between compression and reasoning processes.

## Experimental Setup

Experiments were conducted on diverse benchmarks, including knowledge-intensive QA datasets and multi-hop reasoning tasks. Metrics such as accuracy, computational cost, and retrieval robustness were evaluated. Models were trained using the combined ArcAligner-RelayLLM framework and compared against baseline systems.

# Discussion

The results highlight the effectiveness of integrating adaptive context compression and token-level collaborative reasoning. ArcAligner improved handling of compressed contexts, while RelayLLM enabled efficient reasoning by leveraging SLM capabilities. However, challenges persist in optimizing compression rates for highly complex contexts and ensuring robustness in low-resource settings.

Future research could explore the integration of additional retrieval mechanisms, as proposed in PosIR (Zeng et al., 2026), to further enhance retrieval robustness. Additionally, leveraging frameworks like FROAV (Lin et al., 2026) could democratize the accessibility and evaluation of context-aware reasoning systems.

# Conclusion

This study demonstrates the potential of combining ArcAligner and RelayLLM frameworks to enhance LLM efficiency in knowledge-intensive tasks. By optimizing context compression and token-level collaboration, the proposed approach achieves substantial improvements in accuracy and cost-effectiveness. Our findings pave the way for developing scalable, context-aware, and efficient LLMs for diverse applications.
