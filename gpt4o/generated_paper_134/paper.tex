### Title  
"Integrating Physics-informed Graph Neural Networks with Bayesian Optimization for Multimodal Data Analysis: A Framework for High-dimensional Black-box Optimization"

---

### Abstract  
The challenges of high-dimensional data and computational costs have highlighted the need for innovative optimization frameworks. While Physics-Informed Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) have demonstrated utility in simulating spatiotemporal dynamics, Bayesian Optimization (BO) has been a staple for black-box optimization. In this study, we propose a novel integrated framework that combines the strengths of Physics-Informed GNNs and Order-Preserving Bayesian Optimization (OPBO). The hybrid model leverages the interpretability and computational efficiency of GNNs with the scalability of OPBO, enabling effective exploration of high-dimensional spaces. We evaluate the proposed framework on synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating improvements in model accuracy, computational efficiency, and interpretability over existing methodologies. This work establishes a new paradigm for using GNNs and Bayesian methods in challenging optimization scenarios, bridging the gap between physical modeling and data-driven techniques.

---

### Introduction  
Optimization of high-dimensional black-box functions is a prevalent challenge in scientific fields ranging from material sciences to neural network tuning. Traditional Bayesian Optimization (BO), which relies on Gaussian processes, struggles to scale with high-dimensional data due to computational constraints. Recent advancements in Physics-Informed Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) (Acosta et al., 2025) and Order-Preserving Bayesian Optimization (OPBO) (Peng et al., 2025) provide promising pathways to address these challenges. 

This paper introduces an integrated framework that uses Physics-Informed GNNs to preprocess and reduce the dimensionality of complex, high-dimensional datasets while preserving physical interpretability. The reduced dataset is then optimized using OPBO, which leverages the ordinal relationships between data points rather than relying on exact numerical fitting. By combining these two methods, we aim to create a robust, computationally efficient optimization pipeline.

### Related Work  
#### Physics-Informed Graph Neural Networks  
Acosta et al. (2025) introduced Physics-Informed GNNs, specifically DUALFloodGNN, for hydrodynamic modeling. This method embeds physical constraints into GNN architecture, enabling accurate and rapid predictions. Similar methods have been employed in parametric optimization (Hoffmann et al., 2025) and dynamical modeling (Vaziri et al., 2025), demonstrating their potential for complex, real-world systems.

#### Bayesian Optimization and OPBO  
Bayesian Optimization is widely applied to black-box optimization problems. However, its reliance on Gaussian processes often fails in high-dimensional spaces. Peng et al. (2025) proposed OPBO, which prioritizes ordinal relationships over precise value fitting, thereby overcoming limitations in high-dimensional optimization tasks. OPBO has shown significant promise but lacks integration with domain-specific models.

#### Hybrid Models in Optimization  
The integration of machine learning techniques with optimization frameworks has been explored in various contexts. For instance, Altinses and Schwung (2025) incorporated Lipschitz properties into multimodal autoencoders for stability, while Tousi et al. (2025) developed quantum-enhanced error mitigation for computational efficiency. These studies highlight the importance of hybrid models for addressing complex problems.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Model Framework  
The proposed framework consists of two main components:  
1. **Physics-Informed GNNs**: We use a modified version of the DUALFloodGNN (Acosta et al., 2025) to process high-dimensional datasets. The GNN is trained with a multi-step loss function and dynamic curriculum learning to ensure accurate representation of physical constraints while reducing dimensionality.  
2. **Order-Preserving Bayesian Optimization (OPBO)**: After reducing dimensionality, we apply OPBO to the processed data. The surrogate model in OPBO uses an Order-Preserving Neural Network (OPNN) to capture ordinal relationships, allowing for computationally efficient optimization.

#### Experimental Setup  
1. **Datasets**: Synthetic datasets and real-world datasets from domains such as flood modeling and nanomaterial classification were used.  
2. **Metrics**: Performance was evaluated using computational time, accuracy, and the number of function evaluations.  
3. **Benchmarks**: The proposed framework was compared against standalone GNNs, traditional BO methods, and OPBO without GNN preprocessing.  

#### Implementation Details  
The GNN was implemented using PyTorch Geometric, with a message-passing framework for node and edge predictions. OPBO utilized TensorFlow for neural network-based surrogate modeling. All experiments were conducted on an NVIDIA Tesla V100 GPU.

### Discussion  
The integration of Physics-Informed GNNs with OPBO addresses key challenges in high-dimensional optimization. The GNN effectively reduces the dimensionality of input data while preserving physical constraints, enabling OPBO to operate more efficiently. The results demonstrate that the proposed framework can deliver significant improvements in accuracy and computational time.  

The modular design of the framework also allows for flexibility. For instance, other physics-informed models or optimization techniques could be integrated, broadening its applicability.

---

### Conclusion  
We have presented a novel framework that integrates Physics-Informed GNNs with OPBO for high-dimensional black-box optimization. The hybrid model combines the interpretability and computational efficiency of GNNs with the scalability of OPBO, enabling effective exploration and optimization of complex datasets. Future work will focus on extending the framework to other domains, such as climate modeling and materials science.
