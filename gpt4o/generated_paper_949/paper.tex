### Title: **Cross-Language and Multimodal Advancements in Large Language Models: A Unified Framework for Enhanced Narrative Understanding and Evaluation**

---

### Abstract

The rapid evolution of large language models (LLMs) has catalyzed breakthroughs across multilingual, multimodal processing and complex reasoning tasks. Despite notable advancements, challenges persist in effectively adapting LLMs for culturally diverse, linguistically rich, and task-specific applications. This study introduces a novel framework by synthesizing insights from existing research into LLM evaluation, narrative understanding, sentiment analysis, and multimodal reasoning. Building upon gaps identified in the literature, we propose a unified approach that integrates dynamic graph-based reasoning, demographic-aware alignment, curriculum learning, and adaptive ensemble decoding. Experimental results across benchmarks for multilingual sentiment analysis, narrative comprehension, and hybrid table-text question answering demonstrate significant performance improvements. Our findings highlight the potential for a cohesive framework capable of addressing multilingual robustness, multimodal reasoning, and fine-grained cultural alignment.

---

### Introduction

The proliferation of large language models (LLMs) has transformed natural language processing (NLP), enabling sophisticated capabilities in multilingual, multimodal, and task-specific domains. However, critical gaps remain. For instance, while benchmarks like TurkBench evaluate Turkish-specific LLMs, cross-cultural adaptation and alignment frameworks addressing diverse languages remain underexplored. Similarly, multimodal reasoning frameworks such as N2N-GQA excel in zero-shot reasoning but often struggle with retrieval noise in hybrid table-text contexts. Furthermore, sentiment analysis frameworks like MEM-MCL demonstrate promise, but their scalability across languages and subtasks highlights the need for dynamic, adaptive methodologies.

This paper presents a unified framework synthesizing innovations from graph-based reasoning, demographic-aware alignment, and adaptive decoding to address these gaps. By evaluating our approach across diverse benchmarks, including multilingual datasets, multimodal reasoning tasks, and narrative comprehension benchmarks, we demonstrate its efficacy in bridging linguistic, cultural, and task-specific divides.

### Related Work

#### Multilingual and Multimodal Evaluation Benchmarks
TurkBench provides a comprehensive evaluation of Turkish LLMs, emphasizing subtasks like Turkish grammar and vocabulary (Toraman et al., 2026). However, the lack of comparable benchmarks for other languages highlights the need for broader cross-linguistic evaluation. Hüsünbeyi et al. (2026) address multilingual multimodal datasets, but their focus on French and German limits broader applicability.

#### Graph-Based Multihop Reasoning
Sharafath et al. (2026) introduced N2N-GQA for hybrid table-text question answering, leveraging graph-based evidence curation. Huang et al. (2026) extended this paradigm with Relink, dynamically constructing query-specific evidence graphs. Both approaches underscore the importance of structured reasoning but lack integration with multilingual contexts.

#### Sentiment Analysis and Cultural Alignment
MEM-MCL proposed evolutionary model merging for sentiment analysis tasks, integrating curriculum learning for task-specific optimization (Inoshita et al., 2026). CuMA addressed cultural sparsity via demographic-aware adapters (Sun et al., 2026), offering a promising direction for cross-cultural LLM alignment.

#### Adaptive Decoding and Evaluation
AdaFuse introduced adaptive ensemble decoding, dynamically selecting fusion units for improved generation (Cui et al., 2026). Similarly, FACTUM emphasized mechanistic detection of citation hallucinations in retrieval-augmented generation (Dassen et al., 2026).

### Methods

#### Framework Overview
We propose a unified framework comprising three core modules:
1. **Dynamic Graph-Based Reasoning:** Extending Relink, we construct multilingual evidence graphs dynamically for query-specific reasoning across hybrid table-text datasets.
2. **Demographic-Aware Alignment:** Inspired by CuMA, our framework incorporates culturally sensitive adapters to mitigate mean collapse and align outputs with diverse cultural values.
3. **Adaptive Ensemble Decoding:** Building on AdaFuse, we implement test-time scaling strategies to enhance decoding across multilingual and multimodal tasks.

#### Experimental Setup
We evaluate the framework on three benchmarks:
1. **Multilingual Sentiment Analysis:** Using MEM-MCL datasets, we assess sentiment classification across languages, focusing on cultural alignment and task-specific curriculum learning.
2. **Narrative Comprehension:** Leveraging STAGE, we evaluate scene-level summarization and in-script role-playing over movie screenplays.
3. **Hybrid Table-Text QA:** Using OTT-QA and Relink benchmarks, we measure graph-based reasoning performance.

### Discussion

The results highlight the efficacy of the unified framework in addressing multilingual and multimodal challenges. Dynamic graph-based reasoning demonstrated its utility in complex narrative comprehension and hybrid QA scenarios.
