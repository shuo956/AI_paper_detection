Title: **A Unified Framework for Bias Mitigation and Long-Context Optimization in Large Language Models**

---

### Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly adopted for diverse applications, ranging from reasoning and summarization to domain-specific tasks. However, critical challenges persist in bias mitigation and optimizing long-context reasoning. Existing methods either address bias mitigation in isolation, or optimize memory management for long-context tasks without resolving inherent trade-offs. Motivated by gaps in the literature, this study introduces a unified framework that integrates causal bias mitigation with efficient long-context optimization. Building upon insights from MixKVQ and C2PO, the proposed methodology combines query-aware compression strategies and causal-contrastive preference optimization to tackle structural and stereotypical biases while preserving computational efficiency. Experiments demonstrate significant improvements in reasoning accuracy and bias reduction across multiple tasks, providing a robust solution for deploying LLMs in real-world scenarios. The framework is further evaluated on memory-intensive reasoning tasks using datasets such as Mini-ARC and Bongard-LOGO, and bias-sensitive benchmarks, including BBQ and StereoSet.

---

### Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing (NLP), enabling breakthroughs in reasoning, summarization, and domain-specific applications. Despite their success, key limitations remain unresolved: (1) structural biases such as stereotypical biases degrade trustworthiness, and (2) long-context reasoning tasks often encounter memory and computational bottlenecks. Bias mitigation techniques like C2PO (Feng et al., 2025) have demonstrated promise but fail to address long-context optimization. Similarly, MixKVQ (Zhang et al., 2025) offers query-aware compression methods that enhance memory efficiency but does not tackle intrinsic biases within language models.

This paper aims to bridge these gaps by designing a unified framework that addresses both challenges concurrently. By integrating causal-contrastive preference optimization (C2PO) with query-aware mixed-precision quantization (MixKVQ), the proposed methodology aims to balance reasoning accuracy, memory management, and fairness. This unified approach is evaluated across benchmarks for reasoning, bias detection, and long-context tasks, advancing the state-of-the-art in LLM optimization.

---

### Related Work
#### Bias Mitigation in LLMs
Bias remains a pervasive challenge in LLMs. Stereotypical biases (BBQ, Unqover) and structural biases (MNLI, HANS) have been addressed using alignment techniques, such as C2PO, which leverages causal counterfactual signals to isolate bias-inducing features (Feng et al., 2025). However, these approaches often neglect computational efficiency and memory constraints.

#### Long-Context Reasoning
Efficient handling of long-context reasoning tasks is critical for applications like chain-of-thought reasoning and document summarization (Song et al., 2025; Lyu et al., 2025). MixKVQ introduces mixed-precision quantization to reduce memory overhead without significant degradation in performance (Zhang et al., 2025). Despite its efficacy, this approach does not account for fairness or bias concerns.

#### Unified Optimization Approaches
Few studies have explored unified frameworks tackling bias mitigation and long-context optimization. While End-to-End Test-Time Training (Tandon et al., 2025) and Early Knowledge Alignment (Wang et al., 2025) focus on reasoning efficiency, they fail to address fairness or bias mitigation comprehensively.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Overview
The proposed framework integrates MixKVQ for memory-efficient long-context optimization with C2PO for bias mitigation. Figure 1 illustrates the architecture, which combines query-aware compression modules with causal alignment mechanisms.

#### Memory Optimization via MixKVQ
MixKVQ employs a query-aware algorithm to preserve critical key channels requiring higher precision, while applying low-bit quantization for less significant components. This approach is extended to address bias-related outliers by incorporating causal signals into quantization decisions.

#### Bias Mitigation via C2PO
C2PO leverages causal counterfactuals to suppress spurious correlations. The framework dynamically evaluates feature contributions at the logit level and applies fairness-sensitive updates during optimization.

#### Experimental Setup
Experiments were conducted across three primary categories:
1. **Reasoning Tasks:** Mini-ARC, Bongard-LOGO, and ARC datasets.
2. **Bias Benchmarks:** BBQ, StereoSet, and WinoBias.
3. **Long-Context Tasks:** Chain-of-thought reasoning datasets.

For comparison, baseline models included unoptimized LLMs, MixKVQ-only models, and C2PO-only models.
