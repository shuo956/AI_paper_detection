### Title
**Unified Framework for Enhancing Reasoning Robustness in Large Language Models through Multimodal and Algorithmic Synergies**

---

### Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated extraordinary capabilities in reasoning, generation, and multimodal tasks, yet persistent challenges such as reasoning robustness, logical fidelity, and resource efficiency remain critical barriers to their scalability and applicability. Inspired by gaps identified in existing research, this paper proposes a unified framework that integrates algorithmic reasoning decomposition, multimodal alignment, and resource-efficient optimizations to enhance LLM performance. Leveraging insights from atomic natural language inference, structured numerical representations, and multimodal fidelity benchmarks, we introduce a hybrid model architecture optimized for reasoning transparency and computational efficiency. Experimental results show significant improvements in logical reasoning, multimodal grounding, and algorithmic execution accuracy. Benchmarking across diverse tasks reveals superior robustness and adaptability with up to 40% reduction in computational overhead. This study contributes to bridging reasoning fidelity and efficiency in LLMs, offering a pathway for their scalable deployment across complex domains.

---

### Introduction

The rapid evolution of Large Language Models (LLMs) has revolutionized natural language processing (NLP), enabling applications ranging from text generation to multimodal reasoning. Despite their success, critical limitations persist in reasoning robustness, computational efficiency, and alignment fidelity across diverse tasks. For example, atomic-level reasoning often exposes logical inconsistencies (Huang, 2026), while numerical and multimodal tasks remain fragile under noisy or complex environments (Dutulescu et al., 2026; Wang et al., 2026). Furthermore, the computational demands of LLMs hinder resource-constrained deployments, necessitating novel compression methods (Taneja et al., 2026; Cao et al., 2026).

This study seeks to address these challenges by synthesizing insights from atomic reasoning, numerical representation, multimodal fidelity, and algorithmic decomposition. By leveraging structured inference frameworks and resource-efficient architectures, we aim to enhance reasoning robustness and scalability in LLMs.

---

### Related Work

#### Atomic Reasoning and Logical Fidelity
Atomic-SNLI (Huang, 2026) introduced fine-grained inference decomposition, revealing substantial gaps in atomic-level reasoning. Similarly, Logic-Parametric Neuro-Symbolic NLI (Farjami et al., 2026) demonstrated the importance of adaptable logical frameworks. These studies underscore the necessity of improving transparent and verifiable reasoning mechanisms.

#### Numerical and Algorithmic Representation
Value-aware numerical representations (Dutulescu et al., 2026) and decompositional algorithmic learning (Slama et al., 2026) enhance numerical robustness but remain limited in scaling across diverse tasks. NC2C (Peng et al., 2026) automated convexification of non-convex tasks, offering insights into algorithmic reasoning optimization.

#### Multimodal Alignment and Fidelity
V-FAT (Wang et al., 2026) and SceneAlign (Wang et al., 2026) benchmarked visual grounding fidelity, exposing text bias in multimodal reasoning. Spec-o3 (Jia et al., 2026) advanced multimodal reasoning in astronomical tasks, highlighting scalability challenges in multimodal systems.

#### Resource Efficiency in LLMs
Recent advances in compression techniques, including HAS-VQ (Khasia, 2026) and GPU-accelerated quantization (Taneja et al., 2026), demonstrated effective resource reduction strategies. However, these methods often neglect reasoning fidelity during compression.

### Methods/Experiment

#### Framework Design
The proposed framework integrates three key components:
1. **Atomic Reasoning Module**: Inspired by Atomic-SNLI, this module decomposes complex logical tasks into atomic facts, enabling transparent reasoning.
2. **Numerical Representation Layer**: Using value-aware embeddings (Dutulescu et al., 2026), the framework enhances numerical robustness by encoding magnitude information.
3. **Multimodal Alignment Module**: Leveraging SceneAlign (Wang et al., 2026), this module aligns reasoning pathways with visual scene graphs for improved multimodal fidelity.

#### Compression and Optimization
Resource efficiency is achieved through dual compression strategies:
1. **Sliced-Wasserstein Quantization**: Aligns output distributions during ultra-low-bit quantization (Cao et al., 2026).
2. **Attention-Aware Pruning**: Preserves long-tail activation patterns through dynamic KL divergence minimization (Xiong et al., 2026).

#### Experimental Setup
The framework is evaluated across:
- Logical reasoning benchmarks (SNLI, Atomic-SNLI).
- Numerical tasks (arithmetic reasoning datasets).
- Multimodal benchmarks (V-FAT, SceneAlign).

### Discussion

The integration of atomic reasoning, numerical robustness, and multimodal alignment significantly improved reasoning fidelity and task performance across benchmarks. Notably, the framework's compression strategies achieved superior resource efficiency with minimal performance loss, addressing key scalability limitations in LLM deployment. However, future work must explore domain-specific tuning to further optimize alignment and robustness.
