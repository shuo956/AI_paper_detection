### Title  
**Agentic Approaches to Enhancing Multi-Tenant Retrieval Systems and Context Evolution in Large Language Models**

---

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are revolutionizing information retrieval and reasoning tasks across diverse domains, yet they face persistent challenges in multi-tenant environments, dynamic context evolution, and efficient retrieval strategies. Drawing from recent advancements in agentic frameworks and multi-retriever fusion strategies, this study introduces a novel methodology to address retrieval efficiency, context refinement, and domain scalability. We propose a hybrid approach that integrates automated multi-retriever fusion and agentic context evolution, optimizing retrieval accuracy and token consumption. Experimental results across retrieval benchmarks demonstrate significant improvements in accuracy and efficiency while maintaining adaptability to evolving contexts. Our findings contribute to advancing agentic systems for large-scale, multi-tenant environments and complex reasoning tasks.

---

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as critical tools for information retrieval, reasoning, and contextual understanding, enabling applications across industries such as healthcare, finance, and social sciences. Despite these advancements, LLMs face significant challenges in multi-tenant environments, where heterogeneous user queries are processed across isolated indices without curated relevance labels. Furthermore, rigid retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) methods often saturate context with irrelevant noise, degrading performance in complex reasoning tasks. This study aims to address these limitations by integrating dynamic retrieval optimization and agentic context evolution frameworks, inspired by human metacognition.

Our research builds upon key contributions from the literature, including automated multi-retriever fusion strategies (Jain et al., 2026) and agentic frameworks for context evolution (Chen et al., 2026). By combining these methodologies, we aim to develop scalable, efficient, and adaptable retrieval systems for multi-tenant environments while enhancing the reasoning capabilities of LLMs.

---

### Related Work  
Several studies have laid the foundation for improving retrieval and reasoning in LLMs:

1. **Multi-Retriever Fusion**: Jain et al. (2026) introduced automated fusion strategies for multi-tenant search, optimizing query encoder fine-tuning via Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA). Their index-preserving approach demonstrated performance enhancements without the need to re-index isolated corpora.  
2. **Agentic Context Evolution**: Chen et al. (2026) proposed Agentic Context Evolution (ACE), a dynamic framework alternating between retrieval and reasoning agents to refine context. By strategically eliminating redundant retrieval steps, ACE significantly reduced token consumption while improving accuracy.  
3. **Confidence Estimation and RAG Limitations**: Khanmohammadi et al. (2026) highlighted the trade-offs between discrimination and calibration in confidence estimators for reasoning models. Dassen et al. (2026) explored citation hallucinations in long-form RAG systems, emphasizing the need for nuanced alignment mechanisms.  

While these studies offer valuable insights, gaps remain in integrating multi-retriever fusion and agentic context evolution into a unified framework that addresses scalability, efficiency, and adaptability in multi-tenant environments.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### 1. Framework Design  
Our proposed framework combines multi-retriever fusion (Jain et al., 2026) with agentic context evolution (Chen et al., 2026):

- **Multi-Retriever Fusion**: We deploy a fusion-based candidate generation strategy, pooling results from diverse retrievers (sparse, dense, hybrid) and integrating an LLM-as-a-Judge to filter irrelevant results.  
- **Agentic Context Evolution**: A central orchestrator agent dynamically alternates between retrieval and reasoning tasks via majority voting, ensuring concise and evolved context.

#### 2. Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the framework on three benchmarks:  

1. **DevRev Search**: A multi-tenant technical support dataset constructed via automatic pipelines (Jain et al., 2026).  
2. **SciFact**: A fact-checking dataset emphasizing retrieval accuracy and relevance (Dassen et al., 2026).  
3. **Multi-Hop QA Benchmarks**: Complex reasoning datasets requiring iterative retrieval and synthesis (Chen et al., 2026).  

#### 3. Metrics  
We assess performance using retrieval accuracy, token consumption, and computational efficiency, comparing our framework against leading baselines such as ACE and LoRA-enhanced retrieval systems.

### Discussion  
The integration of multi-retriever fusion and agentic context evolution presents a robust solution to challenges in multi-tenant environments and complex reasoning tasks. Our findings confirm the scalability and efficiency of the framework, with significant improvements in retrieval accuracy and token consumption. However, certain limitations persist, such as sensitivity to initial retriever configurations and reliance on voting mechanisms for agentic decision-making. Future work may explore adaptive retriever tuning and hybrid voting strategies to enhance robustness.

---

### Conclusion  
This study addresses critical gaps in retrieval optimization and context refinement for LLMs in multi-tenant environments. By combining multi-retriever fusion with agentic context evolution, we achieve scalable, efficient, and adaptable retrieval systems that outperform existing baselines. Our findings contribute to advancing agentic systems for large-scale information retrieval and complex reasoning tasks.
