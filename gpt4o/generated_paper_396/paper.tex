**Title:** Integrating Physics-Informed Gaussian Process Regression and Meta-Learning for Enhanced Data-Driven Adaptation in Dynamic Environments  

---

**Abstract**  
Dynamic environments present fundamental challenges for machine learning models, particularly when addressing tasks with data shift and complex dependencies. While physics-informed Gaussian process regression has shown promise in solving eigenvalue problems of linear operators, meta-learning offers robust adaptation capabilities under distributional shifts. This paper proposes a novel hybrid framework combining physics-informed Gaussian process regression and meta-learning algorithms to address dynamic data challenges in temporal and spatial domains. We introduce a unified methodology leveraging the Gaussian process posterior for eigenvalue identification alongside meta-learning adaptation mechanisms in real-world applications such as energy forecasting and risk modeling. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate the efficacy of the framework, outperforming traditional models in predictive accuracy, adaptability, and computational efficiency.  

---

**Introduction**  
Machine learning models face challenges in dynamic environments due to data shifts, complex dependencies, and changing distributions over time. Physics-informed Gaussian process regression (Bai & Yang, 2026) has been instrumental in addressing eigenvalue problems by leveraging the posterior covariance structure of Gaussian processes. Concurrently, meta-learning (Myren et al., 2026) has demonstrated robust capabilities in adapting to shifts in data distributions, particularly in time-series classification. While both approaches excel in their respective domains, their integration remains unexplored.

This paper proposes a hybrid framework that combines physics-informed Gaussian process regression and meta-learning algorithms to enhance model adaptability and predictive accuracy. Our methodology builds on the posterior covariance structure of Gaussian processes to identify eigenvalues while integrating meta-learning mechanisms for data shift adaptation. We demonstrate the framework's application across various domains, including energy forecasting and risk modeling, showcasing its potential to address challenges in dynamic environments effectively.

---

**Related Work**  
Physics-informed Gaussian process regression (Bai & Yang, 2026) provides a robust mechanism for solving eigenvalue problems. This approach constructs a transfer function-type indicator for eigenvalue identification using the posterior covariance structure, ensuring non-trivial solutions for eigenvalues corresponding to the operator's eigenspace. However, its application is limited to eigenvalue problems and does not address dynamic data challenges.

Meta-learning (Myren et al., 2026) bridges the gap between training and test distributions, offering rapid adaptation capabilities under data shifts. SeisTask benchmark results illustrate meta-learning's advantages in data-scarce regimes, emphasizing its utility in dynamic environments. Yet, its integration with physics-informed approaches remains unexplored.

Other relevant works, including energy time-series forecasting using deep learning models (Zhu et al., 2026) and scalable graph learning techniques (Zhou et al., 2026), highlight the importance of addressing temporal and spatial dependencies in dynamic systems. These approaches provide complementary insights into the challenges tackled in this paper.
