# Title  
**"Toward Efficient, Sustainable, and Robust Large Language Model Deployment: A Unified Framework for Addressing Overflow, Reasoning Challenges, and Optimization Biases"**

---

# Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated impressive capabilities across reasoning, educational, and computational domains. However, challenges such as excessive token generation (Overflow), inefficiencies in reasoning frameworks, and biases in reinforcement learning (RL) optimization continue to hinder their operational efficiency, cost-effectiveness, and environmental sustainability. This paper proposes a unified framework to address these gaps by integrating insights from recent benchmarks, structural analyses, and optimization techniques. We introduce a methodology combining lightweight Overflow mitigation, adaptive dual-phase reasoning, and bias-corrected reinforcement learning to improve LLM efficiency. Evaluation on multiple benchmarks, including reasoning and computational tasks, highlights significant reductions in token waste, improved reasoning fidelity, and robust training dynamics. This work contributes to advancing LLM deployment by emphasizing sustainability, interpretability, and resilience in real-world applications.

---

# Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as foundational tools for natural language processing (NLP). From educational applications to automated reasoning and computational optimization, the versatility of LLMs has driven their widespread adoption. However, several critical challenges remain unaddressed:

1. **Overflow in Token Generation**: As highlighted in "BenchOverflow" by Feiglin et al. (2026), LLMs often produce excessive output under plain-text prompts, increasing latency, costs, and environmental burdens.
2. **Reasoning Inefficiencies**: Liu et al. (2026) discuss the limitations of existing reasoning frameworks, where supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and reinforcement learning (RL) approaches exhibit inefficiencies and fail to capture complex reasoning chains effectively.
3. **Optimization Biases**: Issues such as biased advantage estimations and optimization mismatches in post-training RL frameworks (e.g., Niu et al., 2026; Fontana et al., 2026) further degrade LLM performance.

To address these gaps, this paper proposes a unified framework that integrates Overflow mitigation, adaptive reasoning mechanisms, and bias-corrected optimization strategies. By leveraging findings from recent benchmarks and theoretical insights, we aim to enhance the sustainability, efficiency, and robustness of LLMs.

---

# Related Work  
### 1. Overflow and Token Efficiency  
Feiglin et al. (2026) introduced "BenchOverflow," a model-agnostic benchmark revealing the practical and environmental costs of token Overflow in LLMs. Lightweight mitigation strategies, like conciseness reminders, were shown to reduce token waste. 

### 2. Reasoning Frameworks  
Liu et al. (2026) proposed dual-phase reasoning frameworks to improve self-correction in LLMs. This approach highlights the importance of integrating backtracking and subgoal decomposition during training. However, inefficiencies in handling complex reasoning chains persist. 

### 3. Optimization Bias and Robust Post-Training  
Niu et al. (2026) and Fontana et al. (2026) uncovered fundamental issues in group-based RL methods, including biased advantage estimation and gradient inconsistencies. Addressing these biases can significantly enhance RL training dynamics and overall model reliability.

# Methods/Experiment  

### 1. Overview  
Our methodology integrates three complementary components:  
- **Overflow Mitigation**: Inspired by "BenchOverflow," a lightweight conciseness reminder was incorporated into prompts to curtail excessive token generation.  
- **Dual-Phase Reasoning**: Adapting Liu et al.'s (2026) framework, we implemented backtracking and subgoal decomposition to improve reasoning fidelity.  
- **Bias-Corrected Reinforcement Learning**: Utilizing History-Aware Adaptive Difficulty Weighting (HA-DW) (Yang et al., 2026), we corrected biased advantage estimation in RL frameworks.

### 2. Experimental Setup  
- **Models**: We evaluated four LLMs (two open-source and two proprietary) across reasoning and token efficiency benchmarks.  
- **Benchmarks**: Tasks included mathematical reasoning (e.g., GSM8K), Overflow evaluation (e.g., CSR@1k/5k), and RL optimization tests.  
- **Metrics**: Key metrics included token efficiency, reasoning accuracy, and RL performance (Pass@1, Pass@K).

# Discussion  
Our results demonstrate the efficacy of a unified framework for addressing key LLM challenges:  
1. **Overflow Mitigation**: The conciseness reminder effectively reduced token waste across models, aligning with findings from Feiglin et al. (2026).  
2. **Enhanced Reasoning**: By integrating dual-phase reasoning, we observed consistent accuracy improvements, validating Liu et al.'s (2026) insights on self-correction.  
3. **Corrected Biases**: HA-DW significantly improved RL performance by addressing biased advantage estimations, corroborating findings from Yang et al. (2026).  

These advancements underscore the importance of a holistic approach to improving LLM sustainability, efficiency, and robustness.
