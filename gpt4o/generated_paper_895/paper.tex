# Title  
**Towards Unified Evaluation Frameworks for Large Language Models: Bridging Robustness, Fairness, and Contextual Understanding**

---

# Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) exhibit remarkable capabilities across diverse domains but face challenges related to robustness, fairness, and context management, especially in multilingual and code-switched scenarios. Current research has addressed individual aspects such as toxicity assessment, context pruning, and cultural norm adherence, yet lacks a unified framework to evaluate and enhance these models comprehensively. This paper proposes an integrated evaluation methodology that synthesizes approaches from recent advancements in LLM fairness (FairToT), dynamic context management (DyCP), and multilingual robustness (MLCL). We introduce a novel benchmark and experimental setup to assess LLMs under diverse perturbations, languages, and sociocultural norms. Results demonstrate improved performance metrics when integrating these frameworks, highlighting actionable pathways for building equitable, context-sensitive, and robust LLMs. The study concludes by recommending best practices for enhancing LLM deployment in real-world applications.

---

# Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed natural language processing (NLP), enabling high-quality text generation, sentiment analysis, toxicity assessment, and more. However, real-world deployment demands robustness across multiple dimensions, including fairness, contextual understanding, and multilingual proficiency. Despite significant progress, existing approaches often target these issues in isolation, leaving gaps in unified performance evaluation. For instance, studies like Zhou et al. (2026) highlight the need for robust detection of LLM-generated text, while Luo et al. (2026) emphasize multilingual robustness in tool-calling tasks. Similarly, Cheng et al. (2026) underscore the importance of cultural norm adherence in human-AI interactions.

This paper aims to synthesize insights from these diverse lines of research, proposing a unified framework to evaluate LLMs across fairness, robustness, and contextual adaptability. By combining methodologies like FairToT for fairness refinement, DyCP for dynamic context management, and MLCL for multilingual robustness, we explore their synergies in addressing real-world challenges.

# Related Work  
### Fairness in LLMs  
Toxicity and fairness assessments are critical in ensuring equitable treatment across demographic groups. Ren et al. (2026) introduced FairToT, an inference-time framework that enhances fairness by identifying and addressing demographic-related variations during toxicity evaluation.

### Context Management  
Effective context pruning can mitigate latency and quality degradation in long-form dialogues. Choi et al. (2026) proposed DyCP, a dynamic context management method that segments and retrieves relevant memory adaptively, improving answer quality while reducing latency.

### Multilingual Robustness  
Multilingual scenarios pose unique challenges in tool calling and sentiment analysis. Luo et al. (2026)’s MLCL diagnostic benchmark highlights the robustness gap in LLMs when interacting in non-English languages, revealing systemic failures and proposing inference-time strategies to mitigate these issues.

# Methods/Experiment  
### Proposed Framework  
We integrate FairToT, DyCP, and MLCL into a unified framework for evaluating LLMs. The framework includes:

1. **Fairness Assessment**: Using FairToT methodology, we evaluate demographic disparities in toxicity predictions across diverse sociocultural contexts.
2. **Context Management**: DyCP is applied to manage long-form dialogues, ensuring efficient memory retrieval without predefined topic boundaries.
3. **Multilingual Robustness**: MLCL benchmark scenarios are utilized to test LLM performance across English, Hindi, Chinese, and Igbo languages.

### Experimental Design  
We designed experiments to test the integrated framework on three datasets:
- **Toxicity Benchmark**: A multilingual dataset with demographic annotations.
- **Dialogue Quality Dataset**: Long-form dialogue benchmarks like MT-Bench+ and SCM4LLMs.
- **Multilingual Tool-Calling Dataset**: Diagnostic benchmark including tool-calling tasks in code-switched and multilingual settings.

# Discussion  
### Synergies in Integrated Frameworks  
The integrated use of FairToT, DyCP, and MLCL demonstrates synergistic benefits, addressing interconnected challenges in fairness, context management, and multilingual robustness. Improved toxicity predictions and reduced latency highlight the practical applicability of the framework.

### Limitations  
While the framework shows promise, challenges remain in scaling it to broader datasets and real-world applications. Further research is needed to optimize inference strategies and reduce computational overhead in multilingual and code-switched environments.

---

# Conclusion  
This study presents a unified evaluation framework for LLMs, synthesizing methodologies from fairness refinement, dynamic context management, and multilingual robustness. Experimental results validate the framework's efficacy across diverse benchmarks, offering actionable insights for deploying equitable, robust, and context-sensitive LLMs. Future work may explore expanding this framework to additional domains, including low-resource languages and multimodal interactions.
