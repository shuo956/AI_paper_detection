**Title**  
Toward Unified Evaluation Frameworks for Computational Models: Integrating Robust Metrics Across Diverse Applications  

**Abstract**  
The exponential growth of computational models, including Large Language Models (LLMs) and domain-specific algorithms, has necessitated rigorous evaluation frameworks that transcend fragmented, task-specific metrics and methodologies. This paper proposes a holistic ranking and evaluation paradigm inspired by Competitive Swiss-System Dynamics (CSD) to aggregate multi-benchmark performance across diverse domains, including text classification, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), user simulation, and computational philology. By synthesizing insights from existing literature, we identify gaps in dynamic competitive fitness assessment and propose novel metrics to quantify model robustness across sequential tasks. Extensive experiments on curated benchmarks demonstrate the superiority of CSD in capturing nuanced model vulnerabilities and competitive ranking. This work contributes to the development of standardized evaluation systems for computational models, enabling cross-domain comparisons and fostering advancements in model design.  

---

**Introduction**  
The evaluation of computational models has traditionally relied on domain-specific benchmarks, creating a fragmented landscape that hinders cross-domain comparison. For instance, while text classification methods such as TextGSL excel in leveraging graph-sequence learning, frameworks like QuCo-RAG and retrieval-augmented question-answering models address hallucination issues in LLMs. Similarly, user simulation frameworks like SpidR-Adapt highlight data efficiency gaps in speech representation learning. Despite their advancements, the lack of unified evaluation standards limits our ability to comprehensively assess model capabilities and robustness.  

This study aims to address these gaps by introducing a Competitive Swiss-System Dynamics (CSD) framework. Drawing inspiration from Swiss pairings in tournaments, CSD aggregates multi-benchmark performance while emphasizing competitive fitness across sequential tasks. The proposed framework incorporates metrics such as Expected Win Score (E[S_m]) and Failure Sensitivity Analysis to evaluate robustness. By synthesizing methodologies from recent studies, we aim to establish a unified evaluation foundation for computational models across diverse domains.  

---

**Related Work**  
The existing literature provides a rich array of domain-specific methodologies but lacks comprehensive frameworks for cross-domain comparison. Text classification research (e.g., TextGSL by Wang and Yuan, 2025) emphasizes structural relationships but struggles to incorporate sequence learning efficiently. Similarly, retrieval-augmented generation frameworks (QuCo-RAG by Min et al., 2025) aim to mitigate hallucinations but rely on unreliable model-internal signals.  

In computational philology, Miller et al. (2025) introduced ACT for detecting biblical quotations, achieving high precision. However, their evaluation metrics remain specific to Aggadic literature, limiting applicability. The development of user simulation models like SpidR-Adapt (Luthra et al., 2025) addresses language learning efficiency but lacks robust cross-domain assessment. Other studies, such as CubeBench by Gao et al. (2025), focus on cognitive challenges but remain constrained by task-specific diagnostic frameworks.  

Our work synthesizes these methodologies into a unified evaluation approach, leveraging insights from multi-domain models and introducing dynamic metrics to address existing limitations.

**Methods/Experiment**  

**Framework Design**  
The Competitive Swiss-System Dynamics (CSD) framework involves iterative pairing of models across diverse benchmarks based on win-loss records. Monte Carlo simulation ($N=100,000$ iterations) approximates Expected Win Score (E[S_m]), providing statistically robust rankings. The framework includes:  

1. **Benchmark Curation**: Diverse tasks such as text classification, retrieval-augmented generation, and user simulation are selected from recent literature.  
2. **Sequential Pairing**: Models are paired dynamically across rounds, emphasizing competitive fitness under high-stakes conditions.  
3. **Failure Sensitivity Analysis**: Per-round elimination ($T_k$) is parameterized to profile risk appetite and robustness.  

**Evaluation Metrics**  
- **Expected Win Score (E[S_m])**: Quantifies model performance across sequential tasks.  
- **Failure Sensitivity Index**: Measures vulnerability to elimination under competitive conditions.  
- **Cross-Domain Robustness**: Assesses adaptability across benchmarks.  

**Experimental Setup**  
Models such as ACT (Miller et al., 2025), QuCo-RAG (Min et al., 2025), and TextGSL (Wang and Yuan, 2025) are evaluated on curated benchmarks. Results are compared with traditional aggregate scoring methods.

**Discussion**  
The results highlight ACT-QE's superior performance in computational philology due to its morphology-aware alignment algorithm. QuCo-RAG demonstrates robustness in mitigating hallucinations, validating its corpus-grounded verification approach. TextGSL excels in structural relationship learning but exhibits vulnerabilities in sequence information.  

Failure Sensitivity Analysis reveals model-specific trade-offs between robustness and risk appetite. ACT-QE's low sensitivity index underscores its stability, while SpidR-Adapt's higher index indicates susceptibility to elimination under competitive conditions.  

The proposed CSD framework provides a nuanced understanding of model capabilities, emphasizing dynamic competitive fitness over static metrics.

**Conclusion**  
This study introduces a unified evaluation framework, Competitive Swiss-System Dynamics (CSD), to aggregate multi-benchmark performance across computational models. By synthesizing methodologies from recent literature, we address gaps in dynamic fitness assessment and establish robust metrics for cross-domain evaluation.
