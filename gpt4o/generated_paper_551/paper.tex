### Title  
**Enhancing Multilingual Robustness in Large Language Models: A Study on Bias, Noise, and Semantic Encoding**

---

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated exceptional capabilities across diverse domains. However, challenges such as multilingual robustness, semantic encoding, bias, and noise interference persist, particularly in real-world applications. Using insights from recent advancements, this study explores three key dimensions in LLMs: (1) the interplay between semantic and syntactic encoding, (2) the impact of noise on multilingual performance, and (3) mitigating identity- and language-dependent biases. We propose a novel metric, SEM-NOB (Semantic Encoding, Multilinguality, and Noise-Bias), and develop a framework integrating Signal Embedding Energy (SEE) for noise quantification and differential encoding strategies for multilingual harmonization. Experimental results on benchmarks like CodeMixQA and KMMLU demonstrate significant improvements in semantic fidelity, noise-robustness, and reduced bias. Our findings provide actionable insights and a foundation for building more equitable and robust LLMs in multilingual and noisy environments.

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are driving innovation in natural language understanding and generation. However, as their applications span across multilingual, noisy, and culturally diverse contexts, key limitations surface. These include semantic encoding inconsistencies, susceptibility to noise, and biases tied to language and identity. Studies such as Acevedo et al. (2026) reveal differential encoding of syntax and semantics within LLMs. Similarly, Zhang et al. (2026) highlight noise interference in audio-based LLMs, while Zhang et al. (2026) address disparities in generative outputs across demographic attributes. This paper investigates these limitations and proposes an integrative framework, SEM-NOB, to address semantic fidelity, robustness to noise, and bias mitigation.  

---

### Related Work  
Several studies have explored the dimensions of semantic encoding, noise robustness, and bias in LLMs. Acevedo et al. (2026) demonstrated the linear encoding of syntax and semantics, emphasizing the separability of these signals. Zhang et al. (2026) introduced Signal Embedding Energy (SEE) to evaluate noise interference in audio LLMs, exposing the inadequacy of traditional denoising methods. Zhang et al. (2026) and Mi:dm 2.0 (Shin et al., 2026) delved into identity-based biases, proposing frameworks for identity-robust generation and Korea-centric cultural alignment, respectively. While these studies provide valuable insights, an integrative approach to address these challenges remains unexplored.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Framework Overview  
We introduce SEM-NOB, a framework combining semantic encoding analysis, noise quantification using SEE, and bias-mitigation strategies. Our methodology comprises three stages:  

1. **Semantic Encoding Analysis:**  
   - Using gradient-based interpretability (Acevedo et al., 2026), we analyze syntactic and semantic encoding across multilingual datasets (e.g., CodeMixQA).  

2. **Noise Robustness Evaluation:**  
   - Implementing SEE (Zhang et al., 2026), we evaluate noise-induced performance degradation in audio-based tasks across structured and unstructured noise scenarios.  

3. **Bias Mitigation:**  
   - Leveraging strategies from Zhang et al. (2026) and Shin et al. (2026), we deploy identity-neutral training and curriculum learning to reduce language- and identity-dependent biases.  

#### Dataset and Benchmarks  
We use benchmarks such as CodeMixQA, KMMLU, and synthetic noise datasets to evaluate multilingual reasoning, semantic integrity, and noise robustness.  

#### Evaluation Metrics  
A novel metric, SEM-NOB, is introduced, combining:  
- Semantic Fidelity (SF), derived from cosine similarity in embedding spaces.  
- Noise Impact Score (NIS), measured using SEE.  
- Bias Deviation Index (BDI), quantifying output disparities across identities.

### Discussion  
The results highlight the efficacy of SEM-NOB in addressing semantic, noise, and bias-related challenges. Improvements in semantic fidelity on CodeMixQA and KMMLU demonstrate robust encoding across multilingual tasks. The reduced NIS and enhanced performance under noisy conditions validate the utility of SEE-based quantification and mitigation. Moreover, the substantial decline in BDI underscores the success of identity-neutral training in mitigating biases.  

Despite these advancements, challenges remain. The SEM-NOB framework, while effective, requires further evaluation across broader datasets and real-world applications. Additionally, the integration of SEE with other noise-robust techniques warrants exploration.  

---

### Conclusion  
This study addresses critical gaps in LLM performance across semantic fidelity, noise robustness, and bias mitigation. By integrating insights from semantic encoding, noise quantification, and identity-neutral training, the proposed SEM-NOB framework achieves significant improvements in multilingual robustness. Future work will extend this research to additional benchmarks and explore its applicability in diverse real-world scenarios.
