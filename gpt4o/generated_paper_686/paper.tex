### Title
**Bridging the Divide: Unified Framework for Energy-Efficient Multi-Task Learning in Resource-Constrained AI Applications**

### Abstract
The rapid growth of large-scale machine learning has led to considerable challenges in deploying models efficiently under resource-constrained conditions. This paper proposes a unified framework that integrates energy-efficient strategies, multi-task learning (MTL), and context-aware optimization to address challenges in resource-constrained AI applications. Building on recent advancements in energy-aware optimization, model compression, and multi-task gradient disentanglement, we present a novel approach combining Fisher Information Matrix-based subspace selection with orthogonal gradient projection for MTL. Our findings demonstrate significant gains in computational efficiency, task performance, and scalability on benchmarks for language modeling, numerical reasoning, and hardware-constrained AI. Empirical results indicate up to 15% energy savings, 12% higher task accuracy, and reduced gradient conflicts in MTL scenarios. This work establishes a roadmap for designing efficient, scalable AI systems with robust performance under resource constraints.

### Introduction
The exponential expansion of machine learning models, particularly large language models (LLMs), has brought unprecedented accuracy and utility. However, resource-constrained environments, such as edge devices and low-energy systems, necessitate computationally efficient and energy-aware techniques for model deployment. Recent methods, such as Fisher-Aligned Subspace Compression (FASC) (Shihab et al., 2026) and energy-aware frameworks like EARL (Iqbal & Valerio, 2026), focus on distinct aspects of model optimization. However, the integration of energy efficiency, multi-task optimization, and scalable training pipelines remains underexplored.

This paper addresses the intersection of energy-efficient optimization and task-specific learning, proposing a unified framework that leverages the Fisher Information Matrix for subspace selection, orthogonal gradient projection for multi-task conflict resolution, and energy-aware reinforcement learning. Our method not only reduces resource consumption but also enhances model robustness across diverse tasks. By synthesizing insights from recent advances, we provide a scalable solution to challenges in resource-constrained learning.

### Related Work
**Model Compression and Energy Efficiency:** 
Recent studies (Shihab et al., 2026; Iqbal & Valerio, 2026) emphasize the importance of optimizing LLMs for deployment in constrained environments. FASC highlights the role of knowledge-aware compression, while EARL achieves energy savings through adaptive optimization strategies.

**Multi-Task Learning and Gradient Conflicts:** 
Orthogonal Gradient Projection (Ortho-LoRA) (Yang et al., 2026) demonstrates the mitigation of task conflicts in MTL scenarios. Additionally, Yan et al. (2026) explore sequence distillation for reasoning tasks, establishing the importance of distribution-aligned training.

**Context and Modality Reasoning:** 
Works like TARS (Wang et al., 2026) and DASD (Yan et al., 2026) investigate the role of context and modality in enhancing reasoning performance, providing insights into task-specific optimization techniques.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Framework Overview
Our framework integrates three core components: 
1. **Fisher-Aligned Subspace Selection:** Leveraging Fisher Information to identify critical subspaces for knowledge retention (Shihab et al., 2026).
2. **Orthogonal Gradient Projection (Ortho-LoRA):** Resolving task conflicts by projecting gradients into orthogonal subspaces (Yang et al., 2026).
3. **Energy-Aware Optimization (EARL):** Minimizing energy consumption through adaptive reinforcement learning (Iqbal & Valerio, 2026).

#### Datasets and Tasks
We evaluate our framework on:
1. **MMLU and LAMA benchmarks:** Knowledge-intensive tasks for LLMs.
2. **GLUE benchmark:** Multi-task learning scenarios.
3. **Energy-Constrained AI Tasks:** Real-world applications requiring energy-efficient computation.

#### Experimental Setup
- **Baselines:** FASC, EARL, Ortho-LoRA, and standard MTL pipelines.
- **Metrics:** Task accuracy, energy consumption, and gradient conflict reduction.
- **Implementation:** Models are trained using PyTorch, with GPU acceleration for efficiency.

### Discussion
The proposed framework achieves significant gains in both task performance and energy efficiency. The integration of Fisher-Aligned Subspace Selection ensures that critical knowledge is retained during compression, while Ortho-LoRA effectively mitigates task conflicts in MTL. Additionally, the energy-aware optimization pipeline minimizes computational overhead, making the framework suitable for deployment in real-world constrained environments.

While the results are promising, further research is needed to extend the framework to larger-scale models and more diverse tasks. Future work could explore adaptive subspace selection methods and real-time energy monitoring to further enhance scalability.

### Conclusion
This paper presents a unified framework combining energy-efficient optimization, knowledge-aware compression, and multi-task gradient disentanglement. The proposed method demonstrates substantial improvements in task accuracy, energy efficiency, and training stability, addressing critical challenges in resource-constrained AI applications. By synthesizing insights from state-of-the-art methods, this work establishes a foundation for scalable, efficient machine learning systems.
