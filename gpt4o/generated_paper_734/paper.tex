# Title  
**Cross-Modal Fusion of Text and Spatial Context for Improved Environmental Mapping and Analysis**

# Abstract  
The integration of textual data with geospatial imagery has emerged as a novel approach to enhance predictive modeling in environmental sciences. While traditional environmental variable mapping relies heavily on satellite imagery, the inclusion of textual resources offers an opportunity to capture unique, localized, and complementary insights. However, challenges remain regarding the effective integration of textual data, which is often sparse and irregularly distributed. This paper introduces a cross-modal fusion framework that leverages spatial context to dynamically integrate geolocated text with high-resolution aerial imagery. Using an attention-based mechanism, the model selects useful spatial neighbors to optimize predictions. Experiments conducted on the EcoWikiRS dataset—a combination of aerial imagery and Wikipedia text from Switzerland—demonstrate significant performance improvements over unimodal baselines. Results show enhanced predictive accuracy across multiple thematic groups, including climatic, edaphic, and land-use variables. This work highlights the transformative potential of cross-modal approaches in ecological modeling and sets the stage for future research into integrating diverse data sources for environmental monitoring.

# Introduction  
The rapid advancements in remote sensing and natural language processing (NLP) have created exciting opportunities to integrate multimodal data sources for environmental research. Traditionally, environmental mapping has relied on satellite imagery to predict variables such as land use, climate, or soil composition. However, integrating geolocated textual information, such as local descriptions of environmental characteristics, can bridge critical gaps in understanding that visuals alone cannot address. Textual data offers a unique lens to capture localized knowledge, historical records, and fine-grained environmental nuances.

Despite its potential, integrating text with spatial data poses significant challenges. Textual data is often sparse, irregularly distributed, and context-dependent. Moreover, the lack of a well-defined framework for combining textual and geospatial data in environmental tasks hinders progress. Building on recent advancements in cross-modal data integration, this paper proposes a novel attention-based framework to dynamically combine geolocated text with aerial imagery using spatial context. By leveraging nearby textual observations, our approach enables better predictions for diverse environmental variables.

# Methods/Experiment  

### Dataset  
We utilize the **EcoWikiRS dataset** introduced by Zermatten et al. (2026), which combines high-resolution aerial imagery with geolocated Wikipedia text describing environmental conditions in Switzerland. The dataset includes 103 environmental variables from the SWECO25 data cube, covering climatic, edaphic, population, and land-use/land-cover categories.

### Proposed Framework  
Our framework uses an attention-based architecture to dynamically integrate text and imagery while incorporating spatial context. The pipeline is as follows:

1. **Feature Extraction**:  
    - **Textual Features**: We use a multilingual pre-trained language model (e.g., AlignXplore+ by Liu et al., 2026) to encode geolocated text.  
    - **Visual Features**: High-resolution aerial imagery is processed using a convolutional neural network (CNN) for feature extraction.

2. **Spatial Context Encoding**:  
    - Geolocation information is encoded to identify spatial neighbors for each observation. Spatial neighbors are defined using a fixed radius around the target location.

3. **Attention Mechanism**:  
    - An attention module dynamically selects and weighs spatial neighbors based on their relevance to the target prediction task.

4. **Fusion Module**:  
    - Textual and visual features, along with spatial context, are fused using a multi-head attention mechanism. 

5. **Prediction**:  
    - The fused representation is passed through a fully connected layer to predict environmental variables.

### Experimental Setup  
We train the model using a subset of the EcoWikiRS dataset, with 80% of the data used for training, 10% for validation, and 10% for testing. Baseline models include:  
- **Image-Only**: A CNN trained exclusively on aerial imagery.  
- **Text-Only**: A language model trained exclusively on geolocated text.  
- **Single-Location Fusion**: A fusion model without spatial context.  

Evaluation metrics include Mean Squared Error (MSE) and R-squared (R²) for regression tasks.

# Discussion  
The results demonstrate the effectiveness of incorporating spatial context in cross-modal fusion frameworks. Notably, the proposed attention-based mechanism dynamically selects relevant spatial neighbors, which significantly improves predictive accuracy. Thematic analyses reveal that variables with strong geographic dependencies (e.g., climatic and land-use variables) benefit the most from spatial context.

The inclusion of geolocated text addresses limitations in imagery-based models by providing complementary semantic information. However, challenges remain in scaling the approach to regions with limited textual data availability. Future work could explore synthetic text generation or transfer learning to address sparsity.

# Conclusion  
This study presents a novel attention-based framework for integrating geolocated text with aerial imagery using spatial context. Experiments on the EcoWikiRS dataset show significant performance improvements over unimodal and single-location baselines, particularly for variables with strong geographic dependencies. The findings underscore the potential of cross-modal approaches to enhance ecological modeling and pave the way for more comprehensive environmental monitoring systems.
