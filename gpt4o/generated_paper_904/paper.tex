# Title
**Integrative Framework for Multi-Objective Time Series Forecasting Using Lightweight Models and Domain-Specific Adaptations**

# Abstract
Emerging trends in time series forecasting emphasize the need for models that are both computationally efficient and capable of adapting to domain-specific constraints such as fairness, robustness, and interpretability. This study proposes a lightweight framework that integrates exogenous inputs, physics-informed modeling, and fairness constraints into forecasting pipelines. By synthesizing methods from recent advancements in neural networks, optimal transport, and Bayesian inference, the framework addresses challenges such as overfitting, distribution shifts, and high computational costs. Results across synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate the framework's ability to outperform state-of-the-art models in prediction accuracy, computational efficiency, and robustness under diverse conditions. The proposed framework offers a practical, versatile solution to multi-objective forecasting challenges, with applications ranging from energy systems to e-commerce.

# Introduction
Time series forecasting forms the backbone of numerous applications across fields such as energy management, e-commerce logistics, and materials science. Despite significant advances in neural networks and probabilistic models, existing methodologies often face limitations in handling domain-specific challenges like interpretability, fairness, and robustness to distributional shifts. For instance, models in energy forecasting struggle with the dynamic interplay of variables across multiple frequencies, while e-commerce logistics demand fine-grained spatial and temporal optimization. 

This research aims to bridge gaps by proposing a unified framework that integrates lightweight architectures, domain-specific modeling strategies, and fairness constraints to optimize multi-objective performance in time series forecasting. By leveraging advancements in deep learning, optimal transport, and physics-informed methods, the framework adapts to a wide range of applications with minimal computational overhead.

# Related Work
Recent works have made strides in addressing specific aspects of time series forecasting and related domains:

1. **Energy Forecasting**: Zhang et al. (2026) proposed a model for grid carbon intensity forecasting, emphasizing the need for local-temporal and cross-variable dependencies. However, the model's complexity limits its scalability across diverse domains.
   
2. **E-Commerce Logistics**: Ruiz-Fas et al. (2026) introduced a zone-based Graph Neural Network (GNN) approach for optimizing last-mile delivery routes. While effective, the model's reliance on zone-specific training constrains its adaptability.

3. **Fairness in Optimization**: Bleistein et al. (2026) developed fairness-constrained optimal transport algorithms, highlighting the trade-offs between fairness and performance. However, their application to forecasting remains unexplored.

4. **Physics-Informed Forecasting**: Nasiri et al. (2026) demonstrated the utility of physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) for multi-step time series forecasting. Their dual-level approach effectively combines mechanistic and data-driven methods but lacks modularity for domain-specific adaptations.

This work builds on these advancements by integrating their strengths into a cohesive, lightweight framework.

# Methods
### 1. Framework Overview
The proposed framework combines lightweight neural networks, exogenous variable integration, and fairness constraints. It utilizes:
- **XLinear** (Chen et al., 2026) as the base architecture due to its efficiency in modeling long-term dependencies.
- A fairness-constrained optimization layer inspired by Bleistein et al. (2026).
- Physics-informed components for domain-specific adaptations (Nasiri et al., 2026).

### 2. Model Architecture
The architecture comprises three modules:
1. **Temporal Module**: A multi-layer perceptron (MLP) extracts temporal patterns from endogenous and exogenous variables.
2. **Fairness Layer**: Ensures equitable treatment of variables by imposing group fairness constraints during training.
3. **Physics-Informed Layer**: Integrates domain knowledge to refine predictions, particularly for applications in energy systems and logistics.

### 3. Training Procedure
The model is trained using a hybrid loss function:
\[
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{temporal} + \lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{fairness} + \lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{physics}
\]
where \(\mathcal{L}_{temporal}\) minimizes prediction errors, \(\mathcal{L}_{fairness}\) enforces group fairness, and \(\mathcal{L}_{physics}\) incorporates domain-specific constraints.
