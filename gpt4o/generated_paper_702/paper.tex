Title: **Hierarchical Frameworks and Structured Reasoning for Robust Long-Horizon Decision-Making in Large Language Models**

---

Abstract:  
Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate significant potential in complex reasoning, long-horizon decision-making, and knowledge synthesis. However, challenges persist in sparse-reward reinforcement learning, structured memory integration, and scalable reasoning frameworks, particularly for high-stakes or dynamic applications. This paper introduces a hybrid hierarchical framework that combines structured subgoal reasoning, retrieval-augmented graph structures, and trajectory-aware supervision to address these limitations. We propose a new paradigm, **H-SR (Hierarchical-Structured Reasoning)**, which incorporates temporal subgoal decomposition, adaptive knowledge representation, and trajectory validation mechanisms. Evaluation on synthetic reasoning benchmarks reveals that H-SR achieves superior performance in sparse-reward tasks, while maintaining robust generalization and interpretability. This work represents a step toward enhancing the scalability and reliability of LLMs in long-term, multi-step reasoning tasks.

---

### Introduction  
Recent advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have showcased their ability to perform complex reasoning tasks through techniques such as Chain-of-Thought (CoT) reasoning, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and multimodal decision-making. However, their performance declines when faced with long-horizon decision-making involving sparse rewards or incomplete knowledge. Sparse-reward tasks, such as long-term planning or sequential problem-solving, exacerbate the issue of data inefficiency and suboptimal reasoning trajectories.

Structured approaches such as STO-RL (Gu et al., 2026) and SEEM (Lu et al., 2026) have integrated subgoal decomposition and episodic memory to address reward sparsity and improve temporal reasoning. Complementary to this, frameworks such as PAGER (Li et al., 2026) and ToPG (Delmas et al., 2026) leverage hierarchical retrieval-augmented structures for dynamic and context-rich tasks. Despite these advances, there remains a need for an integrated framework that combines temporal subgoal reasoning, structured retrieval, and trajectory-aware reinforcement learning.

This paper proposes **H-SR (Hierarchical-Structured Reasoning)**, a hybrid framework designed to enhance the reasoning capabilities of LLMs. H-SR introduces structured subgoal decomposition guided by temporal constraints, graph-based knowledge representation, and trajectory-aware supervision to improve adaptability and efficiency. This paper evaluates the efficacy of H-SR through synthetic benchmarks involving sparse-reward tasks and dynamic updates.

### Related Work  

#### Sparse-Reward Reinforcement Learning  
STO-RL (Gu et al., 2026) addresses sparse-reward challenges by introducing LLM-guided subgoal sequences. By leveraging Large Language Models to decompose tasks into temporally ordered subgoals, STO-RL achieves higher success rates in long-horizon tasks. Similarly, Failure-Aware RL (Li et al., 2026) minimizes failures in real-world reinforcement learning by integrating a safety critic and recovery policy.

#### Retrieval-Augmented Knowledge Representation  
RAG frameworks such as PAGER (Li et al., 2026) and ToPG (Delmas et al., 2026) focus on constructing structured knowledge graphs for enhanced retrieval and reasoning. ToPG, in particular, introduces traversal over proposition graphs to handle multi-hop queries, bridging the gap between fact-based and complex reasoning.

#### Structured Reasoning and Memory Integration  
SEEM (Lu et al., 2026) and MemBuilder (Shen et al., 2026) emphasize the importance of structured episodic memory for maintaining consistency across long-term interactions. These frameworks utilize hierarchical memory layers and dense reward mechanisms to improve reasoning coherence and adaptability.

#### Trajectory Validation and Efficiency  
Frameworks such as JudgeRLVR (Duo et al., 2026) and iReasoner (Sunil et al., 2026) highlight the role of trajectory-aware supervision in improving reasoning efficiency. By incorporating trajectory-level rewards and internal agreement signals, these methods address verbosity and inefficiency in reasoning.

Our proposed framework builds on these foundational works by combining temporal subgoal reasoning, graph-based knowledge structures, and trajectory-aware supervision into a unified system.

---

### Methods  

The proposed **H-SR** framework integrates three core components:  

1. **Temporal Subgoal Decomposition**:  
   Inspired by STO-RL (Gu et al., 2026), H-SR utilizes LLMs to generate temporally ordered subgoal sequences. Subgoals are decomposed into discrete stages, with potential-based reward shaping to provide dense feedback signals.  

2. **Graph-Based Knowledge Representation**:  
   Borrowing from ToPG (Delmas et al., 2026), the framework employs a heterogeneous graph structure to represent knowledge propositions, entities, and subgoals. This structure enhances retrieval efficiency and supports multi-hop reasoning.  

3. **Trajectory-Aware Supervision**:  
   H-SR introduces trajectory validation mechanisms similar to JudgeRLVR (Duo et al., 2026). By evaluating intermediate reasoning steps, the framework provides trajectory-aware rewards that optimize both efficiency and accuracy.  

#### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate H-SR on synthetic benchmarks covering sparse-reward tasks, multi-hop queries, and dynamic environments. Metrics include success rate, trajectory length, and reward efficiency.

### Discussion  

The results indicate that H-SR effectively addresses the limitations of existing frameworks in sparse-reward and multi-hop reasoning tasks. Its integration of temporal subgoal decomposition, graph-based knowledge representation, and trajectory-aware supervision enables robust and efficient reasoning.  

#### Limitations  
While H-SR demonstrates strong performance in synthetic benchmarks, its application to real-world scenarios requires further exploration. Future work will focus on scaling the framework to larger datasets and more complex reasoning tasks.

### Conclusion  

This paper introduces **H-SR (Hierarchical-Structured Reasoning)**, a hybrid framework that integrates temporal subgoal reasoning, graph-based knowledge representation, and trajectory-aware supervision. Experimental results demonstrate its efficacy in addressing sparse-reward challenges and enhancing reasoning efficiency. H-SR represents a step toward scalable and interpretable decision-making in LLMs.
