### Title  
**Advancing Key Information Extraction with Multi-Step Reasoning: A Framework for Robust Document Understanding and Empathetic Conversational Agents**

---

### Abstract  
Recent advancements in multimodal transformers and large language models (LLMs) have highlighted their potential in visually-rich document understanding (VrDU) and conversational systems. However, existing approaches face challenges such as handling complex layouts, managing noisy contextual distractors, and ensuring reasoning robustness. To address these limitations, this study proposes a unified framework that integrates document understanding pipelines like ROAP with empathetic conversational agents inspired by frameworks such as WISE-Flow and EAF. Our framework introduces multi-step reasoning with trajectory-aware supervision to enhance robustness in noisy environments while aligning intermediate reasoning with empathy needs in dialogue systems. Experiments on benchmarks including FUNSD, CORD, and HumDial demonstrate significant improvements in accuracy, reasoning reliability, and empathetic responses. The findings promise scalable solutions for complex layout analysis and dynamic human-machine interaction.

---

### Introduction  
Key information extraction (KIE) and conversational AI systems have witnessed rapid progress with the advent of multimodal transformers and LLMs. Despite achieving state-of-the-art performance in specific tasks, several challenges persist in handling complex layouts in documents, managing noisy distractors, and ensuring empathetic reasoning in user-facing interactions. For instance, frameworks such as ROAP [Xie et al., 2026] optimize attention mechanisms for VrDU but lack integration with empathetic conversational models like EAF [Randhawa et al., 2026]. Additionally, frameworks like Agent-as-a-Judge [You et al., 2026] and WISE-Flow [Zhou et al., 2026] emphasize robust reasoning but fail to address the nuanced interplay between empathy and reasoning in dynamic environments.

This paper proposes a unified framework that bridges these gaps by integrating trajectory-aware reasoning supervision with empathy modeling. Through experiments on multimodal benchmarks and real-world datasets, we aim to demonstrate improved robustness and scalability in document understanding and conversational systems.

---

### Related Work  
1. **Document Understanding Pipelines**  
   ROAP [Xie et al., 2026] introduced mechanisms such as the Adaptive-XY-Gap tree and Reading-Order-Aware Relative Position Bias to enhance KIE in complex layouts. Agri-R1 [Zhang et al., 2026] extended reasoning capabilities in vision-language models, achieving high accuracy in agricultural domains. 

2. **Conversational Agents and Empathy**  
   EAF [Randhawa et al., 2026] established a framework for empathy applicability, emphasizing anticipatory modeling over reactive responses. HumDial Challenge [Zhao et al., 2026] benchmarks emotional intelligence and interaction mechanisms in conversational systems.

3. **Agentic Reasoning and Noise Management**  
   NoisyBench [Lee et al., 2026] revealed vulnerabilities in reasoning models under noisy conditions, proposing Rationale-Aware Reward mechanisms to enhance robustness. WISE-Flow [Zhou et al., 2026] demonstrated self-evolving agents capable of aligning their execution trajectories with retrieved workflows.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Design  
The proposed framework integrates the following components:  
1. **Enhanced Document Understanding:** Using ROAP’s attention mechanisms to extract hierarchical reading sequences from complex layouts.  
2. **Empathetic Reasoning:** Adopting EAF’s anticipatory empathy modeling for conversational agents.  
3. **Noise Management:** Employing trajectory-aware intrinsic reasoning supervision inspired by iReasoner [Sunil et al., 2026].  

#### Dataset Preparation  
Experiments were conducted on:  
- **FUNSD and CORD:** Benchmarks for VrDU.  
- **HumDial and NoisyBench:** Evaluating conversational systems under noisy conditions.  

#### Metrics  
Performance was evaluated based on accuracy, robustness, and alignment with empathy needs across tasks.

### Discussion  
The integration of ROAP’s hierarchical reading logic and EAF’s empathy modeling has yielded substantial improvements in both document understanding and conversational systems. Enhanced robustness under noisy conditions demonstrates the efficacy of trajectory-aware reasoning. While the proposed framework aligns well with existing benchmarks, future work could explore scalability across diverse domains and languages.

This study presents a novel framework that seamlessly integrates document understanding pipelines with empathetic conversational agents. By addressing challenges in layout analysis, reasoning robustness, and empathy modeling, the framework achieves state-of-the-art performance across benchmarks. These findings pave the way for scalable solutions in multimodal AI systems.
