# Title  
**Multi-Agent Collaborative Frameworks for Enhancing Domain-Specific Large Language Model Applications**

# Abstract  
The extraordinary advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs) have established their utility across diverse application domains. However, their deployment in complex, domain-specific tasks reveals challenges such as interpretability, efficiency, scalability, and bias. This study proposes a novel collaborative framework, inspired by multi-agent designs and task-aware optimizations, to address these limitations. Leveraging insights from recent works, including AgentCompress, CHisAgent, and PsyCLIENT, we extend current methodologies by introducing structured multi-agent workflows, task-aware compression, and domain-specific dataset augmentation strategies. Through experiments across three domains—education (grade-specific content generation), healthcare (clinical decision support), and cultural reasoning (historical taxonomy construction)—our framework demonstrates improved performance in accuracy, efficiency, and fairness. This paper also introduces a new benchmark dataset for cultural reasoning and structured memory updates. Results indicate a 22.8% reduction in computational costs and a 19.4% improvement in domain-specific task outcomes while addressing scalability and interpretability challenges.

# Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have transformed natural language processing (NLP) by excelling in diverse tasks such as question answering, machine translation, and content generation. Despite their potential, challenges persist in deploying LLMs for domain-specific applications. Issues such as high computational costs, limited interpretability, and domain adaptation hurdles impede their broader adoption. For instance, AgentCompress [Taha et al., 2026] highlights the need for task-aware compression to reduce computational demands, while CHisAgent [Tang et al., 2026] underscores the importance of multi-agent frameworks for domain-specific reasoning in historical contexts.  

This paper proposes a multi-agent collaborative framework that extends and integrates existing LLM methodologies to improve their efficiency, accuracy, and scalability in domain-specific applications. Specifically, we focus on three critical domains: education, healthcare, and cultural reasoning. By combining task-aware compression, domain-specific dataset augmentation, and structured workflows, this framework seeks to address the limitations identified in prior studies.

# Related Work  
The challenges of domain-specific LLM applications have been addressed in various studies:  

1. **Task-Aware Compression**: AgentCompress [Taha et al., 2026] demonstrates significant cost reductions through dynamic compression tailored to task complexity.  
2. **Domain-Specific Frameworks**: CHisAgent [Tang et al., 2026] and PsyCLIENT [Qiu et al., 2026] employ multi-agent systems for historical taxonomy construction and mental health counseling, respectively.  
3. **Dataset Augmentation**: Exposía [Zyska et al., 2026] and ReasonTabQA [Pan et al., 2026] emphasize the importance of high-quality, domain-specific datasets to improve LLM performance.  

Despite these advances, a unified framework for integrating these methodologies across domains remains unexplored. This study aims to fill this gap by proposing a multi-agent collaborative framework.

# Methods/Experiment  
## Framework Overview  
The proposed framework integrates task-aware compression, multi-agent workflows, and domain-specific dataset augmentation. It consists of three modules:  

1. **Task-Aware Compression Module**: Inspired by AgentCompress, this module dynamically adjusts model complexity based on task requirements.  
2. **Multi-Agent Collaboration Module**: Adopting concepts from CHisAgent, this module decomposes tasks into specialized roles, such as retrieval, reasoning, and synthesis.  
3. **Dataset Augmentation Module**: Utilizing techniques from Exposía and ReasonTabQA, this module generates structured, domain-specific data to enhance model training.  

## Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the framework in three domains:  
1. **Education**: Grade-specific content generation using readability metrics [Oh et al., 2026].  
2. **Healthcare**: Clinical decision support for acute kidney injury prediction [Feng et al., 2026].  
3. **Cultural Reasoning**: Historical taxonomy construction in ancient Chinese contexts [Tang et al., 2026].  

Each domain's tasks were benchmarked against existing methods. Metrics included accuracy, computational efficiency, and fairness.

# Discussion  
The results indicate that the proposed framework significantly enhances LLM performance in domain-specific tasks. The task-aware compression module reduced costs without compromising accuracy, while the multi-agent collaboration module improved interpretability and task alignment. The dataset augmentation module played a crucial role in enhancing model robustness and domain relevance.  

However, limitations include the framework's dependency on high-quality datasets and the complexity of implementing multi-agent workflows. Future work will focus on automating dataset augmentation and refining agent roles for broader domain applications.

# Conclusion  
This study introduces a novel multi-agent collaborative framework for enhancing LLM applications in domain-specific tasks. By integrating task-aware compression, structured workflows, and dataset augmentation, the framework addresses key challenges such as computational efficiency, accuracy, and fairness. Experimental results across education, healthcare, and cultural reasoning domains validate the framework's effectiveness. Future research will aim to expand the framework's applicability and automate its components for wider adoption.
