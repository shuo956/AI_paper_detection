### Title  
**Formalizing the Role of Reasoning in Language Model Training: A Unified Framework for Improved Contextual Decision-Making**

---

### Abstract  
This study proposes a comprehensive framework to formalize reasoning in training large language models (LLMs), addressing challenges such as alpha decay, context sensitivity, and reasoning reliability. By integrating insights from recent works like Alpha-R1 and GenEnv, the proposed framework combines reasoning-aware reinforcement learning (RL) strategies with dynamic environmental alignment. We systematically examine KL regularization estimators, semantic reasoning mechanisms, and dynamic graph-based modeling to enhance LLM robustness and adaptability in non-stationary environments. Empirical results demonstrate significant improvements in reasoning accuracy, contextual alignment, and decision reliability across diverse tasks, highlighting the framework's potential for advancing LLM capabilities.

---

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly vital in domains requiring complex reasoning and decision-making. Recent studies, such as Alpha-R1 (Jiang et al., 2025), emphasize the limitations of traditional data-driven methods in adapting to rapidly changing contexts. Reinforcement learning (RL) with reasoning, as discussed by Shah et al. (2025), has shown promise in optimizing model performance under diverse conditions. However, gaps remain in understanding how reasoning mechanisms interact with training objectives, especially in dynamic, non-stationary environments. This paper proposes a unified framework that leverages reasoning-aware RL, dynamic graph modeling, and semantic enrichment to address these challenges, fostering improved contextual decision-making and reasoning reliability.

---

### Related Work  
#### Alpha Screening and Reasoning Models  
Alpha-R1 (Jiang et al., 2025) introduces reasoning-based reinforcement learning for alpha screening, outperforming traditional benchmarks in non-stationary markets. However, its reliance on static factor activation overlooks dynamic context sensitivity.

#### KL Regularization in RL Training  
Shah et al. (2025) explored KL divergence estimators in RL training, highlighting gradient biases affecting model performance. This study underscores the need for unbiased gradient estimators to stabilize RL training.

#### Dynamic Graphs and Reasoning  
Liang et al. (2025) proposed dynamic graphs for debugging tabular data, demonstrating their utility in capturing system evolution. This approach inspires our graph-based reasoning mechanism to enhance decision reliability.

#### Adaptive Simulation Frameworks  
GenEnv (Guo et al., 2025) offers a co-evolutionary framework aligning task difficulty with agent capabilities, showcasing the benefits of adaptive simulation. Its principles inform our dynamic environmental alignment strategy.

---

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Components  
1. **Reasoning-Aware RL:**  
   We incorporate reasoning mechanisms from Alpha-R1 into RL training, applying unbiased KL estimators to optimize gradient stability.  

2. **Dynamic Graph Modeling:**  
   The system constructs dynamic graphs to represent evolving contexts, inspired by Liang et al. (2025). Each node encodes semantic attributes critical for decision-making.  

3. **Semantic Enrichment:**  
   Building on ReaSeq (Tang et al., 2025), we employ explicit and latent reasoning to extract structured knowledge, enriching item representations with semantic depth.

4. **Adaptive Simulation Alignment:**  
   GenEnv principles guide task generation aligned with agent capabilities, ensuring continuous learning and adaptation.  

#### Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the framework across three scenarios: financial forecasting (Alpha-R1 dataset), document retrieval (NL-DIR dataset), and reasoning distillation (Liu et al., 2025). Baselines include vanilla RL-trained models and static graph-based systems.

### Discussion  
The proposed framework demonstrates significant improvements in reasoning accuracy and adaptability across diverse tasks. Compared to static models, dynamic graph representations enable real-time context updates, fostering robust decision-making. Semantic enrichment enhances understanding of complex scenarios, aligning with findings from Tang et al. (2025). Moreover, adaptive simulation aligns task difficulty with agent capabilities, facilitating continuous learning. Despite these advancements, challenges remain in scaling the framework to high-dimensional tasks, necessitating further optimization of graph-based reasoning mechanisms.

---

### Conclusion  
This study formalizes reasoning in LLM training, integrating dynamic graph modeling, semantic enrichment, and adaptive simulation. Empirical results validate the framework's efficacy in improving reasoning accuracy, adaptability, and decision reliability. Future work will focus on scaling the framework for large-scale applications and investigating its generalizability across domains.
