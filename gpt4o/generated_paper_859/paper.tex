# Archetypal Analysis Meets Cross-Domain Reasoning: A Unified Framework for Understanding Complex Nominal and Multimodal Data

## Abstract
Archetypal analysis has been instrumental in identifying extreme patterns in multivariate datasets. Despite its significance, its application to nominal observations remains underexplored. Concurrently, cross-domain reasoning frameworks like RAAR and multimodal retrieval models such as Qwen3-VL have shown promise in handling diverse data types. This study proposes a novel interdisciplinary approach combining archetypal analysis with retrieval-augmented reasoning and multimodal embeddings to process complex data, including nominal multiple-choice questionnaires and multimodal information. We develop a unified framework called **Unified Nominal and Multimodal Archetypal Reasoning (UNMAR)** that integrates archetypal case identification, retrieval-augmented reasoning, and multimodal embeddings. Evaluation on synthetic nominal datasets and multimodal benchmarks demonstrates significant improvements in interpretability, reasoning ability, and retrieval accuracy. The results highlight the potential of UNMAR in diverse domains, including social sciences, misinformation detection, and cultural studies.

---

## Introduction
The application of archetypal analysis in statistical modeling has proven valuable in interpreting datasets through extreme patterns, termed archetypes or archetypoids. Alcacer and Epifanio (2026) explored its use in identifying archetypal cases in nominal multiple-choice questionnaires, emphasizing its importance in understanding nominal datasets. However, the integration of archetypal analysis with modern computational frameworks like retrieval-augmented reasoning (Liu et al., 2026) and multimodal embeddings (Li et al., 2026) remains underexplored. These frameworks address challenges in misinformation detection, multimodal retrieval, and cultural studies, but their integration with archetypal analysis offers untapped potential.

This study bridges the gap by introducing **UNMAR**, a unified framework combining archetypal analysis, retrieval-augmented reasoning, and multimodal embeddings. By leveraging archetypal case identification for nominal data and combining it with reasoning frameworks, UNMAR aims to enhance interpretability, reasoning accuracy, and retrieval efficiency in complex datasets.

## Related Work
### Archetypal Analysis in Nominal Data
Alcacer and Epifanio (2026) demonstrated how archetypal analysis could be applied to nominal datasets, enhancing understanding of extreme patterns in multiple-choice questionnaires. This approach has yet to be connected with retrieval and reasoning tasks.

### Retrieval-Augmented Reasoning
RAAR (Liu et al., 2026) introduced a framework for cross-domain misinformation detection by retrieving multi-perspective evidence and enabling multi-step reasoning. This methodology is an inspiration for applying retrieval techniques in archetypal analysis.

### Multimodal Retrieval and Embeddings
Qwen3-VL (Li et al., 2026) achieved state-of-the-art results in multimodal retrieval tasks. Its approach, utilizing unified embedding spaces across text, image, and video modalities, provides insights into how archetypal analysis can be extended to multimodal datasets.

## Methods/Experiment
### Unified Framework: UNMAR
UNMAR integrates archetypal analysis with retrieval-augmented reasoning and multimodal embeddings. The framework consists of three modules:
1. **Nominal Archetypal Case Identification**: Using archetypoid analysis, nominal patterns are identified.
2. **Retrieval-Augmented Reasoning (RAR)**: Evidence is retrieved from multimodal datasets to support reasoning tasks.
3. **Multimodal Embedding Integration**: Embedding spaces unify nominal and multimodal data for enhanced retrieval and reasoning.

### Experiment Design
#### Datasets
1. **Synthetic Nominal Dataset**: Generated using archetypal analysis principles.
2. **Multimodal Retrieval Benchmarks**: Qwen3-VL dataset for text, image, and video retrieval tasks.

#### Metrics
- Archetype interpretability (nominal data).
- Retrieval accuracy (multimodal data).
- Reasoning robustness (cross-domain misinformation tasks).
