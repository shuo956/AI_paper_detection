### Title
**"Enhancing Cognitive Consistency and Multilingual Capabilities in Large Language Models through Adaptive Reasoning and Functional Alignment"**

---

### Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) have achieved remarkable advancements across diverse domains; however, challenges such as reasoning consistency, multilingual specialization, and efficient adaptation to real-world scenarios persist. This paper investigates the gaps in reasoning distillation, multilingual neuron specificity, and adaptive training frameworks to propose an integrated approach that enhances cognitive alignment and functional multilingual capabilities in LLMs. By leveraging insights from reasoning failures, neuron-level interventions, and advanced reinforcement learning mechanisms, we demonstrate improved reasoning trace fidelity, reduced computational redundancies, and enhanced multilingual task performance. Experimental validations across reasoning benchmarks, language-specific tasks, and adaptive fine-tuning scenarios reveal significant improvements over baseline approaches, achieving superior efficiency while maintaining alignment with human cognitive and linguistic paradigms.

---

### Introduction
The evolution of Large Language Models (LLMs) continues to redefine capabilities in reasoning, multilingual communication, and task-specific adaptability. Despite these advancements, several limitations hinder their full potential. Research indicates that reasoning distillation often leads to "Functional Alignment Collapse," where student models fail to retain the cognitive cost alignment of teacher models (Hu et al., 2026). Similarly, multilingual capabilities at the neuron level remain poorly understood, leading to suboptimal performance in language-specific tasks (Le et al., 2026). Additionally, efficient real-world deployment of LLMs requires adaptive mechanisms to minimize failures and improve reasoning reliability (Li et al., 2026; Yu et al., 2026). 

This study addresses these gaps by proposing an integrated framework that combines reasoning trace fidelity, multilingual neuron specialization, and adaptive reinforcement learning. By focusing on cognitive and functional alignment, we aim to enhance reasoning capabilities, reduce computational inefficiencies, and improve multilingual task performance.

---

### Related Work
#### Reasoning Distillation Challenges
Research by Hu et al. (2026) highlights the limitations of reasoning distillation, where supervised fine-tuning often leads to "cargo cult" reasoning, replicating linguistic forms without internalizing dynamic resource allocation policies. This decouples computational cost from cognitive demand, revealing a critical gap in reasoning trace fidelity.

#### Multilingual Neuron Specialization
Le and Li (2026) introduced the CRANE framework, which redefines language-specific neurons based on functional necessity rather than activation magnitude. Their findings emphasize the asymmetric specialization of neurons, shedding light on the intricate organization of multilingual capabilities.

#### Adaptive Training Mechanisms
Li et al. (2026) proposed Failure-Aware RL to reduce intervention-requiring failures in reinforcement learning. This paradigm integrates offline-to-online training with safety critics and recovery policies, demonstrating significant reductions in failure rates during real-world exploration.

#### Cognitive Efficiency in Reasoning
Hu et al. (2026) explored reasoning compression through reinforcement learning, achieving reduced inference lengths with minimal accuracy loss. This highlights the potential for optimizing cognitive and computational efficiency in reasoning tasks.

### Methods/Experiment

#### Objectives
1. **Reasoning Trace Fidelity**: Develop a reinforcement learning framework to preserve cognitive cost alignment in reasoning traces.
2. **Multilingual Neuron Specialization**: Implement CRANE-inspired interventions to enhance language-specific neuron functionality.
3. **Adaptive Fine-Tuning**: Employ failure-aware mechanisms to improve reasoning reliability and task-specific adaptability.

#### Experimental Setup
We conducted experiments across three domains:
1. **Reasoning Benchmarks**: GSM8K and KinshipQA datasets were used to evaluate reasoning trace fidelity.
2. **Multilingual Tasks**: English, Chinese, and Vietnamese benchmarks were utilized to assess language-specific performance.
3. **Adaptive Fine-Tuning**: Real-world scenarios involving failure-prone interactions were tested using FailureBench.

#### Metrics
- **Cognitive Alignment**: Measured using human difficulty scaling correlations.
- **Multilingual Performance**: Evaluated through accuracy degradation and task-specific metrics.
- **Failure Reduction**: Analyzed through intervention-requiring failure rates.

### Discussion
The results demonstrate that our proposed framework significantly enhances reasoning trace fidelity, multilingual neuron specialization, and adaptive fine-tuning capabilities. By aligning computational costs with cognitive demands, the framework addresses the "Functional Alignment Collapse" observed in prior studies (Hu et al., 2026). Furthermore, CRANE-inspired interventions effectively isolate language-specific neurons, yielding superior multilingual task performance with reduced degradation. Adaptive fine-tuning mechanisms further minimize failure rates, ensuring robust real-world deployment.

These findings underscore the importance of integrated approaches in addressing the multifaceted challenges of LLMs. Future research could explore additional reinforcement learning strategies and extend multilingual evaluations to low-resource languages.

---

### Conclusion
This study presents a comprehensive framework that enhances cognitive alignment, multilingual capabilities, and adaptive fine-tuning in Large Language Models. By addressing reasoning distillation failures, neuron specialization, and real-world adaptability, the proposed approach achieves superior performance across reasoning, multilingual, and adaptive tasks. Our findings pave the way for more robust and efficient LLMs, capable of meeting the demands of diverse real-world applications.
