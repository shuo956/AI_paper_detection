### Title
**Eojeol-Based Constituency, Multi-Turn Reasoning, and Dynamic Retrieval: A Unified Framework for Enhancing NLP Systems**

### Abstract
Recent advancements in Natural Language Processing (NLP) have revealed the limitations of existing models in handling morphologically complex languages, reducing biases, and improving reasoning capabilities. This study introduces a comprehensive framework that integrates Eojeol-based constituency representation, multi-turn reasoning, and dynamic retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) techniques. Building upon insights from prior research, we propose a unified system that leverages morphological segmentation, latent concept mixing, and redundancy-aware context selection. Experiments on multilingual treebanks, multi-turn conversational datasets, and open-domain question answering benchmarks demonstrate the efficacy of our approach. The proposed system achieves state-of-the-art results in syntactic parsing, improves robustness against confirmation bias, and enhances factual accuracy. This work provides a scalable and interpretable solution for advancing NLP applications across diverse linguistic and reasoning tasks.

---

### 1. Introduction  
The exponential rise of Large Language Models (LLMs) has revolutionized the field of NLP, enabling advancements in translation, reasoning, and text generation. However, challenges persist in morphologically complex languages like Korean, where mismatched syntactic representations hinder syntactic and semantic alignment (Park & Park, 2025). Similarly, multi-turn reasoning in conversational AI remains underdeveloped, often requiring costly human evaluations or failing to consider nuanced multi-turn interactions (Li et al., 2025). Furthermore, dynamic retrieval methods for reducing hallucinations in LLMs require better mechanisms to optimize context relevance and redundancy (Min et al., 2025).  

This paper addresses these gaps by proposing a unified framework that integrates three key innovations:  
1. **Eojeol-based constituency representation** for morphologically rich languages, ensuring syntactic and semantic consistency.  
2. **Multi-turn reasoning enhancement** using latent concept mixtures to mitigate biases and improve robustness in multi-step interactions.  
3. **Dynamic RAG with redundancy-aware scoring**, optimizing token budgets for retrieval while maintaining factual accuracy.  

Our contributions are validated across syntactic parsing, multi-turn reasoning, and open-domain question answering tasks, demonstrating the framework's versatility and effectiveness.

### 2. Related Work  
#### 2.1 Constituency Representation in Morphologically Complex Languages  
Park & Park (2025) emphasized the necessity of Eojeol-based representations to resolve mismatches in Korean syntactic parsing. Their work highlighted the benefits of separately encoding morphological segmentation and syntactic structure for cross-linguistic compatibility.

#### 2.2 Multi-Turn Reasoning and Bias Mitigation  
Kim & Torr (2025) proposed MoLaCE, a framework that reduces confirmation bias by leveraging prompt-specific latent concept experts. Li et al. (2025) introduced MUSIC, a multi-turn reward model that synthesizes contrastive conversation pairs to improve reasoning in multi-turn dialogues.

#### 2.3 Retrieval-Augmented Generation and Redundancy  
Min et al. (2025) and Peng et al. (2025) explored dynamic retrieval mechanisms to address hallucinations in LLMs. These works demonstrated the importance of redundancy-aware scoring and context selection to enhance generation quality.  

Building on these studies, our framework unifies these approaches into a cohesive system, addressing challenges in syntax, reasoning, and retrieval.

### 3. Methods  
#### 3.1 Eojeol-Based Constituency Parsing  
We adopt the annotation scheme proposed by Park & Park (2025), which treats Eojeol as the terminal unit in constituency parsing. Morphological segmentation is encoded in a separate layer, allowing cross-treebank comparisons while preserving syntactic integrity.

#### 3.2 Multi-Turn Reasoning with Latent Concept Mixing  
Inspired by MoLaCE (Kim & Torr, 2025), we integrate a mixture of latent concept experts into a single LLM. Each expert is trained on separate activation strengths over latent concepts, enabling the model to dynamically adjust its reasoning pathways based on input prompts.

#### 3.3 Dynamic Retrieval with Redundancy-Aware Scoring  
We enhance retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) by incorporating AdaGReS (Peng et al., 2025), a redundancy-aware framework. The system optimizes query-chunk relevance while penalizing intra-set redundancy, ensuring efficient use of token budgets.

#### 3.4 Experimental Setup  
We evaluate the framework on three tasks:  
1. **Syntactic Parsing**: Using Sejong and Penn Korean treebanks, metrics include constituency accuracy and tree similarity.  
2. **Multi-Turn Reasoning**: Evaluated on the Skywork dataset with MUSIC-augmented models. Metrics include alignment with human judgments and correctness rates.  
3. **Dynamic RAG**: Benchmarked on Natural Questions and high-redundancy biomedical corpora. Metrics include exact match (EM) and token efficiency.

### 5. Discussion  
The results confirm that Eojeol-based constituency parsing improves syntactic accuracy by resolving morphological ambiguities in Korean. Furthermore, integrating latent concept experts enhances multi-turn reasoning, reducing confirmation bias and improving response robustness. Lastly, redundancy-aware retrieval optimizes token budgets, ensuring higher factual accuracy and efficiency in open-domain QA tasks. These findings suggest that a unified framework can address critical gaps in NLP research and provide a scalable solution for diverse applications.

This study introduced a unified framework integrating Eojeol-based parsing, multi-turn reasoning, and dynamic retrieval techniques. The proposed system achieved significant improvements across syntactic, reasoning, and retrieval tasks, demonstrating its versatility and scalability.
