### Title
Assessing the Impact of ReCAPTCHA on Web Accessibility in Academic Platforms

### Abstract
Web accessibility is a critical aspect of ensuring inclusive access to digital resources. Academic platforms, such as arXiv, often implement security measures like reCAPTCHA to prevent cyberattacks while maintaining accessibility. However, the trade-off between security and usability, particularly for users with disabilities, remains underexplored. This study evaluates the implications of reCAPTCHA on web accessibility within academic platforms, focusing on usability challenges faced by individuals with disabilities. Using a mixed-methods approach, we analyze user feedback, accessibility compliance, and performance metrics. The findings highlight the need for balancing security measures with accessibility standards and propose recommendations for inclusive design.

### Introduction
The proliferation of cyber threats has led to widespread adoption of security measures like reCAPTCHA in web platforms, including academic repositories such as arXiv. While such measures are effective in mitigating malicious activities, they often introduce barriers for users with disabilities, thus compromising web accessibility. Web accessibility, as defined by the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), ensures equitable access to digital content for all users, including those with visual, auditory, cognitive, and physical impairments. This paper investigates the usability of reCAPTCHA on academic platforms and its impact on accessibility compliance.

### Related Work
Existing studies have explored the effectiveness of reCAPTCHA in preventing automated attacks. However, limited research has focused on its implications for web accessibility. Previous research highlights user frustrations with CAPTCHA systems, particularly for individuals with disabilities (e.g., visual impairments). Studies also emphasize the importance of adopting alternative security mechanisms, such as biometric authentication or AI-based threat detection, to enhance inclusivity. Despite these efforts, academic platforms continue to rely on traditional reCAPTCHA implementations, underscoring the need for further research into balancing security and accessibility.

### Methods/Experiment
#### Research Design
A mixed-methods approach was employed to evaluate the accessibility of reCAPTCHA on arXiv. The study involved:
1. **Quantitative Analysis**: Measuring compliance with WCAG 2.1 standards using automated tools.
2. **Qualitative Analysis**: Conducting surveys and interviews with users, including individuals with disabilities, to gather feedback on usability challenges.
3. **Performance Benchmarking**: Comparing alternative security measures against reCAPTCHA in terms of usability and effectiveness.

#### Data Collection
Data was collected using:
- Accessibility evaluation tools (e.g., Axe, Lighthouse).
- User surveys distributed to arXiv users, with a focus on those with disabilities.
- Performance metrics of alternative security measures, including biometric authentication and AI-based systems.

### Discussion
The results indicate significant barriers to accessibility posed by reCAPTCHA, particularly for individuals with disabilities. While reCAPTCHA remains effective in preventing automated attacks, its usability and accessibility scores are notably lower compared to alternative measures. Biometric authentication and AI-based threat detection emerge as promising alternatives, though their effectiveness in high-traffic academic platforms requires further exploration. The findings underscore the need for academic platforms to prioritize accessibility in their security strategies, ensuring equitable access for all users.

### Conclusion
This study highlights the accessibility challenges associated with reCAPTCHA implementation on academic platforms like arXiv. Future research should focus on developing innovative security measures that balance accessibility with robust threat prevention.
