### Title  
**Integrating Multimodal Retrieval-Augmented Frameworks with Contextual Adaptation: Advancing Spreadsheet Reasoning and Decision-Making**

---

### Abstract  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have made significant strides in understanding structured and semi-structured data, including spreadsheets, yet their scalability and reasoning ability over multimodal enterprise-grade workbooks remain limited. Current approaches often fail to optimize retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) techniques for large-scale multimodal tasks, emphasizing either compression or isolated embeddings. This paper proposes a novel framework, **Context-Adaptive Multimodal Retrieval (CAMR)**, which builds on recent advancements in retrieval augmentation, adaptive embeddings, and multimodal reasoning. CAMR integrates fine-grained row-column embeddings, adaptive alignment modules, and retrieval-aware contrastive decoding, enabling efficient reasoning across complex datasets containing multimodal content such as charts and linked sheets. Experimental results on FRTR-Bench and V-FAT benchmarks demonstrate CAMR's ability to improve reasoning accuracy, scalability, and contextual fidelity while reducing token usage. The proposed framework bridges gaps in spreadsheet reasoning and sets the stage for enhanced decision-making in enterprise environments.

---

### Introduction  
The proliferation of large language models (LLMs) has transformed computational approaches to structured data analysis, including reasoning over spreadsheets. Enterprise-grade workbooks often consist of millions of cells, linked sheets, and embedded multimodal content such as charts and images. Traditional approaches struggle with scalability, reasoning depth, and multimodal integration, often favoring either compression or isolated embeddings.  

Recent studies, such as Gulati et al. (2026), emphasize retrieval-augmented multimodal frameworks using hybrid lexical-dense retrieval and granular embeddings, achieving substantial accuracy improvements over prior methods. However, these frameworks lack adaptive mechanisms to fine-tune retrieval and reasoning across diverse contexts. Similarly, Li et al. (2026) introduced ArcAligner, enhancing LLM understanding of compressed embeddings, but these methods do not fully address semantic coherence in multimodal reasoning tasks. Motivated by these gaps, we propose **Context-Adaptive Multimodal Retrieval (CAMR)**, a novel framework integrating adaptive retrieval, multimodal embeddings, and coherence-driven reasoning to enhance large-scale spreadsheet understanding.

---

### Related Work  
#### Retrieval-Augmented Generation  
The FRTR framework (Gulati et al., 2026) introduced multimodal embeddings and reciprocal rank fusion (RRF) for reasoning over spreadsheets, achieving state-of-the-art accuracy. Similarly, Corallo et al. (2026) presented Parallel Context-of-Experts Decoding (PCED), shifting evidence aggregation to decoding for improved cross-document reasoning. However, these methods require refinements in adaptive alignment and retrieval mechanisms.

#### Compression and Alignment  
Li et al. (2026) proposed ArcAligner, emphasizing adaptive gating systems for compressed context representations. Their results highlight the need for lightweight processing modules capable of dynamically adjusting to complex data.

#### Multimodal Reasoning  
V-FAT (Wang et al., 2026) investigated text bias in visual reasoning benchmarks, emphasizing the importance of visual fidelity in multimodal models. Integrating visual and numerical data remains a challenge in spreadsheet reasoning tasks.

---

### Methods  

#### Framework Overview  
**Context-Adaptive Multimodal Retrieval (CAMR)** integrates three key components:  
1. **Granular Row-Column Embeddings**: Inspired by FRTR, CAMR decomposes spreadsheets into fine-grained row-column and block embeddings.  
2. **Adaptive Alignment Modules**: Drawing from ArcAligner, adaptive gating dynamically adjusts embeddings based on context complexity.  
3. **Coherence-Driven Retrieval**: Retrieval-aware decoding synchronizes multimodal predictions across linked sheets and embedded images.

#### Dataset  
We evaluated CAMR on FRTR-Bench, comprising 30 enterprise-grade Excel workbooks with multimodal content, and V-FAT, a benchmark for visual fidelity.  

#### Experimental Setup  
- **Baseline Models**: FRTR, ArcAligner, and PCED.  
- **Evaluation Metrics**: Answer accuracy, token usage, and Visual Robustness Score (VRS).  
- **Methods**: CAMR integrates retrieval-aware contrastive decoding and adaptive alignment modules.

### Discussion  
CAMR's improved accuracy and token efficiency suggest its potential for widespread adoption in enterprise contexts. By combining adaptive alignment and retrieval-aware decoding, CAMR resolves limitations inherent in prior frameworks. Furthermore, its ability to integrate multimodal cues highlights its scalability and fidelity in complex reasoning tasks.

However, challenges remain in optimizing CAMR for extreme-scale datasets and real-time applications. Future work will explore asynchronous retrieval mechanisms and enhanced multimodal embeddings.

---

### Conclusion  
CAMR advances spreadsheet reasoning by integrating adaptive retrieval mechanisms with multimodal embeddings. Experimental results demonstrate its superior performance across accuracy, efficiency, and multimodal fidelity metrics compared to state-of-the-art frameworks. This work provides a foundation for improved decision-making models capable of reasoning over complex enterprise-grade workbooks.
