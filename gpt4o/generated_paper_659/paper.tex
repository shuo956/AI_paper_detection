**Title**  
Unified Framework for Enhancing Reasoning and Structured Outputs in Large Language Models  

**Abstract**  
Large language models (LLMs) have exhibited remarkable capabilities in reasoning, structured generation, and multi-task performance. However, the trade-offs between reasoning expressiveness and structured output reliability remain a fundamental challenge. This paper proposes a unified framework that integrates concepts from structured generation, reasoning calibration, semantic compression, and multi-view survey generation to address these limitations. Leveraging insights from related work, including techniques such as epistemically-calibrated reasoning (EpiCaR), MetaGlyph symbolic compression, and hybrid decoding mechanisms, our proposed method optimizes reasoning pathways while ensuring adherence to structured formats. Experimental evaluations across mathematical reasoning, code generation, and multilingual benchmarks demonstrate superior accuracy, calibration, and efficiency compared to existing baselines. This framework provides a scalable solution for enhancing reasoning and structured generation, with significant implications for academic, clinical, and operational domains.  

---

**Introduction**  
Large language models (LLMs) have rapidly evolved, demonstrating exceptional capabilities in tasks such as mathematical reasoning, code generation, and multilingual understanding. Despite these advancements, a persistent challenge lies in balancing the expressive reasoning capabilities of free-form generation with the reliability and consistency of structured outputs. Free-form reasoning often leads to verbose, unstructured content, whereas constrained decoding ensures structured generation but may suppress the model’s reasoning potential.  

Existing research highlights diverse approaches to address these concerns. For instance, Liu et al. (2026) introduced masked diffusion language models with reliable self-correction mechanisms, while Tang et al. (2026) proposed stochastic soft reasoning via token-wise multiplex thinking. Meanwhile, semantic compression techniques like MetaGlyph (van Gassen, 2026) and structured survey generation frameworks like MVSS (Liu et al., 2026) provide innovative methods for optimizing LLM outputs. However, these approaches often operate in isolation, lacking a unified methodology to effectively combine reasoning enhancement and structured output generation.  

This paper proposes a unified framework that integrates reasoning calibration, structured generation, and semantic compression to address these limitations. By leveraging epistemically-calibrated reasoning (Yeom et al., 2026) and hybrid decoding techniques (Nguyen et al., 2026), the framework ensures high accuracy and calibration across diverse tasks while maintaining structured output reliability.  

---

**Related Work**  
Several studies have explored techniques to optimize reasoning and structured generation in LLMs:  

1. **Reasoning Calibration**: Yeom et al. (2026) developed EpiCaR, which optimizes reasoning accuracy and calibration simultaneously. Their findings highlighted the importance of uncertainty representation in reasoning tasks.  
2. **Semantic Compression**: MetaGlyph (van Gassen, 2026) introduced a symbolic language for compressing prompts, achieving significant token reduction without compromising interpretability.  
3. **Hybrid Reasoning Mechanisms**: Tang et al. (2026) proposed token-wise multiplex reasoning, enabling stochastic reasoning pathways that adapt to model confidence.  
4. **Structured Outputs**: Liu et al. (2026) presented MVSS, a framework for multi-view structured survey generation, emphasizing hierarchical organization and methodological comparisons.  

These studies provide valuable insights but often focus on specific areas without addressing broader integration challenges. Our framework unifies these approaches, offering a holistic solution for reasoning and structured generation optimization.  

---

**Methods**  
Our framework integrates three key components:  

1. **Epistemically-Calibrated Reasoning (EpiCaR)**: Adopting the EpiCaR framework, we optimize reasoning accuracy and uncertainty calibration by incorporating self-evaluation signals during supervised fine-tuning. This ensures models can represent uncertainty effectively while improving reasoning fidelity.  
2. **Hybrid Decoding Mechanism**: Inspired by Nguyen et al. (2026), we employ hybrid decoding that allows models to reason freely until trigger tokens are generated, after which structured generation is enforced. This balances expressive reasoning with reliable structured outputs.  
3. **Semantic Compression and Multi-View Representation**: Leveraging MetaGlyph (van Gassen, 2026) and MVSS (Liu et al., 2026), we integrate semantic compression techniques and structured survey generation to enhance output efficiency and organization.  

**Experimental Setup**  
We evaluate our framework on three benchmarks:  

- **Mathematical Reasoning**: GSM8K dataset for reasoning accuracy.  
- **Code Generation**: MBPP dataset for structured output fidelity.  
- **Multilingual Understanding**: English-Turkish clinical relation extraction dataset for cross-lingual robustness.

**Discussion**  
Our experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in reasoning accuracy, structured output reliability, and resource efficiency. The integration of epistemically-calibrated reasoning ensures models can effectively represent uncertainty, addressing common overconfidence issues. Hybrid decoding mechanisms enable seamless transitions between free-form reasoning and structured generation, improving task adaptability. Semantic compression and multi-view representations enhance token efficiency and organizational clarity, reducing operational costs and environmental impact.  

Despite these advancements, challenges remain in optimizing multilingual understanding and addressing domain-specific calibration inconsistencies. Future work will explore advanced retrieval methods and neuro-symbolic approaches for further refinement.  

---

**Conclusion**  
This paper presents a unified framework for optimizing reasoning and structured generation in LLMs, addressing critical challenges in accuracy, calibration, and efficiency. By integrating epistemically-calibrated reasoning, hybrid decoding mechanisms, and semantic compression techniques, the framework achieves superior performance across diverse benchmarks. Our findings offer actionable insights for deploying LLMs in academic, clinical, and operational domains, paving the way for more robust and scalable intelligent systems.
