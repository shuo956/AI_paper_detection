**Title**  
**Integrating Flow-Matching Generative Decoders and Hierarchical Machine Learning for Efficient Cross-Domain Applications**

---

**Abstract**  
Flow-matching generative decoders and hierarchical machine learning frameworks present significant advancements across diverse domains, from wireless image transmission to federated learning, yet the integration of these techniques for cross-domain applications remains underexplored. This study introduces a novel framework that combines flow-matching generative decoders with adaptive hierarchical machine learning to tackle challenges in cross-domain representation and data processing. By leveraging deterministic generative decoding and adaptive clustering, the proposed method achieves efficient and interpretable results in domains characterized by complex data distributions and limited resources. Experiments across synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrate superior performance in decoding accuracy, robustness, and generalizability, outperforming existing baseline approaches. These results pave the way for scalable, multi-domain solutions in resource-constrained and heterogeneous environments.

---

**Introduction**  
The increasing complexity of cross-domain applications, such as wireless communications, federated learning, and medical diagnostics, necessitates the development of advanced machine learning frameworks that are both computationally efficient and interpretable. Flow-matching generative decoders have demonstrated success in domains like wireless image transmission by enabling low-latency decoding (Fu et al., 2026). Simultaneously, hierarchical machine learning frameworks, such as federated clustering, provide robust solutions for decentralized data processing under privacy constraints (Cai et al., 2026). However, the integration of these advances for cross-domain applications remains underexplored. This study proposes a unified framework that leverages flow-matching techniques for decoding and hierarchical federated approaches for clustering, addressing challenges in data heterogeneity, scalability, and resource efficiency.

---

**Related Work**  
Flow-matching-based generative decoders have revolutionized wireless image transmission by integrating physical channel models into learned probability flows (Fu et al., 2026). Meanwhile, hierarchical federated clustering has emerged as a promising approach to extract knowledge from decentralized clients while preserving privacy (Cai et al., 2026). Recent advancements in adaptive machine learning, such as the Learn-to-Evolve JKO operator (Feng et al., 2026) and compositional neural operators (Hmida et al., 2026), highlight the potential of dynamic learning mechanisms in improving model generalizability. Despite these developments, the application of such techniques in cross-domain scenarios, where data distributions are highly non-IID and constrained by computational resources, remains underexplored.

**Methods/Experiment**  
The proposed framework integrates a flow-matching generative decoder with a hierarchical federated clustering approach. The system is divided into two components:

1. **Flow-Matching Generative Decoder**  
   - The decoder employs a Gaussian smoothing path with a teacher velocity field for deterministic decoding (Fu et al., 2026).
   - Neural network student fields are trained using conditional flow matching, allowing efficient decoding with linear complexity.

2. **Hierarchical Federated Clustering**  
   - Clients perform local clustering using adaptive radial basis functions to generate fine-grained clusterlets (Chiu et al., 2026).
   - Server-side aggregation fuses clusterlets into a unified representation using multi-granular learning mechanisms (Cai et al., 2026).

**Experimental Setup**  
- **Datasets**: MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, and synthetic federated datasets were used to evaluate the framework.
- **Baselines**: JPEG2000+LDPC, DeepJSCC, and one-shot hierarchical federated clustering served as comparison methods.
- **Metrics**: Decoding accuracy, clustering purity, and computational efficiency were evaluated.

**Discussion**  
The results demonstrate the efficacy of integrating flow-matching generative decoding with hierarchical federated clustering for cross-domain applications. The proposed framework achieved superior decoding accuracy and clustering purity while significantly reducing computational costs. These improvements can be attributed to the deterministic nature of the flow-matching decoder and the adaptive mechanisms of the hierarchical clustering approach.

The ability to generalize across diverse datasets highlights the scalability and robustness of the framework. However, further research is needed to optimize the integration of these components, particularly in highly dynamic and resource-constrained environments.

---

**Conclusion**  
This study presents a novel framework that combines flow-matching generative decoders and hierarchical federated clustering for efficient cross-domain applications. By addressing challenges in decoding accuracy, clustering purity, and computational efficiency, the proposed approach outperforms existing baselines and demonstrates strong potential for real-world deployment in resource-constrained and heterogeneous environments.
