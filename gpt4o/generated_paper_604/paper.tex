Title: Enhancing Multilingual Low-Resource NLP Tasks: A Unified Framework for Task Arithmetic, Robust Evaluation, and Optimization Strategies

---

**Abstract**

This study proposes a comprehensive and unified approach to address challenges in low-resource natural language processing (NLP) tasks, leveraging insights from task arithmetic, evaluation benchmarks, and optimization strategies. Current literature highlights significant advancements in areas such as low-resource automatic speech recognition (ASR), misbehavior monitoring in large language models (LLMs), and multilingual code-switching, yet gaps remain in scalability, robustness, and adaptability under diverse conditions. This paper synthesizes methodologies across these domains to create a novel framework that integrates task vector arithmetic, robust benchmarking, and adaptive optimization techniques. Using a combination of experimental setups, including task-specific benchmarks and parameter-efficient fine-tuning, we demonstrate improved performance on multilingual and low-resource tasks. Our results establish new baselines for several key NLP challenges, supported by rigorous evaluation metrics, including Word Error Rate (WER), semantic fidelity, and alignment scores. The proposed framework advances the state-of-the-art in scalable and efficient solutions for low-resource NLP applications while providing actionable insights for future research.

**Introduction**

Low-resource NLP tasks face persistent challenges due to limited labeled datasets, linguistic diversity, and computational constraints. Recent studies, such as Rafkin et al. (2026), have demonstrated the utility of task arithmetic in combining high-resource and low-resource language models to improve ASR performance. Similarly, Yang et al. (2026) introduced AutoMonitor-Bench for systematically evaluating LLM reliability, emphasizing the tension between safety and utility. These advancements underscore the importance of scalable and robust approaches to address the data scarcity and task-specific requirements in low-resource NLP.

While significant progress has been made, existing methodologies often lack universality and fail to account for cross-lingual alignment, robustness under domain shifts, and efficiency in optimization. For instance, He et al. (2026) revealed structural challenges in LLM robustness, and Mansour (2026) highlighted the efficacy of data augmentation in Sudanese dialect ASR. These findings motivate our study to bridge the gaps by integrating task arithmetic, robust evaluation frameworks, and adaptive optimization strategies to improve performance across diverse low-resource NLP tasks.

This paper proposes a unified framework that synthesizes methodologies from task vector arithmetic, multilingual benchmarking, and parameter-efficient optimization. By combining insights from the aforementioned studies, we aim to provide scalable and efficient solutions for multilingual and low-resource NLP applications.

**Related Work**

The related work spans multiple domains that contribute to the development of low-resource NLP systems:

1. **Task Arithmetic for Low-Resource ASR:** Rafkin et al. (2026) utilized task vectors derived from fine-tuned Whisper models to improve performance in low-resource ASR tasks. Our work builds on this by extending task arithmetic principles to multilingual and cross-lingual NLP tasks.

2. **Benchmarking for Model Reliability:** Yang et al. (2026) introduced AutoMonitor-Bench to evaluate LLM reliability across diverse tasks, highlighting the challenges in balancing safety and utility. We incorporate robust evaluation metrics inspired by these benchmarks.

3. **Code-Switching and Multilingual NLP:** Winata et al. (2026) developed CodeMixQA, a benchmark for evaluating LLM capabilities in mixed-language settings. This study informs our approach to addressing multilingual alignment and semantic fidelity.

4. **Parameter-Efficient Optimization:** Wang et al. (2026) proposed OrthoGeoLoRA for geometric fine-tuning, showcasing efficiency in adapting foundation models. We extend these principles to improve scalability and robustness in low-resource settings.

---

**Methods/Experiment**

To validate our proposed framework, we designed an experiment that integrates task arithmetic, benchmarking, and optimization strategies across three low-resource language scenarios:

1. **Task Arithmetic:** We trained Whisper-based models using task vector arithmetic techniques, combining high-resource and low-resource data. Task vectors were optimized for WER on validation datasets.

2. **Robust Benchmarking:** Using benchmarks like AutoMonitor-Bench and CodeMixQA, we evaluated the models' reliability, multilingual alignment, and semantic fidelity. Metrics included Miss Rate (MR), False Alarm Rate (FAR), and alignment scores.

3. **Optimization Strategies:** Parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, including OrthoGeoLoRA, were applied to reduce computational costs while maintaining accuracy. Experiment setups included both supervised and unsupervised adaptation strategies.

**Discussion**

The results demonstrate the effectiveness of our unified framework in improving performance across low-resource NLP tasks. The integration of task arithmetic with parameter-efficient optimization strategies significantly reduced WER and enhanced semantic fidelity. Robust evaluation metrics confirmed improved alignment and robustness, addressing limitations in previous studies.

These findings validate the scalability and adaptability of our framework, offering a practical roadmap for developing low-resource NLP systems. However, challenges remain in extending these methodologies to highly diverse linguistic contexts and dynamic task requirements.
