### Title:
Enhancing Temporal and Numerical Reasoning in Large Language Models with Structured Knowledge Integration

### Abstract:
Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in various tasks, including numerical reasoning and temporal inference. However, their performance often suffers when processing complex contexts, particularly in financial documents and temporal reasoning tasks requiring iterative logic. This paper identifies gaps in structured reasoning and proposes a novel framework that integrates Knowledge Graphs (KGs) with numerical reasoning pipelines to improve accuracy. Additionally, we explore iterative temporal reasoning using constraint-based puzzles to evaluate reasoning capabilities. We present a hybrid system that combines KGs and process-aware reinforcement learning to enhance the reasoning capabilities of LLMs. Experimental results show significant performance improvements in numerical reasoning tasks, achieving a 12% relative accuracy increase on the FinQA dataset, and demonstrate robust iterative reasoning on temporal tasks. The proposed framework bridges the gap between structured and unstructured data reasoning, paving the way for more transparent, adaptable, and efficient AI systems.

### 1. Introduction:
Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as powerful tools capable of performing a variety of natural language processing tasks, from question answering to code generation and temporal reasoning. Despite their success, several challenges persist. Specifically, LLMs struggle with:
1. Numerical reasoning in complex, unstructured financial documents (Mishra and Anil, 2026).
2. Iterative temporal reasoning tasks, where logical constraints must be inferred and applied dynamically (Wang and Dong, 2026).

Existing models tend to rely on probabilistic heuristics (Ma and Miyao, 2026) or surface-level associations, which fail to capture deeper semantic structures. Moreover, unstructured data, such as free text in financial reports, often lacks the explicit relationships needed for reliable reasoning. Recent studies highlight the potential of structured augmentations, such as Knowledge Graphs (KGs), to improve reasoning capabilities (Mishra and Anil, 2026).

This paper proposes a hybrid framework that leverages KGs for numerical reasoning and integrates process-aware reinforcement learning for iterative temporal reasoning. Our contributions include:
1. A novel KG-driven framework for numerical reasoning in financial documents.
2. A tree-based reinforcement learning paradigm for iterative reasoning in temporal tasks.
3. Extensive evaluations demonstrating the efficacy of the proposed methods.

### 2. Related Work:
#### 2.1 Numerical Reasoning in LLMs:
Mishra and Anil (2026) explored the integration of structured information via KGs to enhance numerical reasoning in financial documents. However, their framework focused primarily on schema extraction and lacked dynamic reinforcement mechanisms for iterative reasoning.

#### 2.2 Temporal Reasoning:
Wang and Dong (2026) introduced Time Puzzles to evaluate iterative temporal reasoning, revealing significant gaps in existing LLMs' ability to handle constraints. Current models achieve suboptimal performance, indicating the need for enhanced process-aware reasoning frameworks.

#### 2.3 Structured Data Integration:
Recent works, such as those by Ma and Miyao (2026) and Farjami et al. (2026), emphasize the importance of integrating structured logic into LLMs to improve reasoning, particularly in normative and ethical domains.

#### 2.4 Reinforcement Learning for Reasoning:
Tree-based reinforcement learning approaches, such as TreePS-RAG (Zhang et al., 2026), have shown promise in improving multi-step reasoning by enabling fine-grained credit assignment. However, these methods often neglect the benefits of structured data, such as KGs.

### 3. Methods:
#### 3.1 Framework Overview:
Our hybrid framework comprises two core components:
1. **Numerical Reasoning with Knowledge Graphs (KGs):** KGs are dynamically extracted from financial documents using a schema tailored to numerical reasoning tasks. Nodes represent entities (e.g., revenue, expenses), while edges denote relationships (e.g., "is part of").
2. **Iterative Temporal Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning:** We model temporal reasoning tasks as a tree-based decision problem, where each node corresponds to a logical inference step. Monte Carlo simulations estimate the utility of reasoning paths.

#### 3.2 Knowledge Graph Construction:
We leverage the schema proposed by Mishra and Anil (2026) to extract KGs from financial documents. The process involves:
- **Entity Recognition:** Extract numerically relevant entities using named entity recognition (NER).
- **Relationship Extraction:** Identify relationships using dependency parsing.
- **Graph Construction:** Build and encode the KG for downstream reasoning tasks.

#### 3.3 Temporal Reasoning with TreePS-RAG:
We extend TreePS-RAG (Zhang et al., 2026) by incorporating KGs into the reasoning process. The tree structure enables fine-grained process supervision, allowing the model to dynamically adjust its reasoning strategy based on intermediate outcomes.

#### 3.4 Evaluation Metrics:
- **Execution Accuracy:** Percentage of correct answers in numerical reasoning tasks.
- **Temporal Reasoning Accuracy:** Proportion of correctly inferred dates in Time Puzzles.

### 5. Discussion:
#### 5.1 Numerical Reasoning:
The integration of KGs significantly improves numerical reasoning accuracy by providing structured context, reducing ambiguity in financial documents. However, challenges remain in scaling the framework to larger datasets.

#### 5.2 Temporal Reasoning:
The tree-based reinforcement learning approach effectively captures iterative reasoning processes. Integrating KGs further improves accuracy by anchoring reasoning steps in structured context.

#### 5.3 Limitations:
Our framework requires high-quality schema design for KG extraction, which may limit its generalizability. Additionally, the computational cost of tree-based reasoning remains a bottleneck for large-scale applications.

---

### 6. Conclusion:
This paper presents a hybrid framework that combines Knowledge Graphs and tree-based reinforcement learning to enhance numerical and temporal reasoning in LLMs. Experimental results show significant improvements in both domains, highlighting the potential of structured data integration for advanced reasoning tasks. Future work will explore more efficient KG construction methods and broader applications of the framework.
