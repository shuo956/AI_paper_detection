# Title  
**Dynamic Reward Mechanisms in Multi-Agent Systems: Bridging Role-Aware Reasoning and Predictive Generalization**

---

# Abstract  
The complexity of multi-agent systems (MAS) requires innovative methodologies to balance role consistency, adaptability, and predictive generalization. While recent advancements such as Character-R1 and LOGO world models have improved role-aware reasoning and local-to-global dynamics prediction, gaps remain in integrating reward mechanisms and generalization capabilities across diverse MAS settings. This paper introduces a novel framework, Adaptive Reward Synergy (ARS), combining cognitive-refined reward modeling with predictive dynamics inspired by object-centric representations. ARS leverages adaptive reward normalization and uncertainty-aware sampling, enhancing generalization and role alignment in high-dimensional, heterogeneous MAS environments. Extensive experiments across multi-agent reinforcement learning benchmarks demonstrate ARS's superior performance in reward distribution consistency, generalization accuracy, and computational efficiency. The findings contribute to better MAS design by bridging role-aware reasoning with scalable generalization under dynamic reward mechanisms.

---

# Introduction  
Multi-agent systems (MAS) are foundational in applications spanning robotics, autonomous vehicles, and distributed computing. A critical challenge in MAS is balancing role-aware reasoning with predictive generalization to ensure robust decision-making in high-dimensional and heterogeneous environments. While frameworks like Character-R1 focus on cognitive consistency through explicit reward signals, and LOGO world models introduce local-to-global dynamics for offline multi-agent reinforcement learning, the integration of adaptive reward mechanisms remains underexplored. This paper proposes Adaptive Reward Synergy (ARS), a framework addressing these gaps by synergizing role-aware reward modeling with predictive dynamics. The study builds on existing literature and aims to provide a scalable solution enhancing MAS performance across diverse scenarios.

# Related Work  
### Role-Aware Reasoning  
Character-R1 (Tang et al., 2026) introduces cognitive-focused rewards to enforce internal consistency among role-playing agents. It employs reference-guided optimization to enhance exploration, but its applicability in MAS lacks full integration of predictive generalization mechanisms.

### Predictive Generalization  
LOGO world models (Li et al., 2026) leverage local predictions to infer global dynamics in offline MARL. However, their policy learning is constrained by dataset distributions, with limited exploration of adaptive reward mechanisms.

### Reward Modeling  
AdaJudge (Miao et al., 2026) improves alignment between reward signals and task-dependent preferences but focuses on static inductive biases, leaving dynamic reward adaptation underexplored.

### Object-Centric Representations  
ObjectZero (Vakhitov et al., 2026) employs graph neural networks for object-level dynamics modeling, offering insights into structured MAS environments. Yet, integration with reward normalization remains absent.

This study combines insights from these works to develop ARS, addressing the gaps in dynamic reward mechanisms and generalization across MAS environments.

# Methods  
### Framework Description  
**Adaptive Reward Synergy (ARS)** integrates cognitive-refined reward modeling with predictive dynamics using object-centric representations. ARS consists of three core components:

1. **Role-Conditioned Reward Normalization**: Inspired by Character-R1, rewards are dynamically adjusted based on role categories to ensure robust optimization across heterogeneous agents.
2. **Uncertainty-Aware Sampling Mechanism**: Building on LOGO, synthetic data sampling is weighted by prediction uncertainty, reducing error propagation in policy learning.
3. **Cross-Attention Predictive Refinement**: Adapting ObjectZero principles, ARS employs cross-attention mechanisms to refine global dynamics predictions using localized observations.

### Experimental Setup  
Experiments were conducted on standard offline MARL benchmarks, including cooperative navigation and distributed task allocation scenarios. Metrics assessed include reward distribution consistency, prediction accuracy, and computational efficiency.
