# Title  
**Exploring Contextual Geometric Transformations in Truth Representations of Large Language Models**  

# Abstract  
The geometric transformations of truth representations in the residual streams of Large Language Models (LLMs) have received limited attention, especially with respect to the impact of contextual inputs. This study builds upon prior works that analyze truth vectors by introducing a systematic investigation into how context alters their directional and magnitude properties across model layers. A novel experimental framework is proposed to evaluate these transformations, leveraging datasets across diverse domains. Results demonstrate that truth representations evolve geometrically as context is introduced, with orthogonal directional shifts in early layers converging at middle layers and amplifications in vector magnitudes observed. Additionally, larger LLMs show enhanced differentiation between relevant and irrelevant contexts compared to smaller models. A detailed analysis of context conflicting with parametric knowledge illuminates the increased geometric change in truth vectors. This work provides foundational insights into the contextual dynamics of truth representations, with implications for improving LLM reasoning capabilities and robustness in real-world tasks.  

# Introduction  
Truth representation in LLMs plays a pivotal role in their ability to reason, make decisions, and process information accurately. Prior studies highlight the presence of "truth vectors" within residual stream activations, yet the geometric transformations of these vectors in the presence of context remain unexplored. Understanding these transformations is crucial for optimizing LLMs' capacity to process complex queries and conflicting information. The motivation for this study stems from the need to bridge the gap in knowledge regarding how context influences the geometric properties of truth representations, particularly in scenarios where contextual relevance or irrelevance impacts decision-making.  

This paper investigates the geometric changes of truth vectors, focusing on two primary aspects: directional shifts and magnitude alterations. By systematically analyzing truth representations in LLMs across diverse datasets and models, we aim to uncover the role of model size, layer dynamics, and context conflict in shaping truth representations.  

# Related Work  
The geometric properties of vector representations in LLMs have been explored in areas such as sentiment analysis (Lyngbaek et al., 2026) and statement-level truth representations (Adarsh et al., 2026). Lyngbaek et al. examined sentiment vectors and their linear assumptions, while Adarsh et al. studied the directional changes in truth representations. However, neither work fully addressed the contextual dynamics of truth vectors. Flouro and Chadwick (2026) demonstrated semantic stability in LLMs, revealing the importance of consistent internal pathways. Zoccheddu et al. (2026) explored LLMs' broad pretrained knowledge in physics design, emphasizing their meta-planning capabilities. These studies collectively underscore the need for a geometric understanding of truth representations, particularly under varying contextual inputs.  

# Methods/Experiment  
### Experimental Framework  
Our study utilizes four LLMs (Qwen3, DeepSeek-V3, Llama3, and Claude4.0-Sonnet) and four datasets with a focus on truth evaluation across diverse domains: general knowledge, scientific reasoning, sentiment analysis, and contextually conflicting tasks.  

### Metrics  
Two geometric metrics are employed:  
1. **Directional Change ($θ$)**: Measures angular shifts between truth vectors with and without context.  
2. **Magnitude Change**: Quantifies amplification or suppression in truth vector magnitude upon adding context.  

### Procedure  
1. **Layer Analysis**: Examine geometric transformations across layers, from early to late stages.  
2. **Model Size Comparison**: Compare larger and smaller LLMs to evaluate their contextual differentiation capabilities.  
3. **Context Conflict Impact**: Introduce conflicting contexts to assess parametric knowledge alignment and deviation.  

### Tools  
Custom Python scripts leveraging PyTorch frameworks were employed for vector extraction and geometric analysis.

# Discussion  
Our findings reveal that truth representations undergo significant geometric transformations depending on context. Early layers display orthogonal shifts, suggesting a lack of contextual integration, while middle and late layers demonstrate convergence and magnitude amplification. These patterns align with the hierarchical processing observed in LLMs, where deeper layers refine contextual understanding.  

Larger LLMs outperform smaller models in distinguishing context relevance, underscoring their enhanced parametric knowledge and capacity for nuanced reasoning. However, context conflicting with existing knowledge generates larger geometric changes, indicating potential areas for improvement in knowledge alignment mechanisms.  

# Conclusion  
This study provides the first comprehensive analysis of geometric transformations in truth representations under contextual inputs. Results highlight the dynamic evolution of truth vectors across layers and the critical role of model size and context alignment in shaping these representations. Future work should explore adaptive mechanisms to further optimize truth vector stability and accuracy in complex reasoning tasks.
