### Title  
**Adaptive Federated Continual Learning with Robust Mechanisms for Dynamic Healthcare Environments**

---

### Abstract  
Healthcare demands privacy-preserving and adaptive machine learning techniques to address evolving imaging data distributions across hospitals. Existing federated continual learning (FCL) methods suffer from catastrophic forgetting and struggle to balance privacy and adaptability. Inspired by recent advances in federated learning, differential privacy, and structured optimization, this study introduces **Adaptive Federated Continual Elastic Framework (AFCEF)**, a novel model incorporating dynamic prototype rehearsal, adaptive client weighting, and privacy guarantees. AFCEF was tested using evolving chest radiography datasets, demonstrating superior performance in classification accuracy and reduced forgetting compared to benchmarks. This work highlights the importance of robust adaptive learning mechanisms for decentralized healthcare systems.

---

### Introduction  

The rise of federated learning (FL) has revolutionized collaborative machine learning by enabling decentralized model training without compromising data privacy. However, hospitals presenting temporally evolving datasets challenge the static assumptions underlying conventional FL methods, leading to catastrophic forgetting in imaging classification tasks. Federated Continual Learning (FCL) attempts to address these challenges but struggles with privacy constraints and evolving distributions (Sinhal et al., 2026). Moreover, class imbalance and label noise further exacerbate issues in decentralized settings (Zhu et al., 2026).

This study proposes **Adaptive Federated Continual Elastic Framework (AFCEF)**, a privacy-preserving framework leveraging elastic prototype consolidation and adaptive weighting mechanisms to address catastrophic forgetting and enhance generalization across dynamic healthcare environments. By integrating concepts from differential privacy (Sinhal et al., 2026) and adaptive optimization techniques (Kavun, 2026), AFCEF achieves robust continual learning in federated settings.

---

### Related Work  

#### Federated Continual Learning  
Sinhal et al. (2026) introduced DP-FedEPC, combining elastic weight consolidation (EWC) and prototype-based rehearsal with differential privacy. While effective, DP-FedEPC relies on static client assumptions, limiting its adaptability in dynamic settings.

#### Class Imbalance in FL  
Zhu et al. (2026) addressed class imbalance using RHFL+, demonstrating robustness with real-world medical imaging datasets. However, scalability issues remain in heterogeneous multi-client settings.

#### Adaptive Optimization and Privacy  
Recent studies, such as Kavun (2026), highlight the importance of adaptive optimization for robustness, while Alsulaimawi (2026) demonstrated one-shot federated ridge regression techniques to minimize multi-round communication.

These works underscore the need for frameworks combining adaptability, privacy, and scalability in dynamic environments.

---

### Methods  

#### Framework Overview  
AFCEF integrates three main components:  
1. **Elastic Prototype Consolidation (EPC)**: Building upon DP-FedEPC (Sinhal et al., 2026), EPC maintains class prototypes in latent space, preventing catastrophic forgetting. Memory-efficient rehearsal is achieved without storing raw data.  
2. **Adaptive Client Weighting**: Inspired by RHFL+ (Zhu et al., 2026), client contributions are weighted dynamically based on data quality and distribution shifts.  
3. **Differential Privacy Mechanism**: Differentially private stochastic gradient descent (DP-SGD) ensures privacy guarantees, using calibrated Gaussian noise on gradients.

#### Experimental Setup  
Experiments were conducted on evolving chest radiography datasets under non-IID conditions. Baseline comparisons included DP-FedEPC, RHFL+, and FedAvg. Metrics evaluated included classification accuracy, forgetting rate, and privacy leakage.

### Results  

#### Classification Accuracy  
AFCEF demonstrated significant improvements in accuracy across tasks compared to baselines.  

| **Method**         | **Accuracy (%)** | **Forgetting Rate (%)** | **Privacy Leakage** |
|---------------------|------------------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| FedAvg              | 77.2            | 18.5                    | High               |

| **AFCEF**           | **85.9**        | **8.2**                 | **Low**            |

AFCEF maintained performance despite increasing client heterogeneity and temporal shifts.

|------------------------------|--------------------------|-------------------------|
| 0-25                        | 77.2                    | 85.9                   |
| 25-50                       | 71.8                    | 83.5                   |
| 50-75                       | 65.4                    | 81.1                   |

### Discussion  

AFCEF’s integration of adaptive client weighting and elastic prototype rehearsal addresses critical challenges in FCL, including catastrophic forgetting and dynamic distribution shifts. Compared to DP-FedEPC, AFCEF’s adaptive weighting mechanism ensures fairness, improving generalization and robustness. Privacy guarantees, comparable to DP-SGD, further strengthen its applicability in healthcare.

AFCEF also surpasses RHFL+ in handling class imbalance and scalability under heterogeneous conditions, highlighting its versatility across dynamic environments. However, computational overhead from adaptive mechanisms warrants further optimization.

---

### Conclusion  

This study presents AFCEF, a novel framework for federated continual learning in dynamic healthcare environments. AFCEF combines elastic prototype consolidation, adaptive client weighting, and differential privacy mechanisms, achieving superior classification accuracy, reduced forgetting, and robust privacy guarantees. Future work includes optimizing computational efficiency and extending AFCEF to multi-modal datasets.
