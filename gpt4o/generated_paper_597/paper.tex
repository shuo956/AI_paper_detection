**Title**: Enhancing Multimodal Representation Learning with Graph Structural Insights and Temporal Dependencies

---

**Abstract**

This paper proposes a novel approach for multimodal representation learning by integrating graph-based insights and temporal dependencies into the learning process. While existing methodologies have advanced multimodal models by incorporating contrastive objectives or generative frameworks, challenges remain in effectively capturing the intricate relationships between multimodal inputs and their associated temporal structures. Leveraging the strengths of Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), we introduce a hybrid framework that combines graph-based spatial modeling with temporal encoding mechanisms. The proposed framework is evaluated across multiple multimodal tasks, including drug-target interaction prediction, subject-independent emotion recognition, and anomaly detection in IoT networks. Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in predictive performance, efficiency, and interpretability across diverse datasets. Our findings reveal the importance of fusing graph and temporal insights to enhance multimodal learning models.

---

**1. Introduction**

The rise of multimodal machine learning has enabled significant advancements in domains such as healthcare, computational biology, and environmental monitoring. However, effectively capturing the relationships between modalities, especially in the presence of temporal dependencies and spatial structures, remains challenging. Recent works, such as Tensor-DTI [Gil-Sorribes et al., 2026], have highlighted the potential of integrating multimodal embeddings for drug-target interaction prediction. Meanwhile, Zhou et al. (2026) demonstrated the importance of local-global feature fusion for subject-independent EEG emotion recognition, emphasizing the role of temporal dependencies.

In this work, we aim to address two key gaps in existing research: (1) the limited integration of graph structural insights into multimodal frameworks and (2) the insufficient modeling of temporal dependencies across modalities. This study proposes a unified framework combining Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) with temporal encoding mechanisms to overcome these limitations and enhance multimodal representation learning.

**2. Related Work**

Our work builds upon several critical advancements in multimodal learning and graph-based modeling:

1. **Multimodal Learning**: Tensor-DTI introduced a contrastive framework for improving drug-target interaction prediction by integrating multimodal embeddings [Gil-Sorribes et al., 2026]. Similarly, HELVAE leveraged Hellinger pooling to efficiently represent multimodal data [Vo & Valera, 2026].

2. **Graph Neural Networks**: The role of GNNs in learning spatial and structural insights has been well-documented. For instance, HOPE proposed orthogonal prototype experts to address the linear projection bottleneck in heterogeneous graphs [Zhou et al., 2026].

3. **Temporal Dependencies**: Recent works, such as Zhou et al. (2026), emphasize the importance of temporal dynamics in EEG emotion recognition. Similarly, Zhang et al. (2026) highlighted the utility of temporal modeling for grid carbon intensity forecasting.

Our study uniquely combines these advancements by integrating graph-based spatial modeling with temporal encoding to enhance multimodal representation learning.

**3. Methods**

### 3.1 Framework Overview

The proposed framework integrates graph-based learning with temporal encoding to model multimodal data effectively. The framework consists of three core components:

1. **Graph Construction**: Multimodal inputs are represented as nodes in a graph, with edges capturing spatial and semantic relationships.
2. **Temporal Encoding**: Temporal dependencies are incorporated using attention-based mechanisms, inspired by Zhou et al. (2026).
3. **Fusion Mechanism**: A dual-branch architecture combines graph-based embeddings with temporal features, enabling robust multimodal representation learning.

### 3.2 Graph Neural Network Module

We employ a GNN module to capture spatial and structural relationships between multimodal inputs. Each node represents a modality, and edge weights are dynamically updated based on feature similarity and temporal proximity.

### 3.3 Temporal Attention Module

Inspired by the successes of attention mechanisms in modeling sequential data, we adopt a temporal attention module. This module encodes the temporal dependencies between multimodal inputs, enabling the model to capture dynamic patterns effectively.

### 3.4 Loss Function

The framework utilizes a contrastive loss function to enhance the alignment of multimodal embeddings while preserving their distinctiveness.
