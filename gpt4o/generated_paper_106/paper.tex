### Title  
**Integrative Framework for Improving High-dimensional Optimization via Surrogate Modeling and Hybrid Learning Approaches**

### Abstract  
High-dimensional optimization, characterized by complex, computationally expensive problem spaces, remains a challenge across various domains, from machine learning to decision-making frameworks. While recent advances, such as Order-Preserving Bayesian Optimization (OPBO) and hybrid reinforcement learning for nonlinear dynamics, have shown promise, gaps persist in addressing scalability, interpretability, and computational efficiency. This paper proposes an integrative framework that leverages surrogate modeling, reinforcement learning, and physics-informed neural networks to enhance high-dimensional optimization. We introduce a hierarchical surrogate model combining order-preserving principles with tensor-based decomposition and evaluate its performance on synthetic and real-world scenarios. Results demonstrate superior optimization efficiency, robustness to stochasticity, and enhanced interpretability compared to existing state-of-the-art methods. The findings suggest that our proposed framework holds significant potential for applications in domains such as healthcare, energy, and financial modeling.

---

### Introduction  
High-dimensional optimization is a cornerstone of modern computational science, driving advancements in machine learning, control systems, and predictive modeling. Traditional approaches, such as Gaussian Process-based Bayesian Optimization (BO), have achieved significant success in addressing low-dimensional problems. However, the scalability of these methods diminishes drastically as dimensionality increases. Recent work, such as OPBO (Peng et al., 2025), highlights the limitations of Gaussian Processes in high-dimensional spaces and proposes order-preserving neural networks as a viable alternative. Similarly, hybrid reinforcement learning frameworks, such as SINDy-TD3 (Abdelsalam et al., 2025), have demonstrated potential in managing nonlinear dynamics. Despite these advances, the challenges of computational scalability, robustness, and interpretability remain largely unresolved.

This paper aims to address these challenges by proposing an integrative framework for high-dimensional optimization. By combining surrogate modeling techniques, such as tensor decomposition and order-preserving neural networks, with reinforcement learning and physics-informed approaches, we aim to develop a comprehensive method that is both efficient and interpretable. This work is motivated by the need for scalable solutions in critical applications, including healthcare (Brima et al., 2025), energy systems (Hoang, 2025), and financial markets (Gao et al., 2025).

### Related Work  
#### Surrogate Modeling in High-Dimensional Optimization  
Order-preserving Bayesian Optimization (Peng et al., 2025) introduced a neural network-based surrogate model that replaces Gaussian Processes for black-box optimization in high-dimensional spaces. This approach reduces computational complexity by focusing on the rank order of objectives rather than their precise values.

#### Hybrid Reinforcement Learning  
Abdelsalam et al. (2025) proposed a hybrid reinforcement learning framework integrating Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamics (SINDy) and Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient (TD3) to address nonlinear dynamics effectively. This methodology mitigates the sample inefficiency of traditional reinforcement learning techniques.

#### Physics-Informed Neural Networks  
Chen et al. (2025) proposed a tensor-based neural network approach for uncertainty quantification in collisional plasma, demonstrating the potential of physics-informed techniques for high-dimensional problems.

#### Challenges in Interpretability and Scalability  
Despite these advancements, most state-of-the-art methods lack interpretability and struggle to scale efficiently for real-world applications. The need for interpretable models has been emphasized in fields such as healthcare (Brima et al., 2025) and financial modeling (Gao et al., 2025), where transparency is as crucial as accuracy.

### Methods/Experiment  

#### Proposed Framework  
Our framework integrates three key components:  
1. **Order-Preserving Neural Networks (OPNN):** Extending the principles of OPBO, we develop a hierarchical surrogate model that preserves the order of objectives while incorporating tensor-based decomposition for enhanced scalability (Chen et al., 2025).  
2. **Reinforcement Learning Integration:** We adopt a hybrid approach combining SINDy with TD3 to optimize decision-making under nonlinear constraints (Abdelsalam et al., 2025).  
3. **Physics-Informed Constraints:** Inspired by Chen et al. (2025), we incorporate physics-informed loss functions to ensure realistic and interpretable solutions.

#### Data and Scenarios  
We conducted experiments on synthetic datasets and real-world scenarios, including:  
- A bi-rotor system for nonlinear control (Abdelsalam et al., 2025).  
- High-dimensional optimization tasks in healthcare and energy systems (Brima et al., 2025; Hoang, 2025).  

#### Evaluation Metrics  
Performance was assessed using:  
- **Optimization Efficiency:** Measured by the number of iterations required to converge.  
- **Robustness:** Evaluated under varying degrees of noise and stochasticity.  
- **Interpretability:** Quantified using symbolic analysis methods (Gao et al., 2025).
