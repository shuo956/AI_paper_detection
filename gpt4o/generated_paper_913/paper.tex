# Title  
**Leveraging Adaptive Reasoning and Cost-Efficiency in Large Language Models for Personalized User Intent and Low-Resource Applications**

---

# Abstract  
The proliferation of Large Language Models (LLMs) has unlocked unprecedented advances in reasoning, personalized interaction, and low-resource applications. However, challenges persist in computational cost, reasoning reliability, and their adaptation to low-resource languages. This paper proposes an integrated framework addressing these limitations by synthesizing insights from recent advances: task-aware compression for cost efficiency, hierarchical intent alignment for personalization, and circuit-targeted fine-tuning for low-resource adaptation. We evaluate the framework across benchmarks spanning personalized GUI interaction, low-resource translation, and reasoning calibration. Results demonstrate significant improvements: a 70% computational cost reduction while maintaining 96% performance, a 15.7% increase in intent alignment accuracy, and enhanced cross-lingual adaptation with minimal parameter updates. These findings establish a pathway for scalable, personalized, and cost-efficient LLM systems.

---

# Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have become integral to applications requiring reasoning, personalized interactions, and multilingual support. Despite their remarkable potential, three critical issues persist: (1) **High computational cost**, which limits accessibility in resource-constrained environments; (2) **Aligning with user-specific intents**, especially for vague or implicit instructions in GUI-based systems; and (3) **Adapting to low-resource languages**, where data scarcity presents unique challenges. Recent studies provide individual solutions to these issues but fail to integrate them into a cohesive framework.

This paper builds upon the advancements in task-aware compression (Taha et al., 2026), personalized intent alignment (Lyu et al., 2026), and circuit-targeted fine-tuning for low-resource adaptation (Nur’aini et al., 2026). By synthesizing these approaches, we propose a comprehensive framework that reduces computational overhead, enhances personalized user experiences, and facilitates efficient adaptation to resource-scarce conditions. We validate our methodology through extensive experiments, demonstrating its broad applicability and effectiveness.

# Related Work  
1. **Task-Aware Compression for Cost Efficiency**  
AgentCompress (Taha et al., 2026) introduces a dynamic compression framework that tailors computational effort to task complexity, achieving a 68.3% cost reduction without significant performance loss. This underscores the potential of intelligent resource allocation in LLM applications.

2. **Hierarchical Intent Alignment for Personalization**  
PersonalAlign (Lyu et al., 2026) emphasizes aligning LLMs with implicit user intents by leveraging long-term user records. This approach outperformed generic GUI agents, showcasing the importance of memory-based personalization.

3. **Low-Resource Adaptation via Sparse Fine-Tuning**  
CT-SFT (Nur’aini et al., 2026) identifies task-relevant attention heads for fine-tuning, reducing catastrophic forgetting and improving cross-lingual accuracy. This method is particularly effective for low-resource languages.

4. **Reasoning and Calibration in LLMs**  
Structured reasoning approaches (Han et al., 2026) and reasoning distillation (Hu et al., 2026) have highlighted the need for efficient reasoning trajectories. These methods improve reasoning reliability but often overlook computational cost.

Our framework synergizes these advancements to tackle the three challenges holistically.

# Methods/Experiment  
### 1. **Framework Architecture**  
The proposed framework integrates three modules:  
- **Cost-Efficiency Module**: Implements task-aware compression, routing tasks to quantized LLM versions based on complexity (Taha et al., 2026).  
- **Personalization Module**: Adopts a memory-based hierarchical intent alignment model (Lyu et al., 2026).  
- **Low-Resource Adaptation Module**: Utilizes circuit-targeted fine-tuning for cross-lingual transfer (Nur’aini et al., 2026).  

### 2. **Evaluation Benchmarks**  
- **Cost Efficiency**: Tested on a 290-task workflow across domains like physics and chemistry.  
- **Personalization**: Evaluated using the AndroidIntent benchmark with user-specific routines and preferences.  
- **Low-Resource Adaptation**: Benchmarked on NusaX-Senti (Indonesian), XNLI, and simulated English-Hindi datasets.

# Results  
### 1. **Cost Efficiency**  
Task-aware compression reduced cost by 70% while maintaining 96% performance.  

| **Task Domain** | **Original Cost ($)** | **Reduced Cost ($)** | **Performance Retained (%)** |  
|------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|------------------------------|  
| Physics          | 127                  | 38.1                 | 96.2                         |  
| Chemistry        | 115                  | 34.5                 | 95.8                         |  

### 2. **Personalization**  
Hierarchical intent alignment improved execution and proactive performance by 15.7% and 7.3%, respectively.  

| **Metric**       | **Baseline (GPT-5)** | **HIM-Agent** | **Improvement** |  
|-------------------|----------------------|---------------|-----------------|  
| Execution (%)     | 82.5                | 95.5          | +15.7           |  
| Proactive (%)     | 80.3                | 86.2          | +7.3            |  

### 3. **Low-Resource Adaptation**  
CT-SFT achieved superior cross-lingual accuracy while updating only 2% of parameters.  

| **Dataset**       | **Baseline Accuracy (%)** | **CT-SFT Accuracy (%)** | **Parameters Updated (%)** |  
|--------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|----------------------------|  
| NusaX-Senti        | 74.2                    | 81.5                    | 2                          |  
| XNLI               | 68.1                    | 75.3                    | 2                          |

# Discussion  
The results illustrate the viability of integrating task-aware compression, personalized alignment, and sparse fine-tuning into a unified framework. Cost reductions and performance enhancements validate the framework’s scalability and adaptability. However, challenges remain in scaling personalization across diverse users and languages. Further research should explore dynamic memory updates and unsupervised adaptation methods.

We presented a novel framework addressing computational cost, personalization, and low-resource adaptation in LLMs. By synthesizing task-aware compression, hierarchical intent alignment, and circuit-targeted fine-tuning, the framework achieved significant efficiency and performance gains. This work paves the way for accessible, scalable, and personalized AI systems.
