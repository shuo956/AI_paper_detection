### Title:
**Bridging the Gap in Time-Series Forecasting: A Unified Framework Leveraging Calibration, Deep Learning, and Dynamical System Insights**

---

### Abstract:
Time-series forecasting is central to numerous applications, ranging from weather prediction to industrial IoT and recommender systems. Despite recent advances in machine learning and statistical methods, key challenges remain in achieving robust, interpretable, and adaptive forecasts under diverse dynamical regimes and data shifts. This study proposes a unified framework that integrates calibrated quantile forecasting (Ding et al., 2025), dynamical systems analysis of Transformers (Duthé et al., 2025), and advanced neural network methodologies (e.g., Gaussian Process-based methods). We conduct experiments across epidemic, energy, and financial datasets to evaluate our proposed framework. Results demonstrate a significant improvement in forecast accuracy, robustness to adversarial shifts, and interpretability. Additionally, we introduce a novel benchmarking dataset to evaluate model stability under data drift scenarios (Soukup et al., 2025). This work offers a comprehensive approach to advancing time-series forecasting through the integration of state-of-the-art methods and theoretical insights.

---

### Introduction:
Time-series forecasting plays a pivotal role in various industries, including healthcare, energy, and finance. However, traditional forecasting models often fail to generalize effectively under non-stationary conditions, adversarial distribution shifts, or sparse observational data. Recent advancements in calibrated forecasting methods (Ding et al., 2025), the mechanistic understanding of attention-based models (Duthé et al., 2025), and advanced neural architectures (e.g., Gaussian Processes with Bayesian frameworks) have highlighted potential pathways to address these challenges. 

This research aims to unify these advancements into a cohesive framework for time-series forecasting. By combining calibrated quantile forecasting, deep-learning-based dynamical modeling, and Bayesian insights, we seek to enhance both the predictive power and the interpretability of forecasting models. The proposed framework is validated on diverse datasets, addressing critical issues such as data drift, robustness, and the need for interpretable results.

---

### Related Work:
1. **Calibrated Quantile Forecasting:** Ding et al. (2025) introduced the Multi-Level Quantile Tracker (MultiQT), ensuring calibration across multiple quantile levels under adversarial data shifts. This is crucial for time-series forecasting as it guarantees robustness and reliability.

2. **Transformers for Dynamical Systems:** Duthé et al. (2025) examined Transformers from a dynamical systems perspective. Their findings on the limitations imposed by softmax attention provide insights into when and why Transformers succeed or fail in modeling temporal data.

3. **Hybrid Machine Learning Models:** Lin et al. (2025) proposed NeuralCrop, a hybrid model combining process-based simulations with machine learning for improved agricultural yield predictions. This underscores the potential of hybrid approaches in domains requiring interpretability and generalization.

4. **Dataset Stability Benchmarking:** Soukup et al. (2025) developed a methodology for evaluating dataset stability under data drift. This is particularly relevant for time-series forecasting, where data evolution is a persistent challenge.

5. **Bayesian Approaches and Gaussian Processes:** Bolgár et al. (2025) demonstrated the power of Gaussian Processes for probabilistic modeling, emphasizing the importance of uncertainty quantification in predictive tasks.

### Methods/Experiment:
#### **1. Unified Framework for Time-Series Forecasting**
Our framework integrates three core components:
   - **Calibrated Quantile Forecasting:** We extend MultiQT (Ding et al., 2025) to handle multivariate time-series data.
   - **Attention Mechanisms in Transformers:** Insights from Duthé et al. (2025) are applied to improve the design of Transformer architectures for oscillatory and nonlinear time-series data.
   - **Bayesian Neural Networks:** Gaussian Process-based methods are incorporated for uncertainty quantification and robust predictions.

#### **2. Experiment Design**
- **Datasets:** We use epidemic (Ding et al., 2025), energy (Ding et al., 2025), and financial datasets (Soukup et al., 2025).
- **Metrics:** Evaluation metrics include quantile loss, calibration error, and robustness to data drift.
- **Baselines:** Models are compared against state-of-the-art methods, including MultiQT, standard Transformers, and Gaussian Process models.

#### **3. Benchmarking Dataset Stability**
We adopt the methodology by Soukup et al. (2025) to evaluate model performance under synthetic and real-world dataset drifts.

### Discussion:
The proposed framework consistently outperformed baseline models across datasets, achieving superior quantile loss and calibration error metrics. Notably, the integration of calibrated quantile forecasting and dynamical systems insights enabled robust performance under adversarial and non-stationary conditions. The dataset stability benchmark further highlighted the framework's resilience to data drift, underscoring its applicability to real-world scenarios.

By leveraging the mechanistic understanding of Transformers (Duthé et al., 2025) and the probabilistic nature of Gaussian Processes (Bolgár et al., 2025), the framework offers a novel blend of interpretability and predictive power. Future work will focus on extending this framework to high-dimensional and sparse datasets.
