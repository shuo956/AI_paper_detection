### Title
**Adaptive Scalable Frameworks for Enhancing Multimodal and Multilingual Large Language Models through Efficient Learning Mechanisms**

---

### Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) and Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated remarkable progress in multimodal reasoning, multilingual tasks, and resource-efficient deployment. Despite these advancements, key challenges persist in areas such as mitigating hallucinations, enhancing multilingual alignment, improving ultra-low-bit quantization, and enabling efficient fine-tuning. This paper proposes a novel framework that integrates insights from diffusion-based learning, curriculum optimization, and sparse representation to address these gaps. We introduce a hybrid methodology combining counterfactual data generation for VLMs with scalable parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) for multilingual tasks. Experimental results show significant improvements in temporal reasoning, multilingual task alignment, and computational efficiency. Additionally, we demonstrate the framework's adaptability across diverse benchmarks, including action recognition for videos and multilingual steering. Our findings contribute to the development of more robust, efficient, and adaptable LLMs and VLMs.

### Introduction
Large Language Models (LLMs) and Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have revolutionized natural language processing (NLP) and computer vision (CV). Despite their successes, challenges remain in addressing issues like hallucination in VLMs, efficient multilingual alignment, and resource-efficient deployment. For instance, Poppi et al. (2026) highlight the issue of action and temporal hallucinations in VLMs, while Farashah et al. (2026) discuss the complexities of multilingual unlearning. Furthermore, the economic and environmental costs associated with deploying LLMs necessitate innovations in ultra-low-bit quantization (Cao et al., 2026) and parameter-efficient fine-tuning (Kim et al., 2026).

This study aims to bridge these gaps by leveraging counterfactual video generation, curriculum learning, and fine-tuned parameter-efficient approaches. By integrating these methodologies, we propose a unified framework that optimizes multimodal reasoning, multilingual alignment, and computational efficiency. Our key contributions include a new dataset for counterfactual video analysis, a scalable fine-tuning approach, and insights into the underlying mechanisms of multilingual reasoning and alignment.

### Related Work
Multiple studies have addressed the limitations of LLMs and VLMs in multimodal and multilingual contexts. Poppi et al. (2026) introduced CounterVid, a dataset tackling action hallucinations in VLMs through counterfactual video generation. Similarly, Mim et al. (2026) showed that unimodal LLMs could provide feedback to improve VLMs. On the multilingual front, Farashah et al. (2026) explored unlearning mechanisms, while Ebing et al. (2026) investigated the impact of romanization on cross-lingual transfer.

In terms of computational efficiency, Cao et al. (2026) proposed a sliced-Wasserstein loss for ultra-low-bit quantization, and Kim et al. (2026) emphasized the role of inherent reasoning in multilingual tasks. Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods like LoRA (Wang et al., 2026) and its orthogonal variants have also shown promise in reducing computational overhead.

### Methods/Experiment
#### 1. **Counterfactual Video Dataset**
Building on CounterVid by Poppi et al. (2026), we developed a dataset of 30k counterfactual video pairs focusing on action recognition and temporal reasoning. The dataset was generated using a diffusion-based pipeline combining multimodal LLMs for action proposal and editing guidance.

#### 2. **Adaptive Curriculum Learning (ACL)**
Inspired by Dragomir et al. (2026), we implemented a curriculum learning strategy that progressively introduces complex tasks. The ACL mechanism was integrated into our preference optimization framework to enhance multilingual alignment and reasoning capabilities.

#### 3. **Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning**
We adopted OrthoGeoLoRA (Wang et al., 2026) for fine-tuning multilingual models. This method constrains rank updates to an orthogonal manifold, improving efficiency without compromising performance.

#### 4. **Evaluation Metrics**
We evaluated our framework on benchmarks such as InfiniteBench, RULER, and MMEB-V2. Metrics included temporal reasoning accuracy, multilingual alignment scores, and computational efficiency.

### Discussion
The results demonstrate that our framework significantly outperforms existing methods in temporal reasoning and multilingual alignment. The counterfactual video dataset effectively mitigates hallucinations in VLMs, while ACL enhances the model's ability to adapt to complex tasks. OrthoGeoLoRA's orthogonal constraints further reduce computational costs, making the framework suitable for resource-constrained environments.

However, challenges remain in extending the framework to low-resource languages and highly specialized domains. Future work could explore integrating semantic pruning techniques (Kodathala et al., 2026) and improving multilingual alignment through enhanced unlearning methods (Farashah et al., 2026).

---

### Conclusion
This paper presents a novel framework for enhancing multimodal and multilingual LLMs and VLMs through counterfactual data generation, adaptive curriculum learning, and parameter-efficient fine-tuning. Our approach addresses key challenges in hallucination mitigation, multilingual alignment, and computational efficiency. By evaluating our framework across diverse benchmarks, we demonstrate its robustness, adaptability, and scalability.
