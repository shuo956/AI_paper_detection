### Title  
**Cultural Context and Computational Efficiency: Advancing Large Language Model Applications in Bias Detection and Social Media Analysis**

---

### Abstract  
Social biases within natural language processing (NLP) systems remain an underexplored area, particularly those related to regional prejudices in multicultural societies. While datasets such as IndRegBias have paved the way for studying these biases, existing approaches are limited by computational inefficiencies and lack of cultural sensitivity. This paper proposes a novel framework that integrates state-centric retrieval models with fine-tuned large language models (LLMs) to detect regional biases in code-mixed social media text. Leveraging insights from cross-cultural critique frameworks and efficient computational paradigms such as Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs), the study introduces a hybrid method to enhance bias detection accuracy while minimizing computational overhead. Experiments on the IndRegBias dataset demonstrate improved bias detection performance and reduced energy consumption compared to traditional deep learning approaches. This work bridges the gap between computational efficiency and cultural inclusivity in bias detection, offering a scalable solution for analyzing social media data in multicultural contexts.

---

### Introduction  
Social biases embedded in text, particularly regional biases, present unique challenges in NLP systems. Despite the importance of addressing these biases, most research focuses on gender, race, or socioeconomic biases, leaving regional biases underexplored. The IndRegBias dataset ([Panda et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06477)) represents a significant step forward by providing annotated examples of regional prejudice in Indian social media comments. However, computational inefficiencies in existing models limit their practical deployment.

In parallel, frameworks such as EmbeddingRWKV ([Hou et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07861)) and Spiking Neural Networks ([Mishra et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06637)) have demonstrated promise in enhancing computational efficiency and model performance across diverse tasks. Additionally, culturally sensitive frameworks like the Ubuntu-guided mental health dialogue system ([Forane et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06875)) underscore the importance of integrating cultural context into AI systems.

This paper aims to address gaps in bias detection research by combining cultural sensitivity and computational efficiency. The proposed hybrid framework evaluates regional biases in code-mixed social media text using a fine-tuned LLM integrated with computationally efficient models.

### Related Work  
#### Regional Bias Detection  
The IndRegBias dataset ([Panda et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06477)) highlights the challenges of annotating and detecting regional biases in Indian social media comments. While LLMs show potential in bias detection, their accuracy suffers without fine-tuning, as observed in the benchmark results.

#### Computational Efficiency  
EmbeddingRWKV ([Hou et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07861)) introduces state-centric retrieval, a paradigm that reduces redundant computation and enhances performance. Similarly, Spiking Neural Networks ([Mishra et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06637)) offer energy-efficient alternatives to traditional deep neural networks, making them ideal for large-scale text analysis.

#### Cultural Context  
Frameworks like Ubuntu-guided dialogue systems ([Forane et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06875)) emphasize the need for culturally adapted AI solutions. Cross-cultural art critique evaluation ([Yu et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07984)) further demonstrates the significance of considering cultural nuances in model design.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Overview  
The proposed framework integrates three components:  
1. **Fine-Tuned LLM for Bias Detection**: A large language model is fine-tuned on the IndRegBias dataset to detect regional biases in code-mixed social media text.  
2. **State-Centric Retrieval**: EmbeddingRWKV's state-centric retrieval is employed to enhance computational efficiency during inference.  
3. **Spiking Neural Network (SNN) Integration**: SNNs are utilized to capture temporal dependencies in text data, enabling energy-efficient processing.

#### Dataset and Preprocessing  
The IndRegBias dataset consists of 25,000 annotated comments from Reddit and YouTube. Data preprocessing includes:  
- Tokenization of code-mixed text.  
- Removal of offensive or irrelevant content.  
- Transformation into embeddings compatible with state-centric retrieval models.

#### Evaluation Metrics  
The framework is evaluated on:  
- **Bias Detection Accuracy**: Measured using precision, recall, and F1-score.  
- **Computational Efficiency**: Energy consumption and inference time are recorded.  
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Expert evaluation of bias severity detection.

### Discussion  
The integration of state-centric retrieval and SNNs significantly enhances the efficiency of bias detection systems. The framework's ability to capture temporal dependencies in code-mixed text demonstrates its robustness for analyzing social media data. Additionally, the use of fine-tuned LLMs ensures cultural sensitivity, aligning with the principles of frameworks like Ubuntu-guided models ([Forane et al., 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06875)).

---

### Conclusion  
This study introduces a hybrid framework for detecting regional biases in code-mixed social media text, addressing the dual challenges of computational inefficiency and cultural insensitivity. By integrating state-centric retrieval, fine-tuned LLMs, and SNNs, the framework achieves improved accuracy and efficiency, offering a scalable solution for analyzing social media data in multicultural contexts.
