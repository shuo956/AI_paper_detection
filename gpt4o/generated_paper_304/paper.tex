### Title
**A Unified Framework for Optimizing High-Dimensional Systems: Bridging Mathematical Optimization, Machine Learning, and Real-World Applications**

---

### Abstract
Optimization problems, particularly those involving high-dimensional, complex systems, are widespread across fields such as machine learning, computational biology, and engineering. Despite advances in methodologies, challenges remain in addressing problems with non-convex landscapes, computationally expensive functions, or distributed data. Motivated by recent works in proximal algorithms, feature selection, and federated learning, this study proposes a unified optimization framework. This framework combines inexact proximal operators, clustering-based surrogate models, and federated learning paradigms to tackle problems in large-scale systems. Specifically, we integrate advances in block-decomposable methods, surrogate-based optimization (SBOC), and efficient federated learning protocols to design robust, scalable solutions. Experimental validation on synthetic and real-world datasets demonstrates significant improvements in convergence rates, accuracy, and computational efficiency compared to baseline methods. The proposed framework paves the way for scalable optimization in data-intensive applications and highlights the synergy between theory and practice.

### Related Work

#### 2.1 Proximal Operators and Trust-Region Methods
Inexact proximal operators have emerged as a powerful tool for addressing optimization problems with non-convex and nonsmooth terms (Maia et al., 2026). These methods extend the applicability of trust-region algorithms by accommodating inexact solutions, thereby enabling efficient optimization in high-dimensional spaces.

#### 2.2 Feature Selection and Surrogate Models
Feature selection and surrogate-based optimization have been pivotal in reducing computational complexity. Bakhmach et al. (2026) introduced ROOFS, a robust feature selection framework, while Ahmad & Karimi (2026) proposed SBOC, a clustering-based surrogate model for global optimization. Both approaches emphasize the importance of dimensionality reduction and efficient sampling strategies.

#### 2.3 Federated and Distributed Learning
Federated learning protocols have revolutionized distributed optimization by enabling collaborative learning without sharing raw data (Alsulaimawi, 2026; Sugawara et al., 2026). Recent advancements in single-round clustered federated learning highlight the potential for reducing communication overhead while maintaining accuracy.

#### 2.4 Applications in Machine Learning and Beyond
Applications of optimization span diverse fields, from biomarker discovery in clinical trials (Bakhmach et al., 2026) to density estimation using optimal transport (Hu & Tabak, 2026). The integration of geometric insights, as proposed by Croquevielle et al. (2026), further enhances the theoretical understanding of optimization landscapes.

### Methods/Experiment

#### 3.1 Framework Overview
The proposed framework integrates three key components:
1. **Inexact Proximal Operators**: Leveraging the δ-Fréchet subdifferential to handle non-convexity and nonsmoothness (Maia et al., 2026).
2. **Clustering-Based Surrogate Models**: Utilizing SBOC to efficiently explore high-dimensional spaces (Ahmad & Karimi, 2026).
3. **Federated Learning with Single-Round Communication**: Adopting DC-CFL for distributed optimization (Sugawara et al., 2026).
