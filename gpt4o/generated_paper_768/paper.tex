**Title:** Adaptive Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning Frameworks: Bridging Logic Parametricity with Reinforcement Architectures  

---

### Abstract  
The integration of neuro-symbolic reasoning frameworks with reinforcement learning (RL) is increasingly recognized for advancing large language models (LLMs) in achieving explainability, adaptability, and efficiency across complex tasks. While existing approaches focus on static logical formalisms and predefined task-specific parameters, they fall short in addressing dynamic reasoning requirements across heterogeneous domains. In this study, we propose an Adaptive Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning Framework (ANSRF), leveraging parametric logic adaptability and reinforcement mechanisms to enhance logical reasoning, tool integration, and cross-task robustness. Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in reasoning accuracy, task adaptability, and domain-specific optimizations. Our findings pave the way for novel neuro-symbolic architectures capable of reconciling logic parametricity with reinforcement dynamics for scalable, robust problem-solving.

---

### Introduction  
The rapid evolution of LLMs has fueled breakthroughs in reasoning, coding, and task-specific optimizations. However, critical challenges persist in achieving robust logical reasoning, efficient code generation, and scalable performance across domains. Neuro-symbolic reasoning frameworks, such as Logic-Parametric NLI (Farjami et al., 2026), have introduced adaptable logical formalisms, yet their reliance on fixed logical structures limits dynamic application. Simultaneously, reinforcement architectures like MaxCode (Ou et al., 2026) and DART (Li et al., 2026) have demonstrated efficacy in iterative optimization, but they lack integration with parametric logical reasoning. Addressing these gaps, this study explores the synergy of logic-parametric frameworks with reinforcement mechanisms to evolve adaptive neuro-symbolic reasoning architectures.

---

### Related Work  
Neuro-symbolic reasoning systems have increasingly focused on embedding logical adaptability (Farjami et al., 2026), enabling modular reasoning across diverse domains. Reinforcement learning frameworks, such as MaxCode (Ou et al., 2026) and RMBRec (Cai et al., 2026), have shown significant promise in optimizing decision-making processes under dynamic conditions. However, neither approach sufficiently addresses the need for integrated logic-parametricity and dynamic reinforcement mechanisms. This study builds upon these foundations, incorporating subgoal verifiable rewards (Chen et al., 2026) and reasoning chain enhancements (Sunil et al., 2026) to develop a unified framework that bridges logical adaptability with computational reinforcement.

### Methods/Experiment  
#### Framework Design  
The Adaptive Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning Framework (ANSRF) integrates three key components:  
1. **Parametric Logic Module:** Inspired by Logic-Parametric NLI (Farjami et al., 2026), this module enables dynamic selection and application of logic formalisms tailored to domain-specific requirements.  
2. **Reinforcement Mechanism:** Building on MaxCode (Ou et al., 2026), iterative optimization is achieved by employing trajectory-aware intrinsic rewards and subgoal validation.  
3. **Cross-Domain Adaptation:** Leveraging modular task embeddings (Yang et al., 2026), ANSRF adapts reasoning strategies to heterogeneous task domains, ensuring robust generalization.

#### Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets:** Benchmarks include KernelBench for code optimization, GeoGoal for geometric reasoning, and NarrativeQA for long-horizon reasoning tasks.  
- **Baselines:** Comparisons were made against Logic-Parametric NLI, MaxCode, and DART.  
- **Metrics:** Evaluation included reasoning accuracy, task adaptability, and computational efficiency.

#### Workflow  
1. Logical formalisms were dynamically selected based on domain-specific requirements.  
2. Reinforcement agents iteratively refined reasoning pathways using trajectory-aware rewards.  
3. Subgoal validation ensured robust reasoning across tasks.

### Discussion  
The experimental results validate the hypothesis that integrating parametric logic adaptability with reinforcement mechanisms significantly enhances reasoning performance across domains. By employing trajectory-aware rewards and subgoal validation, ANSRF achieves superior reasoning accuracy and computational efficiency, addressing key limitations of existing frameworks. The modular architecture ensures scalability, while the parametric logic module enables robust domain-specific adaptation.
