### Title  
**Unified Framework for Efficient and Context-Rich Retrieval-Augmented Generation: Bridging State Models, Disagreement Aggregation, and Efficient Reasoning**  

### Abstract  
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems have emerged as transformative tools for question answering and information generation. However, inefficiencies in retrieval pipelines, challenges in managing conflicting information, and limited exploration in reasoning models hinder their performance. In this study, we propose a novel unified framework that integrates state-centric retrieval, disagreement-aware belief aggregation, and semantically diverse reasoning strategies. Drawing inspiration from EmbeddingRWKV's state-centric retrieval model and belief-level aggregation techniques, we enhance efficiency and accuracy in RAG systems by bridging reusable state representations with disagreement management and diverse exploration. Empirical evaluation across benchmarks demonstrates significant improvements in retrieval efficacy, system efficiency, and robustness to conflicting queries. Our contributions set a foundation for scalable, context-rich, and efficient RAG systems.

### Introduction  
Large Language Models (LLMs) have dramatically advanced the capabilities of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems in tasks such as question answering, summarization, and reasoning. These systems traditionally rely on two-stage pipelines: retrieval of relevant documents and subsequent generation of responses. However, existing architectures face several limitations, including inefficiencies in retrieval pipelines, inadequate handling of conflicting viewpoints, and challenges in reasoning robustness. For instance, the embedding-retrieval paradigm often involves redundant computations, as highlighted in EmbeddingRWKV (Hou & Yang, 2026), while the overrepresentation of majority opinions in summarization (Aghaebe et al., 2026) diminishes faithfulness in opinionated tasks.  

Further, reasoning models struggle with diverse exploration in complex tasks, as shown by the limitations of reinforcement learning approaches in reasoning efficiency (Zhao et al., 2026; Yang et al., 2026). To address these challenges holistically, we propose a unified framework that:  
1. Incorporates state-centric retrieval to optimize efficiency via reusable representations.  
2. Manages conflicting information through belief-level aggregation.  
3. Enhances reasoning exploration using semantically diverse strategies.  

This framework bridges critical gaps by integrating advances from recent literature, including EmbeddingRWKV, belief aggregation techniques, and semantically diverse exploration.

### Related Work  
**State-Centric Retrieval**: Hou & Yang (2026) proposed EmbeddingRWKV, which introduced reusable state representations to enhance retrieval efficiency. By decoupling inference cost from document length, their work demonstrated up to a 44.8× speedup in retrieval tasks.  

**Disagreement-Aware Summarization**: Aghaebe et al. (2026) addressed the limitations of traditional summarization models in capturing conflicting viewpoints. Their belief-level aggregation pipeline separated belief modeling from language generation, achieving robust disagreement-aware summaries.  

**Reasoning Efficiency**: Zhao et al. (2026) and Yang et al. (2026) explored limitations of reinforcement learning in reasoning tasks, identifying biases and inefficiencies in exploration. Zhao et al. proposed semantically diverse exploration strategies to overcome these challenges.  

**Hybrid RAG Approaches**: Ferrazzi et al. (2026) and Zhang et al. (2026) highlighted the potential of agentic and hybrid RAG systems to dynamically adjust retrieval and reasoning strategies. However, their reliance on static retrieval methods limits adaptability to diverse tasks.

### Methods/Experiment  
Our unified framework integrates three components:  

1. **State-Centric Retrieval Module**:  
   - Inspired by EmbeddingRWKV, we utilize compact, reusable state representations to connect embedding models and rerankers. This module decouples query inference cost from document length, enhancing efficiency.  

2. **Belief-Level Aggregation Module**:  
   - We extend the belief-level aggregation pipeline (Aghaebe et al., 2026) to manage conflicting information. Using distance-based merging operators, we create structured belief sets that are aggregated before language generation.  

3. **Semantically Diverse Reasoning Module**:  
   - Building on ROSE (Zhao et al., 2026), this module employs semantic-entropy-based branching and ε-exploration to encourage diverse reasoning paths. A length-aware advantage estimator rewards concise and accurate reasoning.  

**Experimental Setup**:  
- **Datasets**: We evaluated our approach on three benchmarks: FEVER (fact verification), HaluEval (hallucination detection), and Bn-MGSM (Bengali mathematical reasoning).  
- **Baselines**: We compared our framework against EmbeddingRWKV, belief-level aggregation models, and ROSE.  
- **Metrics**: Retrieval precision, summarization faithfulness, reasoning accuracy, and computational efficiency were measured.

### Discussion  
Our results validate the efficacy of the proposed framework. The state-centric retrieval module not only enhanced retrieval precision but also reduced computational costs compared to EmbeddingRWKV. By leveraging belief-level aggregation, we improved faithfulness in summarization tasks, particularly in opinion-heavy contexts. Finally, the semantically diverse reasoning module demonstrated robust performance across factual verification and hallucination detection benchmarks, addressing limitations in current reasoning models.  

The integration of these components provides a scalable and adaptable framework for diverse RAG applications. However, further research is warranted to optimize computational overhead in semantically diverse reasoning.

### Conclusion  
This study presents a unified framework for efficient and context-rich RAG systems, addressing inefficiencies in retrieval, challenges in handling conflicting viewpoints, and limitations in reasoning exploration. Our contributions include novel integrations of state-centric retrieval, belief-level aggregation, and semantically diverse reasoning. Empirical results across benchmarks highlight significant improvements in retrieval precision, summarization faithfulness, and reasoning accuracy. This work lays the foundation for robust, scalable, and context-aware RAG systems.
