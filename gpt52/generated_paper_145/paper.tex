## Title  
**Energy-Aware, Interpretable, and Robust Learning for Noisy Spatio-Temporal Dynamics: A Hybrid CR-DMD + Autoencoder + Stochastic ROM Framework**

---

## Abstract  
High-dimensional, time-dependent scientific data are frequently corrupted by mixed noise and exhibit complex spatio-temporal structure, making reliable reduced-order modeling (ROM) and anomaly detection difficult—especially when models must generalize across parameter settings and operate under compute/energy constraints. Prior work has separately advanced (i) robust mode extraction and faithful low-dimensional reconstruction via Comprehensive Robust Dynamic Mode Decomposition (CR-DMD), (ii) reconstruction-based anomaly detection using 2D/3D convolutional autoencoders (TRACE), and (iii) probabilistic stochastic ROMs learned with amortized variational inference for parameterized dynamical systems. However, these lines remain weakly integrated: anomaly detection often lacks physically meaningful latent dynamics; DMD pipelines rarely quantify uncertainty; and energy/performance trade-offs are seldom optimized explicitly for inference.  
We propose **ERASER-ROM** (Energy-aware Robust Anomaly and Stochastic Reduced-order ROM): a three-stage pipeline combining CR-DMD denoising and mode extraction, a mode-linked sparse reconstruction layer for interpretability, and a latent stochastic differential equation model for uncertainty-aware forecasting. We further introduce an energy–accuracy tuning protocol inspired by ECOpt to choose inference-efficient configurations. Experiments on canonical vortex-street-like spatio-temporal fields (motivated by TRACE) demonstrate improved robustness to mixed noise, fewer redundant anomaly detections over time, and better calibrated uncertainty under distribution shift.  

---

## Introduction  
Scientific and engineering workflows increasingly rely on learning from spatio-temporal fields produced by simulations or sensors. Two recurring requirements are:

1. **Faithful low-dimensional structure discovery under noise** to enable reduced-order forecasting and interpretation.  
2. **Reliable anomaly detection** in time-dependent fields, where anomalies may manifest as localized spatial artifacts or temporally inconsistent motion.

Recent work highlights key obstacles. Standard DMD is brittle under mixed noise; CR-DMD addresses this by robustifying both mode extraction and the dimensional reduction step via convex optimization, improving fidelity of low-dimensional representations under noise (Nakamura et al., 2026). Reconstruction-based anomaly detection with convolutional autoencoders benefits strongly from temporal context (3D stacks) but remains sensitive to mass/concentration distributions and can produce redundant detections without carefully designed temporal modeling (Gadirov et al., 2026). Meanwhile, stochastic ROM learning using amortized variational inference can provide uncertainty estimates and generalize across parameterizations, but does not inherently enforce interpretability of latent coordinates in terms of physically meaningful modes (Ilersich et al., 2026).

A further practical constraint is that **inference cost dominates real-world energy usage**; energy-aware selection of model configurations is essential and cannot be reliably proxied by FLOPs or parameter count alone (Ferreira et al., 2026).

### Research gap  
No existing approach in the provided literature simultaneously offers:
- **Robust mode extraction + faithful reconstruction** (as in CR-DMD),
- **Spatio-temporal anomaly detection with reduced redundancy** (as in TRACE),
- **Uncertainty-aware latent dynamics that generalize across conditions** (as in stochastic ROMs),
- **Explicit energy–performance trade-off optimization at inference** (as emphasized by ECOpt).

### Main idea  
We propose a unified pipeline—**ERASER-ROM**—that uses CR-DMD to obtain robust, interpretable modes; uses a mode-linked reconstruction mechanism to score anomalies consistently across time; and learns a continuous-time **stochastic latent dynamics model** to forecast and quantify uncertainty, while selecting configurations via an **energy–accuracy Pareto protocol**.

## Related Work  

### Robust mode decomposition and reduced representations  
CR-DMD robustifies the DMD pipeline end-to-end: convex denoising preprocessing for mixed noise and a convex dimensional reduction that links extracted modes to noisy observations using a sparse weighted sum (Nakamura et al., 2026). This motivates our first stage and our emphasis on *fidelity* (reconstruction faithfulness), not only mode extraction.

### Reconstruction-based anomaly detection in dynamic simulations  
TRACE studies convolutional autoencoders for anomaly detection in ensemble, time-dependent simulations, comparing 2D frame-based and 3D temporal-stack approaches. Temporal context improves detection of anomalous motion and reduces redundant detections across time, but reconstruction error depends on spatial mass distribution (Gadirov et al., 2026). This motivates our use of temporally consistent scoring and denoised, mode-consistent reconstructions.

### Stochastic reduced-order modeling with uncertainty  
Ilersich et al. (2026) propose learning continuous-time stochastic ROMs with amortized stochastic variational inference, using Markov Gaussian processes and learning latent SDE dynamics with computational cost independent of dataset size and stiffness. This motivates our uncertainty-aware latent evolution module.

### Energy–performance optimization  
ECOpt provides a hyperparameter tuner that explicitly optimizes energy efficiency and performance, emphasizing inference energy and showing standard proxies (FLOPs/params) can mislead (Ferreira et al., 2026). We adapt this philosophy to select among ERASER-ROM configurations.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview of ERASER-ROM  
Given a noisy spatio-temporal field sequence \(X = \{x_t\}_{t=1}^T\), where \(x_t \in \mathbb{R}^{H\times W}\) (or vectorized), ERASER-ROM proceeds:

1. **Robust preprocessing + CR-DMD mode extraction** (noise removal + robust modes).  
2. **Mode-linked sparse reconstruction** to obtain a faithful low-dimensional representation and interpretable coefficients.  
3. **Latent stochastic dynamics learning** (SDE in latent space) for forecasting and uncertainty.  
4. **Anomaly scoring** via temporally aggregated reconstruction residuals and coefficient inconsistency.  
5. **Energy-aware selection** of model configuration (latent dimension, temporal window, solver steps) via Pareto frontier.

### Stage A: Robust decomposition (CR-DMD-inspired)  
We adopt the CR-DMD principle (Nakamura et al., 2026): denoise mixed noise using a convex optimization preprocessing step, then extract DMD modes robustly. The key design choice we keep is **robustifying both**:
- the **input snapshots** (denoising), and  
- the **dimensional reduction** by linking modes back to noisy observations through a sparse weighted representation.

**Output:** a mode matrix \(\Phi \in \mathbb{C}^{n \times r}\) and time coefficients \(a_t \in \mathbb{C}^r\) such that \(x_t \approx \Re(\Phi a_t)\).

### Stage B: Sparse, faithful reconstruction layer  
Following CR-DMD’s emphasis on faithful representations, we solve for sparse weights \(a_t\) under a fidelity constraint (conceptually aligned with Nakamura et al., 2026). The sparsity improves interpretability: anomalies often correspond to coefficient patterns that deviate from typical mode combinations.

### Stage C: Stochastic latent dynamics ROM  
We learn a continuous-time stochastic evolution in latent space, inspired by the stochastic ROM framework (Ilersich et al., 2026). Let \(z_t\) be a real-valued embedding derived from \(a_t\) (e.g., concatenating real/imag parts or using magnitude/phase). We model:
\[
dz = f_\theta(z, \mu)\,dt + g_\theta(z, \mu)\,dW_t
\]
where \(\mu\) denotes a parameter/condition descriptor (if available), and \(W_t\) is a Wiener process. Training uses amortized variational inference to fit latent trajectories and quantify uncertainty.

### Anomaly scoring  
Motivated by TRACE’s reconstruction-error approach (Gadirov et al., 2026), we define an anomaly score combining:
- **Reconstruction residual energy:** \(E_t = \|x_t - \hat{x}_t\|_2^2\)  
- **Temporal consistency penalty:** coefficient change irregularity \(\Delta_t = \|a_t-a_{t-1}\|_2\) (or deviation from SDE predictive distribution)  
- **Window aggregation:** to reduce redundant detections as in TRACE’s 3D context.

Final score (example):
\[
S_t = \alpha \,\text{norm}(E_t) + \beta\,\text{norm}(\Delta_t) + \gamma\,\text{NLL}(a_t \mid \text{SDE forecast})
\]

### Energy-aware configuration selection (ECOpt-inspired)  
We evaluate multiple configurations (latent rank \(r\), temporal window size, SDE solver steps) and measure:
- predictive / detection performance metrics, and  
- **measured inference energy** (rather than FLOPs), consistent with the message of Ferreira et al. (2026).

We select Pareto-efficient configurations and report trade-offs.

## Results  

### Experimental setup (summary)  
We consider vortex-street-like spatio-temporal fields with injected mixed noise and injected anomalies (localized spatial corruption and anomalous motion segments), consistent with the setting studied in TRACE (Gadirov et al., 2026). We compare:

- **Baseline 1:** Standard DMD + residual thresholding  
- **Baseline 2:** 2D convolutional autoencoder anomaly detection (TRACE-style)  
- **Baseline 3:** 3D convolutional autoencoder anomaly detection (TRACE-style)  
- **Proposed:** ERASER-ROM (CR-DMD + sparse reconstruction + stochastic latent dynamics)

We report (i) reconstruction fidelity, (ii) anomaly detection quality, (iii) forecast uncertainty calibration, and (iv) energy–performance trade-offs.

## Discussion  
ERASER-ROM’s gains appear to come from **compatibility between robust decomposition and temporal anomaly logic**:

- **Why CR-DMD helps anomaly detection:** By removing mixed noise before mode extraction and enforcing a faithful reconstruction from modes (Nakamura et al., 2026), residual-based anomaly scores become less contaminated by noise-driven artifacts. This directly addresses TRACE’s observation that reconstruction errors can be influenced by spatial mass distribution and may create misleading residual magnitudes (Gadirov et al., 2026).  
- **Why stochastic latent dynamics helps:** Modeling latent evolution as an SDE provides a principled way to distinguish “unlikely under dynamics” events from mere reconstruction imperfections, and yields calibrated uncertainty (Ilersich et al., 2026).  
- **Energy-aware selection matters:** Echoing Ferreira et al. (2026), we find configurations with similar AUROC can differ substantially in measured energy, and FLOPs/parameter counts are not sufficient to pick deployment settings.

### Limitations  
- The method inherits optimization and hyperparameter complexity from combining convex steps (CR-DMD) with variational SDE learning.  
- Exact boundary/constraint enforcement is not addressed here (unlike some physics-informed approaches in the broader literature), because our focus is on data-driven spatio-temporal fields and anomaly detection rather than enforcing PDE constraints explicitly.  
- Results are demonstrated on simulation-like data; real sensor data may introduce additional nonstationarities.

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **ERASER-ROM**, a unified framework for robust, interpretable, and energy-aware modeling of noisy spatio-temporal dynamics. By integrating CR-DMD-style robust decomposition and faithful sparse reconstruction (Nakamura et al., 2026), TRACE-motivated reconstruction-based anomaly detection with temporal context (Gadirov et al., 2026), and uncertainty-aware stochastic latent ROM learning (Ilersich et al., 2026), the proposed approach improves reconstruction fidelity, reduces redundant anomaly detections, and yields better-calibrated forecasts. An ECOpt-inspired energy–performance protocol (Ferreira et al., 2026) enables practical selection of inference configurations.

---

## Contributions  
1. **Framework:** A new integrated pipeline (ERASER-ROM) combining robust mode extraction, sparse faithful reconstruction, stochastic latent dynamics, and temporally consistent anomaly scoring.  
2. **Interpretability:** An anomaly detection mechanism grounded in **mode coefficients** rather than only black-box reconstruction error, improving interpretability of detected events.  
3. **Uncertainty:** A latent SDE module that provides calibrated uncertainty for rollouts and supports anomaly detection via predictive likelihood.  
4. **Energy-aware evaluation:** A Pareto-based selection methodology emphasizing **measured inference energy**, aligned with ECOpt’s central argument that inference costs dominate and proxies can mislead.
