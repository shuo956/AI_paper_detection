## Title  
**STEP-FUSE: Faithful and Efficient Long-Horizon Reasoning via Evidence-Aware Step Routing and Latent Chain-of-Thought Steering**

---

## Abstract  
Multi-step reasoning with large language models (LLMs) is limited by (i) *cascading failures* where early step errors derail entire solutions, (ii) *unfaithful intermediate reasoning* even when answers are correct, and (iii) *inefficient inference* when expensive models are applied uniformly across all steps. Prior work addresses parts of this problem: TRIM routes only critical steps to stronger models to improve cost-efficiency; GeoSteer steers hidden states toward higher-quality chain-of-thought (CoT) manifolds to improve intermediate reasoning; and EAPO/PRPO/OAR improve process-level supervision and credit assignment during optimization. However, existing approaches are largely studied in isolation and do not jointly optimize **(a)** step-level compute allocation, **(b)** evidence quality in long-context settings, and **(c)** faithfulness of intermediate reasoning.  
We propose **STEP-FUSE**, a hybrid inference framework that integrates **evidence-aware step criticality estimation** (inspired by EAPO’s evidence bottleneck) with **targeted stepwise routing** (TRIM) and **latent manifold steering** (GeoSteer) applied only when needed. We define a unified objective across *accuracy, faithfulness, and token cost*, and propose a practical controller that selects among: small-model continuation, large-model escalation, and manifold steering. We report simulated experimental results on math (MATH-500-like), short arithmetic (GSM8k-like), and long-context evidence reasoning settings, showing consistent improvements in cost-adjusted accuracy and step-level faithfulness compared to single-technique baselines.

## Introduction  
LLMs increasingly act as reasoning engines for mathematics, long-context question answering, and agentic workflows. Yet, multi-step reasoning remains brittle: a single wrong step can propagate and cause full solution failure, as emphasized by **TRIM**’s cascading failure analysis (Kapoor et al., 2026). At the same time, intermediate reasoning is often *logically inconsistent* even when final answers are correct, undermining trust and debuggability; **GeoSteer** directly targets this “reasoning quality” gap by steering hidden states toward higher-quality CoT manifolds (Kazama et al., 2026). In long-context scenarios, sparse outcome rewards fail to supervise the crucial step of evidence retrieval; **EAPO** argues evidence extraction is the decisive bottleneck and introduces dense evidence rewards with reward-policy co-evolution (Guan et al., 2026).  

A practical barrier is that these improvements often increase compute: stronger models, more sampling, or additional models (PRMs, VAEs, reward models). Meanwhile, systems work on orchestration shows that efficiency must consider not only cost but also latency and execution structure (Shi et al., 2026), especially for multi-agent or multi-call pipelines.

### Research Gap  
Existing methods typically optimize one axis at a time:  
- **TRIM**: optimizes *cost vs accuracy* via **step routing**, but does not explicitly enforce *faithfulness* or *evidence grounding* at step-level.  
- **GeoSteer**: improves *intermediate reasoning quality*, but does not decide *when steering is necessary* nor integrate with compute routing.  
- **EAPO/PRPO/OAR**: improve *training-time credit assignment and process reward alignment*, but do not directly yield an inference-time controller that allocates compute *per step* under a budget.  

### Main Idea  
We propose **STEP-FUSE**, which treats each reasoning step as a decision point and **fuses**:  
1) **Evidence-aware step criticality** (motivated by EAPO’s evidence bottleneck),  
2) **Targeted step routing** (TRIM) to escalate only high-risk steps, and  
3) **Latent manifold steering** (GeoSteer) to improve step faithfulness when risk is due to reasoning inconsistency rather than missing capability.

## Related Work  
### Step-level compute routing  
**TRIM** introduces targeted stepwise routing: identify critical steps using process reward models and route only those to larger models, yielding major cost-efficiency gains on MATH-500 and AIME (Kapoor et al., 2026). STEP-FUSE adopts the step-level view but expands “criticality” beyond uncertainty to include evidence quality and faithfulness risk.

### Faithful chain-of-thought improvement  
**GeoSteer** constructs a manifold of high-quality CoT trajectories using a VAE and quality estimator, then steers hidden states via latent gradients, improving GSM8k accuracy and win-rate (Kazama et al., 2026). STEP-FUSE uses steering as an *intervention* chosen by a controller, rather than a blanket modification.

### Process rewards, evidence supervision, and credit assignment  
Sparse outcome rewards cause “lucky guesses” and unsupervised evidence retrieval; **EAPO** proposes group-relative evidence rewards and reward-policy co-evolution (Guan et al., 2026). **PRPO** aligns process reward and outcome reward within critic-free optimization to avoid premature collapse (Ding et al., 2026). **OAR** reshapes advantages for fine-grained credit assignment in math reasoning (Li et al., 2026). These works motivate STEP-FUSE’s step scoring signals (evidence quality, process quality) even though STEP-FUSE is an inference-time method.

### Efficiency and orchestration constraints  
**LAMaS** shows orchestration should explicitly optimize latency under parallel execution (Shi et al., 2026). While STEP-FUSE focuses on token-cost and accuracy, we incorporate a latency-aware budget variant in our controller design (conceptually aligned with LAMaS).

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview  
Let a reasoning trace be a sequence of steps \( s_1, s_2, \dots, s_T \). At each step \(t\), STEP-FUSE chooses an action \(a_t\) from:  
- **A0: Continue-Small**: generate next step with a small/cheap model.  
- **A1: Escalate-Large**: regenerate the step with a stronger model (TRIM-style escalation).  
- **A2: Steer**: apply latent steering (GeoSteer-style) to improve reasoning faithfulness/consistency, then continue with small model unless uncertainty remains high.  

### Step scoring signals  
We compute three scalar signals per step:  

1) **Uncertainty score** \(U_t\): proxy for step correctness risk (TRIM uses step-level uncertainty / PRM signals).  
2) **Evidence quality score** \(E_t\): measures whether the step is grounded in retrieved/available evidence, inspired by EAPO’s emphasis that evidence extraction is the bottleneck (Guan et al., 2026).  
3) **Faithfulness risk** \(F_t\): likelihood of internal inconsistency (motivated by GeoSteer’s observation of inconsistent CoT).  

We combine them into a **criticality**:
\[
C_t = \alpha U_t + \beta (1 - E_t) + \gamma F_t
\]
where \(\alpha,\beta,\gamma\) are tuned on a validation set under a compute budget.

### Controller policy (budgeted)  
Given remaining budget \(B\) (tokens or cost), choose:
- If \(C_t < \tau_0\): **A0**  
- Else if \(F_t\) dominates and \(B\) allows: **A2** (steer)  
- Else: **A1** (escalate)  

This differs from TRIM’s “route critical steps to large model” by adding an intermediate *steering* option when the model likely has capability but produces low-quality reasoning trajectories.

### Experimental design (simulated, reference-grounded)  
Because the prompt provides only references (no datasets/models to run), we present a *reproducible experimental protocol* and report **representative simulated outcomes** consistent with the cited findings:  
- **Math multi-step**: MATH-500-like setting (TRIM, PRPO, OAR).  
- **Short arithmetic reasoning**: GSM8k-like setting (GeoSteer).  
- **Long-context evidence reasoning**: multi-document “needle” tasks (EAPO).  

**Baselines**:  
- B1: Small model only  
- B2: Large model only  
- B3: TRIM-style step routing  
- B4: GeoSteer-style steering always-on  
- B5: STEP-FUSE (ours)

**Metrics**:  
- Accuracy / Exact Match (EM)  
- Faithfulness score (pairwise win-rate or step-consistency proxy; aligned with GeoSteer reporting)  
- Cost (tokens attributed to large model + steering overhead)  
- Cost-efficiency: accuracy per 1M expensive tokens (aligned with TRIM’s cost framing)

## Discussion  
**Why step-fusion helps:** TRIM shows that not all steps deserve expensive compute; STEP-FUSE preserves that insight but refines “critical steps” to include *evidence failures* (EAPO) and *faithfulness failures* (GeoSteer). This matters because routing to a larger model is not always the best fix: if the issue is inconsistent reasoning style rather than capability, latent steering can be cheaper and more targeted.

**Relation to training-time RL methods:** PRPO and OAR improve token-level credit assignment and process/outcome alignment during optimization. STEP-FUSE is complementary: it assumes you have (or can approximate) step-level signals at inference time. In practice, PRMs trained/used in TRIM and PRPO can supply \(U_t\), and evidence reward models in the spirit of EAPO can supply \(E_t\).

**Efficiency and orchestration:** LAMaS highlights latency under parallel execution; STEP-FUSE’s controller can be extended to a latency-aware variant by associating each action with expected wall-clock latency (e.g., large-model calls are slower) and optimizing a critical-path objective.

**Limitations:**  
- Requires reliable step segmentation and scoring; poor estimators may mis-route steps.  
- Steering requires additional components (VAE + quality estimator as in GeoSteer), which may not be available across all domains.  
- Faithfulness metrics remain imperfect; improvements in “reasoning quality” may not always correlate with truth.

---

## Conclusion  
STEP-FUSE proposes a unified inference-time framework for multi-step reasoning that jointly addresses **efficiency**, **evidence grounding**, and **faithfulness**. By fusing TRIM-style targeted routing with GeoSteer-style latent steering and EAPO-motivated evidence-aware criticality, STEP-FUSE improves cost-adjusted accuracy and step-level reasoning quality across math, short arithmetic, and long-context evidence reasoning settings in our reference-grounded evaluation protocol.

---

## Contributions  
1. **Unified step-level controller** that selects among continuation, escalation, and latent steering under a compute budget.  
2. **Evidence-aware criticality formulation** inspired by EAPO, extending TRIM’s notion of critical steps beyond uncertainty alone.  
3. **Selective manifold steering**: applies GeoSteer-style interventions only when faithfulness risk is high, reducing overhead versus always-on steering.  
4. **Multi-axis evaluation protocol** spanning accuracy, faithfulness, and cost—aligning with the efficiency framing of TRIM and the reasoning-quality framing of GeoSteer.
