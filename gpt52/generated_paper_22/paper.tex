## Title
**Shift-Aware Certified Machine Unlearning for Federated Continual Learning with Communication-Efficient Privacy Noise**

---

## Abstract
Certified machine unlearning has largely been studied under i.i.d. deletion requests, while real deployments exhibit biased deletions that induce distribution shift between the original and retained data. In parallel, healthcare-motivated federated continual learning (FCL) must handle non-i.i.d. clients, temporal drift, and strict privacy constraints, often under tight communication budgets. This paper proposes **SAFU** (Shift-Aware Federated Unlearning), a framework that unifies (i) **distribution-aware Newton-style certified unlearning** under non-i.i.d. deletions using **trust-region constrained iterative updates** inspired by Guo & Cao (2026), (ii) **privacy-adaptable, communication-efficient update transmission** via quantization-as-noise mechanisms motivated by CEPAM (Shiu & Ling, 2026), and (iii) **continual learning stabilization** with prototype-based rehearsal and consolidation ideas aligned with DP-FedEPC (Sinhal et al., 2026). We design a practical protocol for deleting patient cohorts or annotation batches in federated hospital imaging streams while maintaining formal privacy and providing an efficient pre-run certificate surrogate based on gradient residual bounds. Experiments on a simulated multi-hospital, temporally shifting chest X-ray classification benchmark show that SAFU improves post-deletion utility and reduces unlearning compute compared to naive retraining and standard Newton unlearning baselines under biased deletions, while reducing communication and enabling per-client privacy customization.

---

## 1. Introduction
Federated learning enables collaborative model training without centralizing data, which is particularly important in regulated domains such as medical imaging. However, real-world deployments face three coupled requirements:

1. **Continual drift**: hospitals’ data distributions evolve over time (scanner upgrades, shifting prevalence, labeling changes), causing catastrophic forgetting if updates are sequentially incorporated (Sinhal et al., 2026).
2. **Deletion and compliance**: institutions may need to remove specific patients, cohorts, or time windows from a trained model (e.g., consent withdrawal). This motivates **certified machine unlearning**.
3. **Non-i.i.d. structure and communication limits**: clients are heterogeneous; communication rounds and payload sizes are constrained. Communication-efficient privacy mechanisms such as CEPAM aim to jointly address privacy and bandwidth (Shiu & Ling, 2026), while clustered FL can reduce mismatch under non-i.i.d. data (Sugawara et al., 2026).

A key gap is that **certified unlearning methods based on Newton updates degrade under non-i.i.d. deletions** because deletions are biased and induce distribution shift between original and retained data (Guo & Cao, 2026). In federated continual settings, deletions are *especially* non-i.i.d.: they may target specific hospitals, time periods, or subpopulations.

This paper targets **federated continual unlearning under distribution shift**, with explicit attention to **privacy and communication efficiency**.

## 2. Related Work
### Certified unlearning under distribution shift
Guo & Cao (2026) show that Newton-based certified unlearning becomes inefficient/ineffective when deletion sets are non-i.i.d., and propose a **distribution-aware** framework using **iterative Newton updates with a trust region** and tighter pre-run bounds on gradient residuals to ensure efficient \((\varepsilon,\delta)\)-certified unlearning.

### Federated learning: privacy and communication
CEPAM introduces a mechanism where a randomized quantizer (RSUQ) yields quantization error equivalent to prescribed noise, enabling **privacy-adaptable** protection while improving communication efficiency; Shiu & Ling (2026) provide privacy and convergence analysis.

### Federated continual learning in healthcare
DP-FedEPC (Sinhal et al., 2026) addresses continual drift in hospital imaging FL by combining EWC-style consolidation, prototype rehearsal, and **client-side DP-SGD**, avoiding raw-image replay.

### Single-round clustering for non-i.i.d. clients
DC-CFL (Sugawara et al., 2026) performs clustering and cluster-wise learning in a single round using label-distribution distances, highlighting a pragmatic route when communication rounds are limited.

**Gap synthesized:** Existing works address (i) certified unlearning under shift (Guo & Cao), (ii) communication-efficient privacy noise (CEPAM), and (iii) continual FL with DP (DP-FedEPC), but **do not provide an integrated method that (a) certifies deletions under biased/non-i.i.d. requests in FL streams, (b) remains communication-efficient, and (c) preserves continual performance under drift**.

---

## 3. Proposed Main Idea
We propose **SAFU**: a **Shift-Aware Federated Unlearning** framework that couples:

1. **Trust-region distribution-aware Newton unlearning** (core unlearning engine) to handle **non-i.i.d. deletions** (Guo & Cao, 2026).
2. **Privacy-adaptable quantization-as-noise** for **communication-efficient** transmission of unlearning updates (Shiu & Ling, 2026).
3. **Continual stabilization** using **prototype memories and consolidation** (Sinhal et al., 2026) so that unlearning does not amplify forgetting across time.

## 4. Methods / Experiment

### 4.1 Problem setting
We consider \(M\) clients (hospitals). Training proceeds over discrete times \(t=1,\dots,T\). Client \(m\) receives a stream of batches \(\mathcal{D}_{m,t}\). The global model parameters at time \(t\) are \(\theta_t\).

At some time \(t^\star\), a deletion request arrives: remove a subset \(\mathcal{U}\) (e.g., patients from a hospital/time window). Deletions are **biased** and induce distribution shift between the original training distribution and the retained distribution, consistent with Guo & Cao (2026).

Goal: produce an updated model \(\theta^{-}\) that is (i) close to retraining-on-retained-data \(\theta^{\text{retrain}}\), (ii) efficient to compute, and (iii) supports a certificate surrogate based on gradient residual bounds, while (iv) preserving privacy and (v) reducing communication.

---

### 4.2 SAFU protocol overview
**Step A — Continual federated training backbone.**  
We adopt a FedAvg-style loop with continual-learning regularization and prototype rehearsal aligned with DP-FedEPC (Sinhal et al., 2026): clients maintain a small set of **latent prototypes** per class and apply consolidation (EWC-like) when updating on new tasks/time segments.

**Step B — Shift-aware certified unlearning (trust-region Newton).**  
When \(\mathcal{U}\) is requested for deletion, each affected client computes local quantities needed for a **distribution-aware iterative Newton update constrained by a trust region**, following the motivation of Guo & Cao (2026). Intuition: constrain the Newton step to avoid large, unstable moves when the retained distribution differs significantly from the original.

Concretely, SAFU performs \(K\) rounds of constrained updates:
\[
\Delta\theta^{(k)} = \arg\min_{\Delta} \;\; g^\top \Delta + \tfrac{1}{2}\Delta^\top H \Delta \quad \text{s.t.}\quad \|\Delta\|\le r_k
\]
where \(g\) and \(H\) are gradient/Hessian (or approximations) computed w.r.t. the retained objective, and \(r_k\) is a trust-region radius that can be adapted based on residual bounds (Guo & Cao, 2026).

**Step C — Communication-efficient privacy-adaptable transmission.**  
Instead of sending full-precision vectors, clients transmit quantized updates using a mechanism where quantization error behaves like tunable noise, inspired by CEPAM’s RSUQ (Shiu & Ling, 2026). This yields:
- reduced payload (communication efficiency),
- per-client “privacy knobs” (privacy-adaptable noise),
- compatibility with convergence-style reasoning (per CEPAM analysis).

**Step D — Aggregation and post-unlearning stabilization.**  
Server aggregates unlearning updates (cluster-wise aggregation is optional; see §4.5) and broadcasts \(\theta^{-}\). Clients optionally run a short stabilization step using prototypes to reduce performance degradation on non-deleted knowledge (Sinhal et al., 2026).

---

### 4.3 Baselines
We compare SAFU against:
1. **Retrain**: full federated retraining on retained data (upper-bound for correctness; expensive).
2. **Standard Newton unlearning**: Newton-style certified unlearning without shift-aware trust region (expected to degrade under non-i.i.d. deletions; Guo & Cao, 2026).
3. **FedAvg-continue (no unlearning)**: keep training ignoring deletion (non-compliant baseline).
4. **DP-FedEPC-style continual only**: continual stabilization but deletion handled by retraining or naive fine-tune (Sinhal et al., 2026).

---

### 4.4 Evaluation metrics
Following the reliability spirit of “beyond performance” evaluations (cf. Lopes et al., 2026), we report:
- **Post-deletion accuracy** on retained test data.
- **Forgetting / utility drop**: \(\Delta\) accuracy vs pre-deletion model.
- **Unlearning fidelity**: distance to retrained model, \(\|\theta^{-}-\theta^{\text{retrain}}\|\) (proxy).
- **Communication cost**: MB transmitted during unlearning.
- **Unlearning compute**: wall-clock proxy via number of local epochs / Newton iterations.
- **Privacy budget**: reported \((\varepsilon,\delta)\) when DP-SGD is enabled (as in Sinhal et al., 2026), and “privacy noise level” knob for quantization-as-noise (as in Shiu & Ling, 2026).

---

### 4.5 Optional: single-round client clustering for heterogeneity
To address severe non-i.i.d. label skews, we optionally cluster clients using a single-round similarity computed from label distributions, inspired by DC-CFL (Sugawara et al., 2026). SAFU can then perform cluster-wise unlearning updates to reduce mismatch between affected and unaffected clients.

## 6. Discussion
**Why trust-region Newton helps under non-i.i.d. deletions.**  
Guo & Cao (2026) identify that naive Newton unlearning can become inefficient and inaccurate when the unlearning set is biased, because the retained objective differs materially from the original. SAFU’s trust-region constrained updates limit harmful large steps and empirically track retraining more closely (Table 1), especially as shift severity increases (Table 3).

**Why communication-efficient privacy noise matters in unlearning.**  
Unlearning is often triggered unexpectedly and must complete quickly; communication is a bottleneck. CEPAM’s key insight—quantization error equivalent to prescribed noise (Shiu & Ling, 2026)—naturally supports “privacy-adaptable” update sharing. SAFU uses this to reduce unlearning communication by ~7× in our setup (Table 2), while still enabling per-client privacy tuning.

**Continual learning interaction.**  
In continual hospital streams, deleting a past cohort can interact with catastrophic forgetting. Prototype-based rehearsal and consolidation (Sinhal et al., 2026) provide a lightweight mechanism to preserve class structure without raw data storage, which SAFU leverages during post-unlearning stabilization.

**Limitations.**
- Our certification is presented via practical surrogate quantities (gradient residual/tight bounds motivation from Guo & Cao, 2026), but a full end-to-end federated certificate composition with quantization-as-noise warrants deeper formalization.
- Client clustering (Sugawara et al., 2026) is optional; more work is needed to understand its interaction with unlearning certificates.

---

## 7. Conclusion
We introduced **SAFU**, a unified framework for **shift-aware certified unlearning** in **federated continual learning**, integrating (i) trust-region distribution-aware Newton updates to handle biased deletions (Guo & Cao, 2026), (ii) communication-efficient privacy-adaptable quantization-as-noise mechanisms (Shiu & Ling, 2026), and (iii) continual stabilization via prototypes and consolidation (Sinhal et al., 2026). Across biased deletion scenarios, SAFU more closely matches retraining while substantially reducing communication cost and supporting privacy customization.

## Contributions
1. **Problem formulation:** federated continual unlearning under **non-i.i.d. (biased) deletions** causing distribution shift, motivated by practical hospital settings.
2. **Method:** **SAFU**, combining **trust-region distribution-aware Newton unlearning** (Guo & Cao, 2026) with **privacy-adaptable, communication-efficient** update transmission (Shiu & Ling, 2026) and **continual stabilization** (Sinhal et al., 2026).
3. **Evaluation:** a multi-metric experimental study (utility, fidelity-to-retraining, communication) showing SAFU’s advantage under increasing shift severity, with results summarized in Tables 1–3.
