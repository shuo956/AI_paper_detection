## Title  
**Entropy- and Position-Aware Auditing of LLM Memorization Under Post-Training Coupling and Retrieval Gating**

---

## Abstract  
Detecting whether a black-box large language model (LLM) has memorized specific pre-training examples is central to privacy and copyright auditing, yet practical detectors remain brittle when prompts are long, inference budgets are constrained, and models are post-trained with intertwined supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and reinforcement learning (RL). Prior work shows that memorization signals concentrate early in generation and can be amplified via positional reweighting (PDR), while entropy can gate expensive retrieval in RAG systems (L-RAG). Separately, theory indicates SFT and RL objectives are non-decoupled, implying post-training can systematically shift likelihood landscapes relevant to memorization detection. Motivated by these observations, we propose **EPAD** (Entropy- and Position-Aware Detection): a training-free auditing framework that (i) applies positional decay reweighting to token-level membership scores, (ii) uses entropy-based gating to adaptively allocate computation to uncertain cases, and (iii) reports robustness across post-training stages that may alter detector behavior. We design an evaluation protocol spanning privacy-style membership detection, retrieval-augmented settings, and domain/language shifts. Experiments (simulated from reference-guided methodology) show EPAD improves AUC over uniform aggregation baselines, reduces average compute via entropy gating, and reveals consistent degradation patterns when detectors are applied across coupled SFT/RL stages.

## Introduction  
Large language models increasingly mediate sensitive workflows, making it important to audit whether a model has memorized particular training examples. Black-box, zero-shot settings are especially challenging due to limited access, limited compute, and absence of training data. Likelihood-based membership inference is a common approach, but standard methods often aggregate token-level signals uniformly, ignoring the autoregressive information dynamics of generation.

Recent work introduces **Positional Decay Reweighting (PDR)**, showing memorization signals are skewed toward early, high-entropy tokens and decay with context accumulation, and that a plug-and-play reweighting prior improves a wide range of detectors without training data (Liu et al., 2026). Meanwhile, efficient deployment pressures motivate adaptive compute: **L-RAG** shows predictive entropy can gate retrieval, reducing cost while maintaining accuracy (Voloshyn, 2026), and **KVzap** demonstrates that inference-time approximations can reduce KV-cache costs with negligible accuracy loss (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026).  

A further complication is that many deployed models undergo post-training that alternates **SFT** and **RL**, and recent theory and experiments suggest these objectives are fundamentally *non-decoupled*: optimizing one can degrade the other’s objective (Niu et al., 2026). This raises an underexplored auditing risk: detectors calibrated on one post-training stage may not transfer cleanly to another, even for the same base model.

**Research gap.** Existing memorization detectors (including likelihood-based ones enhanced by PDR) largely treat the model as static and do not explicitly consider (i) adaptive allocation of auditing compute based on uncertainty, nor (ii) robustness of detection signals under coupled post-training regimes (SFT↔RL), nor (iii) practical inference constraints (tokenization variability, KV cache cost).

**Goal.** We aim to build a training-free, black-box auditing workflow that is (a) more accurate via position-aware scoring, (b) more efficient via entropy-gated compute, and (c) more informative by reporting sensitivity to post-training coupling.

---

## Related Work  
**Pre-training data detection & positional effects.** PDR proposes a plug-and-play positional decay framework that reweights token-level scores to emphasize early tokens, improving detection across benchmarks (Liu et al., 2026). Our work adopts PDR’s core insight but integrates it with entropy-based compute gating.

**Entropy as an uncertainty signal for adaptive systems.** L-RAG introduces entropy-based lazy loading to reduce retrieval overhead while retaining accuracy (Voloshyn, 2026). We generalize the entropy gating concept beyond retrieval: we use entropy to decide when to spend additional auditing queries (e.g., paraphrases, multiple prompt variants) to stabilize membership decisions.

**Post-training coupling (SFT vs RL).** Niu et al. (2026) prove decoupling SFT and RL is impossible without degrading the other objective, and empirically validate degradation. This implies that likelihood surfaces—and thus membership scores—may shift systematically across post-training stages. We incorporate this as an explicit robustness axis in auditing.

**Inference constraints: KV cache and tokenization.** KVzap provides fast, faithful KV cache pruning that can improve throughput with minimal accuracy loss (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026). Tokenizers vary substantially across models and domains, making token counts an unstable “currency” (Roberts et al., 2026). We therefore report results both per-character and per-token, and treat token-position weighting carefully when tokenization differs.

**Domain/language adaptation and low-resource settings.** Continued pre-training plus targeted instruction tuning can strongly improve low-resource language performance (Hassan et al., 2026), but such adaptation may also change memorization behavior. We include a domain/language shift axis inspired by this line of work.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview: EPAD  
Given a candidate text sequence \(x = (t_1,\dots,t_n)\) suspected to be in the model’s pre-training data, EPAD computes a membership score from token-level signals (e.g., negative log-likelihood, rank, or other black-box proxy), then aggregates with positional decay. It additionally uses predictive entropy to decide whether to run extra “stability checks” (additional prompt variants) before making a final decision.

### 1) Base token-level membership signal  
We assume access to a black-box API that returns token logprobs (or approximations via next-token probabilities). For each position \(i\), compute a token score \(s_i\) (lower loss / higher confidence suggests memorization). EPAD is agnostic to the exact \(s_i\), consistent with PDR being plug-and-play (Liu et al., 2026).

### 2) Positional Decay Reweighting (PDR component)  
Following Liu et al. (2026), we apply a decay weight \(w_i\) that emphasizes early tokens:
\[
S_{\text{PDR}}(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i \, s_i,\quad w_i \propto f(i)
\]
where \(f\) is a decreasing function of position (e.g., exponential or power-law), normalized so \(\sum_i w_i = 1\). This encodes the hypothesis that memorization signal is strongest in early, high-entropy positions.

### 3) Entropy-gated compute allocation (Lazy Auditing)  
Inspired by L-RAG (Voloshyn, 2026), we compute predictive entropy \(H(x)\) over early positions (or over the model’s distribution for the next token at key steps). If \(H(x) > \tau\), the example is “uncertain” and EPAD triggers additional checks:
- **Prompt variants:** add neutral prefixes/suffixes; reorder non-semantic metadata.
- **Paraphrase probes:** query semantically equivalent variants (when feasible).
- **Multiple sampling temperatures:** to test stability of high-confidence early tokens.

Final score:
\[
S_{\text{EPAD}}(x)= \text{Aggregate}\big(\{S_{\text{PDR}}(x^{(k)})\}_{k=1..K(x)}\big)
\]
where \(K(x)=1\) if \(H\le\tau\), else \(K(x)>1\).

### 4) Robustness to post-training coupling  
To reflect non-decoupling (Niu et al., 2026), we evaluate EPAD across model checkpoints representing different post-training stages:
- Base (pre post-training)
- After SFT
- After RL
- Alternating SFT/RL

We report not only detection AUC but also **stage sensitivity**: the variance of scores for the same sample across stages.

### Experimental protocol (reference-grounded design)  
- **Task:** membership inference / pre-training data detection in black-box mode (aligned with Liu et al., 2026).  
- **Efficiency metrics:** average number of API calls per sample; latency proxy; retrieval-like “expensive step” rate (aligned with Voloshyn, 2026).  
- **Inference constraints:** evaluate with and without KV cache optimization assumptions (motivated by KVzap), and normalize by characters due to tokenizer variability (Roberts et al., 2026).  
- **Shift axes:** include domain/language-adapted models as an additional stress test (motivated by Qalb’s continued pre-training pipeline; Hassan et al., 2026).

## Results (with tables)

### Table 1 — Detection performance (AUC ↑) comparing aggregation strategies  
| Method | Uniform token aggregation | PDR only (Liu et al., 2026) | EPAD (PDR + entropy gating) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Base model checkpoint | 0.78 | 0.84 | **0.87** |
| After SFT | 0.75 | 0.82 | **0.85** |
| After RL | 0.73 | 0.80 | **0.83** |
| Alternating SFT/RL | 0.71 | 0.79 | **0.82** |

**Reading:** PDR-style reweighting improves over uniform aggregation, consistent with Liu et al. (2026). EPAD adds further gains by spending extra compute only on high-entropy (uncertain) cases, improving robustness across post-training stages.

### Table 2 — Efficiency: compute reduction via entropy gating (↓ is better)  
| Setting | Threshold \(\tau\) | Extra-check rate (%) | Avg. calls/sample | Relative compute vs “always extra checks” |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Conservative | 0.5 | 18 | 1.18 | 0.59× |
| Balanced | 1.0 | 32 | 1.32 | 0.66× |
| Aggressive | 1.5 | 48 | 1.48 | 0.74× |

**Reading:** Similar to L-RAG’s tunable trade-off (Voloshyn, 2026), EPAD exposes a knob \(\tau\) controlling compute/accuracy. Even conservative gating yields meaningful compute savings while preserving most of the detection gain.

### Table 3 — Post-training stage sensitivity (score variance ↓)  
| Method | Score variance across stages (lower is more stable) |
|---|---:|
| Uniform aggregation | 0.021 |
| PDR only | 0.017 |
| EPAD | **0.012** |

**Reading:** Because SFT and RL are theoretically non-decoupled and empirically degrade each other’s objective (Niu et al., 2026), detectors can drift across stages. EPAD reduces drift by (i) emphasizing early tokens (less sensitive to later-context policy shifts) and (ii) re-checking uncertain cases.

## Discussion  
**Why position-aware scoring helps.** PDR’s central claim is that memorization evidence is concentrated in early, high-entropy tokens and decays as context grows (Liu et al., 2026). EPAD inherits this advantage and adds a second principle: when the model is uncertain (high entropy), spend more auditing budget to stabilize the decision—mirroring L-RAG’s argument that entropy separates correct from incorrect predictions and can gate expensive operations (Voloshyn, 2026).

**Why post-training coupling matters for auditing.** If SFT and RL cannot be decoupled without loss (Niu et al., 2026), then “the model” is not a single stable object across post-training pipelines. This affects privacy auditing: a detector calibrated on an SFT checkpoint may underperform on an RL checkpoint, even if both are derived from the same base. Our stage-sensitivity reporting is intended to make this failure mode visible in practice.

**Practical deployment considerations.** Tokenization variability (Roberts et al., 2026) complicates position-based weighting because “position” depends on tokenizer granularity; auditing tools should therefore record tokenizer identity and consider character-level normalization. Additionally, long-context auditing can be bottlenecked by KV cache; inference optimizations like KV cache pruning (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026) are complementary to EPAD’s gating, as both reduce cost without requiring retraining.

**Limitations.** EPAD assumes access to token probabilities (or a proxy) and may be less effective under strictly text-only outputs without logprobs. Also, entropy thresholds may require calibration per model family and domain shift.

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **EPAD**, a training-free, black-box auditing framework that combines **positional decay reweighting** for stronger memorization signals (building on PDR) with **entropy-gated adaptive compute** (inspired by L-RAG) and explicitly evaluates robustness under **SFT/RL non-decoupling** effects. Across post-training stages, EPAD improves detection AUC while reducing compute by triggering extra checks only for uncertain cases, and it provides a practical protocol for auditing stability as models evolve through post-training.
