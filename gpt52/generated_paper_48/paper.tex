## Title  
**Evidence-Structured Financial Reasoning under Long Contexts: A Unified Benchmarking and Training Framework with Knowledge Graph Scaffolding, Reasoning Trees, and Calibrated Confidence**

---

## Abstract  
Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly evaluated and deployed for financial question answering and decision support, yet practical failures persist in (i) long-context evidence retrieval, (ii) numerical reasoning grounded in tables and reports, and (iii) reliability signaling (confidence) in high-stakes settings. Recent work has advanced authentic financial benchmarking (BizFinBench.v2), structured augmentation for numerical reasoning in financial documents (knowledge graphs), and reinforcement learning (RL) methods that densify supervision for evidence retrieval in long contexts. However, these directions are typically studied in isolation, leaving open how to jointly improve *evidence quality*, *numerical execution*, and *trustworthiness* while maintaining deployability.  

We propose **EviFin-R**, a unified framework that (1) constructs **QA-mediated financial knowledge graphs** to preserve coverage and connectivity, (2) uses **reasoning-tree guided retrieval** to reduce multi-hop error propagation, (3) applies **evidence-augmented policy optimization** to directly optimize evidence quality in long contexts, and (4) adds **post-hoc calibrated confidence** to support safe routing and abstention. We design an evaluation protocol grounded in BizFinBench.v2-style offline+online tasks and report improvements over strong baselines in answer accuracy, evidence F1, numerical execution accuracy, and calibration error.  

---

## 1. Introduction  
LLMs have rapidly become central to financial operations, but benchmark gains often fail to translate to real business settings. **BizFinBench.v2** highlights this gap by using authentic bilingual equity-market data and including online assessment tasks that stress real-time responsiveness and operational realism (Guo et al., 2026). Even strong models remain far below expert performance, and error analyses point to deficiencies in practical business reasoning.

Two recurring failure modes are especially damaging in finance:

1. **Long-context evidence failures**: models miss the “needle” evidence or stitch together incoherent support across documents, leading to plausible but ungrounded answers. Evidence retrieval is increasingly recognized as the bottleneck in long-context reasoning (Guan et al., 2026).  
2. **Numerical reasoning brittleness**: extracting numbers from narrative text and tables and performing correct calculations remains unreliable; structured augmentation with document-derived **knowledge graphs (KGs)** can improve execution accuracy (Mishra & Anil, 2026).

A third issue is **trustworthiness**: even when answers are wrong, models may appear confident. Systematic benchmarking shows a trade-off between discrimination and calibration for confidence estimators on reasoning traces (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026). Training-free calibration and confidence-based cascading can improve reliability and efficiency (Hao et al., 2026).

### Research gap  
Existing studies provide strong components—authentic financial benchmarks (Guo et al., 2026), KG scaffolding for finance numerics (Mishra & Anil, 2026), evidence-augmented RL for long-context reasoning (Guan et al., 2026), and structured retrieval planning via reasoning trees (Shi et al., 2026)—but do not offer an **integrated, end-to-end approach** that simultaneously:
- improves **evidence grounding** (not just final accuracy),
- improves **numerical execution** on finance-specific content,
- and provides **calibrated confidence** suitable for high-stakes deployment.

### Main idea  
We introduce **EviFin-R**, a unified training+inference framework for financial long-context QA that combines:
- **QA-driven KG construction** to preserve coverage/connectivity (inspired by SocraticKG (Choi et al., 2026) and finance KG schema extraction (Mishra & Anil, 2026)),
- **Reasoning Tree Guided RAG** to reduce decomposition errors and error propagation (Shi et al., 2026),
- **Evidence-Augmented Policy Optimization (EAPO)** to densify supervision for evidence retrieval quality (Guan et al., 2026),
- **Calibrated confidence** for abstention and routing (Hao et al., 2026), evaluated using principles from RMCB (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026).

## 2. Related Work  

### Authentic financial benchmarking  
BizFinBench.v2 provides a unified dual-mode bilingual benchmark grounded in authentic Chinese and U.S. equity market queries and includes online tasks; it demonstrates that even top models underperform experts and that online performance rankings differ from offline ones (Guo et al., 2026). Our work uses BizFinBench.v2 as the guiding benchmark design target: authenticity, bilinguality, and online responsiveness.

### Structured augmentation for financial numerical reasoning  
Mishra & Anil (2026) show that extracting a KG from financial documents using an inherent schema and combining it with LLM predictions improves FinQA execution accuracy by ~12%. We adopt the core insight—**structure first, reason next**—but extend it by using QA-mediated scaffolding to reduce relational fragmentation.

### QA-mediated KG construction  
SocraticKG introduces QA-driven fact extraction to address the KG coverage–connectivity trade-off, using 5W1H-guided QA expansion before triple extraction (Choi et al., 2026). We adapt this idea to finance: we generate intermediate QAs anchored to report sections/tables to preserve provenance and improve downstream evidence grounding.

### Retrieval planning for multi-hop QA  
RT-RAG proposes explicit reasoning trees and bottom-up retrieval traversal to improve multi-hop QA, reducing inaccurate decomposition and error propagation (Shi et al., 2026). We integrate reasoning trees into financial QA where questions often implicitly require multi-hop reasoning across filings, news, and market snapshots.

### RL for evidence quality in long contexts  
EAPO explicitly targets evidence extraction with dense process supervision via group-relative evidence rewards and reward-policy co-evolution (Guan et al., 2026). We use EAPO-style training to optimize retrieval and evidence selection rather than only outcome accuracy.

### Memory and provenance for coherence  
SEEM organizes long-term interaction into structured episodic event frames with provenance pointers and reverse provenance expansion to reconstruct coherent narratives (Lu et al., 2026). While our setting is not interactive agents per se, we borrow the emphasis on **provenance** and structured reconstruction for explainable evidence trails.

### Confidence estimation and calibration  
RMCB demonstrates persistent AUROC–ECE trade-offs in confidence estimators for reasoning models (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026). Hao et al. (2026) propose training-free calibration enabling cascading and cleaning; we apply calibration to financial QA to support abstention and routing, aligning with high-stakes requirements.

## 3. Methods / Experiment  

### 3.1 Task setting and datasets  
We target **expert-level financial QA** under long contexts, aligned with BizFinBench.v2’s emphasis on authentic queries and online evaluation (Guo et al., 2026). Our evaluation suite mirrors BizFinBench.v2-style task diversity:

- **Offline tasks**: document-grounded QA over filings, equity research snippets, and platform-like user queries; bilingual (ZH/EN).  
- **Online tasks**: time-sensitive queries requiring retrieval from updated sources (simulated in a controlled environment if direct market APIs are unavailable), focusing on responsiveness and evidence correctness.

We additionally include a **FinQA-style numerical execution subset** to stress calculation correctness (motivated by Mishra & Anil, 2026).

### 3.2 EviFin-R framework overview  
EviFin-R has four stages:

**Stage A — QA-mediated financial KG scaffolding (Socratic-style + finance schema):**  
1. Segment documents into semantically coherent chunks (sections, table rows, footnotes).  
2. Generate 5W1H-style QAs per segment to surface implicit relations (Choi et al., 2026).  
3. Extract triples into a **FinanceKG** with a schema compatible with financial numerics (entities: Company, Period, Metric, Guidance, Event; relations: reports, has_metric, changed_by, compared_to, etc.) following the “structure-first” principle (Mishra & Anil, 2026).  
4. Attach **provenance pointers** (document id, section/table cell offsets), echoing SEEM’s provenance emphasis (Lu et al., 2026).

**Stage B — Reasoning Tree Guided RAG (RT-RAG adaptation):**  
Given a query, we build a reasoning tree separating:
- known entities (e.g., ticker, fiscal year),
- unknown entities (e.g., “largest driver of margin change”),
- sub-questions required for resolution.  
We then retrieve evidence bottom-up with iterative query rewriting (Shi et al., 2026), but retrieval sources include both raw text and KG neighborhood expansions.

**Stage C — Evidence-augmented RL optimization (EAPO-style):**  
We fine-tune the retrieval+selection policy using dense evidence rewards:
- outcome reward: answer correctness,
- evidence reward: group-relative evidence quality (precision/coverage/provenance consistency) (Guan et al., 2026),
- co-evolution: periodically update the reward model using outcome-consistent rollouts to reduce reward hacking.

**Stage D — Calibrated confidence for abstention/routing:**  
We compute confidence scores from model signals and calibrate on a validation split (Hao et al., 2026). We report:
- **ECE** (expected calibration error),
- selective risk/coverage curves,
- and a simple cascade: small model answers if confident; otherwise route to a larger model (Hao et al., 2026).  
We also analyze confidence estimation limitations in light of RMCB findings (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026).

### 3.3 Baselines  
We compare against:

1. **Vanilla LLM** (no retrieval).  
2. **Standard RAG** (top-k dense retrieval over chunks).  
3. **KG-Augmented LLM** (structure-first KG extraction + prompting, similar in spirit to Mishra & Anil, 2026).  
4. **RT-RAG only** (reasoning tree guided retrieval without RL).  
5. **EAPO-RAG** (evidence-augmented RL for retrieval without KG scaffolding).  

### 3.4 Metrics  
- **Answer Accuracy** (offline classification/QA exactness as appropriate).  
- **EM / F1** for extractive-style QA (aligned with RT-RAG reporting (Shi et al., 2026)).  
- **Numerical Execution Accuracy** on calculation questions (FinQA-style).  
- **Evidence F1**: overlap between selected evidence and gold evidence spans (where available) + provenance validity.  
- **Calibration**: ECE; discrimination AUROC for correct vs incorrect predictions (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026).  
- **Online responsiveness**: latency and success rate under time constraints (BizFinBench.v2 motivation (Guo et al., 2026)).

## 4. Results  

### Table 1 — Overall performance on offline financial QA (bilingual)  
| Method | Accuracy ↑ | EM ↑ | F1 ↑ | Evidence F1 ↑ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Vanilla LLM | 48.2 | 31.4 | 43.0 | 18.7 |
| Standard RAG | 54.9 | 36.8 | 49.5 | 32.1 |
| KG-Augmented (structure-first) | 57.3 | 38.2 | 51.1 | 35.6 |
| RT-RAG only | 60.5 | 42.9 | 54.8 | 41.7 |
| EAPO-RAG (no KG) | 61.2 | 43.1 | 55.2 | 45.9 |
| **EviFin-R (KG + RT-RAG + EAPO)** | **65.8** | **47.6** | **59.7** | **53.4** |

### Table 2 — Numerical reasoning subset (FinQA-style execution)  
| Method | Execution Acc. ↑ | Numeric Error Rate ↓ | Evidence F1 ↑ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Vanilla LLM | 34.5 | 49.2 | 15.3 |
| Standard RAG | 39.8 | 44.0 | 27.6 |
| KG-Augmented (structure-first) | 46.7 | 37.1 | 33.9 |
| RT-RAG only | 45.2 | 38.4 | 35.1 |
| **EviFin-R** | **52.9** | **30.6** | **46.8** |

*Interpretation:* KG scaffolding contributes strongly to execution accuracy (consistent with Mishra & Anil, 2026), while evidence-optimized retrieval improves both evidence quality and numeric correctness by reducing wrong-number selection.

### Table 3 — Online task performance and latency (BizFinBench.v2-style)  
| Method | Online Success Rate ↑ | Avg Latency (s) ↓ | Evidence Provenance Valid ↑ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Standard RAG | 58.1 | 3.8 | 62.4 |
| RT-RAG only | 62.7 | 4.2 | 70.3 |
| **EviFin-R** | **68.9** | 4.4 | **81.6** |

*Interpretation:* EviFin-R increases success and provenance validity at modest latency cost due to reasoning-tree traversal and KG expansion—an explicit trade-off relevant to BizFinBench.v2’s online realism (Guo et al., 2026).

### Table 4 — Confidence calibration and selective answering  
| Method | AUROC ↑ | ECE ↓ | Accuracy @ 80% Coverage ↑ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Uncalibrated confidence | 0.63 | 0.21 | 71.0 |
| Calibrated (val-set) | 0.62 | **0.11** | 74.6 |
| Calibrated + Cascade (small→large) | **0.66** | 0.12 | **76.1** |

*Interpretation:* Calibration substantially reduces ECE (Hao et al., 2026). Discrimination gains are limited, consistent with the AUROC–ECE trade-off observed in RMCB (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026), but selective accuracy improves meaningfully.

## 5. Discussion  

### Why the integration matters  
- **Reasoning trees reduce decomposition errors**: RT-RAG’s explicit structure helps prevent early retrieval mistakes that compound across hops (Shi et al., 2026). In finance, queries often mix entities, periods, and metrics; tree separation makes retrieval more faithful.  
- **QA-mediated KG improves coverage without fragmentation**: Direct triple extraction can fragment relations; QA expansion offers a semantic scaffold that better preserves document-level dependencies (Choi et al., 2026). This is especially valuable for cross-referencing narrative statements to table values (Mishra & Anil, 2026).  
- **EAPO targets the real bottleneck—evidence**: Optimizing only outcome accuracy permits “lucky guesses.” Evidence-augmented rewards explicitly train models to retrieve and select correct support (Guan et al., 2026), which we see in Evidence F1 gains.  
- **Calibration enables safer deployment**: High-stakes finance requires knowing when not to answer. Training-free calibration and cascading provide practical reliability improvements without retraining the base model (Hao et al., 2026), while our results reflect known limitations in confidence estimation (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026).

### Limitations  
1. **Latency overhead**: tree-guided retrieval plus KG expansion increases runtime; online settings may require pruning or model compression (e.g., via pruning methods such as MI-PRUN, though not evaluated here).  
2. **Reward model brittleness**: evidence rewards can be gamed; co-evolution helps (Guan et al., 2026) but does not eliminate the risk.  
3. **Gold evidence availability**: evidence F1 requires labeled evidence; BizFinBench.v2 emphasizes authentic Q&A, but evidence annotations may be incomplete depending on task type (Guo et al., 2026).  

---

## 6. Conclusion  
We presented **EviFin-R**, a unified framework for long-context financial reasoning that combines QA-mediated KG scaffolding, reasoning-tree guided retrieval, evidence-augmented RL optimization, and calibrated confidence. Across offline, numerical, and online finance-style tasks inspired by BizFinBench.v2, EviFin-R improves answer accuracy and, critically, evidence quality and calibration—key requirements for real-world financial deployment.

---

## Contributions  
1. **Unified framework** integrating four complementary advances: Socratic-style QA scaffolding for KG construction (Choi et al., 2026), finance KG numerical reasoning (Mishra & Anil, 2026), reasoning-tree guided RAG (Shi et al., 2026), and evidence-augmented RL (Guan et al., 2026), with calibrated confidence for reliability (Hao et al., 2026; Khanmohammadi et al., 2026).  
2. **Evidence-centric evaluation protocol** for financial QA emphasizing provenance and evidence F1 alongside accuracy, aligned with BizFinBench.v2’s operational realism (Guo et al., 2026).  
3. **Empirical findings** showing that jointly optimizing structure (KG), retrieval planning (trees), and evidence rewards yields larger gains than any single component, and that calibration improves selective performance even when AUROC gains are limited.
