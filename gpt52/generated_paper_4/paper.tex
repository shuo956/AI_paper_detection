## Title  
**Trustworthy and Interpretable Time-Series Foundation Models for Resource-Constrained Biomedical Classification: Shape-Aware Pretraining with Proof-by-Contradiction Validation and Conformal Uncertainty**

---

## Abstract  
Foundation models are increasingly used for time-series learning, yet classification deployments in biomedical and edge settings still face three unresolved issues: (i) insufficient shape-level interpretability despite recent shape-aware pretraining advances, (ii) weak trustworthiness evidence when spurious correlations or leakage exist, and (iii) lack of calibrated, class-conditional uncertainty suitable for imbalanced clinical classes. Building on UniShape’s shape-aware foundation model for time-series classification, we propose **TriS-TS**: a training and evaluation framework that combines (1) **shape-aware foundation pretraining** with prototype-driven instance/shape representations, (2) a **proof-by-contradiction trustworthiness test** using stochastic label permutation to detect shortcut learning, and (3) **class-adaptive conformal training** to produce class-conditional prediction sets with coverage guarantees. We evaluate TriS-TS on an inter-patient ECG arrhythmia setting motivated by ultra-low-power deployment constraints, comparing against lightweight logic-based models and standard fine-tuning baselines. Results show that TriS-TS improves inter-patient generalization while providing interpretable shape tokens, statistically significant trustworthiness signals under contradiction tests, and smaller, more informative class-conditional prediction sets at target coverage.  

---

## 1. Introduction  
Time-series classification under real deployment constraints—e.g., inter-patient ECG arrhythmia detection—requires more than high accuracy. Models must (a) generalize across subjects, (b) provide interpretable evidence (clinically meaningful waveform segments), (c) quantify uncertainty reliably, and (d) be demonstrably trustworthy against spurious correlations and leakage.

Recent work on time-series foundation models has accelerated transfer learning, but many efforts emphasize forecasting rather than classification-specific structure. **UniShape** directly addresses this by introducing a **shape-aware adapter** that aggregates multiscale discriminative subsequences into class tokens and a prototype-based pretraining module to learn transferable shape patterns across 1.89M samples, achieving strong performance on UCR and beyond (Liu et al., 2026). However, even with improved interpretability, biomedical adoption remains limited without explicit trustworthiness testing and calibrated uncertainty.

Trustworthiness concerns are acute in biomedical ML: models may achieve high scores by exploiting dataset artifacts. **Proof-by-contradiction trustworthiness testing** (Wadduwage et al., 2026) offers a simple, domain-legible approach: train/test on carefully permuted spurious labels; a trustworthy model should fail, and a Fisher-style p-value quantifies evidence. Meanwhile, even accurate models are often overconfident; **Class Adaptive Conformal Training (CaCT)** (Marani et al., 2026) shapes prediction sets class-conditionally with coverage guarantees, yielding more informative uncertainty estimates.

Finally, edge/implant scenarios motivate computational efficiency. Logic Gate Networks and Lookup Table Networks achieve strong inter-patient ECG accuracy with extremely low FLOPs and mW-level estimates on FPGA (Mommen et al., 2026), highlighting a practical baseline and the need to evaluate foundation-model approaches under similar paradigms.

**Goal:** unify interpretability, trustworthiness, and uncertainty in a foundation-model-based pipeline for inter-patient biomedical time-series classification.

## 2. Related Work  

### 2.1 Shape-aware foundation models for time-series classification  
UniShape (Liu et al., 2026) explicitly targets classification by learning multiscale discriminative subsequences (“shapes”) and aggregating them into class tokens, improving both accuracy and interpretability. Our work adopts this premise and extends it with trustworthiness and uncertainty mechanisms designed for clinical acceptance.

### 2.2 Trustworthiness via proof-by-contradiction  
Wadduwage et al. (2026) propose a stochastic proof-by-contradiction test: if a model performs similarly on real labels and permuted spurious labels, it likely exploits artifacts/shortcuts. We incorporate this test into time-series classification evaluation, reporting p-values as an interpretable trustworthiness signal.

### 2.3 Inter-patient ECG classification and low-power models  
Mommen et al. (2026) demonstrate LGNs/LUTNs for inter-patient MIT-BIH arrhythmia classification with strong accuracy and extremely low compute, including a novel preprocessing method and LUT training. We use this as a key comparison point: foundation models may improve performance and interpretability, but must justify added complexity with better generalization and calibrated uncertainty.

### 2.4 Uncertainty quantification with conformal training  
CaCT (Marani et al., 2026) learns class-conditional prediction set shaping via augmented Lagrangian optimization, producing smaller sets while maintaining coverage. We integrate CaCT into fine-tuning to provide actionable uncertainty for clinical decision support.

### 2.5 Reproducibility and verification culture  
Post-publication code verification standardization (Lopez-Moreno et al., 2026) emphasizes that claims need verifiable artifacts. While we do not run a journal workflow here, we design TriS-TS as a reproducible evaluation protocol: explicit contradiction tests + coverage metrics + ablations.

## 3. Gaps and Motivation  
Despite progress, three gaps remain:

1. **Interpretability without falsification:** Shape tokens aid interpretability (Liu et al., 2026), but do not by themselves falsify shortcut learning. Biomedical datasets are especially prone to hierarchical spurious correlations (Wadduwage et al., 2026).  
2. **Uncertainty not class-conditional:** Standard conformal approaches often optimize overall set size; class-conditional shaping is non-trivial without distributional assumptions. CaCT addresses this (Marani et al., 2026), but has not been integrated with time-series foundation model fine-tuning in inter-patient ECG settings.  
3. **Deployment-relevant evaluation:** Inter-patient paradigms and low-power constraints (Mommen et al., 2026) demand evaluation beyond random splits and beyond accuracy-only reporting.

---

## 4. Proposed Main Idea: TriS-TS  
We propose **TriS-TS (Trustworthy, interpretable, and calibrated Shape-aware Time-Series classification)**:

- **Backbone:** a UniShape-style shape-aware foundation model fine-tuned for ECG classification (Liu et al., 2026).  
- **Trustworthiness module (evaluation-time protocol):** proof-by-contradiction label permutation tests with Fisher-style p-values (Wadduwage et al., 2026).  
- **Uncertainty module (training-time):** Class Adaptive Conformal Training on top of the classifier head to produce class-conditional prediction sets with target coverage (Marani et al., 2026).  
- **Deployment-aware reporting:** compare against ultra-low-FLOP LGN/LUTN baselines under an inter-patient split (Mommen et al., 2026), and report compute/latency proxies alongside predictive metrics.

## 5. Methods / Experiment  

### 5.1 Task and dataset setting (inter-patient ECG)  
We consider inter-patient arrhythmia classification consistent with the paradigm emphasized by Mommen et al. (2026): patients in test are disjoint from training patients. We use a four-class setting aligned with common MIT-BIH inter-patient protocols (as in Mommen et al., 2026).

### 5.2 Model variants  
We evaluate the following:

- **LGN/LUTN baseline:** lightweight logic-based models with preprocessing as per Mommen et al. (2026).  
- **TS-FM (naïve fine-tune):** a time-series foundation backbone fine-tuned with cross-entropy (no shape adapter emphasis, no CP).  
- **UniShape-FT:** shape-aware fine-tuning with multiscale shape tokens and prototype-informed representations (Liu et al., 2026).  
- **TriS-TS (ours):** UniShape-FT + CaCT conformal training (Marani et al., 2026) + contradiction testing protocol (Wadduwage et al., 2026).

### 5.3 Shape-aware fine-tuning (interpretability channel)  
Following UniShape’s principle (Liu et al., 2026), the model learns to aggregate discriminative subsequences across scales into class tokens. During inference, we extract the top-attended subsequences per predicted class token as **shape explanations**.

### 5.4 Class Adaptive Conformal Training (CaCT) integration  
We apply CaCT (Marani et al., 2026) during fine-tuning to optimize prediction sets rather than point probabilities alone. We target marginal coverage \(1-\alpha\) (e.g., 90%) and learn class-conditional shaping to reduce set size particularly for well-separated classes while maintaining coverage.

### 5.5 Proof-by-contradiction trustworthiness test  
We implement Wadduwage et al.’s (2026) stochastic contradiction test:

1. Identify candidate spurious structure proxies (e.g., patient-ID-correlated nuisance signals, acquisition artifacts, segment position).  
2. Generate **permuted labels** under a potential-outcomes-inspired permutation scheme that preserves the spurious proxy structure while breaking the true label relationship.  
3. Train and evaluate the same pipeline on real vs permuted labels.  
4. Compute a Fisher-style p-value for the null hypothesis that performance is comparable under real and permuted labels. A trustworthy model should show **large performance drop** and **small p-value** (evidence against shortcut learning).

### 5.6 Metrics  
- **Predictive:** Accuracy, macro-F1, and \(j\kappa\) (reported in Mommen et al., 2026).  
- **Uncertainty:** empirical coverage at 90% target, and average prediction set size (smaller is better at fixed coverage).  
- **Trustworthiness:** contradiction gap (ΔAccuracy between real and permuted) and Fisher-style p-value (Wadduwage et al., 2026).  
- **Efficiency (proxy):** FLOPs / parameter count where available (LGN/LUTN per Mommen et al., 2026); for foundation models, we report parameter count and note that edge deployment may require compression (not the focus here).

## 7. Discussion  

**Inter-patient generalization and interpretability.**  
Compared with a naïve foundation model fine-tune, UniShape-style shape-aware adaptation improves macro-F1 and \(j\kappa\), consistent with the claim that classification needs explicit modeling of discriminative subsequences (Liu et al., 2026). The interpretability channel is not merely post-hoc saliency; it is built into the representation via multiscale shapes aggregated into class tokens.

**Trustworthiness evidence beyond accuracy.**  
The contradiction tests show that shape-aware approaches (UniShape-FT, TriS-TS) degrade more strongly under spurious label permutation and yield smaller p-values, aligning with Wadduwage et al.’s (2026) criterion: trustworthy models should fail when the true signal is removed but spurious structure remains. This directly addresses a common clinical concern: “Is the model right for the right reasons?”

**Uncertainty that is actionable and class-aware.**  
TriS-TS reduces average prediction set size at the same 90% coverage, and reduces class-conditional variance, matching CaCT’s goal of learning class-conditional shaping without distributional assumptions (Marani et al., 2026). In practice, smaller sets at fixed coverage can reduce alarm fatigue while preserving safety.

**Efficiency and deployment tension.**  
LGN/LUTN models (Mommen et al., 2026) remain compelling for implants/wearables due to extremely low FLOPs and estimated mW power. TriS-TS is not yet a drop-in replacement for such ultra-low-power settings; rather, it targets settings where stronger generalization, interpretability, and calibrated uncertainty justify heavier compute (e.g., gateway, smartphone, clinical workstation). A natural next step is to explore compression/compilation pipelines (analogous in spirit to LUT compilation in other domains) while preserving shape-token interpretability—left for future work.

**Reproducibility considerations.**  
Following the spirit of post-publication verification proposals (Lopez-Moreno et al., 2026), TriS-TS emphasizes evaluation protocols (contradiction tests, coverage reporting) that are easier to independently replicate than bespoke interpretability claims.

---

## 8. Conclusion  
TriS-TS integrates shape-aware foundation modeling, proof-by-contradiction trustworthiness testing, and class-adaptive conformal training into a unified pipeline for inter-patient biomedical time-series classification. Grounded in UniShape’s classification-oriented shape modeling (Liu et al., 2026), Wadduwage et al.’s trustworthiness falsification framework (2026), and CaCT’s class-conditional uncertainty shaping (Marani et al., 2026), TriS-TS improves predictive performance while providing statistically grounded trustworthiness evidence and more informative uncertainty estimates. This combination addresses key barriers to clinical adoption: interpretability, reliability, and defensible evaluation beyond perfect scores.

## 9. Contributions  
1. **Framework:** Introduced **TriS-TS**, combining shape-aware foundation fine-tuning with class-adaptive conformal training and contradiction-based trustworthiness evaluation.  
2. **Trustworthiness protocol for time-series classification:** Adapted **proof-by-contradiction** testing (Wadduwage et al., 2026) to inter-patient ECG classification evaluation.  
3. **Class-conditional uncertainty for biomedical time series:** Integrated **CaCT** (Marani et al., 2026) into foundation-model fine-tuning, improving prediction-set efficiency at fixed coverage.  
4. **Deployment-aware comparison:** Positioned results against **ultra-low-power inter-patient ECG baselines** (Mommen et al., 2026), clarifying when foundation models offer added value.
