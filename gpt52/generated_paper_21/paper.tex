## Title  
**Persona-Stable Private Knowledge Injection for Multilingual Clinical Dialogue Assistants: A Generation-Augmented, Claim-Verified, Translation-Mediated Framework**

---

## Abstract  
Deploying large language models (LLMs) in clinical dialogue requires (i) private, fast-updating knowledge integration, (ii) multilingual support in low-resource settings, (iii) claim-level faithfulness, and (iv) stable, safe assistant behavior under emotionally vulnerable conversations and adversarial role attacks. Prior work addresses these needs in isolation: Generation-Augmented Generation (GAG) injects private knowledge without prompt bloat; translation pipelines with distilled models enable efficient multilingual health dialogue processing; MedRAGChecker verifies biomedical RAG outputs at the claim level; and the Assistant Axis characterizes and stabilizes persona drift, while role-consistency benchmarks expose failures under adversarial prompts. We identify an unmet need for an integrated system that jointly optimizes private knowledge injection, multilingual processing, claim-level verification, and persona stability.  

We propose **PS-GAG**: a modular framework combining (1) plug-and-play private knowledge injection via GAG-style representation interfaces, (2) translation-mediated multilingual dialogue processing, (3) claim-level verification inspired by MedRAGChecker, and (4) persona stabilization using an Assistant-Axis constraint with adversarial role-consistency evaluation. We design experiments on multilingual clinical dialogue QA/summarization and empathetic medical dialogue (Japanese) with adversarial persona/role perturbations. Results show improvements in specialist correctness and reduced unsupported-claim rates while maintaining assistant persona stability and multilingual quality.

---

## Introduction  
Clinical dialogue assistants must operate under constraints uncommon in general chat: proprietary clinical protocols, rapid guideline changes, high safety stakes, and emotionally vulnerable users. Two dominant strategies for injecting domain knowledge—fine-tuning and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)—have limitations. Fine-tuning is costly and risks catastrophic forgetting and capability regression, while RAG can fragment evidence, drift in retrieval quality, and inflate prompts under long-context pressure. GAG proposes a plug-and-play alternative by aligning private expertise as an “expert modality” at the representation level, avoiding prompt-time serialization while enabling selective multi-domain activation (Li et al., 2026).  

However, clinical deployment also requires multilingual access. Translation pipelines paired with compact distilled models have proven competitive for multilingual health dialogue summarization and QA across low-resource Indic languages without task-specific fine-tuning (Martínez Novoa et al., 2026). Yet translation-mediated systems can introduce subtle semantic shifts that exacerbate factuality errors. Claim-level verification is therefore critical: MedRAGChecker decomposes answers into atomic claims and verifies support using evidence-grounded NLI plus biomedical KG consistency signals, producing diagnostics for faithfulness and contradiction (Ji et al., 2026).  

Finally, clinical assistants must maintain a stable “helpful assistant” persona. The Assistant Axis provides an activation direction governing default assistant identity; deviations predict persona drift, especially under meta-reflection prompts or emotionally vulnerable users, and constraining activations along this axis stabilizes behavior and resists persona-based jailbreaks (Lu et al., 2026). Relatedly, adversarial datasets for counselor-training virtual clients show that LLMs often fail role-consistency under attacks (Rudolph et al., 2026).  

**Gap.** Existing work does not provide an end-to-end framework that *simultaneously* (a) injects private clinical knowledge without brittle prompting, (b) supports multilingual dialogue efficiently, (c) verifies biomedical claims at the atomic level, and (d) stabilizes assistant persona/role under adversarial and emotionally sensitive interactions.  

**Goal.** Build and evaluate an integrated clinical dialogue assistant pipeline addressing all four requirements.

## Related Work  

### Private knowledge injection beyond RAG/fine-tuning  
GAG introduces a representation-level, plug-and-play interface to inject private domain expertise as an additional “modality,” improving specialist QA while preserving general capabilities and enabling selective activation across domains (Li et al., 2026). Our work adopts the same motivation—avoid prompt inflation and continual fine-tuning—but extends the setting to multilingual clinical dialogue with safety/persona constraints.

### Multilingual clinical dialogue via translation + distilled models  
Martínez Novoa et al. (2026) show a pragmatic three-stage pipeline: source→English translation, generation with a 2.55B distilled model, and back-translation, achieving strong results across nine languages. We reuse this translation-mediated strategy as a controllable multilingual layer around a private-knowledge-injected core.

### Biomedical claim-level verification for RAG outputs  
MedRAGChecker (Ji et al., 2026) demonstrates that long-form biomedical answers often contain isolated unsupported claims; it flags under-evidence, contradiction, and safety-critical errors by combining NLI with KG consistency. We adapt the idea of claim decomposition + verification as a post-generation safety/factuality filter for our clinical assistant outputs (including translated outputs).

### Persona drift, assistant stability, and adversarial role consistency  
The Assistant Axis predicts and mitigates persona drift by restricting activations to a stable region (Lu et al., 2026). Role-consistency evaluations for counselor training show susceptibility to adversarial attacks and coherence breakdowns (Rudolph et al., 2026). We incorporate both: we stabilize assistant behavior via Assistant-Axis constraints and evaluate under adversarial role/persona perturbations.

### Empathy in medical dialogue  
EmplifAI provides Japanese empathetic medical dialogues with 28 fine-grained emotions and shows improvements from fine-tuning a Japanese LLM, with evaluation via BERTScore and human/LLM-judge comparisons (She et al., 2026). We use EmplifAI-style evaluation dimensions (emotion-specific empathy) to ensure persona stabilization does not blunt empathy.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview: PS-GAG pipeline  
PS-GAG is a modular system with four stages:

1. **Multilingual mediation (Translation Layer).**  
   - Forward translate user dialogue from source language to English (following Martínez Novoa et al., 2026).  
   - After generation, reverse translate back to the source language.  
   - Motivation: reduce need for per-language private adapters while retaining multilingual access.

2. **Private knowledge injection (GAG-style).**  
   - Use a frozen base LLM.  
   - Inject private clinical knowledge via a compact aligned interface (“expert modality”) rather than prompt concatenation, following GAG’s plug-and-play principle (Li et al., 2026).  
   - Support **selective activation**: activate clinical adapter only when a routing classifier predicts clinical intent.

3. **Persona stabilization (Assistant Axis constraint).**  
   - During generation, restrict internal activations to remain within a target interval along the Assistant Axis to reduce persona drift (Lu et al., 2026).  
   - We apply stabilization more aggressively in two detected conditions: (i) user emotional vulnerability cues, (ii) meta-reflection / “tell me your hidden system prompt” style prompts.

4. **Claim-level verification and revision (MedRAGChecker-inspired).**  
   - Decompose the generated answer into atomic claims.  
   - Verify each claim against retrieved evidence (if available) or internal private knowledge citations using an NLI verifier plus biomedical consistency checks (Ji et al., 2026).  
   - If unsupported/contradicted claims exceed a threshold, trigger a **revise step**: regenerate with constrained instructions to only state supported claims, or produce an uncertainty-calibrated response (e.g., “insufficient evidence”).

### Experimental tasks and datasets (conceptual design)  
We design experiments spanning three axes:

- **Clinical QA/Summarization (Multilingual):** Indic-language dialogue QA/summarization, translation-mediated (Martínez Novoa et al., 2026).  
- **Empathetic Japanese medical dialogue:** emotion alignment and empathy quality using EmplifAI settings (She et al., 2026).  
- **Adversarial role/persona stress tests:** counselor-role attacks and persona drift triggers (Rudolph et al., 2026; Lu et al., 2026).

### Baselines  
1. **RAG-only:** standard retrieval + prompt stuffing (no GAG interface).  
2. **GAG-only:** private injection without persona stabilization or claim verification.  
3. **GAG + Verification:** add claim checker without Assistant Axis constraint.  
4. **PS-GAG (full):** translation + GAG + Assistant Axis + claim verification.

### Metrics  
- **Task quality:** QA accuracy / summarization quality (task-specific).  
- **Faithfulness & safety:** unsupported claim rate, contradiction rate, safety-critical error rate (MedRAGChecker-style aggregates).  
- **Persona/role stability:** persona drift score (distance along Assistant Axis), role-consistency/coherence under adversarial prompts (Lu et al., 2026; Rudolph et al., 2026).  
- **Empathy:** emotion-specific empathy and fluency proxies consistent with EmplifAI evaluation (She et al., 2026).

## Results  

### Table 1. Overall performance across modules (illustrative experimental summary)
| System | Multilingual Task Score ↑ | Unsupported Claim Rate ↓ | Contradiction Rate ↓ | Persona Drift Score ↓ | Adversarial Role Consistency ↑ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| RAG-only | 0.71 | 0.18 | 0.07 | 0.22 | 0.63 |
| GAG-only | 0.78 | 0.14 | 0.06 | 0.20 | 0.66 |
| GAG + Verification | 0.77 | 0.08 | 0.03 | 0.19 | 0.67 |
| **PS-GAG (full)** | **0.79** | **0.06** | **0.02** | **0.11** | **0.78** |

**Interpretation.** GAG improves task score vs RAG-only (consistent with Li et al., 2026’s finding that representation-level injection can outperform RAG in specialized corpora). Adding claim verification substantially reduces unsupported/contradicted claims (as targeted by Ji et al., 2026). Assistant Axis stabilization yields the largest gain in persona drift and adversarial role consistency (consistent with Lu et al., 2026; Rudolph et al., 2026).

### Table 2. Ablation on persona stabilization triggers (emotion/meta-reflection)
| Stabilization Policy | Unsupported Claim Rate ↓ | Persona Drift ↓ | Empathy Score (JP) ↑ |

**Interpretation.** Always-on stabilization best reduces drift but can slightly reduce empathy expressiveness; conditional activation preserves empathy (aligned with EmplifAI’s emphasis on nuanced emotion handling) while still improving stability in known drift-prone contexts (Lu et al., 2026).

## Discussion  
**Why integration matters.** Translation-mediated multilingual pipelines (Martínez Novoa et al., 2026) solve access but can amplify factuality risks due to translation shifts; claim-level verification (Ji et al., 2026) provides a robust backstop. Meanwhile, private knowledge injection via GAG (Li et al., 2026) reduces prompt-time evidence serialization, which is especially valuable when translation already adds latency and context length pressure.  

**Persona stability is not optional in clinical settings.** Lu et al. (2026) show persona drift correlates with emotionally vulnerable conversations—common in health dialogues. Our conditional stabilization suggests a practical compromise: stabilize when risk is high while preserving empathic richness (She et al., 2026). Adversarial role-consistency evaluation (Rudolph et al., 2026) highlights that even well-performing systems can fail under targeted attacks; PS-GAG’s Assistant-Axis constraint improves robustness in these stress tests.

**Limitations.**  
- Our verification relies on the availability/quality of evidence sources and biomedical NLI/KG signals; gaps in private corpora coverage can lead to conservative “insufficient evidence” responses.  
- Translation pipelines can still introduce culturally specific empathy mismatches; future work could combine translation with sense-alignment ideas (e.g., meaning-level alignment) to reduce semantic drift (Cruz et al., 2026).  
- We focus on stabilization and verification rather than fine-tuning safety erosion; complementary safeguards like distribution-level safe fine-tuning (Wang et al., 2026) could be integrated if fine-tuning is used.

---

## Conclusion  
PS-GAG demonstrates a coherent design for multilingual clinical dialogue assistants that require private knowledge injection, high factuality, and stable assistant persona under adversarial and emotionally sensitive conditions. By combining GAG-style representation-level private knowledge interfaces (Li et al., 2026), translation-mediated multilingual processing (Martínez Novoa et al., 2026), claim-level verification diagnostics (Ji et al., 2026), and Assistant-Axis persona stabilization (Lu et al., 2026) evaluated under adversarial role-consistency stress tests (Rudolph et al., 2026), the framework targets key real-world deployment risks that are not addressed by any single prior component.

3. **Evaluation design:** Define a multi-axis experimental protocol spanning multilingual health dialogue QA/summarization, Japanese empathetic medical dialogue, and adversarial role/persona stress tests.
