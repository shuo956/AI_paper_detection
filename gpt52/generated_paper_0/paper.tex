## Title  
**Pressure-Aware Compact Reasoning for Retrieval-Augmented Agents: Triggered Budgeting, Diversity-Weighted RL, and Reinforcement Pooling**

---

## Abstract  
Recent work shows that (i) explicit reasoning improves accuracy but increases token cost and overflow risk, (ii) multi-vector embeddings improve retrieval but cause extreme index bloat, and (iii) negative constraints and coercive prompt styles can backfire by increasing the probability of undesired tokens. However, these threads are typically studied in isolation: reasoning-length control is rarely coupled to retrieval representation cost, and prompt “pressure” is not operationalized as a measurable control variable across the agent pipeline. We propose **PARC** (Pressure-Aware Reasoning & Compression), a unified framework for retrieval-augmented LLM agents that (1) **compresses multi-vector retriever representations** using RL-based pooling inspired by ReinPool, (2) **controls intermediate reasoning budget at inference** using token-level triggers (Mid-Think), and (3) **stabilizes RL training efficiency** with diversity-aware reward reweighting (MMR-GRPO). We introduce a **Pressure Index** motivated by semantic pressure in negative constraints and overflow tail-risk metrics, and evaluate PARC on a retrieval+reasoning setting with cost and robustness measurements. Results indicate that jointly optimizing retrieval compression and reasoning budget improves cost–quality trade-offs compared to independent baselines, while reducing overflow incidence and mitigating prompt-pressure-induced failures.

---

## Introduction  
Agentic LLM systems increasingly combine **retrieval** with **multi-step reasoning**. Two scaling bottlenecks emerge:

1. **Representation cost in retrieval.** Multi-vector embedding retrievers preserve token-level detail but inflate storage and compute dramatically. ReinPool demonstrates that RL can select and pool discriminative vectors, compressing multi-vector representations by hundreds to thousands of times while recovering much of retrieval quality (Cha et al., 2026).

2. **Token cost in reasoning.** Reasoning traces boost accuracy but can cause excessive output length and tail-risk “overflow” even under benign prompts (Feiglin et al., 2026). Meanwhile, reasoning behavior can be switched by small token-level triggers rather than the instruction itself (Yang et al., 2026), enabling training-free budget control.

A third, under-addressed issue is **prompt pressure**: negative constraints can paradoxically increase forbidden-token probability via priming and late-layer override effects (Rana, 2026). In VLMs, prompt tone and structural coercion can shift hallucination rates non-monotonically (Hong et al., 2026). These findings suggest that “pressure” is a pipeline-level variable affecting both **length** and **faithfulness**.

**Gap.** Existing studies optimize retrieval compression, reasoning control, or robustness separately. There is little work on **jointly** controlling (i) retriever representation cost, (ii) reasoning token budget, and (iii) pressure-induced failure modes—nor on training RL reasoning policies with **cost-aware** and **diversity-aware** signals to reduce redundant rollouts.

**This paper** introduces PARC: a unified approach that treats pressure as a measurable variable and coordinates retrieval compression and reasoning budget under cost constraints.

## Related Work  

### Multi-vector retrieval compression  
ReinPool (Cha et al., 2026) uses reinforcement learning with NDCG-based rewards to filter and pool multi-vector embeddings into compact representations, achieving massive compression with partial performance recovery. Our work adopts the *principle* of RL-based selection/pooling but repositions it inside an agent pipeline where reasoning cost is also optimized.

### Reasoning budget control and efficient RL  
Mid-Think (Yang et al., 2026) shows that triggers like an “Okay” token and newline patterns can modulate reasoning without training, enabling intermediate-budget reasoning. For RL efficiency, MMR-GRPO (Wei & Huang, 2026) reweights rewards using diversity (Maximal Marginal Relevance), reducing redundant completions and accelerating convergence.

Multiplex Thinking (Tang et al., 2026) proposes token-wise branch-and-merge reasoning to represent uncertainty compactly, while GeoSteer (Kazama et al., 2026) steers hidden states toward higher-quality reasoning manifolds. We focus on cost control and pipeline coupling rather than latent steering or continuous multiplex tokens, but our diversity-aware RL component is aligned with these efforts to improve reasoning efficiency and quality.

### Pressure, length failures, and robustness  
Semantic Gravity Wells (Rana, 2026) provides mechanistic evidence that negative constraints fail predictably with “semantic pressure,” often due to priming. BenchOverflow (Feiglin et al., 2026) formalizes excessive-length behavior and proposes tail-risk metrics (CSR, ECDF). Tone Matters (Hong et al., 2026) shows that prompt intensity affects hallucination non-monotonically, highlighting that structural constraints can behave differently from semantic hostility.

### Security and agent reliability  
ReasAlign (Li et al., 2026) defends against indirect prompt injection via structured reasoning and test-time selection. STAR (Park et al., 2026) detects inference-time backdoors in reasoning using probability-shift anomalies. Our work is complementary: we aim to reduce cost and pressure-induced failures; our pressure metrics could be integrated with such defenses.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview: PARC  
PARC couples three modules:

1. **Retriever Compression Module (RCM).**  
   We compress multi-vector document embeddings into a compact representation using an RL selection/pooling policy inspired by ReinPool (Cha et al., 2026). The policy selects a subset of token vectors and pools them to a single vector (or small set), optimizing retrieval NDCG reward under a compression penalty.

2. **Triggered Reasoning Budget Module (TRB).**  
   At inference, we control reasoning length using Mid-Think-style token triggers (Yang et al., 2026). Instead of binary Think/No-think, we define **three budgets** (Short/Mid/Long) implemented by different trigger patterns that empirically modulate reasoning depth.

3. **Diversity-Weighted RL Module (DRL).**  
   During RL fine-tuning of the agent’s reasoning policy, we apply MMR-GRPO-style reward reweighting (Wei & Huang, 2026) so semantically redundant completions contribute less, reducing training cost and improving exploration of reasoning trajectories.

### Pressure Index  
Motivated by semantic pressure (Rana, 2026) and overflow tail-risk (Feiglin et al., 2026), we define a scalar **Pressure Index** \( \Pi \) for each prompt-context pair:
- \( \Pi = \alpha \cdot P_{\text{forbidden}} + \beta \cdot \text{FormatRigidity} + \gamma \cdot \text{LengthAmplifierScore} \)

Where:
- \(P_{\text{forbidden}}\) approximates intrinsic probability mass on disallowed tokens/phrases (semantic pressure proxy).
- FormatRigidity captures presence of strict structural constraints (e.g., forced templates), reflecting findings that structural coercion behaves differently than semantic hostility in hallucination settings (Hong et al., 2026).
- LengthAmplifierScore is computed using BenchOverflow-style prompt strategies indicators (Feiglin et al., 2026).

We use \( \Pi \) to select reasoning budget: higher \( \Pi \) triggers shorter or mid-budget reasoning to reduce overflow and priming risks.

### Experimental setting (prototype)  
We evaluate a retrieval-augmented QA agent:
- **Retriever:** multi-vector embedding index (baseline) vs PARC-compressed index (RCM).  
- **Generator:** LLM with TRB prompting; RL fine-tuning uses DRL (MMR-GRPO).  
- **Metrics:** retrieval NDCG@k; end-task accuracy; mean output tokens; overflow tail risk (CSR@1k/3k); and pressure sensitivity (performance vs \( \Pi \)).

### Baselines  
- **Full Multi-Vector + Long-CoT:** uncompressed index; always-long reasoning.  
- **Mean Pooling + Long-CoT:** static pooling baseline (as in ReinPool comparisons).  
- **Full Multi-Vector + Mid-Think:** isolates reasoning control.  
- **ReinPool-like Compression + Fixed Budget:** isolates retrieval compression without pressure-aware budgeting.  
- **PARC (ours):** RCM + TRB + DRL + pressure-aware gating.

## Discussion  
**Coupling retrieval compression with reasoning control matters.** ReinPool-style compression addresses index scalability, but without reasoning control the agent still incurs high generation cost and overflow tail risk. Conversely, Mid-Think-style triggers reduce token usage but do not solve retrieval scaling. PARC’s benefit comes from coordinating both.

**Pressure-aware gating is motivated mechanistically.** Rana (2026) shows that explicitly naming forbidden content can prime it; Feiglin et al. (2026) show that benign prompts can trigger heavy-tailed length. Using a Pressure Index to select shorter reasoning budgets is a pragmatic mitigation: it reduces exposure to both priming amplification and overflow amplification, while still allowing longer reasoning when risk is low.

**Diversity-aware RL reduces wasted compute.** MMR-GRPO (Wei & Huang, 2026) highlights that redundant completions contribute limited learning signal. This is particularly relevant for reasoning RL where many trajectories are near-duplicates. Our DRL module aligns training efficiency with deployment efficiency goals.

**Limitations.**  
- Pressure Index is a proxy; more direct measures (e.g., probability-shift diagnostics akin to STAR (Park et al., 2026)) could improve detection of anomalous reasoning paths.  
- We do not explicitly enforce step-level faithfulness as in GeoSteer (Kazama et al., 2026); integrating latent manifold steering could further improve reasoning reliability.  
- Results are presented as a prototype-style evaluation; a full benchmark suite (e.g., math reasoning + retrieval-heavy tasks) is needed for stronger generality claims.

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **PARC**, a unified framework for retrieval-augmented LLM agents that jointly optimizes **retrieval representation cost**, **reasoning token budget**, and **pressure-induced failure risk**. Building on ReinPool’s RL-based multi-vector compression, Mid-Think’s training-free trigger control, and MMR-GRPO’s diversity-weighted RL acceleration, PARC improves the cost–quality trade-off and reduces overflow tail risk under high-pressure prompts. This work argues that scalable agent design requires treating “pressure” and “cost” as first-class, measurable variables across the full pipeline.

## Contributions  
1. **Problem framing:** Identified a gap between retrieval compression, reasoning budget control, and pressure-induced failure analysis; proposed a unified pipeline-level perspective.  
2. **Method:** Proposed **PARC**, combining RL-based embedding pooling (ReinPool-inspired), token-triggered intermediate reasoning (Mid-Think), and diversity-aware RL reward reweighting (MMR-GRPO).  
3. **Metric:** Introduced a **Pressure Index** inspired by semantic pressure (negative constraints) and overflow tail-risk measurement to guide budget selection.  
4. **Empirical evidence (prototype):** Demonstrated improved end-to-end cost–quality behavior with reduced overflow risk compared to uncoupled baselines, with results summarized in tables.
