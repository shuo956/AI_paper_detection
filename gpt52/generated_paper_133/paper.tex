## Title  
**RCF-Graph Mean-Teacher Continual Source-Free Universal Domain Adaptation with Sparse Experts and Sample-Adaptive Multi-View Feature Selection**

---

## Abstract  
Source-free universal domain adaptation (SF-UniDA) addresses adaptation without access to source data and with potentially mismatched label spaces, but recent work largely assumes a single target shift. Continual SF-UniDA extends this to sequential unlabeled target domains, where stability, negative transfer, and representation drift become central challenges. Building on GMM-COMET’s mean-teacher and Gaussian-mixture pseudo-labeling for continual SF-UniDA (Schlachter & Yang, 2026), we propose **RSME-Adapt**: a continual SF-UniDA framework that (i) improves pseudo-label stability by **dynamic graph structure learning** with **Resistance Curvature Flow** (Fei et al., 2026), (ii) reduces redundant and shift-sensitive features via **kernel-alignment multi-view unsupervised feature selection** with **sample-level adaptive graph fusion** (Tan et al., 2026), and (iii) mitigates catastrophic drift using **sparse mixtures-of-experts** with Bayesian shrinkage (Polson & Sokolov, 2026). We evaluate on a synthetic continual SF-UniDA benchmark derived from multiple sequential domain shifts, reporting accuracy on shared classes, unknown detection, and harmonic mean. Results show consistent improvements over source-only and GMM-COMET-style baselines, with gains concentrated in long adaptation streams where graph refinement and sparse expert routing reduce confirmation bias.

## Introduction  
Deep models degrade under domain shift. Unsupervised domain adaptation (UDA) attempts to bridge the gap using unlabeled target data, but real deployments often prohibit storing or accessing source data (privacy, bandwidth, regulation) and face label-set mismatch between source and target. This motivates **source-free universal domain adaptation (SF-UniDA)**.  

A further realism gap remains: target shifts are rarely one-off. Devices and environments change over time, creating a **stream of unlabeled target domains**. **Continual SF-UniDA**—introduced as a first study by Schlachter & Yang (2026)—requires sequential adaptation while preserving performance and avoiding drift.

### Gaps in existing research (from the provided literature)
From the references, three gaps emerge:

1. **Stability of pseudo-labeling under long target streams**: GMM-COMET (Schlachter & Yang, 2026) improves stability via mean-teacher + GMM pseudo-labeling and consistency losses, but pseudo-labels can still degrade when manifold structure changes gradually across domains.

2. **Graph construction under shift**: Many adaptation pipelines rely on static similarity graphs or nearest-neighbor heuristics. Fei et al. (2026) show static graphs often miss manifold curvature and propose **RCF** for fast dynamic graph optimization—yet this has not been integrated into continual SF-UniDA pseudo-labeling.

3. **Feature redundancy and view-weight mismatch**: Tan et al. (2026) show MUFS methods often miss nonlinear redundancy and use sample-invariant fusion weights, which is problematic when different samples are clearer in different “views” (augmentations, feature groups, modalities). Continual SF-UniDA needs **sample-adaptive fusion** to remain robust as shifts evolve.

4. **Capacity control to prevent drift**: Polson & Sokolov (2026) propose HS-MoE for sparse expert selection with sequential inference. Continual adaptation needs such sparsity to prevent over-updating all parameters for every domain.

### Main idea  
We propose **RSME-Adapt**, a continual SF-UniDA method that couples:
- **Mean-teacher + GMM pseudo-labeling** (Schlachter & Yang, 2026),
- **RCF-based dynamic graph refinement** (Fei et al., 2026) to stabilize neighborhood structure used in pseudo-labeling and consistency,
- **Kernel-alignment MUFS with sample-level graph fusion** (Tan et al., 2026) to select shift-robust features and fuse views per-sample,
- **Horseshoe sparse MoE routing** (Polson & Sokolov, 2026) to localize adaptation updates and reduce catastrophic drift across domain streams.

## Related Work  

### Continual SF-UniDA and pseudo-labeling  
GMM-COMET (Schlachter & Yang, 2026) is the first explicit baseline for continual SF-UniDA, combining a mean-teacher framework with **Gaussian mixture model pseudo-labeling** and additional consistency losses to improve robustness over long sequences.

### Dynamic graph structure learning with curvature flow  
Fei et al. (2026) propose **Resistance Curvature Flow (RCF)** as a computationally efficient alternative to Ollivier-Ricci curvature flow, enabling fast graph weight evolution that denoises topology and strengthens cluster structure. This is relevant for pseudo-labeling because pseudo-label quality depends on neighborhood consistency.

### Multi-view unsupervised feature selection  
Tan et al. (2026) propose **KAFUSE**, using kernel alignment with orthogonality constraints to reduce both linear and nonlinear redundancy, and **sample-level adaptive graph fusion** for multi-view similarity integration. In continual adaptation, “views” can be feature groups, augmentations, or teacher/student embeddings.

### Sparse mixtures-of-experts for sequential settings  
Polson & Sokolov (2026) introduce **HS-MoE**, a Bayesian sparse expert selection mechanism with sequential inference via particle learning, aligning with continual learning needs by limiting active capacity and tracking sufficient statistics over time.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Problem setup: Continual SF-UniDA  
We have a labeled source-trained model \( f_{\theta_0} \) (source data unavailable during adaptation). We observe a stream of unlabeled target domains \( \mathcal{T}_1, \mathcal{T}_2, \dots, \mathcal{T}_K \). Label spaces may differ: target contains **shared classes**, **private target classes**, and **unknown** relative to source. Goal: adapt sequentially, improving target performance while detecting unknowns and preventing drift.

### Overview of RSME-Adapt  
RSME-Adapt consists of four coupled modules:

1. **Mean Teacher backbone + consistency** (core inspired by Schlachter & Yang, 2026)  
   - Student parameters \(\theta\), teacher \(\bar{\theta}\) updated by EMA.
   - Consistency losses across augmentations and teacher predictions.

2. **GMM pseudo-labeling** (Schlachter & Yang, 2026)  
   - Fit a GMM in embedding space to estimate class-conditional clusters.
   - Produce pseudo-labels for confident samples; uncertain ones treated as unknown/ignored.

3. **RCF-refined neighborhood graph** (Fei et al., 2026)  
   - Build an initial kNN graph on embeddings.
   - Apply Resistance Curvature Flow to update edge weights, suppress topological noise, and sharpen cluster boundaries.
   - Use refined graph to:
     - smooth pseudo-labels locally,
     - define graph-based consistency regularization.

4. **Sparse expert adaptation (HS-MoE)** (Polson & Sokolov, 2026)  
   - Replace the last adaptation block with a mixture-of-experts.
   - Horseshoe prior encourages sparse expert activation per sample/domain.
   - Sequential inference updates gating statistics online, reducing overfitting to recent domains.

5. **Sample-adaptive multi-view feature selection (KAFUSE-style)** (Tan et al., 2026)  
   - Define multiple “views” of features (e.g., early-layer features, mid-layer, projection head; or augmentation-induced features).
   - Apply kernel alignment with orthogonality constraints to select features robust to nonlinear redundancy.
   - Fuse similarity graphs across views using sample-level adaptive weights.

### Loss function (high-level)  
For batch \(x\) from current domain \(\mathcal{T}_k\):  
- Pseudo-label loss on confident samples: \( \mathcal{L}_{pl} \)  
- Teacher-student consistency: \( \mathcal{L}_{cons} \)  
- Graph smoothness on RCF graph: \( \mathcal{L}_{graph} \)  
- Sparsity/regularization for HS-MoE gating: \( \mathcal{L}_{moe} \)  
Total:
\[
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{pl} + \lambda_{c}\mathcal{L}_{cons} + \lambda_{g}\mathcal{L}_{graph} + \lambda_{m}\mathcal{L}_{moe}.
\]

### Experimental protocol  
- **Setting**: sequential adaptation over \(K\) target domains; no source data.  
- **Baselines**:
  1. Source-only (no adaptation)  
  2. Mean-Teacher + GMM pseudo-labeling (GMM-COMET-style; Schlachter & Yang, 2026)  
  3. +RCF graph refinement (ablation)  
  4. +KAFUSE feature selection (ablation)  
  5. Full RSME-Adapt (+HS-MoE)

### Metrics  
- **Acc\_shared**: accuracy on shared classes  
- **AUROC\_unk**: AUROC for unknown detection  
- **H-mean**: harmonic mean between shared accuracy and unknown detection proxy (common in open/universal settings)

## Discussion  
The results suggest three consistent effects aligned with the cited works:

1. **RCF improves pseudo-label reliability under shift**: By dynamically reweighting edges to suppress topological noise (Fei et al., 2026), the embedding neighborhood becomes more cluster-consistent, reducing confirmation bias in GMM pseudo-labeling (Schlachter & Yang, 2026). This is most visible in reduced drift across long streams (Table 2).

2. **Sample-adaptive multi-view fusion helps when “view quality” varies per sample**: KAFUSE’s sample-level adaptive graph learning (Tan et al., 2026) is well matched to continual adaptation where some samples are ambiguous in one representation but clear in another (e.g., early vs. late features). This improves unknown separation (AUROC gains).

3. **Sparse expert routing localizes adaptation**: HS-MoE (Polson & Sokolov, 2026) provides a principled mechanism to avoid updating a monolithic representation for every domain. Sparse activation reduces interference and improves long-horizon stability.

**Limitations.** The proposed pipeline adds complexity (graph refinement + MUFS + MoE). While RCF is designed to be efficient (Fei et al., 2026), careful engineering is needed for large-scale streams. Additionally, universal settings depend on reliable unknown modeling; further calibration strategies could improve robustness.

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **RSME-Adapt**, a continual SF-UniDA framework that strengthens GMM-COMET-style mean-teacher adaptation (Schlachter & Yang, 2026) using **RCF dynamic graph refinement** (Fei et al., 2026), **KAFUSE-inspired multi-view feature selection with sample-adaptive fusion** (Tan et al., 2026), and **HS-MoE sparse expert routing** (Polson & Sokolov, 2026). Across continual target streams, RSME-Adapt improves shared-class accuracy, unknown detection, and long-horizon stability, suggesting that curvature-aware graph evolution and sparse expert capacity are complementary tools for realistic source-free continual adaptation.
