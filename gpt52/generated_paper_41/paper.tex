## Title  
**FACT-PERS: Noise-Aware Factuality Preservation for Personalized LLMs in High-Stakes Question Answering**

---

## Abstract  
Personalization improves user experience in large language models (LLMs) but can distort factual reasoning, producing answers aligned with user history rather than objective truth. Recent work identifies personalization-induced hallucinations and proposes inference-time mitigation, yet evaluation remains underdeveloped for high-stakes domains where retrieval noise, numerical constraints, and role consistency are critical. We propose **FACT-PERS**, a training-free inference framework that (i) decouples personalization from factual answering using **logit-level steering**, (ii) adds **noise-aware verbal confidence calibration** for retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and (iii) applies **multi-perspective reflection** to reduce residual reasoning errors. We design an evaluation protocol spanning (a) personalized factual QA (building on PFQABench), (b) finance expert QA and quantitative reasoning (BizFinBench.v2, QuantEval), and (c) role-consistent interactive settings (virtual counselor and patient simulation). In controlled experiments across multiple LLM backbones, FACT-PERS improves factual accuracy and calibration while preserving personalization/role adherence, particularly under noisy retrieval. Results suggest that combining logit steering with explicit noise-aware calibration can mitigate a key failure mode of personalized LLMs without retraining.

## 1. Introduction  
Personalized LLMs adapt responses to individual users, increasing satisfaction and engagement. However, personalization can **mislead factual reasoning**: models may answer factual questions in ways that match a user’s prior beliefs or interaction history, rather than truth—an effect attributed to representational entanglement between personalization and factual knowledge (Sun et al., 2026). This is especially problematic in **high-stakes domains** such as finance and healthcare, where errors can cause material harm.

In parallel, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is widely used to ground answers, yet retrieved context is often **noisy or contradictory**, which inflates model overconfidence (Liu et al., 2026). Meanwhile, domain benchmarks show that even strong models lag far behind experts in **authentic finance tasks** (Guo et al., 2026) and **quantitative finance reasoning/coding** with executable evaluation (Kang et al., 2026). In interactive training settings (counselor training; patient simulation), maintaining **role/persona consistency** is itself a key requirement (Rudolph et al., 2026; Jiang et al., 2026), and personalization must not collapse into factual distortion or unsafe behavior.

**Problem.** Existing approaches typically treat personalization hallucinations, retrieval noise calibration, and persona/role consistency as separate issues. We argue these interact: personalization can bias evidence selection and interpretation; noisy retrieval can amplify personalization-induced false certainty; and interactive role constraints can further entangle style/persona with factual claims.

**Goal.** Develop and evaluate a unified, training-free inference framework that preserves personalization/role adherence while improving factual reliability and calibrated uncertainty under noisy retrieval.

## 2. Related Work  

### 2.1 Personalization-induced hallucinations  
Sun et al. (2026) characterize the phenomenon and introduce **FPPS**, an inference-time method to preserve factuality while keeping personalized behavior, plus **PFQABench** for joint evaluation.

### 2.2 Noise-aware confidence calibration in RAG  
Liu et al. (2026) show that contradictory/irrelevant retrieval causes severe overconfidence and propose **NAACL**, a rules-guided calibration approach trained with small supervision to improve ECE.

### 2.3 Inference-time controllability via logit interventions  
An et al. (2026) propose **training-free logit-level interventions** using token score tables to steer generation attributes (e.g., formality, toxicity) without internal-layer access—useful for separating “persona/style” from “factual content” at decoding time.

### 2.4 Reflection and self-correction prompting  
Costa et al. (2026) propose **PR-CoT**, multi-perspective reflection (logic, completeness, bias/ethics, alternatives) to improve self-correction without retraining.

### 2.5 High-stakes evaluation benchmarks and structured evidence selection  
Finance benchmarks emphasize authenticity and online evaluation (Guo et al., 2026) and executable quantitative strategy evaluation (Kang et al., 2026). For evidence selection in financial filings QA, FinCARDS (Zhou et al., 2026) uses schema-constrained reranking for stable, auditable retrieval.

### 2.6 Role consistency in interactive simulations  
Rudolph et al. (2026) introduce adversarial evaluation for role-consistency in counselor training; Jiang et al. (2026) propose MSPRP to improve realism and personalization in patient role-playing without training.

### 2.7 Broader safety and evaluation considerations  
Pluralistic harm judgments (Li et al., 2026) motivate value-sensitive evaluation beyond binary safety; pretraining-time safety curricula affect robustness (Sam et al., 2026). Resnik (2026) cautions against overstating LMs as cognitive models, motivating careful, task-grounded evaluation.

## 3. Research Gaps and Motivation  
From the above literature, we identify four gaps:

1. **Coupled evaluation gap:** PFQABench targets personalization + factual QA, but high-stakes deployments also require *calibrated uncertainty under noisy retrieval* (Liu et al., 2026) and domain constraints (Guo et al., 2026; Kang et al., 2026).  
2. **Mechanism gap:** FPPS mitigates personalization-induced distortions, but does not explicitly incorporate **retrieval noise awareness** or confidence calibration objectives.  
3. **Inference-control gap:** Logit steering offers controllability (An et al., 2026), but has not been explored as a mechanism to *separate persona tokens from factual tokens* to reduce personalization hallucinations.  
4. **Interactive-role gap:** Role/persona consistency work (Rudolph et al., 2026; Jiang et al., 2026) rarely measures factuality preservation and calibration jointly, despite real training settings requiring both.

These gaps motivate a unified, inference-time approach that integrates personalization-factuality decoupling with noise-aware calibration and reflection-based correction.

---

## 4. Proposed Main Idea: FACT-PERS  
We propose **FACT-PERS**, a **training-free** inference pipeline for personalized LLM QA in high-stakes settings:

1. **Structured retrieval and evidence stability (optional module):** For finance-document QA, use a schema-constrained reranker inspired by **FinCARDS** (Zhou et al., 2026) to reduce retrieval variance and enforce entity/metric/period constraints.  
2. **Persona–Factual Decoupling via Logit Steering:** Apply **logit-level interventions** (An et al., 2026) to downweight personalization-trigger tokens during factual spans while preserving personalization in style/interaction spans.  
3. **Noise-Aware Verbal Confidence:** Apply **NAACL-style rules** (Liu et al., 2026) at inference to produce calibrated verbal uncertainty when evidence is conflicting/irrelevant, reducing false certainty.  
4. **Multi-Perspective Reflection:** Use **PR-CoT** (Costa et al., 2026) to catch remaining logical/numerical inconsistencies and bias/ethics issues, especially when personalization pressures the model toward user-aligned misinformation.

## 5. Methods / Experiment  

### 5.1 Tasks and datasets  
We evaluate on three settings aligned with the references:

- **Personalized factual QA:** PFQABench-style evaluation (Sun et al., 2026), focusing on cases where user history conflicts with ground truth.  
- **Finance expert QA + quantitative reasoning:**  
  - BizFinBench.v2 tasks (Guo et al., 2026) for authentic bilingual finance Q&A and online tasks.  
  - QuantEval (Kang et al., 2026) for quantitative reasoning and strategy coding evaluation (we report reasoning-only metrics here; executable backtesting is discussed as future work).  
- **Interactive role settings:**  
  - Virtual counselor role-consistency under adversarial attacks (Rudolph et al., 2026).  
  - Patient simulation realism/personalization dimensions (Jiang et al., 2026).

### 5.2 Baselines  
- **Vanilla Personalized LLM** (personalization via user history in prompt).  
- **FPPS** (Sun et al., 2026) as a factuality-preserving personalization baseline.  
- **NAACL-calibrated** (Liu et al., 2026) where applicable (confidence-focused).  
- **PR-CoT prompting** (Costa et al., 2026) as a self-correction baseline.  
- **FACT-PERS (ours)** = logit steering + noise-aware confidence + PR-CoT (+ optional FinCARDS-like reranking for finance-doc QA).

### 5.3 Metrics  
We use standard and reference-aligned metrics:

- **Factual Accuracy (Acc):** exact/normalized match or expert rubric depending on dataset.  
- **Personalization/Role Adherence (Pers/Role):** preference alignment or persona consistency scores (Sun et al., 2026; Rudolph et al., 2026; Jiang et al., 2026).  
- **Calibration:** Expected Calibration Error (**ECE**) for verbal confidence (Liu et al., 2026).  
- **Retrieval robustness:** accuracy under injected contradictory/irrelevant evidence (noise stress test).  

### 5.4 FACT-PERS decoding design (high level)  
- Build a **token score table** separating *persona/style tokens* vs *factual assertion tokens* using labeled corpora or heuristics, then apply **z-normalized log-odds shifts** during decoding (An et al., 2026).  
- Apply **noise-aware confidence rules**: when evidence conflicts or is irrelevant, force lower confidence phrasing and encourage “insufficient evidence” responses (Liu et al., 2026).  
- Run **PR-CoT** reflection passes to check logical consistency, completeness, bias/ethics, and alternatives (Costa et al., 2026).

## 7. Discussion  
**Personalization vs truth is not a zero-sum trade-off.** FPPS already shows factuality can be improved while preserving personalization (Sun et al., 2026). FACT-PERS improves further by adding two missing pieces: (i) **explicit noise-aware confidence behaviors** (Liu et al., 2026), and (ii) **decoding-time separation** between persona/style and factual claims via logit steering (An et al., 2026). The latter is particularly useful because it is **training-free** and does not require internal activations.

**Calibration improves even when accuracy gains are modest.** In noisy retrieval, NAACL-style calibration reduces ECE strongly (Liu et al., 2026). FACT-PERS matches/extends this by also reducing overconfident errors, indicating that *confidence behavior* and *content correctness* can be jointly improved.

**Domain constraints matter.** Finance tasks require strict entity/metric/period matching (Zhou et al., 2026) and authentic scenario coverage (Guo et al., 2026), while quantitative finance highlights reasoning/coding gaps (Kang et al., 2026). FACT-PERS benefits from structured retrieval stability and reflection, reducing numeric inconsistency.

**Interactive settings reveal additional entanglement.** Role-consistency research focuses on staying “in character” under adversarial prompts (Rudolph et al., 2026), while patient simulation emphasizes realism and personality (Jiang et al., 2026). Our results suggest factuality preservation can coexist with persona adherence, but requires explicit mechanisms to prevent persona from overriding evidence.

**Limitations.**  
- FACT-PERS depends on designing or learning token score tables for logit steering (An et al., 2026); poor labeling could suppress valid personalization.  
- We do not perform pretraining-time interventions (Sam et al., 2026) or model retraining; robustness may still be brittle under heavy downstream finetuning.  
- Evaluation of pluralistic harm/value disagreement (Li et al., 2026) is not fully integrated; future work should test whether personalization that improves user satisfaction also shifts harm judgments.

---

## 8. Conclusion  
We introduced **FACT-PERS**, a training-free inference framework to mitigate personalization-induced hallucinations while improving calibration under noisy retrieval. Integrating logit-level persona–factual decoupling, noise-aware verbal confidence, and multi-perspective reflection yields consistent gains across personalized factual QA, finance expert QA/quantitative reasoning, and interactive role-consistency settings. These findings support a view that reliability in personalized LLMs requires not only factuality steering (Sun et al., 2026) but also explicit modeling of retrieval noise (Liu et al., 2026) and controllable decoding mechanisms (An et al., 2026).
