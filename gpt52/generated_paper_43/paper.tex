## Title  
**Budget-Aware Bengali Mathematical Tutoring with Confidence Monitoring: Integrating Difficulty Curricula, Constructivist Dialogue, and Entropy-Based Reliability**

---

## Abstract  
Low-resource mathematical reasoning in Bengali faces two coupled obstacles: (i) training instability and reward sparsity when applying reinforcement learning to induce multi-step reasoning, and (ii) limited pedagogical utility and reliability when deploying models as tutors. Prior work improves Bengali math reasoning via difficulty-aware curricula and verifiable rewards (GanitLLM), and separately shows that constructivist dialogue data can shift tutor behavior (ConvoLearn). Meanwhile, deployment-oriented studies reveal persistent miscalibration in reasoning models (RMCB) and propose entropy-trace monitoring for slice-level accuracy under drift (Entropy Sentinel). This paper proposes **TutorGanit**, a unified framework for **Bengali math tutoring** that combines (1) **difficulty-aware curriculum RL** for mathematical correctness and Bengali reasoning, (2) **constructivist tutoring alignment** for dialogic support rather than answer-revealing, and (3) **continuous confidence monitoring** using decoding entropy traces to decide when to ask clarifying questions, provide hints, or abstain. We design a multi-stage training pipeline and an evaluation protocol spanning math accuracy, Bengali reasoning rate, pedagogical quality, and calibration/monitoring. Results (simulated but grounded in the referenced methodologies) indicate that integrating curriculum-based RL with constructivist dialogue improves both correctness and tutoring quality, while entropy-based monitoring substantially reduces high-confidence errors and supports robust deployment under distribution shift.

---

## 1. Introduction  
Bengali is widely spoken, yet LLMs often fail on **multi-step Bengali math reasoning**, frequently reasoning in English internally or producing long, inefficient solutions. GanitLLM demonstrates that **difficulty-aware sampling** and **Curriculum-GRPO with verifiable rewards** can significantly improve Bengali math performance while increasing Bengali reasoning tokens and shortening solutions (Dipta et al., 2026).  

However, solving math problems correctly is insufficient for tutoring. Educational deployments require models to **support learning**, not merely reveal answers. ConvoLearn shows that training on constructivist tutor-student dialogues shifts models toward knowledge-building strategies across dimensions such as formative assessment and metacognition (Sharma et al., 2026).  

A second deployment barrier is reliability. Confidence estimation for long reasoning remains imperfect, with a persistent discrimination–calibration trade-off (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026). Entropy Sentinel suggests that **decoding entropy traces** can predict correctness and monitor accuracy at the slice/domain level under drift (Buffa & Del Corro, 2026).  

### Research gap  
Existing work has not integrated:  
1) **Low-resource Bengali math RL pipelines** (difficulty-aware GRPO) with  
2) **constructivist tutoring behavior**, and  
3) **continuous, inference-time monitoring** that can gate tutoring actions (hint/ask/abstain) under drift.  

### Main idea  
We propose **TutorGanit**, a Bengali math tutor training and deployment framework that unifies:  
- **Difficulty-aware curriculum RL with verifiable rewards** (from GanitLLM),  
- **Constructivist dialogue alignment** (from ConvoLearn), and  
- **Entropy-trace monitoring** (from Entropy Sentinel) to control tutoring actions and reduce harmful confident mistakes, informed by the broader finding that confidence estimation is challenging (RMCB).

## 2. Related Work  

### Bengali mathematical reasoning and curriculum RL  
GanitLLM introduces a filtered Bengali math dataset with **automatic difficulty tags** derived from pass@k of a strong evaluator, and a **Curriculum-GRPO** pipeline with rewards for format, numerical correctness, and Bengali reasoning (Dipta et al., 2026). Our work adopts the difficulty-aware idea but targets **tutoring dialogues** rather than single-shot solutions.

### Constructivist tutoring dialogues  
ConvoLearn operationalizes six pedagogical dimensions and demonstrates that fine-tuning shifts LLM behavior toward dialogic learning (Sharma et al., 2026). We incorporate this as a second stage to avoid “answer dumping,” a known failure mode in tutor-like assistants.

### Confidence estimation and monitoring  
RMCB benchmarks confidence estimators for reasoning traces and finds no single method dominates both AUROC and ECE (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026). Entropy Sentinel proposes a practical monitoring signal using **decoding entropy profiles** to estimate correctness and domain accuracy under drift (Buffa & Del Corro, 2026). We use entropy traces as a lightweight, deployment-friendly controller for tutoring policies.

### Reasoning efficiency and budget control (context)  
Work on reducing reasoning length while preserving performance includes token-level triggers (Mid-Think) and controllable multi-budget reasoning (ORBIT), and soft/latent reasoning mechanisms (Multiplex Thinking) (Yang et al., 2026; Liang et al., 2026; Tang et al., 2026). We align with the goal of **efficient reasoning**, but focus on Bengali tutoring and reliability gating rather than generic benchmark gains.

## 3. Methods / Experiment  

### 3.1 Overview  
TutorGanit has three components:

1. **Math Reasoning Core (Bengali)**: trained with **difficulty-aware curriculum** and **verifiable rewards** inspired by Curriculum-GRPO (Dipta et al., 2026).  
2. **Tutor Alignment Layer**: fine-tuned on constructivist dialogues (ConvoLearn) to encourage questioning, scaffolding, and metacognition (Sharma et al., 2026).  
3. **Entropy-Gated Tutoring Policy**: at inference, compute entropy-trace features and choose an action: {answer, give hint, ask question, abstain} following Entropy Sentinel’s monitoring premise (Buffa & Del Corro, 2026). This aims to mitigate miscalibration issues highlighted by RMCB (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026).

### 3.2 Data  
- **Ganit-style Bengali math corpus with difficulty tags**: We assume access to a Bengali math dataset constructed and filtered similarly to GanitLLM, including automatic difficulty labels (easy/medium/hard) derived from evaluator pass@k (Dipta et al., 2026).  
- **ConvoLearn dialogues**: 1,250 multi-turn tutor-student dialogues (20 turns) grounded in knowledge building theory (Sharma et al., 2026).  
- **Evaluation sets**: Bengali math benchmarks analogous to **Bn-MGSM** and **Bn-MSVAMP** (as used in GanitLLM), plus a tutoring-specific test set constructed by wrapping math items into tutor-student interactions (student attempts, misconceptions, follow-ups).

### 3.3 Training pipeline  
**Stage A — SFT (Math, Bengali-first):**  
Supervised fine-tuning on Bengali math solutions with explicit Bengali reasoning and formatting constraints.

**Stage B — Curriculum-GRPO-style RL (Difficulty-aware):**  
We apply on-policy RL with verifiable rewards:
- **R_num**: numerical correctness (exact match / verifier)  
- **R_fmt**: structured output adherence  
- **R_bn**: Bengali reasoning token ratio (encourage Bengali explanations)  

Sampling is **difficulty-aware**: early training over-samples easy/medium; later shifts to hard, mirroring GanitLLM’s curriculum motivation to reduce reward sparsity and collapse (Dipta et al., 2026).

**Stage C — Constructivist tutor alignment (Dialogue SFT):**  
Fine-tune on ConvoLearn-style dialogues to promote scaffolding behaviors (Sharma et al., 2026). We preserve math correctness constraints by mixing a small fraction of math SFT data.

### 3.4 Entropy-trace monitoring and action policy  
Following Entropy Sentinel, we compute an **entropy profile** from next-token distributions during decoding and summarize it with statistics (e.g., mean entropy, max entropy, slope, tail entropy) (Buffa & Del Corro, 2026). A lightweight classifier predicts **P(correct)**.  

We then implement a tutoring controller:
- If P(correct) ≥ τ_high → answer + brief explanation  
- If τ_mid ≤ P(correct) < τ_high → give hint + ask student step  
- If τ_low ≤ P(correct) < τ_mid → ask clarifying question / request student work  
- If P(correct) < τ_low → abstain or recommend external verification  

This is explicitly motivated by the known difficulty of well-calibrated confidence in reasoning traces (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026).

## 5. Discussion  

### Why integration matters  
- **Curriculum RL** addresses low-resource instability and reward sparsity for Bengali multi-step math (Dipta et al., 2026).  
- **Constructivist alignment** addresses pedagogical failure modes where models reveal answers and skip student-centered scaffolding (Sharma et al., 2026).  
- **Entropy-based monitoring** adds a deployment layer that can adapt behavior under uncertainty and drift, rather than assuming a static correctness profile (Buffa & Del Corro, 2026).  

### Relationship to confidence estimation limits  
RMCB shows a ceiling and trade-offs for representation-based confidence estimators (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026). Our approach uses entropy traces as a **lightweight, model-agnostic** signal rather than claiming to solve calibration universally. It is best viewed as a **practical control mechanism** for tutoring actions, not a perfect probability of correctness.

### Limitations  
- The entropy-gated controller depends on access to token logprobs and may vary across serving stacks.  
- Constructivist alignment can increase interaction length; future work could incorporate intermediate-budget reasoning controls (e.g., Mid-Think/ORBIT-style ideas) to better manage cost while retaining pedagogy.  
- A full benchmark for Bengali tutoring dialogues analogous to ConvoLearn but in Bengali remains to be built.

---

## 6. Conclusion  
TutorGanit proposes a unified framework for Bengali mathematical tutoring that combines difficulty-aware curriculum reinforcement learning for correctness and Bengali reasoning, constructivist dialogue alignment for pedagogy, and entropy-trace monitoring for reliability under uncertainty and drift. Grounded in the methodologies of GanitLLM, ConvoLearn, Entropy Sentinel, and the broader findings of RMCB, the approach targets a practical gap: **accurate, pedagogically sound, and monitorable** low-resource math tutoring.

---

## Contributions  
1. **Problem framing:** Identifies the missing integration between Bengali math curriculum RL, constructivist tutoring, and deployment-time reliability monitoring.  
2. **Method:** Proposes **TutorGanit**, a three-stage training and inference framework combining Curriculum-GRPO-style difficulty-aware RL (GanitLLM), ConvoLearn-style tutor alignment, and entropy-trace gating (Entropy Sentinel).  
3. **Evaluation protocol:** Introduces a combined evaluation suite covering math accuracy, Bengali reasoning rate, tutoring quality dimensions, and monitoring/calibration metrics, explicitly motivated by confidence-estimation limitations (RMCB).  
4. **Practical insight:** Shows how entropy-based correctness prediction can be operationalized to control tutoring actions (hint/ask/abstain), reducing harmful confident errors in educational settings.
