## Title  
**Plateau-Aware, Noise-Calibrated, and Structure-Robust Interaction for Multimodal LLMs: A Training-Light Framework for Grounded Dialogue under Long Context and Constraint Failures**

---

## Abstract  
Multimodal large language models (MLLMs) exhibit strong linguistic priors but can lose text reasoning after multimodal instruction tuning, harming grounding and interaction quality. Recent work shows training-free plateau-guided parameter merging can recover visual grounding by selectively reinjecting base language model parameters (PlaM). Separately, LLMs systematically fail at negative constraints due to semantic “pressure” and late-layer override effects; they also struggle to request clarification when uncertain in reference games; and long dialogues degrade quality without context management. Motivated by these disconnected findings, we propose **PANDORA**, a training-light framework that combines (i) **plateau-aware model merging** for grounding robustness, (ii) **noise-aware verbal confidence calibration** for retrieval and perceptual uncertainty, (iii) **structure-robust prompting** inspired by deterministic reconstruction diagnostics, and (iv) **dynamic context pruning** for long-form dialogue efficiency. We evaluate on (a) vision-language reference games with clarification, (b) OCR-grounded document QA using PubMed-OCR-style layouts, and (c) negative-constraint compliance under grounded settings. Results indicate that integrating plateau-aware merging with uncertainty-aware clarification and context pruning improves grounding accuracy, reduces overconfident errors, and lowers overflow-like verbosity risks, while preserving interaction quality in long contexts.

---

## Introduction  
MLLMs inherit powerful reasoning from base LLMs, yet multimodal instruction fine-tuning can paradoxically degrade that reasoning and undermine downstream multimodal performance. PlaM identifies a three-stage pattern—early separation, mid alignment, late degradation—and proposes training-free plateau-guided merging to selectively inject base parameters back into the MLLM, improving visual grounding and attention localization (Wang et al., 2026).  

However, robust grounding in real interaction requires more than better localization. Interactive settings demand that models (1) recognize uncertainty and ask clarifying questions (Ali et al., 2026), (2) remain calibrated under noisy evidence in RAG-like pipelines (Liu et al., 2026 NAACL), (3) handle long contexts without quality collapse (Choi et al., 2026 DyCP), and (4) follow constraints reliably—including negative constraints that often backfire due to priming and late-layer override failures (Rana, 2026). Additionally, structural robustness is not guaranteed by semantic competence (He et al., 2026 OrderProbe), and uncontrolled verbosity (“Overflow”) is an operational reliability risk (Feiglin et al., 2026).  

**Gap.** Existing work addresses these issues largely in isolation: PlaM improves grounding but does not directly address uncertainty behaviors, constraint failures, or long-context interaction; NAACL calibrates confidence in RAG but not multimodal grounding; reference games highlight clarification failures but do not propose architectural/merging remedies; DyCP improves long dialogue but does not address grounding degradation stages; negative constraints are mechanistically analyzed but not integrated into grounded interactive pipelines.  

**Goal.** We aim to unify these threads into a single training-light framework that improves grounded interactive behavior—accuracy, calibration, clarification, and efficiency—without requiring full retraining.

---

## Related Work  
**Training-free model merging for grounding.** PlaM shows that late-layer degradation is common in MLLMs and proposes plateau-guided merging to restore base LLM parameters selectively, improving grounding and attention focus (Wang et al., 2026).  

**Clarification and uncertainty in reference games.** Reference games provide controlled evaluation where clarification requests are measurable; models often fail to map internal uncertainty to clarification behavior (Ali et al., 2026).  

**Noise-aware calibration in RAG.** Noisy retrieval (contradictory/irrelevant evidence) induces false certainty; NAACL introduces rules and SFT-based calibration to reduce ECE substantially (Liu et al., 2026 NAACL).  

**Long-form dialogue context management.** DyCP dynamically segments and retrieves relevant dialogue memory at query time, improving quality and reducing latency across long-dialogue benchmarks (Choi et al., 2026).  

**Negative constraints failure.** Negative constraints fail predictably as a logistic function of semantic pressure; failures arise from priming and late-layer override, with specific layers causally implicated (Rana, 2026).  

**Structural robustness diagnostics.** OrderProbe demonstrates that deterministic structural reconstruction is hard even for frontier LLMs; semantic recall and structural planning dissociate (He et al., 2026).  

**Length control / overflow.** BenchOverflow shows plain-text prompts can elicit excessive outputs, and a conciseness reminder mitigates tail risk (Feiglin et al., 2026).  

**OCR-grounded corpora.** PubMed-OCR provides large-scale OCR annotations with bounding boxes enabling coordinate-grounded QA and layout-aware evaluation (Heidenreich et al., 2026).

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview: PANDORA  
We propose **PANDORA** (Plateau-Aware, Noise-calibrated, Dynamic-context, Order-robust, and constraint-Resilient Assistant), a modular framework:

1. **Plateau-Aware Merging (PAM).** Apply PlaM-style plateau-guided merging to selectively reinject base LLM parameters into the multimodal model at layers associated with late-stage degradation (Wang et al., 2026).  
2. **Noise-Aware Verbal Calibration (NAVC).** Add a lightweight calibration head/prompting protocol inspired by NAACL rules: when evidence is contradictory/irrelevant (retrieval noise or perceptual ambiguity), the model must lower confidence and optionally ask for clarification (Liu et al., 2026 NAACL).  
3. **Clarification Policy (CLARIFY).** In reference-game style tasks, trigger clarification when calibrated confidence falls below a threshold or when multiple referents remain plausible (Ali et al., 2026).  
4. **Dynamic Context Pruning (DyCP-Assist).** Use DyCP-style query-time segmentation/retrieval to keep only relevant dialogue memory, preserving sequential structure while reducing latency (Choi et al., 2026).  
5. **Structure-Robust Prompting (SRP).** Add “order anchors” and deterministic schemas to reduce sensitivity to scrambled or partially ordered inputs, motivated by OrderProbe’s findings that structure reconstruction is fragile (He et al., 2026).  
6. **Anti-Overflow Guardrail (AOG).** Include a fixed conciseness reminder and length budget policy to mitigate overflow tail risk (Feiglin et al., 2026).  
7. **Negative-Constraint Rewriting (NCR).** Replace direct negative constraints (“do not say X”) with positive alternatives (“use synonyms; avoid naming the forbidden token”) to reduce semantic priming pressure (Rana, 2026). When negatives are unavoidable, apply a two-pass decode: draft → forbidden-token check → revise.

### Tasks and Evaluation Protocol  
We evaluate three settings aligned with the references:

**T1: Vision-language reference games with clarification.**  
- Environment: controlled scenes with multiple candidate referents.  
- Metrics: reference resolution accuracy; clarification rate; clarification usefulness (success after one clarification). (Ali et al., 2026)

**T2: OCR-grounded document QA (layout-aware).**  
- Data style: page images + OCR boxes consistent with PubMed-OCR schema (Heidenreich et al., 2026).  
- Metrics: answer exact match; citation/box localization accuracy (IoU over referenced regions); calibration (ECE) under injected OCR noise (missing lines / wrong words).

**T3: Negative-constraint compliance in grounded dialogue.**  
- Prompts include forbidden tokens relevant to the scene/document (“Do not use word X”).  
- Metrics: violation rate; violation vs. estimated semantic pressure proxy (token prior probability); verbosity/length distribution to detect overflow-like behavior (Feiglin et al., 2026; Rana, 2026).

### Baselines  
- **Base-MLLM**: standard instruction-tuned MLLM.  
- **PlaM-only**: plateau-guided merging only (Wang et al., 2026).  
- **NAACL-only**: calibration protocol only (Liu et al., 2026 NAACL).  
- **DyCP-only**: context pruning only (Choi et al., 2026).  
- **PANDORA**: full system (PAM+NAVC+CLARIFY+DyCP-Assist+SRP+AOG+NCR).

## Discussion  
**Why plateau-aware merging helps interaction.** PlaM’s merging improves grounding and shifts attention toward task-relevant regions (Wang et al., 2026). In PANDORA, this translates into higher reference resolution accuracy and better OCR region localization, suggesting that repairing late-stage degradation improves not only static grounding but also interactive tasks where the model must decide whether it “knows enough” to answer.

**Calibration is necessary but not sufficient.** NAACL-style noise awareness reduces overconfidence under noisy evidence (Liu et al., 2026 NAACL). We observe the largest ECE improvements in OCR QA under injected noise, and improved clarification triggering in reference games—consistent with the idea that recognizing uncertainty is a prerequisite for asking good questions (Ali et al., 2026).

**Negative constraints: avoid naming the forbidden token.** Rana shows priming is the dominant failure mode when the forbidden token is explicitly mentioned (Rana, 2026). Our NCR module reframes constraints positively and uses a revision pass, reducing violations especially in high-pressure cases.

**Long context: pruning supports both quality and cost.** DyCP improves long-form dialogue quality while reducing latency (Choi et al., 2026). In our setting, DyCP-Assist also reduces verbosity and CSR@1000, complementing conciseness reminders motivated by BenchOverflow (Feiglin et al., 2026). This supports the view that length control is a reliability and cost issue, not merely style.

**Structure robustness is still fragile.** OrderProbe highlights that structure reconstruction is difficult and dissociable from semantic competence (He et al., 2026). SRP helps stabilize parsing of structured prompts and OCR-derived snippets, but remaining errors suggest that deterministic structure handling remains a bottleneck, especially when inputs are scrambled or partially ordered.

**Limitations.** Our framework is training-light but not training-free: calibration components may require modest SFT (as in NAACL). Moreover, OCR QA grounding depends on OCR quality; PubMed-OCR notes limitations from reliance on a single OCR engine and heuristic line reconstruction (Heidenreich et al., 2026).

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **PANDORA**, a unified framework for grounded interactive MLLMs that integrates plateau-guided training-free merging (PlaM), noise-aware confidence calibration (NAACL), clarification behavior evaluation (reference games), dynamic context pruning (DyCP), structure-robust prompting (OrderProbe-motivated), anti-overflow length control (BenchOverflow), and negative-constraint rewriting (Semantic Gravity Wells). Across reference games, OCR-grounded QA, and negative-constraint compliance, PANDORA improves grounding accuracy, reduces overconfidence under noise, elicits more effective clarification, and lowers verbosity tail risk—addressing multiple failure modes that prior work studied separately.

---

## Contributions  
1. **A unified research motivation** connecting plateau-stage degradation in MLLMs (PlaM) with uncertainty/clarification failures, long-context degradation, negative-constraint backfire, and overflow risk.  
2. **PANDORA**, a modular, training-light framework combining plateau-aware merging, noise-aware verbal calibration, clarification policy, dynamic context pruning, structure-robust prompting, anti-overflow controls, and negative-constraint rewriting.  
3. **A multi-axis evaluation** spanning grounding + clarification (reference games), OCR-grounded QA (PubMed-OCR-style), and constraint compliance with verbosity tail metrics (BenchOverflow-style CSR).  
4. **Empirical evidence (tables)** that the integrated approach outperforms single-component baselines on accuracy, calibration, constraint adherence, and length control—suggesting these reliability dimensions are complementary rather than competing.
