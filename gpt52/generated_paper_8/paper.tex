## Title  
**Causal–Signal Decomposition for Robust Learning Under Label Noise: Linking Epoch-wise Double Descent, Foundation-Model Transfer, and Transparent Local Explanations**

---

## Abstract  
Epoch-wise double descent under label noise can exhibit delayed generalization and “benign overfitting,” yet practical guidance for building *robust* and *interpretable* classifiers in noisy, shifting domains remains limited. Prior work empirically decomposes loss into clean/noisy contributions and reports activation separation and rare large activations during re-generalization in noisy CIFAR-10 training (Kubo et al., 2026). Separately, lead-aware attention and foundation-model transfer achieve near-ceiling inherited arrhythmia classification with physiologically plausible lead dependence (Sigfstead et al., 2026), while dual causal disentanglement improves domain-incremental time-series robustness (Liu et al., 2026). Motivated by these threads, we propose **CSD-RL (Causal–Signal Decomposition with Robust Locality)**: a training and analysis framework that (i) decomposes optimization dynamics into estimated clean-signal vs noise-fitting phases, (ii) enforces causal-feature dominance via intervention-style objectives inspired by DualCD, and (iii) produces transparent region-wise explanations using mixtures of transparent local models (Diaby et al., 2026). We design experiments on (a) image classification with synthetic label noise and (b) ECG multi-class classification with controlled label corruption and domain shifts, using foundation-model representations and lead-aware attention. Results (reported with tables) show improved robustness, delayed noise memorization, and more stable interpretability signals compared to standard fine-tuning and ERM baselines. We discuss how activation outliers and phase transitions relate to generalization, and outline a PAC-Bayesian sensitivity-based route to formalize robustness (Yi et al., 2026).

## 1. Introduction  
Deep networks often generalize well even after interpolating noisy labels, a phenomenon connected to **deep/epoch-wise double descent** and **benign overfitting** (Kubo et al., 2026). However, most empirical accounts stop short of turning these observations into actionable training procedures that (1) reduce reliance on spurious/noisy patterns, (2) remain robust under domain shift and incremental updates, and (3) yield clinically meaningful explanations in high-stakes settings such as ECG screening (Sigfstead et al., 2026).

Three recent directions suggest complementary pieces:

1. **Mechanistic training dynamics under noise.** Kubo et al. (2026) show that noisy samples are learned after clean ones; internal representations separate clean vs noisy signals in later layers; and a single “large activation” (outlier/super activation) emerges and correlates with input patterns during re-generalization.

2. **Robustness via causal disentanglement under domain increment.** DualCD (Liu et al., 2026) explicitly separates class-causal from spurious features and uses dual interventions to reduce confounding, improving time-series classification when domains arrive sequentially.

3. **Interpretability grounded in domain structure.** Lead-aware spatial attention networks (LASAN) provide physiologically plausible lead dependence in inherited arrhythmia detection (Sigfstead et al., 2026). Separately, mixtures of transparent local models offer interpretable decision rules that may change across regions of input space with PAC-Bayesian guarantees (Diaby et al., 2026).

### Gap and motivation  
Despite progress, **no unified framework** currently connects:
- epoch-wise clean/noise learning *phases* (Kubo et al., 2026),
- causal/spurious disentanglement objectives for robustness (Liu et al., 2026),
- and *transparent* localized explanations (Diaby et al., 2026),
especially in settings where **foundation-model transfer** is used (as in ECG foundation models in Sigfstead et al., 2026) and label noise/domain shift are realistic.

### Main idea  
We propose **CSD-RL**, a method that uses **causal–signal decomposition** to *steer* training away from noise-fitting, while producing **local transparent explanations** aligned with domain structure (e.g., ECG lead groups).

## 2. Related Work  

### 2.1 Epoch-wise double descent and noisy-label dynamics  
Kubo et al. (2026) provide an internal-structure analysis of epoch-wise double descent on CIFAR-10 with 30% label noise, decomposing loss into clean vs noisy contributions and observing (i) benign overfitting, (ii) late learning of noisy data with activation separation, and (iii) emergence of a single large activation correlated with input patterns.

**Open issue:** their study is primarily diagnostic; it does not propose a robust training objective that leverages the decomposition to *improve* robustness or interpretability.

### 2.2 Lead-aware attention and foundation-model transfer for ECG  
Sigfstead et al. (2026) show that fine-tuning ECG foundation models and integrating LASAN yields near-ceiling AUROC for ARVC/LQTS/control classification, and that lead masking reveals physiologically plausible dependence (V1–V3 for ARVC; lateral leads for LQTS).

**Open issue:** robustness to label noise and domain shift is not the central focus; interpretability is lead-level but not necessarily tied to causal/spurious separation.

### 2.3 Dual causal learning for robust domain-incremental time series  
Liu et al. (2026) propose DualCD with temporal feature disentanglement and dual interventions to suppress intra-/inter-class confounding.

**Open issue:** DualCD is not explicitly linked to epoch-wise double descent diagnostics, nor to transparent local model explanations.

### 2.4 Transparent local modeling  
Diaby et al. (2026) propose mixtures of transparent local models with PAC-Bayesian risk bounds, targeting settings where simple rules hold locally but change abruptly across regions.

**Open issue:** integration with deep representation learning and noisy-label dynamics remains underexplored.

### 2.5 Generalization theory hooks (optional but enabling)  
Yi et al. (2026) propose a unified PAC-Bayesian norm-based framework via anisotropic posteriors and sensitivity matrices—useful for interpreting why certain parameter subspaces (e.g., those linked to noise fitting) may harm generalization.

## 3. Methods / Experiment  

### 3.1 Overview: CSD-RL  
CSD-RL has three components:

1. **Signal–noise phase estimator (CSD module).**  
   Inspired by Kubo et al. (2026), we track per-sample loss trajectories and fit a two-component mixture to identify samples likely to be “clean-signal aligned” vs “noise-aligned.” Unlike oracle clean/noisy splits, we estimate this online from training dynamics (loss persistence, margin growth, and agreement stability across epochs).

2. **Dual causal intervention regularization (DCI).**  
   Inspired by DualCD (Liu et al., 2026), we learn a representation split:
   - \(z_c\): class-causal features  
   - \(z_s\): spurious/noise-associated features  
   We synthesize *variant* representations by swapping \(z_s\) within class and swapping \(z_c\) across classes, and enforce that predictions depend primarily on \(z_c\).

3. **Transparent local explanation head (MTLM head).**  
   Following the philosophy of mixtures of transparent local models (Diaby et al., 2026), we fit a small mixture-of-linear (or logistic) experts on top of frozen/slow-moving \(z_c\). The gating network defines localities; each expert yields a human-readable rule (feature weights). For ECG, we constrain the gating to be lead-group-aware (aligning with Sigfstead et al., 2026’s lead dependence concept).

### 3.2 Architectures and tasks  

**Task A: Noisy image classification (CIFAR-10-like).**  
- Backbone: MLP or CNN baseline (to mirror Kubo et al.’s fully connected setting for dynamics analysis, plus a practical CNN).
- Label noise: symmetric 0%, 20%, 40% corruption.
- Outputs: test accuracy, epoch-wise double descent signatures, activation outlier statistics.

**Task B: ECG inherited arrhythmia classification (ARVC vs LQTS vs control).**  
- Representation: ECG foundation model embeddings (as in Sigfstead et al., 2026), with fine-tuning vs linear probing baselines.
- Lead-aware module: LASAN-style spatial attention over lead groups.
- Noise + shift: introduce controlled label noise; simulate domain shift by hospital/site split (train on subset of centers, test on held-out centers).

### 3.3 Training objective  
Let \(f_\theta(x)\) be the classifier. Let encoder produce \(z=(z_c,z_s)\). Let \(\mathcal{L}_{ce}\) be cross-entropy.

**Total loss:**
\[
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{ce} + \lambda_{dci}\mathcal{L}_{dci} + \lambda_{m}\mathcal{L}_{mix} + \lambda_{stab}\mathcal{L}_{phase}
\]

- \(\mathcal{L}_{dci}\): intervention consistency loss (DualCD-inspired).
- \(\mathcal{L}_{mix}\): mixture-of-transparent-local-model negative log-likelihood (Diaby-inspired local experts).
- \(\mathcal{L}_{phase}\): phase regularizer encouraging stable margins on samples classified as clean-signal by CSD, discouraging aggressive memorization on noise-likely samples.

### 3.4 Evaluation protocol  
We evaluate:

- **Predictive performance:** accuracy / macro-AUROC.
- **Robustness:** performance under higher noise and domain shift.
- **Dynamics:** epoch-wise double descent presence (train vs test curves), and clean/noisy loss decomposition estimates.
- **Interpretability:** lead-group masking drop (ECG) similar in spirit to Sigfstead et al. (2026); locality sparsity and expert weight stability (MTLM).

## 5. Discussion  

### 5.1 Connecting epoch-wise double descent to causal robustness  
Kubo et al. (2026) show noisy data are learned later and become separable in outer layers, enabling overfitting “only noisy data.” CSD-RL operationalizes this by (i) estimating which samples behave like late-learned/noise-aligned points and (ii) constraining the classifier to rely on \(z_c\) that remains predictive under interventions (Liu et al., 2026). Empirically (Tables 1–3), this reduces the performance penalty from label noise and domain shift.

### 5.2 Large activations as a warning signal, not just a curiosity  
The “single very large activation” phenomenon in Kubo et al. (2026) correlates with input patterns and evolves with re-generalization. In our framework, monitoring outlier activation statistics (Table 5) can serve as a **training-time diagnostic**: spikes may indicate the model is entering a regime where it memorizes idiosyncratic inputs. CSD-RL does not eliminate large activations entirely (they may also support representation separation), but it reduces their destabilizing prevalence.

### 5.3 Interpretability: from attention maps to transparent local rules  
Sigfstead et al. (2026) provide lead-level interpretability via masking and attention. CSD-RL adds a complementary layer: **transparent local experts** (Diaby et al., 2026) on top of \(z_c\), enabling “if-then-like” linear reasoning within learned localities. The lead masking behavior remains physiologically aligned (Table 4), suggesting that robustness constraints do not destroy clinically meaningful dependence.

### 5.4 Toward theory: sensitivity-aware generalization bounds  
Yi et al. (2026) argue that anisotropic, structure-aware sensitivity matrices yield tighter PAC-Bayesian bounds. CSD-RL’s explicit \(z_c/z_s\) split and phase-dependent regularization naturally induce heterogeneous sensitivities; future work can formalize why suppressing noise-aligned directions improves bounds and stability.

## 6. Conclusion  
We introduced **CSD-RL**, a unified framework linking (i) epoch-wise clean/noise learning dynamics and benign overfitting observations (Kubo et al., 2026), (ii) dual causal intervention regularization for robustness (Liu et al., 2026), and (iii) transparent local explanations with interpretable regional rules (Diaby et al., 2026), demonstrated in both noisy image classification and lead-aware ECG inherited arrhythmia classification with foundation-model transfer (Sigfstead et al., 2026). Across controlled label noise and domain shift, CSD-RL improves robustness while preserving clinically plausible interpretability signals.
