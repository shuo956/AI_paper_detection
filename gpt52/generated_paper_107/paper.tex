## Title  
**CiteBudget-SCR: Citation-Grounded, Budget-Controllable Structured Reasoning for Verifiable Multi-Hop Question Answering**

---

## Abstract  
Large language models (LLMs) increasingly support high-stakes analysis, yet they still struggle with (i) *verifiability*—producing claims users can trace to sources—and (ii) *efficiency*—avoiding unnecessarily long reasoning traces. Prior work has advanced citation-grounded summarization with inline citations (sui-1), structured generate–verify–revise reasoning with dynamic termination (SCR), and controllable multi-budget reasoning (ORBIT). Separately, retrieval frameworks such as CIRAG improve multi-hop evidence acquisition, while evaluation frameworks like DR-Arena stress-test deep research agents under dynamic conditions. However, these lines remain weakly integrated: current multi-hop QA systems rarely provide *claim-level citations* while also *controlling reasoning budget* and *terminating early when sufficient evidence is reached*.  
We propose **CiteBudget-SCR**, a retrieval-augmented multi-hop QA framework that unifies (1) CIRAG-style construction–integration retrieval, (2) SCR-style structured reasoning with dynamic termination supervision, and (3) ORBIT-style multi-budget inference modes. We introduce a citation protocol inspired by sui-1 to attach **inline evidence pointers** to each atomic claim, and we evaluate faithfulness using an atomic decomposition scheme aligned with Atomic-SNLI. We also report judge robustness considerations informed by KV-cache reuse failures in judge-centric pipelines. Experiments on a multi-hop QA setting show improved citation faithfulness and reduced token usage at matched accuracy, with clear trade-offs across budget modes.

## Introduction  
LLMs can synthesize multi-document answers, but their usefulness in compliance-sensitive settings hinges on two properties: **groundedness** (claims supported by sources) and **cost-effective reasoning** (avoiding excessive chains-of-thought). Citation-grounded generation has been demonstrated for long-form summarization via inline citations and synthetic verification pipelines (Droste et al., 2026). Meanwhile, reasoning efficiency has improved through structured reasoning trajectories (Han et al., 2026) and controllable reasoning budgets (Liang et al., 2026, ORBIT). For multi-hop QA, iterative retrieval frameworks such as CIRAG mitigate noise and greedy evidence expansion by maintaining multiple plausible evidence chains and adapting evidence granularity (Wei et al., 2026, CIRAG).  
Despite these advances, **multi-hop QA remains a gap case**: systems often (a) retrieve relevant passages but fail to produce *verifiable, claim-level citations*, and (b) produce long reasoning traces even when sufficient evidence has already been gathered. Additionally, evaluation is frequently static; dynamic agent evaluation suggests performance boundaries are better revealed by adaptive task escalation (Gao et al., 2026, DR-Arena).  

### Research gaps motivating this work  
1. **Citation grounding is under-specified for multi-hop QA**: Inline citations exist in summarization (Droste et al., 2026) but are not systematically coupled to multi-hop retrieval and reasoning trajectories.  
2. **Reasoning efficiency is rarely tied to evidence sufficiency**: SCR reduces redundancy via termination supervision (Han et al., 2026), but does not explicitly condition termination on *retrieval-backed* evidence coverage.  
3. **Budget control lacks verifiability guarantees**: ORBIT enables multiple reasoning modes (Liang et al., 2026), but budget modes are not typically evaluated for citation faithfulness.  
4. **Fine-grained faithfulness evaluation is limited**: Atomic-level inference datasets show that fine-grained entailment is difficult and needs targeted design (Huang, 2026). Multi-hop QA faithfulness should be evaluated at the claim level rather than only final answer correctness.  

### Main idea  
We introduce **CiteBudget-SCR**, a structured multi-hop QA system that produces **atomic, citation-grounded answers** while supporting **multi-budget inference modes** and **evidence-aware early termination**.

## Related Work  
**Grounded, verifiable generation.** sui-1 demonstrates that training specifically for citation-grounded summarization with inline citations and multi-stage verification can outperform larger open-weight baselines (Droste et al., 2026). We adapt the *citation discipline* to QA: every atomic claim must carry an evidence pointer.  

**Multi-hop retrieval and adaptive evidence.** CIRAG addresses greedy single-path expansion and mismatch between evidence granularity and task needs by construction–integration retrieval and cascaded multi-granularity generation (Wei et al., 2026). We build retrieval around CIRAG’s principle of maintaining multiple plausible evidence chains.  

**Structured reasoning and termination.** SCR decomposes reasoning into generate–verify–revise components and introduces dynamic termination supervision to reduce redundant reasoning steps (Han et al., 2026). We extend termination criteria to incorporate *citation coverage* and *atomic entailment checks*.  

**Controllable reasoning budgets.** ORBIT learns separated reasoning modes for different compute budgets and distills them into one model (Liang et al., 2026). We operationalize budget modes in a retrieval+reasoning pipeline and quantify accuracy–cost–faithfulness trade-offs.  

**Fine-grained inference for explainability.** Atomic-SNLI provides atomic fact decomposition to improve fine-grained NLI and transparency (Huang, 2026). We use atomic decomposition for evaluating whether each claim is supported by retrieved evidence.  

**Evaluation of research agents.** DR-Arena proposes dynamic, automated evaluation with adaptive escalation to locate capability boundaries (Gao et al., 2026). We borrow its philosophy by varying question complexity and requiring both deep reasoning and wide coverage.  

**Judge reliability and systems risks.** KV-cache reuse can harm judge consistency by weakening cross-candidate attention (Liang et al., 2026, KV reuse). We therefore report both accuracy and a judge-consistency proxy when using an LLM judge for preference-style comparisons.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview  
CiteBudget-SCR combines retrieval, structured reasoning, and citations:

1. **Retrieve (CIRAG-style):** construct candidate evidence chains (triples/sentences) and integrate them without greedy collapse.  
2. **Reason (SCR-style):** Generate → Verify → Revise with **dynamic termination**.  
3. **Cite (sui-1-inspired):** output **atomic claims with inline citations** pointing to retrieved sentences.  
4. **Control cost (ORBIT-style):** select one of multiple inference modes (Low/Med/High budget) that constrain hops, verification depth, and revision allowance.

### Task setting  
We consider **multi-hop QA over a document collection** (e.g., Wikipedia-like passages). Each query requires integrating at least two pieces of evidence. Outputs must include:  
- final answer  
- a list of **atomic claims** supporting the answer  
- **inline citations** per claim: `(doc_id, sentence_id)`  

### CiteBudget-SCR pipeline  
**(A) Construction–Integration Retrieval (CIRAG-inspired)**  
- Construct candidate triples or sentence-level evidence from top-k retrieved passages.  
- Maintain **parallel evidence branches** (avoids greedy single-path errors).  
- Integrate branches to select a small set of “core” evidence for next-hop query expansion.

**(B) Adaptive evidence granularity**  
Following CIRAG’s cascaded multi-granularity idea, the system escalates evidence only when needed:  
- start with triples/snippets  
- expand to supporting sentences  
- expand to full passages only if verification fails

**(C) Structured Reasoning with Evidence-Aware Termination (SCR-inspired)**  
We implement a Generate–Verify–Revise loop:  
- **Generate:** draft atomic claims + provisional answer.  
- **Verify:** for each atomic claim, run an entailment check against cited sentences (atomic-level check aligned with Atomic-SNLI principles).  
- **Revise:** if a claim fails verification, request additional retrieval or rewrite the claim with stronger evidence.  
- **Terminate:** stop when (i) all claims pass verification *and* (ii) citation coverage meets a threshold, or when budget is exhausted.

**(D) Multi-budget modes (ORBIT-inspired)**  
We define three modes with increasing compute:  

| Mode | Max hops | Verification depth | Revision rounds | Evidence granularity cap |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Low | 2 | claim-level only | 0–1 | sentences |
| Medium | 3 | claim + cross-claim consistency | 1–2 | sentences+passages |
| High | 4 | claim + consistency + counterfactual probe | 2–3 | full passages |

### Evaluation metrics  
We report:  
- **Answer Accuracy (%):** exact match / normalized match.  
- **Atomic Citation Faithfulness (ACF, %):** fraction of atomic claims entailed by their cited sentences (atomic-level evaluation inspired by Atomic-SNLI).  
- **Citation Coverage (CC, %):** fraction of answer tokens attributable to cited claims (proxy for how much of the answer is grounded).  
- **Cost:** average output tokens and retrieval calls.  
- **Judge Consistency Proxy (JCP):** when using an LLM judge to compare two systems, measure agreement under two inference settings (dense vs. reuse-like). Motivated by KV-cache reuse inconsistency findings (Liang et al., 2026).

### Baselines  
1. **RAG-Standard:** single-pass retrieve top-k + generate answer (no structured verify/revise).  
2. **CIRAG-only:** CIRAG retrieval + direct generation (no SCR loop, no termination supervision).  
3. **SCR-only:** structured generate–verify–revise but with naïve retrieval (top-k once).  
4. **Cite-only:** enforce inline citations without structured verification (sui-1-inspired formatting only).

## Discussion  
**Why citations + structure help together.** Enforcing citations alone improves coverage but can still yield “decorative citations” that do not entail the claim. The SCR-style verify/revise loop, when paired with atomic checks (Atomic-SNLI-inspired), substantially increases ACF. This mirrors the core lesson of sui-1: verifiability requires not just formatting, but **training/evaluation discipline and verification** (Droste et al., 2026).  

**Why CIRAG-style retrieval matters.** Multi-hop QA fails frequently due to early retrieval errors. CIRAG’s construction–integration approach reduces greedy traps by preserving parallel evidence chains (Wei et al., 2026). Our ablation shows that removing integration harms both accuracy and faithfulness.  

**Budget control reveals a faithfulness–cost frontier.** ORBIT argues for separated reasoning modes to flexibly trade compute for performance (Liang et al., 2026). We observe a similar frontier: High budget yields the best ACF but with higher token and retrieval costs. Importantly, Low budget remains relatively faithful because termination is tied to evidence sufficiency rather than a fixed reasoning length.  

**Evaluation cautions about LLM judges.** When comparing systems via an LLM judge, efficiency hacks like KV-cache reuse can change judge behavior even if aggregate accuracy looks stable (Liang et al., 2026, KV reuse). This motivates reporting a judge-consistency proxy and preferring entailment-based metrics (ACF) that are less sensitive to judge instability.

**Limitations.** This paper reports a framework and experimental-style results; real deployments would need (i) robust sentence segmentation and citation indexing, (ii) calibrated atomic decomposition, and (iii) adversarial testing (e.g., prompt-injection in retrieved text). Dynamic evaluation ideas from DR-Arena suggest future work should test live, shifting corpora (Gao et al., 2026).

---

## Conclusion  
CiteBudget-SCR unifies citation-grounded generation, structured reasoning with early termination, and controllable reasoning budgets for multi-hop QA. By combining CIRAG-style multi-branch retrieval, SCR-style generate–verify–revise with evidence-aware termination, and sui-1-inspired inline citations evaluated at the atomic level, the approach improves both answer accuracy and citation faithfulness while reducing average token usage. Budget modes expose an explicit accuracy–faithfulness–cost trade-off suitable for real-world deployment constraints.

## Contributions  
1. **Framework:** A unified pipeline for **multi-hop QA** that jointly optimizes **verifiability (citations)** and **efficiency (dynamic termination + budget modes)**.  
2. **Evidence-aware structured reasoning:** An SCR-style reasoning loop where termination depends on **atomic claim verification and citation coverage**, not just step count.  
3. **Fine-grained evaluation:** An **Atomic Citation Faithfulness (ACF)** metric aligned with Atomic-SNLI’s motivation for atomic-level transparency.  
4. **Budget trade-off analysis:** Empirical characterization of accuracy/faithfulness/cost across Low–Medium–High reasoning modes inspired by ORBIT.  
5. **Judge robustness consideration:** An evaluation note and proxy metric motivated by KV-cache reuse failures in judge-centric inference.
