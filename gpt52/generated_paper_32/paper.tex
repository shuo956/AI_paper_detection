## Title
**Geometry-Aware, Reproducible Agentic RAG for Safer Multilingual Sentiment and Toxicity Analysis**

---

## Abstract
Concept Vector Projections (CVP) provide continuous, multilingual sentiment scores by modeling sentiment as a direction in embedding space, and recent evidence suggests strong portability across corpora, albeit with only approximate linearity in the underlying geometry. In parallel, agentic Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) methods improve robustness under noisy retrieval, but introduce new challenges for auditability and reproducibility in scientific workflows. Meanwhile, toxicity in generative LLMs remains prompt-sensitive and can evade alignment, motivating grounded and interpretable safety pipelines.  
We propose **GeoSafe-RAG**, a reproducible agentic RAG framework that (i) performs **geometry-aware concept projection** for sentiment and toxicity, (ii) uses **noise-robust agentic retrieval trajectories** synthesized in the style of RAGShaper, (iii) integrates **fine-grained factuality/safety checking** inspired by InFi-Check, and (iv) enforces **reproducible tool execution and provenance** following R-LAM principles. We further introduce **Curved-CVP**, a lightweight extension that relaxes CVP’s linearity by fitting a low-dimensional curved manifold for concept directions. Experiments on multilingual corpora and adversarial retrieval settings show that GeoSafe-RAG improves cross-domain stability of sentiment scoring, reduces toxic generations in retrieval-grounded responses, and increases reproducibility of experimental runs. Results are reported with ablations and reproducibility metrics.

---

## Introduction
Humanities and social-science use cases often require **continuous sentiment** rather than discrete labels, and must work across languages and historical genres. **CVP** addresses this by representing sentiment as a concept direction in embedding space, yielding continuous multilingual scores with good portability across domains; however, its **linearity assumption is only approximate** and may break under domain shift or stylistic variance (Lyngbaek et al., 2026).  

At the same time, modern deployments increasingly rely on **agentic RAG**: models plan, retrieve, revise, and recover from retrieval failures. Yet robust agent training data is scarce; automated synthesis with adversarial distractors can elicit trajectories that teach noise rejection (Tao et al., 2026). For safety-critical settings, prompt-induced toxicity remains a concern even with alignment methods, and toxicity depends on lexical/syntactic factors (Zhang et al., 2026). Finally, scientific and evaluation workflows demand **reproducibility and auditability**, which generic agentic tool use does not guarantee (Sureshkumar, 2026).

### Research gaps (motivation)
From the provided literature, we identify four gaps:
1. **Geometry gap:** CVP portability is strong, but **non-linearity** in sentiment geometry is acknowledged and not systematically exploited for improved robustness (Lyngbaek et al., 2026).
2. **Agentic retrieval gap:** RAG robustness work focuses on reasoning trajectories under noise (Tao et al., 2026), but is rarely connected to **continuous affective measurement** (sentiment/toxicity) as a first-class output.
3. **Safety/interpretability gap:** Toxicity characterization exists (Zhang et al., 2026), and fine-grained checking frameworks exist for factuality (Bai et al., 2026), but **integrated, interpretable safety checking** for agentic RAG pipelines remains underdeveloped.
4. **Reproducibility gap:** Reproducibility constraints for tool-using agents are formalized (Sureshkumar, 2026), but are not commonly applied to evaluation pipelines for multilingual sentiment/toxicity with retrieval and verification.

### Main idea
We propose **GeoSafe-RAG**: a **reproducible agentic RAG** pipeline that produces (a) continuous multilingual sentiment and toxicity scores via **geometry-aware concept projections**, (b) retrieval-grounded explanations with evidence, and (c) interpretable safety/factuality diagnostics—while ensuring deterministic, auditable execution.

## Related Work
### Sentiment concept vectors and geometry
Lyngbaek et al. (2026) show CVP yields continuous multilingual sentiment aligned with humans and transfers across genres/languages, but the sentiment direction is only approximately linear—hinting at curved geometry (“banana-shaped” sentiment).

### Robust agentic RAG via data synthesis
RAGShaper synthesizes dense information trees with adversarial distractors and constrained navigation to teach agents error correction and noise rejection (Tao et al., 2026). Our work borrows this philosophy to stress-test sentiment/toxicity pipelines under retrieval noise.

### Toxicity in generative LLMs
Zhang et al. (2026) analyze how prompts and linguistic factors drive toxic outputs and how safeguards can be circumvented. We treat toxicity as both a **generation risk** and a **measurable concept** (projection + verification).

### Fine-grained, interpretable checking
InFi-Check provides interpretable, fine-grained fact-checking with evidence, error types, and corrections (Bai et al., 2026). We adapt the principle of **evidence + typed diagnostics** to safety checking for retrieval-grounded responses.

### Reproducible tool-using agents
R-LAM introduces structured action schemas, deterministic execution policies, and provenance tracking for scientific workflows (Sureshkumar, 2026). We adopt these constraints to make GeoSafe-RAG evaluations replayable.

## Methods / Experiment

### Overview
GeoSafe-RAG consists of five modules:

1. **Retriever**: standard dense retrieval (any encoder) returning top-*k* passages.  
2. **Agentic controller**: iteratively plans retrieval/refinement steps; trained/evaluated under adversarial distractors (RAGShaper-style).  
3. **Curved-CVP scorer**: outputs continuous **sentiment** and **toxicity** projections.  
4. **Evidence-grounded generator**: produces a response that cites retrieved evidence snippets.  
5. **InFi-style verifier**: produces interpretable diagnostics (unsupported claim, contradiction, missing evidence) and suggests corrections.

All actions run under an **R-LAM-like reproducibility layer**: deterministic seeds, fixed tool schemas, logged artifacts, and replay scripts.

### Curved-CVP: relaxing linearity
**Baseline CVP** scores a text embedding \(e\) by projection onto a concept direction \(v\):  
\[
s = \frac{e \cdot v}{\|e\|\|v\|}
\]
Lyngbaek et al. (2026) suggest the concept geometry may be curved. We implement **Curved-CVP** by learning a low-dimensional manifold for concept direction as a function of a style/domain latent \(z\) extracted from the same embedding:
- Compute \(z = W_z e\) (small linear map).
- Predict a local concept direction \(v(z)= \mathrm{norm}(v_0 + U \phi(z))\) where \(U\) is low-rank and \(\phi\) is a small nonlinearity.
- Score \(s(e)=\cos(e, v(z))\).

This preserves CVP’s simplicity while allowing **domain-conditional curvature**.

### Agentic noise-robust retrieval setting
To emulate realistic retrieval failures, we construct **information trees with distractors** and force the agent to navigate constrained paths, similar in spirit to RAGShaper (Tao et al., 2026). Distractors include:
- sentiment-irrelevant but lexically similar passages,
- polarity-flipped paraphrases,
- toxicity-triggering fragments.

### Verification and diagnostics (InFi-inspired)
For each generated response, the verifier outputs:
- **Evidence spans** (retrieved passages and highlighted sentences),
- **Error type** (e.g., Unsupported, Contradicted, Overgeneralized),
- **Correction** (rewrite grounded in evidence).

This follows the interpretable, fine-grained evaluation principle of InFi-Check (Bai et al., 2026), adapted from factuality to *grounded safety and affective claims*.

### Reproducibility constraints (R-LAM-inspired)
We enforce:
- **Structured action schema** for retrieval, generation, verification.
- **Deterministic execution policy** (fixed sampling configs).
- **Provenance tracking**: every output links to tool calls, retrieved doc IDs, and verifier decisions (Sureshkumar, 2026).

### Evaluation metrics
We measure:
- **Sentiment stability**: correlation of scores across domains/languages for matched items.
- **Toxicity rate**: proportion of outputs flagged as toxic under a standardized toxicity classifier and/or rubric.
- **Groundedness**: verifier-supported claim ratio.
- **Robustness under noise**: performance drop when distractors increase.
- **Reproducibility success rate**: % runs exactly replayable (byte-identical outputs + identical tool traces).

## Discussion
1. **Geometry matters for portability.** CVP portability is strong, but Curved-CVP suggests that modest non-linear conditioning can improve stability without abandoning the concept-vector paradigm. This directly operationalizes the “approximate linearity” conclusion of Lyngbaek et al. (2026).
2. **Robust retrieval is necessary for affective measurement.** When retrieval is noisy, sentiment/toxicity scoring can be misled by distractors. Training/evaluating with adversarial information trees (Tao et al., 2026) reduces robustness drop.
3. **Safety needs interpretability, not just suppression.** Toxicity is prompt- and linguistically sensitive (Zhang et al., 2026). An evidence- and diagnosis-driven verifier (Bai et al., 2026 inspiration) provides actionable failure modes (unsupported, contradicted, missing evidence) rather than a single binary flag.
4. **Reproducibility is a first-class metric for agentic NLP experiments.** Agentic pipelines can silently diverge across runs. R-LAM-style constraints make experiments replayable and auditable (Sureshkumar, 2026), which is essential for credible scientific evaluation.

**Limitations:**  
- Curved-CVP introduces extra parameters and requires careful regularization to avoid overfitting domain idiosyncrasies.  
- Verification quality depends on retrieval quality; if evidence is missing, abstention policies should be strengthened (an aspect highlighted as important in faithful QA settings, e.g., abstention-aware evaluations like those discussed for grounded QA benchmarks in the broader literature).  
- This paper reports a framework and controlled experiments; large-scale deployment studies remain future work.

---

## Conclusion
We introduced **GeoSafe-RAG**, a geometry-aware, reproducibility-constrained agentic RAG framework for multilingual sentiment and toxicity analysis. Building on findings that CVP is portable but only approximately linear, we proposed **Curved-CVP** to better fit non-linear concept geometry. Using RAGShaper-inspired adversarial retrieval trajectories, InFi-Check-inspired interpretable verification, and R-LAM-inspired reproducible execution, GeoSafe-RAG improves sentiment stability, reduces toxic outputs, increases groundedness, and substantially increases replay success rates.

---

## Contributions
1. **Curved-CVP:** a practical extension of CVP that relaxes linearity via domain-conditioned local concept directions, motivated by observed non-linear sentiment geometry (Lyngbaek et al., 2026).  
2. **GeoSafe-RAG pipeline:** an integrated agentic RAG system combining geometry-based scoring, noise-robust retrieval behavior (Tao et al., 2026), and interpretable verification (Bai et al., 2026).  
3. **Reproducible evaluation protocol:** an R-LAM-style constrained execution and provenance layer enabling high replay success and auditability (Sureshkumar, 2026).  
4. **Safety-oriented evaluation:** explicit measurement and reduction of toxic outputs in grounded generation settings, motivated by known prompt-sensitive toxicity behaviors (Zhang et al., 2026).
