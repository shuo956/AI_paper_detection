## Title  
**Thermal-Bridge Post-Training for Retrieval-Augmented Agents: Finite-Temperature Initialization, Plan-Purification, and Value-of-Information Querying**

---

## Abstract  
Post-training pipelines for large reasoning models commonly rely on supervised fine-tuning (SFT) followed by reinforcement learning (RL), yet this sequence can induce distributional collapse that harms exploration and degrades downstream RL performance. Recent work proposes finite-temperature Gibbs initialization (GIFT) to bridge objectives between SFT and RL, while separate lines of research improve retrieval-augmented reasoning via dual-agent planning and purification (D²Plan), step-level experience seeking (ExpSeek), and decision-theoretic clarification via value of information (VoI). However, these advances are largely studied in isolation: initialization methods are not evaluated under retrieval noise, planning methods are not coupled to principled exploration-preserving initialization, and clarification policies are not integrated as first-class actions in post-trained retrieval agents.  

We propose **Thermal-Bridge RAG Agent (TBR-Agent)**, a unified post-training and inference framework that (i) uses **finite-temperature Gibbs initialization** for exploration-preserving cold start, (ii) trains a **dual-agent planner–purifier** with **plan-oriented RL rewards** under retrieval distractors, and (iii) adds a **VoI-based clarification action** to mitigate underspecified queries without hand-tuned thresholds. We report results on a suite of retrieval-augmented QA settings inspired by D²Plan-style multi-hop reasoning and robustness to irrelevant evidence. Across ablations, finite-temperature initialization improves RL stability and final task success, purification reduces distractor hijacking, and VoI clarification improves utility under high-cost errors.

## Introduction  
Retrieval-augmented LLM agents must interleave querying, reading, and reasoning while operating under partial observability and noisy evidence. Two recurring failure modes are (1) **poor search chain construction** and (2) **reasoning hijacking by distractors**, especially as context grows and includes irrelevant retrievals, as highlighted by D²Plan’s analysis and solution via explicit global planning and a purifier module (Luo et al., 2026). Meanwhile, post-training remains dominated by **SFT → RL**, yet SFT can collapse the model distribution and reduce exploration, creating an intrinsic mismatch with subsequent RL (Zhao et al., 2026).  

At inference time, agents also face a communication dilemma: act on underspecified requests or interrupt users for clarification. Threshold heuristics are brittle; VoI provides a parameter-free decision-theoretic alternative that adapts to stakes and ambiguity (Dong et al., 2026). Complementarily, ExpSeek argues that step-level intrinsic signals (entropy) can trigger proactive experience injection during interaction, improving agent performance under changing observations (Zhang et al., 2026).  

**Gap.** Current research provides strong components—objective-consistent initialization (GIFT), retrieval planning/purification (D²Plan), proactive intervention signals (ExpSeek), and rational clarification (VoI)—but lacks a unified agentic pipeline showing how *exploration-preserving post-training* interacts with *retrieval noise*, *planning structure*, and *communication actions*.  

**Goal.** We study whether a retrieval-augmented reasoning agent can be made simultaneously:  
1) easier to optimize in RL (less collapse, more stable learning),  
2) more robust to irrelevant evidence, and  
3) more reliable under underspecified, high-stakes prompts via principled clarifications.

## Related Work  
**Finite-temperature post-training initialization.** GIFT reframes SFT as a degenerate zero-temperature limit and proposes finite-temperature Gibbs initialization to maintain a distributional bridge to RL, improving global optimality prospects and downstream RL performance (Zhao et al., 2026).  

**Retrieval-augmented reasoning with structure.** D²Plan introduces a dual-agent paradigm with a Reasoner forming global plans and a Purifier condensing relevant evidence, trained via SFT cold start plus plan-oriented RL rewards, improving robustness to distractors (Luo et al., 2026).  

**Step-level intervention and intrinsic signals.** ExpSeek triggers experience seeking based on step-level entropy thresholds and tailors experience content, improving web-agent benchmarks and showing small experience models can assist larger agents (Zhang et al., 2026).  

**Human-agent communication.** VoI formalizes when to ask clarifying questions by trading off expected utility gain vs user cost, without hyperparameter tuning, and performs well across diverse domains (Dong et al., 2026).  

**Reliability and confidence.** RMCB shows persistent trade-offs between discrimination and calibration for confidence estimation in large reasoning models, suggesting that naive confidence thresholds are insufficient in high-stakes settings (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026). This motivates decision-theoretic clarification (VoI) rather than pure confidence gating.  

**Additional context.** Work on controlled exploration in iterative improvement (Hu et al., 2026) reinforces that initialization bias can trap search in poor regions—conceptually aligned with GIFT’s motivation.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview: TBR-Agent  
We propose **TBR-Agent**, combining:  
1. **Thermal initialization (post-training):** Replace standard SFT cold start with **finite-temperature Gibbs initialization** (GIFT) before RL.  
2. **Structured retrieval reasoning (architecture + training):** Use a **Reasoner** that maintains an explicit plan and a **Purifier** that filters/condenses retrieved evidence (D²Plan).  
3. **Clarification as an action (inference + RL):** Add a **VoI-based “Ask” action** to request missing information when expected utility exceeds user cost (Dong et al., 2026).  
4. **Optional step-triggered intervention (inference):** Use an entropy-trigger signal inspired by ExpSeek to decide when to consult an experience buffer (Zhang et al., 2026).  

### Agent formulation  
At each step \(t\), the agent chooses among actions:  
- **Retrieve(q)**: issue a search query \(q\)  
- **Read/Condense**: Purifier selects salient evidence and compresses it  
- **PlanUpdate**: Reasoner updates global plan based on feedback  
- **Answer(a)**  
- **Ask(c)**: ask user a clarification question \(c\)  

The state includes current plan, condensed evidence, and dialogue context.

### Post-training pipeline  
We compare two cold-starts:  
- **SFT-init:** standard supervised fine-tuning trajectories (as in typical D²Plan pipelines).  
- **GIFT-init:** treat supervision as a finite-temperature energy potential and initialize the policy from the resulting Gibbs distribution (Zhao et al., 2026).  

Then we apply **plan-oriented RL** similar in spirit to D²Plan (Luo et al., 2026), with additional reward terms for clarification.

### Rewards  
We use a composite reward:  
- \(r_{\text{task}}\): correctness / task success  
- \(r_{\text{plan}}\): plan coherence and completion of required subgoals (D²Plan-style)  
- \(r_{\text{purify}}\): penalize inclusion of irrelevant evidence; reward compression of relevant evidence  
- \(r_{\text{ask}}\): VoI-based net utility for clarification  
  \[
  r_{\text{ask}} = \text{VoI}(s_t) - \text{Cost}_{\text{user}}
  \]
VoI is computed as in Dong et al. (2026): expected improvement in downstream utility if the agent asks vs acts immediately, minus user effort cost.

### Experimental design  
Because the references span methods rather than a single unified benchmark for retrieval planning + clarification, we define an evaluation suite consistent with the failure modes emphasized by D²Plan: multi-hop retrieval with distractors and growing context. We report:  
- **Task Success (%)**: final answer correctness  
- **Distractor Hijack Rate (%)**: cases where final rationale cites distractor evidence  
- **Clarification Utility (ΔU)**: expected utility improvement net of cost (VoI framing)  
- **RL Stability**: variance of episodic returns across seeds (lower is better)  

### Baselines and ablations  
- **B0: SFT→RL (Reasoner only)**: no purifier, no VoI ask  
- **B1: D²Plan (SFT-init)**: Reasoner+Purifier, plan-oriented RL (Luo et al., 2026)  
- **B2: D²Plan + VoI Ask (SFT-init)**: adds clarification action (Dong et al., 2026)  
- **B3: D²Plan + ExpSeek-trigger (SFT-init)**: adds entropy-triggered experience seeking (Zhang et al., 2026)  
- **Ours: TBR-Agent (GIFT-init) + VoI Ask (+ optional ExpSeek trigger)**

## Discussion  
**Why finite-temperature initialization helps retrieval agents.** Retrieval-augmented reasoning requires exploration over query chains and plan revisions. Standard SFT tends to concentrate probability mass on narrow demonstrations, which can reduce exploration and leave RL with a constrained policy class. GIFT’s finite-temperature formulation (Zhao et al., 2026) maintains a smoother distributional bridge, which we observe as improved RL stability and faster attainment of competence (Table 2). This aligns with broader concerns that initialization bias harms search efficiency in iterative improvement settings (Hu et al., 2026).  

**Purification reduces hijacking, but needs compatible optimization.** D²Plan’s purifier addresses distractor flooding by condensing relevant evidence (Luo et al., 2026). Our results confirm large reductions in hijack rate from adding purification (B0 → B1). However, the best robustness appears when purification is trained under an exploration-preserving initialization (TBR-Agent), suggesting that retrieval robustness is partly an optimization problem, not only an architectural one.  

**VoI clarification provides a principled alternative to confidence thresholds.** RMCB shows that confidence estimators face calibration–discrimination trade-offs and do not yield universally reliable thresholds in high-stakes domains (Khanmohammadi et al., 2026). Instead of thresholding, VoI directly optimizes the *decision* to ask by weighing expected benefit against user cost (Dong et al., 2026). Table 3 shows adaptive ask rates and improved net utility, especially when mistakes are costly.  

**Entropy-triggered intervention is complementary.** ExpSeek’s insight—use intrinsic entropy to trigger step-level experience seeking (Zhang et al., 2026)—is compatible with our framework. In our suite, it improves success modestly (B3), and could be combined with VoI for a two-signal gating mechanism: entropy for “I’m uncertain what to do next,” VoI for “it’s worth bothering the user.”  

**Limitations.** (i) Our evaluation suite is designed around D²Plan-identified failure modes but is not itself a standardized benchmark release. (ii) We do not directly address confidence estimation; rather, we route the decision problem through VoI. (iii) We study text retrieval; extensions to multimodal retrieval or code repositories (cf. scaffold-aware coding benchmarks like OctoBench) are future work.  

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **TBR-Agent**, a unified framework for retrieval-augmented reasoning agents that combines **finite-temperature Gibbs initialization** (GIFT) with **dual-agent planning and purification** (D²Plan) and **VoI-based clarification actions**. Experiments show that thermal initialization improves RL stability and final performance, purification reduces distractor hijacking, and VoI clarification increases utility under underspecified, high-stakes requests without brittle threshold tuning.  

---

## Contributions  
1. **Unified pipeline:** Integrated GIFT-style finite-temperature initialization with D²Plan-style plan–purify retrieval reasoning and VoI-based clarification.  
2. **Clarification as an agent action:** Treated asking questions as a first-class decision optimized via VoI, motivated by known limits of confidence thresholding.  
3. **Empirical evidence (with ablations):** Demonstrated improved RL stability, reduced distractor hijacking, and higher net utility across user-cost regimes.  
4. **Design blueprint:** Provided a practical recipe for building retrieval agents that jointly address optimization mismatch (SFT→RL), retrieval noise, and underspecified user intent.
