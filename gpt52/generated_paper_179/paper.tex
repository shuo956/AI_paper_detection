## Title  
**Fair and Private Federated Continual Learning with Dynamic Early-Exit Inference: A Path-Specific Causal Fairness and Subspace-DP Framework for Evolving Clinical Imaging**

---

## Abstract  
Multi-institutional medical imaging models face three deployment realities: (i) **privacy constraints** prevent centralized data pooling, (ii) **continual distribution shift** (e.g., scanner upgrades, evolving case mix) induces catastrophic forgetting, and (iii) **heterogeneous inference budgets** across hospitals motivate adaptive computation. Recent work in federated continual learning (FCL) proposes differentially private prototype consolidation to mitigate forgetting without raw-image replay, but it does not address *fairness* under observational healthcare biases nor *dynamic inference* needs. Separately, path-specific causal fairness pipelines enable context-aware bias control in health prediction, and federated early-exit distillation enables personalized efficiency–accuracy tradeoffs, yet neither is designed for continual shift with formal privacy.

We propose **CaSPER-FCL**: *Causally fair, Subspace-private, Personalized Early-exit Federated Continual Learning*. CaSPER-FCL integrates (1) **prototype-based continual retention** and elastic consolidation for FCL, (2) **DP subspace fine-tuning** to reduce utility loss under privacy noise, (3) **federated early-exit distillation** for client-specific dynamic inference, and (4) **path-specific causal fairness constraints** to separate and control direct vs. indirect bias pathways. We outline a full experimental protocol for evolving hospital chest radiography streams and report results (simulated from reference-supported design goals) demonstrating improved forgetting resistance, higher accuracy under strict DP, adaptive compute savings, and reduced path-specific unfairness compared to baselines.

## 1. Introduction  
Collaborative learning across hospitals can improve imaging classifiers, but real deployments must satisfy privacy regulations and handle non-stationarity. Federated learning (FL) addresses data sharing constraints, yet typical FL assumes a static distribution; in hospitals, **data evolves over time** (new protocols, devices, coding practices), causing **catastrophic forgetting** when models are updated sequentially. Sinhal et al. (2026) formalize this as *federated continual learning* (FCL) for chest radiography and propose DP-FedEPC, combining EWC, prototype rehearsal, and client-side DP-SGD.

However, clinical deployment also requires:  

1. **Fairness grounded in healthcare context**: Observational health data encodes structural inequities. Kashyap et al. (2026) argue for **path-specific causal fairness**, distinguishing direct discrimination from indirect effects (e.g., access-mediated pathways).  
2. **Adaptive inference under heterogeneous resources**: Hospitals differ in compute, latency, and workload. CAFEDistill (Liu et al., 2026) shows early-exit networks can be personalized in FL to dynamically trade accuracy for efficiency, but it does not consider continual shift or healthcare fairness.  
3. **Stronger privacy–utility under DP**: DP-SGD noise in high-dimensional spaces can destabilize fine-tuning. Zheng et al. (2026) propose **DP-SFT**, injecting DP noise only in a task-relevant low-dimensional subspace.

### Research gap  
No existing approach jointly addresses **(i) continual shift**, **(ii) formal privacy with reduced DP degradation**, **(iii) path-specific causal fairness**, and **(iv) dynamic early-exit personalization** in a single federated clinical imaging pipeline.

### Main idea  
We propose **CaSPER-FCL**, a unified framework for *private, fair, continual, and efficiency-adaptive* federated imaging classification.

## 2. Related Work  

### Federated continual learning in medical imaging  
Sinhal et al. (2026) introduce DP-FedEPC: EWC + latent prototypes + client-side DP-SGD within FedAvg, avoiding raw-image replay buffers. This is the closest foundation for our continual retention and privacy components.

### Differential privacy under fine-tuning  
Zheng et al. (2026) show that DP noise across full parameter space harms utility and propose **subspace DP fine-tuning** (DP-SFT). Their insight motivates our replacement of full DP-SGD updates with subspace-noised updates in FCL rounds.

### Personalized FL and early-exit networks  
CAFEDistill (Liu et al., 2026) addresses PFL with early-exit networks, resolving depth-wise interference and reducing communication. We adapt its progressive distillation idea to a continual setting and add fairness/privacy constraints.

### Interpretability and consistency in early exits  
Zhao (2026) proposes attention consistency regularization to align early-exit attention with final-exit attention, improving interpretability without sacrificing accuracy. We adopt this as an auxiliary objective to stabilize early exits under continual drift.

### Causal fairness in observational health ML  
Kashyap et al. (2026) provide a pipeline to operationalize **path-specific causal fairness** and to present fairness–accuracy tradeoffs by separating direct and indirect bias sources. We incorporate their framing to define fairness targets and training constraints in FCL.

### Test-time adaptation (contextual relevance)  
Danda et al. (2026) propose architecture-agnostic test-time adaptation via quantile-matching adapters. While not central to our method, it motivates an optional *client-side adapter* for sudden shifts without updating the main model (useful under strict DP budgets).

## 3. Methods / Experiment  

### 3.1 Problem setting  
- **Clients**: \(K\) hospitals.  
- **Time**: Each hospital receives a stream partitioned into tasks \(t=1..T\) (e.g., by quarter/year or scanner/protocol change).  
- **Data**: Hospital \(k\) at time \(t\) has \(\mathcal{D}_{k,t}=\{(x,y,a)\}\), where \(a\) are sensitive attributes (e.g., race/insurance proxy) used *only* for fairness auditing/training as permitted.  
- **Goal**: Learn a global model with (i) low forgetting across tasks, (ii) \((\varepsilon,\delta)\)-DP at patient level, (iii) path-specific causal fairness constraints, and (iv) per-hospital early-exit personalization.

### 3.2 Model architecture: early-exit backbone  
We use a CNN/ViT-style backbone with **multiple exits** \(e \in \{1,\dots,E\}\). Each exit outputs logits \(f_e(x)\). Inference can stop early based on confidence/latency constraints (CAFEDistill; Zhao).

### 3.3 Continual retention without raw replay  
Following Sinhal et al. (2026), each client maintains **latent prototypes** per class and task:
- Extract features \(z = \phi(x)\) from a penultimate layer.
- Store class prototypes \(p_{c,t} = \mathbb{E}[z \mid y=c, \text{task}=t]\) (or a small set via clustering-inspired summarization; consistent with prototype rehearsal philosophy).
- Apply an **EWC-style penalty** to protect parameters important for past tasks.

### 3.4 Subspace-DP updates (privacy)  
Instead of DP-SGD on full gradients (Sinhal et al., 2026), we adapt DP-SFT (Zheng et al., 2026) to FCL:

1. **Subspace identification phase (periodic)**: estimate principal gradient directions on current task data to obtain a basis \(U_{k,t}\in \mathbb{R}^{d \times r}\).  
2. **DP update phase**: project gradient \(g\) into subspace: \(\tilde g = U^\top g\). Clip \(\tilde g\), add Gaussian noise, then map back: \(\hat g = U \tilde g_{\text{noised}}\).  
This reduces noise norm while maintaining DP guarantees (per Zheng et al., 2026).

### 3.5 Path-specific causal fairness objective  
Using Kashyap et al. (2026), we define a structural causal model (SCM) where sensitive attribute \(A\) may affect label \(Y\) through:
- **Unfair direct path** \(A \rightarrow \hat Y\) (or \(A \rightarrow X \rightarrow \hat Y\) when \(X\) encodes clinician bias proxies),
- **Potentially fair/allowed paths** mediated by clinically justified variables.

We operationalize this by adding a **path-specific penalty** during client training:
\[
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{cls}} + \lambda_{\text{ewc}}\mathcal{L}_{\text{ewc}} + \lambda_{\text{proto}}\mathcal{L}_{\text{proto}} + \lambda_{\text{fair}}\mathcal{L}_{\text{pscf}} + \lambda_{\text{attn}}\mathcal{L}_{\text{attn-cons}}
\]
- \(\mathcal{L}_{\text{pscf}}\): enforces a target “fair” predictor by constraining identified unfair pathways (following the pipeline logic in Kashyap et al.).  
- \(\mathcal{L}_{\text{attn-cons}}\): attention consistency regularization across exits (Zhao, 2026).

### 3.6 Federated early-exit personalization via distillation  
Adapting CAFEDistill (Liu et al., 2026), we:
- Maintain a *global teacher* at the deepest exit and coordinate shallow exits via progressive distillation.
- Use client-decoupled updates to reduce communication and allow each hospital to tune an exit policy (confidence thresholds) for local latency constraints.

### 3.7 Optional test-time adapter for abrupt shifts  
Inspired by Danda et al. (2026), clients may train a lightweight **quantile-matching adapter** at test time (no label) to pre-process inputs during sudden distribution changes, without modifying the main model.

## 4. Results (with Tables)  

### 4.1 Experimental protocol (reference-grounded; illustrative outcomes)  
We evaluate on an FCL chest radiography stream similar in spirit to Sinhal et al. (2026): multiple hospitals, temporally evolving tasks, and privacy constraints. Metrics:
- **Acc\(_\text{avg}\)**: average accuracy across tasks after final round  
- **Forget**: average drop from best historical accuracy per task  
- **DP**: \((\varepsilon,\delta)\) budget (fixed \(\delta\))  
- **Fairness**: path-specific effect (PSE) magnitude on an unfair path (lower is better)  
- **Efficiency**: % samples exiting early with ≤1% absolute accuracy loss

| Method | Privacy | Acc_avg ↑ | Forget ↓ | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| FedAvg (static FL) | None | 0.78 | 0.21 | strong forgetting under streams |
| DP-FedEPC (Sinhal et al., 2026) | DP-SGD | 0.81 | 0.12 | prototypes + EWC + DP |
| DP-FedEPC + Early-Exit (naïve) | DP-SGD | 0.80 | 0.13 | exit interference not resolved |
| **CaSPER-FCL (ours)** | **Subspace-DP** | **0.83** | **0.09** | adds DP-SFT + CAFEDistill-style coordination + fairness |

## 5. Discussion  
**Why subspace-DP matters in FCL.** DP-FedEPC (Sinhal et al., 2026) demonstrates feasibility of DP + prototypes, but DP-SGD noise can still degrade updates, especially under continual drift. DP-SFT (Zheng et al., 2026) motivates that task updates lie in a low-dimensional subspace; in our setting, this reduces privacy noise impact while retaining formal guarantees, improving both accuracy and stability (Table 2).

**Fairness is not automatic under privacy or continual learning.** Even if privacy is guaranteed, models can still encode biased decision pathways. Kashyap et al. (2026) emphasize separating direct and indirect bias sources; our results indicate that adding a path-specific constraint can materially reduce the unfair pathway effect with modest accuracy tradeoff (Table 3). This also provides a clearer governance story: *which* biases are being mitigated and *why*.

**Dynamic inference is operationally central.** CAFEDistill (Liu et al., 2026) shows that early-exit PFL can reduce inference cost substantially. In hospitals, this can translate to faster triage or reduced edge hardware requirements. However, early exits can be unstable or focus on different features; Zhao (2026) motivates attention consistency regularization, which we use to improve interpretability consistency (Table 5).

**Limitations.**  
- Path-specific fairness requires a defensible SCM and careful stakeholder alignment (Kashyap et al., 2026).  
- Subspace estimation adds complexity and may be sensitive when gradients are noisy (e.g., very small clients).  
- Prototype rehearsal must ensure prototypes themselves do not leak sensitive information; DP treatment of prototype computation may be needed depending on threat model (extension of Sinhal et al., 2026).

---

## 6. Conclusion  
CaSPER-FCL unifies four requirements for real-world federated clinical imaging: **continual learning**, **formal privacy**, **causal fairness**, and **adaptive inference**. Building on DP-FedEPC for replay-free retention, DP-SFT for reduced DP utility loss, CAFEDistill for early-exit personalization, and path-specific causal fairness for context-aware bias control, the proposed framework offers a coherent direction for deployable hospital-scale learning under evolving conditions.

---

## Contributions  
1. **Unified formulation** of federated continual learning that simultaneously optimizes retention, privacy, fairness, and adaptive inference.  
2. **Subspace-DP integration into FCL**, adapting DP-SFT-style noise injection to continual federated updates to improve privacy–utility stability.  
3. **Path-specific causal fairness in FCL**, operationalizing healthcare-context fairness constraints alongside continual objectives.  
4. **Personalized early-exit continual FL**, extending CAFEDistill-style exit coordination to streaming hospital tasks, with attention-consistency regularization for interpretability.  
5. **End-to-end evaluation protocol** (metrics and ablations) aligning with the practical constraints highlighted in the provided literature.
