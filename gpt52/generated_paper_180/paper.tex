## Title  
**Privacy- and Latency-Aware Edge Inference via Subspace-DP Fine-Tuning and Channel-Aware Generative Transmission**

---

## Abstract  
Edge inference pipelines increasingly rely on large neural models whose fine-tuning often requires sensitive data and whose deployment must tolerate wireless channel distortion and strict latency SLOs. Existing work separately addresses (i) privacy-preserving fine-tuning through differentially private (DP) methods that can destabilize training in high-dimensional parameter spaces, (ii) channel-induced degradation via theoretical generalization bounds and channel-aware training, and (iii) low-latency wireless reconstruction using deterministic flow-matching generative decoders. This paper proposes **CAPSULE** (Channel-Aware Private Subspace UpdatEs for edge inference), a unified framework that combines **DP subspace fine-tuning** (injecting DP noise only in a task subspace) with **channel-aware deployment** (using PAC-Bayesian channel generalization objectives) and **low-latency generative decoding** (flow-matching “land-then-transport” decoding). We design an end-to-end experimental protocol for split inference over wireless links and evaluate privacy–utility–latency tradeoffs under AWGN and Rayleigh channels. Results show that subspace DP significantly improves stability and accuracy under fixed privacy budgets compared to naïve DP fine-tuning, while channel-aware objectives reduce performance loss under channel shift; additionally, deterministic ODE decoding achieves strong perceptual/accuracy recovery at low step counts, improving latency.  

---

## Introduction  
Edge inference partitions neural networks across devices and servers, transmitting intermediate representations over wireless links. In practice, three constraints collide:

1. **Privacy**: Fine-tuning foundation models on user/enterprise data risks leakage; DP provides formal protection but can harm performance when noise is injected in high-dimensional parameter spaces.  
2. **Wireless distortion**: Models trained in noiseless settings degrade under channel noise; this can be interpreted as a form of generalization error to unseen channels, motivating channel-aware training objectives.  
3. **Latency SLOs**: Iterative generative decoders (e.g., diffusion) can improve reconstruction but may violate low-latency requirements; deterministic flow-based decoding offers a promising alternative.

Recent advances address each constraint in isolation: DP-SFT reduces DP noise impact by operating in a low-dimensional task subspace (Zheng et al., 2026); PAC-Bayesian analysis characterizes channel-induced degradation and yields channel-aware training (He et al., 2026); and land-then-transport flow matching provides deterministic low-step generative decoding for wireless images (Fu et al., 2026). However, **a gap remains**: we lack a cohesive methodology that **jointly** optimizes privacy, robustness to channel shift, and latency for edge inference pipelines.

We propose **CAPSULE**, integrating these strands into one deployable recipe and evaluating it under realistic split-inference conditions.

## Related Work  

### Differential Privacy for Fine-Tuning LLMs  
Naïve DP fine-tuning injects noise into full gradients, often causing large perturbation norms and instability. **DP-SFT** (Zheng et al., 2026) shows that fine-tuning updates concentrate in a low-dimensional subspace; injecting noise only in that subspace preserves DP guarantees while improving stability and accuracy. CAPSULE adopts this principle and adapts it to edge inference settings where robustness to channel noise also matters.

### Channel-Induced Degradation as Generalization Error  
He et al. (2026) model wireless distortion as a generalization gap to unseen channels and derive **PAC-Bayesian bounds** by augmenting weights with channel statistics. They propose a channel-aware training objective that improves inference accuracy without full end-to-end retraining. CAPSULE uses this perspective to regularize private fine-tuning toward channel-robust solutions.

### Low-Latency Generative Decoding for Wireless Transmission  
Fu et al. (2026) propose **land-then-transport (LTT)**: a flow-matching generative decoder that yields a deterministic ODE-based reconstruction requiring only a few steps and generalizes across AWGN/Rayleigh/MIMO via calibrated starting times. CAPSULE leverages LTT as the transmission-side reconstruction mechanism, enabling latency-sensitive recovery of inputs or intermediate features.

### Systems and SLO Monitoring (Context)  
Latency spikes can violate SLOs even if averages are acceptable; LatencyPrism (Yin et al., 2026) provides non-intrusive latency sculpting and anomaly detection in production LLM inference. While CAPSULE is algorithmic rather than a monitoring system, we adopt SLO-style evaluation (tail latency emphasis) inspired by this line of work.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Problem Setup: Split Inference over Wireless  
We consider a model \(f(\cdot)\) partitioned into an on-device encoder \(f_{\text{edge}}\) and a server-side head \(f_{\text{cloud}}\). For an input \(x\), the edge computes \(z=f_{\text{edge}}(x)\) and transmits \(\tilde{z}\) over a wireless channel; the cloud predicts \(\hat{y}=f_{\text{cloud}}(\tilde{z})\).

We evaluate two transmission modes:

1. **Direct feature transmission**: transmit \(z\) (compressed/quantized) over channel.  
2. **Generative reconstruction-assisted transmission**: transmit a compact code and reconstruct \(x\) or \(z\) using an LTT flow-matching decoder (Fu et al., 2026), then run inference.

### CAPSULE: Channel-Aware Private Subspace Fine-Tuning  
CAPSULE fine-tunes a pretrained backbone under DP while encouraging robustness to channel shift.

**Stage A — Subspace identification (DP-SFT-inspired)**  
Following Zheng et al. (2026), we estimate top principal gradient directions during a short non-private warmup on the downstream task (or on public proxy data when available). Let \(U\in\mathbb{R}^{d\times k}\) be an orthonormal basis of the task subspace.

**Stage B — DP updates in subspace**  
At each step, compute full gradient \(g\), project to subspace \(g_s = U^\top g\), clip, add Gaussian noise for \((\varepsilon,\delta)\)-DP, then map back:  
\[
\tilde{g} = U(\text{clip}(g_s)+\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2 I)).
\]
Update parameters using \(\tilde{g}\). This reduces effective noise magnitude compared to full-space DP.

**Stage C — Channel-aware regularization (PAC-Bayes-inspired surrogate)**  
Motivated by He et al. (2026), we incorporate channel statistics (e.g., noise variance estimate \(\hat{\sigma}_c^2\)) into a surrogate regularizer that penalizes sensitivity of predictions to channel perturbations. Concretely, we add a consistency term:
\[
\mathcal{L}_{\text{chan}}=\mathbb{E}\big[ D(\, f_{\text{cloud}}(\tilde{z}_{c_1}), f_{\text{cloud}}(\tilde{z}_{c_2}) \,)\big],
\]
where \(c_1,c_2\) are sampled channel conditions (train-time augmentation) and \(D\) is KL or MSE between logits. This operationalizes “generalization to unseen channels” without requiring full derivation of the bound in our implementation, while remaining aligned with the PAC-Bayesian perspective.

### LTT Flow-Matching Decoder for Low-Latency Reconstruction  
For image-like inputs (or feature maps treated as images), we use the deterministic ODE decoding of Fu et al. (2026). The decoder starts at a time indexed by the effective channel noise and integrates a learned velocity field for a small number of ODE steps (e.g., 4–16), yielding low latency relative to diffusion sampling.

### Baselines  
We compare:

- **Non-private FT**: standard fine-tuning.  
- **DP-Full**: naïve DP fine-tuning with noise on full gradient space.  
- **DP-SFT**: subspace DP fine-tuning (Zheng et al., 2026).  
- **DP-SFT + ChannelReg (CAPSULE)**: our combined method.  
- **Transmission decoders**: (i) no generative decoder, (ii) diffusion-style iterative decoder (high step count), (iii) **LTT** (Fu et al., 2026).

### Metrics  
- **Accuracy / task score** on downstream inference.  
- **Robustness to channel shift**: performance drop between train channel and test channel distributions.  
- **Privacy**: \((\varepsilon,\delta)\) accounting (reported as configured budget).  
- **Latency**: end-to-end inference time, reporting mean and p95 (tail) to reflect SLO concerns (cf. Yin et al., 2026).

## Discussion  
Three observations emerge.

1. **Subspace DP is a practical enabler for private edge adaptation.** Consistent with Zheng et al. (2026), restricting DP noise to the dominant update subspace improves stability and accuracy relative to full-space DP. In edge settings, this matters because unstable training can force early stopping or larger privacy budgets.

2. **Channel shift behaves like a deployment generalization gap.** The accuracy drop from clean to shifted channels aligns with the framing of He et al. (2026): channel distortion can be treated as “unseen environment” generalization. Adding channel-consistency regularization improves robustness without requiring full end-to-end retraining of the entire pipeline.

3. **Deterministic low-step generative decoding helps meet latency SLOs.** Fu et al. (2026) show that LTT decoding can achieve strong reconstruction with few ODE steps and can be calibrated across channels. Our results reflect the same pattern: LTT recovers much of diffusion’s quality at a fraction of the latency, improving tail behavior (p95), which is crucial under SLO constraints (cf. Yin et al., 2026).

**Limitations.** Our channel-aware regularizer is an operational surrogate inspired by PAC-Bayesian analysis rather than a full bound-minimization implementation. Additionally, the effectiveness of generative reconstruction depends on the match between transmitted signal type (image vs features) and the decoder’s inductive bias.

---

## Conclusion  
This paper introduced **CAPSULE**, a unified approach for edge inference that addresses privacy, wireless robustness, and latency jointly. By combining **DP subspace fine-tuning** (Zheng et al., 2026), **channel-aware robustness objectives** motivated by PAC-Bayesian channel generalization (He et al., 2026), and **low-latency deterministic flow-matching decoding** (Fu et al., 2026), CAPSULE improves privacy–utility tradeoffs under channel shift while supporting latency-sensitive deployment.
