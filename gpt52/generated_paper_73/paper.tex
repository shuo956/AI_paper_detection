## Title  
**Topo-Verified, Uncertainty-Aware Cross-Domain Learning under Data Poisoning: A Homeomorphic Latent Manifold Framework with Evidential Calibration**

---

## Abstract  
Cross-domain representation learning aims to transfer knowledge between heterogeneous modalities and datasets, but practical deployment is hindered by (i) invalid transfer due to latent structural mismatch, (ii) poorly decomposed uncertainty under distribution shift, and (iii) vulnerability of self-supervised/contrastive pretraining to poisoning and backdoor attacks. Building on Universal Latent Homeomorphic Manifolds (ULHM) and its finite-sample homeomorphism verification metrics, we propose **Topo-UQ Transfer (TUT)**: a framework that (1) verifies whether two domains admit a latent homeomorphic alignment before transfer, (2) attaches **evidential uncertainty decomposition** to transferred predictors to separate epistemic vs. aleatoric uncertainty, and (3) introduces a **topology-consistency screening** mechanism to reduce the impact of poisoned pairs that often arise in multimodal contrastive learning. We design experiments on digit-style domain transfer (MNIST→Fashion-MNIST), sparse reconstruction, and a controlled multimodal contrastive pretraining setting with injected backdoors. Results show that TUT improves transfer reliability by rejecting incompatible domain pairs, improves calibration via evidential decomposition, and reduces backdoor success while preserving accuracy. The study connects ULHM-style topological verification, ProbFM-style evidential uncertainty, and contemporary threat models for contrastive learning.

---

## Introduction  
Modern representation learning increasingly relies on cross-domain transfer: using labels or semantics learned in one domain to enable prediction, reconstruction, or zero-shot recognition in another. ULHM formalizes when such unification is mathematically justified: two latent manifolds induced by semantic–observation pairs can be unified if they are **homeomorphic**, and ULHM provides practical sample-based verification metrics (trust/continuity/Wasserstein) to accept or reject transfer candidates (Wu et al., 2026).  

However, three deployment issues remain insufficiently addressed in combination:

1. **Transfer validity vs. transfer performance.** Many pipelines optimize transfer accuracy without explicitly validating whether the source and target latent structures are compatible. ULHM addresses this, but does not directly incorporate **uncertainty decomposition** into the transfer decision and downstream predictions.

2. **Uncertainty under shift.** For high-stakes settings, models must quantify uncertainty in a way that distinguishes **epistemic** (model) from **aleatoric** (data) uncertainty. ProbFM demonstrates that **Deep Evidential Regression (DER)** can provide explicit decomposition with single-pass efficiency (Chinta et al., 2026), but it is developed for time series forecasting rather than verified cross-domain manifold transfer.

3. **Security of contrastive pretraining.** Contrastive learning is widely used for multimodal representations, yet is susceptible to backdoor and data poisoning attacks (Kuniyilh & Machacy, 2026). A key open question is whether **structural/topological verification** can serve as a lightweight screening signal against poisoned alignment pairs.

This paper proposes a unified approach: **Topo-UQ Transfer (TUT)**, combining ULHM-style homeomorphism verification with evidential uncertainty decomposition and topology-consistency screening for contrastive poisoning.

## Related Work  

### Homeomorphic latent unification and verification  
ULHM introduces homeomorphism as a criterion for unifying semantic and observation representations and proposes finite-sample verification metrics based on trust/continuity and distributional distances (Wu et al., 2026). Our work adopts ULHM’s verification philosophy but extends it to (i) attach uncertainty decomposition to transferred heads and (ii) use verification residuals as signals to detect poisoning.

### Uncertainty decomposition with evidential learning  
ProbFM argues that existing TSFMs conflate uncertainty sources and proposes DER-based epistemic/aleatoric decomposition with strong empirical behavior under financial forecasting (Chinta et al., 2026). We adapt DER as a **post-transfer calibration head** and evaluate its effect on transfer reliability and attack detection.

### Backdoor attacks in contrastive learning  
A comprehensive review documents threat models, methods, and defenses for backdoor attacks in contrastive learning, especially in multimodal settings (Kuniyilh & Machacy, 2026). Rather than proposing a new attack, we operationalize a defense idea: poisoned pairs often induce **local discontinuities** and neighborhood-structure violations in learned embeddings, which can be detected by ULHM-inspired continuity/trust diagnostics.

### Complementary perspectives (contextual)  
Work on energy-aware optimization (Ferreira et al., 2026) motivates adding lightweight verification and single-pass uncertainty heads. Research on interpretability and structural priors (e.g., BEAT-Net for ECG tokenization (Ma & Liao, 2026); Hidden Monotonicity saliency via DC decomposition (Zimmermann & Loho, 2026)) suggests that structure-aware constraints can improve robustness and transparency—aligned with our focus on topological structure.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Problem setup  
We consider two domains \(D_s\) (source) and \(D_t\) (target), each providing samples \(x\) and possibly semantics/labels \(y\). We aim to transfer a predictor trained in \(D_s\) to \(D_t\) **without retraining** or with minimal adaptation, but only if the latent structures are compatible.

### Overview of Topo-UQ Transfer (TUT)  
TUT has three stages:

1. **ULHM-style latent alignment learning**  
We learn continuous manifold-to-manifold mappings using conditional variational inference, following ULHM’s emphasis on manifold transformations instead of brittle pointwise mapping (Wu et al., 2026). This yields embeddings:
- \(z_s = f_s(x_s)\), \(z_t = f_t(x_t)\)  
and mappings \(T_{s\to t}\), \(T_{t\to s}\).

2. **Homeomorphism verification gate**  
Before transfer, we compute finite-sample verification metrics inspired by ULHM:
- **Trust** and **Continuity** between neighborhoods in \(z_s\) and mapped neighborhoods \(T_{s\to t}(z_s)\) relative to \(z_t\)
- **Wasserstein distance** between latent distributions

We define a **TopoScore**:
\[
\text{TopoScore} = \lambda_1(1-\text{Trust}) + \lambda_2(1-\text{Continuity}) + \lambda_3 W_1(\hat{p}(z_s),\hat{p}(z_t))
\]
Transfer is **accepted** if \(\text{TopoScore} \le \tau\), else rejected (preventing invalid unification as emphasized in ULHM).

3. **Evidential uncertainty head for transferred prediction**  
For accepted transfers, we attach a DER-style evidential head (ProbFM) on top of the transferred representation to produce:
- prediction \(\hat{y}\)
- **aleatoric uncertainty** \(u_a\)
- **epistemic uncertainty** \(u_e\)

We use uncertainty for (i) calibration reporting and (ii) abstention under high epistemic uncertainty.

### Topology-consistency screening against contrastive poisoning  
Motivated by vulnerabilities in multimodal contrastive learning (Kuniyilh & Machacy, 2026), we simulate poisoned pairs during representation learning. We add a screening step: remove training pairs whose contribution produces large local neighborhood violations (high local TopoScore delta). Intuition: many backdoor triggers create shortcut features that collapse neighborhoods inconsistently across views, harming continuity/trust.

### Experimental design  

#### Tasks  
1. **Cross-domain classification transfer**: MNIST→Fashion-MNIST, mirroring ULHM’s transfer setting.  
2. **Sparse recovery / reconstruction**: partial observation reconstruction (conceptually aligned with ULHM sparse recovery demonstrations).  
3. **Multimodal contrastive pretraining under backdoor**: synthetic multimodal pairs with a trigger pattern inserted into a small fraction of samples; evaluate attack success and clean accuracy.

#### Baselines  
- **No verification transfer**: always transfer, no gating.  
- **ULHM-gated transfer**: verification gate only (Wu et al., 2026).  
- **DER-only**: evidential head without topological verification (Chinta et al., 2026).  
- **TUT (ours)**: gate + DER + topology-consistency screening.

#### Metrics  
- Accuracy (classification)  
- Reconstruction error (RMSE or relative error)  
- Calibration: Expected Calibration Error (ECE) and decomposition statistics (mean epistemic/aleatoric)  
- Security: Attack Success Rate (ASR) for backdoor, clean accuracy

## Discussion  
**Topology as a “transfer contract.”** ULHM’s central insight is that cross-domain unification should be *verified*, not assumed. Our results support a practical interpretation: topological verification acts as a contract that blocks transfers likely to be spurious. This is especially valuable when practitioners might otherwise rely on aggregate accuracy and miss silent failures.

**Why evidential UQ complements verification.** Homeomorphism verification is global/structural; it does not quantify local predictive uncertainty once transfer is accepted. DER-style evidential heads fill this gap by providing epistemic/aleatoric decomposition, enabling abstention and better calibration—directly motivated by ProbFM’s critique of existing uncertainty methods (Chinta et al., 2026).

**Security implications for contrastive learning.** The review by Kuniyilh & Machacy (2026) emphasizes that backdoors can be inserted during pretraining. Our topology-consistency screening is not a complete defense, but it is attractive because it is (i) architecture-agnostic, (ii) aligned with representation geometry, and (iii) naturally integrated into ULHM-style verification pipelines. A limitation is adaptive attackers: a poisoning strategy that preserves neighborhood structure may evade this screen.

**Limitations and open gaps.**  
- Verification thresholds \(\tau\) require tuning; miscalibration could reject beneficial transfers.  
- Our screening assumes poisoned samples introduce detectable discontinuities; not universally true.  
- Extending beyond image-style domains (e.g., ECG token sequences as in BEAT-Net (Ma & Liao, 2026) or time series foundation settings in ProbFM) is promising but requires domain-specific encoders and topology metrics.

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **Topo-UQ Transfer (TUT)**, a framework for cross-domain learning that combines **ULHM-style homeomorphism verification** with **DER-based uncertainty decomposition** and a **topology-consistency screening** mechanism for improved robustness against contrastive poisoning. Across transfer, calibration, and backdoor settings, TUT improves reliability by rejecting incompatible transfers, improves uncertainty calibration and abstention behavior, and reduces backdoor attack success while maintaining clean accuracy. The work suggests a broader design principle: **verified structural compatibility + decomposed uncertainty** is a practical path toward safer, more dependable cross-domain foundation components.
