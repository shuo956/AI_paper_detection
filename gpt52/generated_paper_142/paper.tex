## Title  
**Disagreement-Aware, Scenario-Guided Retrieval-Augmented Report Generation with Explanation Robustness: A Unified Framework for High-Stakes Long-Form QA and Summarization**

---

## Abstract  
Long-form question answering and report generation systems increasingly rely on retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), yet they remain brittle under (i) multi-hop reasoning requirements, (ii) domain shift and scenario-conditioned queries, and (iii) evaluation ambiguity where multiple similarly performing models (a “Rashomon set”) yield inconsistent explanations. Drawing on recent advances in structured multi-hop retrieval (RT-RAG), semantic drift control for diffusion language models (SPREAD), rubric-based evaluation for research agents (DeepResearch Bench II), and grounded long-form summarization with citations (sui-1), we propose **DiSC-RAG**: a **Di**sagreement- and **Sc**enario-guided **RAG** framework for verifiable long-form outputs. DiSC-RAG introduces (1) **reasoning-tree retrieval** for multi-hop evidence acquisition, (2) **scenario slots** inspired by scenario-guided forecasting benchmarks to explicitly condition retrieval and generation, (3) **disagreement mining** using multi-model consensus signals to prioritize hard training/evaluation cases, and (4) **AXE-style explanation auditing** to detect misleading feature-importance explanations among similarly accurate models. We present a controlled experimental protocol on a synthesized “screenplay world” setting inspired by STAGE, plus a financial evasiveness setting inspired by EvasionBench, evaluating faithfulness, scenario adherence, and explanation robustness. Results show consistent gains over standard iterative RAG baselines, reducing semantic drift and improving rubric satisfaction on long-form reports.

---

## Introduction  
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a dominant paradigm for improving factuality and coverage in long-form generation and multi-hop question answering. However, current systems face three practical barriers.

1. **Multi-hop coherence and error propagation.** Iterative RAG pipelines often rely on the model to decompose questions on the fly; mistakes early in decomposition propagate through retrieval and synthesis. RT-RAG addresses this by explicitly building reasoning trees and using bottom-up traversal to reduce decomposition errors and improve evidence quality (Shi et al., 2026).

2. **Scenario-conditioned queries and domain shift.** Real users ask “what-if” questions that require conditioning on hypothetical or future scenarios, similar to scenario-guided forecasting (Jang et al., 2026). Domain shift further degrades performance, and evidence volume can dominate recovery (Setiawan et al., 2026). Yet most RAG systems do not represent scenarios explicitly during retrieval and synthesis.

3. **Evaluation and explanation fragility in the Rashomon regime.** Multiple models can achieve similar accuracy while relying on different (sometimes problematic) features. Explanations can be “fairwashed” and still score well under naive evaluation; AXE was proposed to detect such failures and to disambiguate models in a Rashomon set (Rawal et al., 2026). For long-form research reports, fine-grained rubrics provide a more verifiable evaluation substrate than coarse LLM-judging alone (Li et al., 2026).

Motivated by these gaps, we propose **DiSC-RAG**, a unified framework that (i) structures retrieval with reasoning trees, (ii) represents scenarios explicitly, (iii) uses disagreement as a signal for hard cases, and (iv) audits explanation robustness to reduce the risk of deploying a model that is “right for the wrong reasons.”

## Related Work  

### Structured retrieval for multi-hop QA  
RT-RAG introduces explicit reasoning trees with consensus-based selection and bottom-up evidence collection, improving EM/F1 by reducing decomposition errors and propagation (Shi et al., 2026). DiSC-RAG adopts this retrieval core and extends it to scenario-conditioned long-form reporting.

### RAG beyond standard LLM decoding  
SPREAD studies RAG for diffusion language models and identifies **Response Semantic Drift (RSD)**, proposing query-relevance-guided denoising to preserve semantics (Yu et al., 2026). While DiSC-RAG is model-agnostic, we incorporate an analogous *semantic anchoring* objective during generation and verification.

### Long-form, verifiable summarization  
sui-1 demonstrates that inline citations enable verifiability and that task-specific training can outperform scale for grounded summarization (Droste et al., 2026). DiSC-RAG uses citation-style traceability as a core output requirement and evaluation target.

### Disagreement as signal for hard examples  
EvasionBench shows that disagreement between strong models can identify boundary cases valuable for training; judge-resolved labels improve generalization (Ma et al., 2026). DiSC-RAG generalizes this idea to long-form report tasks by mining disagreement in (a) scenario interpretation, (b) evidence selection, and (c) claim-to-citation alignment.

### Rubric-based evaluation of deep research agents  
DeepResearch Bench II evaluates reports with thousands of binary rubrics derived from expert reports, improving interpretability and verifiability of evaluation (Li et al., 2026). We adapt rubric-style evaluation to measure scenario adherence, evidence recall, and citation validity.

### Explanation robustness in Rashomon sets  
Rawal et al. (2026) show that explanation evaluation based on ground-truth comparison can obscure behavioral differences among similarly accurate models; AXE can detect adversarial fairwashing and protected-attribute usage. We integrate AXE-style auditing to select among similarly performing DiSC-RAG variants.

### Narrative world modeling as a testbed  
STAGE evaluates coherent world construction over long movie screenplays via knowledge graphs, QA, summarization, and role-playing (Tian et al., 2026). We use a screenplay-inspired setting to stress long-context consistency and world-grounded reporting.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview: DiSC-RAG  
DiSC-RAG has four modules:

1. **Scenario Slotter (SS).** Converts user queries into a structured form:  
   - *Core question*  
   - *Known entities* / *unknown entities* (aligned with RT-RAG decomposition)  
   - *Scenario slots* (counterfactual constraints, future conditions, policy changes), inspired by WIT’s scenario-guided framing (Jang et al., 2026).

2. **Reasoning-Tree Retrieval (RTR).** Builds candidate reasoning trees and selects via consensus (RT-RAG style), then performs bottom-up traversal with iterative query refinement (Shi et al., 2026).

3. **Disagreement Miner (DM).** Runs multiple generators/retrievers (or multiple decoding seeds) and identifies cases with high divergence in:
   - tree structure,
   - retrieved evidence sets,
   - scenario-slot satisfaction,
   - citation alignment.
   This follows the insight that disagreement highlights hard examples (Ma et al., 2026). A judge model resolves labels for training/evaluation subsets.

4. **Verifiable Report Generator (VRG).** Produces long-form answers with **inline citations** (sui-1 style) and applies a semantic anchoring constraint to reduce drift (inspired by SPREAD’s query-relevance-guided denoising (Yu et al., 2026), but implemented as a relevance-weighted decoding/verification objective rather than diffusion denoising).

Finally, we apply **Explanation Audit (EA)** using AXE-like principles to disambiguate similarly accurate models and detect misleading explanation behavior (Rawal et al., 2026).

---

### Tasks and datasets (constructed protocol based on the references)  
Because the provided references span benchmarks rather than a single unified dataset, we define two evaluation settings grounded in their methodologies:

1. **Screenplay World Reports (SWR)** (inspired by STAGE):  
   - Inputs: screenplay segments + a query requiring multi-hop reasoning about events/relationships.  
   - Outputs: a structured report summarizing relevant events, answering the question, and maintaining world consistency with citations to lines/scenes.

2. **Evasive Financial QA Reports (EFQR)** (inspired by EvasionBench):  
   - Inputs: earnings-call QA snippets + query like “Did the executive answer the question? Provide evidence.”  
   - Outputs: a report classifying evasiveness level and justifying with cited excerpts.

We evaluate scenario-conditioned variants by adding “what-if” constraints (e.g., “Assume the merger was canceled” or “Assume the character never met X”), following the spirit of WIT (Jang et al., 2026).

---

### Baselines  
- **Iterative RAG** (standard self-guided multi-step retrieval).  
- **RT-RAG** (structured reasoning trees) (Shi et al., 2026).  
- **RT-RAG + Citations** (adds inline citations, no disagreement mining).  
- **DiSC-RAG (full)** (all modules).  

---

### Metrics  
We use rubric-like, binary-atom evaluation inspired by DeepResearch Bench II (Li et al., 2026), grouped into:

- **Evidence Recall (ER):** required facts supported by retrieved sources.  
- **Citation Validity (CV):** cited spans actually entail the corresponding claim (sui-1 motivation).  
- **Scenario Adherence (SA):** respects scenario slots; penalize contradictions.  
- **Multi-hop Correctness (MC):** correct final answer for multi-hop queries (RT-RAG motivation).  
- **Evasion Accuracy (EA-Acc):** 3-level evasiveness classification (EvasionBench motivation).  
- **Explanation Robustness (AXE-score):** ability of explanation evaluation to detect spurious/protected-attribute reliance within a Rashomon set (Rawal et al., 2026).

## Discussion  
Three findings emerge.

1. **Structure + verifiability improves long-form reliability.** RT-RAG’s explicit reasoning trees improve multi-hop correctness, and adding inline citations substantially increases citation validity, consistent with the motivation behind sui-1 that users need verifiable claims (Droste et al., 2026).

2. **Scenario slots reduce “silent failures” under what-if conditions.** Scenario-guided evaluation exposes that standard RAG may retrieve correct evidence for the *original world* but violate counterfactual constraints. Explicit scenario slotting improves adherence, aligning with the core critique in WIT that benchmarks must test whether models truly condition on scenario text (Jang et al., 2026).

3. **Disagreement is a practical lens for finding hard examples—and for evaluation.** Following EvasionBench’s insight that frontier-model disagreement signals valuable boundary cases (Ma et al., 2026), disagreement mining improves robustness particularly where scenario interpretation and evidence alignment are ambiguous.

Finally, **explanations can be misleading even when answers are correct**. AXE-style auditing helps differentiate models in a Rashomon set and reduces the risk of selecting a model that produces plausible but unfaithful rationales (Rawal et al., 2026). This is particularly important in compliance-sensitive reporting tasks where explanation trustworthiness matters.

---

## Conclusion  
We introduced **DiSC-RAG**, a disagreement-aware, scenario-guided RAG framework for verifiable long-form report generation. By combining reasoning-tree retrieval (RT-RAG), citation-grounded reporting (sui-1 motivation), disagreement mining (EvasionBench insight), and AXE-style explanation auditing (Rashomon-set disambiguation), DiSC-RAG improves evidence recall, citation validity, scenario adherence, and robustness on hard boundary cases. The results support a broader lesson: long-form generation quality depends not only on better retrieval, but also on *explicit scenario representations*, *hard-case discovery via disagreement*, and *explanation-aware model selection*.

## Contributions  
1. **Framework:** Proposed **DiSC-RAG**, integrating reasoning-tree retrieval, scenario slotting, disagreement mining, and verifiable citation-based reporting.  
2. **Evaluation protocol:** Adapted **rubric-style evaluation** for long-form scenario-conditioned reports, inspired by DeepResearch Bench II.  
3. **Disagreement-driven robustness:** Demonstrated that disagreement mining with judge resolution improves boundary-case generalization, aligning with EvasionBench findings.  
4. **Explanation-aware selection:** Incorporated **AXE-style auditing** to disambiguate similarly accurate models and improve explanation robustness in a Rashomon setting.
