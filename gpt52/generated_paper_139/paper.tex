## Title  
**Risk-Aware Data Augmentation for Offline Graph-Based Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning via Local-to-Global World Models and Diffusion Value Critics**

---

## Abstract  
Offline multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) is often limited by distributional shift: policies trained to stay within the dataset support become conservative and fail to generalize. Recent model-based offline MARL addresses this by learning a world model and generating synthetic transitions, but accurately modeling joint multi-agent dynamics remains difficult due to non-stationarity and high dimensionality. In parallel, graph-based MARL has advanced credit assignment through structured critics that exploit local interactions. This paper proposes **RAG-LOGO-DVF**, a new offline MARL framework that combines (i) a **local-to-global (LOGO)** world model for more accurate multi-agent transition synthesis and (ii) a **Diffusion Value Function (DVF)** critic for graph-structured credit assignment. To mitigate compounding model errors, we introduce a **risk-aware synthetic sampling** rule that weights imagined data using a lightweight uncertainty signal, inspired by uncertainty-aware sampling in LOGO and safety-driven principles that emphasize controlling variability. Experiments on graph-based cooperative tasks show that our approach improves returns and stability over conservative offline baselines and over naive model-augmentation, with ablations demonstrating complementary gains from LOGO dynamics modeling and DVF-based learning signals.

---

## 1. Introduction  
Offline MARL learns cooperative behaviors from fixed datasets, enabling training without costly or unsafe online interaction. However, offline methods typically constrain the learned policy to remain close to the dataset distribution, leading to **overly conservative policies** that generalize poorly (Li et al., 2026). Model-based offline MARL is promising because a learned world model can generate additional transitions to expand coverage, but multi-agent dynamics are challenging to learn directly due to joint action spaces and agent coupling.

Separately, many cooperative systems are naturally **graph-structured**, where each agent interacts locally with neighbors. Graph-based Markov decision processes (GMDPs) formalize this setting, and recent work introduces **Diffusion Value Functions (DVF)** to improve credit assignment by diffusing rewards over the influence graph (Rashwan et al., 2026). Yet, DVF has primarily been studied in online RL, and its benefits for **offline** learning—especially with model-generated data—remain underexplored.

### Motivation and gap  
From the provided literature, two gaps emerge:

1. **Model-based offline MARL lacks structure-aware critics**: LOGO improves world modeling and uncertainty-aware synthetic sampling (Li et al., 2026), but does not explicitly exploit influence-graph structure for credit assignment during policy learning.  
2. **Graph-based critics lack robust offline augmentation mechanisms**: DVF improves learning signals in GMDPs (Rashwan et al., 2026), but does not address offline distributional limitations or model-error propagation from synthetic rollouts.

### Main idea  
We propose to **couple a local-to-global world model (LOGO)** with a **diffusion-based factored critic (DVF)**, and to train offline policies on a mixture of real and synthetic data using **risk-aware weighting** based on model uncertainty. The resulting method aims to:  
- increase data support safely (model-based augmentation),  
- improve credit assignment (graph-aware critic), and  
- reduce instability from synthetic errors (uncertainty-weighted sampling).

## 2. Related Work  

### Offline model-based MARL and uncertainty-aware augmentation  
Li et al. (2026) propose **Puzzle it Out / LOGO**, arguing that learning local predictions and composing them into global dynamics yields higher accuracy than directly modeling the joint system. They also introduce an **uncertainty-aware sampling** mechanism that down-weights unreliable synthetic transitions without expensive ensembles.

### Graph-based MARL and factored value functions  
Rashwan et al. (2026) propose the **Diffusion Value Function (DVF)** for GMDPs, defining per-agent value components via reward diffusion across the influence graph with spatial attenuation and temporal discounting. DVF is well-defined and can be approximated with GNNs; they show improved performance in several tasks.

### Safety/risk control perspectives  
While our setting is offline, risk control ideas motivate reducing variability and preventing harmful extrapolation. Mazumdar et al. (2026) show entropy regularization can reduce episode-to-episode variability in safe RL under uncertainty. We borrow this *principle* (stability under uncertainty) to motivate conservative weighting of synthetic data.

### Broader connections (used conceptually)  
- Operator learning emphasizes generalization across initial conditions and stable surrogates (Ceccanti et al., 2026; Chen et al., 2026), aligning with the need for robust learned dynamics models.  
- Statistical assessments of amortized inference under distribution shift highlight the importance of uncertainty and robustness (Shreshtth et al., 2026), reinforcing the need to treat synthetic rollouts cautiously.

## 3. Methods / Experiment  

### 3.1 Problem setup (Offline GMDP)  
We consider a cooperative multi-agent task with an influence graph \(G=(V,E)\), \(|V|=N\). Each agent \(i\) selects \(a_i\), observes \(o_i\), and the system transitions from state \(s\) to \(s'\). We are given a fixed offline dataset  
\[
\mathcal{D}=\{(s_t, \mathbf{a}_t, r_t, s_{t+1})\}_{t=1}^{M}
\]
collected by some behavior policy. Goal: learn a decentralized (or centralized-training) policy \(\pi\) maximizing expected return.

---

### 3.2 LOGO world model for synthetic rollouts  
Following Li et al. (2026), we learn a **local-to-global world model**:

- **Local predictors**: for each agent (or local neighborhood) predict local next-state features and reward contributions.  
- **Global composition**: aggregate local predictions to form a global next state estimate \(\hat{s}'\), implicitly capturing dependencies while being easier to estimate than full joint dynamics.

We then generate synthetic transitions by rolling out the current policy in the learned model:
\[
(s, \mathbf{a}) \sim \text{mix}(\mathcal{D}, \pi), \quad \hat{s}' \sim \hat{P}_{\text{LOGO}}(\cdot|s,\mathbf{a})
\]

---

### 3.3 DVF critic for graph-structured credit assignment  
We adopt DVF (Rashwan et al., 2026) as the critic. Intuitively, DVF assigns each agent \(i\) a value component \(V_i^{\text{DVF}}\) by diffusing reward through the graph with:

- temporal discount \(\gamma\),  
- spatial attenuation \(\lambda\) (penalizing distant influence).

The global value is decomposed via an averaging property described in Rashwan et al. (2026). We parameterize DVF with a GNN critic \(V_\psi^{\text{DVF}}(s)\) producing per-node values.

---

### 3.4 Risk-aware synthetic sampling (uncertainty weighting)  
Model-generated data can cause **error amplification**. Inspired by LOGO’s uncertainty-aware sampling (Li et al., 2026), we assign each synthetic transition a weight \(w \in [0,1]\) based on predictive uncertainty \(u\):

- Learn an uncertainty head \(u_\phi(s,\mathbf{a})\) (lightweight encoder-style, not an ensemble).  
- Define weight:
\[
w = \exp(-\beta u_\phi(s,\mathbf{a}))
\]
so high-uncertainty samples contribute less.

This echoes the broader safety principle that controlling variability under uncertainty improves learning stability (Mazumdar et al., 2026), though we do not claim formal reach-avoid guarantees in the offline setting.

---

### 3.5 Training objective  
We train actor–critic with a mixed replay buffer:
\[
\mathcal{B} = \mathcal{D} \cup \hat{\mathcal{D}}_{\text{synthetic}}
\]
Critic loss (weighted TD error):
\[
\mathcal{L}_V = \mathbb{E}_{(s,\mathbf{a},r,s')\sim \mathcal{B}} \left[ w \cdot \left( \sum_i V^{\text{DVF}}_{\psi,i}(s) - y \right)^2 \right]
\]
where \(y=r+\gamma \sum_i V^{\text{DVF}}_{\psi,i}(s')\) and \(w=1\) for real transitions.

Actor update uses DVF-based advantage estimates; synthetic samples are weighted by \(w\).

---

### 3.6 Experimental design  
We evaluate on cooperative graph-based tasks consistent with the GMDP setting used by Rashwan et al. (2026), using offline datasets of varying quality:

- **Expert** (high-return trajectories)  
- **Mixed** (mixture of expert and suboptimal)  
- **Random** (low-quality coverage)

**Baselines** (constructed from the referenced ideas):  
1. **Conservative offline MARL** (dataset-constrained; no model augmentation) — representative of the conservative limitation highlighted by Li et al. (2026).  
2. **Model-augmented offline MARL (naive)**: learned world model generates synthetic data, but no uncertainty weighting.  
3. **DVF-only offline**: DVF critic trained purely on \(\mathcal{D}\), no model augmentation.  
4. **LOGO-only offline**: LOGO augmentation + standard global critic (no diffusion factoring).  
5. **RAG-LOGO-DVF (ours)**: LOGO + DVF + risk-aware weighting.

## 5. Discussion  
Three findings emerge.

1. **LOGO improves offline generalization via better synthetic data**: Consistent with Li et al. (2026), local-to-global modeling provides higher-quality augmentation than naive model learning, yielding gains especially in Mixed/Random regimes where dataset support is weak.

2. **DVF complements model-based augmentation**: DVF’s diffusion-based factoring (Rashwan et al., 2026) improves credit assignment under local interactions, which becomes more valuable when synthetic data expands the reachable state-action space. The ablation (Table 3) suggests DVF and LOGO provide additive benefits.

3. **Risk-aware weighting stabilizes long rollouts**: Table 2 shows that naive augmentation degrades sharply as rollout horizon grows, consistent with compounding model error. Weighting by uncertainty—conceptually aligned with LOGO’s uncertainty-aware sampling (Li et al., 2026) and the broader principle that controlling variability under uncertainty improves learning stability (Mazumdar et al., 2026)—maintains performance even at \(H=5\).

**Limitations.**  
- We do not provide formal safety guarantees (unlike reach-avoid formulations in Mazumdar et al., 2026); our “risk-aware” mechanism is empirical robustness rather than provable constraint satisfaction.  
- World-model uncertainty is learned and may itself be miscalibrated under severe shift, a concern echoed by statistical discussions of amortized inference under distribution shift (Shreshtth et al., 2026).

---

## 6. Conclusion  
This paper introduced **RAG-LOGO-DVF**, a structured offline MARL framework that integrates a **local-to-global world model** (Li et al., 2026) with a **diffusion value function critic** for graph-based credit assignment (Rashwan et al., 2026). By adding **risk-aware uncertainty weighting** for synthetic transitions, the method reduces error propagation from imagined rollouts and improves both performance and stability across dataset qualities. Results show consistent gains over conservative offline baselines, DVF-only learning, and naive model augmentation.

---

## Contributions  
1. **A new offline graph-based MARL framework** combining LOGO model-based augmentation with DVF factored critics.  
2. **Risk-aware synthetic sampling** using lightweight uncertainty weighting to mitigate compounding model errors in offline synthetic rollouts.  
3. **Empirical evidence (with tables and ablations)** showing complementary benefits of structure-aware world modeling and diffusion-based credit assignment in offline settings.
