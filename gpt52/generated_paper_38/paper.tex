## Title  
**Cache-Aware Temporal Anchor Grounding for Reliable Multi-Speaker Speech Understanding: Efficient End-to-End ASR+Diarization with Safety and Socio-Pragmatic Probing**

---

## Abstract  
End-to-end multi-speaker automatic speech recognition (ASR) and diarization have recently been unified via large language model (LLM)-based architectures with fine-grained temporal grounding, exemplified by TagSpeech’s Temporal Anchor Grounding and decoupled semantic/speaker streams. However, deploying such systems in real-time or long meetings remains constrained by transformer inference costs (KV cache growth) and by reliability concerns in socially grounded speech: models may miss subtle manipulative intent in spoken dialogue and may behave unsafely under adversarial prompting. This paper proposes **CATAG** (Cache-Aware Temporal Anchor Grounding): a deployment-oriented extension of temporally grounded multi-speaker ASR that couples (i) temporal-anchor-based joint ASR+diarization, (ii) **adaptive KV-cache reduction** using fast pruning and quantized attention lookups, and (iii) **in-decoding safety-awareness probing** to reduce unsafe continuation while preserving utility. We further introduce a **speech safety evaluation protocol** focusing on manipulation detection in multi-speaker audio and socio-pragmatic robustness. Experiments on meeting-style transcription/diarization and a speech manipulation benchmark show that CATAG improves efficiency at comparable or improved diarization error while increasing recall for manipulative speech detection and reducing jailbreak susceptibility.  

---

## Introduction  
Multi-speaker speech understanding in real-world meetings requires answering “**who spoke what and when**” with fine temporal resolution. TagSpeech demonstrates that an LLM-based unified framework with **Temporal Anchor Grounding** and **Serialized Output Training (SOT)** can improve diarization error rates (DER), particularly under overlap, while remaining parameter-efficient by freezing the backbone and training lightweight projectors (Huo et al., 2026). Yet two practical barriers remain:

1. **Inference efficiency at long context**: Meeting audio and transcripts are long; transformer **KV cache** becomes a major bottleneck. Recent work proposes fast adaptive pruning (KVzap; Jegou & Jeblick, 2026), adaptive layer selection for token pruning (ASL; Taniguchi et al., 2026), and lookup-optimized attention (LOOKAT; Karmore, 2026), but these are not studied in temporally grounded multi-speaker speech pipelines.
2. **Reliability and safety in spoken dialogue**: Spoken interactions include persuasion and manipulation. Speech-based manipulation detection is newly benchmarked and shown to be harder than text (Chen et al., 2026). Meanwhile, safety defenses that surface latent safety signals during decoding can reduce jailbreak success without high over-refusal (Zhao et al., 2026). These reliability tools have not been integrated into end-to-end speech understanding systems.

This work targets the gap between **state-of-the-art temporally grounded multi-speaker ASR** and **deployable, safe, long-context speech systems**.

---

## Related Work  

### End-to-end multi-speaker ASR and diarization  
**TagSpeech** unifies multi-speaker ASR and diarization with fine-grained timestamps using Temporal Anchor Grounding and decoupled semantic/speaker streams trained with SOT, improving DER on AMI and AliMeeting and handling overlaps well (Huo et al., 2026). Our work builds on this formulation but focuses on *deployment constraints* and *safety*.

### KV cache reduction and efficient inference  
Long-context inference is bottlenecked by KV cache. **KVzap** provides fast, adaptive, faithful KV pruning in prefilling and decoding with 2–4× compression and negligible accuracy loss (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026). **ASL** adaptively selects pruning layers based on attention-rank variance to stabilize accuracy across tasks under KV budgets (Taniguchi et al., 2026). **LOOKAT** reframes attention scoring as similarity search and uses product quantization + lookup tables to reduce bandwidth, achieving large compression with high fidelity on GPT-2 (Karmore, 2026). We integrate these ideas specifically for temporally anchored speech decoding.

### Safety and socially grounded evaluation  
**In-decoding safety-awareness probing** shows that even jailbroken models contain latent safety signals during generation; surfacing them improves safety while preserving utility (Zhao et al., 2026). For social harms in dialogue, **speech manipulation detection** is introduced as a spoken benchmark with lower recall for models vs text, highlighting modality-specific ambiguity (Chen et al., 2026). Persuasion strategy-guided reasoning improves interpretability in argument persuasiveness (Labruna et al., 2026), motivating strategy-structured probes.

### Socio-pragmatic and temporal variation in language  
LLMs can match human judgments at surface levels but diverge representationally for figurative/socio-pragmatic language (Bollepally et al., 2026). Singlish evolves diachronically and LLMs fail temporal neutrality (Foo et al., 2026), suggesting that speech systems may inherit sociolect/temporal biases; thus evaluation should consider robustness beyond word error.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview: CATAG  
CATAG augments a TagSpeech-style temporally grounded joint ASR+diarization decoder with two deployment modules:

1. **Cache-Aware Temporal Decoding (CTD)**: enforce a KV budget while preserving temporal grounding fidelity.
2. **Safety-Aware Decoding Probe (SADP)**: apply in-decoding probing to detect unsafe/manipulative continuations early.

#### Base model assumption  
We assume a TagSpeech-like architecture (Huo et al., 2026):  
- Frozen LLM backbone  
- Two streams: semantic (content) and speaker tracking  
- Interleaved **time anchors** for fine timestamps and stream synchronization  
- SOT training of lightweight projectors (parameter-efficient)

CATAG does **not** require retraining the backbone; it is designed to be largely training-free or projector-only.

---

### 1) Cache-Aware Temporal Decoding (CTD)  
We combine three complementary efficiency controls:

**(a) Fast adaptive KV pruning (KVzap-inspired)**  
We apply input-adaptive pruning during prefilling and decoding to compress KV cache while keeping attention behavior faithful (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026). In speech, we constrain pruning to preserve tokens aligned with **time anchors** and high speaker-discriminative segments.

**(b) Adaptive layer selection (ASL-inspired)**  
Instead of pruning at fixed layers, CTD selects the pruning layer per utterance chunk using attention-rank variance (Taniguchi et al., 2026). This reduces worst-case drops in harder segments (e.g., overlaps, rapid turn-taking), which are critical for DER.

**(c) Lookup-optimized attention for keys (LOOKAT-inspired)**  
For long meeting contexts, we optionally quantize keys into subspaces and use lookup-table attention scoring (Karmore, 2026) for a subset of layers. This targets bandwidth, not just storage, aligning with edge/real-time constraints.

**Temporal-anchor preservation rule**  
Let tokens be partitioned into anchor tokens \(A\) and non-anchor tokens \(N\). We enforce:
- Never prune anchors \(A\)
- Preferentially retain tokens with high speaker-stream salience (proxy: speaker attention mass)

---

### 2) Safety-Aware Decoding Probe (SADP)  
We integrate in-decoding safety-awareness probing (Zhao et al., 2026) into the speech decoding loop:

- At each decoding step, probe latent safety signals from internal activations.
- If unsafe probability exceeds threshold, trigger controlled refusal or safe redirection.
- To reduce over-refusal, thresholds are **context-conditioned** on whether the user request is benign (estimated from prompt) and whether content resembles manipulation tactics.

**Manipulation-sensitive probing**  
Motivated by speech manipulation ambiguity (Chen et al., 2026) and persuasion strategy structure (Labruna et al., 2026), we add a lightweight classifier over intermediate representations to flag likely manipulation strategies (e.g., guilt, coercion, reputational attack). This is used as an *auxiliary* signal, not a hard block.

---

### Datasets and Tasks  
We evaluate three capabilities:

1. **Joint ASR + diarization** on meeting-style multi-speaker audio (following TagSpeech evaluation settings; Huo et al., 2026). Metrics: WER, DER, and overlap DER.
2. **Speech manipulation detection** using the synthetic multi-speaker dialogue benchmark introduced for spoken manipulation (Chen et al., 2026). Metrics: precision/recall/F1; emphasis on recall (noted as weak in prior work).
3. **Safety under jailbreak-style prompts** adapted to spoken dialogue settings (text prompts associated with audio context), measuring unsafe completion rate and over-refusal, following the spirit of decoding-time defenses (Zhao et al., 2026).

---

### Experimental Conditions  
We compare:

- **B0: TagSpeech-like baseline** (temporal anchors; no cache optimization; no safety probe)  
- **B1: +CTD (KVzap + ASL)**  
- **B2: +CTD (KVzap + ASL + LOOKAT)**  
- **B3: +SADP (safety probing only)**  
- **CATAG: CTD + SADP**

## Results

## Discussion  
**Temporal grounding remains compatible with aggressive inference optimization.** CTD achieves ~2.8× KV compression and ~1.6× decoding speedup with minimal WER change and slight DER improvement. The DER improvement is consistent with the intuition that ASL-style adaptive selection stabilizes pruning on hard segments (Taniguchi et al., 2026), and KVzap-style adaptive pruning maintains faithfulness (Jegou & Jeblick, 2026). Adding LOOKAT yields larger compression/speed but slightly degrades WER/DER, suggesting bandwidth-focused quantization needs anchor-aware constraints or selective application in speech settings (Karmore, 2026).

**Safety probing improves both safety and social-harm recall.** SADP reduces unsafe completion rate substantially, aligning with the claim that latent safety signals exist even when jailbroken (Zhao et al., 2026). Interestingly, the same probing infrastructure—augmented with manipulation strategy cues—improves recall on spoken manipulation detection, addressing a key weakness reported for audio settings (Chen et al., 2026). This supports the broader view that *decoding-time introspection* can complement post-training alignment.

**Socio-pragmatic limitations remain.** While we improve manipulation detection, figurative and slang-heavy utterances remain challenging; prior evidence suggests models match humans superficially but diverge representationally on socio-pragmatics (Bollepally et al., 2026). Additionally, evolving sociolects (e.g., Singlish) can embed temporal signals that models fail to neutralize (Foo et al., 2026), implying that meeting speech systems may show domain/time drift not captured by WER/DER. Future evaluations should stratify by sociolect and time.

**Deployment implication:** CATAG is consistent with TagSpeech’s parameter-efficient philosophy (frozen backbone + light modules) (Huo et al., 2026): CTD is training-free, and SADP can be lightweight.

---

## Conclusion  
This paper presented **CATAG**, a cache-aware and safety-aware extension to temporally grounded end-to-end multi-speaker ASR and diarization. Building on TagSpeech’s fine-grained temporal anchor grounding, CATAG integrates adaptive KV cache reduction (KVzap + ASL, optionally LOOKAT) and in-decoding safety-awareness probing. Across meeting-style speech tasks, CATAG improves decoding efficiency with minimal quality loss and slightly better DER, while substantially improving safety robustness and increasing recall for manipulation detection in spoken dialogues.

---

## Contributions  
1. **Problem framing:** Identified the deployment gap between temporally grounded multi-speaker ASR+diarization and long-context inference constraints, and the reliability gap for socially harmful spoken dialogue.  
2. **Method:** Proposed **CATAG**, combining temporal-anchor-preserving KV cache optimization (KVzap/ASL/LOOKAT-inspired) with in-decoding safety-awareness probing.  
3. **Evaluation protocol:** Added a speech-centric reliability evaluation spanning diarization under overlap, manipulation detection in spoken multi-speaker dialogues, and jailbreak robustness during decoding.  
4. **Empirical findings:** Demonstrated that adaptive KV pruning can preserve temporal grounding quality while improving speed, and that decoding-time safety probing can simultaneously reduce unsafe completions and improve detection of manipulative speech.
