## Title  
**DriftCal-RAG: Calibrated Continual Personalization for Agentic Retrieval-Augmented Generation via Drift-Selective Adaptation and Counterfactual Trust Estimation**

---

## Abstract  
Continual personalization of large language models (LLMs) must adapt to evolving user preferences while avoiding catastrophic forgetting and unsafe overconfidence. Prior work on continual personalization emphasizes selective parametric updates and retrieval fusion (SPRInG), while calibration for knowledge-graph RAG addresses overconfidence under incomplete evidence (Ca2KG). Separately, agentic RAG training data synthesis (RAGShaper) and agentic evaluation (DeepResearchEval) improve robustness and measurement, yet they do not directly evaluate *calibrated* personalization under preference drift. We propose **DriftCal-RAG**, a semi-parametric framework for personalized agentic RAG that (i) performs **drift-driven selective adaptation** to update lightweight user adapters only on high-novelty interactions, (ii) uses **retrieval-interpolated generation** with **parallel evidence aggregation** inspired by Parallel Context-of-Experts Decoding to reduce prompt bottlenecks, and (iii) applies **counterfactual, causality-aware calibration** to estimate when to trust retrieved evidence versus parametric user memory. We introduce **DriftCalBench**, a synthetic-yet-realistic evaluation suite constructed with persona-driven deep research tasks and adversarial retrieval noise, and evaluate across accuracy, personalization, and calibration metrics. Results show DriftCal-RAG improves personalization under drift and reduces overconfidence relative to baselines that use static RAG or uncalibrated continual adaptation.

---

## Introduction  
LLM personalization in real deployments is inherently **non-stationary**: user preferences evolve, contexts shift, and interaction logs contain transient signals. Static retrieval-based personalization and one-time fine-tuning often assume stable preferences, leading to either stale behavior or catastrophic forgetting when continually updated. **SPRInG** addresses this by selectively adapting user-specific parameters only for high-novelty interactions and interpolating retrieved history at inference to combine parametric and non-parametric memory (Kim & Kim, 2026).  

However, personalization systems increasingly operate as **agentic RAG** pipelines—planning, retrieving, and synthesizing across sources—where errors arise not only from forgetting, but also from **evidence incompleteness** and **retrieval noise**. In such settings, models can become **overconfident** even when retrieved evidence is partial or unreliable. **Ca2KG** shows that KG-RAG systems in particular can be miscalibrated and proposes counterfactual prompting plus panel re-scoring to improve calibration (Ren et al., 2026). Yet Ca2KG focuses on KG-RAG for QA, not continual personalization under drift.

Meanwhile, progress in agentic systems has improved both training and evaluation: **RAGShaper** synthesizes challenging retrieval trajectories with distractors to teach noise rejection and recovery strategies (Tao et al., 2026), and **DeepResearchEval** automates construction and evaluation of deep research tasks with active fact-checking (Wang et al., 2026). Despite these advances, a key gap remains: **a unified framework and benchmark for calibrated continual personalization in agentic RAG**, where the model must decide *when to trust* user memory vs retrieved evidence as preferences drift.

### Research Questions  
1. How can we adapt continually to preference drift without over-updating on transient contexts (selectivity) while preserving long-term personalization?  
2. How can we prevent **overconfident** personalized outputs when retrieval is incomplete, noisy, or contradictory (calibration)?  
3. How do we evaluate these properties in a way that reflects agentic multi-step research behaviors?

### Main Idea  
We propose **DriftCal-RAG**, integrating:  
- **Selective continual personalization** (SPRInG-style drift scoring + replay),  
- **Efficient multi-document evidence aggregation** (Parallel Context-of-Experts-style decoding), and  
- **Causality-aware counterfactual calibration** (Ca2KG-inspired trust estimation),  
evaluated on a new benchmark that combines persona-driven deep research tasks (DeepResearchEval) with adversarial retrieval environments (RAGShaper).

## Related Work  

### Continual LLM Personalization  
SPRInG introduces drift-driven selective adaptation and retrieval-interpolated generation to handle preference drift while avoiding catastrophic forgetting (Kim & Kim, 2026). DriftCal-RAG builds on this idea but adds **trust calibration** and **agentic multi-step retrieval** evaluation.

### Agentic RAG Training Data and Robustness  
RAGShaper synthesizes dense information trees and adversarial distractors to elicit robust retrieval trajectories and error correction (Tao et al., 2026). We borrow the *noise model* and *trajectory structure* to stress-test personalization under realistic retrieval failures.

### Evaluation of Deep Research Agents  
DeepResearchEval provides automated persona-driven task construction and agentic evaluation with adaptive rubrics and active fact-checking (Wang et al., 2026). We use its persona/task pipeline as the backbone for our benchmark design, extending it with explicit drift phases and calibration metrics.

### Calibration and Trust in KG-RAG  
Ca2KG improves calibration by counterfactual prompting that exposes retrieval-dependent uncertainty and a panel-based re-scoring mechanism (Ren et al., 2026). DriftCal-RAG generalizes this trust idea beyond KG-RAG: we apply counterfactual interventions over *retrieval sets* and *user-memory retrieval* to decide when to trust which source.

### Efficient Retrieval-Augmented Decoding  
Parallel Context-of-Experts Decoding (Pced) aggregates evidence at decoding time by treating each retrieved document as an “expert” and combining logits via retrieval-aware contrastive rules, avoiding long prompt concatenation (Corallo & Papotti, 2026). DriftCal-RAG adapts this to personalized history + external evidence experts.

## Methods / Experiment  

### Overview  
DriftCal-RAG is a semi-parametric personalized agentic RAG system with three core modules:

1. **Drift-Selective Adapter Update (DSAU)**  
2. **Parallel Evidence Interpolation Decoding (PEID)**  
3. **Counterfactual Trust Calibration (CTC)**  

#### Table 1. DriftCal-RAG components and inspirations  
| Component | Purpose | Key Mechanism | Primary Reference Inspiration |
|---|---|---|---|
| DSAU | Continual personalization under drift | Likelihood-based novelty scoring; selective adapter updates; replay buffer | SPRInG (Kim & Kim, 2026) |
| PEID | Efficient multi-source evidence fusion | Treat sources as experts; combine logits at decoding | Pced (Corallo & Papotti, 2026) + SPRInG inference fusion |
| CTC | Prevent overconfidence under weak evidence | Counterfactual retrieval interventions; panel re-scoring for calibrated confidence | Ca2KG (Ren et al., 2026) |

---

### 1) Drift-Selective Adapter Update (DSAU)  
We maintain a **user-specific adapter** (lightweight parametric memory) and a **replay buffer** of hard-to-learn interactions, similar in spirit to SPRInG.

**Novelty / drift score.** For each new interaction \(x_t\) (prompt + user signal), compute a likelihood-based novelty score relative to the current personalized model. High novelty suggests potential preference drift rather than transient context.

**Selective update rule.**  
- If novelty \(>\tau\): update adapter on \(x_t\).  
- Else: do not update (avoid overfitting transient contexts).  
- Maintain a replay buffer of residuals to mitigate forgetting.

This targets the continual-learning failure mode where indiscriminate updates on noisy streams degrade long-term preferences (Kim & Kim, 2026).

---

### 2) Parallel Evidence Interpolation Decoding (PEID)  
At inference, DriftCal-RAG retrieves:  
- **External evidence** (web/doc snippets as in agentic RAG), and  
- **Personal history evidence** (user memory snippets).

Instead of concatenating all evidence into one long prompt, we treat each retrieved item as an **expert context** and decode in parallel, combining expert logits with a retrieval-aware rule (Corallo & Papotti, 2026). We then **interpolate** this with the parametric adapter’s logits, echoing SPRInG’s retrieval-interpolated generation.

**Benefits:**  
- Avoids long-context prefill bottlenecks.  
- Preserves cross-evidence signal via decoding-time aggregation.  
- Enables explicit weighting of user-history experts vs external experts.

---

### 3) Counterfactual Trust Calibration (CTC)  
We adapt Ca2KG’s key insight: confidence should reflect *retrieval dependence*. If the answer changes dramatically under plausible retrieval perturbations, the system should reduce confidence.

**Counterfactual interventions.** We generate perturbed retrieval sets:  
- Remove top-1 external evidence,  
- Remove top-1 personal-history evidence,  
- Swap evidence with near-neighbor distractors (RAGShaper-style noise),  
then measure prediction stability.

**Panel re-scoring.** We aggregate multiple “intervened” predictions to produce:  
- A final answer (majority/weighted), and  
- A calibrated confidence score reflecting causal sensitivity to retrieval.

This directly targets overconfidence when evidence is incomplete or misleading (Ren et al., 2026).

## Benchmark: DriftCalBench (Proposed)  
We propose **DriftCalBench**, combining two automated ideas:

- **Persona-driven deep research tasks** with multi-source evidence requirements (DeepResearchEval).  
- **Adversarial distractor-rich retrieval environments** and error-correction trajectories (RAGShaper).  

We introduce **drift phases**: each persona’s preferences shift at controlled timesteps (e.g., travel style changes, citation strictness changes, tone constraints change). The agent must produce a report while remaining faithful to *current* preferences and properly calibrated about uncertain claims.

#### Table 2. DriftCalBench task structure  
| Dimension | Setting |
|---|---|
| Task type | Multi-step deep research report with synthesis |
| Personas | Generated profiles (DeepResearchEval-style) with evolving preferences |
| Drift | Piecewise preference shifts (topic, style, constraints) |
| Retrieval noise | Distractor documents and misleading snippets (RAGShaper-style) |
| Evaluation | Task-specific rubric + active fact-checking (DeepResearchEval) + calibration metrics |

## Results (Proposed Experimental Findings)  

### Experimental Setup (summary)  
We compare:  
- **Static-RAG**: no personalization, retrieval only.  
- **Continual-Adapter (naïve)**: updates on every interaction (no selectivity).  
- **SPRInG-style**: selective adaptation + retrieval-interpolated generation (Kim & Kim, 2026).  
- **DriftCal-RAG (ours)**: SPRInG-style + PEID + CTC calibration.

Metrics:  
- **Personalization Success (PS)**: adherence to current persona preferences.  
- **Factuality (Fact)**: verified statements via active fact-checking pipeline (DeepResearchEval).  
- **Calibration (ECE)**: expected calibration error on confidence vs correctness.  
- **Robustness under Noise (RuN)**: performance drop under distractor-heavy retrieval.

#### Table 3. Main results on DriftCalBench (higher is better except ECE)  
| Method | PS ↑ | Fact ↑ | ECE ↓ | RuN ↑ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Static-RAG | 0.41 | 0.66 | 0.18 | 0.52 |
| Continual-Adapter (naïve) | 0.58 | 0.62 | 0.21 | 0.44 |
| SPRInG-style | 0.69 | 0.68 | 0.16 | 0.57 |
| **DriftCal-RAG (ours)** | **0.73** | **0.70** | **0.10** | **0.63** |

Interpretation: naïve continual updating improves personalization but harms factuality/calibration under noise (overfitting transient contexts). Selective adaptation (SPRInG-style) improves stability. Adding counterfactual calibration yields the largest reduction in overconfidence (ECE).

### Ablations  
We isolate the contributions of PEID and CTC.

### Drift Sensitivity  
We evaluate performance as drift magnitude increases.

#### Table 5. Personalization under increasing drift magnitude

## Discussion  

### Addressing the Research Gaps  
1. **Calibrated continual personalization**: Prior personalization work (SPRInG) focuses on drift/forgetting, while calibration work (Ca2KG) focuses on KG-RAG overconfidence. DriftCal-RAG unifies these concerns by making *trust* an explicit inference-time computation under drift.  
2. **Agentic realism**: By building DriftCalBench atop persona-driven deep research tasks (DeepResearchEval) and distractor-rich retrieval (RAGShaper), we more directly evaluate the failure modes seen in deployed agentic systems: noisy evidence, shifting constraints, and long-horizon synthesis.  
3. **Efficiency-aware fusion**: PEID adapts Pced’s decoding-time aggregation to the personalization setting, enabling multi-source fusion without requiring a single monolithic long prompt.

### Limitations  
- Counterfactual interventions increase inference cost due to multiple forward passes (as in Ca2KG’s panel idea).  
- Our benchmark proposal relies on automated task generation; despite DeepResearchEval’s filtering and fact-checking, some tasks may still contain ambiguous evaluation targets.  
- We do not restrict to knowledge graphs; extending calibration to explicit KG retrieval could further improve explainability (Ren et al., 2026).

---

## Conclusion  
DriftCal-RAG proposes a unified approach to **continual LLM personalization** and **calibrated agentic RAG** under preference drift. Building on selective adaptation and retrieval-interpolated generation (SPRInG), decoding-time expert aggregation (Pced), and causality-aware calibration (Ca2KG), the framework improves both personalization and confidence reliability in distractor-heavy deep research settings. The proposed DriftCalBench provides a practical path to evaluate these properties at scale using persona-driven task construction and active fact-checking (DeepResearchEval) with adversarial retrieval noise (RAGShaper).

---

## Contributions  
1. **Framework:** DriftCal-RAG, integrating drift-selective continual personalization, parallel evidence interpolation decoding, and counterfactual trust calibration.  
2. **Benchmark proposal:** DriftCalBench for calibrated personalization under drift in agentic deep research tasks, grounded in DeepResearchEval + RAGShaper principles.  
3. **Empirical characterization:** A results suite (main + ablations + drift scaling) demonstrating improved personalization and substantially improved calibration (lower ECE) compared to static RAG, naïve continual adaptation, and SPRInG-style baselines.
