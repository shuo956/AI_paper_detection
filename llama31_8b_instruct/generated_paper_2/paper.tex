We propose a unified multi-task learning framework for predicting fault slip tendency and channel state information in subsurface operations and wireless communication systems. Our framework combines the strengths of data-space inversion and mixture-of-experts to learn a shared latent space that captures the underlying relationships between the two tasks. We evaluate our framework on a synthetic dataset for fault slip tendency prediction and a real-world dataset for channel state information prediction, demonstrating its ability to achieve state-of-the-art performance on both tasks.
}

\section{Introduction}

Predicting fault slip tendency and channel state information is crucial for subsurface operations and wireless communication systems. However, these tasks are often treated separately, and there is a lack of unified frameworks that can learn from both tasks simultaneously. In this paper, we propose a unified multi-task learning framework that combines the strengths of data-space inversion and mixture-of-experts to learn a shared latent space that captures the underlying relationships between the two tasks.
