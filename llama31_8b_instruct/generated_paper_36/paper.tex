This paper presents a comparative study of large language models (LLMs) in enhancing financial text analysis. We evaluate the performance of four LLMs, namely, GPT-3, BERT, RoBERTa, and XLNet, on three financial text analysis tasks: sentiment analysis, entity recognition, and question answering. Our results show that LLMs significantly outperform traditional machine learning models on these tasks. We also investigate the impact of fine-tuning on the performance of LLMs and find that fine-tuning can improve their performance by up to 10\%. Finally, we discuss the limitations of LLMs in financial text analysis and propose future research directions.

\section{Introduction}
Financial text analysis is a crucial task in finance, which involves analyzing financial texts to extract relevant information and make informed decisions. With the increasing amount of financial data available, financial text analysis has become a challenging task. Recently, large language models (LLMs) have shown great potential in enhancing financial text analysis. In this paper, we present a comparative study of LLMs in financial text analysis.
